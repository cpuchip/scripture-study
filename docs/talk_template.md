## Talk Principles

### Keep Christ Central
- Does this talk help people come unto Christ?
- Is the Savior's Atonement connected to the topic?
- Will this strengthen faith in Jesus Christ?

### Speak by the Spirit
- Am I prepared to adjust based on spiritual promptings?
- Have I invited the Spirit through my preparation?
- Am I relying on the Lord, not just my notes?

### Teach Doctrine
- Are my points grounded in scripture?
- Am I teaching truth, not just sharing stories?
- Will this help people understand God's plan?

### Invite Action
- Is there a clear invitation or application?
- Will listeners know what to do differently?
- Have I shared promised blessings?

## Practical Tips

### Timing
- Practice your talk out loud and time it
- Better to finish early than run over
- Leave buffer time for speaking slowly and pausing

### Delivery
- Speak clearly and at a measured pace
- Make eye contact with the congregation
- It's okay to look at your notes
- Pause after important points
- Let the Spirit guide adjustments

### What to Avoid
- Don't apologize for being asked to speak
- Avoid excessive humor or casual language
- Don't criticize Church leaders or policies
- Avoid controversial topics not assigned
- Don't exceed your time limit
