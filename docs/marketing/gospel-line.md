# The Gospel Line: When Does Sharing Cross Into Priestcraft?

Personal reflections on monetization, platform-building, and the priestcraft question — applied to my own work.

> **Related study:** [Priestcraft: Satan's Counterfeit](../study/priestcraft.md)

## The Question

I'm building things that cost real money and real time:
- A becoming website (hosting, development time)
- YouTube videos about the study process
- Teaching people about AI companionship for scripture study

Is charging for any of this priestcraft? Where's the line?

---

## The Scriptural Framework

From the priestcraft study, the three-element test (2 Nephi 26:29):

1. **Who is the light?** — Am I pointing people to Christ, or to myself/my method?
2. **What is the motive?** — Would I still do this if nobody paid?
3. **Who benefits?** — Does this build the kingdom or build my brand?

And McConkie's nuance: "The laborer is worthy of his hire." CES teachers are paid. Seminary teachers are paid. Authors receive royalties. The question is **which direction the service flows.**

## The Honest Questions

- Am I building a platform or building Zion?
- If the money went away, would the work continue?
- Am I sharing what the Spirit teaches me, or packaging it for consumption?
- Do people come away wanting to study their scriptures, or wanting to watch my next video?
- Is this Mosiah 18:26 — labor yielding grace and spiritual power — or Alma 1:3 — priests who ought to be popular and supported?
