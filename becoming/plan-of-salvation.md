# Becoming: Living Inside the Plan

> "Whatever principle of intelligence we attain unto in this life, it will rise with us in the resurrection." — [D&C 130:18](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/130.md)

*See also: [Study: Plan of Salvation](../study/plan-of-salvation/plan-of-salvation.md)*

---

## The Insight

The Plan of Salvation study revealed three things that change how I live:

1. **Mortality is a school, not just a test.** Webster 1828's "probation" includes "becoming qualified for a happier state." Every hard thing is curriculum, not punishment. Opposition is the gravity that builds the muscle.

2. **The comfort I can offer is real.** The promises for those who are sick and dying aren't platitudes — they're verified, sourced, and specific. Rest from suffering. Body restored. Relationships continue. God judges by desire. Nothing learned is lost. The door doesn't close.

3. **Judgment is about becoming, not just doing.** "They are their own judges" ([Alma 41:7](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/41.md)). The restoration principle means I'm building the person I'll be forever — right now, today, in the small choices.

## The Practice

### Reframing Difficulty

When something hard happens — illness, opposition, frustration:

| Old Response | New Response |
|-------------|-------------|
| "Why is this happening to me?" | "What is this meant to teach me?" |
| "I need to get through this." | "I need to learn from this." |
| "This is unfair." | "He shall consecrate thine afflictions for thy gain." |

Not toxic positivity. Real trust — the same trust that made us shout for joy at the prospect of this journey.

### Words for Friends

The study produced a comprehensive comfort section. But the real practice is *having the conversation*. Not sending a doctrinal essay — sitting with someone and finding the three sentences that the Spirit will carry.

**Practice:** Before visiting or calling a friend who is sick, write down what you would actually say. Not theology. Not footnotes. Human words. Then let the Spirit edit them.

**Some starting places** (distilled from the study):

- "The pain ends. The scriptures promise rest — real rest, from all troubles and all care."
- "You won't be alone there. The scriptures describe an innumerable company gathered together, conversing, rejoicing."
- "God sees your heart. He knows what you would have done. That counts."
- "The separation is temporary. Spirit and body will be reunited permanently."
- "Nothing you've become is lost. Everything you've learned rises with you."

These aren't scripts. They're seeds. The Spirit will tell you which one to plant.

### The Daily Restoration Check

Each evening, a one-minute review:

- What did I send out today — patience or irritation? Love or distraction? Curiosity or indifference?
- What came back?
- "Intelligence cleaveth unto intelligence; mercy hath compassion on mercy and claimeth her own" ([D&C 88:40](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)). The echo is real.
