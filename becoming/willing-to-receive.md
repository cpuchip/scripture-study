# Becoming: Willing to Receive

> "And they who remain shall also be quickened; nevertheless, they shall return again to their own place, to enjoy that which they are willing to receive, because they were not willing to enjoy that which they might have received." — [D&C 88:32](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)

*See also: [Study: What Was the Atonement For?](../study/what-was-the-atonement-for.md)*

---

## The Insight

I am in the 3 Nephi 6:18 category.

Not as accusation. As fact. I have been taught. I have read the death and sufferings of Christ in Nephi and Mormon and Paul. I have testified of His resurrection. I know the will of God concerning me in the matters where He has given me clear instruction — daily prayer, the sacrament, honesty, the way I treat my wife, the use of my time, the covenants I have made. The Mosiah 3:11 shelter does not cover those things for me. I will not be judged for whether I knew. I will be judged for what I did with the knowing.

That is a heavy reading on the surface. Underneath it is a lighter one.

The 3 Nephi 6:18 category is not the category of the sinless. It is the category of those who know enough to keep getting back up. The verse names *willful rebellion* — a hardening of the heart that refuses to depart from iniquity even when warned ([Mosiah 16:12](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/16.md), "they would not"). That is not what striving looks like. Striving is the verb of someone who *did* depart — and stumbled, and came back, and departed again. The willful rebel makes the refusal his settled posture. The striver keeps coming back to the arms of mercy and reaching.

So when I say "I willfully choose the Lord, though it looks more like striving, for I make mistakes a lot" — both halves are true. The willing is real. The mistakes are real. The willing does not require the mistakes to disappear before it counts.

What D&C 88:32 says is that *willingness to receive* is the criterion, not the cleanness of the receiver. The Lord will give me what I am willing to receive. He will not coerce a higher kingdom on a will that refused it. But he will also not withhold a higher kingdom from a will that kept reaching, kept asking, kept getting up, even while the flesh kept fumbling. The verb the Lord uses is *willing*. Not *flawless*. Not *finished*. Willing.

## The Practice

The commitment, then, is at the level of *receiving*, not *performing*.

Each week, pick one thing the Lord has been teaching me — through scripture, the Spirit, a calling, a conversation, a study — that I am still treating as optional. Move it into the not-optional column. Not because I will then do it perfectly. Because I am willing to receive it. The doing will look like striving. That is fine. Striving is what willing looks like in mortal flesh.

The aim is the Joseph F. Smith line:

> "[Our] obedience must be voluntary; it must not be forced, there must be no coercion. Men must not be constrained against their will to obey the will of God; they must obey it because they know it to be right, because they desire to do it, and because it is their pleasure to do it." — Joseph F. Smith, *Teachings of Presidents of the Church: Joseph F. Smith*, ch. 30

*Pleasure*. That is the target word. Not duty under threat. Not compliance under fear of the 3 Nephi 6:18 verdict. Pleasure. The pleasure of giving freely what cannot be given any other way than freely. The atonement is for that. So am I.

## The Reframe

Before this study, the verses about being taught read to me as warning verses. *Be careful what you learn; knowing makes you accountable.*

After this study, they read as gift verses. *The Lord is teaching you because eternal life is His life, and His life is voluntary love, and there is no way to enter that life except to learn enough to choose it.*

The teaching is not the trap. The teaching is the door.
