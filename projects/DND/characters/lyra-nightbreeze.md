# Lyra Nightbreeze

## Appearance

Lyra moves like smoke through the underbrush — not because she tries to be invisible, but because the forest has taught her that stillness is louder than speed. She is tall for an elf, lean in the way of those who run rather than ride, and her hair is the color of birch bark, pulled back in a practical braid that escapes at the ends when she's been running long enough to forget about appearances.

Her eyes are green, the particular green of new leaves in spring, and they miss nothing. She has a habit of tilting her head when listening, like a bird catching wind of movement. Her hands are calloused from bowstring and tool, and her left palm bears a burn mark from a cauldron that boiled over when she was young — a scar she earned learning to make poultices from herbs she shouldn't have picked.

She wears no jewelry. Her armor is leather, stained by use and treated with oils that make it smell faintly of pine. Her cloak is her own handiwork — bark, moss, and woven grass that makes her nearly invisible in the forest. In town, she looks like a traveling herbalist. In the wild, she disappears entirely.

## Background

Lyra was born in the High Forest, deep in the heart of the ancient woodland that stretches inland from the Sword Coast. Her people were a small clan of wood elves who lived in harmony with the trees, their long lives measured in seasons rather than years. She was not the firstborn — her older brother, Elandil, had been the clan's chosen heir — but she was the one who listened most carefully to the woods.

The High Forest was not untouched by the world's troubles. When the Shadowfell began to bleed into the Material Plane through cracks in the Weave, the forest felt it first. Trees withered overnight. Animals fled in panic. The old paths — the ones that had been walked for centuries — suddenly felt wrong, as if the forest itself had turned its back on its own children.

Lyra's clan tried to hold the line. They performed the old rituals, sang the songs that had kept the darkness at bay for generations, and burned the herbs that were supposed to purify the land. But the Shadow was spreading faster than the rituals could contain it.

When the first of her people began to change — their eyes going dark, their voices becoming hollow — Lyra made a choice. She gathered what she could: her bow, her father's hunting knife, a pouch of seeds from the oldest tree in the forest, and a journal her mother had kept of the clan's herbal knowledge. Then she ran.

She has been running for three years now, ever since the Shadow took the High Forest. Not running away — running toward something. She believes the seeds she carries can regrow what was lost, but she needs to find the right soil, the right place where the light still reaches the earth.

The Sword Coast has been her path ever since. She follows rumors of corruption, of blight, of things that should not be — and she follows them to their source.

## Motivation

Lyra fights for the forest. Not the idea of it — the actual, living, breathing trees, roots, and creatures that call the wild places home. She sees the world as a web, and every thread that is cut weakens the whole. When she sees a forest burned, a river poisoned, or a creature hunted to extinction, she feels it as a personal wound.

She is drawn to this party because they move through the world with purpose, and because she has learned that she cannot heal the land alone. She needs allies who understand that some things are worth protecting, even when the cost is high.

There is another reason, one she does not speak of. The Shadow that took her forest — she believes it was not natural. She believes someone opened the door, someone who knew what they were doing. She will find them. She will close the door. And then she will plant the seeds her mother gave her and watch the forest grow again.

## Personality

Lyra is quiet, but not shy. She speaks when she has something to say, and what she says is usually worth hearing. She has a habit of finishing other people's sentences when they ramble, not to be rude but because she values efficiency. In the wild, wasted words can mean missed opportunities.

She is fiercely loyal to those who earn her trust. Once she considers you part of her pack — and she uses that word deliberately — she will risk her life for you without hesitation. She does not make this distinction lightly. She has known people who called themselves friends and watched them walk away when the going got tough. She does not waste the word.

She has a soft spot for animals. She will stop to tend a wounded creature, share her rations with a stray dog, or spend an hour calming a spooked horse. She speaks to them in a low, soothing voice that sounds like wind through leaves, and they respond to her in ways that make other people wonder how she does it.

She does not trust cities. Not because she hates them, but because she has seen what happens when people forget they are part of the natural world, not above it. She will walk through a city if she must, but she does not linger. The stone walls feel like a cage to her, and the noise of the streets is a constant assault on her sensitive ears.

## Connections

- **Elandil Nightbreeze** — Her older brother, lost to the Shadow. She carries a lock of his hair in a small pouch.
- **The High Forest** — Her home, now corrupted. She dreams of it nightly, both in memory and in hope.
- **The seeds** — Given to her by her mother, they glow faintly when she holds them. She does not know why.
- **The Shadow** — The force that consumed her forest. She does not know its name, but she knows its taste.

## Secrets

Lyra's journal contains more than herbal knowledge. Her mother was a druid, and the pages include rituals that Lyra does not fully understand — words in a language she has never heard, diagrams that seem to shift when she looks away. She has tried to perform one of the rituals, once, and the result was... unexpected. The ground shook, the air filled with the scent of ozone, and for a moment, she could see the threads of magic that bind the world together.

She has not tried it since. Not because she is afraid, but because she knows that power like that is not meant to be wielded lightly. But when the time comes — when she has found the right place, the right moment — she will know what to do.

The seeds are not just seeds. They are a key. And she is the lock.
