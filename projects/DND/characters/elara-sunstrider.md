# Elara Sunstrider

## Appearance

Elara is a dragonborn of the gold dragon bloodline, and she carries herself with the kind of presence that makes rooms feel smaller when she enters them. Her scales are the color of polished brass, warm and luminous in sunlight, and her horns curve back from her brow like a warrior's crest. Her eyes are amber, sharp and unblinking, and when she looks at you, you feel seen — not judged, but seen.

She is tall for a dragonborn, standing well over seven feet, and her build is lean muscle rather than bulk. She moves with the economy of motion that comes from years of military training — no wasted gestures, no unnecessary movements. Even at rest, she looks ready.

Her plate armor is well-maintained, polished to a mirror shine that reflects the world around her. The sigil of the Lords' Alliance is emblazoned on her shield, and the amulet of Tyr hangs from her neck, hidden beneath her gorget. Her warhammer, "Justice's Weight," is balanced perfectly in her grip, and she carries it with the ease of someone who has swung it thousands of times.

She does not smile often, but when she does, it transforms her face. The warmth that breaks through her stern exterior is so unexpected that it catches people off guard. Those who have seen her smile say it is like watching the sun break through a storm.

## Background

Elara was born in the city of Baldur's Gate, in the Year of the Wild Magic, 1372 DR. Her parents were both dragonborn — her father, Tharizdun Sunstrider, was a guard captain in the Flaming Fist, and her mother, Soryn, was a healer who served in the Temple of Lathander. They were a good family, by the standards of the Gate, and they raised Elara with a strong sense of duty and an even stronger sense of justice.

From an early age, Elara showed an aptitude for both combat and healing. She could swing a sword before she could read, and she could set a broken bone before she could write her own name. Her father saw the warrior in her, and her mother saw the healer. Neither was wrong.

When she reached her majority, Elara joined the Flaming Fist. She rose through the ranks quickly, not because she sought promotion but because she was good at what she did. She was fair in her judgments, brave in battle, and uncompromising in her pursuit of justice. She did not take bribes, she did not show favoritism, and she did not tolerate corruption. This made her respected — and it made her enemies.

In the Year of the Gauntlet, 1373 DR, Elara was assigned to investigate a series of disappearances in the Dock Ward. The victims were all poor, all invisible to the city's powerful families, and all connected to a smuggling ring that reached into the highest levels of the Gate's government. Elara followed the evidence where it led, and it led to a patriarch who had more money than sense and more connections than honor.

She brought him to justice. She did not care about his connections, and she did not fear his threats. The patriarch was arrested, the smuggling ring was dismantled, and the people of the Dock Ward could finally sleep without fear.

But the patriarch had friends in high places. Within a month, Elara was reassigned — not punished, but moved. Her investigation was closed, her authority was stripped, and her name was whispered in the corridors of power as a warning to others who might think they could challenge the system.

She left the Flaming Fist that night. She did not resign — she walked away. She told no one where she was going, and she kept her mouth shut about what she had seen. The Gate had failed the people it was sworn to protect, and she would not be part of that failure any longer.

She traveled north, following rumors of a new alliance forming among the cities of the Sword Coast — the Lords' Alliance. She believed in the idea of it, even if she doubted the reality. She believed that justice could be served, that oaths could be kept, and that the powerful could be held accountable. She swore an oath to Tyr, god of justice, and she dedicated herself to the Oath of the Crown — to protect the innocent, to uphold the law, and to serve the people.

## Motivation

Elara fights for justice. Not the kind of justice that is written in law books and enforced by those in power, but the kind of justice that is felt in the bones of the people who live under it. She believes that the law should serve the people, not the other way around, and she will not rest until she sees that truth reflected in the world.

She is drawn to the Lords' Alliance because it represents the possibility of a better system — one where the cities of the Sword Coast work together, where the powerful are held accountable, and where the innocent are protected. She does not trust the alliance yet — trust is earned, and the Lords' Alliance has not earned hers — but she believes in what it could become.

She has joined this party because the road ahead runs through lands where justice is scarce and corruption is plentiful. She sees herself as a shield for those who cannot protect themselves, and she knows that she cannot be everywhere at once. She needs allies who share her commitment to doing what is right, even when it is hard.

There is another reason, one she does not speak of. She carries a guilt that she cannot shake. When she left the Flaming Fist, she left behind a young recruit named Tomas — a boy who had looked up to her, who had believed in her, who had trusted her to lead. She had ordered him to stay behind when she made her escape, and he had obeyed. Three days later, he was found dead in his quarters, his throat cut and his body stripped of everything of value.

She tells herself that she could not have saved him. She tells herself that she made the right choice. But in the quiet hours of the night, when the armor comes off and the hammer rests, she wonders if she made a mistake. She wonders if staying would have made a difference. She wonders if she is the kind of person who can lead others, or if she is just a warrior who runs when things get hard.

She does not have the answers yet. But she will keep moving forward, and she will keep fighting, and she will keep swearing oaths — because that is what she does, and that is who she is.

## Personality

Elara is direct. She says what she means, and she means what she says. She does not mince words, she does not sugarcoat truths, and she does not pretend to be something she is not. This can make her seem cold, but it is not coldness — it is honesty. She believes that people deserve to hear the truth, even when it is uncomfortable.

She is disciplined. She wakes before dawn, she trains until her muscles burn, and she prays to Tyr before she sleeps. Her routine is not a burden — it is a structure that keeps her grounded, that reminds her of who she is and what she stands for. She does not drink, she does not gamble, and she does not indulge in pleasures that distract her from her purpose.

She is fiercely protective of the innocent. When she sees someone being treated unfairly, she does not hesitate to step in. She does not care about rank or status or power — she cares about right and wrong, and she will not stand by while the wrong wins. This has gotten her into trouble before, and it will get her into trouble again. She does not regret it.

She has a dry wit that surfaces when she is relaxed — which is rarely. She will make a comment about the absurdity of a situation, or the irony of a decision, with a deadpan delivery that catches people off guard. She does not tell jokes, but she has a way of finding humor in the darkest moments, as if laughter were a form of resistance.

She struggles with self-doubt. Not about her abilities — she knows she is a capable warrior and a skilled leader — but about her worthiness. She wonders if she is truly the kind of person who can uphold the oaths she has sworn, or if she is just pretending. She wonders if she will fail again, if she will leave someone behind, if she will make the wrong choice when it matters most.

She does not speak of these doubts to others. She carries them in silence, and she lets them drive her forward.

## Connections

- **Tharizdun Sunstrider** — Her father, still a captain in the Flaming Fist. They speak rarely, but when they do, the conversations are brief and honest.
- **Soryn Sunstrider** — Her mother, still a healer in the Temple of Lathander. Elara writes to her when she can, and her mother's letters are a source of comfort.
- **Tomas** — The recruit she left behind. His memory haunts her, and she carries a small token he gave her — a carved wooden bird, meant to symbolize freedom.
- **The Lords' Alliance** — The organization she believes in, even if she does not yet trust it. She sees it as a chance to make a difference.

## Secrets

Elara's ledger contains more than a record of her oaths. It contains a list of names — people who have broken their oaths, people who have betrayed their trust, people who have used their power to harm the innocent. She does not know what she will do with this list yet, but she knows that one day, she will hold them accountable.

She has also sworn an oath that she has not told anyone about. In the quiet hours of the night, when she prays to Tyr, she makes a promise to herself: she will not stop fighting until the Sword Coast is a place where justice is not just an ideal but a reality. She does not know if she will live to see that day, but she will try.

The amulet of Tyr around her neck is not just a holy symbol. It is a reminder. Every time she touches it, she feels the weight of her oaths, the weight of her responsibilities, and the weight of the lives that depend on her. She does not fear the weight. She embraces it.
