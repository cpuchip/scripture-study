# Kaelen Ironheart

## Appearance

Kaelen stands with the square shoulders and iron jaw typical of his people, though his beard is braided with copper wire rather than the gold his father might have chosen. His eyes are the color of storm clouds over the Spine of the World, and when he squints — which is often — they narrow to slits that miss nothing. A scar runs from his left temple to his jawline, a souvenir from the night Mithral Hall fell. He moves with the deliberate precision of someone who has spent a lifetime measuring twice and cutting once.

He wears a cloak of deep blue wool, the color of the deep places of the earth, and the shield on his back bears the Ironheart sigil: a hammer striking an anvil, encircled by a ring of flame.

## Background

Kaelen was born in Mithral Hall in the Year of the Banner, 1360 DR, the eldest son of Thardir Ironheart, master smith of the Ironheart clan. The Ironhearts were not the most powerful family in the hold, but they were respected — their forges produced weapons that held their edge through campaigns and armor that turned blows that would have killed lesser men. His mother, Brinveig, was a scholar of the old ways, keeper of the clan's genealogies and oral histories.

The fall of Mithral Hall came when Kaelen was twenty-five. The drow assault, led by the assassin regent Jarlaxle's former master, shattered the hold's defenses in a single night. Kaelen was away on a commission in Mirabar when the attack came. He arrived three days too late to fight, and three days too early to grieve — the halls were still warm, still reeking of smoke and blood, but the living were gone.

His father had sent him away. "The smith who stays at the forge dies with it," Thardir had said, pressing Dawnbreaker into Kaelen's hands. "The smith who returns builds what comes next."

Kaelen survived. The guilt of that survival has shaped him since.

He spent two years in Mirabar, working the forges there, rebuilding what he could of his strength and his focus. But the city felt hollow without Mithral Hall's echoes. The other dwarves there spoke in hushed tones about the fall, about the drow, about the Shining Lights that had once been the pride of the North. None of them knew what it was to have been inside. None of them had felt the stone breathe beneath their feet.

In the Year of the Gauntlet, 1372 DR, Kaelen joined a mercenary company heading south toward the Sword Coast. He told himself it was to earn coin, to gain experience, to become stronger. The truth was simpler: he needed to be somewhere where the ground still felt solid beneath his boots.

## Motivation

Kaelen fights for two things, and they are intertwined.

First, he is driven by the oath he swore over his father's empty forge: to rebuild the Ironheart name. Not the fortune — though that would be welcome — but the reputation. The word "Ironheart" should mean something in the North again. It should mean unbreakable.

Second, and more quietly, he carries a debt to the dead. Every dwarf who died at Mithral Hall, every life lost in that night, rests on his shoulders because he was the one who walked away. He does not speak of this to others. But when a battle ends and the immediate danger passes, Kaelen sometimes touches the hammer at his belt and whispers a single Dwarvish word: *Dun* — "Stone." It is both a prayer and a promise.

He has joined this party because the road ahead runs through lands he does not know, and because the people he travels with remind him that not all dwarven steel was broken that night.

## Personality

Kaelen is patient to a fault. He listens before he speaks, measures before he acts, and plans before he strikes. In battle, he is methodical — reading opponents, finding weaknesses, exploiting gaps with the same precision he once brought to shaping metal. His Battle Master training is less a choice than a natural extension of how his mind works.

He has a dry wit that catches people off guard. He will deliver a cutting observation about a situation with the same tone he uses to comment on the weather, and the contrast is so sharp it sometimes draws laughter he never intended.

He does not trust easily. Not because he is cynical, but because he has learned that trust is earned the same way good steel is — through repeated testing. He watches how people act under pressure, how they treat those weaker than themselves, and whether they keep their word when no one is watching.

## Connections

- **Brinveig Ironheart** — His mother, lost at Mithral Hall. Her journals, somehow preserved, are among his most treasured possessions.
- **Thardir Ironheart** — His father, master smith. Kaelen carries his voice in his head when he hammers metal.
- **Mirabar** — The city that gave him shelter after the fall. The forges there still sound like home, though they are not home.
- **The drow** — Kaelen does not hate them. He understands them. They are what they are. But he will not forget what they took.

## Secrets

Kaelen's grandfather forged Dawnbreaker using ore from the deepest levels of Mithral Hall — ore that had been blessed by the old gods of the deep places. The hammer has responded to Kaelen's presence in ways he cannot explain. It glows faintly when near ancient dwarven magic, and once, in a dream, he heard his grandfather's voice speaking through it. He has not told anyone about this. Not yet.
