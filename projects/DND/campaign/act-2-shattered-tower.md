# Act 2: The Shattered Tower

## The Dungeon Structure

The Shattered Tower has three accessible levels, plus a sealed lower level that the party cannot reach yet. Each level is an escape-room challenge — the party must solve puzzles to progress, and failure has consequences that are real but not lethal.

**Time pressure:** The tower's magic is unstable. Every hour inside, the party must make a DC 12 Wisdom save or suffer one level of exhaustion from magical radiation. Resting inside the tower does not remove this exhaustion — they must leave and return. This encourages efficient play.

---

## Level 1: The Outer Ruins

**Appearance:** Collapsed walls, scattered stones, magic crackling in the air. The entrance is a拱 door, half-buried, glowing faintly blue.

### Trap 1: The Warning (Visible, Avoidable)

The first ten feet of the entrance are littered with stones that hum when touched. A DC 10 Wisdom (Perception) check notices the hum. Touching a stone triggers a **glyph of warding** — 2d4 force damage, DC 13 Dex save for half.

**This trap is meant to be noticed.** It teaches the party that the tower's defenses are active, magical, and predictable.

### Puzzle 1: The Three Stones

**The entrance seals behind the party.** The only way forward is through a doorway blocked by three floating stones, each inscribed with a symbol:

- **Stone 1:** A hammer striking an anvil (Kaelen recognizes this — the Ironheart sigil, but older)
- **Stone 2:** A tree with roots reaching into water (Lyra feels a pull — the Neverwood)
- **Stone 3:** An eye watching through flame (Malachi's orb pulses — his patron knows this symbol)

**The puzzle:** The party must touch the stones in the correct order. The order is not random — it is based on the tower's history:

1. **Tree** (the land came first)
2. **Hammer** (the tower was built upon the land)
3. **Eye** (the seal was activated last)

**Clues:**
- Inscriptions on the wall describe the tower's founding: *"First the earth, then the stone, then the watch."*
- Tilly can read the inscriptions with a DC 14 Intelligence (Arcana) check
- Finn's music resonates with the stones — playing a minor chord makes the correct stone glow faintly

**Failure:** Wrong order triggers a **force wave** — 3d6 force damage, DC 14 Dex save for half. The stones reset. The party can try again, but the tower shakes, and the time pressure increases (add one level of exhaustion to everyone).

**Success:** The stones sink into the floor. The doorway opens. A voice speaks — *"Welcome, travelers. You are early."* This is the **first voice** Remi warned about. It is an illusion, a test. The party should not trust it.

## Level 2: The Hall of Echoes

**Appearance:** A long corridor, walls intact, floor cracked. The air is thick with magic, and every sound echoes three times — but the echoes are not identical. They are slightly different, as if the corridor is rewriting what was said.

### Trap 2: The Assumption (Less Visible, Learnable)

The floor tiles are marked with symbols. Some tiles are safe. Some trigger **magic missile** attacks (3 missiles, unsaveable). The pattern is not random — it follows a sequence based on the Weave's colors:

- **Blue tiles** are safe (abjuration, protection)
- **Red tiles** trigger traps (evocation, damage)
- **Green tiles** are safe but slow (conjuration, delay)
- **Purple tiles** are unknown (the party has not seen these before)

**Tilly can identify the pattern** with a DC 15 Intelligence (Arcana) check. She has studied the Weave, and the colors match the schools of magic. Without her, the party must test tiles carefully — each failed test triggers a trap but reveals the pattern.

**This trap teaches the party to rely on Tilly's expertise.** If they ignore her and charge ahead, they pay for it. If they listen, they learn.

### Puzzle 2: The Echo Chamber

**At the end of the corridor is a door with no handle.** Above it, an inscription reads:

> *"Speak your truth, and the door will open. Speak a lie, and the door will close. Speak nothing, and the door will wait."*

**The puzzle:** Each party member must speak one true thing about themselves — not a fact, but a truth. A vulnerability. A fear. A regret. The door judges honesty, not content.

**Mechanics:** Each character makes a DC 12 Wisdom (Insight) check to determine if their statement is "true enough." Fail, and the door shakes, the echoes grow louder, and the character must try again. Three failures trigger a **phantasmal killer** illusion — the character's deepest fear, manifested. It deals no real damage, but it imposes frightened condition until the character speaks a true statement.

**Suggested truths** (guided by character backstories):
- **Kaelen:** "I survived Mithral Hall, and I do not deserve to have lived."
- **Lyra:** "I ran from my forest, and I have not been brave since."
- **Malachi:** "I made a pact to save my mother, and I do not know if it was worth it."
- **Elara:** "I left Tomas behind, and I do not know if staying would have saved him."
- **Finn:** "I play music to connect with others, but I am afraid no one is listening."
- **Tilly:** "I caused Oren's death, and I do not know how to forgive myself."

**The first voice returns:** *"You do not need to do this. I can open the door for you. Just trust me."* This is the illusion, trying to shortcut the puzzle. If the party trusts the voice, the door opens — but it leads to a dead end, and the party loses an hour (exhaustion increases). The voice is helpful but dishonest. Remi's warning applies.

**Success:** The door opens. The party enters the tower's heart.

## Level 3: The Seal Chamber

**Appearance:** A circular room, walls lined with runes, floor marked with a pentagram. In the center, a pedestal holds a crystal — the seal's core. It is cracked, leaking magic like light from a broken lamp.

**But there is a complication:** A scholar is trapped in the chamber — **Oren's mentor from Candlekeep**, an old gnome named **Professor Vell** who came to study the seal and was trapped when the tower shifted. He is alive, but weakened, and he is holding a piece of the seal's mechanism. Without him, the seal cannot be stabilized. Without the mechanism, he cannot leave.

### Puzzle 3: The Choice

**The party must choose:**
- **Stabilize the seal** — requires all six party members to activate runes around the chamber. Professor Vell must let go of the mechanism, and he will be trapped until the seal is fully repaired (weeks, possibly months).
- **Rescue the scholar** — requires the party to carefully extract Vell and the mechanism, but the seal will continue to weaken. The party can buy time (10 days) but not solve the problem.

**There is no perfect choice.** The DM should present both options clearly, give the party time to deliberate, and let them decide. Their choice will have consequences:

- **If they stabilize the seal:** Vell is trapped. He is grateful, but he is also a scholar of immense knowledge. The party loses access to his expertise for the rest of the campaign. Seal #1 is secure.
- **If they rescue Vell:** He joins the party as an NPC ally, providing knowledge and guidance. But Seal #1 continues to weaken, and the party must find another way to address it later. The goblin raids intensify.

### Puzzle 4: The Rune Circle (If stabilizing the seal)

**If the party chooses to stabilize the seal,** they must activate six runes around the chamber — one per party member. Each rune requires a specific action:

| Rune | Required Action | DC | Skill |
|------|----------------|-----|-------|
| Hammer | Strike the rune with a metal object | 12 | Strength (Athletics) |
| Tree | Place a natural object (leaf, seed, wood) on the rune | 10 | Wisdom (Nature) |
| Eye | Speak a word of power (Infernal, Celestial, or Primordial) | 14 | Intelligence (Arcana) |
| Shield | Cast a protective spell or use a protective item | 12 | Wisdom (Religion) |
| Song | Play a musical instrument or sing a true note | 11 | Charisma (Performance) |
| Crystal | Channel magic through an arcane focus | 13 | Intelligence (Arcana) |

**The runes must be activated in sequence,** and the sequence is not obvious. The correct order is: Tree → Hammer → Shield → Song → Eye → Crystal (land, structure, protection, harmony, awareness, activation).

**Clues:** Professor Vell can guide the party, but he is weakened. He can give two clues for free. Additional clues require the party to use a healing potion on him (restores his strength temporarily).

**Failure:** Wrong sequence triggers a **magic surge** — 4d6 radiant damage to the entire party, DC 15 Con save for half. The runes reset. The party can try again, but the crystal cracks further, and the time pressure increases.

**Success:** The crystal seals. The magic stabilizes. The tower stops shaking. Seal #1 is secure — for now.

## The Vision

**Whether the party stabilizes the seal or rescues Vell,** they receive a vision as they leave the tower:

**The vision shows:**
- The five seals, glowing faintly on a map of the Sword Coast
- Seal #1 (the Tower) — stable or cracking, depending on their choice
- Seal #2 (Sunspring Villa) — weakened, cult activity visible
- Seal #3 (Neverwood Blight) — active spread, the Blight consuming the forest
- Seal #4 (Undermountain) — stable but threatened, shadows moving in the deep
- Seal #5 (Cormyrean Milestone) — unknown, hidden behind a veil of fog

**A voice speaks** — not the first voice, but a different one, older, warmer:

> *"You have seen the seals. You have made your choice. The coast depends on what you do next. Remember: you cannot save all five. Choose wisely, for the two you abandon will remember your name."*

The vision ends. The party is outside the tower. The sun is rising. The road ahead is long.

## Road Encounter: The Return Journey

**On the way back to Daggerford,** the party faces one final challenge:

### Road Encounter: The Goblin Ambush (Reprise)

The goblins attack again, but this time they are desperate — the seal's stabilization (or continued weakening) has affected their magic siphons, and they are retaliating.

**Combat:**
- 8 Goblin Bandits (CR 1/4 each) — enraged, fighting to the death
- 2 Goblin Sorcerers (CR 2 each) — casting *shatter* and *lightning bolt*
- 1 Goblin Boss (CR 1) — carries a magic siphon device, will flee if reduced to 0 HP

**Tactics:** The goblins are coordinated, aggressive, and unwilling to retreat. This is a harder fight than Act 1's ambush, testing the party's growth.

**After the fight:** The goblin boss drops a letter — addressed to someone in the Sunspring Villa, mentioning "the warm wells" and "the second seal." The party now knows about Seal #2.
