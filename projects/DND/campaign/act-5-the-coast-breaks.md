# Act 5: The Coast Breaks

## The Setup

The party races to **Waterdeep** as Neverember moves to seize the released seal power. The campaign converges: every thread, every choice, every relationship leads to this moment.

**Time pressure:** 7 days. Neverember has activated his final plan. The seals are failing in sequence, and the Cormyrean Milestone is the last line of defense.

---

## Session 1: Waterdeep — The City Trembles

### Scene 1: The Dragonward Falters

**Trigger:** The party arrives in Waterdeep to find the city in panic. The dragonward — the magical barrier that has protected Waterdeep for centuries — is flickering. Without it, the city is vulnerable to dragon attack, magical assault, and the pressure building beneath Undermountain.

**Laeral Silverhand** addresses the city from the Open Lord's Palace:
> *"The wards are failing. The source is Undermountain. I need volunteers to descend into the dungeon and stabilize the deep seals. I cannot send the Watchwarriors — they are needed above ground. This is a mission for adventurers."*

**This is Elara's moment.** As a Paladin of the Crown, she is exactly the kind of person Laeral needs. But Elara must choose: lead the mission to Undermountain (Seal #4) or protect the Cormyrean Milestone (Seal #5). She cannot do both.

---

### Scene 2: The Faction Summit

**The party is summoned to a secret meeting** in the Castle Ward, hosted by Remi Haventree. Representatives from the Five Factions are present:

| Faction | Representative | Stance |
|---------|---------------|--------|
| Harpers | Remi Haventree | Protect the Milestone at all costs |
| Order of the Gauntlet | General Esvele Greycrone | Assault Undermountain, stop Neverember |
| Emerald Enclave | Warden representative | Heal the Blight's damage, protect nature |
| Zhentarim | Anonymous delegate | Profit from the chaos, sell information |
| Cult of Bane | Malakor (if converted in Act 3) | Redeem the cult, prevent Bane's return |

**Each faction makes a case** for which seals to prioritize. The party must decide, and their decision is final.

#### The Final Choice

**The party can save three seals.** The other two will fail, with lasting consequences:

| If Seal Fails | Consequence |
|--------------|-------------|
| #1 Shattered Tower | Goblin raids intensify, Way of the Lion becomes impassable, magical anomalies spread |
| #2 Sunspring Villa | Cult of Bane grows stronger, warm wells spread corruption, Risen Road becomes dangerous |
| #3 Neverwood Blight | The Neverwood dies, the Wardens scatter, Lyra's forest is lost forever |
| #4 Undermountain | The dungeon breaks free, Waterdeep is flooded with monsters, the dragonward fails permanently |
| #5 Cormyrean Milestone | ALL seals fail immediately. The Sword Coast is destroyed. This seal CANNOT fail if the party wants to survive. |

**Seal #5 is non-negotiable.** The keystone must be protected. That leaves two more choices from Seals #1-4.

---

### Scene 3: The Split (Optional)

**The DM can offer the party a choice:**

- **Stay together** — the party focuses on two seals, abandoning the other two entirely. Safer, but more seals fail.
- **Split the party** — three go to Undermountain (Seal #4), three go to a failing seal of their choice (Seals #1-3). Riskier, but more seals can be saved. The DM should make this difficult but not impossible.

**If the party splits,** the DM runs parallel tracks, converging at the Cormyrean Milestone for the climax.

---

## Session 2: Undermountain — The Deep Seal

### Scene 4: Descent

**The party (or the Undermountain subgroup) enters Undermountain** through a secret passage known only to the Open Lord and the Harper agent. The dungeon is alive — walls shift, corridors rearrange, and the air is thick with magic and danger.

#### Environmental Hazard: The Pressure

**Undermountain is affected by the failing seals.** Magical pressure builds with every descent:

| Depth | Effect | Save |
|-------|--------|------|
| Level 1-2 | Magical surges, spells fizzle 20% of the time | None (mechanical) |
| Level 3-4 | Force damage, 1d6/round | DC 14 Con |
| Level 5+ | Reality warping, random magic table | DC 15 Wis |

**Tilly can craft pressure dampeners** from her tinker's tools (1 hour, DC 15 Intelligence (Arcana)). The dampeners grant advantage on Constitution saves but do not help with mechanical effects.

#### Trap 5: The Final Test

**Undermountain's traps** are the most dangerous yet. They combine elements from previous acts:

- **Color-coded tiles** (from the Shattered Tower and Sunspring Villa) — but the pattern has shifted again. The party must adapt.
- **Voice illusions** (from the Shattered Tower) — but this time, the voice is Neverember's, taunting the party, trying to distract them.
- **Blight spores** (from the Neverwood) — Neverember has seeded the dungeon with Blight, accelerating the seal failures.

**This trap is the culmination** of everything the party has learned. If they have been paying attention, they can navigate it. If they have not, they will pay the price.

---

### Scene 5: Seal #4 — The Deep Chamber

**At the heart of Undermountain,** the party finds Seal #4 — a massive crystal, embedded in the rock, pulsing with light. It is stable, but cracks are forming, and the pressure is building.

**Neverember is here.** He has set up a base of operations, surrounded by guards, cultists, and goblins — a coalition of the desperate and the ambitious. He is preparing to seize the seal's power when it releases.

#### The Boss Fight — Stage 1: Neverember's Forces

**Neverember's forces** include:
- 10 Elite Guards (CR 1/2 each) — Neverember's personal soldiers, well-trained, well-equipped
- 4 Cult Fanatics (CR 1/8 each) — Bane worshipers, fanatical, willing to die
- 2 Goblin Sorcerers (CR 2 each) — the same goblins from the Shattered Tower, now working for Neverember
- 1 Zhentarim Assassin (CR 3) — hired to eliminate the party, will flee if the fight goes badly

**Tactics:** Neverember's forces fight in formation, protecting Neverember at all costs. The elite guards form a shield wall, the cultists flank, the goblin sorcerers provide ranged support, and the assassin targets the party's casters.

**The chamber** is large, circular, walls lined with runes. The seal crystal is in the center, and Neverember stands beside it, channeling its power. Every round the fight continues, the crystal cracks further, and the pressure builds.

---

### Scene 6: The Confrontation

**After Neverember's forces are defeated,** the lord himself faces the party.

#### Dagult Neverember — Boss Stats

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| CR | 5 |
| HP | 85 |
| AC | 18 (plate armor + shield) |
| Attack | +7 to hit, 1d10+4 damage (longsword) |
| Spells | *shield*, *mage armor*, *fireball*, *counterspell*, *teleport* |
| Special | **Seal Channeling** — every round, Neverember draws power from the crystal, gaining +1 to AC and attack rolls (max +5). When reduced to 0 HP, he attempts to detonate the crystal. |

**Neverember's argument** (during the fight, between attacks):
> *"You think you are saving the coast. You are preserving a cage. The seals were built by people who feared power they could not control. I am freeing what they imprisoned. I am giving the coast its strength back. When this crystal shatters, the pressure will release — and I will be there to guide it. I will restore Neverwinter. I will reclaim Waterdeep. I will make the coast great again."*

**He is not lying.** He believes every word. The question is not whether he is sincere. The question is whether he is right.

#### The Boss Fight — Stage 2: Neverember

**The party must defeat Neverember** while protecting the seal crystal. If the crystal shatters before Neverember is defeated, Seal #4 fails, and the pressure releases prematurely.

**Mechanics:**
- Every round, the crystal cracks further (reduce HP by 10). At 0 HP, the crystal shatters.
- Neverember's **Seal Channeling** makes him stronger every round. The party must act quickly.
- **Elara's Oath of the Crown** is particularly effective here — her *Sacred Edict* can force Neverember to choose between his ambition and his duty. He has sworn oaths as Lord of Neverwinter, and breaking them weakens his channeling.
- **Malachi's knowledge of fiends** reveals that Neverember is not working with fiends — he is working alone. His power comes from ambition, not patronage. This is both good news (no backup from the Nine Hells) and bad news (Neverember is entirely self-reliant, which makes him dangerous).

**After Neverember is defeated,** he does not die. He is captured, weakened, but alive. His final words:
> *"You have stopped me. But you have not stopped the pressure. The seals are failing. The coast is breaking. And when the keystone turns, your choices will not matter."*

He is referring to the Cormyrean Milestone. The final test is coming.

## Session 3: The Cormyrean Milestone — The Keystone

### Scene 7: The Race to the Milestone

**The party travels to the Cormyrean Milestone** — 95 miles from Baldur's Gate, on the Risen Road. The milestone is glowing now, the third language visible, the inscription readable.

**Tilly's moment:** She can finally read the third language. The inscription says:

> *"When the five seals fail, the network breaks. The pressure releases. The coast breaks. But when five travelers stand at the keystone, each carrying a piece of the truth, the network can be restored — not by force, but by choice. Choose wisely, for the coast remembers."*

**The prophecy is complete.** The party are the five travelers (plus one — Tilly is the sixth, the scholar who reads the truth). They must make the final choice.

---

### Scene 8: The Final Puzzle

**The Cormyrean Milestone is not just a marker.** It is a control panel — a device that can restore the seal network, but only if the party makes the right choices.

#### Puzzle 5: The Network Restoration

**The milestone has five dials** — one for each seal. The party must set each dial to one of three positions:

| Position | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| Restore | The seal is repaired, the pressure is contained, the coast is safe |
| Release | The seal is broken, the pressure is released, the coast is changed |
| Redirect | The seal is modified, the pressure is channeled elsewhere, the coast is adapted |

**The puzzle:** The party can restore three seals, release one seal, and redirect one seal. They cannot restore all five. They must choose which seals to save, which to sacrifice, and which to modify.

**Each choice has consequences:**

| Seal | Restore | Release | Redirect |
|------|---------|---------|----------|
| #1 Tower | Goblin raids stop, magic stabilizes | Goblins gain power, Way of the Lion becomes a goblin territory | Magic is channeled into the High Forest, healing the Blight's damage |
| #2 Villa | Cult is destroyed, wells cool | Cult grows stronger, Bane returns | Warm wells are purified, becoming a healing spring |
| #3 Neverwood | Forest heals, Blight recedes | Neverwood dies, Wardens scatter | Blight is transformed into fertile soil, new growth emerges |
| #4 Undermountain | Dungeon seals, Waterdeep is safe | Monsters flood the surface, Waterdeep falls | Undermountain becomes a controlled dungeon, adventurers can explore safely |
| #5 Milestone | NETWORK FAILS — the keystone cannot be restored, only protected | NETWORK FAILS — the keystone cannot be released, only protected | The milestone becomes a permanent seal, the network is stabilized |

**Seal #5 must be set to Redirect.** There is no other option. The keystone cannot be restored or released — it must be modified, becoming a permanent part of the network.

**The other four seals** are the party's choice. They can restore three, release one, and redirect one (besides the Milestone). Their choices will shape the future of the Sword Coast.

---

### Scene 9: The Convergence

**As the party sets the dials,** the milestone activates. The ground shakes, the air hums, and the seal network responds. The party receives a vision — a glimpse of the future, shaped by their choices.

**The vision shows:**
- The seals they restored, glowing bright, stable, secure
- The seal they released, breaking free, changing the land
- The seals they redirected, flowing into new channels, adapting, evolving
- The Sword Coast, transformed but alive, shaped by the party's decisions

**A voice speaks** — the same voice from the Shattered Tower, older, warmer, satisfied:

> *"You have chosen. The coast remembers. The network adapts. The pressure is contained — not by force, but by wisdom. You are the five travelers. You are the catalyst. And you have saved what could be saved."*

The vision ends. The milestone is quiet. The seals are stable. The coast is safe.

**For now.**

## Epilogue: The Coast Remembers

**The campaign ends** with an epilogue that reflects the party's choices. Each character receives a personal resolution:

### Kaelen Ironheart
- **If Seal #1 was restored:** Kaelen returns to Mirabar and begins rebuilding the Ironheart name. The mithral ore he recovered is used to forge new weapons, and the Ironheart clan is reborn.
- **If Seal #1 was released:** Kaelen travels the Way of the Lion, fighting goblins, protecting travelers, earning the respect of those who remember Mithral Hall.
- **If Seal #1 was redirected:** Kaelen discovers that the magic channeled into the High Forest has awakened ancient dwarven forges. He leads an expedition to reclaim them.

### Lyra Nightbreeze
- **If Seal #3 was restored:** Lyra returns to the Neverwood, planting seeds, healing the forest, becoming a Warden herself. The Wardens accept her, and she earns the right to carry the forest's name.
- **If Seal #3 was released:** Lyra travels the coast, searching for places where the Blight has not reached, carrying her seeds to new soil, hoping to grow a new forest.
- **If Seal #3 was redirected:** Lyra discovers that the Blight has transformed into fertile soil, and new growth is emerging — plants she has never seen, trees that glow faintly. She studies them, learns from them, and becomes a guardian of the new forest.

### Malachi Vane
- **Regardless of seal choices:** Malachi's patron senses the shift in the network. The obsidian orb pulses, and Malachi receives a vision — his patron is weakening, their power reduced by the seal stabilization. Malachi has a choice: renew the pact, renegotiate the terms, or sever the connection entirely. The campaign leaves this open, allowing the player to decide.

### Elara Sunstrider
- **If Seal #4 was restored:** Elara returns to Waterdeep as a hero. Laeral Silverhand offers her a position in the City Watch, and Elara accepts, dedicating herself to protecting the city that nearly fell.
- **If Seal #4 was released:** Elara leads a campaign to contain the monsters flooding from Undermountain, earning the respect of the Flaming Fist and the people of Waterdeep.
- **If Seal #4 was redirected:** Elara becomes the first commander of the Undermountain Explorer's Guild, regulating dungeon delves, protecting adventurers, and ensuring that the dungeon's power is used responsibly.

### Finnian Quickstep
- **Regardless of seal choices:** Finn composes a song — the song he has been searching for since he left Daggerford. The song tells the story of the five travelers, the seals, the choices, and the coast that was saved. He plays it in taverns, festivals, and temples, and the song spreads across the Sword Coast. People remember the story, and the story becomes legend.

### Tilly Cogsworth
- **Regardless of seal choices:** Tilly returns to Candlekeep as a hero. The High Avowed offers her a position among the Avowed, and she accepts, dedicating herself to studying the seal network, understanding its origins, and ensuring that it never fails again. She writes a book — *"The Network of the Sword Coast"* — and it becomes required reading for every scholar who enters the keep.

---

## Final Scene: The Boar's Tooth

**The party returns to The Boar's Tooth tavern in Daggerford** — the place where it all began. The tavern is the same, the ale is the same, the people are the same. But the party is different.

They sit around a table, the way they did on that first night, and they raise their mugs.

**Finn speaks:**
> *"To the five travelers. And the sixth who read the truth."*

**They drink. The coast is safe. The network is stable. The seals hold.**

**For now.**

**THE END.**
