# Seals of the Sword Coast — Campaign Overview

## Structure

| Act | Title | Location | Level | Focus |
|-----|-------|----------|-------|-------|
| 1 | Converging Paths | Daggerford | 3 | Party assembly, hook, first mystery |
| 2 | The Shattered Tower | Way of the Lion | 3-4 | Escape-room dungeon, puzzles, first seal |
| 3 | The Sunspring Villa | Risen Road | 4-5 | Cult infiltration, social combat, second seal |
| 4 | The Blight Unbound | Neverwinter + Neverwood | 5 | Race against time, environmental hazards, third seal |
| 5 | The Coast Breaks | Multiple cities | 5-6 | Climax, choices, final seals |

## How It Works

Each act contains:
- **Road encounters** — travel between locations, dangers on the path
- **Set-piece scenes** — puzzles, traps, escape-room challenges
- **Combat encounters** — scaled for a party of 6 at the act's level range
- **Roleplay moments** — NPC interactions, faction negotiations, moral choices
- **Seal discovery** — each act reveals one of the five ancient seals and its status

## Party Assembly

The characters converge in Daggerford through the **Caravan Incident** (Act 1). Each character has a reason to be there, and a shared crisis forces them together. The Harper agent is the glue — they have been watching all six, recruited by the prophecy, and they orchestrate the first meeting without revealing their hand.

## Key Design Principles

- **Puzzles have failsafes.** If the party fails a puzzle, the consequence is real but not lethal. A failed lock opens a different door. A failed riddle releases a different creature. The game continues.
- **Traps teach.** The first trap in any area is a warning — visible, avoidable, informative. The second trap assumes the party learned. The third trap punishes those who didn't.
- **Roads are alive.** Travel is not downtime. Every road has hazards, encounters, and rumors that advance the plot.
- **Factions lie.** Every faction tells the truth — but never the whole truth. The Harpers withhold the prophecy. The Gauntlet hides the seal failures. The Zhentarim profit from both. Let the party piece it together.
- **Choices matter.** The party cannot save all five seals. They must choose which three to prioritize. The two that fail will have lasting consequences for the campaign world.
