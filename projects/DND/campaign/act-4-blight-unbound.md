# Act 4: The Blight Unbound

## The Setup

The party returns to **Neverwinter**, drawn by multiple converging threats:

- **Lyra's connection:** The Blight is spreading faster now, accelerating after the party stabilized Seal #2. The Neverwood is dying, and the seeds she carries pulse with increasing urgency.
- **The Duke's message:** Neverember's old allies are making their move, using the Blight as cover to seize power.
- **The Wardens' warning:** The druids have seen the Blight's source. It is not natural. It was planted, and it is being fed.
- **The Harper intelligence:** Seal #3 is the most time-sensitive. At 60 days, it has the longest timeline, but the Blight's acceleration is reducing that window. The Harper agent estimates 40 days remaining.

**The critical decision:** The party now knows all five seals. They must choose which three to save. The Cormyrean Milestone (Seal #5) is the keystone — if it fails, all five fail. It must be protected. That leaves two more choices from Seals #1, #2, #3, and #4.

---

## Session 1: Neverwinter — The City Under Pressure

### Scene 1: The Lord's Ward

**Trigger:** The party arrives in Neverwinter to find the city on edge. The Blight is visible from the walls — a gray-green haze at the edge of the Neverwood, creeping closer each day.

#### The Neverember Plot

**General Esvele Greycrone** approaches the party. She has evidence that Neverember's agents are sabotaging the city's defenses, weakening the walls, and spreading panic. She needs the party to infiltrate a meeting of Neverember's allies in the Warrens.

**This is a side quest** that runs parallel to the Blight investigation. The party can:
- **Prioritize the Blight** — ignore Neverember's plot, focus on Seal #3. The city's defenses weaken, and Neverember's agents gain ground. This does not fail the campaign, but it makes Act 5 harder.
- **Prioritize the politics** — infiltrate the meeting, expose Neverember's agents, secure the city. This buys time for the Blight investigation but costs 2-3 days.
- **Split the party** — risky but possible. Half investigates the Blight, half infiltrates the meeting. The DM should make this difficult but not impossible.

#### Puzzle 1: The Warrens Infiltration (If pursuing the Neverember plot)

**The Warrens** are a maze of tunnels, stairs, and rope bridges. Neverember's agents meet in an abandoned warehouse, and the party must enter without being detected.

**Three entry points:**
1. **The sewer entrance** — disgusting but effective. DC 12 Constitution save to resist disease spores. Success means clean entry. Failure means one level of exhaustion.
2. **The roof** — requires climbing the warehouse exterior. DC 13 Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics). Failure means falling into a courtyard with guards.
3. **The front door** — disguise as merchants delivering supplies. DC 14 Charisma (Deception). Failure means combat with warehouse guards.

**Inside the warehouse:** The party finds evidence that Neverember's agents are not just sabotaging defenses. They are also funding the goblin raids at the Shattered Tower and the cult at the Sunspring Villa. Neverember knows about the seals, and he is accelerating their failure — because he believes the pressure release will give him the power to reclaim his throne.

**This changes everything.** The party now knows that a single person is behind multiple seal failures. Neverember is not just a political threat. He is an existential one.

---

### Scene 2: The Wardens

**The Wardens** — the trio of druids who patrol the Neverwood's edge — approach the party. They do not trust cities, they do not trust politics, and they do not trust most adventurers. But they recognize Lyra.

**Lyra's connection:** The Wardens knew her clan. They watched the High Forest fall to the Shadow, and they see the same patterns in the Blight. They believe the Blight and the Shadow are connected — two expressions of the same force.

#### What the Wardens Reveal

- The Blight was seeded **deliberately**, 60 days ago, at the heart of the Neverwood.
- The seed is not natural. It is a **device** — mechanical, magical, ancient. It was placed by someone who knew exactly what they were doing.
- The device is protected by **druidic wards** — old wards, older than the current druids, older than the Neverwood itself. The Wardens cannot penetrate the wards without destroying them, and destroying the wards would release the Blight immediately.
- The Wardens offer to guide the party to the Blight heart, but they cannot enter. Their presence would trigger the wards. The party must go alone.

**The Wardens give Lyra a gift:** A **ward stone** — a piece of the old forest, carved with runes, warm to the touch. It will protect her from the Blight's worst effects, and it will guide her to the seed. The other party members do not receive this protection. They must rely on their own resources.

## Session 2: Into the Neverwood

### Scene 3: The Forest Edge

**The party enters the Neverwood** at dawn. The forest is ancient, dense, and alive — but the Blight is visible, a gray-green corruption spreading from the center like a stain.

#### Environmental Hazard: The Blight's Edge

**The Blight is not uniform.** It has zones, each with different effects:

| Zone | Distance from Heart | Effect | Save |
|------|-------------------|--------|------|
| Outer | 1-2 miles | Poison spores, 1d6 poison damage/round | DC 13 Con |
| Middle | 0.5-1 mile | Magical distortion, spells fizzle 25% of the time | None (mechanical) |
| Inner | 0-0.5 miles | Reality warping, random magic table effects | DC 15 Wis |

**Tilly can craft Blight filters** from her tinker's tools and herbal ingredients (2 hours, DC 14 Intelligence (Arcana)). The filters grant advantage on Constitution saves in the Outer zone but do not help in the Middle or Inner zones.

**Lyra's ward stone** protects her from all Blight effects. She can shield one other party member at a time by holding their hand, but she cannot fight while doing so.

#### Road Encounter: The Wardens' Last Gift

**As the party crosses into the Middle zone,** the Wardens appear one last time. They give the party a **map of the wards** — showing the paths that are safe and the paths that trigger traps. The map is incomplete (the Wardens have not mapped the Inner zone), but it is enough to avoid the worst dangers.

**The Wardens' final words:**
> *"The forest remembers you, Lyra Nightbreeze. Go now. We will hold the edge. Do not make us regret this."*

---

### Scene 4: The Middle Zone — Puzzle Gauntlet

**The Middle zone** is where the druidic wards are strongest. The party must navigate a series of challenges, each testing a different aspect of their abilities.

#### Puzzle 2: The Tree of Names

**A massive oak blocks the path.** Its bark is inscribed with names — hundreds of them, carved by generations of druids. The tree will not move unless the party speaks a name that belongs to them.

**The puzzle:** Each party member must speak a name that has personal significance — not their own name, but the name of someone they love, someone they lost, someone they carry with them.

- **Kaelen:** "Thardir." (His father)
- **Lyra:** "Elandil." (Her brother)
- **Malachi:** "Elara." (His mother — note the coincidence with the paladin's name)
- **Elara:** "Tomas." (The recruit she left behind)
- **Finn:** "Lirael." (His mentor)
- **Tilly:** "Oren." (His friend)

**Mechanics:** DC 12 Wisdom (Insight) to speak a name that is "true enough." The tree judges sincerity, not content. Fail, and the tree's roots entangle the character — restrained until they speak a true name or the party frees them (DC 13 Strength (Athletics)).

**The tree's response:** When all six names are spoken, the tree steps aside. Its leaves whisper: *"You carry the dead with you. That is why you will succeed where others failed."*

#### Trap 4: The Blight's Counter

**Beyond the tree,** the Blight fights back. The ground is covered in **spore mines** — invisible clusters of spores that explode when disturbed.

**Pattern:** The spore mines follow the same color-coding as the Shattered Tower and Sunspring Villa. Blue zones are safe, red zones trigger traps. But the Blight has adapted — some blue zones are now trapped, and some red zones are safe. The pattern has shifted.

**Tilly must re-analyze** the pattern with a DC 16 Intelligence (Arcana) check. The Blight has learned from the party's previous successes, and it has adjusted its defenses. This is a direct consequence of stabilizing Seals #1 and #2 — the network is fighting back.

**Failure:** Triggering a spore mine deals 4d6 poison damage, DC 14 Con save for half. The party can learn the new pattern through trial and error, but each failure costs time and health.

**Success:** The party navigates the gauntlet and reaches the Inner zone.

---

### Scene 5: The Inner Zone — The Blight Heart

**The Inner zone** is where reality breaks down. The air shimmers, the ground pulses, and the Blight's source is visible — a device, mechanical and magical, embedded in the heart of the ancient forest.

#### The Device

**The device** is a sphere of black metal, covered in runes, pulsing with gray-green light. It is not natural. It is not magical. It is **technological** — built by a civilization that understood both magic and machinery, long before the current age.

**Tilly recognizes the technology.** It matches the symbols from the Cormyrean Milestone, the Shattered Tower, and the Sunspring Villa. The device is part of the seal network — but it has been corrupted, repurposed, turned against the forest.

**Malachi recognizes the runes.** They match the Infernal script his patron uses — but older, deeper, more fundamental. The device was not built by fiends. It was built by something that predates fiends, something that existed before the planes were named.

**Lyra feels the seeds in her pouch pulse.** They are responding to the device, not with fear but with recognition. The seeds and the device are connected — they are two halves of the same system. The seeds were meant to replace the device, to restore the forest naturally. But someone activated the device first, and the seeds have been waiting ever since.

#### Puzzle 3: The Seed and the Machine

**The party must choose:**

- **Destroy the device** — shatter the sphere, stop the Blight immediately. But the device is part of the seal network, and destroying it will weaken Seal #3 permanently. The Blight stops, but the seal is broken. The party cannot save this seal.
- **Replace the device with the seeds** — Lyra plants her seeds in the device's housing, and the seeds absorb the device's corruption. This takes time (1 hour, during which the Blight attacks), but it restores the seal and heals the forest. The seeds are consumed in the process, and Lyra loses her connection to the old forest.
- **Repurpose the device** — Tilly attempts to reprogram the device, reversing its function. This is the hardest path (DC 18 Intelligence (Arcana), requires all six party members to contribute), but it saves the seal, heals the forest, AND preserves the device as a tool for future seal emergencies.

**The Blight attacks during the puzzle.** Every round, the Blight sends waves of corruption:

| Round | Effect | Save |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1-2 | Poison spores, 2d6 poison damage | DC 14 Con |
| 3-4 | Reality warping, random magic table | DC 15 Wis |
| 5-6 | Blight creatures spawn (2 Blight Plants, CR 1/2 each) | Combat |
| 7+ | The device pulses, 3d6 necrotic damage to all | DC 15 Dex |

**The party must complete the puzzle before Round 10.** After Round 10, the device overloads, and the Blight releases fully. Seal #3 fails, and the Neverwood is lost.

---

### Scene 6: The Discovery

**As the party works on the device,** they discover something hidden inside:

**A journal page** — written in Common, dated 60 days ago. It belongs to **Dagult Neverember**.

> *"The device is active. The Blight spreads. The seals weaken. When the coast breaks, the pressure will release — and I will be there to control it. The power of the ancient network will be mine, and with it, I will reclaim what was stolen from me. Let the scholars keep their books. Let the druids keep their trees. I will keep the coast."*

**Neverember seeded the Blight.** He is behind the goblin raids, the cult recruitment, the seal failures. He is accelerating the network's collapse so he can seize the released power for himself.

**This changes everything.** The party now knows the enemy's identity, his plan, and his timeline. The question is no longer "what is happening?" It is "can they stop him?"

## Session 3: The Aftermath

### Scene 7: Leaving the Neverwood

**The party exits the Neverwood** at dusk, the Blight behind them (stabilized, destroyed, or repurposed, depending on their choice). The forest is quiet. The Wardens are waiting at the edge.

**The Wardens' response:**
- **If the Blight is stopped:** The Wardens bow to Lyra. *"The forest remembers. You have earned the right to carry its name."* Lyra gains a permanent bonus to Nature checks (+1) and can speak with plants once per day.
- **If the device is destroyed:** The Wardens are grim. *"The Blight stops, but the seal is broken. The coast will pay for this."* Seal #3 is lost, and the Neverwood will never fully recover.
- **If the device is repurposed:** The Wardens are awed. *"You have done what the old druids could not. The forest will sing your names."* The party gains a permanent ally in the Wardens, who will provide intelligence and assistance in Act 5.

---

### Road Encounter: The Message

**On the road back to Neverwinter,** the party intercepts a messenger — a Flaming Fist courier carrying a letter from Baldur's Gate.

**The letter reveals:**
- Neverember has fled Neverwinter. He is heading south, toward Waterdeep and Undermountain.
- His final message: *"The seals will break. The pressure will release. And I will be waiting."*
- The Harper agent confirms: Neverember is targeting Seal #4 (Undermountain) and Seal #5 (the Cormyrean Milestone). He knows the keystone is the most important, and he is going after it.

**The race is on.** The party must reach Waterdeep before Neverember, protect the Cormyrean Milestone, and prepare for the final confrontation.

---

## Act 4 Conclusions

- **Seal #3** is addressed (healed, broken, or repurposed, depending on party choice)
- **Neverember is identified** as the mastermind behind the seal failures
- **The party knows the timeline** — Neverember is moving fast, and Act 5 is the climax
- **The party must finalize their priorities** — which three seals to save, which two to abandon
- **The Cormyrean Milestone (Seal #5) is the keystone** — it must be protected at all costs
- **The road ahead** leads to Waterdeep, Undermountain, and the final battle
