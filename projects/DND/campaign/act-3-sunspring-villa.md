# Act 3: The Sunspring Villa

## The Setup

The party travels the **Risen Road** from Daggerford toward Candlekeep, passing through Baldur's Gate to resupply. The goblin boss's letter points to the Sunspring Villa, 70 miles from the Gate, and the Harper agent confirms: Seal #2 is weakening, and the Cult of Bane is accelerating the process.

**Time pressure:** 45 days until Seal #2 fails. The party has 3-4 weeks of travel and investigation before the seal reaches critical status.

---

## Session 1: Baldur's Gate — The Spider's Web

### Scene 1: The Flaming Fist

**Trigger:** The party arrives in Baldur's Gate and seeks audience with Marshal Ulder Ravengard.

**Elara's connection:** As a former Flaming Fist officer, Elara can fast-track the audience. The others must wait outside, where they encounter:

#### Road Encounter (in-city): The Underground Recruitment

**While waiting,** a figure approaches the party — a member of the Underground, the criminal network beneath Baldur's Gate. The figure offers information about the Sunspring Villa in exchange for a favor: smuggle a package into Candlekeep.

**The package** contains magic siphon devices — the same technology the goblins used at the Shattered Tower. The Underground is not just criminal. They are connected to the seal failures.

**Mechanics:**
- **Accept the package:** The party gains information about the villa (cult layout, guard rotations, the warm wells) but becomes compromised. The Flaming Fist will search them at the city gates, and failing a DC 15 Dexterity (Sleight of Hand) check means the package is discovered.
- **Refuse the package:** The party remains clean but lacks intelligence. They must investigate the villa blind, which increases difficulty.
- **Report the Underground member:** The Flaming Fist rewards the party (100 gp, a letter of thanks) but the Underground marks them as enemies. Future encounters in Baldur's Gate are hostile.

---

### Scene 2: Marshal Ravengard

**The audience** reveals:

- The Flaming Fist knows about the cult at the Sunspring Villa but cannot act openly — the villa is on private land, and the owner (a deceased noble) has no heirs. Legal proceedings would take months.
- Ravengard has been monitoring the villa secretly. He confirms the warm wells, the basement activity, and the growing number of recruits.
- He offers the party **official authority** to investigate — a letter of marque that allows them to enter the villa, search the premises, and arrest suspects. But he cannot accompany them. The Flaming Fist is stretched thin.
- He warns: *"The cult is not just worshiping Bane. They are using the villa's basement for something else. Something older. Be careful what you find down there."*

**Tilly's connection:** Gremma Mountainfoot, the dwarf armorer, recognizes Kaelen's hammer. She reveals that the forging style matches weapons found in the Undermountain — weapons that should not exist, because they were forged before Undermountain was built. This hints at Seal #4's ancient origins.

**Finn's connection:** A bard in the tavern plays a melody that matches the one Finn has been hearing. The bard says the melody came from a dream — a dream about five travelers, a tower, and a villa with warm wells. The prophecy is spreading, through music, through dreams, through the Weave itself.

## Session 2: The Sunspring Villa

### Scene 3: Approaching the Villa

**The party arrives at the villa at dusk.** The estate is overgrown, the walls crumbling, the gardens wild. But the basement windows glow faintly — warm light, like a hearth fire.

#### Puzzle 1: Gaining Entry

The villa is guarded by **four cultists** (CR 1/8 each, commoners with daggers and fanaticism). They are not warriors, but they are devoted, and they will not surrender.

**Options:**
- **Fight:** Straightforward combat. The cultists are weak but numerous, and they will shout for help if the fight lasts more than one round.
- **Deceive:** Pose as recruits. The cult is growing, and new faces are expected. DC 14 Charisma (Deception) to convince the guards. Success means entry without combat. Failure means combat.
- **Stealth:** Enter through the gardens, avoid the guards entirely. DC 13 Dexterity (Stealth) for each character. The gardens are overgrown, which helps, but the warm wells attract insects that buzz loudly, making stealth harder (-2 to checks).
- **Intimidate:** Demand entry. The cultists are fanatics, not cowards. Intimidation triggers combat immediately, but the party gains advantage on the first round (the cultists hesitate, confused by the party's authority).

---

### Scene 4: The Ground Floor

**Inside the villa,** the ground floor is abandoned but not empty. Dust covers everything, furniture is rotted, and the air smells of copper and old stone. But the walls are marked with symbols — the same symbols from the Cormyrean Milestone, the Shattered Tower, and Candlekeep's Restricted Wing.

#### Trap 3: The Test (Visible to Those Who Learned)

The floor has pressure plates — but the party should recognize the pattern from the Shattered Tower. Blue tiles are safe, red tiles trigger traps. This time, the trap is **hold person** — DC 13 Wisdom save. The villa is testing whether the party learned from the Tower.

**If the party navigates carefully:** They avoid the trap entirely.
**If the party charges in:** They trigger the trap, but it is weaker than the Tower's traps (the villa is testing, not punishing).

#### Puzzle 2: The Symbol Wall

**The symbols on the wall** form a map — not of the villa, but of the Sword Coast. The five seals are marked, and lines connect them. The lines form a pattern: a pentagram, with the Shattered Tower at the top and the Cormyrean Milestone at the center.

**Tilly can decipher the map** with a DC 15 Intelligence (Arcana) check. The map reveals:
- The seals are not independent. They are connected, like nodes in a network.
- Breaking one seal accelerates the others. The party's choice at the Tower affected the villa's timeline.
- The Cormyrean Milestone (Seal #5) is the keystone. If it fails, all five seals fail immediately.

**This is critical information.** The party now knows that Seal #5 is the most important, and they must protect it at all costs. But they do not know where it is, or what it contains.

---

### Scene 5: The Basement

**The basement stairs** are intact, the door is locked, and the warm light grows brighter as the party descends. The air is hot, humid, thick with the smell of copper and something older.

#### The Warm Wells

**At the bottom of the stairs,** three wells are visible — stone circles, water glowing faintly orange. The warmth is intense, and the air is hard to breathe.

**Environmental hazard:** Each round spent in the basement without protection imposes 1d6 fire damage (the warmth is magical, not natural). A DC 13 Constitution save negates the damage. Tilly can craft **cooling amulets** from her tinker's tools (1 hour, DC 14 Intelligence (Arcana)) that grant advantage on the save.

#### Puzzle 3: The Cult Ritual

**The basement is large,** circular, walls lined with the same symbols as the villa above. In the center, a circle of **twelve cultists** stands around a pit — the pit leads deeper, into the rock, and the warm water flows from below.

**The cult leader** is a human named **Malakor**, a former priest of Helm who turned to Bane after his temple was destroyed. He is charismatic, intelligent, and deeply convinced that he is saving the Sword Coast — not destroying it.

**Malakor's argument:**
> *"The seals are not protecting the coast. They are imprisoning it. The pressure they hold back is not a threat — it is a gift. The old scholars were afraid of power they could not control, so they built cages and called them shields. I am freeing what they imprisoned. I am giving the coast its strength back."*

**He is not entirely wrong.** The seals were built by people who feared magic, who wanted control, who did not trust the land. Malakor believes that releasing the pressure will restore the Sword Coast's natural power — magic, fertility, life. He does not know (or does not care) that the pressure will also destroy everything in its path.

#### Social Combat: Turning the Cult

**The party can try to disrupt the ritual** through combat, persuasion, or a combination.

**Combat approach:**
- 12 Cult Fanatics (CR 1/8 each) — weak but numerous, fighting in a circle around the pit
- Malakor (CR 3) — can cast *command*, *zone of truth*, and *bestow curse*
- If the pit is not sealed during combat, warm water erupts every round — 2d6 fire damage to everyone in the basement, DC 13 Dex save for half

**Persuasion approach:**
- The party can try to convince the cultists to stand down
- DC 16 Charisma (Persuasion) for each cultist — Malakor's fanaticism gives the cultists advantage on their Wisdom saves against persuasion
- **Elara's Oath of the Crown** can help — her *Sacred Edict* Channel Divinity forces creatures to choose between her and Malakor. Those who choose her stand down.
- **Finn's Bardic Inspiration** can help — his music reaches the cultists' humanity, reminding them of who they were before the cult
- **Malachi's knowledge of fiends** can help — he knows that Bane does not care about the cultists. He is using them, and when the seal breaks, he will discard them like tools. Malakor knows this too, but he does not care.

**The ritual:** The cultists are channeling magic into the pit, weakening the seal. Each round the ritual continues, the seal weakens further (reduce the timeline by 3 days). The party must disrupt the ritual within 6 rounds or the seal reaches critical status.

---

### Scene 6: The Choice

**Whether through combat or persuasion,** the ritual is disrupted. The pit seals temporarily, the warm water recedes, and the basement goes dark.

**But the party must choose:**

- **Destroy the villa** — collapse the basement, seal the pit permanently. The cult is trapped, the seal is secure, but the villa's secrets are lost forever. The party cannot learn what lies beneath the pit.
- **Seal the villa** — use magic to lock the basement, trap the cult inside but alive. The seal is stabilized (30 days bought), but the cult survives. They can escape later, and the party must deal with them again.
- **Convert the cult** — convince Malakor and his followers to abandon Bane and join the party's cause. This is the hardest path (DC 20 Charisma (Persuasion), requires all six party members to contribute), but it turns enemies into allies. The cultists become a network of informants, and Malakor's knowledge of the seals is invaluable.

**Malakor's final words** (regardless of the choice):
> *"You think you are saving the coast. You are only delaying the inevitable. The seals were built by people who feared the truth. The truth is coming. And when it does, your choices will not matter."*

He is not entirely wrong.

## Session 3: The Intelligence

### Scene 7: What the Villa Reveals

**After securing the villa,** the party finds:

- **Letters** — correspondence between Malakor and someone in Undermountain. The letters mention "the deep seal" and "the pressure beneath Waterdeep." This confirms Seal #4's location and its connection to the cult.
- **A map** — shows the five seals, but also a sixth location: the **Cormyrean Milestone**. The map marks it as "The Keystone — do not approach until the other five are addressed."
- **A journal** — Malakor's personal records, detailing his research into the seals. He believes the seals were built to suppress the Sword Coast's natural magic, and that releasing the pressure will restore balance. He is wrong about the outcome but right about the seals' origin.
- **A warning** — carved into the basement wall, in the third language from the Milestone. Tilly cannot read it yet. But she recognizes the style — it matches the Cormyrean Milestone's inscription. The warning says: *"When the keystone turns, the network fails. Protect the center at all costs."*

---

### Road Encounter: The Return to Daggerford

**On the way back,** the party travels the Risen Road again, but the road is different now:

- **The Lastwall Gate** — the symbols on the rock faces are glowing faintly. The seal in the mountains is affected by the villa's stabilization.
- **Chionthar's Bend** — the river bandits are gone. Their boats are burned, their camp abandoned. The cult at the villa was supporting them, and without that support, they have scattered.
- **The Cormyrean Milestone** — as the party passes it, the inscription glows brighter than before. The third language is almost readable. Tilly can make out one word: *"Soon."*

---

## Act 3 Conclusions

- **Seal #2** is addressed (destroyed, sealed, or converted, depending on party choice)
- **Seal #4** is revealed (Undermountain, connected to the cult)
- **Seal #5** is identified (Cormyrean Milestone, the keystone — must be protected)
- **The party is level 5** and knows they must prioritize: three seals can be saved, two must be abandoned
- **The Harper agent** is waiting with new intelligence about Undermountain
- **The road ahead** leads north, toward Neverwinter and the Blight
