# Act 1: Converging Paths

## Session 1 — The Caravan Incident

### Opening Scene: The Boar's Tooth Tavern, Daggerford

The party does not start together. They arrive in Daggerford separately, each drawn by their own reasons:

- **Kaelen** has tracked a shipment of mithral ore — the kind used in Mithral Hall — moving through the city. The caravan carrying it was robbed two nights ago.
- **Lyra** has sensed the Blight spreading south from the Neverwood. The river water at Daggerford tastes wrong, and the burial mounds beyond the Farms are humming.
- **Malachi** has followed a trail of fiendish magic — his patron's influence is strong here, and the obsidian orb pulses when he walks through the Market Ward.
- **Elara** has been asked by the Duke to investigate the caravan robberies. She carries a letter of marque and a mandate from the Lords' Alliance.
- **Finn** has heard a song — a fragment of a melody that pulls him south, toward Daggerford and beyond. His lute hums in response.
- **Tilly** has received a letter from Candlekeep, urging her to travel the Way of the Lion and investigate "anomalies in the old road."

They all end up at **The Boar's Tooth** on the same night. This is not coincidence.

---

### Scene 1: The Dead Messenger

**Trigger:** As the party members settle into the tavern, a rider stumbles through the doors — wounded, bleeding, riding a dead horse. He collapses at the bar and whispers a single word: *"Tower."* He is dead before anyone reaches him.

**On his body:**
- A sealed letter addressed to "The Five Who Walk the Old Road" (the prophecy language)
- A dagger forged in mithral (Kaelen recognizes the style — Mithral Hall)
- A pouch of seeds that glow faintly (Lyra's seeds pulse in response)
- A coin stamped with the symbol of Bane (Malachi's orb flares red)
- A Harper pin (Elara's mandate aligns)
- A sketch of a tower, annotated with arcane symbols (Tilly recognizes the notation)
- A small wooden flute, carved by an elf (Finn's grandmother's style)

**Each character recognizes something.** The messenger was carrying pieces of all of their stories.

---

### Scene 2: The Robbed Caravan

**Trigger:** At dawn, Master of Caravans Gromnar arrives at the tavern, seeking volunteers to investigate the robbery. He offers 50 gp per survivor, 100 gp per recovered item.

**The caravan site** is 5 miles outside Daggerford, on the Trade Way near Trollclaw Ford.

#### Puzzle 1: The Bandit Camp

The bandits have fled, but their camp remains. The party must piece together what happened:

**Clues (DC 12 Intelligence (Investigation) checks):**
1. **Boot prints** — six bandits, but the prints are too uniform. Military training. These are not common thieves.
2. **A torn map** — shows the Trade Way, but also the Way of the Lion. The bandits were not just robbing caravans. They were scouting the old road.
3. **A broken compass** — points not north, but toward the Shattered Tower. Modified with arcane components.
4. **A ledger page** — lists shipments, but one entry is circled: *"Mithral ore — Tower delivery — priority."* The bandits were not stealing the ore. They were trying to STOP it from reaching the Tower.
5. **Blood trail** — leads into the woods. Follow it, and the party finds a wounded bandit.

#### Roleplay: The Wounded Bandit

The bandit is a former Daggerford guard, discharged for asking questions (like Kestrel at the ford). He will talk if healed:

> "We weren't robbing the caravan. We were protecting it. Something in that tower — it's hungry. The ore was going to feed it. I know that sounds mad, but the captain showed us the reports. The tower's waking up."

He reveals that the guard captain has been secretly investigating the Shattered Tower, that the robberies are a cover for something larger, and that the messenger who died in the tavern was one of them.

**Reward:** The bandit gives the party the captain's signet ring, which opens a locked chest at the caravan site containing the mithral ore — and a note: *"For the five who come. The Tower waits. — R.H."* (Remi Haventree, the Harper)

---

### Scene 3: The Harper's Letter

**Trigger:** Back in Daggerford, the party breaks the seal on the messenger's letter.

**The letter reads:**

> *"To those who carry the marks of the old road:*
>
> *You have been chosen, though you do not yet know it. The Sword Coast is built upon seals — five of them, placed by scholars and warriors long dead. They hold back something that should not wake.*
>
> *The first seal is failing. It lies in the Shattered Tower, on the Way of the Lion. The goblins are not raiding by chance. They are being directed. The magic in the stones is not residual. It is alive.*
>
> *I cannot go. My handlers would stop me. But you — you are not bound by factions, not yet. Go to the Tower. Learn what is buried there. And when you are ready, come find me.*
>
> *Look for the blue candle."*

## Session 2 — The Blue Candle

### Scene 4: The Harper Safehouse

**Trigger:** The party searches Daggerford for "the blue candle."

**Puzzle 2: Finding the Safehouse**

The Harper safehouse is in the Market Ward, but its location is hidden. The party must solve a series of clues:

1. **The blue candle** is sold at a specific shop — the one that also sells herbal remedies (Sister Alinthe's temple supplies them). The candle is marked with a Harper symbol, visible only in moonlight.
2. **Buying the candle** requires a DC 15 Charisma (Persuasion) check — the seller tests whether the buyer is genuine. Fail, and the candle is plain wax. Succeed, and it glows faintly blue.
3. **Following the candle** — the candle burns brighter as the party approaches the safehouse. It is a simple homing mechanism, but it draws attention. Street urchins follow. A Watchwarrior asks questions. The party must navigate the ward without raising alarms.

**The safehouse** is behind a bookshelf in a rented townhouse. The bookshelf opens with a pressure plate (DC 10 Intelligence (Arcana) to find, or just push the right book — *"The History of the Dessarin,"* volume 3).

---

### Scene 5: Remi Haventree

**Inside the safehouse:** Maps, supplies, and a woman waiting.

**Remi Haventree** is a Harper agent, widow of the late Masked Lord Arthagast Ulbrinter. She is sharp, grieving, and determined. She has been watching the party since they arrived in Daggerford.

#### What She Reveals

- The five seals are real. They were placed by an alliance of scholars (Candlekeep), warriors (Order of the Gauntlet), and druids (Emerald Enclave) centuries ago.
- Each seal contains something — not a monster, but a *pressure*. Magical pressure, like steam in a boiler. If all five seals fail, the pressure releases, and the Sword Coast is torn apart.
- The Shattered Tower is Seal #1. It is cracking. The goblins are being used to siphon magic from the tower, weakening the seal faster.
- Remi cannot accompany the party. The Harpers are monitoring her, and if she leaves Daggerford, she will be recalled. But she can supply them.

#### What She Gives Them

- **Supplies** — rations, healing potions (2 per party member), rope, torches
- **A map** — shows the Way of the Lion, the Shattered Tower, and three other marked locations (Seals #2-5, though Remi does not know what they are yet)
- **A warning** — *"The Tower will test you. Not with monsters, but with choices. It was built to keep people out, but also to keep something in. Do not trust the first voice you hear inside."*

---

### Scene 6: The Road to the Tower

**Trigger:** The party leaves Daggerford at dawn, heading east on the Way of the Lion.

#### Road Encounter 1: The Winding Water Crossing

The river is high from recent rain. The ford is dangerous.

**Challenge:** DC 12 Strength (Athletics) check for each character to cross safely. Fail by 5 or more, and the character loses a item to the current. The party can help each other — success with advantage helps a nearby failure.

**Complication:** As they cross, the party spots movement on the far bank — goblins, watching. The goblins do not attack. They watch, and then they vanish into the trees. The party is being tracked.

---

#### Road Encounter 2: The Ambush

**Location:** 20 miles from Daggerford, in a narrow stretch of road surrounded by forest.

**The party is ambushed by six goblins** — but these are not ordinary goblins. They carry modified weapons (arcane-enhanced blades), they fight in formation, and they have a leader — a **goblin sorcerer** (CR 2) who can cast *burning hands* and *ray of frost*.

**Combat stats:**
- 5 Goblin Bandits (CR 1/4 each) — but with +2 to attack rolls (arcane weapons)
- 1 Goblin Sorcerer (CR 2) — spellcaster, targets the party's casters first

**Tactics:** The goblins do not fight to kill. They fight to delay. After reducing the party to 50% HP, the sorcerer casts *misty step* and they all retreat. They are buying time for something.

**After the fight:** The goblins drop a device — a crude machine, powered by a glowing crystal. Tilly identifies it as a **magic siphon** — designed to drain magical energy from a source and store it. The source is the Shattered Tower.

---

### Climax: Arrival at the Shattered Tower

**Session ends** as the party reaches the Shattered Tower at dusk. The ruins loom ahead, magic crackling in the air, the stones shifting faintly. The goblins are gone. The tower is waiting.

**Final image:** The party stands at the edge of the ruins, the siphon device in hand, Remi's words echoing: *"Do not trust the first voice you hear inside."*

The tower's entrance is dark. The air hums. Something is stirring in the lower levels.
