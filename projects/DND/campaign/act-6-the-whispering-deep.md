# The Whispering Deep — Campaign Overview

## Home Base: Vaelen's Reach

Vaelen's Reach is a free port city built on the ruins of an ancient Netheril outpost, where the Weave is thin and unpredictable. The city is a crossroads of trade, magic, and secrets. It is not safe. It is not kind. It is alive.

**The Triad:** Three merchant lords rule the city through compromise and backroom deals. They are not evil — they are practical. They keep the city running, and they take a cut for it. The party will need them, hate them, and eventually choose between them.

**The Fault Line:** A crack in reality runs through the city's underbelly. Wild magic surges are getting worse. Tilly's goggles glow when she walks near it. The locals have learned to live with it — they carry iron charms, they avoid the lower wards after dark, they don't ask questions.

**The Party's Anchor:** The Boar's Tooth is gone. The party needs a new home base. Options: a guildhall in the upper wards (expensive, visible), a warehouse in the docks (cheap, dangerous), or a tower in the scholar's quarter (isolated, full of books). The choice they make here says something about who they are.

## How It Works

Each act contains:
- **City beats** — Vaelen's Reach is alive. Things change between acts. The Triad shifts. The fault line grows. The cult recruits.
- **Road encounters** — the Shattered Coast is not a safe road. Wild magic storms, cult raiders, Zhentarim patrols, and things that shouldn't exist.
- **Set-piece scenes** — puzzles that have real consequences. A failed lock doesn't just open a different door — it releases something.
- **Combat encounters** — scaled for a party of 6 at the act's level range. Combat is not the default resolution.
- **Moral choices** — every act has one choice that cannot be undone. The party cannot have it both ways.

## The Three Paths

The campaign has three endings, determined by the party's choices across all five acts:

**Containment (Law):** The Deep is resealed. The fault line is sealed. Vaelen's Reach is safe — for now. But the Deep is still sleeping, and the next time it wakes, no one will be there to stop it. The party becomes the Wardens, guardians of a lie.

**Release (Chaos):** The Deep is freed. It is not evil — it is ancient, and it does not understand the world as it is now. Wild magic floods the coast. Some things are beautiful. Some things are terrible. The party becomes the architects of a new age, for better or worse.

**Transformation (Balance):** The Deep is neither contained nor released. It is changed — merged with something or someone. This is the hardest path. It requires a sacrifice. One character must give up what they spent the campaign protecting. The Deep becomes something new. The world becomes something new. The party becomes something new.

## The Sacrifice

The final act requires a character to make a choice that costs them everything they are. Not a death — a transformation. The character does not die. They become something else. Something permanent. Something that cannot come home.

**The setup:** Across the campaign, each character's core identity is tested and reinforced. The reader (the player) knows what each character wants, what they protect, what they are. The sacrifice works because the reader has seen them at rest — eating, joking, just being — and they know what is being subtracted.

**The choice:** At the climax, the party discovers that the Deep can be transformed — but it requires an anchor. A living mind, tethered to the Weave, permanently. The anchor does not die. They become part of the Weave — conscious, aware, but unable to leave. They become the guardian of the thing they spent the campaign studying, fighting, or understanding.

**Who chooses:** The sacrifice is not forced. The character volunteers. The reader has seen them struggle with this possibility across the campaign. The choice feels inevitable in hindsight, shocking in the moment.

**The aftermath:** The character is gone. Not dead — gone. The empty chair at the table. The object they left behind. The promise they made that someone else has to keep. The grief belongs to the survivors.
