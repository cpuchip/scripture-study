# Vaelen's Reach

## The City That Shouldn't Exist

Vaelen's Reach is built on top of something older. The Netheril ruins beneath the city are not just foundations — they are a lock. The Fault Line is the crack where the lock is failing.

The city is divided into three tiers:

**The Upper Wards:** Where the Triad lives. Marble buildings, clean streets, the kind of architecture that says "we have money and we want you to know it." The guildhalls here are fortified — not against invaders, but against each other. The party can rent a guildhall here if they can afford it. It is expensive. It is visible. It is safe — for now.

**The Middle Market:** Where everyone else lives. Narrow streets, crowded shops, the smell of fish and magic and something else — something like ozone, like the air before a storm. This is where the party will spend most of their time. It is alive. It is loud. It is honest in the way that markets are honest — everything has a price, and everyone knows what it is.

**The Lower Wards:** Where the Fault Line bleeds through. Wild magic surges are common here. Buildings lean at angles that shouldn't be possible. The locals carry iron charms, they avoid the alleys after dark, they don't ask questions. The party will go here. They will not like it. They will need to.

## The Triad

Three merchant lords rule the city through compromise and backroom deals. They are not evil — they are practical. They keep the city running, and they take a cut for it.

**Lord Vaelen Drakus** — The founder. A dwarf of the Ironheart clan's rival house, he came here after Mithral Hall fell and built this city on Netheril ruins. He is the containment faction. He believes the Fault Line must be sealed, no matter the cost. He is not wrong. He is just wrong *enough*. He will burn the Lower Wards to save the city. He believes this is mercy.

**Lady Seraphina Moonwhisper** — A half-elf bard who inherited her family's trade empire. She is the compromise faction. She believes the Fault Line can be studied, understood, and eventually controlled. She funds the scholars, the wizards, the researchers. She is the one the party will trust first — and the one who will betray them last. Not out of malice. Out of pragmatism.

**Lord Kaelen Thorne** — A human merchant who controls the docks. He is the profit faction. He believes the Fault Line is an opportunity — wild magic artifacts, unstable reagents, things that shouldn't exist but do. He trades in all of it. He is not cruel. He is indifferent. The Lower Wards are a cost of doing business, and costs are meant to be minimized.

## The Fault Line

The Fault Line is a crack in reality, running through the city's underbelly. It is not a dungeon. It is a wound.

**Wild Magic Surges:** The closer you get to the Fault Line, the more unpredictable magic becomes. Spells fizzle, backfire, or do things they shouldn't. Tilly's goggles glow when she walks near it. Malachi's orb pulses. Finn's flute hums. The party will feel it before they see it.

**The Iron Charms:** The locals carry iron charms — not as superstition, but as survival. Iron dampens wild magic. It is not a perfect shield, but it is enough. The party will need these. They will learn why.

**The Lower Ward Refugees:** People are leaving the Lower Wards. They are not fleeing — they are being relocated. The Triad is "redeveloping" the area. The refugees are being moved to camps outside the city. They are not being killed. They are being erased. The party will see this. They will have to choose what to do.

## The Weave Cult

A new religion, born from the Fault Line. They worship the Whispering Deep — not as a monster, but as a god. They believe the Deep is waking, and that its awakening is salvation, not destruction.

**The Preachers:** They are not violent. They are not aggressive. They are kind, gentle, and utterly convinced. They offer food to the hungry, shelter to the homeless, and comfort to the grieving. They are popular in the Lower Wards. They are growing. The party will not be able to simply arrest them — the people *want* them there.

**The Zealots:** A smaller faction within the cult. They are willing to accelerate the Deep's awakening. They sabotage containment efforts, they attack the Wardens, they steal artifacts from the Sunken Library. They are not the majority. They are the loud minority. The party will fight them. The city will blame the cult. The cult will blame the Triad. The cycle continues.

**The Prophet:** No one knows who the Prophet is. Some say it's a child. Some say it's an old woman. Some say it's the Deep itself, speaking through a vessel. The Prophet's words are simple: "The Weave remembers. The Deep remembers. When it wakes, it will not be angry. It will be lonely. And loneliness is a hunger that eats worlds."

## The Wardens

Drakus's private army. They are not the city guard — they are something else. They are containment specialists, trained to seal breaches, suppress wild magic, and eliminate threats to the Fault Line's stability.

**The Wardens are not evil.** They are doing a terrible job with good intentions. They believe the Lower Wards must be sacrificed to save the city. They believe the refugees are being protected, not displaced. They believe the party is a threat — not because the party is dangerous, but because the party is unpredictable.

**The Warden Commander:** A dragonborn paladin, Oath of Devotion. She is not corrupt. She is not cruel. She is absolutely certain that she is right. She will look the party in the eye and tell them why they are wrong. She will not blink. She will not back down. She is the mirror to Elara — the same oath, the same certainty, the same willingness to make the hard choice. The difference is that she is willing to burn the innocent to save the many.

## The Party's Anchor

The Boar's Tooth is gone. The party needs a new home base. The choice they make here says something about who they are:

**The Upper Ward Guildhall:** Expensive (200 gp/month), visible, safe. The Triad will know where the party is. The Wardens will know. The cult will know. The party will have resources, but they will have no privacy.

**The Middle Market Warehouse:** Cheap (50 gp/month), hidden, dangerous. The party will have privacy, but they will be surrounded by the city's underbelly — smugglers, informants, and things that shouldn't exist. The Fault Line is closer here. Wild magic surges are more frequent.

**The Scholar's Tower:** Free (if the party can convince Seraphina to fund it), isolated, full of books. The party will have access to knowledge, but they will be cut off from the city. The tower is old. It is built on Netheril ruins. The Fault Line runs beneath it. The party will hear things at night.

## The First Night

The party arrives in Vaelen's Reach on a night when the Fault Line is active. The sky is wrong — not dark, but *thin*, like the world is becoming glass. The party will feel it before they see it. Magic will behave strangely. Spells will fizzle, backfire, or do things they shouldn't.

The first thing they will see is not the city. It is the refugees — a line of people from the Lower Wards, being marched out of the city by the Wardens. They are not being killed. They are being erased. The party will have to choose: intervene, observe, or help the Wardens.

There is no right answer. There is only the choice, and the consequence.
