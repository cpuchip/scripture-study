# Baldur's Gate — The Gate

## Appearance

Baldur's Gate sits where the River Chionthar breaks through the Lastwall Mountains, a fortress city built into the cliffs themselves. The walls are not added to the city — they are the city. Stone rises from the river in sheer faces, carved with gates, bridges, and watchtowers that make the whole place look like a single enormous fortification.

The air smells of spice — cumin, cinnamon, cloves, pepper — carried on the river wind from the warehouses of the Dock Ward. It mixes with the smell of salt, sweat, and smoke from the forges of the Trades Ward. The sound of the Gate is the sound of water against stone, of crates being loaded and unloaded, of a hundred languages arguing over prices.

Baldur's Gate does not welcome visitors. It tolerates them, taxes them, and hopes they spend coin before they leave. The city is a machine for turning trade into power, and everyone in it — from the Patriarchs in their silk-draped mansions to the dockworkers hauling crates in the rain — is a gear in that machine.

## History

The Gate was founded as a toll house, a choke point on the River Chionthar where merchants had to pay to pass. Over centuries, it grew into one of the most important cities in Faerûn, controlling the flow of goods between the Sword Coast and the lands beyond. The Five Merchant Houses — the Patriarchs — rule the city through wealth rather than blood, buying loyalty, hiring soldiers, and outmaneuvering rivals with the same cold calculation they bring to every negotiation.

The Flaming Fist is the city's military, hired by the Patriarchs and answerable to them. They are professional, well-equipped, and brutally efficient. They keep the peace in the streets, guard the walls, and suppress riots with equal competence. The people respect them — and fear them.

Beneath the city lies the Tunnel, a network of passages built by refugees during a siege centuries ago. It was abandoned, forgotten, and then reclaimed by criminals, smugglers, and outcasts. The Flaming Fist knows about the Tunnel. The Patriarchs know about the Tunnel. They pretend it does not exist, because acknowledging it would mean dealing with it.

## Districts

**Castle Ward** — Perched on the highest cliffs, the Castle Ward is dominated by the Castle of the Seven Lords, now the seat of the Patriarchs and the headquarters of the Flaming Fist. The buildings here are grand, the streets are clean, and the guards are numerous. The air smells of money — literally, the mint is here, and the scent of fresh coin carries on the wind. This is where the city's power lives, and where decisions are made that affect the entire region.

**Trades Ward** — The industrial heart of the Gate. Forges, workshops, and warehouses fill the district, their chimneys smoking, their doors open to the constant flow of goods. The air is hot, noisy, and thick with the smell of metal and sweat. The people here are workers — smiths, carpenters, loaders — and they are proud of what they do. They do not trust the Patriarchs, they do not trust the Flaming Fist, and they trust each other just enough to get the work done.

**Dock Ward** — Where the river meets the sea, the docks are a maze of piers, cranes, and warehouses. Ships from across the Sea of Swords dock here, unloading spices, silks, metals, and magic. The air smells of salt, fish, and exotic goods, and the sound is a constant roar of activity. The dockworkers are organized, powerful, and dangerous when crossed. They control the flow of goods, and they know it.

**Slums** — Where the walls meet the river, the slums cling to the cliffs like barnacles. Buildings are cramped, streets are narrow and dark, and the water is foul. This is where the poor live, where the desperate survive, and where the Underground begins. The Flaming Fist patrols here, but they do not stay long. The people of the slums have learned to look after themselves, and they have learned to hide what they do not want found.

**Fields Ward** — Beyond the walls, the countryside is a mix of farmland and ruins. The fields provide food for the city, and the ruins hold secrets that most people are wise enough to leave alone. The soil is rich, the water is clean, and the farmers are hardworking. They do not care about politics or trade or power. They care about the harvest, the weather, and keeping the things in the ruins buried.

**The Tunnel** — Beneath the city, the passages stretch for miles. They were built for survival, and they serve that purpose still — for those who use them. Smugglers, criminals, and outcasts move through the Tunnel, carrying goods that do not appear on any ledger, paying taxes to no one, answering to no authority. The Tunnel is dark, damp, and dangerous. It is also the only way into the city that the Flaming Fist cannot control.

## Notable NPCs

- **Ulder Ravengard** — Marshal of the Flaming Fist, a human veteran who commands the city's military with discipline and integrity. He is honest, capable, and deeply frustrated by the Patriarchs' corruption. He has asked the party to investigate the Underground, offering official authority and a substantial reward.
- **Patriarch Haluin** — Leader of the House of Haluin, the spice merchants. She is shrewd, ambitious, and willing to use anyone to maintain her house's dominance. She has approached the party with an offer — information in exchange for favors. Her terms are always precise, and her memory is impeccable.
- **Gremma Mountainfoot** — A dwarf who runs the largest armory in the Trades Ward. She is gruff, honest, and the best smith in the Gate. She has a particular interest in the party's dwarf — not because of his heritage, but because of his hammer. She recognizes the style of the forging, and it troubles her.
- **The Spider** — A criminal mastermind who operates from the Tunnel. No one has seen their face. No one knows their name. They control the flow of illegal goods through the Gate, and they have offered the party a position in their network. The offer has not been repeated. It does not need to be.

## Sights & Sounds

Baldur's Gate never sleeps. The river flows, the ships arrive, the crates are loaded, and the coin changes hands. The city runs on a clock that does not stop, and everyone who lives here has learned to move with it. The streets are always full, the markets are always open, and the taverns are always noisy.

The food here is exotic — spices from the south, fruits from the east, meats from the north. The wine is strong, the ale is cheap, and the danger is real. Baldur's Gate is not a city for the faint of heart. It is a city for survivors, for those who can navigate its streets, outwit its schemes, and walk away with something worth having.

At night, the city does not quiet. It changes. The streets fill with shadow, the alleys grow darker, and the Tunnel opens its doors. The Flaming Fist patrols, but they cannot be everywhere. The Patriarchs sleep, but their enemies do not. And beneath it all, the river flows, carrying secrets downstream.

## Secrets

The Underground is not just a criminal network. It is a movement. The people of the Tunnel are planning something — a takeover, a rebellion, a coup — and they are close to being ready. The Flaming Fist knows this. The Patriarchs know this. Neither has acted, because acting would mean acknowledging the scale of the problem, and neither is willing to do that.

A temple to Bane has been reopened in the slums, hidden beneath a ruined building that the Flaming Fist condemned years ago. The cult is small but growing, recruiting from the desperate and the angry. They are waiting for a sign — a moment of chaos, a crack in the city's defenses — to make their move. They have patience. They have always had patience.

The ruins in the Fields Ward are not empty. Something sleeps there, something old and hungry and patient. The farmers know this. They do not farm too close to the ruins, they do not send their children near them, and they do not speak of what they have seen rising from the earth at night. The Patriarchs know this too. They have bought the land around the ruins, sealed the records, and hoped that forgetting would be enough.
