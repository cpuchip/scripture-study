# Waterdeep — The City of Splendors

## Appearance

Waterdeep rises from the coast like a fist clenched against the Sea of Swords. From the harbor, the city is a forest of spires and towers — the Castle Ward dominates the eastern skyline, its walls sheathed in white stone that catches the dawn light and holds it until noon. The North Ward sprawls behind it, a maze of merchant houses, tenement blocks, and guildhalls, each vying for vertical space. The Sea Ward clings to the waterfront, its buildings leaning into one another like drunkards sharing a secret.

The air smells of salt, roasted chestnuts, and the faint metallic tang of the shipyards. The sound of the city is a layered thing — hammers on hulls in the docks, merchants shouting in the markets, temple bells marking the hours, and beneath it all, the constant murmur of a hundred thousand conversations in a dozen languages.

Waterdeep does not feel like one city. It feels like seven cities stacked on top of each other, each with its own rhythm, its own rules, its own people.

## History

Founded centuries ago by the Nimoar tribe, Waterdeep grew from a fishing village into the most powerful city-state on the Sword Coast. The Council of Lords — part elected, part appointed, part hidden — governs with a balance of power that has kept the city stable through wars, plagues, and magical catastrophes. The Open Lord rules in public; the Masked Lords pull strings in secret. No one knows exactly how many Masked Lords there are. That is by design.

Under the city lies Undermountain, the largest dungeon complex in Faerûn. It has claimed the lives of thousands of adventurers, swallowed entire expeditions, and produced treasures beyond counting. Most Waterdhavians pretend it does not exist. They are wrong to do so.

## Districts

**North Ward** — The heart of commerce. Guildhalls line the streets, their facades carved with sigils and boasts. The air here smells of ink, parchment, and ambition. The Watchwarriors patrol in pairs, their armor polished, their expressions unreadable. This is where deals are made, where contracts are signed, where fortunes are built and lost in a single afternoon.

**Sea Ward** — The working waterfront. Cranes swing over the docks, warehouses stack to the sky, and the smell of fish and tar is everywhere. The people here are rough, practical, and suspicious of strangers. They do not care about politics or lords or magic. They care about the tide, the cargo, and whether the coin they were promised will actually arrive.

**Dock Ward** — Between the Sea Ward and the river, this is where the city's underclass lives. Tenements crowd together, streets are narrow and poorly lit, and the Watchwarriors here are thinner on the ground. Crime thrives in the shadows, but so does community. The people of the Dock Ward look out for each other, because no one else will.

**Castle Ward** — The seat of power. The Open Lord's Palace dominates the district, surrounded by government buildings, noble estates, and the temples of the major faiths. The streets are wide, clean, and heavily guarded. The air smells of lavender and authority. This is where the city's elite live, and where the Masked Lords hold their secret councils.

**Hill Ward** — The residential district for merchants and professionals. Houses are well-built, gardens are tended, and the streets are quiet except for the sound of carriages and the occasional argument between neighbors. This is where the city's middle class lives — not rich enough to be nobles, not poor enough to be desperate.

**Field Ward** — The outer edge of the city, where the walls meet the farmland. This is where caravans enter and leave, where the gates are guarded, and where the city's defenses are strongest. Beyond the walls, the countryside stretches toward the coast, dotted with farms, villages, and the occasional ruin.

**Undermountain** — Beneath the city, the dungeon stretches for miles. Its levels are numbered, mapped, and remapped by generations of adventurers, but no one knows how deep it goes. The lower levels are uncharted, unmapped, and unbreathable. Things live down there that have never seen sunlight. They do not want to.

## Notable NPCs

- **Laeral Silverhand** — The Open Lord of Waterdeep, a wizard of immense power and political skill. She is fair, intelligent, and ruthless when necessary. She does not suffer fools, and she does not forget slights.
- **Sorbren** — Captain of the Watchwarriors in the North Ward. A veteran of a dozen campaigns, he is respected by his men and feared by criminals. He has a reputation for honesty that is rare in the city.
- **Ganra Morgrave** — A member of the Wizards Three, the council that regulates magical practice in the city. She is old, sharp, and unimpressed by anyone's title. She has a particular interest in the party's warlock.
- **Jarlaxle Baenre** — A drow noble who operates a mercenary company from Waterdeep. He is charming, dangerous, and impossible to read. He has offered the party employment, twice. They have declined, twice.

## Sights & Sounds

Walk the streets of Waterdeep at dawn, and the city wakes like a beast stretching. The temple bells ring, the market stalls open, the dockworkers start their songs. By midday, the streets are a river of bodies — merchants, guards, beggars, nobles, all moving in a current that has its own logic. By evening, the taverns fill, the streets narrow with shadow, and the city becomes something else — something older, something that remembers every secret ever told in its walls.

The food is excellent. The wine is cheaper than in most cities. The danger is real but hidden — Waterdeep's threats do not come from bandits on the road. They come from a signed contract, a whispered rumor, a letter delivered by a courier who never existed.

## Secrets

Skullport lies beneath the Sea Ward, a city of criminals, smugglers, and outcasts built into the cliffs beneath the harbor. It is connected to Waterdeep by hidden tunnels, secret lifts, and deals that are never written down. The Masked Lords know about Skullport. They tolerate it. Some of them use it.

The Witchlight Carnival appears in the Castle Ward once every few years, materializing from a portal to the Feywild. Its performers are fey, its games are dangerous, and its prizes are real. Those who win gain boons that last a lifetime. Those who lose... disappear. The Carnival leaves as suddenly as it arrives, and no one can predict when it will return.

Manshoon, the City of the Spy, has agents in every ward, every guild, every temple. He knows everything that happens in Waterdeep, and he sells that knowledge to the highest bidder. He has files on the party. He has had them for longer than they realize.
