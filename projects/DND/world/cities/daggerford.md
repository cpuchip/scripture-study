# Daggerford — The Duchy

## Appearance

Daggerford sits where the River Dessarin meets the Shining River, a walled city of timber and stone that looks like it was built by people who valued defense over beauty. The walls are thick, the gates are few, and the watchtowers are numerous. The city does not try to impress visitors. It tries to survive them.

The streets are straight and well-paved, a legacy of the military discipline that shaped the duchy. Buildings are two or three stories, built of stone at the base and timber above, with steep roofs that shed rain efficiently. The air smells of bread, leather, and river water, and the sound of the city is the sound of hammers, horses, and the occasional shout from the market.

Daggerford is a crossroads city. The Trade Way runs through it, connecting Waterdeep to Baldur's Gate, and caravans arrive and depart daily. The people here are used to strangers, used to noise, used to the constant flow of goods and information. They are also used to keeping their own counsel. Daggerfordians are not unfriendly, but they are not open either. They watch, they listen, and they decide for themselves who deserves trust.

## History

Daggerford was founded as a military outpost, a fortified position to control the Trade Way and protect the Sword Coast from raids. Over centuries, it grew into a duchy, ruled by a line of dukes who balanced the needs of the city with the demands of the guilds that controlled its economy. The current duke, Isteval, is a retired soldier who earned his position through service rather than birth. He is respected, practical, and deeply suspicious of politics.

The city's location makes it strategic — anyone who controls Daggerford controls the flow of trade between the north and south. This has made it a target for ambition, for invasion, for subversion. The walls have held. The duke has held. The guilds have held. But the pressure is constant, and pressure finds cracks.

## Districts

**Castle District** — The duke's keep dominates the district, a square fortress of gray stone with four corner towers and a gatehouse that has never been breached. The district is small, clean, and heavily guarded. The duke's soldiers patrol the streets, the temples are well-maintained, and the air smells of wax and polish. This is where the city's business is conducted, where treaties are signed, and where decisions are made that affect the entire Sword Coast.

**Market Ward** — The heart of the city's commerce. Stalls line the streets, selling everything from fresh produce to imported spices, from handmade tools to enchanted trinkets. The air is thick with the scent of food, the sound of haggling, and the occasional argument between merchants. The guilds watch this district carefully — trade is the lifeblood of Daggerford, and anything that threatens trade is treated as a threat to the city.

**Riverside Quarter** — Where the rivers meet, the district is built on pilings, its streets following the curves of the water. Boats dock here, fish are sold here, and the smell of the river is everywhere — fresh, muddy, alive. The people who live here are fishers, boatmen, and laborers. They are rough, practical, and deeply connected to the water. They know its moods, its secrets, and its dangers.

**The Farms** — Beyond the walls, the countryside is a patchwork of fields, orchards, and pastures. The soil is rich, the water is clean, and the harvests are good. The farmers are hardworking, independent, and suspicious of city folk. They provide food for Daggerford and the caravans that pass through, and they expect fair prices in return. They do not tolerate haggling.

**Lizard's Isle** — A small island in the Dessarin, named for the giant lizards that nest there in spring. The island is mostly uninhabited, except for a ruined watchtower and the occasional fisher brave enough to camp overnight. The lizards are not dangerous unless provoked, but they are territorial, and their eggs are valuable. Poachers come to the island, and they do not always leave.

## Notable NPCs

- **Duke Isteval** — A retired Purple Dragon knight from Cormyr, he brings military discipline to the duchy and a deep distrust of political maneuvering. He is fair, direct, and unwilling to tolerate corruption. He has asked the party to investigate the caravan robberies, offering coin and a letter of marque.
- **Master of Caravans Gromnar** — A half-orc who runs the Caravan Masters Guild. He is enormous, surprisingly gentle, and fiercely protective of the traders who pass through Daggerford. He believes the robberies are the work of an inside job and wants proof.
- **Sister Alinthe** — A healer at the Temple of Lathander who treats the poor for free. She is kind, tireless, and deeply religious. She has noticed that the river water has been tasting different lately, and she is worried.
- **The Harper Agent** — A figure who appears in the Market Ward, always wearing a different face, always asking the same questions. They are gathering information about the threats to the Sword Coast, and they have taken an interest in the party's movements.

## Sights & Sounds

Daggerford wakes early. The farmers are up before dawn, the fishers are on the river by first light, and the market stalls are being set up while the rest of the city is still sleeping. By midday, the streets are full — caravans arriving and departing, merchants shouting prices, guards patrolling the walls, and the constant sound of the rivers flowing beneath the city.

The food here is simple and good — bread, cheese, river fish, roasted meat. The ale is strong, the wine is decent, and the taverns are full of travelers who have stories to tell. The people of Daggerford listen to those stories, file them away, and use them when needed. Information is currency here, and everyone trades in it.

At night, the city quiets. The gates close, the lamps are lit, and the rivers carry the sounds of the city downstream. The walls stand silent, the watchtowers are manned, and the duchy sleeps — but not deeply. Daggerford has been threatened too many times to trust the dark.

## Secrets

The Harper safehouse in the Market Ward is active and well-supplied. Its agent has been monitoring the caravan robberies and has identified a pattern — the attacks are coordinated, well-funded, and targeted. Someone is trying to disrupt trade between Waterdeep and Baldur's Gate, and they are close to succeeding. The Harper has not revealed this to the duke yet. They are waiting for the right moment.

The river is changing. The fishers have noticed it first — the water tastes metallic, the fish are smaller, and something large moves beneath the surface at night. Sister Alinthe has tested the water and found traces of magic — old magic, the kind that should not be in a river. She has not reported her findings, because she does not know who to trust.

The burial mounds in the Farms are warded with druidic magic, older than the duchy, older than the Trade Way. Something sleeps beneath them, and the wards are weakening. The farmers do not know this. The duke does not know this. The druids who maintain the wards are few, old, and running out of time.
