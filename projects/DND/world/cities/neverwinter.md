# Neverwinter — The City of Skilled Hands

## Appearance

Neverwinter clings to the base of a volcano — Mount Hotenow — like a cluster of barnacles on the side of a ship. The city is built in tiers, rising from the harbor to the Lord's Ward at the summit, and the air always carries a faint warmth, a breath of sulfur that reminds everyone that the mountain is alive and watching.

The buildings are smaller than Waterdeep's, more practical. Timber and stone, pitched roofs, narrow streets that wind uphill like veins. The harbor is busy but not grand — fishing boats, merchant cogs, the occasional warship. The sound of the city is the sound of hammers on wood, saws on timber, and the occasional rumble from the mountain that makes everyone pause and listen.

The Neverwood surrounds the city on three sides, a dense forest of ancient oaks and pines that feels older than the city, older than the mountain, older than the people who walk beneath its canopy. The trees do not welcome visitors. They tolerate them, and that is generous.

## History

Neverwinter was once the jewel of the North, a city of beauty and culture whose gardens were legendary across Faerûn. Then the Blight came — a magical corruption that withered the gardens, poisoned the wells, and turned the city into a graveyard. The population fled, the walls fell, and the mountain erupted.

The city was rebuilt, piece by piece, by people who had nowhere else to go. They did not restore the gardens. They built walls, stockpiled supplies, and learned to live with the mountain's moods. The new Neverwinter is harder, rougher, less beautiful. It is also alive, and its people are proud of that.

Dagult Neverember ruled the city for a time, with an iron fist and a clear vision. He was effective, ruthless, and unpopular. He was ousted by Waterdeep and exiled, leaving behind a power vacuum that has never been fully filled. The provisional council that governs now is competent but cautious, and the people are restless.

## Districts

**Lord's Ward** — At the summit of the city, the Lord's Ward is dominated by Neverwinter Castle, a fortress built into the mountain itself. The castle is cold and empty since Neverember's exile, but the guards still patrol, the gates still close at dusk, and the banners still fly — though no one remembers who they represent. The district is quiet, too quiet, and the people who live here keep to themselves.

**Harbor District** — The city's lifeline. The harbor is protected by a breakwater and a lighthouse, and the docks are busy with ships from Waterdeep, Baldur's Gate, and beyond. The air smells of fish, tar, and salt, and the sound of the district is the sound of ropes, pulleys, and shouted orders. The people here are practical, superstitious, and deeply attached to the sea.

**Artisans' Quarter** — The heart of the city's economy. Woodcarvers, shipwrights, armorers, and craftsmen of every trade work here, their shops lined up along narrow streets that smell of sawdust, oil, and hot metal. The quality of the work is exceptional — Neverwinter's craftsmen are the best in the North, and they know it. The guilds are powerful, protective, and slow to trust outsiders.

**The Warrens** — The lowest district, built into the hillsides and connected by tunnels, stairs, and rope bridges. This is where the poor live, where the streets are dark and the alleys are darker. Crime is common, but so is resilience. The people of the Warrens have survived plagues, earthquakes, and political collapses. They will survive whatever comes next.

**The Neverwood** — Beyond the city walls, the forest stretches for miles. It is ancient, dense, and alive with magic. Druids patrol its edges, rangers hunt within its depths, and things that are older than either prowl in the shadows. The Blight that once consumed the city still lingers at the forest's heart, fed by something that should not exist.

## Notable NPCs

- **Viconia Del'rish** — A drow exile who leads a group of outcasts in the Warrens. She is fierce, pragmatic, and deeply distrustful of surface dwellers. She has a particular interest in the party's tiefling — not because of his heritage, but because of his patron.
- **General Esvele Greycrone** — A half-elf commander of the Order of the Gauntlet, stationed in Neverwinter. She is honorable, battle-hardened, and determined to root out corruption in the city's provisional council. She has asked the party for help, indirectly.
- **Master Artificer Borin Stonehand** — A dwarf who runs the largest armory in the Artisans' Quarter. He is gruff, honest, and the best metalworker in the North. He has offered to repair the party's equipment at half price, which is suspicious in itself.
- **The Wardens** — A trio of druids who patrol the edge of the Neverwood. They do not speak Common, they do not trust cities, and they will attack anyone who threatens the forest. They have been watching the party.

## Sights & Sounds

Neverwinter wakes with the mountain. The first light of dawn catches the volcanic rock and turns it red, and the city stirs slowly, reluctantly, as if waking from a dream it does not want to end. The air is warm, the streets are quiet, and the sound of the city is the sound of people moving carefully, as if afraid of disturbing something.

By midday, the harbor is alive, the artisans' quarter is noisy, and the streets fill with the scent of bread, metal, and woodsmoke. The people move faster here, talk louder, work harder. Neverwinter does not have time for leisure. It has a mountain to watch, a forest to defend, and a past that refuses to stay buried.

At dusk, the city closes. Gates shut, lamps are lit, and the mountain's rumble grows louder. The people of Neverwinter know that the mountain speaks at night, and they do not argue with it.

## Secrets

The Blight in the Neverwood is not natural. It was seeded — deliberately, carefully, by someone who wanted the city weakened but not destroyed. The source is deep in the forest, protected by druidic wards and ancient magic. The Wardens know this. They have not acted, because acting would mean entering the Blight, and no one who enters the Blight returns unchanged.

Beneath the old temple of Bane — destroyed decades ago, its ruins left to rot — lies a crypt that was never emptied. The seals are failing. Something is stirring in the dark, something that remembers the temple's purpose and intends to resume it. The provisional council knows. They are waiting for someone else to deal with it.

Neverember is not gone. He is in Waterdeep, plotting, building alliances, waiting for the right moment to return. He has spies in the city, agents in the council, and a plan that does not include the people who live in the shadows of his castle.
