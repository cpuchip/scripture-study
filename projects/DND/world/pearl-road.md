# The Pearl Road — The Shattered Coast

## The Road That Remembers

The Pearl Road follows the coast from Vaelen's Reach south to the Way of the Pearl, where the Sunken Library lies beneath the waves. It is not a safe road. It is not a kind road. It is a road that remembers what happened to this coast when the seals broke.

**The Terrain:** The coast is scarred. The seals of the Sword Coast held back the pressure, but the coastline took the brunt. Cliffs have collapsed. Beaches are gone. The sea is higher here, lapping at places that were solid ground when the party left Daggerford. The road winds through ruins — not Netheril ruins, but newer ones. Places that were towns before the seals failed. They are not abandoned. They are *uninhabitable*. Wild magic surges make the land unstable. Buildings lean. The ground shifts. The sea is patient, and it is hungry.

**The Weather:** The coast has its own weather. Wild magic storms roll in without warning — not rain, but *magic*. Spells fizzle, backfire, or do things they shouldn't. Tilly's goggles glow when a storm is coming. Malachi's orb pulses. Finn's flute hums. The party will learn to read the signs. They will not always be fast enough.

**The Dangers:**
- **Wild Magic Storms:** Random effects. Spells go wrong. Creatures mutate. The Weave is thin here, and it is frayed. Tilly can predict them with her goggles, but she cannot stop them.
- **Cult Raiders:** The Weave Cult has cells along the coast. They are not just preachers — they are raiders. They steal artifacts, they sabotage containment efforts, they attack travelers who carry iron. They are not mindless. They are convinced. They believe they are saving the world.
- **Zhentarim Patrols:** The black market is alive on the Pearl Road. The Zhentarim control the smuggling routes, and they don't like competition. The party will cross their path. They will not like it. The Zhentarim are not evil — they are merchants. They sell wild magic artifacts to the highest bidder. They are indifferent to the consequences.
- **The Sea:** The sea is higher here. The tides are wrong. The party will see things in the water — shapes, shadows, things that shouldn't exist. The sea remembers the seals. It remembers the pressure. It is waiting.

## The Waypoints

**Day 1: The Shattered Cliffs**
The road follows the coast, winding through cliffs that have collapsed. The party will see the ruins of a town — not Netheril, but newer. A place that was alive before the seals failed. There are no bodies. There are no graves. The people left. The buildings remain, leaning at angles that shouldn't be possible. The party will feel the weight of what was lost. They will not speak of it.

**Day 2: The Iron Market**
A black market town, built in the ruins of an old lighthouse. The Zhentarim control it, but they don't rule it. The Iron Market is a free-for-all — smugglers, informants, scholars, and things that shouldn't exist. The party can buy supplies, information, or wild magic artifacts. Everything has a price. Everyone knows what it is. The party will be watched. They will be followed. They will not be attacked — not yet.

**Day 3: The Fault Line Crossing**
The Pearl Road crosses the Fault Line — not the one in Vaelen's Reach, but a branch. The ground is unstable. Wild magic surges are frequent. The party will need to cross quickly, or they will be caught in a storm. Tilly can predict the surges, but she cannot stop them. The party will feel the Fault Line — the air will thin, the magic will warp, the world will become glass. They will not like it. They will need to.

**Day 4: The Way of the Pearl**
The Sunken Library lies beneath the waves at the Way of the Pearl. It is not a dungeon — it is a tomb. The Netheril outpost here was not destroyed. It was *sealed*. The party will find the entrance, but they will not find the key. The key is in the city. The key is in the Deep. The key is in the choice they will make in Act 5.

## The Road Encounters

**The Refugee Caravan:** The party will cross a caravan of refugees from the Lower Wards of Vaelen's Reach. They are not fleeing — they are being relocated. The Wardens are escorting them, not protecting them. The refugees are silent. They are not angry. They are tired. The party will have to choose: help them, ignore them, or help the Wardens. There is no right answer.

**The Cult Preacher:** A Weave Cult preacher will approach the party on the road. He is not violent. He is not aggressive. He is kind, gentle, and utterly convinced. He will offer food, shelter, and comfort. He will ask the party to listen, not to fight. He will tell them the Prophet's words: "The Weave remembers. The Deep remembers. When it wakes, it will not be angry. It will be lonely. And loneliness is a hunger that eats worlds." The party will have to decide what to do with him. Arresting him will make the cult a martyr. Killing him will make the cult a martyr. Letting him go will make the cult stronger. There is no good choice.

**The Zhentarim Offer:** The Zhentarim will approach the party at the Iron Market. They will not threaten. They will offer. They will offer supplies, information, or wild magic artifacts. They will ask for nothing in return — not yet. They will say they are merchants, and merchants trade. They are not lying. They are not lying about anything. The party will take the offer. They will regret it later.

**The Wild Magic Storm:** The party will be caught in a wild magic storm on the Fault Line crossing. Spells will go wrong. Creatures will mutate. The Weave will thin. Tilly will try to predict it, but she will not be fast enough. The party will survive, but they will be changed. Something will happen that cannot be undone. A spell will backfire. A creature will appear. A memory will surface. The party will carry it with them.

## The Moral Weight

The Pearl Road is not just a road. It is a mirror. The party will see what the coast looks like after the seals fail. They will see the refugees, the ruins, the wild magic, the cult, the merchants. They will see the consequences of the choices they made in the first campaign. They will not like what they see. They will not be able to undo it. They will have to carry it.

The road teaches. The road remembers. The road does not forgive.
