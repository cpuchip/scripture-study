# The Trade Way — Waterdeep to Baldur's Gate

## The Route

The Trade Way is the spine of the Sword Coast, the road that connects the north to the south, the sea to the inland, the rich to the poor. It runs from Waterdeep through Daggerford to Baldur's Gate, two hundred miles of paved stone that has been walked, ridden, and wheeled for centuries. The road is wide enough for two carriages, lined with milestones, and maintained by a combination of city funds and merchant taxes.

The landscape shifts as you travel. Leaving Waterdeep, the road cuts through farmland — wheat fields, orchards, vineyards — the kind of countryside that looks peaceful until you remember that peace is maintained by swords. By Daggerford, the terrain rises into hills, the River Dessarin crossing the road at Zundbridge, and the air grows thinner, sharper. Beyond Daggerford, the road follows the Shining River south, through Beregost and into the Lastwall Mountains, where the gorge narrows and the shadows lengthen.

The Trade Way is busy. Caravans move daily, merchants haggle at every stop, and the inns are always full. If you are traveling light, you will move fast. If you are traveling heavy, you will move slowly, and you will be watched.

## Along the Way

**Zundbridge (50 miles from Waterdeep)** — The major crossing of the River Dessarin, a stone bridge wide enough for three carriages and guarded by a garrison of Daggerford soldiers. The toll is reasonable — two copper pieces per person, five per carriage — but the guards are thorough. They check papers, inspect cargo, and ask questions. Travelers with nothing to hide pass through quickly. Travelers with something to hide do not pass through at all. The bridge is well-maintained, the guards are professional, and the river beneath is deep, fast, and unforgiving. Drowning is the fate of those who try to swim it.

**Trollclaw Ford (75 miles from Waterdeep)** — A shallow crossing of the Dessarin, an alternative to Zundbridge for those who want to avoid the toll — or the questions. The ford is passable in dry weather, but after rain, the water rises fast and the current turns vicious. A lone warrior guards the ford — a human named Kestrel, exiled from Daggerford's military for reasons she will not discuss. She charges half the bridge toll and asks no questions. She is armed, armored, and patient. She has driven off bandits, rescued drowning travelers, and refused bribes. She will not join a party. She will watch one, from a distance, and she will remember what she sees.

**The Greenacres Village (100 miles from Waterdeep)** — A farming community on the outskirts of Daggerford, peaceful and practical. The villagers grow wheat, raise chickens, and sell produce to travelers. The inn here is basic — clean beds, hot stew, cold ale — but it is safe, and the people are friendly. The village elder, a woman named Martha, keeps a ledger of every traveler who stays overnight. She does not share it with the guards. She does not share it with anyone. She says it is for her own records. Her handwriting is precise, her memory is flawless, and her reasons are her own.

**Beregost (140 miles from Waterdeep)** — A trading town built around a temple of Oghma, known for its smiths and its scholars. The streets are narrow, the buildings are stone, and the air smells of hot metal and old books. The smiths here produce weapons and armor of exceptional quality, and the temple offers healing, wisdom, and the occasional prophecy. The town is small but proud, and its people tolerate strangers as long as the strangers respect the temple and pay for what they take. The innkeeper, a dwarf named Thardrin, has a particular interest in visitors who carry hammers. He asks about their forging, their training, their lineage. He is not nosy. He is thorough.

**The Lizard's Chasm (165 miles from Waterdeep)** — The road cuts through a narrow gorge here, the walls rising fifty feet on either side, the floor narrow enough that only one carriage can pass at a time. The acoustics are extraordinary — voices echo, footsteps ring, and the wind carries sound for miles. Giant lizards nest in the crevices above, territorial and attracted to noise. They do not usually attack, but they do watch, and their eyes glow faintly in the dark. Travelers are advised to move quickly, speak softly, and avoid striking metal against stone. The chasm is beautiful. It is also dangerous, and beauty does not stop teeth.

## Dangers

The Trade Way is the most traveled road on the Sword Coast, and it is also one of the most dangerous. The dangers are not random — they are organized, patient, and selective.

Caravan robberies near Trollclaw Ford are the primary threat. The bandits are well-armed, well-organized, and well-informed. They know which caravans are valuable, which guards are corrupt, and which travelers are worth targeting. They strike at night, in groups of six to ten, and they disappear into the hills before the militia can respond. The pattern suggests inside information — someone is feeding them schedules, routes, and cargo manifests. The source is unknown. The damage is real.

Road agents operate beyond Zundbridge, collecting unofficial tolls from travelers who have already paid the official ones. They are not guards. They are not militia. They are armed men who claim authority they do not have, and they are violent when resisted. The Daggerford council knows about them. They have not acted, because acting would mean acknowledging a failure of control, and the council does not fail publicly.

The River Dessarin floods in spring, turning Zundbridge into a death trap and Trollclaw Ford into a memory. Travelers who ignore the warnings and try to cross have been swept away, their carriages destroyed, their cargo lost. The river does not care about schedules. It does not care about deadlines. It rises, it takes, it recedes.

Giant lizards in the Chasm are territorial. They do not hunt travelers, but they do defend their nests, and a startled lizard is a dangerous one. Travelers who make noise, strike metal, or provoke the creatures have been bitten, scratched, and chased off the road. The lizards are not evil. They are hungry, and they are patient.

## Rumors

- The guards at Zundbridge are not checking every carriage. Some are being waved through without inspection, and the pattern matches the bandit attacks. Someone on the bridge is working with the robbers.
- Kestrel at Trollclaw Ford was not exiled from Daggerford for incompetence. She was exiled for asking questions — about the road agents, about the council, about the disappearances. She has evidence. She keeps it hidden, and she trusts no one.
- The temple at Beregost has received a vision — a prophecy about five travelers who will walk the Trade Way, each carrying a piece of a greater truth. The priests are watching the road. They are watching the party.
- The Lizard's Chasm is not just a gorge. It is a seal — an ancient barrier, warded by druids, designed to keep something buried in the rock from escaping. The wards are weakening. The lizards know this. They have been restless for months.
