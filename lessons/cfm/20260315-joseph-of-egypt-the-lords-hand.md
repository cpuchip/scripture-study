# Joseph of Egypt: Tracing the Lord's Hand
**Come, Follow Me — Genesis 37–41 | March 15, 2026 | Sunday School**

> "ye thought evil against me; but God meant it unto good, to bring to pass, as it is this day, to save much people alive." — [Genesis 50:20](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/gen/50.md)

## Approach

Start at the end — Joseph as governor of Egypt, feeding his father's household through famine — and trace backward through every link in the chain the Lord used to put him there. The question isn't "what happened to Joseph?" but "what was God doing the whole time?"

## The Pattern

Three things repeat at every level:

### 1. "The Lord Was with Joseph"
This phrase appears in [Genesis 39:2](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/gen/39.md), [39:21](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/gen/39.md), and [39:23](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/gen/39.md). It's the refrain. The Lord's presence doesn't mean the absence of suffering — it means the suffering has purpose.

### 2. Faithfulness Makes Things Worse Before They Get Better
Good stewardship in Potiphar's house → false accusation → prison. Faithful interpretation in prison → the butler forgets him for two more years. Each act of faithfulness doesn't produce immediate reward. It produces the *next step*.

### 3. Joseph Always Credits God
- "Do not interpretations belong to God?" ([Gen 40:8](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/gen/40.md))
- "It is not in me: God shall give Pharaoh an answer" ([Gen 41:16](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/gen/41.md))
- Even Pharaoh sees it: "Can we find such a one as this is, a man in whom the Spirit of God is?" ([Gen 41:38](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/gen/41.md))

## Psalm 105 — God's Aerial View

> "He sent a man before them, even Joseph, who was sold for a servant: Whose feet they hurt with fetters: he was laid in iron: Until the time that his word came: the word of the Lord tried him. The king sent and loosed him." — [Psalm 105:17-20](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/ps/105.md)

"The word of the Lord **tried** him." The waiting, the prison, the forgotten promise — it wasn't neglect. It was refinement.

## Discussion Questions

Pick 2-3. Let the Spirit guide which ones land.

1. **Starting at the summit:** Joseph tells his brothers "God meant it unto good" ([Gen 50:20](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/gen/50.md)). When have you been able to look back and see that God was working through something hard?

2. **The chain:** Trace Joseph's path backward — governor, interpreter, prisoner, slave, sold, dreamer. Removing any single link breaks the chain. What does that tell you about how God works? Does He need our cooperation, or does He work around human agency?

3. **The refrain:** "The Lord was with Joseph" appears three times in Genesis 39 alone — but it never says "the Lord removed Joseph's problems." How do we distinguish between God being *with* us and God making things *easy* for us?

4. **The two-year wait:** The butler forgot Joseph for two full years ([Gen 40:23](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/gen/40.md); [41:1](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/gen/41.md)). What do you think that silence felt like? How do you stay faithful when it seems like God's promises have been forgotten?

5. **"It is not in me":** Every time Joseph interprets, he redirects credit to God. Pharaoh ends up recognizing "the Spirit of God" in Joseph. How does humility about our gifts actually *increase* their impact?
