# The Eleven-Step Creation Cycle

The pattern underneath the practices. The software industry has explicitly named four
of these steps as disciplines of working with AI (marked **[eng]**); the other seven
come from a much older source — the creation pattern in Abraham 4–5, where the work is
counseled before it begins, watched until it obeys, and rested into when it is done.
You don't need the theology to use the cycle; if you want it, it's in
[*Beyond the Prompt*](https://github.com/cpuchip/scripture-book).

| # | Step | The verb | In practice |
|---|------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | **Intent** [eng] | naming the why | `intent.md` — the purpose, values, and decision boundaries the work optimizes for when instructions run out |
| 2 | **Covenant** | binding | `covenant.md` — mutual commitments, both sides; when either side slips, the output degrades (that's a consequence, not a punishment) |
| 3 | **Stewardship** | entrusting | Real authority handed over within bounds: scope, budget, report-back (Practice 3) |
| 4 | **Specification** [eng] | blueprinting | The plan made *before* the building — complete enough to be checkable, item by item (Practices 1–2) |
| 5 | **Line upon Line** [eng] | context | The library built, the window curated; understanding accrues across sessions through the journal (Practices 4–5) |
| 6 | **Physical Creation** [eng] | executing | The work itself — the session, the code, the draft |
| 7 | **Watching** | observing | Output reviewed until it obeys the design; trust earned through repeated verification, then watching relaxes (Practices 6–7) |
| 8 | **Atonement** | re-aiming | When work drifts: name what was wrong, carry the correction forward, and move on changed — never delete-and-pretend. Forward recovery, not blank restart |
| 9 | **Sabbath** | resting | Stop. Look back at the finished cycle. Name what was good — honestly, including what wasn't. The work that never closes is never finished (Practice 8, Coda) |
| 10 | **Consecration** | giving | What you build, you share: the tool, the template, the lesson (Practice 9's "then give it away") |
| 11 | **Zion** | harmonizing | Many agents, one intent: shared purpose at every level, so coordination needs almost no protocol |

Not a rigid sequence — a reference frame. Most working sessions live in steps 4–8;
most *projects* that fail skipped 1–3; most that burn out skipped 9.
