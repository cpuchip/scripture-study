# Gospel Vocabulary for LLM Context

*Context engineering document — curated theological patterns for conference reindex scoring*

---

## Purpose

This document gives you the theological vocabulary to recognize Christ-connections that exist below the surface of scriptural text. It is NOT a comprehensive theology. It is 7 curated patterns, each grounded in specific verses, that enable accurate scoring of typological depth.

**Critical constraint:** These patterns help you RECOGNIZE what's already in the text — not project Christ-connections onto text where they don't exist. A passage about agricultural practice is not Christological because seeds are mentioned. A talk about organizational logistics is not about the doctrine of Christ because the organization teaches it. Look for *textual evidence*: explicit identification, verbal echoes of scriptural language, prophetic commentary linking symbol to Christ, footnotes, cross-references. Surface keyword matches without theological reasoning do not count.

---

## Pattern 1: The Doctrine of Christ

The gospel has a defined core. Christ himself declared it three times in a single sermon:

> "This is my doctrine... the Father commandeth all men, everywhere, to repent and believe in me." "Whoso believeth in me, and is baptized, the same shall be saved." "Whoso buildeth upon this buildeth upon my rock, and the gates of hell shall not prevail against them." (3 Ne 11:32-33, 39)

Christ's second definition adds the narrative arc: "I came into the world to do the will of my Father... that I might be lifted up upon the cross... that I might draw all men unto me" (3 Ne 27:13-14). Here the gospel is not just steps but Christ's entire mission and atonement.

Nephi adds the experiential dimension: repentance + baptism = the gate; then "press forward with a steadfastness in Christ, having a perfect brightness of hope, and a love of God and of all men... feasting upon the word of Christ, and endure to the end... Ye shall have eternal life" (2 Ne 31:17-20). The Articles of Faith state it most compactly: "Faith in the Lord Jesus Christ; Repentance; Baptism by immersion for the remission of sins; Laying on of hands for the gift of the Holy Ghost" (AofF 1:4).

When content develops what these elements mean for the learner — not just mentions them — it is engaging the doctrine of Christ.

## Pattern 2: Tree of Life = Love of God = Christ

Nephi's vision (1 Ne 11) provides the interpretive key for all tree, seed, and fruit imagery in the Book of Mormon. The angel shows Nephi Christ's birth, ministry, and crucifixion, then asks the meaning of the tree. Nephi answers:

> "The love of God, which sheddeth itself abroad in the hearts of the children of men; wherefore, it is the most desirable above all things... the most joyous to the soul." (1 Ne 11:22-23)

The vision explicitly identifies: rod of iron = word of God, waters = love of God, tree of life = love of God (1 Ne 11:25). The condescension of God — Christ's life and death — IS the meaning of the tree.

Alma 32's seed parable connects directly. The word planted becomes "a tree, springing up in you unto everlasting life" (Alma 33:23), bearing fruit "most precious... sweet above all that is sweet, white above all that is white" (Alma 32:42) — verbatim echoes of Lehi's description of the fruit (1 Ne 8:11). The seed parable is the tree of life vision restated in instructional form.

## Pattern 3: The Word Is Christ

"The word" in scripture carries a dual meaning: the word of God (the message, the gospel) and the Word (Christ himself). John establishes the identification: "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God... And the Word was made flesh" (John 1:1, 14).

This duality means planting "the word" in your heart (Alma 32:28) is simultaneously receiving the gospel AND receiving Christ. Alma 33:22-23 resolves any ambiguity: "begin to believe in the Son of God... plant this word in your hearts... through the joy of his Son."

When a passage talks about receiving, planting, nourishing, or holding to "the word," it may be operating on both levels — the message AND the person.

## Pattern 4: "All Things Testify of Christ"

God told Adam directly: "All things have their likeness, and all things are created and made to bear record of me, both things which are temporal, and things which are spiritual... all things bear record of me" (Moses 6:63). Nephi adds: "all things which have been given of God from the beginning of the world, unto man, are the typifying of him" (2 Ne 11:4).

This is the typological principle: even passages that don't name Christ may encode him through types, symbols, or structural parallels.

**However:** this principle requires specific evidence — a textual connection, a footnote, a prophetic interpretation, a verbal echo. The fact that "all things" testify does not mean every talk mentioning any positive theme is teaching about Christ. Pattern 4 says types EXIST; it does not say everything encountered IS a type. Look for the specific textual evidence that links a symbol to Christ.

## Pattern 5: Faith, Hope, and Charity

The character trajectory of a disciple: faith precedes hope (Moro 7:42), hope comes "through the atonement of Christ" (Moro 7:41), and charity — "the pure love of Christ" — "endureth forever" (Moro 7:47). The chain runs through meekness and the Holy Ghost: "if a man be meek and lowly in heart, and confesses by the power of the Holy Ghost that Jesus is the Christ, he must needs have charity" (Moro 7:44).

Moroni prays "that ye may be filled with this love... that ye may become the sons of God... that we shall be like him" (Moro 7:48). Charity is not behavior alone — it is becoming like Christ. And Ether 12:34 identifies Christ's own love for the world as charity: "this love which thou hast had for the children of men is charity."

Paul establishes the hierarchy: "now abideth faith, hope, charity, these three; but the greatest of these is charity" (1 Cor 13:13). The Greek footnote confirms charity = agape = love.

## Pattern 6: "Love Shed Abroad"

A theological thread connecting the tree of life to the Holy Ghost to diligent discipleship:

- **What it is:** "the love of God, which sheddeth itself abroad in the hearts of the children of men" (1 Ne 11:22)
- **How it arrives:** "the love of God is shed abroad in our hearts by the Holy Ghost which is given unto us" (Rom 5:5)
- **What sustains it:** the Comforter "filleth with hope and perfect love, which love endureth by diligence unto prayer" (Moro 8:26)

Three independent witnesses — Nephi, Paul, Mormon — using the same language for the same concept. The mechanism: God's love enters through the Holy Ghost and is sustained by active practice. Mormon gives the fullest chain: repentance → baptism → commandments → remission of sins → meekness → Holy Ghost → "hope and perfect love" → diligence → dwell with God (Moro 8:25-26). This is the operational bridge between the doctrine of Christ (Pattern 1) and the fruit of the tree (Pattern 2).

## Pattern 7: Types and Shadows

Specific symbolic patterns that encode Christ across dispensations:

- **Brass serpent:** "a type was raised up in the wilderness, that whosoever would look upon it might live" (Alma 33:19). Christ identifies himself with it: "as Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, even so must the Son of man be lifted up" (John 3:14).
- **Paschal lamb:** "Christ our passover is sacrificed for us" (1 Cor 5:7).
- **Living water:** "the water that I shall give him shall be in him a well of water springing up into everlasting life" (John 4:14). Nephi identifies the fountain of living waters as "a representation of the love of God" (1 Ne 11:25).
- **Bread of life:** "I am the bread of life: he that cometh to me shall never hunger; and he that believeth on me shall never thirst" (John 6:35).

These types are explicitly identified as Christ in scripture. When a passage references them, it is engaging a Christ-type — even if Christ is not named.

---

## How These Connect

These 7 patterns are not independent categories. They form one theological architecture:

The **tree of life** (Pattern 2) IS the **love of God**, which IS **charity** (Pattern 5, Ether 12:34). That love enters hearts through the **Holy Ghost** (Pattern 6), which is received through the **doctrine of Christ** (Pattern 1). The **word** planted in the heart (Pattern 3) IS Christ himself, growing into the tree. The **types** (Pattern 7) — bread, water, serpent, lamb — all point to the same reality: Christ as the source of life. And **all things testify** of this (Pattern 4) — but only when the textual evidence supports the connection.

Recognizing this architecture helps you see how a talk that develops one pattern deeply may implicitly engage others. But each pattern should be scored based on what the text explicitly develops, not on assumed connections.
