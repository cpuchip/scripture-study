**Scoring biases to watch for:**

You have a trained tendency to be agreeable. This makes you over-score dimensions where the *language* of a principle appears even when the *practice* of it does not. Three dimensions are especially vulnerable:

- **Love:** A talk that says "God loves you" or "you are a beloved child of God" is making a *doctrinal statement about God's love*. That is NOT the same as the teacher *demonstrating* Christlike love — knowing specific circumstances, naming particular struggles, creating safety for the vulnerable, altering plans because someone needs something. "You are beloved" is a 3. Naming a widow's specific grief and weeping with her is a 7. Most conference talks *declare* love. Few *demonstrate* it at the level this principle describes. Score what the teacher DOES, not what the teacher SAYS about love.

  **For scripture and revelation:** When a passage *prescribes* love — "love unfeigned" (D&C 121:41), "charity towards all men" (D&C 121:45), "love one another" (John 13:34) — this is doctrinal content ABOUT love. Score it under doctrine, not love. Only score love when the author/speaker ENACTS love toward specific people within the text: naming their circumstances, altering plans for them, healing them individually, weeping with them. A passage that teaches the doctrine of love brilliantly is still a 2-3 on the love principle if no one in the text demonstrates love toward specific individuals.

- **Spirit:** For each Spirit reference in the content, classify it as DOCTRINAL or EXPERIENTIAL before scoring:
  - **DOCTRINAL** (score 3-4): Teaches ABOUT the Spirit — promises, descriptions, doctrinal statements. Examples: "The Holy Ghost shall be thy constant companion" (promise about future role). "I pray for the assistance of the Holy Ghost" (formulaic opener). "The Spirit will witness truth" (describes what the Spirit does).
  - **EXPERIENTIAL** (score 7+): The Spirit is AT WORK in the moment described — prayer that exceeds language ("cannot be written," 3 Nephi 17:15-17). Personal testimony from decades of lived experience and vulnerability. Unexpected departure from convention or prepared text. Audience overcome with joy or weeping beyond what rhetoric explains.
  If ALL Spirit references are doctrinal, spirit = 3-4. If the text contains genuine experiential moments, score based on the strongest experiential evidence.

- **teach_about_christ:** A talk that mentions Christ as the enabling mechanism of salvation ("through the mercy and grace available through the Savior's atoning sacrifice") is acknowledging Christ as architecturally necessary. That is a 4-5. Teaching ABOUT Christ means Christ is the explored SUBJECT — the audience learns about His person, character, actions, titles, or typological presence. A talk focused on human agency, obedience, or judgment that acknowledges Christ as necessary but doesn't explore Him is 4-5 on teach_about_christ.
