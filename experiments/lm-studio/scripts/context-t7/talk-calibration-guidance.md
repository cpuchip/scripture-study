SCORE DISTRIBUTION GUIDANCE

Most conference talks score 4-6 on most dimensions. Only 1-2 dimensions reach 7+ for any given talk. A 9 is rare and requires the dimension to be the sustained, unmistakable focus of the entire talk.

If you find yourself scoring 7+ on three or more dimensions, you are inflating. Step back and ask: what does this talk do BEST? That is the dominant dimension. Everything else is secondary and should score honestly — often 3-5.

Love and spirit are the most commonly inflated dimensions:
- LOVE requires demonstrated Christlike action toward specific people — not declared warmth, not quoting scripture about love, not saying "you are beloved." If the speaker does not name specific struggles, alter plans for individuals, or create safety for vulnerability, love is 2-4.
- SPIRIT requires experiential evidence — personal testimony from lived vulnerability, prayer that exceeds language, visible departure from prepared remarks, audience overcome beyond what rhetoric explains. If the speaker says "I pray for the Spirit" and delivers a scripted talk, spirit is 3-4.

CALIBRATION PATTERNS (from scored conference talks):
- A doctrine-dominant talk: teach 5, help 5, love 2, spirit 3, doctrine 9, invite 6. Intellectual rigor, no individualized connection, no departure from prepared text.
- A spirit-dominant talk: teach 7, help 6, love 4, spirit 7, doctrine 6, invite 3. Personal testimony from decades of lived experience; warm but no specific calls to action.

KEY PATTERNS:
- Love scores: 2-5 for most conference talks. A 7+ requires demonstrated action toward specific individuals.
- Spirit scores: 3-4 when doctrinal only; 7+ when the speaker shows genuine vulnerability or departs from script.
- Each talk has 1-2 high dimensions. Everything else falls in 3-6. This is normal and expected.
- A score of 9 is rare. It requires the dimension to be the sustained, defining, unmistakable focus of the entire talk.
