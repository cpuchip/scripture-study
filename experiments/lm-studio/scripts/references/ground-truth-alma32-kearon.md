# Ground Truth Study — Alma 32 and Kearon's "Receive His Gift"

*Created Mar 28, 2026 during TITSW prompt refinement. This document captures the deep reading that a local LLM cannot do on its own — the cross-references, typological connections, and theological architecture that make a 0-9 scoring scale meaningful.*

---

## Alma 32 — The Seed Is Christ

### Surface Reading

Alma teaches the poor Zoramites about faith using a seed metaphor. Christ is barely mentioned by name. The chapter focuses on epistemology (how do you know something is true?) and the process of spiritual growth.

A model scoring `teach_about_christ` from surface text alone gets a 1-2. Defensible at that level.

### The Tree of Life Connection

Alma 32 is NOT just a metaphor about faith. It's the tree of life vision in instructional form.

**The seed → tree → fruit progression:**

| Alma 32 | 1 Nephi 8 / 11 | Interpretation (1 Ne 11:22-25) |
|---------|-----------------|-------------------------------|
| "the word" (seed, v.28) | "the rod of iron" (1 Ne 8:19) | "the word of God" (1 Ne 11:25) |
| plant in heart, nourish | hold to the rod, press forward | pursue the word/Christ |
| tree springing up to everlasting life (v.41) | the tree of life (1 Ne 8:10) | "a representation of the love of God" (1 Ne 11:25) |
| fruit: "most precious, sweet above all that is sweet, white above all that is white" (v.42) | "beauty thereof was far beyond, yea, exceeding of all beauty; and the whiteness thereof did exceed the whiteness of the driven snow" (1 Ne 8:11) | "the love of God, which sheddeth itself abroad in the hearts of the children of men; wherefore, it is the most desirable above all things" (1 Ne 11:22) |

The language in Alma 32:42 is nearly verbatim from 1 Nephi 8:10-11. Footnote 42b explicitly cross-references 1 Ne 8:10. This is not coincidence — the text links Alma's seed parable to Lehi's tree of life vision.

### The Word Is Christ

The dual meaning of "the word" in Alma 32:28:

1. **The word of God** — the message, the gospel, the teaching (Luke 8:11 — parable of the sower, where the seed is "the word of God"; footnote 28b points here)
2. **The Word** — Christ himself (John 1:1 — "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God")

Alma 33 makes the Christ-identification explicit:
- Alma 33:11 — Zenos: "it is because of thy **Son** that thou hast been thus merciful unto me"
- Alma 33:19 — Moses raised "a **type**" in the wilderness (brass serpent = type of Christ)
- Alma 33:22 — "believe in the **Son of God**, that he will come to redeem his people, and that he shall suffer and die to **atone** for their sins"
- Alma 33:23 — "plant this word in your hearts... it will become a tree, springing up in you unto everlasting life... through the joy of **his Son**"

So planting the seed = receiving Christ into your heart. The tree growing = Christ's love taking root. The fruit = experiencing the love of God, which IS Christ's atonement made personal.

### The "Sheddeth Itself Abroad" Thread

A single theological thread runs through multiple books:

1. **1 Nephi 11:22** — the love of God "**sheddeth itself abroad** in the hearts of the children of men"
2. **Romans 5:5** — "the love of God is **shed abroad** in our hearts by the Holy Ghost which is given unto us"
3. **Moroni 8:26** — the Comforter "filleth with hope and perfect love, which love **endureth by diligence** unto prayer"
4. **Alma 32:42** — "because of your **diligence** and your faith and your patience with the word in nourishing it... ye shall pluck the fruit thereof"

All four passages describe the same process: the love of God (= Christ) entering the heart through the Spirit's witness, sustained by diligence. Alma 32 is the how-to manual for something 1 Nephi 11 reveals in vision and Romans 5 and Moroni 8 confirm doctrinally.

## Kearon — "Receive His Gift"

### Surface Reading

Explicitly about Christ. The gift = knowing we are God's children. The greatest gift = Christ himself. Easy high scores on Christ-centered dimensions.

### Deeper Connections

**Footnote 5 links to 1 Nephi 11:17** — "I know that he loveth his children; nevertheless, I do not know the meaning of all things." This is the same chapter where the tree of life = the love of God. Kearon is building on this foundation: the gift of knowing we are God's children IS the fruit of the tree of life.

**The gift metaphor maps to Alma 32's seed metaphor:**

| Kearon (Gift) | Alma 32 (Seed) | Both |
|---------------|----------------|------|
| Gift given by the Father | Seed available to plant | The truth/Christ is offered |
| Accept and open the gift | Give place for the seed | Create receptivity in the heart |
| Receive the gift deeply | Nourish the tree | Internalize, act, let it grow |
| Transformed by the gift | Feast on the fruit | Experience the love of God |

"Just let it in" (Kearon) = "give place, that a seed may be planted in your heart" (Alma 32:28). Same process from the grace side (Kearon) vs the agency side (Alma).

**D&C 88:33** (Kearon's footnote 9) — "what doth it profit a man if a gift is bestowed upon him, and he receive not the gift?" The receiving problem connects to Alma 32:38-39 — if you neglect the tree, it withers. Not because the seed was bad but because "your ground is barren, and ye will not nourish the tree."

### Ground-Truth TITSW Scoring (Kearon)

| Dimension | Score (0-9) | Reasoning |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| teach_about_christ | 8 | Christ is named repeatedly, identified as "the greatest of all the Father's gifts," the Lamb of God, the Redeemer. Every gift flows from and is activated by His atonement. |

| doctrine | 7 | Strong scriptural anchoring (James 1:17, Ether 12:11, D&C 88:33, Moroni 10:8,30, 2 Ne 2:8, 2 Ne 2:25). The three-part gift framework (giving → accepting/opening → receiving) is original doctrinal synthesis. |
| invite | 8 | Multiple direct, specific invitations: "Will you please accept, open, and receive this gift?" "Will you do so more deeply, more freely, more abundantly?" "Will you pray to understand?" The strongest dimension. |

## What This Means for Prompt Engineering

1. **A 0-3 scale cannot capture typological depth.** The difference between surface and informed scoring on Alma 32's `teach_about_christ` is 1 vs 7-8. That's invisible on a 0-3 scale.

2. **Models need gospel vocabulary.** A local LLM can only trace connections present in its context window or training data. Training data is unreliable for this depth. Context engineering is the answer.

3. **Conference talks vs scriptures need different treatment.** Talks are usually explicit about Christ-connections (Kearon scores similarly surface and deep). Scriptures often encode Christ typologically (Alma 32 looks nothing like a Christ-centered text on its surface). The pipeline may need different prompts or different context levels.

4. **The curated context set should include:**
   - The tree of life interpretive key (1 Ne 11:21-25)
   - The "word = Christ" connection (John 1:1-14)
   - The "sheddeth itself abroad" thread (1 Ne 11:22, Rom 5:5, Moro 8:26)
   - Core typological patterns (brass serpent, paschal lamb, tree of life, living water)
   - The TITSW framework itself as context so the model can reason about teaching dimensions
