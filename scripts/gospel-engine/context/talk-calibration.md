SCORE DISTRIBUTION GUIDANCE

Most conference talks score 4-6 on most dimensions. Only 1-2 dimensions reach 7+ for any given talk. A 9 is rare and requires the dimension to be the sustained, unmistakable focus of the entire talk.

If you find yourself scoring 7+ on three or more dimensions, you are inflating. Step back and ask: what does this talk do BEST? That is the dominant dimension. Everything else is secondary and should score honestly — often 3-5.

Love and spirit are the most commonly inflated dimensions:
- LOVE requires demonstrated Christlike action toward specific people — not declared warmth, not quoting scripture about love, not saying "you are beloved." If the speaker does not name specific struggles, alter plans for individuals, or create safety for vulnerability, love is 2-4.
- SPIRIT requires experiential evidence — personal testimony from lived vulnerability, prayer that exceeds language, visible departure from prepared remarks, audience overcome beyond what rhetoric explains. If the speaker says "I pray for the Spirit" and delivers a scripted talk, spirit is 3-4.

---

CALIBRATION EXAMPLE 1 — Doctrine-Dominant Talk

Speaker: Elder David A. Bednar, "They Are Their Own Judges" (Oct 2025)
DOMINANT: doctrine
MODE: doctrinal
PATTERN: definition→scripture chain→principle→application
TEACH_SCORE: 5  (Christ acknowledged as enabling mechanism — "through the Savior's atoning sacrifice" — but not the explored subject. The talk is about agency and judgment.)
HELP_SCORE: 5  (helps people understand judgment, implicitly Christ-ward, but the mechanism is "use your agency correctly" not "receive Christ's power")
LOVE_SCORE: 2  (no individualized connection; teaching delivered to an abstract audience; no naming of struggles; "I pray for the Holy Ghost" is formulaic, not relational)
SPIRIT_SCORE: 3  (Spirit mentioned doctrinally; no departure from prepared text; no spontaneous testimony; intellectual rigor is the teaching mode, not spiritual spontaneity)
DOCTRINE_SCORE: 9  (the defining dimension — sustained doctrinal exposition across the entire talk; dictionary definitions, 7+ scriptural citations building causally, premortal council, doing-vs-becoming framework, worldly-vs-godly fear distinction)
INVITE_SCORE: 6  ("deal justly, judge righteously, and do good continually" — the framework functions as an implicit invitation, but intellectually framed, no specific "will you do X this week?")

CALIBRATION EXAMPLE 2 — Spirit-Dominant Talk

Speaker: Elder Jeffrey R. Holland, "And Now I See" (Oct 2025)
DOMINANT: spirit, teach_about_christ
MODE: declared
PATTERN: story→doctrine→testimony
TEACH_SCORE: 7  (Christ is the framework — John 9 healing, priesthood authority traced to Him, Book of Mormon as "Another Testament of Jesus Christ." Every section connects to Christ. Not 8+ because middle sections connect institutionally rather than revealing His person.)
HELP_SCORE: 6  (testimony designed to stir faith; moves hearts; but mechanism is "believe the evidence" not "here's how to receive His power")
LOVE_SCORE: 4  (warm tone, acknowledges tragedies, "love unfeigned" — but declared love and general pastoral warmth, not demonstrated love at the individual level)
SPIRIT_SCORE: 7  (the defining dimension alongside teach — personal testimony from decades of lived experience: "I have been at the edge of death and back, walked with kings and prophets, been overwhelmed by the Holy Spirit of God." This is vulnerability, not formula. The "Amazing Grace" ending departs from typical conference script.)
DOCTRINE_SCORE: 6  (solid John 9 exegesis, priesthood authority chain, temple theology — but doctrine serves the testimony arc; breadth across four topics rather than sustained depth on one)
INVITE_SCORE: 3  ("Are we willing to persevere?" is rhetorical; no specific calls to action; no "go home and do this"; the closing is testimony and hymn, not invitation)

---

KEY PATTERNS TO NOTICE:
- Love scores: 2 and 4. Most conference talks score 2-5 on love. A 7+ requires demonstrated action toward specific individuals.
- Spirit scores: 3 and 7. The difference is doctrinal mention (Bednar) vs lived testimony and visible vulnerability (Holland). A formulaic "I pray for the Spirit" is NOT evidence of teaching by the Spirit.
- Each talk has 1-2 high dimensions. Everything else falls in 3-6. This is normal and expected.
- A score of 9 (Bednar doctrine) is rare. It requires the dimension to be the sustained, defining, unmistakable focus of the entire talk.
