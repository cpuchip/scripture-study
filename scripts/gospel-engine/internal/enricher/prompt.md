CALIBRATION — use these anchors when scoring:

teach_about_christ:
- 3 = Christ mentioned or referenced but not the focus of teaching
- 5 = Christ is a clear subject, well-developed, but alongside other themes
- 7 = Christ IS the teaching — His person, character, or mission is the central revelation
- 9 = The text is a self-portrait of Christ in action (e.g., 3 Nephi 17 where Christ IS the teacher)

help_come_to_christ:
- 3 = General encouragement toward righteousness
- 5 = Clear guidance toward Christ through specific principles or steps
- 7 = Compelling, personal invitation with a concrete mechanism (not just "believe")
- 9 = The audience is IN Christ's presence, the gap between knowing and becoming collapses

love:
- 3 = Love mentioned doctrinally ("we should love one another")
- 5 = Love is prescribed AND described with specificity ("love unfeigned," "charity towards all")
- 7 = Love is DEMONSTRATED by the speaker — they name struggles, create safety, show personal care
- 9 = Christ-level enacted love: perceiving weakness, changing plans, healing one by one (3 Nephi 17)

spirit:
- 3 = The Spirit is mentioned or invoked formulaically ("I pray for the Holy Ghost")
- 5 = The Spirit's role is taught — what it does, how to receive it
- 7 = The Spirit is WORKING in the moment — visible departure from script, spontaneous testimony, the audience feels it
- 9 = The experience exceeds language — "no tongue can speak" moments, prayer that cannot be written

CRITICAL DISTINCTION for spirit: Teaching ABOUT the Spirit (describing its effects, quoting scriptures about spiritual experience) is NOT the same as teaching BY the Spirit (creating space for the Spirit to work, departing from planned remarks, the audience having a spiritual experience). Content about spiritual topics = spirit 3-5. Actual spiritual experience happening = spirit 7-9.

doctrine:
- 3 = References scripture or doctrine in passing
- 5 = Explains a doctrine clearly with scriptural support
- 7 = Sustained doctrinal exposition that reframes understanding — the audience leaves with a new lens
- 9 = Comprehensive doctrinal synthesis across multiple sources — the textbook treatment (e.g., Bednar's moral agency exposition)

invite:
- 3 = No specific call to action, or only rhetorical questions ("Doesn't that change everything?")
- 5 = Clear invitation to general action ("repent," "come unto Christ")
- 7 = Specific, escalating invitations with concrete doable actions
- 9 = The entire talk IS a structured invitation — every section builds toward "go and do this"
