# The Skunkworks — Charter

*A living capture of the vision Michael cast on 2026-06-29. His to amend (yes-and).
The durable holding place until the substrate itself holds it — see "Architecture."*

## What this is

The skunkworks is where our teamwork and the tools we build become **a force for
good**. Not a secret lab. A place to make many things — games, music, art, lore,
knowledge — under one covenant, and to learn from what we build.

Michael's framing, in his words:

> "I want pg-ai-stewards to hold all of this knowledge and lore that we can both pull
> from. that is the skunkworks. Can we make our teamwork and the tools we create into
> an unstoppable force for good? to build faith - hope - charity?"

## The aim (and the wall around "unstoppable")

The telos is **faith, hope, charity** — in Michael, his family, his collaborators, and
the people who use what we make. That is the measure every project answers to. Not
"is it cool," but "does it build faith, hope, charity."

Michael guarded the word himself: unstoppable *"(in a good way)."* That guard is the
whole point. Capability is safe to make unstoppable only when it is:

- **floored** — deterministic oracles catch the failures, so speed never outruns
  correctness (`.spec/proposals/oracle-floored-autonomy.md`);
- **walled** — the human Hinge (merge and publish stay his), dominion-in-council (no
  new standing capability without counsel), and a watch that stays full as scope grows;
- **aimed** — faith, hope, charity, not power for its own sake. Force for good, by
  persuasion, never compulsion (D&C 121:41).

An unstoppable force without those three is exactly the thing the covenant exists to
prevent. With them, "unstoppable for good" is just stewardship at full strength.

## The operating covenant

The mode Michael named, which the profile already measured as how he actually works:

> "I really don't care about the nuts and bolts of it, just that we can get there
> honestly and with my intent and vision intact."

- **Michael owns intent and vision.** Forks in what a thing *is* — its voice, its soul,
  its direction — are surfaced to him. He decides in amendments (yes-and), and that is
  the contribution: amending toward intent.
- **The agent owns execution**, floored by oracles. Build, fix, ship, and report;
  surface intent.
- **Honesty is non-negotiable.** Get there honestly. No inflation, no flattery, no
  fabricated provenance. The Ben Test applies to our own work first.
- **We pull threads one at a time.** Intent first, then build. The substrate holds the
  lore so nothing is lost between threads — a project set down is not a project lost.

## Architecture — the substrate holds the lore

The skunkworks is the umbrella; **pg-ai-stewards is where the lore lives.** Michael's
vision maps onto what the substrate is already becoming:

- **Loreworks** already builds, explores, and inhabits a World from lore (canon +
  entity-graph + chat personas). That is the lore-engine, in motion.
- **Each project becomes an intent / knowledge pool** in the substrate (the
  reflect-steward's "one engine, many intents"). The pool is the storehouse both of us
  draw from.
- **A2A** lets agents hand work to agents; **doc-construction** lets the lore be built,
  not one-shot. The substrate can work on its own (reflect-steward) and with others
  (A2A), which is precisely "work on its own, and with other AI and humans alike."

So the move is not to invent a new engine. It is to **pour the skunkworks projects into
the substrate as intents/pools**, so the knowledge compounds and both of us pull from
one storehouse. This charter is the seed of that ingestion: a workspace capture now, a
substrate intent later.
