## Why this matters beyond the fixes

This is the book's thesis with field data. "If I had the sources present,
AI agents did a good job of looking up" — TRUE, measured: sources-read
error rate ≈ one pronoun per sixteen quotes. Sources-recalled error rate ≈
total fabrication wearing perfect formatting (right links, right titles,
invented words). The failure mode isn't sloppiness; it's *fluent
confidence about unread sources* — which is exactly why the read-before-
quoting covenant clause and the cite-count rule exist. The walk is
producing the empirical justification for the tooling timeline, file by
file.

The Wells find is the sober one: a false biography of a named, living man
sat in an EQ lesson document for four months. Nobody recalls maliciously;
the model filled a story-shaped hole with a plausible story. Teaching-
extension stakes: that document was *teachable material*.
