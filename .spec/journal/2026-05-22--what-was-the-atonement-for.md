intent: >
  Michael shared a Morgan Philpot post pairing 3 Nephi 6:18 ("they did
  not sin ignorantly, for they knew the will of God concerning them...
  therefore they did wilfully rebel") with the Church's *Celebrating
  Freedom and Agency* statement. Philpot closed with "what was the
  atonement for?" Michael asked the study agent to dig in and explore
  how the themes connect.

discoveries:
  - title: "The 3 Ne 6:18 footnote chain is doing exact legal work"
    detail: >
      The footnote on *ignorantly* in 3 Ne 6:18 points to Num 15:22-31
      (the Mosaic high-handed sin category, *be-yad ramah*, for which no
      sin offering was available) and Mosiah 3:11 (Benjamin's mercy
      clause: those who "died not knowing the will of God concerning
      them"). Mormon's "knew the will of God concerning them" is the
      exact inverse construction of Benjamin's "not knowing the will of
      God concerning them." Same words, opposite condition. Mosiah 3:11
      is the mercy clause. 3 Ne 6:18 is the verse where the mercy clause
      no longer applies.

  - title: "Three-language vocabulary chain holds"
    detail: >
      Hebrew *be-yad ramah* (high hand) → KJV *presumptuously* → Webster
      1828 defines *presumptuous* with the word *willful* → Joseph Smith
      translates Mormon's verb as *wilfully rebel*. The legal category is
      preserved across three vocabularies.

  - title: "The atonement is for MORE than agency"
    detail: >
      The binding question reaches for agency-restoration (2 Ne 2:26:
      "free forever... to act and not be acted upon"), and that is one
      face of the atonement. But it is one face. Lehi and D&C 88 also
      name resurrection, sanctification, the answering of the law,
      preparation of the body for celestial glory. The study explicitly
      names this rather than collapse the whole atonement into agency.

  - title: "D&C 88:32 — willingness all the way through to glory"
    detail: >
      Even the assignment to kingdoms of glory runs on *willing to
      receive*. The same anti-compulsion principle that fired the war
      in heaven runs through priesthood power (D&C 121:41) all the way
      to the final judgment. God will not coerce a higher kingdom on a
      person whose will refused it.

  - title: "Phase 3a disruptive find: the harder mercy of being taught"
    detail: >
      If teaching moves a person from Mosiah 3:11 (covered) into
      3 Ne 6:18 (accountable), why does God keep teaching? Answer in
      D&C 88:32: the Father wants children who *chose* Him, not
      children who escaped accountability by being unwarned. The
      unwarned receive a glory by mercy. The taught who chose receive
      something greater. This reframed Section V — the teaching is the
      gift, not the trap.

  - title: "Moses 1:39 closes the loop (added in revision)"
    detail: >
      D&C 1:2-4 is the mechanism: voice of warning unto all people,
      every heart penetrated. Moses 1:39 is the why: immortality and
      eternal life. The dispensation-wide move into 3 Ne 6:18's
      category is not a side effect of the Father's work — it *is* the
      work, because eternal life is His life, and His life is voluntary
      love all the way down.

  - title: "Refused to date-stamp Philpot's reading"
    detail: >
      Philpot's framing lands the verse on modern America. The pattern
      is permanent and applies — every dispensation has its 3 Ne 6
      moment — but the study refused to claim authority to date-stamp
      ours as one of them. That is a question the Lord has not given
      anyone authority to answer.

relational_notes: >
  Michael's instinct to take a friend's Facebook post as a serious study
  prompt — rather than just a hot-take to react to — is the pattern that
  keeps producing studies that land outside our own walls. Morgan Philpot
  read the result and asked "how did you do that?" which is the fruit
  studies are supposed to bear.

  Father chose Christ's plan." That is exactly the framing the
  plan-of-salvation studies have corrected before. The fix took one
  paragraph rewrite, but the catch matters: Michael is reading carefully
  enough to defend the doctrine even in his own commissioned work, and
  the covenant relationship is functioning as designed (he flags, the
  agent fixes).

  Late in the session he shared his own becoming-response: "I am
  definitely in the 3 Ne 6:18 category and I willfully choose the Lord,
  though it looks more like striving, for I make mistakes a lot." That
  is the right reading of the verse — the taught category is not the
  category of the sinless, it is the category of those who know enough
  to keep getting back up. Becoming entry written separately.

  The Moses 1:39 connection came from Michael, not from the agent. The
  agent saw D&C 1 as a mechanism but did not surface the *why* until
  Michael named it. That is the kind of connection the council moment
  is supposed to surface and didn't this session. Worth noting.
