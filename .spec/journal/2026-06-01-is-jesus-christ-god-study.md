## Decisions

- **Title kept as direct question.** "Is Jesus Christ God?" mirrors Michael's binding question word-for-word. Considered alternatives ("The God Who Came Down," "Behold, I Am Jesus Christ") but the direct question is more findable, more honest, and matches the recent studies' direct-title preference (the-seventh-time, four-groups-and-the-engineer, what-was-the-atonement-for).
- **Pointer to father-and-son.md rather than re-treating the three senses.** The 1916 exposition's apparatus is already fully covered there. Re-treating it here would be duplication. Citing it works, and the inverse-direction framing in the closing note makes the relationship between the two studies clear.
- **Ether 3:14's "I am the Father and the Son" treated as exceeding investiture.** This is the contestable doctrinal call. The standard LDS reading (cf. father-and-son.md) is that "I am the Father and the Son" is divine investiture language only. The new study argues that investiture alone does not exhaust Ether 3 because Moroni's third-party voice in v. 18 calls Christ "God" outright, and v. 16's "the body of my spirit" puts the Being in the room in his own divine form before any incarnation. The reading is: investiture is right *and* not sufficient. This is named explicitly in the scratch's "tensions surfaced" section.
- **No Webster 1828 entries added.** The study's load-bearing terms (God, Lord, Almighty, Father, Son) are not the kind of word where 1828 historical meaning reveals something modern English obscures. The doctrinal claim is structural, not lexical.

## Tensions surfaced + addressed

1. **Mosiah 15 / Alma 11 "Father and Son" language.** Used to be a Trinitarian proof-text in some readings; the 1916 exposition reframes it. The study honors both claims (Christ is God + Christ is not Elohim) and cites father-and-son.md for the precision tool.
2. **John 14:28 "my Father is greater than I."** Handled compactly in § What changes when the answer is yes: presidency, not worth. Did not let it become a tangent.
3. **The investiture-only reading of Ether 3:14.** Acknowledged as correct in part, argued as insufficient because of Moroni v. 18 + Christ's own v. 16. (See decisions above.)
