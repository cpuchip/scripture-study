## The finding (the part worth remembering)
**The best DM in the hobby presides — he does not compel.** "You cannot force your
players to roleplay" (S3) is D&C 121 at the table. And two of his rules are
machinery we already built: **spotlight-sharing = the Callie/DM deference rule**
(don't seize another's moment); **reward-not-scold = voice-michael's
kindness-over-anger + the covenant's reprove-then-increase-trust**. So the
gamemaster persona shouldn't bolt on a separate "be a good DM" instruction — it can
**inherit the presiding covenant directly.** The craft of the best human DM and our
presiding framework are the same shape. Lovely cross-domain confirmation.

## Concrete artifacts (hand-off to skills + personas)
- **`believable-villains`:** inside-out motivation ("twisted righteousness"; the
  villain is the hero of his own story) + a **5-archetype palette** (scheming liar /
  tyrant / sophisticate / misguided fool / monster, from *Book of Vile Darkness* 3.5)
  + honor-a-clever-win over the scripted climax.
- **`character-voice`:** a **voice schema** (pitch · texture · placement · accent ·
  cadence), tracked per recurring NPC, reloaded on return — "they always remember."
- **`sacrifice-and-loss` / `emotional-resonance`:** danger-or-no-stakes ·
  cinematic-beat · crystallized-last-line · in-fiction-wake · epilogue-to-the-
  bereaved · persistent-callbacks · tend-the-people.
- **New skill to author — `therefore-but-not-and-then`** (only a principle today):
  the 7 causal reincorporation hooks + disposition-flips-from-action.
