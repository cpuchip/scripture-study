## The last five chapters

- **Moroni 6** — baptism for the broken heart + witnessed repentance; the church exists to nourish members toward "relying alone upon the merits of Christ"; discipline + unlimited forgiveness are one; the church is Spirit-led top to bottom (completing the chs 2-6 thread).
- **Moroni 7** — Mormon's great sermon. The Light of Christ given to every man to know good from evil "as plain as daylight from dark"; the discernment test ("every thing which inviteth to do good, and to persuade to believe in Christ, is sent forth by the power and gift of Christ"); faith → hope → charity rooted in meekness; "charity is the pure love of Christ… bestowed," prayed for to be filled with.
- **Moroni 8** — the letter on infant baptism. Little children alive in Christ (safe by the atonement, not by natural innocence); the children's safety as a *necessary consequence of God's unchangeable character*; "perfect love casteth out all fear" — the boldness is the fruit of charity.
- **Moroni 9** — the darkest chapter, and Christ at the bottom. The hardened heart rejects both sharpness and gentleness; "past feeling" + loss of love are the terminal marks of a people without the Spirit; we labor not for results but as a duty owed to God, lest we be hardened too; and the close — "may Christ lift thee up."
- **Moroni 10** — the capstone. The promise (ask with a sincere heart, real intent, faith in Christ → the Spirit *will* manifest the truth; by the Holy Ghost ye may know the truth of all things); the gifts of the Spirit; "speaking out of the dust" (Isaiah 29:4 fulfilled — and we are the readers); the summit "come unto Christ, and be perfected in him… by his grace ye may be perfect in Christ"; and the very last verse names Jesus "the great Jehovah, the Eternal Judge."

## Discoveries / what surprised me

- **Moroni 7's order is the teaching.** The "charity chapter" doesn't begin with charity, or even faith — it begins with *discernment*, because you can't exercise faith in the good until you can recognize the good. I'd read past that for years.
- **Moroni 8's deepest argument isn't about children — it's about God.** "If not so, God is a partial God, and also a changeable God, and a respecter to persons." The salvation of little children is a necessary consequence of God's unchangeable character; the doctrine protects God's nature as much as the children. Deny it and the whole nature of God collapses.
- **The unchanging God is the spine of the whole Moroni close.** Mormon 9, Moroni 7, 8, and 10 all keep arriving at God's immutability as the ground of every assurance — the gifts never cease on His side (Moroni 10:19, 24), only by our unbelief.
- **Moroni 10:22 — "despair cometh because of iniquity."** Despair isn't a mood; at root it traces to sin, because sin severs the connection to the atonement that hope rests on. The way out is repentance, not optimism. Same medicine Mormon handed his son one chapter earlier ("may Christ lift thee up").
- **The book witnesses itself.** It opens with a family leaving Jerusalem and closes by telling the reader exactly how to become holy, then turning to look out of the page: *come unto Christ.* I came to walk it; it ended by inviting me.
