## Key Reflections

### The Calling (Q2 — Covenant, Q8 — Atonement)

The biggest failure of the week wasn't technical. Sunday School president calling was neglected. A bishopric assignment to counsel with councilors went unfulfilled and was reported at ward conference. The irony is sharp: Michael delegates effectively to AI agents — building multi-agent frameworks, governance hooks, agent specs — but struggles to delegate to human councilors. The skills being built for AI have direct parallels to presidency leadership, but they haven't been translated into that domain yet.

Three studies seeded: "The Art of Presidency," "The Art of Delegation," "Zion in a Presidency."

### Working While Sick (Q9 — Rest)

Head cold with dizziness all week. Pushed through instead of resting. Mosiah 4:27 territory. The output happened but the cost was too high. The collaboration with AI does make the heavy lifting easier — but that's not license to skip rest.

### No Monitoring (Q7 — Review)

"The Gods watched until they obeyed" (Abraham 4:18) — and we didn't. The old server filled to 100% with no alerts. Five machines running with zero automated health reporting. Professional monitoring practices not carried forward to personal infrastructure. Dashboard and monitoring is seeded as a squad task for the multi-agent work.

### The Tool and the Study (Q11 — Zion)

What started as a scripture-study repo has morphed into a multi-project workspace. The original unstated goal was to study the gospel to learn how to use AI better — AI usage as a type and shadow for how God organizes intelligences. The tools work. The Only Begotten study proved it. But the tool is also driving the agenda. Michael's assessment: the 11-step process is the correction, and it's playing out in real time. D&C 88:119 — "Organize yourselves; prepare every needful thing" — surfaced as a framework for the next cycle. The organizing comes before the house gets named.

### The Micro-Correction Burden (Q10 — Consecration)

Even though AI writes 99.9% of the code, the review-test-correct cycle consumes significant time and attention. Every phase of work has micro-corrections after. The multi-agent framework is the hope for relieving this, but it's unproven. This is the biggest open question for the next cycle.

**It was good.** The house is being built. The learning is real. The practice is producing fruit. The correction needed is not in the direction — it's in the pace.
