# Teacher Council Framework — Teaching the Savior's Way

*Built from Tad R. Callister's "Teaching the Savior's Way", [Jan E. Newman's April 2021 General Conference talk of the same title](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2021/04/14newman?lang=eng), the [Teaching in the Savior's Way (2022)](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/teaching-in-the-saviors-way-2022/01-introduction/03a-overview-of-christlike-teaching?lang=eng) manual structure, and the [Hope](../../study/hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs.md) and [Tree of Life](../../study/tree-of-life-and-the-chain.md) studies.*

This document is your map for the Sunday teacher council and for any time you teach *Teaching in the Savior's Way* itself. It folds Callister's video into the manual's two-part architecture so you have a framework, not just a list of points.

## The Architecture

TSW (2022) has two layers, and the order matters:

**Part 1 — Focus on Jesus Christ** (the *target*)
1. Teach about Jesus Christ no matter what you are teaching
2. Help learners come unto Jesus Christ

**Part 2 — Principles of Christlike Teaching** (the *means*)
3. Love those you teach
4. Teach by the Spirit
5. Teach the doctrine
6. Invite diligent learning

Part 1 says *what teaching is for*. Part 2 says *how a teacher does it*. If you skip Part 1, Part 2 collapses into method — techniques without a target. If you skip Part 2, Part 1 becomes pious aspiration without practice. Both layers, in that order.

Callister's video is roughly 20% Part 1 and 80% Part 2. He spends the opening five minutes anchoring everything in Part 1 ("the first question is how can I live more like the Savior") and then the remaining 33 minutes giving four practices that are direct expressions of Part 2.

## The Foundational Question (Part 1)

> "The first question we all should probably ask is not what should I teach or how should I teach but the first question really should be how can I live more like the Savior so that when I teach I teach with power and authority from God." — Callister, [4:30](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GybaxBERWMk&t=270)

This is TSW Part 1 in one sentence. The reason it matters is what Callister adds next: "There was no gap between his message and his life. They were perfect in accord, and thus his words did have power and authority when he spoke."

The teacher's authority comes from the gap being closed. Not from credentials, not from preparation alone, not from skill at running a discussion. From living the thing you teach.

This connects directly to the hope study. [Moroni 7:48](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/7?lang=eng&id=p48#p48) and [1 John 3:2-3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/1-jn/3?lang=eng&id=p2-p3#p2) and *Lectures on Faith* 7:16 each give the same doctrine: when He shall appear we shall see Him as He is, and every man that hath this hope in him purifieth himself, even as He is pure. *The seeing-ahead is what shapes the soul.* Callister stated the principle ("become like Him") without pulling the verse explicitly. But Jan E. Newman, one year earlier, *closed his entire talk on the same subject* with that exact verse, footnoted as Moroni 7:48 ([14newman.md fn 11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2021/04/14newman?lang=eng)). Newman, then Second Counselor in the Sunday School General Presidency, gave the talk in April 2021 General Conference. Callister, former Sunday School General President (2014–2018), gave his video in April 2022, as the updated TSW manual rolled out. Two Sunday School general leaders, one verse anchor, one year apart. That is the witness law operating across two Sunday-School-presidency voices on the same doctrine.

## A Second Witness — Newman, April 2021

Elder Jan E. Newman, then Second Counselor in the Sunday School General Presidency, gave the talk "Teaching in the Savior's Way" in the April 2021 General Conference. The 2022 TSW manual update followed; Callister's video came in April 2022, essentially alongside the manual's release. Read together, the two talks form a doublet that establishes the Part 1 / Part 2 architecture from above (Newman, in conference) and from in-the-room practice (Callister, demonstrating it).

Newman's Part 1 anchor uses different words for the same instruction Callister gives:

> "First and foremost, take it upon yourself to learn all you can about the Master Teacher Himself. How did He show love for others? What did they feel when He taught? What did He teach? What were His expectations of those He taught? After you explore questions like these, evaluate and adjust your way of teaching to be more like His." — [Newman, April 2021](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2021/04/14newman?lang=eng)

Same target. Same order of operations. *Become like Him first; teach from there.* Two witnesses to the same Part 1 claim.

Newman adds three things Callister does not:

1. **Apostolic-level authorization for TSW itself.** Newman explicitly invites the church to read TSW: "I invite you to read and study its every word. Its principles will assist you in your efforts to be more Christlike in your teaching." That sentence, given in conference, is the prophetic warrant for treating TSW as the operating manual rather than an optional supplement.
2. **The St. Andrews analogy** — strengthens commitment #4 below.
3. **The council itself** — "I invite leaders and teachers in every organization of the Church to counsel together with parents and youth in order to dramatically improve teaching at every level — in stakes, in wards, and in homes." Your teacher council exists *because Newman invited it.* The framework is not an extra structure you laid on top of the calling; it is the structure the talk asked for.

The chain works for the teacher and for the class together:
- **Faith** trusts the Savior is real and the becoming is possible
- **Hope** fixes the eye on Him as the prototype
- **Charity** is what the teacher (and the learner) becomes after the eye has been fixed long enough

The teacher's job is to keep the eye fixed — their own and the class's. Every other principle in TSW is downstream of that.

## The Four Practical Commitments (Part 2 in operation)

Callister gives four. Each one corresponds to a TSW Part 2 chapter:

### 1. Live more like the Savior → "Love those you teach"

The lived example is what makes the love credible. The class can tell whether you are practicing what you teach. Callister's ["no gap between his message and his life"](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/teaching-in-the-saviors-way-2022/04-part-1/05-teach-about-jesus-christ?lang=eng) is the substrate of [TSW chapter 8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/teaching-in-the-saviors-way-2022/07-part-2/08-love-those-you-teach?lang=eng): "see learners the way God sees them; seek to know them; pray for them by name."

You cannot love a class you don't know, and you cannot teach attribute-acquisition you haven't begun yourself. The love and the lived example are the same practice from two angles.

**TSW Chapter 8 closes with [Moroni 7:48](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/7?lang=eng&id=p48#p48) directly:** "Pray unto the Father with all the energy of heart, that ye may be filled with [the pure love of Christ]." The bridge to your hope study is built into the manual.

### 2. Ask for revelation in your calling → "Teach by the Spirit"

Callister's anchor: ["Inherent in every single calling in this church is the right to receive revelation. The Lord will never give us a responsibility… without giving us the right to receive his revelation."](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GybaxBERWMk&t=390)

[TSW chapter 9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/teaching-in-the-saviors-way-2022/07-part-2/09-teach-by-the-spirit?lang=eng) opens: "*The Holy Ghost is the true teacher.* No mortal teacher, no matter how skilled or experienced, can replace His role… But all teachers can be instruments."

The teacher is an instrument. The Spirit is the teacher. The implication: your job is to make yourself askable — to live in a way that the Spirit can give you the next line. Joseph Smith, Callister says, was "the master receiver of revelation because he was the master asker."

### 3. Read the scripture block first → "Teach the doctrine"

> "When you read the scripture block to me that's where you're getting the true doctrine and principles, and then when you read the lesson you're getting the inspiration on how to teach." — sister in Callister's audience, [14:55](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GybaxBERWMk&t=895)

[TSW chapter 10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/teaching-in-the-saviors-way-2022/07-part-2/10-teach-the-doctrine?lang=eng): "Learn the doctrine of Jesus Christ for yourself." [D&C 18:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/18?lang=eng&id=p3#p3): "Rely upon the things which are written."

The doctrine itself has converting power ([Alma 31:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/31?lang=eng&id=p5#p5): "the word… had a more powerful effect upon the minds of the people than the sword"). The lesson manual is a teaching aid; the scripture block is the source. Going to the manual first prejudices what you'll find in the scripture. Going to the scripture first lets the manual fill in the *how* after you already know the *what*.

### 4. Record spiritual impressions → "Invite diligent learning"

This one is both for the teacher and for the class. Callister names it as a "cultural change" the Church is trying to make.

[TSW chapter 11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/teaching-in-the-saviors-way-2022/07-part-2/11-invite-diligent-learning?lang=eng), in the "Some Ways to Apply" section, explicitly lists: "Marking meaningful passages… Inviting revelation through pondering and prayer… Writing down spiritual impressions… Keeping a study journal… Setting goals to act on what they learn."

Callister stacks four supports for this:
- The teenage-son thought experiment: imagine your 14-year-old says "Mom, hold on, that's good counsel, I want to write it down." How would you feel about giving him more counsel?
- Elder Scott: "Knowledge carefully recorded is knowledge available in time of need… this practice enhances the likelihood of your receiving further light."
- Joseph Smith: "If you proceed to discuss important questions and feel to write them down. Perhaps for neglecting to write those things when God had revealed them, not esteeming them of sufficient worth, the Spirit may withdraw."
- [D&C 6:22-23](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/6?lang=eng&id=p22-p23#p22): "Cast your mind upon the night that I cried unto your heart… did I not speak peace unto your mind?"

The pivot verse is [Helaman 11:23](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/hel/11?lang=eng&id=p23#p23): "they did receive many revelations daily." Callister: "How can anybody receive many revelations daily? But I believe now that if we do record spiritual impressions, however small or faint they may be, we can receive multiple spiritual impressions in our life to be a constant guide to us."

The act of recording is what trains the soul to receive more. Not because God is grading effort, but because the recording is the *acknowledgment* that what came was worth keeping.

**Newman gives the same principle through a different image.** He tells of playing the Old Course at St. Andrews with his son and trying afterward to convey the experience: "Photos, videos, and my best descriptions were totally inadequate. I finally realized the only way for someone to know the grandeur of St. Andrews is to experience it." Therefore, he says:

> "So it is with the word of God. We can teach it, we can preach it, we can explain it. We can talk about it, we can describe it, we can even testify of it. But until a person feels the sacred word of God distill upon his or her soul like the dews from heaven through the power of the Spirit, it will be like looking at a postcard or someone else's vacation photos. You have to go there yourself." — [Newman, April 2021](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2021/04/14newman?lang=eng)

This sets the teacher's task precisely. The teacher cannot give the class the experience; the teacher creates conditions for the firsthand experience and then hands them the practice (recording) that captures it. Callister's Helaman 11:23 ("they did receive many revelations daily") and Newman's St. Andrews are the same point from two angles — revelation accumulates *in the recipient*, drop by drop, and the recording is what trains the soul to notice the next drop. Newman pairs the experience analogy with the parable of the Ten Virgins: "this precious oil is acquired one drop at a time, patiently and persistently. No shortcut is available." Drops of oil. Drops of revelation. Same accumulation, same source, same need to be present for each drop as it arrives.

## Folded Together

| TSW Part 2 chapter | Callister's commitment | One-line operating question |
|---|---|---|
| Love those you teach | Live more like the Savior; pray for them by name | *Do they know I love them and that I'm working on the same becoming I'm asking of them?* |
| Teach by the Spirit | Ask for revelation that is yours by right of calling | *Did I ask the Lord what these specific people need this week?* |
| Teach the doctrine | Read the scripture block before the manual | *Did I go to the source first and let the manual fill in the how?* |
| Invite diligent learning | Record impressions; prepare a week (or more) ahead | *Am I writing down what I receive — and inviting them to do the same?* |

All four sit underneath Part 1's foundational question: *am I becoming more like the Savior so that when I teach, the gap between my life and my message is shrinking?*

## For Sunday's Teacher Council

You said last Sunday's class on TSW didn't land well. A few specific suggestions for the council:

**1. Open with one question and let the room answer.**
Callister's whole video is built on "I'd like to ask you a question." He hands the microphone around and lets attendees answer in their own voices. He confirms, adds the punctuation, and moves on. The doctrine arrives *through* the room, not over the top of it. This is the simplest practice you can lift directly.

A good opening: *"What's one practice you've added to your teaching that helped you live more like the Savior — not just teach more like Him?"* That question makes the foundational claim before anyone has to argue for it.

**2. Pick one of the four commitments — don't try to teach all four.**
The video is 38 minutes. A teacher council segment is shorter. Going wide turns it into a recap. Going deep lets the room actually practice the thing in front of each other.

If your gut tells you which one your teachers most need, follow it. If you don't have a gut answer yet, *recording spiritual impressions* is the highest-leverage one — it's the practice that compounds, and it's the one most teachers haven't built yet. Helaman 11:23 + the Joseph Smith warning quote are unforgettable hooks for it.

**3. Bring the verse — and name the second witness.**
The verse is [Moroni 7:48](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/7?lang=eng&id=p48#p48) / [1 John 3:2-3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/1-jn/3?lang=eng&id=p2-p3#p2): "When he shall appear we shall be like him; for we shall see him as he is. And every man that hath this hope in him purifieth himself, even as he is pure."

Callister stated the principle without quoting the verse. Newman closed his entire General Conference talk with it. Bringing both into the room is doing what scripture does — establishing the word in the mouth of two witnesses. The seeing-ahead is what shapes the soul. The teachers' work is, at root, helping the class fix its eye on the Savior so the class gets shaped by what it's looking at, and the same is true for the teachers themselves.

**4. Demonstrate, don't just describe.**
The most effective thing in Callister's video is not what he says — it's what he does. He prepares spiritually before he teaches. He asks. He waits. He hands the microphone to the room. He confirms what the Spirit gave through the attendee. He moves on without dominating.

Your council format already invites this. Sunday, slow down. Ask. Wait three full seconds longer than feels comfortable. Let two or three teachers speak before you confirm. The teachers will absorb the *form* of teaching by the Spirit while you're discussing teaching by the Spirit.

## The One-Line Frame for Yourself

If you can carry one sentence into Sunday:

*The teacher's first question is not "what should I teach?" but "how can I become more like the Savior so what I teach has His authority behind it?" Every TSW principle is downstream of that question. Every Callister commitment is its operational form.*

That sentence holds Part 1, Part 2, and the four practices together in one breath.
