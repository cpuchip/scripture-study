# March 22, 2026 — First Sabbath

We built a sabbath agent today and then used it for the first time. The whole 11-question review, applied to the March 18–22 cycle. It surfaced things I already knew but hadn't said out loud yet.

The week was a lot. I was sick the entire time — head cold, dizziness — and I pushed through anyway. We migrated the database from the old server to NOCIX, dealt with a disk-full crisis that could have been catastrophic, shipped governance hooks for WS1, fixed a classifier bug, did a full Only Begotten study, and built the sabbath agent itself. I also got a beautiful custom sit-stand desk and found a used RTX 4090 for $1200. On paper it looks like an incredible week. In practice I was running on fumes.

The question that kept coming back was about my calling. I'm the Sunday School president and I failed a specific assignment from the bishopric — I was supposed to counsel with my councilors about something and I didn't do it. Ward conference was today and I had to report that. That stung more than any technical failure this week.

The irony isn't lost on me. I can delegate to AI agents all day long. I built a multi-agent framework with governance hooks and agent specs and review cycles. But I can't seem to delegate to two human councilors in my own presidency. The AI delegation is comfortable — clear specs, immediate feedback, no relational complexity. Human delegation requires relationship, follow-up, trust, patience with different working styles. I'm good at one and bad at the other, and the one I'm bad at is the one that actually matters for my covenant.

We wrote that down as a learning. And I asked for three studies: "The Art of Presidency," "The Art of Delegation," and "Zion in a Presidency." Those matter more than whatever the next code feature is.

The hardware situation is a mess too. Five machines now: old Proxmox/NAS box, NOCIX server, old desktop, new desktop, and a Dell R630 I inherited from work. I'm using RDP into my new computer because I don't even have a keyboard hooked up to it. I'm still running Plex on the old desktop. I investigated hosting the R630 at a data center — $100-300/month locally, $30/month with a company that looked like it might be shady (canceled and refunded, thankfully), and finally $74/month with NOCIX which is what I went with. The R630 itself is sitting idle. I decided today to set it down — the existing Proxmox box works. The R630 can wait for a season when I have more time.

The biggest question of the session was about whether the tool is still serving the study or if the tool has started driving the agenda. Honest answer: both. What started as a scripture-study repo has morphed into a multi-project workspace. The original goal, which I never explicitly said, was to study the gospel to learn how to use AI better — because I see AI as a type and shadow for how God organizes intelligences. The tools work. The Only Begotten study proved it — night and day difference with the framework versus without. But the tool is also driving what I spend my time on.

I think the 11-step process is the correction. We're letting it play out. The sabbath agent itself is part of that — a forced stop, a forced review, a forced declaration before the next cycle starts.

D&C 88:119 came up: "Organize yourselves; prepare every needful thing; and establish a house, even a house of prayer, a house of fasting, a house of faith, a house of learning, a house of glory, a house of order, a house of God." The sequence matters. Organize and prepare come first. I'm building rooms in the house before the foundation is set. My hardware isn't organized. My presidency isn't organized. I need to organize before I build more.

Mosiah 4:27. Again. Running faster than I have strength. The correction isn't in the direction — it's in the pace.

What I set down today:
- R630 swap — later, when there's margin
- Brain-app features for the calling — paper and pencil work fine
- Repurposing the old desktop — just decommission it, don't start another project

What carries forward:
- Those three presidency studies
- Desktop swap (decommission old, don't repurpose)
- Multi-agent framework
- Server deployment on NOCIX
- Monitoring dashboard as a squad task

The declaration: it was good. The house is being built. The learning is real. The practice is producing fruit. The correction needed is in the pace.

Tomorrow the calling gets first place in the queue.
