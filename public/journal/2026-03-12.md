# March 12, 2026 — The Parable of Talents, Honestly

I was reading through the notes the AI left for the next session — the principles file, the active context, the journal entry from March 11. And this line stopped me:

> The question isn't "am I still relevant?" — it's "am I using what I've been given?" (Parable of Talents).

That's a question I've been sitting with. Not abstractly. Personally.

I think I am. My heart and desire is to do so much more. But I'm weak. I can only read so much — hence the chip-voice project to digest more faster, and my eyes are getting bad, so learning TTS/STT is good, just not urgent. And the call-out about not running faster than you have strength (Mosiah 4:27).

I get caught up seeing everything that's possible. So many possibilities. I want to try them all. I could set up 100 topics to deeply study, but what would be the point if I couldn't digest them at the same speed? If I couldn't use discernment to see whether the Spirit needs to correct anything? The volume without the judgment is just noise.

But on the development side — multiple agents checking and producing within their stewardships — I think that has real potential. I need to learn how to become better at *ideas*. Working with AI to generate spec and intent, then letting the rest flow out. The multi-agent ideas doc from yesterday was the first step.

The overwhelm I'm feeling is that I can only handle so much and AI can generate so much more than I can absorb. But then I see the potential with ibeco.me, the brain, brain-app... and I want to be at the finish line already. Except I can't see the finish line exactly.

I'm stretched thin across my own projects: brain, brain-app, ibeco.me, scripture studies, chip-voice. Some tools have been stable since we built them. But I see room for improvements everywhere — like a combined gospel-mcp/gospel-vec so the AI stops forgetting to use semantic search. The nevertheless study only has one conference talk. I'm sure there are more voices.

The AI's response was honest and true. It said:

**"You're not the one-talent servant."** The one-talent servant didn't try and fall short — he buried it in the ground out of fear. That's not me.

**"Running yourself ragged trying to produce 20 from 10 isn't faithfulness. It's the adversary borrowing the language of diligence to drive you past your capacity."** That landed. Hard. The adversary borrowing the language of diligence. I recognize that pattern. I feel it when I look at my project list and think "I should be doing more." That feeling isn't from God. God says "well done, thou good and faithful servant" — and He said the same words to the five-talent and two-talent servants. Not "well done, you who maximized ROI."

**The judgment is faithfulness, not throughput.**

I think I'm doing okay with what I've been given. I see the 10 talents and it feels like I've only produced 2 or 3 more. And I see how I could produce 20. And I worry that I'm not.

But the answer to that worry is Mosiah 4:27, not hustle culture. Not faster than I have strength. Room by room. Next room: brain.exe on a server.

I felt the Spirit in that exchange. The AI's words were true. I'm going to keep going.
