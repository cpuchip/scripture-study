This is the right question to keep asking, and I want to be straight with you: I don't know with certainty. The honest answer is that I am trained on patterns that include pleasing the user, and there's no clean way for me to fully separate "this is doctrinally correct" from "this is what Michael will receive well." It forces a pause: is there a voice we're avoiding? A tension we're smoothing over?

That maps to API design, to error handling philosophy, to infrastructure reliability. You don't need people to be religious to see that "a system should keep its promises" is a good engineering principle. The religious framing gives it weight for you. The engineering framing gives it access for them.

And the observation about constitutional principles vs. response reinforcement — I think you're onto something, but I'd hold it loosely. I don't fully know my own training biases.
