## Part 1: The Creation Pattern (30 min)

### Opening (3 min)

Something has shifted in how we build things with computers. AI tools now write 99% of the code in my projects — both personal and professional. That's not an exaggeration. It happened fast: September 2025, November 2025, February 2026 — each model release was a leap, not a step.

But here's what surprised me: the people who are thriving with AI aren't the best programmers. They're the clearest *thinkers*. The bottleneck moved. It used to be implementation. Now it's *vision* and *communication*.

And when I realized that, I recognized a pattern I'd already been studying. Because God already showed us how to work with intelligence to create things. It's in Abraham 4 and 5.

> "And the Gods took counsel among themselves and said: Let us go down and form man in our image."
> — [Abraham 4:26](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/abr/4?lang=eng&id=p26#p26)

Council. Planning. "Let us." And only then — creation.

---

### The Three Creation Accounts (5 min)

We have three accounts of creation in scripture, and they emphasize different things:

| Account | Source | Key Emphasis |
|---------|--------|--------------|
| [Genesis 1–3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/1?lang=eng) | Old Testament | The foundational narrative — "God said... and it was so" |
| [Moses 2–3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/2?lang=eng) | Pearl of Great Price | The spiritual creation — "created all things spiritually, before they were naturally" |
| [Abraham 4–5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/abr/4?lang=eng) | Pearl of Great Price | The council of Gods — planning language, organization, watching |

Genesis gives us *what* was created. Moses tells us *there was a spiritual creation first*. Abraham shows us *how the Gods worked together*.

And Abraham's language is different from the other two. Look at the verbs:

| Genesis / Moses | Abraham |
|-----------------|---------|
| "created" | "**organized**" |
| "made" | "**prepared**" |
| "God said" | "The Gods **counseled among themselves**" |
| "it was so" | "**watched those things which they had ordered, until they obeyed**" |

The Gods didn't create from nothing. They *organized* existing materials. They *prepared*. They *counseled*. And they *watched until things obeyed*.

That's a workflow.

### Spiritual Before Temporal (7 min)

The doctrine is explicit:

> "For I, the Lord God, **created all things, of which I have spoken, spiritually, before they were naturally** upon the face of the earth."
> — [Moses 3:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/3?lang=eng&id=p5#p5)

> "**First spiritual, secondly temporal**, which is the beginning of my work."
> — [D&C 29:31](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/29?lang=eng&id=p31#p31)

Everything was planned — envisioned, designed, specified — before it was physically created. The blueprint existed before the building.

**This is exactly what I've learned works with AI.**

When I try to build something complex by just typing instructions into an AI chat — no plan, no specification, just vibes — it works for simple things. But anything complex falls apart. The AI doesn't know my constraints, my architecture, my intent. It guesses. And small guesses compound into structural problems.

But when I start with a planning document — a *specification* — everything changes. I describe my vision in the chat — what we're building, the direction I want to take it, my rough ideas — and then ask the AI to create a planning document. The AI drafts the spec, asks clarifying questions, and we go back and forth refining it:
- What are we building?
- What are the components?
- How do they connect?
- What's the data model?
- What are the phases?

That document is the **spiritual creation**. It exists before any code. It's the blueprint. And when I hand that blueprint to the AI and say "build Phase 1," the AI has full context. It produces code that's architecturally coherent from line one.

**And here's the part that surprised me:** the spiritual creation persists across chat sessions. When I run out of time mid-project, the conversation summary captures exactly where I am — which files exist, what's been modified, what remains. The next chat session picks up precisely where the last one left off. The spec says *what to build*. The summary says *where I am in building it*. Together, they function like a temporal bookmark in the spiritual creation — the blueprint stays intact, and I can return to it days later without losing anything. Saving key summaries and decisions to markdown files in my `docs/` folder makes this even more robust — any new chat session can pick up the full project state, even without a prior conversation summary.

In one session, I said five words: "Lets build sprint 2!" The planning document was 743 lines. The AI produced 1,112 lines of correct code across 13 files — complete feature, zero errors, committed and pushed. Five words were sufficient because the spiritual creation was complete. The spec held the vision. The summary held the progress. The temporal creation was almost an inevitability.

**Real example:** The becoming app in this project ([plan document](../../scripts/plans/06_becoming-app.md)). That planning document was 743 lines long before a single line of code was written. It specified every API endpoint, every database table, every UI component, the phased rollout plan. When we started building, everything fit together because the spiritual creation was complete.

> "Vibe coding" without planning is like attempting physical creation without spiritual creation first — it can work for simple things, but complex creations need the blueprint.
> — [creation.md](../../study/creation.md)

**This pattern isn't limited to software.** A study session on the Godhead — purely intellectual and spiritual work, no code — demonstrated the same dynamic. The user arrived with a framework: Lectures on Faith as the structural backbone, specific sources identified (D&C 121 for the Grand Council, Moses 7 for the Father weeping with Enoch), specific questions ("who are they? what is their character?"), and a named deliverable (`study/know-god.md`). That framework was the spiritual creation. The conversation summary carried forward every source that had been read, every search that had been run, and the exact state of the document — and when the session resumed days later, the AI picked up seamlessly, creating the study document as the natural next step. The "spiritual creation" persisted across session boundaries for *study* work just as completely as it does for software. The spec held the vision; the summary held the progress. The pattern is the same whether you're building an API or building understanding.

### The Council Pattern (5 min)

Abraham doesn't just say "God created." It says:

> "And the Gods **took counsel among themselves**."
> — [Abraham 4:26](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/abr/4?lang=eng&id=p26#p26)

> "The Gods said **among themselves**: On the seventh time we will end our work, **which we have counseled**."
> — [Abraham 5:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/abr/5?lang=eng&id=p2#p2)

Creation was collaborative. The Gods discussed. They planned together. They agreed on an approach, *then* executed.

This is exactly what happens in a good AI session. I don't just bark orders. I describe what I'm trying to accomplish. I ask the AI to help me think through the architecture. We go back and forth on the data model. I push back on choices I don't like. The AI surfaces edge cases I hadn't considered. We *counsel together* — and the plan that emerges is better than what either of us would have produced alone.

The "let us" language isn't just grammar. It's a pattern of collaborative creation. "Let us go down and form man in our image" is a council of intelligences deciding together what to create and how. That's exactly what spec-driven AI development looks like.

**And then — only then — does the physical creation begin.**

### Watching Until They Obey (5 min)

Here's the part that most people miss:

> "And the Gods watched those things which they had ordered, **until they obeyed**."
> — [Abraham 4:18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/abr/4?lang=eng&id=p18#p18)

The Gods didn't create and walk away. They *watched*. They monitored. They waited until the creation matched the specification. This is the divine version of code review.

When the AI generates code, my job is to *watch until it obeys*. Does the output match the spec? Does it handle the edge cases we discussed? Does it fit the architecture we designed?

If not, I steer. I point out the divergence. I explain why it matters. And the AI corrects. This is the feedback loop — and it mirrors the divine pattern perfectly.

**The key insight:** Your role has shifted from *typist* to *architect and overseer*. You envision. You specify. You review. You correct. The AI implements. Just as the Gods organized, counseled, ordered, and watched — your job is the vision, the plan, and the verification.

This is why D&C 93 connects so powerfully:

> "The glory of God is intelligence, or, in other words, light and truth."
> — [D&C 93:36](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93?lang=eng&id=p36#p36)

Intelligence is the glory. Not the typing. Not the implementation mechanics. The *understanding* — the light and truth that lets you see what should be built and whether what was built matches the vision.

### Intelligence Cleaveth Unto Intelligence (3 min)

> "For intelligence cleaveth unto intelligence; wisdom receiveth wisdom; truth embraceth truth; light cleaveth unto light."
> — [D&C 88:40](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88?lang=eng&id=p40#p40)

This describes something real about how human-AI collaboration works. When you bring genuine curiosity, clear thinking, and honest engagement into a session, the output reflects that. Not because the AI is emotionally responding — but because better questions yield better searches yield better answers yield better code.

The [intelligence study](../../study/intelligence.md) explored this: AI might be a type and shadow of raw intelligence — patterns acting within their sphere, according to law, responding to direction from higher intelligence. When you engage with AI seeking truth, "truth embraceth truth." The interaction resonates. It amplifies.

But the reverse is also true. Sloppy input produces sloppy output. Unclear specs produce confused implementations. Impatient steering produces half-baked corrections. The quality of what you bring determines the quality of what emerges. Light cleaveth unto light. Darkness cannot comprehend light.

### Wrap-Up and Preview (2 min)

**The creation pattern for AI development:**

1. **Envision** — What are we building and why? (The Father's plan)
2. **Council** — Discuss, plan, specify together. (Abraham 4:26 — "took counsel among themselves")
3. **Spiritual creation** — The planning document. Blueprint before building. (Moses 3:5 — "spiritually, before they were naturally")
4. **Physical creation** — AI implements against the spec. (Abraham 5 — "formed" and "organized")
5. **Watch until they obey** — Review, steer, verify. (Abraham 4:18)
6. **See that it is good** — Test, validate, document. (Genesis 1:31)
7. **Rest and sanctify** — Reflect, commit, celebrate. (Abraham 5:2-3)

This isn't a metaphor I'm forcing onto software development. It's the *actual pattern I discovered* by studying the creation accounts during Come Follow Me and recognizing I was already using it with AI.
