# The Two Triplets: One Ascent, Two Descriptions

**Binding Question:** How do *Faith, Hope, and Charity* and *The Way, The Truth, and The Life* relate? Are they the same point seen from different angles? Different concepts? Is one human-centered and the other Christ-centered?

---

## Both chains move, and the order matters

Moroni states the first chain like a chemist names a reaction:

> "Wherefore, there must be faith; and if there must be faith there must also be hope; and if there must be hope there must also be charity." ([Moroni 10:20](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/10?lang=eng&id=p20#p20))

Pull a link out and the chain collapses. Two verses later: "if ye have no hope ye must needs be in despair; and despair cometh because of iniquity" ([Moroni 10:22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/10?lang=eng&id=p22#p22)). Faith does not reach charity if hope is missing.

Paul writes the same chain in Romans 5: "tribulation worketh patience; and patience, experience; and experience, hope: and hope maketh not ashamed; because the love of God is shed abroad in our hearts by the Holy Ghost which is given unto us" ([Romans 5:3-5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/rom/5?lang=eng&id=p3-p5#p3)). Same sequence, different starting verb. Tribulation drives the engine. Love is what comes out.

The second chain comes out of the upper room. Thomas had asked, "How can we know the way?" Jesus answered with himself: "I am the way, the truth, and the life: no man cometh unto the Father, but by me" ([John 14:6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/14?lang=eng&id=p6#p6)). The order does the same work it did in Moroni: enter the way, grow in truth, receive life. The [way-truth-life](way-truth-life.md) study reads this as a grace-for-grace progression: "...he received not of the fulness at the first, but received grace for grace... continued from grace to grace, until he received a fulness" ([D&C 93:12-13](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93?lang=eng&id=p12-p13#p12)).

Both chains are sequenced, cumulative, and self-correcting if any link is removed. What they progress toward is where the question sharpens.

---

## Both chains converge at the veil

The two triplets do not run on parallel tracks. They meet at one point: the veil.

Hebrews names the veil as Christ's flesh: "Having therefore, brethren, boldness to enter into the holiest by the blood of Jesus, By a new and living way, which he hath consecrated for us, through the veil, that is to say, his flesh" ([Hebrews 10:19-20](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/heb/10?lang=eng&id=p19-p20#p19)). The author equates Christ with the veil and "the way" with passing through. [way-truth-life](way-truth-life.md) anchors its temple reading here: "Christ IS the veil. Passing through requires communing with Him, being found worthy through His merits, and entering into His presence."

Hope anchors at the same place: "Which hope we have as an anchor of the soul, both sure and steadfast, and which entereth into that within the veil" ([Hebrews 6:19](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/heb/6?lang=eng&id=p19#p19)). [hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs](hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs.md) presses the geometry: "Paul's hope-anchor enters within the veil — into the Holy of Holies, into the unseen presence of God."

Then the eye. "...if your eye be single to my glory, your whole bodies shall be filled with light, and there shall be no darkness in you; and that body which is filled with light comprehendeth all things. Therefore, sanctify yourselves that your minds become single to God" ([D&C 88:67-68](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88?lang=eng&id=p67-p68#p67)). [discernment-and-the-comprehending-eye](discernment-and-the-comprehending-eye.md) reads this as the gate through which both triplets operate. The eye fills the body. The body comprehends what fills it.

Faith, hope, and charity tune the eye. The way, the truth, and the life are what the tuned eye sees. Same temple, same passage, same Person at the end.

---

## Both triplets end in Christ

If one chain were "us-centric" and the other "Christ-centered," they would terminate in different places. They do not.

Ether 12:28 is direct: "...faith, hope and charity bringeth unto me—the fountain of all righteousness" ([Ether 12:28](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/ether/12?lang=eng&id=p28#p28)). [enoch-charity](enoch-charity.md) lands the same point: "The triad doesn't just make us better people. It brings us *to Christ*. And Christ is the fountain from which Zion flows."

Charity itself is finally his, not ours. Moroni names it: "charity is the pure love of Christ" ([Moroni 7:47](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/7?lang=eng&id=p47#p47)). The next verse names the means: pray "with all the energy of heart, that ye may be filled with this love, **which he hath bestowed**" ([Moroni 7:48](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/7?lang=eng&id=p48#p48)). The love is bestowed, not manufactured. Praying is the asking; the love itself is a gift. Even the most interior virtue of the first triplet — the one most likely to read as a human achievement — is finally Christ's love bestowed on the true follower.

The second triplet is even more direct. [way-truth-life](way-truth-life.md): "Christ does not merely show the way, teach truth, or give life — He IS all three." After John 14:6, Philip asks to see the Father, and Jesus answers, "...he that hath seen me hath seen the Father" ([John 14:9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/14?lang=eng&id=p9#p9)). The way to the Father is a Person. The truth about the Father is a Person. The life of the Father is a Person. "I am the resurrection, and the life" ([John 11:25](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/11?lang=eng&id=p25#p25)).

The "us-centric versus Christ-centered" framing does not hold. Both end in Christ. The first names the soul's progressive capacity to receive him. The second names him as the reality received.

---

## The first prepares the traveler; the second is the road

The two triplets are different concepts, but they are not unrelated. The first names what happens to the traveler. The second names what the traveler walks into.

[discernment-and-the-comprehending-eye](discernment-and-the-comprehending-eye.md) treats faith-hope-charity as the progressive purification of the eye. Faith moves; hope aims; charity resolves. This is the seer's side. By contrast, [truth](truth.md) names the seen: "...truth is knowledge of things as they are, and as they were, and as they are to come" ([D&C 93:24](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93?lang=eng&id=p24#p24)). And the comprehension principle, "...light cleaveth unto light" ([D&C 88:40](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88?lang=eng&id=p40#p40)) — the seer must become like the reality seen, or the connection cannot happen.

Lectures on Faith 7 names what Moroni 7:48 enacts: God "proposed to make them like unto himself... And for any portion of the human family to be assimilated into their likeness is to be saved; and to be unlike them is to be destroyed: and on this hinge turns the door of salvation." Charity enables likeness; likeness enables sight; sight sustains hope; hope completes purification. Then the One who is the way, the truth, and the life can be received as he is.

D&C 4:5 merges both chains in one line: "...faith, hope, charity and love, with an eye single to the glory of God, qualify him for the work" ([D&C 4:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/4?lang=eng&id=p5#p5)). The interior triplet, exercised with a single eye, aimed at the glory of God — which glory is "intelligence, or, in other words, light and truth" ([D&C 93:36](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93?lang=eng&id=p36#p36)) — qualifies a soul for the work of salvation.

2 Nephi 31:20 compresses the journey into one verse: "...press forward with a steadfastness in Christ, having a perfect brightness of hope, and a love of God and of all men" ([2 Nephi 31:20](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/31?lang=eng&id=p20#p20)). Steadfastness in Christ is the way. Brightness of hope is the eye fixed forward. Love of God and all men is charity. Pressing forward is faith in motion. The verse names neither triplet by its label and enacts both at once.

An honest caveat from [tree-of-life-and-the-chain](tree-of-life-and-the-chain.md): "No prophet writes 'the seed is faith, the tree is hope, the fruit is charity.' The mapping is built by reading the two systems against each other." The same is true here. Faith does not equal way; hope does not equal truth; charity does not equal life. What the text enforces is the shared progression, the shared veil, and the shared Person at the end. The point-by-point correspondence is a reading, not a doctrine — useful, suggestive, and not load-bearing.

---

## Becoming

The practical question is where the eye rests. [hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs](hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs.md) warns that "despair is the eye-state where there is no forward orientation." If hope dims, the eye loses direction. If the eye loses direction, charity has nothing to focus on. Keeping hope is the fight to keep the eye single.

The temple acts this out. The veil is not a metaphor for something else; it is the physical enactment. A traveler comes with faith that the covenants are real, hope fixed on what lies past the veil, and charity — however incomplete — as the only garment that fits. The covenants open the way Christ opened. Truth is received in the going. Life is what waits.

The work this week is simple and severe: check the eye. Is it single? Is it aimed at Christ as prototype, or at something else — a political outcome, a family ideal, a personal project, even a theological system? Whatever the eye stays on, the body fills with. And what the body fills with is what the body finally comprehends.
