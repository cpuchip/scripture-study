# Episode: What "Enjoy" Really Means

**Series:** Enjoy the Words of Eternal Life
**Episode:** 001
**Target length:** ~5 minutes

## Hook (30 sec)

"God's very first lesson to Adam — the first time He ever explained the plan of salvation to anyone on this earth — ends with a word we totally gloss over. He says you can be sanctified and *enjoy* the words of eternal life. And we read right past it. But that word doesn't mean what you think it means."

---

## Setup (1 min)

- This is Moses 6:59 — God speaking to Adam directly
- It's not a sermon in a church. It's the first Gospel lesson ever given
- The verse lays out the whole plan: fall, death, rebirth, sanctification... and then the payoff. The *purpose* of all of it
- Two parallel promises:
  - **In this world:** enjoy the *words* of eternal life
  - **In the world to come:** eternal life itself
- We usually focus on that second one. But the first promise is the one for *right now*

---

## The Discovery (3 min)

### The word "enjoy"

- We hear "enjoy" and think — light, casual. *Enjoy your weekend.*
- But in 1828, when this language was being revealed, "enjoy" meant something much heavier
- Webster 1828: **"To have, possess, and use with satisfaction"**
- There's *ownership* in that word. You don't just feel something. You *hold* it. You *use* it.
- So "enjoy the words of eternal life" means: **have them, possess them, carry them, use them** — and take deep satisfaction in doing so

### Where the footnotes point

- The footnote on "enjoy" doesn't send you to the Topical Guide. It sends you to one man: Nephi.

**Read aloud — [2 Nephi 4:15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/4?lang=eng&id=p15#p15):**
> "For my soul delighteth in the scriptures, and my heart pondereth them, and writeth them for the learning and the profit of my children."

- *That's* what enjoying the words of eternal life looks like. Delight. Pondering. Writing.
- And here's the thing — this is Nephi in the middle of his psalm of anguish. He's weeping over his sins, exhausted by his enemies. And *in the middle of that*, the scriptures are his anchor.

### What Peter knew

- The other footnote sends you to John 6:68 — Peter's declaration

**Read aloud — [John 6:68](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/6?lang=eng&id=p68#p68):**
> "Lord, to whom shall we go? Thou hast the words of eternal life."

- This comes right after the Bread of Life discourse — Jesus said things so hard that "many of his disciples went back, and walked no more with him." He turns to the Twelve: "Will ye also go away?"
- Peter doesn't say "I understand everything." He says: *Where else would we go?*
- He'd recognized something. The words themselves carried life in them. Not the miracles — thousands saw those and walked away. The *words*.
- And five verses earlier, Jesus had explained why:

**Read aloud — [John 6:63](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/6?lang=eng&id=p63#p63):**
> "The words that I speak unto you, they are spirit, and they are life."

- *(Let that land)* Not *about* spirit. Not *about* life. They **are** spirit. They **are** life.

---

## Landing (30 sec)

"So the next time you open your scriptures — or the next time you're sitting in a meeting and something hits you — that's not just information. According to Jesus, those words are spirit and life. And according to God's very first lesson to Adam, you're not just meant to *read* them. You're meant to *enjoy* them — to have them, possess them, carry them with you. That's the whole purpose of the plan."
