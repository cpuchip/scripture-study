# Translated Beings: The Change Wrought, the Promise, and the Reunion

*Study date: June 2025*
*Flows from: [Zion Blueprint](zion-blueprint.md), [Enoch](enoch.md), [Enoch & Charity](enoch-charity.md)*

---

## The Question

What does it mean to be translated? What is the change wrought upon a mortal body that makes it something *more* than mortal but *less* than resurrected? Who are the beings who have undergone this change, and what carried them there? And can that same process begin in us — not for the benefits, but because the purpose is pure?

> "I think the heart must be pure in purpose, like the 3 Nephites, and that purpose I think has to be to build and live a Zion life."

This isn't a study about wanting superpowers. The Three Nephites didn't ask for invulnerability — they asked to bring souls to Christ. Everything else followed from that desire.

---

## I. What Is Translation?

### The Words

**Translate** — Webster 1828, definition 3: *"To remove to heaven, as a human being, without subjecting to death."* The primary definition is about *carrying from one place to another* — and that's exactly what happens. The body is carried from one state to another without passing through death.

**Transfigure** — Webster 1828: *"To change the outward form or appearance; to metamorphose; to transform. Especially, to change to something exalted and glorious."* This is the word the scriptures use for the *process* — the visible, experiential change.

**Quicken** — Webster 1828: *"To make alive; to vivify; to revive or resuscitate, as from death or an inanimate state."* This is the word the Doctrine and Covenants uses for what glory does to a body — it *quickens* it, makes it alive in a way it wasn't before.

### The Definitive Description

Mormon didn't understand it either. He wrote the account of the Three Nephites, wasn't sure whether they were mortal or immortal after their transfiguration, and said so honestly. Then he did the most important thing a truth-seeker can do — he asked the Lord:

> "Since I wrote, I have inquired of the Lord, and he hath made it manifest unto me that there must needs be a change wrought upon their bodies, or else it needs be that they must taste of death" ([3 Nephi 28:37](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/28?lang=eng&id=p37#p37))

The Lord's answer is the most detailed description of translation anywhere in scripture:

1. **Freedom from pain and sorrow** — "that they might not suffer pain nor sorrow save it were for the sins of the world" (v. 38)
2. **Power over Satan** — "Satan could have no power over them, that he could not tempt them" (v. 39)
3. **Sanctification in the flesh** — "they were sanctified in the flesh, that they were holy" (v. 39)
4. **Power over the elements** — "the powers of the earth could not hold them" (v. 39)
5. **An intermediate state** — "this change was not equal to that which shall take place at the last day" (v. 39)
6. **A promise of more** — "at that day they were to receive a greater change, and to be received into the kingdom of the Father to go no more out, but to dwell with God eternally in the heavens" (v. 40)

That fifth point is critical. Translation is not the final destination. It is a *waypoint* — a body changed enough to endure the work, but not yet changed into its eternal, resurrected form. It is, in the language of D&C 88, a body quickened by a *portion* of glory, awaiting its fulness.

### The Hebrew Witness

Paul, writing to the Hebrews, gives us the simplest scriptural summary:

> "By faith Enoch was translated that he should not see death; and was not found, because God had translated him: for before his translation he had this testimony, that he pleased God." ([Hebrews 11:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/heb/11?lang=eng&id=p5#p5))

Two things stand out: it happened *by faith*, and it required *a testimony that he pleased God*. Not just belief — but a life that pleased the Lord. That's the prerequisite.

## II. Who Has Been Translated?

### The Known

**Enoch and his entire city** — "Enoch and all his people walked with God, and he dwelt in the midst of Zion; and it came to pass that Zion was not, for God received it up into his own bosom; and from thence went forth the saying, Zion is Fled" ([Moses 7:69](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/7?lang=eng&id=p69#p69)). This wasn't one man — this was a *people*, gathered in righteousness over 365 years of building among the wicked. They still live. They will return ([Moses 7:63](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/7?lang=eng&id=p63#p63)).

**Elijah** — Confirmed by the Lord himself in the Kirtland Temple: "Elijah...who was taken to heaven without tasting death" ([D&C 110:13](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/110?lang=eng&id=p13#p13)). He appeared on the Mount of Transfiguration with Moses and Christ ([Matthew 17:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/matt/17?lang=eng&id=p3#p3)), and again in the Kirtland Temple to commit the sealing keys ([D&C 110:13-16](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/110?lang=eng&id=p13-p16#p13)).

**The Three Nephites** — Jesus himself granted the change. Their desire: "that ye might bring the souls of men unto me, while the world shall stand" ([3 Nephi 28:9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/28?lang=eng&id=p9#p9)). Mormon saw them ([3 Nephi 28:26](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/28?lang=eng&id=p26#p26)). Moroni saw them: "the disciples of Jesus, who did tarry in the land...my father and I have seen them, and they have ministered unto us" ([Mormon 8:10-11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/morm/8?lang=eng&id=p10-p11#p10)). They are still here. "They will be among the Gentiles, and the Gentiles shall know them not" (v. 27).

**John the Beloved** — "Lord, give unto me power over death, that I may live and bring souls unto thee" ([D&C 7:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/7?lang=eng&id=p2#p2)). The Lord called this "a greater work" than Peter's desire to come quickly to the kingdom (v. 5). John was made "as flaming fire and a ministering angel" (v. 6). The same desire. The same purpose. *Bring souls unto thee.*

### The Probable

**Moses** — The case for Moses being translated (rather than simply dying and being resurrected) is strong but not airtight. Alma tells us he was "taken up by the Spirit, or buried by the hand of the Lord, even as Moses" ([Alma 45:19](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/45?lang=eng&id=p19#p19)) — using Moses as the archetype for mysterious departure. Moses appeared *with his body* on the Mount of Transfiguration before Christ's resurrection ([Matthew 17:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/matt/17?lang=eng&id=p3#p3)), which raises the question: where did that body come from? If Moses had died normally, he would have been a disembodied spirit until the resurrection. His appearance in a body suggests translation — or a special early resurrection. The D&C 84 account notes "the Lord...took Moses out of their midst" ([D&C 84:25](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/84?lang=eng&id=p25#p25)).

**Alma** — "He said unto Helaman...it is as well said that he was buried by the hand of the Lord as to say he was taken up by the Spirit" ([Alma 45:18-19](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/45?lang=eng&id=p18-p19#p18)). Mormon deliberately leaves the door open. We don't know.

### A Caution

We should be careful about learning the mechanics of translation from those who haven't experienced it. The scriptures give us what the Lord chose to reveal — primarily through 3 Nephi 28, where the Lord himself explained the change to Mormon in response to direct inquiry. Beyond what's revealed, we're in the territory of speculation. That's honest ground to stand on, as long as we name it.

## III. Translation vs. Resurrection — The Critical Distinction

This is where the study gets precise. Translation and resurrection are *not* the same thing. They overlap in appearance but differ in nature, permanence, and glory.

### Translation: The Partial Change

A translated being has a body that has been *changed* — freed from pain, sorrow, temptation, and the power of death — but this change is explicitly "not equal to that which shall take place at the last day" ([3 Nephi 28:39](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/28?lang=eng&id=p39#p39)). The body is still, in some sense, a *mortal* body that has been sanctified and elevated. It is a terrestrial-order body — quickened beyond the telestial world we inhabit, but not yet celestial.

The Three Nephites themselves experienced this as a *transfiguration*: "it did seem unto them like a transfiguration of them, that they were changed from this body of flesh into an immortal state" ([3 Nephi 28:15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/28?lang=eng&id=p15#p15)). The word "seem" is doing real work there. It *felt* immortal. But it wasn't the full, permanent change.

### Resurrection: The Full and Final Union

Resurrection is the permanent, inseparable reunion of spirit and body:

> "The spirit and the body are the soul of man. And the resurrection from the dead is the redemption of the soul." ([D&C 88:15-16](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88?lang=eng&id=p15-p16#p15))

In resurrection, the body is *quickened* by the glory it has chosen to abide:

> "They who are of a celestial spirit shall receive the same body which was a natural body; even ye shall receive your bodies, and your glory shall be that glory by which your bodies are quickened." ([D&C 88:28](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88?lang=eng&id=p28#p28))

And each kingdom receives its fulness — celestial bodies quickened by celestial glory, terrestrial by terrestrial, telestial by telestial ([D&C 88:29-31](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88?lang=eng&id=p29-p31#p29)).

### The Twinkling Upgrade

Here's the beautiful bridge between the two states. The Three Nephites were not left in their translated condition permanently. Christ told them plainly:

> "When I shall come in my glory ye shall be changed in the twinkling of an eye from mortality to immortality" ([3 Nephi 28:8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/28?lang=eng&id=p8#p8))

Paul described the same event:

> "We shall not all sleep, but we shall all be changed, In a moment, in the twinkling of an eye, at the last trump: for the trumpet shall sound, and the dead shall be raised incorruptible, and we shall be changed." ([1 Corinthians 15:51-52](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/1-cor/15?lang=eng&id=p51-p52#p51))

Two groups. Same moment. The dead are *raised* (resurrection). The translated are *changed* (the upgrade from partial to full). Both happen "in the twinkling of an eye." The translated beings don't die first — they step directly from one glory to another.

This is the "greater change" of 3 Nephi 28:40 — "to be received into the kingdom of the Father to go no more out, but to dwell with God eternally in the heavens."

### A Table of Distinction

| | Translation | Resurrection |
|---|---|---|
| **Nature** | Partial change — body sanctified, not yet perfected | Full change — permanent union of spirit and body |
| **Pain/Sorrow** | Removed (except sorrow for sins of the world) | Fully removed |
| **Death** | Bypassed — "shall never taste of death" | Conquered — "death is swallowed up in victory" |
| **Satan's Power** | Cannot tempt them | Has no claim on them |
| **Duration** | Temporary — until the Second Coming | Eternal — "to go no more out" |
| **Glory** | Terrestrial-order (partial quickening) | Celestial-order (fulness of quickening) |
| **Scriptural Example** | Three Nephites, Enoch's city, John, Elijah | Christ, Moroni, the saints who arose at His death |

## IV. D&C 129 — Joseph Smith's Three Grand Keys

Joseph Smith gave a practical framework for distinguishing messengers from beyond the veil:

1. **A resurrected being** (angel) has a body of flesh and bones — "offer him your hand and request him to shake hands with you. If he be an angel he will do so, and you will feel his hand." ([D&C 129:4-5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/129?lang=eng&id=p4-p5#p4))

2. **A spirit of a just man made perfect** — "he will come in his glory; for that is the only way he can appear." He will not shake hands, "because it is contrary to the order of heaven for a just man to deceive." ([D&C 129:6-7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/129?lang=eng&id=p6-p7#p6))

3. **The devil as an angel of light** — "when you ask him to shake hands he will offer you his hand, and you will not feel anything." ([D&C 129:8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/129?lang=eng&id=p8#p8))

Notice what's missing from this framework: *translated beings as a category*. Joseph lists resurrected beings and disembodied spirits — but where do the Three Nephites, John, and Elijah fit? They have bodies (so they're not spirits), but they haven't died and been resurrected. They're in that in-between space.

3 Nephi 28:30 may give the answer: "they are as the angels of God." They function like resurrected messengers — they have tangible bodies, they minister, they appear and disappear. But their bodies are awaiting the upgrade. The practical effect may be indistinguishable, even if the underlying nature differs.

## V. Resurrected Beings — The Greater Change

If translation is the waypoint, resurrection is the destination. And it has already begun.

### Christ — The Firstfruits

"But now is Christ risen from the dead, and become the firstfruits of them that slept" ([1 Corinthians 15:20](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/1-cor/15?lang=eng&id=p20#p20)). He was the first to receive a permanent, glorified, physical body after death. Every resurrection that follows depends on his.

### The Saints Who Arose

At Christ's death, "the graves were opened; and many bodies of the saints which slept arose, And came out of the graves after his resurrection, and went into the holy city, and appeared unto many" ([Matthew 27:52-53](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/matt/27?lang=eng&id=p52-p53#p52)).

The Nephite record confirms this from the other hemisphere — Jesus himself commanded it be written: "I commanded my servant Samuel, the Lamanite, that he should testify unto this people, that at the day that the Father should glorify his name in me that there were many saints who should arise from the dead, and should appear unto many, and should minister unto them" ([3 Nephi 23:9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/23?lang=eng&id=p9#p9)). And when the Nephites hadn't recorded it, he corrected them: "How be it that ye have not written this thing?" (v. 11).

He *wanted us to know* that resurrection had already begun.

### Moroni — A Resurrected Messenger

When Moroni appeared to Joseph Smith, he came as a resurrected being — not translated, not a disembodied spirit, but a man with a glorified body:

> "His whole person was glorious beyond description, and his countenance truly like lightning" ([JS—H 1:32](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/js-h/1?lang=eng&id=p32#p32))

He was "a messenger sent from the presence of God" (v. 33). He had died around 421 AD. By 1823, he had a resurrected body — tangible, glorified, capable of appearing and departing. This is what awaits all of us. And it's what awaits the translated beings too, once the "twinkling" happens.

## VI. Transfiguration — The Temporary Window

There's a third category that deserves attention: *transfiguration* — a temporary change that enables a mortal to endure divine contact, without translation's permanence.

**Moses** experienced this directly: "But now mine own eyes have beheld God; but not my natural, but my spiritual eyes, for my natural eyes could not have beheld; for I should have withered and died in his presence; but his glory was upon me; and I beheld his face, for I was transfigured before him" ([Moses 1:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/1?lang=eng&id=p11#p11)).

Without transfiguration, mortality cannot endure the presence of God. This same pattern appeared on the Mount of Transfiguration, where Peter, James, and John saw Christ glorified, with Moses and Elijah appearing ([Matthew 17:1-3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/matt/17?lang=eng&id=p1-p3#p1)). The Lord later referenced this event as a *pattern* for the future:

> "He that endureth in faith and doeth my will, the same shall overcome, and shall receive an inheritance upon the earth when the day of transfiguration shall come; When the earth shall be transfigured, even according to the pattern which was shown unto mine apostles upon the mount" ([D&C 63:20-21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/63?lang=eng&id=p20-p21#p20))

The *earth itself* will be transfigured — changed from its current telestial state to a terrestrial state during the Millennium, and eventually to a celestial state. The pattern of translation applies to the entire creation. What happens to individual bodies (Enoch, the Three Nephites) will happen to the planet.

## VII. The Building Blocks — What Got Them There

We probably can't reverse-engineer translation from the outside. But the scriptures give us the *spiritual prerequisites* — and these are things we can pursue right now.

### Faith

"By faith Enoch was translated that he should not see death" ([Hebrews 11:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/heb/11?lang=eng&id=p5#p5)). "By faith the three disciples obtained a promise that they should not taste of death" ([Ether 12:17](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/ether/12?lang=eng&id=p17#p17)). Faith isn't just the starting point — it's the *mechanism*. Not faith in translation, but faith in God — faith sufficient to please him.

### Pure Purpose

Every translated being we know of was driven by the desire to *serve*, not to *receive*:

- The Three Nephites: "ye have desired that ye might bring the souls of men unto me, while the world shall stand" ([3 Nephi 28:9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/28?lang=eng&id=p9#p9))
- John the Beloved: "give unto me power over death, that I may live and bring souls unto thee" ([D&C 7:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/7?lang=eng&id=p2#p2))
- Enoch: he wept over the wicked, pled for generations unborn, refused to be comforted until the Lord showed him redemption ([Moses 7:41-47](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/7?lang=eng&id=p41-p47#p41))

Not one of them asked, "How can I avoid death?" or "How can I escape pain?" They asked, "How can I serve longer? How can I do more? How can I bring *them* to *you*?"

The Lord called John's desire "a greater work" than Peter's desire to come quickly to the kingdom ([D&C 7:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/7?lang=eng&id=p5#p5)). Not that Peter's desire was wrong — "this was a good desire" (v. 5). But the desire to stay and labor is, in Christ's own estimation, *greater*.

### Walking with God

"Enoch walked with God: and he was not; for God took him" ([Genesis 5:24](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/5?lang=eng&id=p24#p24)). The simplest sentence in scripture about translation doesn't describe a dramatic event. It describes a relationship. He *walked* with God. Day after day, year after year, for 365 years of building Zion among the wicked — and one day the walking simply continued past the boundary of this world.

### The Priesthood Promise

There's a verse in D&C 84 that most people read as metaphorical. I'm not sure it is:

> "For whoso is faithful unto the obtaining these two priesthoods of which I have spoken, and the magnifying their calling, are sanctified by the Spirit unto the renewing of their bodies." ([D&C 84:33](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/84?lang=eng&id=p33#p33))

"Sanctified by the Spirit unto the renewing of their bodies." That is *translation language*. The same Spirit. The same sanctification. The same renewal. The difference may be one of degree rather than kind — the Three Nephites received the fulness of this promise, while faithful priesthood holders receive a *portion* of it now. Enough to do the work. Enough to endure.

## VIII. The Reunion

This is where the Zion arc completes. The translated beings were not taken away forever. They were taken *until the time is right*.

Enoch saw the end from the beginning. He saw the Flood, the cross, the apostasy, the Restoration. And the Lord made him a promise:

> "Righteousness will I send down out of heaven; and truth will I send forth out of the earth, to bear testimony of mine Only Begotten...and righteousness and truth will I cause to sweep the earth as with a flood, to gather out mine elect from the four quarters of the earth, unto a place which I shall prepare, an Holy City, that my people may gird up their loins, and be looking forth for the time of my coming; for there shall be my tabernacle, and it shall be called Zion, a New Jerusalem." ([Moses 7:62](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/7?lang=eng&id=p62#p62))

And then the reunion:

> "Then shalt thou and all thy city meet them there, and we will receive them into our bosom, and they shall see us; and we will fall upon their necks, and they shall fall upon our necks, and we will kiss each other" ([Moses 7:63](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/7?lang=eng&id=p63#p63))

Elder Bruce R. McConkie saw this clearly:

> "We shall see the New Jerusalem coming down from God in heaven to join with the Holy City we have built. We shall mingle with those of Enoch's city while together we worship and serve the Lord forever." ([The Coming Tests and Trials and Glory](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1980/04/the-coming-tests-and-trials-and-glory?lang=eng), April 1980)

The translated city meets the gathered Saints. Those who were taken up and those who built anew. And in that moment, when Christ comes in his glory, the translated are changed "in the twinkling of an eye from mortality to immortality" ([3 Nephi 28:8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/28?lang=eng&id=p8#p8)), and the dead are raised, and *all* receive the fulness of the glory they have chosen to abide.

The **reserved** city ([D&C 45:12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/45?lang=eng&id=p12#p12)) and the **built** city become one. The translated and the resurrected become one. And the earth itself undergoes the same transformation — transfigured "according to the pattern which was shown unto mine apostles upon the mount" ([D&C 63:21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/63?lang=eng&id=p21#p21)).

## IX. Can We Pursue This?

Yes and no. And the distinction matters.

**No** — in the sense that translation isn't a self-improvement project. Nobody has ever achieved it by trying to achieve it. It came to those who were so consumed with the Lord's work that they forgot to ask what was in it for them. The Three Nephites didn't know they'd be translated until Jesus told them. John didn't negotiate terms — he simply asked for more time to serve. Enoch walked with God for 365 years without it being a technique.

**Yes** — in the sense that everything that *prepared* them for translation is available to us right now:

- **Faith that pleases God** (Hebrews 11:5) — not just belief, but a life organized around trust
- **Desire to bring souls to Christ** (3 Nephi 28:9; D&C 7:2) — the Zion impulse, the "one heart and one mind" impulse
- **Walking with God** (Genesis 5:24) — daily, sustained, unglamorous faithfulness
- **The oath and covenant of the priesthood** (D&C 84:33) — faithfully obtaining and magnifying, which the Lord promises will *renew your body*
- **Sanctification in the flesh** (3 Nephi 28:39) — which is the work of the Holy Ghost in us, making us holy while we still inhabit mortal bodies

The pattern suggests that the *spiritual* transformation precedes and enables the physical one. Enoch didn't become righteous because he was translated — he was translated because he had become righteous. The Three Nephites didn't become holy because they were changed — they were changed because they *desired holiness for others*.

And D&C 84:33 suggests the process has already begun for anyone faithful in the priesthood. The "renewing of their bodies" isn't waiting for the Second Coming. It's happening now, incrementally, by the Spirit. Perhaps that's what we can actually pursue: not the destination of translation, but the same path that leads there — faith, service, walking with God, and letting the Spirit do its sanctifying work in the flesh.

## X. What We Don't Know

Honesty demands naming the gaps:

- **The mechanics of the change** — we know *that* a change is wrought, but not *how*. The scriptures describe the effects, not the process.
- **Where they are** — the Three Nephites are "among the Gentiles" (3 Nephi 28:27) and "among the Jews" (v. 28), but we don't know what that looks like day to day.
- **The experience of it** — Mormon said "whether they were in the body or out of the body, they could not tell" (3 Nephi 28:15). Even the people who experienced it couldn't fully describe it.
- **Whether Moses was translated or resurrected** — the evidence points in both directions, and the scriptures deliberately leave it open.
- **The full account of the Mount of Transfiguration** — the Lord himself said "of which account the fulness ye have not yet received" ([D&C 63:21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/63?lang=eng&id=p21#p21)). There is more to be revealed.

These gaps are not failures of the record. They are invitations. The Lord has told us what we need for now. The rest will come "according to the time and the will of the Father" ([3 Nephi 23:4](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/23?lang=eng&id=p4#p4)).

## XI. Becoming

This study doesn't end with knowledge. It ends with an honest question: *So what?*

I'm a mortal. I have allergies and a sore neck and the full catalogue of human frailty. And I look forward to my resurrection — when body and spirit will be joined permanently, in a fulness of joy that I can barely imagine, "coupled with eternal glory" ([D&C 130:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/130?lang=eng&id=p2#p2)).

But in the meantime, the path that led Enoch and the Three Nephites and John to translation is the same path that leads anyone to Christ. The building blocks are not exotic:

1. **Walk with God daily** — not as a project, but as a relationship. Enoch walked. That's the verb. Just walk.
2. **Let purpose be pure** — not "what can I get?" but "who can I bring?" The translated ones all wanted to serve, not to escape.
3. **Magnify the priesthood** — D&C 84:33 promises a *renewing of the body* to those who are faithful in this. Let that promise land.
4. **Let the Spirit sanctify** — The same sanctification that made the Three Nephites "holy in the flesh" (3 Nephi 28:39) is the same Spirit working in every covenant keeper. The degree differs. The source doesn't.
5. **Build Zion** — Enoch's translation wasn't personal. It was communal. He didn't walk with God alone — his people walked with him. Translation happened to a *city*. If we want what Enoch had, we need to build what Enoch built.

The benefits — no pain, no temptation, no power of death — those followed the purpose. They didn't precede it. The heart must be pure first. Always first.

> "And ye shall be even as I am, and I am even as the Father; and the Father and I are one" ([3 Nephi 28:10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/28?lang=eng&id=p10#p10))

That's the promise at the end of the road. Not just survival — *oneness*. Not just endurance — *joy*. The translated are on their way there. The resurrected arrive. And we, walking the same path, are somewhere on the same road.
