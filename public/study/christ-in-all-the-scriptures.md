# The Things Concerning Himself

> *"And beginning at Moses and all the prophets, he expounded unto them in all the scriptures the things concerning himself."*
> — [Luke 24:27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/luke/24?lang=eng&id=p27#p27)

**Catalyst:** A Sabbath-evening question after stake conference — the road to Emmaus, where the risen Lord walked with two disciples who did not know him and opened the scriptures until their hearts burned.

**Binding questions:** What did Jesus expound as he taught? And how do we see Christ in all the scriptures?

---

## His first sermon was a scripture lesson

On the afternoon of the Resurrection, two disciples walk seven miles from Jerusalem to Emmaus, turning over the worst three days of their lives. A stranger joins them. He asks what they're discussing, draws the whole grief out of them, and then, before he reveals who he is, he teaches. "Beginning at Moses and all the prophets, he expounded unto them in all the scriptures the things concerning himself" ([Luke 24:27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/luke/24?lang=eng&id=p27#p27)).

The risen Lord, on the first day anyone could possibly recognize him, does not lead with "it is I." He leads with the Old Testament. His first act as the conqueror of death is to walk two heartbroken men through the scriptures they already had and show them he had been on every page the whole time. Recognition comes later, at the table, in the breaking of bread — and the instant it comes, he vanishes ([Luke 24:30–31](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/luke/24?lang=eng&id=p30-p31#p30)). What lingers is the exposition. Looking back, the only thing they can name is the burning: "Did not our heart burn within us, while he talked with us by the way, and while he opened to us the scriptures?" ([Luke 24:32](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/luke/24?lang=eng&id=p32#p32)).

He says it once more that same night, to the gathered eleven, and this time he names the whole shape of the canon: "all things must be fulfilled, which were written in the law of Moses, and in the prophets, and in the psalms, concerning me. Then opened he their understanding, that they might understand the scriptures" ([Luke 24:44–45](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/luke/24?lang=eng&id=p44-p45#p44)). The law, the prophets, the writings — the entire Hebrew Bible, sorted into its three parts and all of it "concerning me." And note the two openings: he opened their eyes at the table (v. 31), and he opened their understanding here (v. 45). Seeing the text and seeing through it are two different gifts, and he gives both.

Then he compresses the content of all that exposition into a single sentence: "Thus it is written, and thus it behoved Christ to suffer, and to rise from the dead the third day" ([Luke 24:46](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/luke/24?lang=eng&id=p46#p46)). Suffer, then rise the third day. That two-beat pattern is the thing the scriptures were "concerning." Every sermon the apostles preach after Pentecost turns out to be a variation on it.

So the binding question is really his question, handed to us: if the scriptures are "concerning himself," what did he point to, and how do we learn to see it?

## The claim the scriptures make about themselves

This is not a clever reading we bring to the text. It is a claim the text makes about itself, repeatedly and in plain words.

Jesus says it twice in John. "Search the scriptures; for in them ye think ye have eternal life: and they are they which testify of me" ([John 5:39](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/5?lang=eng&id=p39#p39)). And a few verses later, of the Torah specifically: "had ye believed Moses, ye would have believed me: for he wrote of me" ([John 5:46](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/5?lang=eng&id=p46#p46)). The scriptures' whole function, in his telling, is testimony — and the one they testify of is him.

The Book of Mormon prophets press the claim to its limit. Jacob makes it categorical: "they truly testify of Christ. Behold, I say unto you that none of the prophets have written, nor prophesied, save they have spoken concerning this Christ" ([Jacob 7:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/jacob/7?lang=eng&id=p11#p11)). Not most prophets. None excepted. Nephi grounds the same claim in the design of creation: "all things which have been given of God from the beginning of the world, unto man, are the typifying of him" ([2 Nephi 11:4](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/11?lang=eng&id=p4#p4)). And the Lord widens it further still in the Book of Moses: "all things are created and made to bear record of me, both things which are temporal, and things which are spiritual... all things bear record of me" ([Moses 6:63](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/6?lang=eng&id=p63#p63)).

The New Testament writers say the same of prophecy itself. Peter writes that the prophets searched their own words because "the Spirit of Christ which was in them... testified beforehand the sufferings of Christ, and the glory that should follow" ([1 Peter 1:10–11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/1-pet/1?lang=eng&id=p10-p11#p10)) — the same suffer-then-glory pattern Jesus named on the road. John puts it as a definition: "the testimony of Jesus is the spirit of prophecy" ([Revelation 19:10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/rev/19?lang=eng&id=p10#p10)). To prophesy, at root, is to witness of Christ. And the doctrine reaches backward to cover everyone who ever believed: salvation comes to "as many as were before he came, who believed in the words of the holy prophets... who truly testified of him in all things" ([D&C 20:26](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/20?lang=eng&id=p26#p26)).

The word at the center of it all is a title. Webster's 1828 dictionary, compiled in the language of the King James Bible, defines *Christ* as "THE ANOINTED; an appellation given to the Savior of the World, and synonymous with the Hebrew Messiah," and gives the reason in the same entry: "It was a custom of antiquity to consecrate persons to the sacerdotal and regal offices by anointing them with oil." *Christ* and *Messiah* are the same word in two languages, and the word means *anointed*. The whole Old Testament anoints people with oil, consecrating prophets, priests, and kings to stand between God and the people.

 Every one of those anointings is a small rehearsal for the Anointed One who would be prophet, priest, and king at once. "Jesus Christ" is not a first and last name. It reads "Jesus the Anointed."

## What he actually pointed to

So when he "expounded the things concerning himself," what was on the list?

The clearest case is one he draws in his own mouth, to Nicodemus: "as Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, even so must the Son of man be lifted up" ([John 3:14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/3?lang=eng&id=p14#p14)). In [Numbers 21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/num/21?lang=eng), Israel is dying of serpent bites; the Lord tells Moses to mount a brass serpent on a pole, and "every one that is bitten, when he looketh upon it, shall live" ([Numbers 21:8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/num/21?lang=eng&id=p8#p8)). Nothing in Numbers calls it a prophecy. Jesus supplies the antitype himself: the serpent lifted up to heal the dying by a look is him, lifted up on the cross to heal the perishing by faith. A strange bronze relic in a wilderness story turns out to have been a picture of Calvary the whole time.

He does it again with Jonah, and again with the Psalms. "As Jonas was three days and three nights in the whale's belly; so shall the Son of man be three days and three nights in the heart of the earth" ([Matthew 12:40](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/matt/12?lang=eng&id=p40#p40)): the reluctant prophet swallowed and disgorged becomes a sign of burial and resurrection. Quoting Psalm 110, he stumps the Pharisees with their own scripture: if David calls the Messiah "my Lord" ([Matthew 22:44](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/matt/22?lang=eng&id=p44#p44)), then the Messiah is more than David's son, and "no man was able to answer him a word" ([Matthew 22:46](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/matt/22?lang=eng&id=p46#p46)). At Nazareth he reads Isaiah 61 aloud and sits down with the line "This day is this scripture fulfilled in your ears" ([Luke 4:21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/luke/4?lang=eng&id=p21#p21)) — and tellingly, he stops reading mid-sentence, at "the acceptable year of the Lord," omitting Isaiah's next phrase, "the day of vengeance of our God." The first coming is the acceptable year; the vengeance waits. Where he stopped reading was itself an interpretation.

The verb that keeps recurring is *expound*: to lay open. He does it a second time, on a second continent, after his resurrection: among the Nephites he commands them to "search the prophets" ([3 Nephi 23:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/23?lang=eng&id=p5#p5)), and "when Jesus had expounded all the scriptures in one, which they had written, he commanded them that they should teach the things which he had expounded" ([3 Nephi 23:14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/23?lang=eng&id=p14#p14)). "All the scriptures in one" — the whole library read as a single book with a single subject. There is a quiet thunderclap buried in the cross-reference here. To the Nephites he says, "Behold, I am he of whom Moses spake... and... all the prophets from Samuel and those that follow after, as many as have spoken, have testified of me" ([3 Nephi 20:23–24](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/20?lang=eng&id=p23-p24#p23)). Peter says the identical thing in Jerusalem, citing the same prophet-like-Moses passage from Deuteronomy and the same roll of witnesses: "all the prophets from Samuel and those that follow after... have likewise foretold of these days" ([Acts 3:24](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/acts/3?lang=eng&id=p24#p24)). The same exposition, the same proof-texts, given on opposite sides of the world to people who had never met. That is not a literary habit of Luke's. That is the actual shape of revelation showing through in two places at once.

After Pentecost the apostles do nothing but this. Philip finds the Ethiopian eunuch reading Isaiah 53 aloud, baffled, asking "of whom speaketh the prophet this?", and "began at the same scripture, and preached unto him Jesus" ([Acts 8:35](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/acts/8?lang=eng&id=p35#p35)). That little scene is the whole binding question dramatized: a man holding the suffering-servant chapter and asking *who is this about*, and the answer is a person. Paul "reasoned with them out of the scriptures, Opening and alleging, that Christ must needs have suffered, and risen again" ([Acts 17:2–3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/acts/17?lang=eng&id=p2-p3#p2)) — "opening," the same word as the Emmaus road. At the very end of Acts he is still at it in Rome, expounding "out of the law of Moses, and out of the prophets, from morning till evening" ([Acts 28:23](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/acts/28?lang=eng&id=p23#p23)). And the gospel itself, in its earliest written form, is anchored twice to the older text: "Christ died for our sins according to the scriptures... and... rose again the third day according to the scriptures" ([1 Corinthians 15:3–4](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/1-cor/15?lang=eng&id=p3-p4#p3)). According to the scriptures. The suffer-and-rise pattern was never new information.

## A figure of something to come

Webster's 1828 defines a *type* as "a sign; a symbol; a figure of something to come," and gives as its own example the very figures this study turns on: "Abraham's sacrifice and the paschal lamb, were types of Christ. To this word is opposed antitype. Christ, in this case, is the antitype." The shadow exists to be cast off when the substance arrives — "a shadow of things to come; but the body is of Christ," as Paul will put it. That is why the law of Moses, having pointed forward for centuries, was fulfilled and ceased in Christ.

The surest types are the ones a later scripture names outright. The Passover lamb is one: a male "without blemish," its blood marking the doorposts so death would "pass over," with the strict instruction "neither shall ye break a bone thereof" ([Exodus 12:46](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/ex/12?lang=eng&id=p46#p46)). Paul names the antitype flatly: "Christ our passover is sacrificed for us" ([1 Corinthians 5:7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/1-cor/5?lang=eng&id=p7#p7)), and John records that the soldiers, finding Jesus already dead, broke no bone of his, "that the scripture should be fulfilled, A bone of him shall not be broken" ([John 19:36](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/19?lang=eng&id=p36#p36)). A rule about handling a lamb in Egypt turns out to have been a detail of the crucifixion fixed in advance.

Isaac is another, and the most tender. Abraham takes his only beloved son up a mountain, the boy carrying the wood for his own sacrifice. When Isaac asks where the lamb is, Abraham answers, "My son, God will provide himself a lamb" ([Genesis 22:8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/22?lang=eng&id=p8#p8)). Jacob, in the Book of Mormon, says plainly that this "is a similitude of God and his Only Begotten Son" ([Jacob 4:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/jacob/4?lang=eng&id=p5#p5)). There is even a seam in the story that points past Isaac: the lamb Abraham promised is not the ram that gets caught in the thicket. Isaac is spared; the true Lamb God would provide himself was still centuries off.

The brazen serpent is the best-attested type in all of scripture — confirmed by Jesus ([John 3:14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/3?lang=eng&id=p14#p14)), by Nephi son of Helaman ([Helaman 8:14–15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/hel/8?lang=eng&id=p14-p15#p14)), and by Alma, who uses the technical word: "a type was raised up in the wilderness, that whosoever would look upon it might live. And many did look and live" ([Alma 33:19](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/33?lang=eng&id=p19#p19)). The list runs on: Melchizedek, "king of Salem" and "priest of the most high God" who brings out bread and wine ([Genesis 14:18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/14?lang=eng&id=p18#p18)), whose priesthood order the Messiah holds forever ([Psalm 110:4](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/ps/110?lang=eng&id=p4#p4); [Hebrews 7:1–3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/heb/7?lang=eng&id=p1-p3#p1)). The manna and the rock that followed Israel, which Paul identifies outright: "that Rock was Christ" ([1 Corinthians 10:4](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/1-cor/10?lang=eng&id=p4#p4)). The entire tabernacle and its sacrifices, which Hebrews calls "the example and shadow of heavenly things" ([Hebrews 8:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/heb/8?lang=eng&id=p5#p5)) and "a shadow of good things to come" ([Hebrews 10:1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/heb/10?lang=eng&id=p1#p1)). Paul gathers all of it into one line: these ordinances "are a shadow of things to come; but the body is of Christ" ([Colossians 2:17](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/col/2?lang=eng&id=p17#p17)). The earliest sacrifice of all carried the meaning openly. When Adam asks why he offers the firstlings of his flock, the angel answers, "this thing is a similitude of the sacrifice of the Only Begotten of the Father" ([Moses 5:7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/5?lang=eng&id=p7#p7)). The blood on the first altar already meant Calvary.

Alongside the enacted types runs a second stream: spoken prophecy, where a prophet foretells the Messiah in words rather than figures. A virgin and Immanuel ([Isaiah 7:14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/isa/7?lang=eng&id=p14#p14)); a child called "Wonderful, Counsellor, The mighty God... The Prince of Peace" ([Isaiah 9:6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/isa/9?lang=eng&id=p6#p6)); the servant "wounded for our transgressions" by whose "stripes we are healed" ([Isaiah 53:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/isa/53?lang=eng&id=p5#p5)); the pierced hands and parted garments of [Psalm 22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/ps/22?lang=eng); a ruler from Bethlehem "whose goings forth have been from of old, from everlasting" ([Micah 5:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/micah/5?lang=eng&id=p2#p2)). The promise runs all the way back to the first pages: to the serpent in Eden, "he shall bruise thy head, and thou shalt bruise his heel" ([Moses 4:21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/4?lang=eng&id=p21#p21)) — the Restoration text sharpening the King James "it shall bruise" to "he." The wound and the victory are promised in the same breath, in the garden, before the gate even closes.

## The book that hands you the key

The Bible enacts Christ-in-all-scripture; the Book of Mormon names the method and explains why it had grown hard to see.

Nephi states the practice in four words: "I did liken all scriptures unto us, that it might be for our profit and learning" ([1 Nephi 19:23](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/19?lang=eng&id=p23#p23)) — and he says he did it specifically to "persuade them to believe in the Lord their Redeemer." Likening is not a devotional flourish; it is reading the text toward its center on purpose. His brother Jacob frames the law the same way: "we keep the law of Moses, it pointing our souls to him" ([Jacob 4:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/jacob/4?lang=eng&id=p5#p5)). Amulek takes it to the limit: "this is the whole meaning of the law, every whit pointing to that great and last sacrifice; and that great and last sacrifice will be the Son of God" ([Alma 34:14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/34?lang=eng&id=p14#p14)). Every whit. Abinadi turns it into a teaching rule: "if ye teach the law of Moses, also teach that it is a shadow of those things which are to come" ([Mosiah 16:14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/16?lang=eng&id=p14#p14)). And the Nephites do not only re-read the Bible's types; they generate their own. Alma takes the family compass and asks, "is there not a type in this thing?", then answers that giving heed to the word of Christ is as simple, and as life-or-death, as their fathers giving heed to the Liahona ([Alma 37:43–45](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/37?lang=eng&id=p43-p45#p43)). The most distilled version of the whole doctrine is a sleeper verse in one of the shortest books: the early Nephites taught the people to "look forward unto the Messiah, and believe in him to come as though he already was" ([Jarom 1:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/jarom/1?lang=eng&id=p11#p11)). To read the scriptures rightly is to believe in him to come as though he already was.

Why does this need restating at all? Because, the Book of Mormon says, Christ became harder to see. Nephi blames partly "the manner of prophesying among the Jews," which left Isaiah's words "not plain" to those without the spirit of prophecy ([2 Nephi 25:1–5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/25?lang=eng&id=p1-p5#p1)). More starkly, he foresees "many plain and precious things taken away from the book" of the Lamb, so that people "stumble" ([1 Nephi 13:28–29](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/13?lang=eng&id=p28-p29#p28)) — and he says the latter-day records exist to "make known... that the Lamb of God is the Son of the Eternal Father, and the Savior of the world" ([1 Nephi 13:40](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/13?lang=eng&id=p40#p40)). The Book of Mormon's own title page states the same purpose: it was written "to the convincing of the Jew and Gentile that Jesus is the Christ, the Eternal God, manifesting himself unto all nations." The book exists to put the plainness back.

And once, it drops the veil entirely. In the oldest narrative it contains, the brother of Jared sees a finger, then a whole body, and the premortal Lord says, "Behold, I am Jesus Christ. I am the Father and the Son... even as I appear unto thee to be in the spirit will I appear unto my people in the flesh" ([Ether 3:14–16](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/ether/3?lang=eng&id=p14-p16#p14)). The type collapses into presence. The whole machinery of shadow and figure exists because, at the center of it, there is a person who can simply be met. So Nephi can sum up the entire enterprise in a sentence that has been quoted in General Conference more than seventy-five times: "we talk of Christ, we rejoice in Christ, we preach of Christ, we prophesy of Christ... that our children may know to what source they may look for a remission of their sins" ([2 Nephi 25:26](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/25?lang=eng&id=p26#p26)).

## The verse almost no one quotes

The witnesses keep coming. From the pulpit in our own day, apostles have walked the same road. Elder Robert D. Hales retraced Emmaus and then swept the whole canon: "From Genesis to Malachi and from Moses to Abraham, it was prophesied the Savior would come... the prophets all testify that Jesus Christ, the long-awaited Messiah, has come and will come again" (["Holy Scriptures: The Power of God unto Our Salvation," Oct. 2006](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2006/10/holy-scriptures-the-power-of-god-unto-our-salvation?lang=eng)). Elder Dallin H. Oaks named the through-line directly: "The Messiah's atoning sacrifice is the central message of the prophets of all ages. It was prefigured by the animal sacrifices prescribed by the law of Moses" (["What Think Ye of Christ?," Oct. 1988](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1988/10/what-think-ye-of-christ?lang=eng)) — and to make the point he reached for Amulek's "great and last sacrifice," the same verse from Alma 34. The scriptures cite the scriptures, and the loop closes.

Thirteen days before he died, Elder Bruce R. McConkie described what a lifetime of this reading finally produces: "I shall use my own words, though you may think they are the words of scripture... they are now mine, for the Holy Spirit of God has borne witness to me that they are true... I have thereby heard his voice and know his word" (["The Purifying Power of Gethsemane," Apr. 1985](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1985/04/the-purifying-power-of-gethsemane?lang=eng)). The end of expounding the scriptures concerning Christ is that the words become your own. Scripture even keeps its own roll of the witnesses: President Joseph F. Smith's vision lists them by name: Noah, Abraham, Isaac, Jacob, Moses, Isaiah, Ezekiel, Daniel, Malachi, and "many more, even the prophets who dwelt among the Nephites and testified of the coming of the Son of God" ([D&C 138:41–49](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/138?lang=eng&id=p41-p49#p41)).

But here is the surprise the citation record turns up. The verse this study is anchored on, Luke 24:27, the one where the risen Lord opens all the scriptures concerning himself, has been cited in General Conference only a handful of times, and not once in the modern correlated era; the indexed citations trace back to LeGrand Richards in the 1960s and three nineteenth-century sermons. Meanwhile "they testify of me" ([John 5:39](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/5?lang=eng&id=p39#p39)) has been quoted more than seventy times, and "we talk of Christ" ([2 Nephi 25:26](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/25?lang=eng&id=p26#p26)) more than seventy-five. The road to Emmaus is among the best-loved stories we have, and the sentence at its very heart is one the pulpit has almost never paused on. The doctrine is everywhere; this particular witness of it sits quietly in the corner. The same thing happened to Abinadi's keystone verse in an earlier study — the structural center goes uncited while the tradition gathers around the edges. There is room left to find things here.

## Finding him, or putting him there

A doctrine this expansive has an obvious danger, and honesty requires naming it. If "all things bear record of me," is any reading that finds Christ a good reading? Can I make any verse mean Jesus if I squint hard enough?

No. The discipline is in the difference between the types scripture names and the ones tradition supplies. The brazen serpent is Christ because Jesus said so. The Passover lamb is Christ because Paul said so. Isaac is a similitude because Jacob said so. Those are bedrock; you can build a life on them. But Joseph of Egypt, the beloved son sold for silver, suffering and then exalted to save the very brothers who betrayed him, is never once called a type of Christ in scripture, however striking the parallel. Neither is Boaz the kinsman-redeemer, nor the scapegoat driven into the wilderness. These are beautiful, and they may well be intended, but they are our reading, not the text's claim, and a faithful study holds them with a lighter hand. Even Adam, whom Paul does call "the figure of him that was to come" ([Romans 5:14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/rom/5?lang=eng&id=p14#p14)), is a type by opposition, one man's fall answered by one man's grace, not by likeness. The named types anchor the offered ones; it never works the other way around.

And the deeper correction is that the scriptures never promise Christ will be obvious. Nephi is blunt that Isaiah is "hard" without the spirit of prophecy ([2 Nephi 25:1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/25?lang=eng&id=p1#p1)). Plain things were taken away ([1 Nephi 13:28](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/13?lang=eng&id=p28#p28)). The two on the Emmaus road had the entire Old Testament memorized and still did not see him until he opened it and their hearts burned. This is the hinge an [earlier study on Abinadi](abinadi-how-to-read.md) turned on: the priests of King Noah could quote Isaiah from memory and missed Christ entirely, because, Abinadi told them, "ye have not applied your hearts to understanding" ([Mosiah 12:27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/12?lang=eng&id=p27#p27)). The instrument that reads Christ out of scripture is not cleverness; it is a heart turned toward him, taught by the Spirit, disciplined by the living text and the living prophets. Seeing him everywhere is not a trick of interpretation. It is the fruit of a particular kind of attention — and the proof that the attention is real is the burning, not the ingenuity.

## Becoming

I read scripture, often, for the architecture. I notice the clever cross-reference, the ring composition, the verse the tradition overlooked, and I write it down, and I feel the satisfaction of having found something. That satisfaction is not the burning heart. The two disciples did not say *what a remarkable exegesis*; they said *did not our heart burn*. The test of whether I have actually expounded the things concerning himself is not whether the argument is tight. It is whether I came away loving him more.

There is a particular pull for me here, because I spend my days finding patterns — in scripture and in the systems I build, where I have argued that good engineering is itself a kind of type and shadow of eternal order. The skill of seeing the pattern is the same skill. But this study is the guardrail on it. A pattern is not the point; the Person the pattern points to is. It is possible to get very good at finding Christ-shaped figures everywhere and never once let the figure become a presence, the way the brother of Jared was not left with a symbol but with the Lord himself in the room. The whole apparatus of types exists to be cast off when the substance comes.

The one thing I want to carry: the next time I open the Old Testament, with its genealogies and sacrifices and wars and the parts I skim, I want to read them the way he taught the two on the road. Not hunting for hidden ciphers, but asking the honest question the eunuch asked over Isaiah 53: *of whom speaketh the prophet this?* And then waiting, with an applied heart, for the answer that is always, in the end, a person. He was on every page the whole time. The work is to let him open my understanding, and then to notice whether anything burns.
