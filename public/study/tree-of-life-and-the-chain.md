# Tree of Life and the Chain

*How the faith-hope-charity chain (Romans 5, Moroni 7, 1 Corinthians 13) maps onto the tree of life (Alma 32, 1 Nephi 8 and 11) — and what it means that the fruit is Christ Himself.*

## I. A Phrase Held in Common

Two passages, written six centuries and an ocean apart, share a phrase the King James English brings into focus.

Paul, writing to the Roman saints:

> "Hope maketh not ashamed; because the love of God is shed abroad in our hearts by the Holy Ghost which is given unto us." — [Romans 5:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/rom/5?lang=eng&id=p5#p5)

Nephi, writing on plates in the wilderness, asked by an angel for the meaning of the tree his father had seen:

> "Yea, it is the love of God, which sheddeth itself abroad in the hearts of the children of men; wherefore, it is the most desirable above all things." — [1 Nephi 11:22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/11?lang=eng&id=p22#p22)

The verb is the same. The object is the same. The location is the same. *The love of God / sheddeth abroad / in the hearts.* Joseph Smith translating the plates was steeped in KJV diction, and the parallel may be partly translation echo. But the doctrine the two verses carry is the same doctrine, and reading them against each other opens up something neither says alone.

The 1828 Webster's gives "shed" as "to spill; to suffer to flow out, as a fluid; to pour out." Both verses describe the love of God as something poured. In Paul's verse, the Holy Ghost does the pouring. In Nephi's verse, the love of God pours itself. Different agents, identical motion: love of God moving outward, into hearts.

This study takes that shared phrase as a hinge and asks what swings open if you read the faith-hope-charity chain and the tree-of-life vision as describing the same reality from two angles.

## II. Alma 32 Is the Faith Half of the Tree

Alma, preaching to the Zoramite poor on the hill Onidah, gave them an experiment.

> "We will compare the word unto a seed... if it be a true seed, or a good seed, if ye do not cast it out by your unbelief, that ye will resist the Spirit of the Lord, behold, it will begin to swell within your breasts." — [Alma 32:28](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/32?lang=eng&id=p28#p28)

The seed is the word. The soil is the heart. The experiment is whether you give the word room. Faith here is not certainty; faith is *making room* — refusing to cast the seed out before it has grown. Alma calls this "even no more than desire to believe" ([Alma 32:27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/32?lang=eng&id=p27#p27)).

Then he traces the growth:

> "But if ye will nourish the word, yea, nourish the tree as it beginneth to grow, by your faith with great diligence, and with patience, looking forward to the fruit thereof, it shall take root; and behold it shall be a tree springing up unto everlasting life." — [Alma 32:41](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/32?lang=eng&id=p41#p41)

Faith and patience together produce the tree. And what is the tree? "A tree springing up unto everlasting life." The fruit, when finally plucked,

> "is most precious, which is sweet above all that is sweet, and which is white above all that is white, yea, and pure above all that is pure; and ye shall feast upon this fruit even until ye are filled, that ye hunger not, neither shall ye thirst." — [Alma 32:42](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/32?lang=eng&id=p42#p42)

Hold those descriptors. *Sweet. White. Filled. Hunger no more.* Alma is using Lehi's vocabulary on purpose. Lehi tasted the fruit "most sweet, above all that I ever before tasted... white, to exceed all the whiteness that I had ever seen... it filled my soul with exceedingly great joy" ([1 Nephi 8:11-12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/8?lang=eng&id=p11-p12#p11)). And the "hunger not, neither shall ye thirst" of Alma 32:42 is the language Christ uses of Himself in John — "he that cometh to me shall never hunger" ([John 6:35](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/6?lang=eng&id=p35#p35)), "the water that I shall give him shall be in him a well of water springing up into everlasting life" ([John 4:14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/4?lang=eng&id=p14#p14)).

The fruit Alma promises is the same fruit Lehi tasted. And it produces the same satisfaction Christ promised the woman at the well.

## III. Nephi Is Told What the Tree Means

Lehi tasted the fruit and described it. Nephi, two chapters later, asked what it meant. The angel's answer was not a definition; it was a sequence of pictures.

The angel showed Nephi the virgin in Nazareth ([1 Nephi 11:13-15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/11?lang=eng&id=p13-p15#p13)). Then she bore a child, and the angel said:

> "Behold the Lamb of God, yea, even the Son of the Eternal Father! Knowest thou the meaning of the tree which thy father saw?" — [1 Nephi 11:21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/11?lang=eng&id=p21#p21)

The tree-question comes immediately after the Lamb-vision. The angel does not ask, "Knowest thou the meaning of the love of God?" He shows Nephi the Lamb and then asks about the tree. The juxtaposition is the answer before Nephi answers.

> "Yea, it is the love of God, which sheddeth itself abroad in the hearts of the children of men; wherefore, it is the most desirable above all things." — [1 Nephi 11:22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/11?lang=eng&id=p22#p22)

> "Yea, and the most joyous to the soul." — [1 Nephi 11:23](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/11?lang=eng&id=p23#p23)

And then, four verses later, the angel ties it together:

> "The rod of iron, which my father had seen, was the word of God, which led to the fountain of living waters, or to the tree of life; which waters are a representation of the love of God; and I also beheld that the tree of life was a representation of the love of God." — [1 Nephi 11:25](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/11?lang=eng&id=p25#p25)

Notice what happens in the symbol-system. The rod is the word of God. The tree is the love of God. The fountain is also the love of God. Two separate symbols (tree and fountain) collapse into one referent. The word leads to the love.

And put 1 Nephi 11:25 next to Alma 32:28. Alma says the *seed* is the word. Nephi says the *rod* is the word. Same word, two functions in the same vision. The word is what you grasp to walk through the mist *and* what you plant in the heart so it grows. The same word both guides and germinates.

## IV. Romans 5 on the Same Tree

Now look at Paul. He opens Romans 5 with what looks like the chain stated forward:

> "Therefore being justified by faith, we have peace with God through our Lord Jesus Christ: By whom also we have access by faith into this grace wherein we stand, and rejoice in hope of the glory of God." — [Romans 5:1-2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/rom/5?lang=eng&id=p1-p2#p1)

Faith. Hope. Then the love of God will arrive in v. 5. The whole triplet is laid down in five verses.

But between v. 2 and v. 5, Paul shows the work that produces the chain:

> "And not only so, but we glory in tribulations also: knowing that tribulation worketh patience; And patience, experience; and experience, hope: And hope maketh not ashamed; because the love of God is shed abroad in our hearts by the Holy Ghost which is given unto us." — [Romans 5:3-5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/rom/5?lang=eng&id=p3-p5#p3)

Tribulation produces patience. Patience produces experience. Experience produces hope. And hope does not produce the love of God; hope *receives* it, shed in the heart.

Compare this to Alma's tree. Alma says the tree grows "by your faith with great diligence, and with patience" ([Alma 32:41](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/32?lang=eng&id=p41#p41)). Paul says the same things — tribulation, patience, experience — produce hope. And the fruit Paul ends with is the love of God, the same thing Nephi was told the tree represents.

A caveat the text earns: Alma 32:27-43 does not name "hope" in the seed-to-fruit section. The word "hope" is in Alma's chapter (v. 21: "if ye have faith ye hope for things which are not seen, which are true"), but the seed-to-tree allegory uses faith, diligence, patience, long-suffering. The chain is not encoded in Alma 32 word-for-word. The mapping is by family resemblance: both passages describe the same motion of the soul — faith planted, hope grown through patience, love of God received as the end.

This is enough to read the two passages as describing the same tree from different angles. Paul names the steps without the agricultural picture. Alma gives the agricultural picture and names some of the steps. Nephi names what the tree IS. Take all three together and the picture fills in.

The Church's curriculum has already noticed the textual neighborhood. Preach My Gospel chapter 6, on patience, lists Alma 32:41-43 immediately adjacent to Romans 5:3-5 as cross-references ([Preach My Gospel ch. 6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/preach-my-gospel-2023/14-chapter-6?lang=eng)). Two passages, sitting next to each other in a missionary's study guide, both quietly pointing at the same tree.

## V. The Fruit Is Christ Himself

If the tree is the love of God, and the fruit is what one finally plucks from the tree, then the fruit is the love of God *received*. And the love of God in 1 Nephi 11 is not abstract. Verse 21 shows the Lamb. Verse 22 names the love. Verse 24 shows the Son of God going forth among men. The angel does not separate the Lamb from the love. They are shown together, asked about together, answered together.

John writes it as a definition: "God is love" ([1 John 4:8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/1-jn/4?lang=eng&id=p8#p8)). Christ does not merely teach about God's love or convey it as a message; He is the love, embodied. The condescension Nephi sees in chapter 11 is what God's love looks like when it takes flesh.

So the fruit of the tree is the love of God, and the love of God is Christ Himself. The two readings are not in tension. The fruit is the experience of God's love (subjective: "most joyous to the soul"); the fruit is Christ in the flesh (objective: "the Lamb of God going forth among the children of men"). The text holds both at once because both are true at once. To partake of the fruit is to receive Christ. The hunger no more, the thirst no more, of Alma 32:42 is the hunger no more of John 6:35. Same fruit. Same satisfaction. Same Person.

This is what the chain has been heading toward all along. Faith is the seed planted in the heart. Patience is the long work of nourishing it. Hope is the eye fixed on the fruit while the tree grows. And charity, the greatest of the three, the "pure love of Christ" Mormon names ([Moroni 7:47](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/7?lang=eng&id=p47#p47)), is what you become *after* you have eaten. Once the fruit is in you, the love is in you. You shed it abroad as it has been shed in you.

The chain in [1 Corinthians 13:13](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/1-cor/13?lang=eng&id=p13#p13), [Moroni 7:40-48](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/7?lang=eng&id=p40-p48#p40), and Romans 5 is the same chain because it ends at the same place. Charity is greatest because charity is the fruit. Charity is the love of God shed abroad. Charity is what the tree gives.

The synthesis here, that the faith-hope-charity chain *is* the tree of life, is not stated by any single verse. No prophet writes "the seed is faith, the tree is hope, the fruit is charity." The mapping is built by reading the two systems against each other and noticing they share vocabulary, motion, and end. That makes this a reading, not a doctrine. The reading is honest because both passages independently say what they say; what is new is putting them side by side.

## VI. Becoming

Michael, you teach Sunday School. Every Sunday you stand in front of a class with a portion of the word and offer it.

The fruit is Christ. The teaching is the offering of the fruit. You are not the source of the fruit, and the class is not coming to you to be filled by you. You are pointing at the tree.

But here is what 1 Nephi 8 says about Lehi the moment he tasted: "I began to be desirous that my family should partake of it also; for I knew that it was desirable above all other fruit" ([1 Nephi 8:12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/8?lang=eng&id=p12#p12)). The taste creates the reaching. The eating produces the sharing. If you have not tasted, you cannot beckon. If you have tasted, you cannot help beckoning.

So the order of operations matters. Before the lesson, eat the fruit. Read the verse not to find the teaching point but to be filled. Sit with the love of God shed abroad in your own heart before you stand in front of anyone else's. The love you offer the class is the love you have received. There is no other supply.

And when the chain shows up in your life, in the tribulation and the patience and the experience and the hope, recognize what is happening. The Lord is growing the tree in you. Patience is not a delay before the fruit; patience is part of how the tree grows. The hope you feel in the middle of waiting is not a consolation prize; it is the chain working. And the love that arrives at the end of the chain is Christ Himself, made present, in you.

The fruit is real. It is most sweet, white above all that is white, and it fills the soul. Eat it. Then beckon.
