# Without Compulsion

> *"He wanted us to choose Him freely, to choose light, truth, and goodness without compulsion."*
> — [Celebrating the Founding of the United States and the U.S. Constitution](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/celebrating-freedom-and-agency/01?lang=eng)

> *"Now they did not sin ignorantly, for they knew the will of God concerning them, for it had been taught unto them; therefore they did wilfully rebel against God."*
> — [3 Nephi 6:18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/6?lang=eng&id=p18#p18)

**Catalyst:** A 5th-Sunday lesson on freedom and agency (*Celebrating Freedom and Agency*, lesson 01, with Elder Oaks's April 2021 talk), pressed against a harder question: from Abel's altar to our own data centers, what does the difference between a freely-given offering and a compelled one reveal — and are we standing where the Nephites stood in 3 Nephi 6?

**Binding question:** Why is *without compulsion* the heart of freedom — from Abel's offering to Oaks's account of agency — and what does it ask of a prosperous, infrastructure-building people who have been taught?

**Sub-threads:**
- Why was Abel's offering accepted and Cain's rejected, and what does Cain's turn to compulsion (Master Mahan, the secret oath, murder for gain) reveal about the inversion of agency?
- What does AI — which *acts* but cannot offer a sacrifice or bear moral agency — show us by contrast about the human stakes?
- Is the 3 Nephi 6 pattern (equity → prosperity → pride → infrastructure → compulsion → loss of liberty → willful rebellion) a type for us now — and how do we read that *without date-setting*?

---

## Provisional outline (Phase 1 — to be filled)

I. **Two offerings.** Cain and Abel — the first sacrifice. Abel's in faith ("the similitude of the Only Begotten," Moses 5:7); Cain's in his own way, rejected. Why.
II. **Cain's inversion.** From a rejected offering to compulsion: Master Mahan, the secret oath, murder for gain (Moses 5:29–33, "I am free"). Secret combinations as the engine that inverts a free offering into forced tribute (→ Gadianton; Ether 8:25, "overthrow the freedom of all lands").
III. **Why God will not compel.** The freedom doctrine: war in heaven over agency (Moses 4:1–3), priesthood only by persuasion (D&C 121:41–42), Joseph F. Smith's "no coercion," the *Celebrating Freedom and Agency* materials, and Oaks on agency / freedom / the Constitution. [Builds on what-was-the-atonement-for.md §III.]
IV. **What AI cannot offer.** Moral agency by contrast — AI acts within its placed law but cannot choose God or the devil, cannot sacrifice, cannot bear accountability (book Ch 0/Ch 4; agency.md). AI can build the infrastructure; it cannot carry the moral weight.
V. **The 3 Nephi 6 mirror — written for our day.** equity (6:4) → prosperity → pride/ranks (6:12) → **highways and infrastructure (6:7–8)** → compulsion / secret combination to unseat liberty (6:27–30) → willful rebellion (6:18) → collapse into tribes (ch 7). Bednar's *In the Space of Not Many Years* (Oct 2024), citing Benson, gives the warrant: the Book of Mormon was *written for our day* — and prosperity → pride → collapse comes fast. The date-stamp discipline still holds: see the type, don't set the date. [Builds on what-was-the-atonement-for.md §I, V.]
VI. **Whose blood?** Abel's blood only cried (Moses 5:35); Christ's "speaketh better things than that of Abel" ([Hebrews 12:24](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/heb/12?lang=eng&id=p24#p24)). "Redeemed the land by the shedding of blood" ([D&C 101:80](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/101?lang=eng&id=p80#p80)) — the founders were instruments God raised up, not redeemers; fallen man cannot redeem himself. The deepest freedom was bought at Gethsemane and Calvary, not only at Lexington and Yorktown. Humbles civil religion under Christ.
VII. **The uncomfortable application — us, now.** A prosperous, infrastructure-building (AI, data centers) America; the creep of compulsion (forcing others to pay, secret combinations, boasting in our own strength, surrendering agency to what cannot hold it); Oaks's warning. Faithful, not partisan; pattern, not date.
VIII. **Becoming** (and the lesson frame).
