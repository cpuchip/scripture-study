# The Language of Adam — Speculative Threads

*An exploratory study threading revealed Adamic vocabulary with global etymology research. This is play, not proof. It names its uncertainty and invites exploration, not conclusions.*

**Companion to:** [The Language of Adam](language-of-adam.md) — which catalogs the revealed vocabulary and its scriptural context. This study takes that foundation and asks: do any of those fragments show up elsewhere?

---

## The Question

If God confounded one language into many at Babel — and Webster 1828 defines "confound" as "to mingle and blend, so that different elements cannot be distinguished" — then the original language wasn't *destroyed*. It was *scattered*. Mingled into every tongue on earth, so thoroughly blended that the original can't be reassembled.

But what if fragments survived the blending? Not full sentences. Not grammar. Just... echoes. A root here. A sound-meaning pair there. The skeleton beneath every language on earth.

We have two unusual data sources to work with:

1. **Revealed Adamic words** — roughly 20 terms preserved by revelation, not linguistic descent ([Moses 6:57](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/6?lang=eng&id=p57#p57); [Abraham 3:13](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/abr/3?lang=eng&id=p13#p13); [D&C 95:17](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/95?lang=eng&id=p17#p17); [Ether 2:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/ether/2?lang=eng&id=p3#p3))
2. **Global etymologies** — a small set of words that linguists have proposed might predate all known language families

This study lays them side by side and asks what happens.

### The "Sample of Pure Language" (ca. March 1832)

A Q&A document dictated by Joseph Smith, transcribed by John Whitmer. Three manuscript copies exist (Whitmer's original plus two discovered by Brent Metcalfe). The spelling evolved through several stages: *Awmen → Awman → Ah Man → Ahman* (standardized in the 1835 D&C).

The document's most striking feature: Joseph appeared to believe that English itself carried Adamic fragments. "Sons" Ahman. "Anglo-man" for angels — as though *angls* and *man* were recognizable Adamic roots that English had inherited. Whether this is prophetic insight or folk etymology is exactly the kind of question this study is designed to hold without resolving.

### The Global Etymologies

In 1994, linguists John Bengtson and Merritt Ruhlen published "Global Etymologies" — 27 proposed roots that appear across ALL major language families (Afro-Asiatic, Indo-European, Sino-Tibetan, Austronesian, Niger-Congo, Amerindian, Australian, and others). These are controversial — most historical linguists are skeptical — but they represent the most systematic attempt to identify words that might predate the diversification of all known languages.

Selected roots most relevant to our question:

| Proto-World Root | Meaning | Distribution |
|---|---|---|
| **ku** | who | Cross-family (Latin *quis*, Mandarin *shei*, etc.) |
| **ma** | what | Nearly universal |
| **pal** | two | Appears on all inhabited continents |
| **tik** | finger, one | Afro-Asiatic, Sino-Tibetan, Amerindian |
| **aq'wa** | water | Latin *aqua*, Japanese *aka*, Quechua *yaku* |
| **mana** | stay | Cross-family |
| **maliq'a** | suck, nurse, breast | Cross-family |
| **kuna** | woman | Cross-family |
| **bur** | ashes, dust | Cross-family |

(Full list: Bengtson & Ruhlen, "Global Etymologies," in *On the Origins of Languages: Studies in Linguistic Taxonomy*, Stanford University Press, 1994.)

## II. Threading the Needle

### Thread 1: Kokob — The Strongest Case

**Revealed:** Kokob = star ([Abraham 3:13](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/abr/3?lang=eng&id=p13#p13))
**Hebrew:** Kochav (כוכב) = star
**Arabic:** Kawkab (كوكب) = star, planet

This is the clearest case we have. The Adamic word and the Semitic cognate family are virtually identical — *kokob*, *kochav*, *kawkab*. The sound-meaning pair has survived for millennia across multiple languages.

The open question: Was Abraham being taught the *Adamic* word, or just the Semitic word he already knew sotto voce? Abraham 3 says the knowledge was communicated via the Urim and Thummim ([Abraham 3:1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/abr/3?lang=eng&id=p1#p1)). If the terms are genuinely Adamic — pre-Babel vocabulary — then Hebrew and Arabic "star" is a direct fragment of the original language that survived the confounding intact.

**Kolob** may extend this. Scholar John Tvedtnes connected Kolob to Arabic *qalb* (قلب), meaning "heart" or "center." Kolob as "the heart/center" — the governing star nearest God's throne. If the Adamic root for "star-center" gave us both *Kolob* (via revelation) and *qalb* (via Arabic), we're seeing the same fragment from two directions.

**Confidence level: High.** This is real Semitic linguistics, not speculation.

### Thread 2: Ahman — Suggestive but Unproven

**Revealed:** Ahman = God; "the being which made all things in all its parts" ([D&C 78:20](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/78?lang=eng&id=p20#p20))
**Egyptian:** Amon/Amun = the hidden god, king of the gods
**Hebrew:** Amen (אָמֵן) = from *aman*, to strengthen, confirm, be faithful
**Arabic:** Aman (أمان) = safety, security, trust

Hugh Nibley connected Ahman to the Egyptian Amon/Amun. The phonetic similarity is real. And there's something theologically resonant about it: if Ahman was the original name for God, and Amon was what Egypt remembered of it (dimly, as "the hidden god"), that would fit the confounding pattern — fragments preserved, but with the meaning blurred.

But here's the honest counterpoint: Hebrew *amen* has a well-established independent etymology. It comes from the root א-מ-נ (*aman*), meaning "to be firm, reliable." The connection to Ahman could be coincidence. Phonetic similarity across languages is the number one trap in amateur etymology. Languages evolve independently into similar sounds all the time.

**Confidence level: Suggestive.** Worth holding. Not worth building on.

### Thread 3: "Man" — The Root Joseph Saw

In the 1832 "Sample of Pure Language," the root *man* appears everywhere:
- Ah-**man** (God)
- Son Ah-**man** (Christ)
- Sons Ah-**man** (humans)
- Anglo-**man** (angels)

Joseph appears to have seen *man* as an Adamic root meaning "being" or "intelligent being." This tracks with [Moses 6:57](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/6?lang=eng&id=p57#p57): "**Man** of Holiness is his name" — where *man* isn't gendered but categorical. God is a Man. Christ is the Son of Man. We are sons and daughters of a Man.

Cross-linguistically: English *man* comes from Proto-Germanic \**mann-*, from Proto-Indo-European \**mon-* (human being). Sanskrit has *manu* — the first man, ancestor of all humanity. German *Mann*. This root is widespread... but only within Indo-European languages. It's NOT one of Bengtson and Ruhlen's global etymologies. It doesn't appear in Sino-Tibetan, Niger-Congo, or Austronesian languages.

So was Joseph right that English *man* is Adamic? Indo-European languages cover a huge swath of humanity, but not all of it. This is either an Adamic fragment that survived primarily in one language family, or Joseph was reading theological meaning into an English word. Both readings are available. Neither is provable.

**Confidence level: Theologically rich, linguistically weak.** The *meaning* Joseph attached to "man" — an ontological category, not a gender — is profound. Whether the *word itself* is Adamic is uncertain.

### Thread 4: Deseret — The Jaredite Transmission

**Revealed:** Deseret = honey bee ([Ether 2:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/ether/2?lang=eng&id=p3#p3))

This word occupies a unique position. The Jaredites' language was specifically *not* confounded at Babel ([Ether 1:35](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/ether/1?lang=eng&id=p35#p35)). If anyone carried the genuine, un-mingled Adamic language into the post-Babel world, it was them. "Deseret" may be one of the purest Adamic words we have — arriving in the Book of Mormon through a transmission line that bypassed the confounding entirely.

The Egyptian connection: *dšr.t* in Egyptian means something like "the red one" (the red crown of Lower Egypt). More intriguingly, the bee was the symbol of Lower Egypt — the hieroglyph for "bee" was part of the royal title. Nibley and others have noted the overlap: an Adamic/Jaredite word for "bee" that echoes the Egyptian language where bees also held special symbolic significance.

This is one of the more tantalizing cases because the Jaredite transmission is explicitly un-confounded. If *deseret* genuinely survived in recognizable form in Egyptian, it's not a fragment that resisted the blending — it's a word that traveled a different route entirely.

**Confidence level: Moderate.** The Jaredite transmission line is scripturally established. The Egyptian parallel is noted by scholars. But phonetic similarity between *deseret* and *dšr.t* could still be coincidental.

### Thread 5: Sabaoth — A Definition That Changed

**Revealed:** "Lord of Sabaoth, which is by interpretation, the creator of the first day, the beginning and the end" ([D&C 95:7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/95?lang=eng&id=p7#p7))
**Hebrew:** Tseva'ot (צְבָאוֹת) = hosts, armies — "Lord of Hosts"

The D&C definition is *creation-focused*: the creator of the first day, the beginning and the end. The Hebrew meaning is *military*: the Lord of Hosts (armies). These are not the same meaning.

Speculative thread: If the Adamic "Sabaoth" meant "creator/initiator/beginning," and the Hebrew inherited the *sound* but shifted the *meaning* toward "hosts/armies," we're watching the confounding in real time. Not a change in phonetics, but in semantics. The word survived; the meaning drifted.

This fits the "mingle and blend" model of confounding. The sounds persist. The meanings scatter.

**Confidence level: Speculative but structurally interesting.** The gap between the D&C definition and the Hebrew meaning is a real textual observation. The explanation is speculative.

### Thread 6: The Mama/Papa Phenomenon

In virtually every language on earth, words for "mother" and "father" sound like *mama* and *papa* (or close variations: *ama*, *baba*, *tata*).

The mainstream linguistic explanation: these aren't cognates at all. Babies produce bilabial consonants (/m/, /p/, /b/) and the open vowel /a/ first because they're the easiest sounds to make. Parents hear baby babble and assign meaning. The universality comes from physiology, not from a common ancestor language.

This is probably mostly right. But a faith-informed observer can ask the next question: *why* are those the easiest sounds? If God designed human bodies to produce language, and if the first language was given by God, is it possible that the Adamic words for the most fundamental relationships were grounded in the most fundamental sounds? Not coincidence, but *design* — the architecture of the mouth reflecting the architecture of the language.

This is untestable. It's the kind of thought that belongs in a study labeled "speculative." But it's worth sitting with.

**Confidence level: Pure speculation.** Honest about it.

## III. What the World Says

Merritt Ruhlen and Joseph Greenberg spent careers arguing for linguistic monogenesis — the theory that all human languages descend from a single proto-language. Their 27 global etymologies are the core evidence. The scholarly establishment has been mostly skeptical: the time depth involved (~100,000+ years since language began) is so vast that any original signal should have decayed beyond recognition.

But the Restoration offers a different timeline. The confounding at Babel is recent — roughly 2200 BC in traditional chronology. If global etymologies exist, they don't need to have survived 100,000 years. They need to have survived ~4,200 years. That's still a stretch by rigorous linguistic standards (the comparative method is reliable to about 6,000-8,000 years), but it's not impossible.

The irony: Ruhlen's controversial "proto-world" hypothesis, which the scholarly establishment treats with skepticism, maps more cleanly onto the Babel narrative than it does onto mainstream evolutionary linguistics. A single ancestral language → catastrophic diversification → fragments surviving in all descendant languages. That's... exactly what the scriptures describe.

This doesn't prove Ruhlen right. His methodology has real problems. But it's interesting that the *shape* of his hypothesis mirrors a story we already know.

## IV. Honest Assessment

**What's real:**
- The revealed Adamic vocabulary exists in scripture. These are canonical data points.
- The "Sample of Pure Language" is a real historical document, attested in the Joseph Smith Papers.
- Kokob/Kochav/Kawkab is a genuine cognate family — this is standard Semitic linguistics.
- The 27 global etymologies are a real (if controversial) scholarly proposal from credentialed linguists.
- Deseret's Egyptian parallels have been noted by serious scholars.

**What's pattern-matching:**
- Ahman → Amon → Amen → a single Adamic root. Possible, but phonetic similarity ≠ genetic relationship.
- "Man" as a global Adamic root. It's Indo-European, not demonstrated to be universal.
- Any direct comparison between global etymologies and revealed Adamic words — the datasets are too small (27 roots vs. ~20 words) for meaningful statistical comparison.

**What this study can't do:**
- Prove that any revealed word is genuinely "Adamic" in a linguistic sense.
- Demonstrate that global etymologies derive from the Adamic language.
- Reconstruct the Adamic language from fragments.

**What it *can* do:**
- Notice that the *pattern* predicted by the Babel narrative (one language → catastrophic diversification → fragments remaining) is exactly the pattern that a controversial branch of linguistics claims to have found.
- Observe that at least one revealed Adamic word (Kokob) demonstrably belongs to a real cognate family.
- Ask honest questions about whether other connections are real or imagined.

## V. The Language Beneath Languages

Zephaniah promises:

> "For then will I turn to the people a pure language, that they may all call upon the name of the Lord, to serve him with one consent."
> — [Zephaniah 3:9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/zeph/3?lang=eng&id=p9#p9)

The confounding was a scattering. The restoration will be a gathering — of people, of truth, and perhaps of language itself. If words like *kokob* survived the scattering recognizably, and if words like *Ahman* echo across language families even dimly, they might be the first notes of a melody that will one day be heard whole.

The existing [Language of Adam study](language-of-adam.md) traces the arc: a pure language given → confounded at Babel → preserved by Jaredites → revealed in fragments through prophets → promised to be restored. This speculative companion asks whether the *middle section* — the confounded period we're living in — might itself contain more of the original than we think.

Twenty words aren't enough to reconstruct a language. But they might be enough to recognize a family resemblance.

## Becoming

This study is speculative, and that's the point. Not every question needs an answer in order to be worth asking.

What struck me most isn't the linguistic data — it's the theological implication. If the confounding "mingled" one language into many, then every language on earth carries something of the original. Every tongue that praises God is doing so, dimly, in fragments of the language God first gave Adam. When the Korean speaker says *eomma* and the Quechua speaker says *mama* and the English speaker says *mother*, they may all be reaching for the same word through different phonetic histories — and the reason babies reach for it first might be that it's the most fundamental thing a human voice can say.

That's not a conclusion. It's a way of listening.

The practical takeaway is simpler: pay attention to words. Not just definitions — *histories*. When Zephaniah says God will "turn to the people a pure language," that restoration will mean something. And we might already be holding more of it than we realize.
