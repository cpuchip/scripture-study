# Alma 6 — After the Word, the Order: The Church Cleansed and Set in Order

*(Short administrative bridge — Alma ordains leaders, the repentant are received and the unrepentant prideful removed; the word is liberal to all; the church fasts for the unconverted; Alma departs to Gideon. ~83 BC. 8 verses; note-grade.)*

**Binding idea:** The searching sermon (ch 5) is over; now comes the ordering. What does Alma *do* after the word has done its work — and how does the church divide?

**Read:** After the sermon, Alma "**ordained priests and elders, by laying on his hands according to the order of God**, to preside and watch over the church" (v1). "Whosoever did not belong to the church who **repented**... were baptized... and were received" (v2); but "whosoever did belong to the church that did not repent... those who were lifted up in the **pride** of their hearts—the same were rejected, and their **names were blotted out**" (v3). "Thus they began to establish the order of the church" (v4). "The **word of God was liberal unto all**, that none were deprived of the privilege of assembling... to hear the word of God" (v5); but the children of God were commanded to "gather themselves together oft, and join in **fasting and mighty prayer in behalf of the welfare of the souls of those who knew not God**" (v6). Alma departs to the valley of Gideon — named for the man "slain by the hand of Nehor" (v7) — and declares the word "according to the **testimony of Jesus Christ, the Son of God, who should come to redeem his people from their sins**" (v8).

**Thoughts:**
- *Word first, then order (vv1-4).* The sequence is the point. Alma bore pure testimony (ch 5) to reach the heart; *now* he sets the institution in order — ordaining leaders by the laying on of hands, receiving the repentant, removing the unrepentant. The word and the structure work together: testimony convicts the individual heart; order regulates the body. Neither alone suffices — a church with only fervor dissolves into contention; a church with only structure has no life. Alma does both, in that order: he preached the change, then he organized around those who accepted it. And the ordering fulfills the sermon's own closing charge — "come ye out from the wicked, and be ye separate" (5:57).
- *The sermon was the sieve (vv2-3).* The cleansing of the church is not Alma imposing a verdict; it follows the people's own *response* to the call. Those who repented — members or not — are received; those "lifted up in the pride of their hearts" who would not humble themselves "were rejected, and their names were blotted out." The dividing line is each person's answer to the searching questions. Church discipline here is not arbitrary expulsion but the visible registration of a choice the people made when the word was preached. (The Mosiah 26 pattern: the repentant numbered, the unrepentant blotted out — discipline triggered by, and aimed at, repentance.)
- *"The word of God was liberal unto all" (vv5-6).* Two complementary notes close the order. First, *access* is universal — "none were deprived of the privilege of assembling... to hear the word"; the word is freely available to everyone, member or not (the anti-priestcraft principle — the word is not a commodity withheld behind a paid clergy, but liberal to all). Second, the church's posture turns *outward*: they are commanded to fast and pray "in behalf of the welfare of the souls of those who knew not God." The covenant community does not gather only for itself; it intercedes for the unconverted — the missionary-anguish of Mosiah 25:11 and 28:3 made a standing practice. A healthy church is open-doored and outward-praying.
- *The order of God vs. priestcraft (vv1, 7-8).* The chapter quietly answers chapter 1. Leaders are ordained "according to the order of God" by the laying on of hands — proper authority, conferred, not seized; the structural opposite of Nehor's self-appointed, money-taking priestcraft. And Alma goes next to the *valley of Gideon* — named for the very man Nehor murdered — to preach "the testimony of Jesus Christ... who should come to redeem his people." The true order, the true authority, and the true Christ are being re-established precisely where the false priestcraft had drawn blood.

**Notable / flag:** No spin-off (8-verse bridge). The pattern is **word first, then order**: after the searching sermon (ch 5) reaches the heart, Alma sets the church in order — ordaining by the laying on of hands, receiving the repentant, removing the prideful (names blotted out). The cleansing is **by the people's own response to the sermon**, not an arbitrary verdict — the sermon was the sieve. The **word is "liberal unto all"** (open access, anti-priestcraft, v5), and the church turns **outward in fasting and prayer for the unconverted** (v6 — the missionary-anguish of Mosiah 25/28 made a standing practice). And the true **order of God** (ordination by laying on of hands) is re-established precisely in **Gideon's valley** — named for Nehor's victim — answering the priestcraft of ch 1 where it drew blood.
