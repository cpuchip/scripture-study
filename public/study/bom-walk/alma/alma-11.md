# Alma 11 — Not Saved IN Their Sins: Amulek, the Bribe, and the Resurrection

*(The Nephite coinage; Zeezrom's bribe exposed; Christ saves *from* sin, not *in* it; the Son is the very Eternal Father; the resurrection restores the perfect body, universal and permanent; Zeezrom begins to tremble. ~82 BC. Mini-study.)*

**Binding idea:** Zeezrom, the city's foremost lawyer, comes to trap Amulek with money and clever questions. Instead the questions become the occasion for Amulek's great testimony — and the lawyer who came to destroy ends the chapter trembling. What is the distinction Amulek draws that the whole Nehor heresy denies, and how does the briber expose himself?

**Read:** The judges are paid by time — a senine of gold or senum of silver per day (vv1-3); the Nephite coinage is laid out (vv4-19). "Now, it was for the **sole purpose to get gain**... therefore, they did **stir up the people to riotings**... that they might have more employ, that they might get money according to the suits" (v20). Zeezrom, "expert in the devices of the devil," offers: "here are **six onties of silver**, and all these will I give thee if thou wilt **deny the existence of a Supreme Being**" (v22). Amulek: "O thou **child of hell**, why tempt ye me? Knowest thou that the righteous yieldeth to no such temptations?" (v23); "thou knowest that there is a God, but thou **lovest that lucre more than him**" (v24); "thou hadst it in thy heart to retain them from me; and it was only thy desire that I should deny the true and living God, **that thou mightest have cause to destroy me**" (v25). "Is there more than one God? ... No" (vv28-29); "How knowest thou? An angel hath made them known unto me" (vv30-31); "Is it the Son of God? Yea" (vv32-33). "**Shall he save his people in their sins?** ... I say unto you he shall **not**, for it is impossible for him to deny his word" (v34). "He cannot save them in their sins... he hath said that **no unclean thing can inherit the kingdom of heaven**; therefore, how can ye be saved, except ye inherit the kingdom of heaven? Therefore, ye **cannot be saved in your sins**" (v37). "Is the Son of God the very Eternal Father? ... **Yea, he is the very Eternal Father of heaven and of earth**... the beginning and the end, the first and the last" (vv38-39); "he shall **take upon him the transgressions of those who believe on his name**... and salvation cometh to none else" (v40); "the **wicked remain as though there had been no redemption made, except it be the loosing of the bands of death**" (v41). "The **spirit and the body shall be reunited again in its perfect form**; both limb and joint shall be restored... knowing even as we know now, and have a **bright recollection of all our guilt**" (v43); "this restoration shall come to **all**... there shall not so much as a hair of their heads be lost" (v44); "this mortal body is raised to an immortal body... that they can **die no more**" (v45). "The people began again to be astonished, and also **Zeezrom began to tremble**" (v46).

**Thoughts:**
- *Saved FROM sin, not IN it — the distinction the whole Nehor heresy denies (vv34-37).* This is the doctrinal hinge, and it refutes the comfortable gospel of Ammonihah at its root. Zeezrom asks, "Shall he save his people *in* their sins?" — and Amulek's answer is flat: "he shall *not*." Christ saves people *from* their sins, never *in* them. The reasoning is airtight: salvation means inheriting the kingdom of heaven; "no unclean thing can inherit the kingdom"; therefore one cannot be saved while remaining in (and unclean from) one's sins. Salvation is not being *excused* while staying dirty; it is being *cleansed*. This destroys Nehor's "all men shall have eternal life" (Alma 1:4) and every version of the false gospel that promises heaven without change. The Atonement does not lower the kingdom's standard to admit the unclean; it makes the unclean clean so they can enter. To want to be "saved in your sins" is to want a contradiction — a kingdom of heaven with uncleanness in it, which would no longer be the kingdom of heaven.
- *The bribe exposes the briber (vv21-25).* Zeezrom offers six onties of silver — a substantial sum — for Amulek to deny God. And Amulek sees through it completely: it was never a real offer. "Thou hadst it in thy heart to *retain* them from me; and it was only thy desire that I should deny the true and living God, **that thou mightest have cause to destroy me**" (v25). The money was bait to manufacture a charge (a recorded denial of God) they could use to kill him; Zeezrom never intended to pay. And the deepest exposure: "thou knowest that there is a God, but thou **lovest that lucre more than him**" (v24). The briber is not an atheist; he *knows* there is a God and would pay a man to deny what they both know — because he loves money more than the God he knows is real. The attempt to buy a denial reveals that the buyer believes the thing he's paying to have denied. And against it, the simple wall of integrity: "the righteous yieldeth to no such temptations" — there are men whose testimony is not for sale at any price, and the offer itself insults them.
- *Lawyers who manufacture conflict for fees (v20).* The economic engine of the corruption, stated plainly: the lawyers were paid per suit, "for the sole purpose to get gain... therefore, they did **stir up the people to riotings**... that they might have more employ." Those whose income depends on disputes have an incentive to *multiply* disputes — so they sow discord to generate fees. It is the most cynical institutional corruption: a profession that profits from the very conflict it exists to resolve, and therefore foments it. (This is why their "unrighteousness" is the foundation of the people's destruction, 10:27 — they have a financial stake in the society's strife.)
- *"The very Eternal Father" — the condescension affirmed under pressure (vv38-40).* Zeezrom's hardest trap question — "Is the Son of God the very Eternal Father?" — is meant to catch Amulek in a contradiction (he just said there is one God; now is the Son also the Father?). Amulek does not dodge: "**Yea, he is the very Eternal Father of heaven and of earth**... the beginning and the end, the first and the last." This is the same Father-Son condescension Christology as Abinadi (Mosiah 15:4) and Benjamin's angel (Mosiah 3:8) — Christ is the Eternal Father in the sense of *Creator* and the One in whose name salvation comes, while being the Son in the flesh. Amulek meets the trap by affirming the mystery plainly rather than retreating from it. The consistent Nephite Christology holds under hostile cross-examination.
- *The resurrection — perfect, universal, permanent (vv42-45).* Amulek gives one of the Book of Mormon's clearest resurrection statements. It is *bodily and perfect*: "the spirit and the body shall be reunited again in its **perfect form**; both limb and joint shall be restored... not so much as a hair of their heads be lost." It is *universal*: "all, both old and young, both bond and free, both male and female, both the wicked and the righteous." It is *permanent*: "raised to an immortal body... that they can **die no more**... never to be divided." And it carries *memory*: "knowing even as we know now, and have a **bright recollection of all our guilt**" — the unrepentant rise carrying the vivid memory of every sin, the recollection itself a torment (cf 2 Ne 9:14; Alma 5:18). The resurrection is the great equalizer (everyone rises) and the great revealer (everyone remembers). And it sets the two-tier truth (v41): the bands of death are loosed for *all*, but beyond that universal resurrection "the wicked remain as though there had been no redemption made" — universal resurrection, conditional salvation (Mosiah 16:5).
- *Zeezrom begins to tremble (v46).* The chapter ends with the first crack in the hardest man in the room. The corrupt lawyer who came to trap and bribe and destroy "began to tremble" — not yet converted, but no longer untouched. The Spirit has reached the man who *knew* there was a God and loved lucre more, and the knowing is beginning to overpower the loving. (Zeezrom's turn — from foremost accuser to trembling to converted disciple, chs 12-15 — is one of the record's great conversions, and it starts here, with a trembling he cannot suppress.)

**Notable / flag:** No spin-off (the doctrinal high-point of the Ammonihah mission is the **12-13** discourse, a flagged spin-off candidate; this chapter is dense but handled inline). The hinge doctrine is **saved FROM sin, not IN it** (vv34-37): Christ "shall not" save people in their sins, because "no unclean thing can inherit the kingdom" — salvation is *cleansing*, not *excusing*, which destroys Nehor's "all men shall have eternal life" at the root (the Atonement makes the unclean clean, not the kingdom unclean). **The bribe exposes the briber** — Zeezrom would pay six onties to make Amulek deny a God Zeezrom *knows* is real ("thou lovest that lucre more than him," v24), the offer a trap never meant to be paid; integrity isn't for sale. The corrupt **lawyers manufacture conflict for fees** (v20 — paid per suit, so they multiply disputes). Amulek affirms the **Son as "the very Eternal Father"** under hostile cross-examination (vv38-39, the consistent Abinadi/Benjamin Christology). And he gives one of the clearest **resurrection** statements — perfect ("not a hair lost"), universal ("all... wicked and righteous"), permanent ("die no more"), carrying "**a bright recollection of all our guilt**" (v43) — with the two-tier truth that resurrection is universal but salvation conditional (v41, Mosiah 16:5). It ends with the first crack in the hardest man: **Zeezrom began to tremble** (v46) — the great conversion begins.
