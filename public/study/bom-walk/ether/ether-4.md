# Ether 4 — the greater things wait on the greater faith; "rend that veil of unbelief"

**Binding idea:** Moroni has written and sealed up "the very things which the brother of Jared saw" — "there never were greater things made manifest than those which were made manifest unto the brother of Jared" (4:4) — with the interpreters sealed alongside (4:5). The sealed portion will not come "unto the Gentiles until the day that they shall repent of their iniquity, and become clean before the Lord. And in that day that they shall exercise faith in me... even as the brother of Jared did... then will I manifest unto them the things which the brother of Jared saw, even to the unfolding unto them all my revelations, saith Jesus Christ, the Son of God, the Father of the heavens and of the earth" (4:6-7). And Christ's direct call: "Come unto me, O ye Gentiles... Come unto me, O ye house of Israel... when ye shall *rend that veil of unbelief* which doth cause you to remain in your awful state of wickedness, and hardness of heart, and blindness of mind, then shall the great and marvelous things which have been hid up from the foundation of the world... be made manifest" (4:13-15). "I am the Father, I am the light, and the life, and the truth of the world" (4:12).

**Read:** Ether 4 (full, verbatim via gospel_get). 19 verses. The conditions on which the sealed record comes forth, and Christ's call to rend the veil.

## Thoughts

This chapter completes the thought begun in chapter 3 and turns it directly on the reader. There, the brother of Jared's faith became so perfect that the veil "could not hold" him — he passed into knowledge and saw the Lord. Here, Moroni and Christ explain what that means for *us*: the brother of Jared's full vision — "the very things which the brother of Jared saw," the *greatest* things ever made manifest to any man — is sealed up, and the condition for its release is that we attain the same faith he had. "In that day that they shall exercise faith in me... even as the brother of Jared did... then will I manifest unto them the things which the brother of Jared saw, even to the unfolding unto them all my revelations" (4:7). The greater things are not withheld arbitrarily; they wait on a *capacity* — the capacity that perfect faith creates. The sealed portion of the plates is not locked by God's reluctance but by our unreadiness. When a people's faith becomes what the brother of Jared's was, the seal comes off. The two-thirds of the plates Joseph Smith could not translate (2 Ne 27) remain sealed not because God hoards them but because no one has yet exercised faith enough to receive them. The greater revelation waits on the greater faith.

And the speaker names Himself, again, in terms the *is-jesus-christ-god* question rests on: "saith Jesus Christ, the Son of God, *the Father of the heavens and of the earth, and all things that in them are*" (4:7). Christ is the Son of God and the Father of heaven and earth — the Creator. The same doubled identity as Ether 3:14 ("I am the Father and the Son") and Mosiah 15: the Son in His relationship to the Father, and the Father in His role as Creator and as the one who bears the Father's name and authority. And it intensifies in verse 12, in one of the great "I AM" declarations of the Book of Mormon: "he that will not believe my words will not believe me—that I am; and he that will not believe me will not believe the Father who sent me. For behold, *I am the Father, I am the light, and the life, and the truth of the world*." This gathers up the great Johannine "I am" sayings — "I am the light of the world" (John 8:12), "I am the way, the truth, and the life" (John 14:6), "before Abraham was, I am" (John 8:58) — and puts them in the mouth of the premortal Christ speaking to the brother of Jared and through Moroni to us. (Another datapoint for the study: Christ's self-identification as "the Father... the light, and the life, and the truth of the world," and the explicit "me—that I am.")

Verses 11-12 also state, in Christ's own voice, the principle Moroni will expand in Moroni 7: "he that believeth these things which I have spoken, him will I visit with the manifestations of my Spirit, and he shall know and bear record. For because of my Spirit he shall know that these things are true; for it persuadeth men to do good. And whatsoever thing persuadeth men to do good is of me; for good cometh of none save it be of me." This is the test the whole Book of Mormon hands the reader, given here by Christ Himself: the Spirit confirms truth by *persuading to good*, and whatever persuades to good is of God. It is the same logic as Moroni 7:16-17 and Moroni 10:4-5 — the witness of truth is inseparable from the pull toward goodness. A revelation that draws you toward good is of Christ; "good cometh of none save it be of me." This gives the reader a working criterion: not abstract proof, but the direction a thing pulls the soul. The Book of Mormon's claim to truth and its claim to goodness are the same claim, because the God who is true is "the same that leadeth men to all good."

But the heart of the chapter is the image in verses 13-15: "rend that veil of unbelief." Christ calls twice — "Come unto me, O ye Gentiles... Come unto me, O ye house of Israel" — and tells us why the greater things have not reached us: "it hath not come unto you, *because of unbelief*." And then the central instruction: "when ye shall rend that veil of unbelief which doth cause you to remain in your awful state of wickedness, and hardness of heart, and blindness of mind, then shall the great and marvelous things which have been hid up from the foundation of the world... be made manifest." This is the doctrine of Ether 3:19-20 turned into a commandment to the reader. There, we learned that the veil is a function of unbelief — that the brother of Jared's perfect faith dissolved it. Here, Christ tells us the veil between us and the greater things is *our own* unbelief, and the verb He uses is active: *rend* it. The veil is not something God places to keep us out; it is something we maintain, and we are commanded to tear it down. And the means are named precisely: "when ye shall call upon the Father in my name, with a broken heart and a contrite spirit" (4:15) — the same broken heart and contrite spirit that is the only sacrifice Christ asks (3 Ne 9:20). The veil of unbelief is rent not by intellectual achievement but by the humbled, contrite approach to God. The brother of Jared's "exceeding faith" and our "broken heart and contrite spirit" are the same key in different hands: both are the posture of a soul that has stopped doubting and come to God with nothing held back.

There is something both humbling and electrifying in this. Humbling, because it locates the obstacle in *us* — the great and marvelous things are right there, "hid up from the foundation of the world," and the only thing keeping them from us is our own "wickedness, and hardness of heart, and blindness of mind." We are the ones holding the veil shut. But electrifying, because it means the veil is *rendable* — that the same access the brother of Jared had is, in principle, open to anyone whose faith becomes what his was. The greater things are not reserved for one ancient prophet; they are "laid up for you, from the foundation of the world" (4:14), waiting behind a veil we are commanded and able to tear. The whole Restoration runs on this: the heavens are not closed, the greater revelation is not exhausted, the sealed portion is not lost — all of it waits on the other side of a veil of unbelief that we are summoned to rend.

The chapter closes by tying the coming-forth of the record to the gathering and the gospel call (4:16-19). When the record is received, "ye may know that the work of the Father has commenced upon all the face of the land" (4:17) — the same sign Mormon and Moroni keep naming (3 Ne 29:1; Morm 8:35): the book's arrival is the evidence the gathering has begun. And the final call is the same one that has closed book after book in this record: "repent all ye ends of the earth, and come unto me, and believe in my gospel, and be baptized... he that believeth and is baptized shall be saved... blessed is he that is found faithful unto my name at the last day, for he shall be lifted up to dwell in the kingdom prepared for him from the foundation of the world. And behold it is I that hath spoken it. Amen." The greater things wait behind the veil of unbelief; the way through the veil is the same simple gospel — come, believe, be baptized, endure faithful. The sealed vision and the open invitation hang on the same hinge: faith enough to come to Christ with nothing held back.
