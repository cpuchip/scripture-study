# Mosiah 18 — The Waters of Mormon: A Covenant to Carry Each Other

*(Alma — a repentant priest of Noah — teaches in hiding, sets forth the baptismal covenant, baptizes, organizes the church of Christ, and flees Noah into the wilderness. ~147–145 BC. Mini-study.)*

**Binding idea:** The one convert from Noah's court now founds a church. At its door stands the most-quoted covenant in the Book of Mormon — and it is strikingly *horizontal*. What does it mean that the entry into "the fold of God" is mostly a promise to *each other*, and who is the man administering it?

**Read:** Alma, fled from Noah, "**repented of his sins and iniquities**, and went about privately... to teach the words of Abinadi" (v1) — repentance, redemption, "faith on the Lord" (vv2-3). Believers gather at Mormon, a "fountain of pure water" by a thicket where Alma hid from the king (vv4-7). He sets the covenant: "as ye are desirous to come into the **fold of God**... and are willing to **bear one another's burdens, that they may be light**; yea, and are willing to **mourn with those that mourn**... and **comfort those that stand in need of comfort**, and to **stand as witnesses of God at all times and in all things, and in all places**... even until death" (vv8-9) — "what have you against being **baptized in the name of the Lord, as a witness... that ye have entered into a covenant**... that he may **pour out his Spirit more abundantly upon you?**" (v10). "They clapped their hands for joy, and exclaimed: **This is the desire of our hearts**" (v11). Alma baptizes Helam — "O Lord, pour out thy Spirit upon thy servant, that he may do this work with **holiness of heart**" (v12) — "Helam, I baptize thee, **having authority from the Almighty God**" (v13); both "buried in the water," rising "rejoicing, being filled with the Spirit" (v14). About 204 souls (v16); "they were called the **church of God, or the church of Christ**" (v17). Alma ordains priests, one to fifty (v18), to teach "nothing save... the things which he had taught, and... the holy prophets" (v19), "no contention... but that they should look forward with **one eye, having one faith and one baptism, having their hearts knit together in unity and in love**" (v21). The priests "should **labor with their own hands for their support**" (v24), "**not to depend upon the people**" (v26). "Impart of their substance, every one according to that which he had... and to him that had not should be given" (v27), "of their own free will" (v28). "The place of Mormon, the waters of Mormon... **how beautiful are they to the eyes of them who there came to the knowledge of their Redeemer**... they shall **sing to his praise forever**" (v30). Discovered, the king "said that Alma was **stirring up the people to rebellion**" (v33), sent his army, and Alma's people — "about **four hundred and fifty souls**" (v35) — "departed into the wilderness" (v34).

**Thoughts:**
- *The covenant is mostly horizontal (vv8-9).* The Book of Mormon's defining baptismal covenant names three commitments, and two of the three point *sideways*, toward the others in the water: "**bear one another's burdens, that they may be light**," and "**mourn with those that mourn... comfort those that stand in need of comfort**." Only the third — "stand as witnesses of God" — points upward and outward. To "come into the fold of God" is, first and most concretely, to join a people who carry each other's weight and grieve each other's griefs. Belonging to God is enacted as belonging to *them*. This is Benjamin's "are we not all beggars?" (4:19) and Paul's "bear ye one another's burdens" (Gal 6:2) installed as the very entry-rite of the church. The covenant does not produce a community as a side effect; the community *is* the covenant. (And the burdens are made "light" not by removal but by distribution — shared weight is lighter weight.)
- *The founder is a repentant insider (v1).* Easy to rush past: Alma "**repented of his sins and iniquities**." He was one of Noah's priests — a member of the corrupt order, complicit in the flattery and the gilded-seat lies (11:5-11). The church of Christ is founded not by a man who was always clean but by one who sat in the wickedness, heard the truth, believed, and turned. The first thing the founder does is repent. This deepens ch 17's "one convert": the one who believed was one of the *guilty*, and his first act of leadership is to confess he needed the redemption he now preaches. The church is built by the repentant, not the pure — which means it can be built by anyone who will turn.
- *Born in hiding, hunted from the first breath (vv4-5, 30-34).* Everything here happens underground: Alma hiding in a thicket by day, teaching "privately, that it might not come to the knowledge of the king," baptizing at a concealed fountain in the borders of the land, the assembly finally discovered and the whole church forced to flee. The church of Christ is born as a hunted thing, criminalized before it is a month old. And yet the chapter's most lyrical line is reserved for this hunted birthplace: "the place of Mormon, the waters of Mormon, the forest of Mormon, **how beautiful** are they to the eyes of them who there came to the knowledge of their Redeemer... they shall **sing to his praise forever**" (v30). The fugitive water becomes the beloved memory; the place of fear becomes the place sung about forever. (And "how beautiful" deliberately echoes the "beautiful feet" of Isaiah 52 / Mosiah 15 — beauty attaches to wherever redemption is found.)
- *The anti-Noah church order (vv19-26).* Every structural choice deliberately inverts the apostate model the converts just escaped. Noah taxed a fifth to fund priests who lived in luxury and "spoke lying and vain words" from gold-plated seats (11:3-11); Alma's priests "**labor with their own hands for their support**" and are "**not to depend upon the people**" (vv24, 26). Noah's priests flattered (11:7); Alma's teach "nothing save... repentance and faith on the Lord" (v20). Noah's people "labored exceedingly to support iniquity" (11:6); Alma's "impart of their substance... to him that had not" (v27). The new church is consciously built as the photographic negative of the corruption it came out of — and it restores Benjamin's servant-king labor pattern (2:14) to the priesthood itself. A church that *remembers* what it was rescued from builds the opposite.
- *Consecration in seed (vv27-29).* The horizontal covenant of v8 takes economic form: "if he have more abundantly he should impart more abundantly; and of him that had but little, but little should be required; and **to him that had not should be given**" — proportional, free-will, "according to their needs and their wants." Bearing one another's burdens is not a sentiment; it has a ledger. This is Benjamin's beggar-theology (ch 4) institutionalized into a sharing people, and the seed of the later law of consecration (4 Ne 1:3). The covenant to make burdens light is kept with substance.
- *The sedition charge again (v33).* Noah accuses Alma of "stirring up the people to rebellion" — the exact charge the priests used to burn Abinadi (17:12). The wicked king cannot see a gathering of believers as anything but a political threat; worship looks like insurrection to a throne built on lies. So the church's first corporate act is *flight* — to leave a state that criminalizes its existence rather than fight it. (The number grew from 204 baptized, v16, to 450 fleeing, v35 — the secret teaching kept bearing fruit even as the noose tightened.)

**Notable / flag:** No spin-off (self-contained; treated fully inline). This is the **founding of the church of Christ**, and its door is the Book of Mormon's **most-quoted covenant** (vv8-10) — strikingly *horizontal*: "**bear one another's burdens, that they may be light**," "**mourn with those that mourn**," "**stand as witnesses of God**." Two of three commitments point sideways — *the community is the covenant*, Benjamin's beggar-theology made the entry-rite. The founder is a **repentant insider** (Alma, a priest of Noah, "repented of his sins" first — the church is built by the repentant, not the pure). It is **born in hiding and hunted** (taught privately, baptized at a concealed water, forced to flee) — yet the fugitive birthplace gets the chapter's most lyrical line: "**how beautiful... they shall sing to his praise forever**" (v30, echoing Isaiah 52's beautiful feet). And it is the **anti-Noah church**: self-supporting priests who "labor with their own hands" and "depend not upon the people" (vv24-26 — the inverse of Noah's tax-funded gilded clergy), teaching repentance not flattery, sharing substance (consecration in seed, vv27-29), "hearts knit together in unity and in love" (v21). Noah criminalizes it as **sedition** (v33 — the charge that burned Abinadi), and the church flees, grown from 204 to 450. Next: Mosiah 19 (back to Noah's kingdom — Gideon's rebellion, the Lamanite invasion, Noah's own death **by fire** at the hands of his people [fulfilling 12:3 + Abinadi's 17:18 prophecy], Limhi made king and the bondage of ch 7 begins).
