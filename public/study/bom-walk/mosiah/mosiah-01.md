# Mosiah 1 — The Record Is What Kept Them From Dwindling

*(The Book of Mosiah begins — Mormon's abridgment proper. Benjamin teaches his sons; the kingdom + the relics pass to Mosiah. Mini-study.)*

**Binding idea:** Benjamin states outright the lesson Omni 1 taught by contrast — and hands his son a kingdom defined by the sacred objects of its founding. What kept the Nephites from becoming the Mulekites, and what does Benjamin entrust to the next king?

**Read:** Peace reigns in Zarahemla (v1). Benjamin's three sons are "taught in all the language of his fathers, that thereby they might become men of understanding" (v2). He teaches them of the brass plates: "were it not for these plates... we must have suffered in ignorance, even at this present time, **not knowing the mysteries of God**" (v3); Lehi could read them only because "taught in the language of the Egyptians" (v4). "**Were it not for these things, which have been kept and preserved by the hand of God... even our fathers would have dwindled in unbelief, and we should have been like unto our brethren, the Lamanites, who know nothing concerning these things**" (v5). "**Search them diligently, that ye may profit thereby**; and... keep the commandments of God, that ye may prosper in the land" (v7). Aging, Benjamin confers the kingdom on Mosiah (vv9-10) and promises the people "a name... **that never shall be blotted out, except it be through transgression**" (vv11-12) — but if they transgress, the Lord "will no more preserve them by his matchless and marvelous power" (v13). He gives Mosiah charge of "the records which were engraven on the plates of brass; and also the plates of Nephi; and also, the **sword of Laban**, and the **ball or director**" (the Liahona) "which was prepared by the hand of the Lord that thereby they might be led, **every one according to the heed and diligence which they gave unto him**" (v16); "as they were unfaithful they did not prosper... but were driven back" (v17). Mosiah proclaims the gathering to the temple (v18).

**Thoughts:**
- *The record is the bulwark against dwindling (vv3-5) — Omni 1's lesson stated outright.* Omni 1 showed by contrast what happens without the record: the Mulekites "brought no records... and they denied the being of their Creator." Benjamin now states the thesis directly: "were it not for these plates... **even our fathers would have dwindled in unbelief, and we should have been like unto our brethren, the Lamanites, who know nothing concerning these things**." Scripture is not enrichment; it is the thing that keeps a people knowing God exists. The whole small-plates record-keeping chain — every keeper apologizing for how little he added — is here given its purpose by a king teaching his sons: this is why we kept it. Without it, we would have forgotten God. The defense against dwindling is a preserved, searched text.
- *"Search them diligently, that ye may profit thereby" (v7).* Benjamin's charge is not merely *have* the records but *search* them. The profit comes from diligent searching, not from possession. (The same word — "search" — runs from Nephi's "liken" through Jacob's plainness to here; it is the project's own ethos.) A record unsearched is a record half-kept; the Mulekites' problem and a complacent Nephite's problem differ only in degree.
- *The four relics of the kingdom (v16).* Benjamin entrusts Mosiah with four sacred objects: the **brass plates** (the scriptures of the fathers), the **plates of Nephi** (their own record), the **sword of Laban** (the defense), and the **ball or director** — the Liahona (the guidance). The kingdom is handed down not as territory or treasury but as *the word, the defense, and the means of guidance.* What makes a Nephite king is custody of how the people know God, how they are protected, and how they are led. (And the Liahona's principle is restated, v17: it worked "according to the heed and diligence" — guidance is responsive to obedience, not automatic.)
- *A name that can only be lost by transgression (vv11-13).* Benjamin will give the people a name (the name of Christ, Mosiah 5) "that never shall be blotted out, except it be through transgression." The name — the covenant identity — is permanent by God's giving, forfeitable only by the people's choosing. And the matchless preserving power is conditional: transgress, and God "will no more preserve them" as he has. The name and the protection are gifts held by faithfulness, not entitlements.

**Notable / flag:** No spin-off — **the Book of Mosiah begins** (`study/bom-walk/mosiah/`); Mormon's abridgment of the large plates now drives the narrative. The chapter states **the record-keeping thesis outright**: without the preserved, searched record, "even our fathers would have dwindled in unbelief... like unto our brethren, the Lamanites" (v5) — the lesson Omni 1 taught by the Mulekite contrast, now made explicit. The **four relics** (brass plates, plates of Nephi, sword of Laban, Liahona) pass to Mosiah as the kingdom's true inheritance: the word, the defense, the guidance. And the people's coming **name** (Mosiah 5, the name of Christ) is previewed — permanent "except through transgression."
