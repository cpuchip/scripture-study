# Alma 12–13: Redemption and Priesthood — Source Log

*Working document. Kept as research provenance — traces how observations were reached.*

## Binding Question

How do Alma 12–13 move from the captivity of the devil and the Fall (ch. 12) to the holy order of the priesthood, foreordination, and entering into the rest of the Lord (ch. 13) — and what binds the two halves into one argument about how a fallen people is brought back to God?

## Verified Quotes

### Alma 12:1–11 — Chains of Hell / Mysteries of God
> "It is given unto many to know the mysteries of God; nevertheless they are laid under a strict command that they shall not impart only according to the portion of his word which he doth grant unto the children of men, according to the heed and diligence which they give unto him." (v. 9)

> "And therefore, he that will harden his heart, the same receiveth the lesser portion of the word; and he that will not harden his heart, to him is given the greater portion of the word, until it is given unto him to know the mysteries of God until he know them in full." (v. 10)

> "And they that will harden their hearts, to them is given the lesser portion of the word until they know nothing concerning his mysteries; and then they are taken captive by the devil, and led by his will down to destruction. Now this is what is meant by the chains of hell." (v. 11)

> "And behold I say unto you all that this was a snare of the adversary, which he has laid to catch this people, that he might bring you into subjection unto him, that he might encircle you about with his chains, that he might chain you down to everlasting destruction, according to the power of his captivity." (v. 6)

**Observation:** The chains are epistemic before they are moral. The process: harden → receive less word → know less → receive less still → know nothing → captive. The narrowing is cumulative and self-reinforcing. "Know nothing" at the end of v. 11 is a total epistemic collapse — not ignorance of details but loss of the category itself (TG fn: Apostasy of Individuals). The word "chains" in v. 11 is explicitly defined: THIS is what "chains of hell" means — not a metaphor added from outside but the text's own definition.
**Connects to:** Section I. Also: "will not harden" (v. 10) is the hinge phrase — active not-hardening is the condition for receiving more.

### Alma 12:21–37 — The Fall, Cherubim, Probationary State, Plan of Redemption
> "Now we see that Adam did fall by the partaking of the forbidden fruit, according to the word of God; and thus we see, that by his fall, all mankind became a lost and fallen people." (v. 22)

> "And we see that death comes upon mankind, yea, the death which has been spoken of by Amulek, which is the temporal death; nevertheless there was a space granted unto man in which he might repent; therefore this life became a probationary state; a time to prepare to meet God; a time to prepare for that endless state which has been spoken of by us, which is after the resurrection of the dead." (v. 24)

> "Now, if it had not been for the plan of redemption, which was laid from the foundation of the world, there could have been no resurrection of the dead; but there was a plan of redemption laid, which shall bring to pass the resurrection of the dead, of which has been spoken." (v. 25)

> "And now behold, if it were possible that our first parents could have gone forth and partaken of the tree of life they would have been forever miserable, having no preparatory state; and thus the plan of redemption would have been frustrated, and the word of God would have been void, taking none effect." (v. 26)

> "And after God had appointed that these things should come unto man, behold, then he saw that it was expedient that man should know concerning the things whereof he had appointed unto them; Therefore he sent angels to converse with them... God conversed with men, and made known unto them the plan of redemption, which had been prepared from the foundation of the world; and this he made known unto them according to their faith and repentance and their holy works." (vv. 28–30)

> "But God did call on men, in the name of his Son, (this being the plan of redemption which was laid) saying: If ye will repent, and harden not your hearts, then will I have mercy upon you, through mine Only Begotten Son; Therefore, whosoever repenteth, and hardeneth not his heart, he shall have claim on mercy through mine Only Begotten Son, unto a remission of his sins; and these shall enter into my rest." (vv. 33–34)

> "And now, my brethren, seeing we know these things, and they are true, let us repent, and harden not our hearts... but let us enter into the rest of God, which is prepared according to his word." (v. 37)

**Observation:** The cherubim / tree of life passage (vv. 21–26) is the pivot. Antionah asks a death question and Alma uses it to introduce the plan of redemption. The cherubim are not God's cruelty — they are the gate that forced the probationary state into existence. Without the gate, Adam eats, becomes immortal in sin, the plan of redemption is frustrated. The guardian sword is what makes repentance possible. Also note: "harden not your hearts" appears at vv. 33, 35, 36, 37 — four times in the closing five verses. The repetition is structural, not rhetorical decoration. It answers vv. 9–11: the condition for "entering rest" is the same condition for receiving the greater portion of the word.
**Connects to:** Sections I and V. The "rest" first appears here in ch. 12 (v. 34) before ch. 13 builds it out.

### Alma 13:1–9 — Holy Order, Foreordination, Called from the Foundation
> "And again, my brethren, I would cite your minds forward to the time when the Lord God gave these commandments unto his children; and I would that ye should remember that the Lord God ordained priests, after his holy order, which was after the order of his Son, to teach these things unto the people." (v. 1)

> "And those priests were ordained after the order of his Son, in a manner that thereby the people might know in what manner to look forward to his Son for redemption." (v. 2)

> "And this is the manner after which they were ordained—being called and prepared from the foundation of the world according to the foreknowledge of God, on account of their exceeding faith and good works; in the first place being left to choose good or evil; therefore they having chosen good, and exercising exceedingly great faith, are called with a holy calling, yea, with that holy calling which was prepared with, and according to, a preparatory redemption for such." (v. 3)

> "And thus they have been called to this holy calling on account of their faith, while others would reject the Spirit of God on account of the hardness of their hearts and blindness of their minds, while, if it had not been for this they might have had as great privilege as their brethren." (v. 4)

> "Or in fine, in the first place they were on the same standing with their brethren; thus this holy calling being prepared from the foundation of the world for such as would not harden their hearts, being in and through the atonement of the Only Begotten Son, who was prepared—" (v. 5)

> "And thus being called by this holy calling, and ordained unto the high priesthood of the holy order of God, to teach his commandments unto the children of men, that they also might enter into his rest—" (v. 6)

> "This high priesthood being after the order of his Son, which order was from the foundation of the world; or in other words, being without beginning of days or end of years, being prepared from eternity to all eternity, according to his foreknowledge of all things—" (v. 7)

> "Thus they become high priests forever, after the order of the Son, the Only Begotten of the Father, who is without beginning of days or end of years, who is full of grace, equity, and truth. And thus it is. Amen." (v. 9)

**Observation:** The foreordination text (v. 3) is dense and careful. It does NOT say "called because God knew they would choose good" as a bare predestination. It says: "in the first place being left to choose good or evil; therefore they having chosen good." The sequence is: equal standing → real choice → good chosen → foreknown by God → called. The foreknowledge tracks the choice, not the other way around. V. 4 makes this explicit: the others "might have had as great privilege" — the calling was equally available; it was hardness of heart that separated them. V. 5 repeats it: "in the first place they were on the same standing." This is not unconditional election. It is calling grounded in foreknown, freely made choices. The phrase "preparatory redemption" (v. 3) is striking — the calling is prepared alongside and through the Atonement. Also: v. 6 states the PURPOSE of the high priesthood: that "they also might enter into his rest." The priests are ordained TO HELP OTHERS enter rest. The priesthood is functionally redemptive.
**Connects to:** Sections III and V. The contrast with "hardness of hearts" (v. 4) directly connects to Section I's chains of hell.

### Alma 13:10–13 — Sanctified, Garments White, Enter Into Rest
> "Therefore they were called after this holy order, and were sanctified, and their garments were washed white through the blood of the Lamb." (v. 11)

> "Now they, after being sanctified by the Holy Ghost, having their garments made white, being pure and spotless before God, could not look upon sin save it were with abhorrence; and there were many, exceedingly great many, who were made pure and entered into the rest of the Lord their God." (v. 12)

> "And now, my brethren, I would that ye should humble yourselves before God, and bring forth fruit meet for repentance, that ye may also enter into that rest." (v. 13)

**Observation:** The arrival at rest follows a specific sequence: called → sanctified by Holy Ghost → garments white through Atonement → pure and spotless → cannot look on sin → entered rest. This is not a moment but a state. The language "could not look upon sin save it were with abhorrence" indicates that the change is ontological, not just behavioral. Also: "exceedingly great many" — Alma does not present this as rare or elite. It is the normal outcome of the process.
**Connects to:** Section V.

### Alma 13:14–19 — Melchizedek
> "Yea, humble yourselves even as the people in the days of Melchizedek, who was also a high priest after this same order which I have spoken, who also took upon him the high priesthood forever." (v. 14)

> "Now this Melchizedek was a king over the land of Salem; and his people had waxed strong in iniquity and abomination; yea, they had all gone astray; they were full of all manner of wickedness; But Melchizedek having exercised mighty faith, and received the office of the high priesthood according to the holy order of God, did preach repentance unto his people. And behold, they did repent; and Melchizedek did establish peace in the land in his days; therefore he was called the prince of peace, for he was the king of Salem; and he did reign under his father." (vv. 17–18)

> "Now, there were many before him, and also there were many afterwards, but none were greater; therefore, of him they have more particularly made mention." (v. 19)

**Observation:** Melchizedek's people are described in terms that precisely mirror Alma's audience at Ammonihah (12:1 — the city that rejected Alma, full of wickedness). "Had waxed strong in iniquity and abomination; yea, they had all gone astray." This is not accidental — Alma is holding up a mirror. Melchizedek's people repented. The point to Ammonihah: your situation is not unprecedented. Salem did this. You can too. The "prince of peace" designation (v. 18) connects to the JST Genesis 14 account; in the JST he is even called "the king of heaven by his people." The peace he establishes is the consequence of the priesthood working through repentance.
**Connects to:** Section IV.

### Alma 13:27–30 — Alma's Closing Plea / Chains of Hell Bookend
> "And now, my brethren, I wish from the inmost part of my heart, yea, with great anxiety even unto pain, that ye would hearken unto my words, and cast off your sins, and not procrastinate the day of your repentance;" (v. 27)

> "But that ye would humble yourselves before the Lord, and call on his holy name, and watch and pray continually, that ye may not be tempted above that which ye can bear, and thus be led by the Holy Spirit, becoming humble, meek, submissive, patient, full of love and all long-suffering;" (v. 28)

> "Having faith on the Lord; having a hope that ye shall receive eternal life; having the love of God always in your hearts, that ye may be lifted up at the last day and enter into his rest." (v. 29)

> "And may the Lord grant unto you repentance, that ye may not bring down his wrath upon you, that ye may not be bound down by the chains of hell, that ye may not suffer the second death." (v. 30)

**Observation:** The discourse ends exactly where it started. Ch. 12 opened with the chains of hell (v. 6, 11). Ch. 13 closes with "the chains of hell" (v. 30). The bookend is deliberate: enter into rest (v. 29) or be bound by chains (v. 30). These are the only two destinations from the Fall. The middle of the discourse — the plan of redemption, the probationary state, the holy order, Melchizedek — has been one long argument for why the first destination is accessible. Also: "from the inmost part of my heart, yea, with great anxiety even unto pain" (v. 27) — this is Alma speaking about Ammonihah, the city that will eventually burn him and the believers. He knows what is coming. The "pain" is not rhetorical.
**Connects to:** Sections I and V. Closes the ring.

### D&C 84:19–24 — Priesthood Holds Key of Mysteries / Rest = Fulness of Glory
> "And this greater priesthood administereth the gospel and holdeth the key of the mysteries of the kingdom, even the key of the knowledge of God." (v. 19)

> "Therefore, in the ordinances thereof, the power of godliness is manifest." (v. 20)

> "And without the ordinances thereof, and the authority of the priesthood, the power of godliness is not manifest unto men in the flesh;" (v. 21)

> "But they hardened their hearts and could not endure his presence; therefore, the Lord in his wrath, for his anger was kindled against them, swore that they should not enter into his rest while in the wilderness, which rest is the fulness of his glory." (v. 24)

**Observation:** D&C 84:19 supplies the connective tissue between Alma 12 and 13. The Melchizedek Priesthood "holdeth the key of the mysteries of the kingdom." In Alma 12:9, the mysteries of God are given according to heed and diligence. In Alma 13:1–9, the high priesthood is the institutional form of that impartation. D&C 84:24 defines "rest" as "the fulness of his glory" — this is not mere peaceful death or respite, it is theosis-adjacent. Also: "hardened their hearts" (v. 24) is the direct cause of Israel's exclusion from rest in the wilderness — the same "hardened" language Alma uses six times across the two chapters.
**Connects to:** Sections I, III, V.

### Abraham 3:22–23 — Noble and Great Ones / Chosen Before Born
> "Now the Lord had shown unto me, Abraham, the intelligences that were organized before the world was; and among all these there were many of the noble and great ones;" (v. 22)

> "And God saw these souls that they were good, and he stood in the midst of them, and he said: These I will make my rulers; for he stood among those that were spirits, and he saw that they were good; and he said unto me: Abraham, thou art one of them; thou wast chosen before thou wast born." (v. 23)

**Observation:** "God saw these souls that they were good" — the calling follows observation of character, not arbitrary selection. The parallel to Alma 13:3 is direct: called "on account of their exceeding faith and good works" (Alma) / "God saw these souls that they were good" (Abraham). The Restoration reading is consistent: foreordination = foreknown choices → God's call grounded in what He knew they would be.
**Connects to:** Section III.

### Hebrews 3:7–11 — Harden Not / Enter Into Rest (NT Parallel)
> "Harden not your hearts, as in the provocation, in the day of temptation in the wilderness:" (v. 8)

> "So I sware in my wrath, They shall not enter into my rest." (v. 11)

**Observation:** Alma's "first provocation" language (Alma 12:36) is a direct allusion to Hebrews 3:8's "as in the provocation." The author of Hebrews (quoting Psalm 95) and Alma are reading the same wilderness narrative. The phrase "enter into my rest" appears in Hebrews 3–4 multiple times; Alma's "rest of the Lord" is drawing on this tradition. The intertextual link suggests Alma 12–13 is a midrashic reading of the same wilderness failure — warning Ammonihah not to repeat it.
**Connects to:** Sections II and V.

### JST Genesis 14:25–40 — Melchizedek / Holy Order Powers
> "Now Melchizedek was a man of faith, who wrought righteousness; and when a child he feared God, and stopped the mouths of lions, and quenched the violence of fire." (v. 26)

> "And thus, having been approved of God, he was ordained an high priest after the order of the covenant which God made with Enoch, It being after the order of the Son of God; which order came, not by man, nor the will of man; neither by father nor mother; neither by beginning of days nor end of years; but of God;" (vv. 27–28)

> "Now they were ordained after this manner — being called with a holy calling, and ordained with a holy ordinance, and taking upon them the high priesthood of the holy order, which calling, and ordinance, and high priesthood, is without beginning or end—" (Alma 13:8 — echoes JST Gen 14:27–28 directly)

> "And men having this faith, coming up unto this order of God, were translated and taken up into heaven." (JST Gen 14:32)

> "And now, Melchizedek was a priest of this order; therefore he obtained peace in Salem, and was called the Prince of peace." (JST Gen 14:33)

**Observation:** The JST vastly expands what Alma 13 compresses. Alma says "none were greater" (13:19) — the JST shows why: Melchizedek's people were translated. The peace of Salem was not merely civic calm but the peace that translates. The child-Melchizedek detail (JST v. 26) — "when a child he feared God" — indicates that his calling was not despite humble origins but through long-established righteousness (parallel to Alma 13:3's "exceeding faith and good works"). The JST establishes that the order comes from Enoch → Melchizedek → down to Abraham; Alma 13 places this same order as the means by which fallen Ammonihah could be redeemed.
**Connects to:** Section IV.

## Webster 1828

### probationary
- **1828 Def:** "Of or pertaining to probation; serving for trial." ("To consider this life... as a probationary state. Paley.")
- **Study relevance:** The word in 1828 carried its full legal/trial weight. A probationary state is not just a waiting period — it is a state under active evaluation, where performance determines outcome. Alma 12:24's "time to prepare to meet God" is a trial with stakes.

### sanctify
- **1828 Def 1:** "To make sacred or holy; to set apart to a holy or religious use; to consecrate by appropriate rites; to hallow."
- **1828 Def 2:** "To make free from sin; to cleanse from moral corruption and pollution; to purify."
- **Study relevance:** Both senses operate in Alma 13:11–12. The high priests are sanctified in BOTH meanings — set apart (consecrated to the holy order) AND cleansed from sin (garments washed white through the blood of the Lamb). The two meanings are not in tension; the Alma passage requires both.

### mystery
- **1828 Def 2:** "A kind of secret religious celebration, to which none were admitted except those who had been initiated by certain preparatory ceremonies — usually plural; as, the Eleusinian mysteries."
- **1828 Def 1:** "A profound secret... beyond human comprehension."
- **Study relevance:** The 1828 definition reveals that "mysteries" in Alma 12:9-10 had a richer semantic range than today's colloquial "strange/unexplained." The Eleusinian sense — initiatory secrets given to the prepared — maps directly onto the Alma 12 structure: the mysteries are given "according to the heed and diligence" given, i.e., based on preparation and worthiness. The priesthood in Alma 13 is the institution that conveys those initiatory mysteries.

### foreknowledge
- **1828 Def:** "Knowledge of a thing before it happens... prescience. If I foreknew, Foreknowledge had no influence on their fault. Milton."
- **Study relevance:** The 1828 dictionary quotes Milton making exactly the Restoration point: God's foreknowledge of a choice does not cause the choice, does not "influence their fault." This is the precise distinction Alma 13:3 requires — called "according to the foreknowledge of God, on account of their exceeding faith and good works" — the foreknowledge tracks the choices, not vice versa. Milton was writing within the same Calvinist debate; the Book of Mormon resolves it the same way.

   - TEXT CHECK: v. 11 — "to them is given the lesser portion of the word until they know nothing... and then they are taken captive by the devil... Now this is what is meant by the chains of hell." 3. CLAIM: "The priesthood's purpose in Alma 13 is functionally redemptive — it exists to help others enter rest."
   - TEXT CHECK: v. 6 — "ordained unto the high priesthood of the holy order of God, to teach his commandments unto the children of men, that they also might enter into his rest." CONFIRMED.

4. CLAIM: "The foreordination in 13:3 is conditional, not unconditional — grounded in foreknown free choices."

THREE verses supporting the conditional reading. CONFIRMED. The text works hard to prevent a Calvinist reading.

**Ring check (does it answer the binding question?):**

BINDING QUESTION: How do these two chapters move from the captivity of the devil and the Fall (ch. 12) to the holy order of the priesthood, foreordination, and entering into the rest of the Lord (ch. 13) — and what binds the two halves into one argument?

ANSWER IN HAND: The binding element is the human heart and its direction from the Fall. Ch. 12 traces one direction (harden → narrow → nothing → captive → chains). Ch. 13 traces the other (not harden → receive more → called → sanctified → enter rest). The Fall created the probationary state (the cherubim enforced it); the plan of redemption was laid to give the fallen people a means back; the holy order of the priesthood is the institutional embodiment of that means — the priests are ordained specifically "that they also might enter into his rest" (13:6). The discourse IS one argument, not two. ✓ ANSWERED.

**Posture check:**

Am I confirming a hypothesis or discovering something? Honest audit: I came in with the thesis that chs. 12–13 are one argument about two directions of the heart. That thesis is correct — the text confirms it repeatedly and explicitly. But there were two genuine discoveries during the source-gathering that I was not expecting:
