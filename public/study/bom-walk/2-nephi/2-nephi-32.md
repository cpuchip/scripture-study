# 2 Nephi 32 — "The Words of Christ Will Tell You All Things"

*(Nephi's own teaching — the doctrine of Christ continued: how to walk the path after the gate. Mini-study.)*

**Binding idea:** Once you are on the path (ch 31), how do you know what to do next? Nephi gives two guides — and then grieves that his people will not use them.

**Read:** "I suppose that ye ponder somewhat in your hearts concerning that which ye should do after ye have entered in by the way" (v1). The answer: "feast upon the words of Christ; for behold, **the words of Christ will tell you all things what ye should do**" (v3) — angels speak by the Holy Ghost, and "they speak the words of Christ." "If ye cannot understand them it will be because ye ask not, neither do ye knock; wherefore, ye are not brought into the light, but must perish in the dark" (v4). "If ye will enter in by the way, and receive the Holy Ghost, **it will show unto you all things what ye should do**" (v5). "This is the doctrine of Christ, and there will be no more doctrine given until after he shall manifest himself unto you in the flesh" (v6). Then the lament: "I, Nephi, cannot say more; **the Spirit stoppeth mine utterance, and I am left to mourn** because of the unbelief, and the wickedness, and the ignorance, and the stiffneckedness of men; for **they will not search knowledge, nor understand great knowledge, when it is given unto them in plainness, even as plain as word can be**" (v7). "If ye would hearken unto the Spirit which teacheth a man to pray, ye would know that ye must pray; for **the evil spirit teacheth not a man to pray, but teacheth him that he must not pray**" (v8). "Ye must pray always, and not faint... that he will **consecrate thy performance unto thee, that thy performance may be for the welfare of thy soul**" (v9).

**Thoughts:**
- *Two guides for the path — the word and the Spirit (vv3, 5).* After the gate, the question is "what do I do now?" Nephi answers twice, almost identically: "the words of Christ will tell you all things what ye should do" (v3), and "the Holy Ghost... will show unto you all things what ye should do" (v5). The path is not navigated by a rulebook covering every case; it is navigated by *feasting* on the word and *receiving* the Spirit, which together address "all things." This is the answer to over-pondering (v1): you do not need a separate instruction for each fork — you need the two guides, and they will tell you in the moment.
- *Feasting connects to the rod (v3).* "Feast upon the words of Christ" is the phrase from 31:20 — and the walk's whole graph says it: feasting on the word *is* holding the rod of iron (1 Ne 8:24). So the path of 31 is walked by the feasting of 32. Not a glance at the word, not a ration — a feast.
- *Nephi's lament — the grief of refused plainness (v7).* The emotional center of the chapter, and one of the saddest verses in Nephi's record: "the Spirit stoppeth mine utterance, and I am left to mourn... for they will not search knowledge... when it is given unto them in plainness, even as plain as word can be." He has spent his whole book getting plain (25:4, 31:3), and the Spirit stops him because the people will not *search* what he has plainly given. The teacher is not silenced by lack of more to say; he is silenced by listeners who will not receive what is already said. Plainness offered and refused. (This is the cost of the "we have enough" posture from 28-29, felt from the teacher's side.)
- *The diagnostic of prayerlessness (v8).* "The evil spirit teacheth not a man to pray, but teacheth him that he must not pray." A practical, almost clinical tell: the very *reluctance* to pray is itself the adversary's instruction. When you do not feel like praying, that feeling is the signal you most need to — because the impulse against prayer has a source, and it is not God. The cure is to recognize the resistance as data, not as truth.
- *Pray always, consecrate the performance (v9).* "Pray always, and not faint... that he will consecrate thy performance unto thee, that thy performance may be for the welfare of thy soul." Not just pray, but pray *first* — before any performance — so that God consecrates it. The ordinary work of the day, offered in prayer first, becomes consecrated, turned toward the soul's welfare. (The consecration thread of the creation cycle: every act offered before it is done.)
