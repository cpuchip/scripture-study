# 2 Nephi 1 — Lehi's Farewell: Awake, Arise from the Dust

**Binding idea:** What does a dying prophet most want to leave his children — and how does he reframe the "sharpness" they resented in Nephi? *(The prosperity covenant restated here (v20) is spun off → `studies/2ne1_prosperity-covenant.md`.)*

**Read:** Lehi, near death, rehearses the mercies of the journey and prophesies of the land: "choice above all other lands," "consecrated" to those the Lord brings, "a land of liberty... they shall never be brought down into captivity; if so, it shall be because of iniquity." The covenant: "inasmuch as those whom the Lord God shall bring out of the land of Jerusalem shall keep his commandments, they shall prosper upon the face of this land." But if they "dwindle in unbelief... reject the Holy One of Israel," He "will bring other nations unto them... and cause them to be scattered and smitten." Then the plea: "O that ye would awake; awake from a deep sleep, yea, even from the sleep of hell, and shake off the awful chains by which ye are bound... Awake! and arise from the dust, and hear the words of a trembling parent." Lehi gives his own witness: "the Lord hath redeemed my soul from hell; I have beheld his glory, and I am encircled about eternally in the arms of his love." He restates the covenant (v20). He defends Nephi against Laman and Lemuel's charges — that Nephi "sought power and authority," that "he hath used sharpness," "been angry": "his sharpness was the sharpness of the power of the word of God, which was in him; and that which ye call anger was the truth... which he could not restrain." He conditions the birthright: hearken to Nephi and receive "my first blessing"; refuse, and it "shall rest upon him." He blesses Zoram as "a true friend unto my son, Nephi, forever."

**Thoughts:**
- *"Encircled about eternally in the arms of his love" (v15).* The deathbed center. Amid all Lehi's fear for his sons, he speaks his own settled assurance: redeemed from hell, having beheld God's glory, "encircled... in the arms of his love." A dying man's last word about *himself* is not anxiety but embrace — the arms of mercy (Alma 5:33; 3 Ne 9:14). His fear is all for his children; his peace is all in being held. That is the shape of a parent who actually knows God: terrified for the kids, settled about himself.
- *"His sharpness was... the power of the word of God" (v24-27) — the prophet's edge misread as temper.* Lehi corrects the accusation that has dogged Nephi all along: that he was harsh, angry, power-hungry. He distinguishes the man from the word: "that which ye call anger was the truth... which he could not restrain." What the unrepentant heart experiences as the prophet's hostility is often just truth it cannot bear — the same diagnosis as 16:2 ("the guilty take the truth to be hard"). The sharpness was the word's edge, not the speaker's temper (cf D&C 121:43, "reproving betimes with sharpness, when moved upon by the Holy Ghost"). And Lehi insists Nephi "hath not sought for power... but he hath sought the glory of God, and your own eternal welfare" — the leader cleared of the very charge Laman projected (16:38).
- *"Awake... arise from the dust" (v13-23).* Lehi's imagery for repentance: sin is a "deep sleep," even "the sleep of hell," and "awful chains"; the remedy is to "awake," "shake off the chains," "arise from the dust," and "put on the armor of righteousness." The dust one rises from is the same dust his own "limbs ye must soon lay down" in (v14) — a dying father telling his children to rise from the death he is about to enter (Isa 52:1-2; the "arise from the dust" motif recurs across the book).
- *The land of liberty is conditional (v5-9).* "A land of liberty... they shall never be brought down into captivity; if so, it shall be because of iniquity." Liberty is the covenant's gift and bondage its forfeiture — the political form of the prosperity covenant. The land is "consecrated," kept hidden from other nations (v8), held only on the terms of righteousness. (The full chain → the spin-off study.)
- *The birthright turns on hearkening to the younger (v28-29).* Lehi formalizes the ruler-inversion (2:22): the "first blessing" goes to whoever will hearken to Nephi; refuse, and it rests on Nephi. The blessing follows obedience, not birth order — Reuben's forfeit (Gen 49:3-4) re-run.
- *Zoram blessed (v30-32).* The freed servant of ch 4 receives a patriarchal blessing and a perpetual bond with Nephi's seed. The man grafted in at the cost of Laban's life is now named "a true friend... forever" — the redemption arc that began at the city wall reaches the deathbed blessing.

**Notable / flag — spin-off study COMPLETE:** `studies/2ne1_prosperity-covenant.md` (sonnet subagent; provenance `study/.scratch/2ne1_prosperity-covenant.md`). **Takeaway:** the covenant enters in two asymmetric halves — "prosper / led to a land" (temporal, to Nephi) and "cut off from my presence" (relational/spiritual, to the brothers) — fused at 2 Ne 1:20 into the biconditional that runs every generation as both promise and interpretive lens. **It is not ethnic:** Ether 2:7-12 proves the *same* terms governed the Jaredites ("whatsoever nation shall possess it shall serve God, or be swept off"), with Moroni aiming the words at the Gentile reader. Its sharpest internal danger: prosperity triggers *forgetting* at the very moment it arrives (Hel 12:2 — ease removes the friction of remembering). The two halves run at different registers at once — one can be materially prosperous yet spiritually cut off, or in God's presence while the communal covenant collapses around them. Self-implicating, not triumphalist. **Walk's own contribution (this chapter):** the sharpness-is-the-word reframe (v24-27) and Lehi's "arms of his love" peace amid fear for his sons.
