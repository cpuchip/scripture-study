# 2 Nephi 30 — The Covenant by Repentance, Not Race — and "Pure and a Delightsome People"

*(Nephi's own prophecy — the covenant fulfilled, the remnant restored, the millennial peace. Mini-study. Re-quotes Isaiah 11:4-9 in vv9-15. Contains the documented white→pure edition change, v6.)*

**Binding idea:** Nephi closes his prophesying on who the covenant people actually are. What makes someone covenant — and what does it mean that the change in this very verse (white→pure) bears on the hard verse back in chapter 5?

**Read:** "I, Nephi, would not suffer that ye should suppose that ye are more righteous than the Gentiles shall be... except ye shall keep the commandments of God ye shall all likewise perish" (v1). "**As many of the Gentiles as will repent are the covenant people of the Lord; and as many of the Jews as will not repent shall be cast off; for the Lord covenanteth with none save it be with them that repent and believe in his Son**" (v2). After the book comes forth, many believe and carry it to "the remnant of our seed" (v3); they learn "they are descendants of the Jews" (v4), and "shall be restored unto the knowledge of their fathers, and also to the knowledge of Jesus Christ" (v5); "their scales of darkness shall begin to fall from their eyes; and many generations shall not pass away among them, save they shall be **a pure and a delightsome people**" (v6). The scattered Jews "also shall begin to believe in Christ... and as many as shall believe in Christ shall also become a delightsome people" (v7); "the Lord God shall commence his work among all nations, kindreds, tongues, and people, to bring about the restoration of his people" (v8). Then Isaiah 11 returns: "with righteousness shall the Lord God judge the poor... he shall smite the earth with the rod of his mouth" (v9); "the wolf [shall] dwell with the lamb" (v12); "**they shall not hurt nor destroy in all my holy mountain; for the earth shall be full of the knowledge of the Lord as the waters cover the sea**" (v15). And the close: "the things of all nations shall be made known... **there is nothing which is secret save it shall be revealed**... nothing which is sealed upon the earth save it shall be loosed" (vv16-17); "Satan shall have power over the hearts of the children of men no more, for a long time. And now, my beloved brethren, I make an end of my sayings" (v18).

**Thoughts:**
- *The covenant is response, not lineage (vv1-2).* Nephi's first move is to deflate his own people's presumption: "ye [Nephites are not] more righteous than the Gentiles." Then the rule: "as many of the Gentiles as will repent are the covenant people of the Lord; and as many of the Jews as will not repent shall be cast off." Membership in the covenant is determined by repentance and belief in the Son, not by descent. This is the same division-by-belief-not-bloodline that runs from 1 Ne 17:35 ("he that is righteous is favored of God") through 2 Ne 26:33 ("all are alike unto God"). Bloodline gives no standing; response does.
- *"Pure and a delightsome people" (v6) — the change that bears on chapter 5.* The current text reads "a **pure** and a delightsome people." The 1830 first edition read "a **white** and a delightsome people"; **Joseph Smith himself changed "white" to "pure" in the 1840 edition** (the reading restored to the official text in 1981). This is a documented, verifiable textual fact, and it is directly relevant to the flagged 2 Ne 5:21 complexion language: the trajectory of the text — and the Prophet's own emendation — points the meaning *away* from skin color and *toward* purity and righteousness. Read with v2 ("repent and believe") and v5 ("the knowledge of Jesus Christ"), the "delightsomeness" here is plainly spiritual restoration, not pigment. (This is another data point for the with-Michael study of 5:21; the interpretation of 5:21 itself I still leave for Michael and the Spirit — but the white→pure change is a fact that belongs in that study.)
- *Scales of darkness, not skin (v6).* What falls away is "scales of darkness... from their eyes" — a blindness-to-sight image (the same as Paul's scales, Acts 9:18), not a complexion change. The restoration Nephi foresees is the recovery of *sight* and *knowledge* ("the knowledge of their fathers, and... of Jesus Christ," v5), the lifting of spiritual blindness. The people become "pure" by coming to see.
- *Nephi ends on the peaceable kingdom (vv9-15).* He closes his prophesying by re-quoting Isaiah 11 — the Messianic judge and the wolf-with-the-lamb — landing again on the verse that anchors the whole project: "the earth shall be full of the knowledge of the Lord as the waters cover the sea" (v15; see ch 21). The destination of the covenant by repentance is the world saturated with knowing, where none hurt or destroy. Nephi's last prophetic word is peace-by-knowledge.
- *Total revelation, Satan bound (vv16-18).* "Nothing which is secret save it shall be revealed... nothing which is sealed... save it shall be loosed." The millennial transparency: the sealed book (ch 27) finally opened, all things known, every work of darkness brought to light. And "Satan shall have power over the hearts of the children of men no more, for a long time" — bound by the righteousness of a knowing people (cf 1 Ne 22:26). Then Nephi sets down the prophesying: "I make an end of my sayings."
