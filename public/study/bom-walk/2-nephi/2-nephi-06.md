# 2 Nephi 6 — Jacob Takes Up Isaiah: The Likening Passed On

**Binding idea:** How does Jacob inherit Nephi's method — and what does he see in Isaiah 49 for the scattered and the Gentiles? *(Jacob's sermon, 2 Ne 6-10, expounds Isaiah 49-52 — the thread held from 1 Ne 21.)*

**Read:** Jacob — "called of God, ordained after the manner of his holy order... consecrated by my brother Nephi, unto whom ye look as a king or a protector" — speaks "for the welfare of your souls." At Nephi's request he reads Isaiah, naming the method: "they may be likened unto you, for ye are of the house of Israel." He quotes Isaiah 49:22-23 (the Lord lifts his hand to the Gentiles; "kings shall be thy nursing fathers... they shall not be ashamed that wait for me"), then expounds: those at Jerusalem "have been slain and carried away captive" but "should return again"; "the Holy One of Israel" will "manifest himself unto them in the flesh," and they will "scourge him and crucify him"; afterward they are "smitten and afflicted," "scattered, and smitten, and hated," yet "not suffered to perish, because of the prayers of the faithful," and gathered when "they shall come to the knowledge of their Redeemer." The Gentiles are "blessed... if it so be that they shall repent and fight not against Zion, and do not unite themselves to that great and abominable church." The Messiah "will set himself again the second time to recover them... in power and great glory." Then Isaiah 49:24-26: "even the captives of the mighty shall be taken away... for the Mighty God shall deliver his covenant people."

**Thoughts:**
- *The likening method passes from Nephi to Jacob (v5).* Nephi taught it (19:23, "I did liken all scriptures unto us"); now Jacob names it as his own hermeneutic: "they may be likened unto you, for ye are of the house of Israel." Note who asked: "the words which my brother [Nephi] has desired that I should speak" (v4). The reading-key is deliberately handed down — Nephi commissions Jacob to do with Isaiah what Nephi did. Method, not just content, is the inheritance.
- *Jacob's authority and his anxiety (v2-3).* He establishes his calling — "ordained after the manner of his holy order, consecrated by my brother Nephi" — and his motive: "I am desirous for the welfare of your souls. Yea, mine anxiety is great for you." The Nephite preacher's posture is anxiety for souls, not performance (he will voice it again, Jacob 2). And note the polity: Nephi is "a king or a protector" the people "depend on for safety," while Jacob holds the holy order — a quiet separation of the civic and the priestly from the start.
- *The Jews preserved "because of the prayers of the faithful" (v11).* A tender, easily-missed line in the middle of judgment. The scattered Jews are "not suffered to perish" — and the stated reason is intercession: "the prayers of the faithful." Across all the centuries of scattering, the survival of a hated and driven people is credited to prayer. Mercy carried by the praying, again (the intercessory thread, 1 Ne 7).
- *The Christology holds (v9).* Jacob, like Nephi (19:7-10), names the crucified one as "the Holy One of Israel... manifest himself unto them in the flesh," whom "they should scourge... and crucify." Jehovah, the God of Israel, is the one the Jews will crucify — the consistent pre-Christian identification.
- *The Gentile condition (v12).* Blessed "if it so be that they shall repent and fight not against Zion, and do not unite themselves to that great and abominable church." The Gentiles' blessing is conditional on three things — repent, don't fight Zion, don't join the church of the devil (the two-churches frame, 1 Ne 14:10). Mercy offered, terms named.
- *"The Mighty God shall deliver his covenant people" (v17) — the BoM reading of Isaiah 49:25.* Where the received Isaiah is ambiguous about who delivers the "lawful captive," the Book of Mormon's rendering (and the JST) makes it explicit: "the Mighty God shall deliver his covenant people." Another instance of the BoM clarifying an Isaiah passage toward Christ and the covenant (cf the 1 Ne 20-21 variants).
