# 1 Nephi 22 — Nephi Expounds Isaiah: Scattering, Gathering, and a Bound Satan (1 Nephi closes)

**Binding idea:** How does Nephi read the Isaiah he just gave his brothers — and how does the whole first book end?

**Read:** The brothers ask whether Isaiah's words are "spiritual... or... the flesh." Nephi answers: "things both temporal and spiritual." Israel "will be scattered upon all the face of the earth"; the lost tribes are "led away... whither... none of us knoweth." But "after they shall be nursed by the Gentiles" (Isaiah 49:23), the Lord will "raise up a mighty nation among the Gentiles... upon the face of this land," by whom "our seed shall be scattered" — then "a marvelous work among the Gentiles" of "great worth unto our seed," fulfilling the covenant "In thy seed shall all the kindreds of the earth be blessed." God will "make bare his arm in the eyes of all the nations," gather Israel "out of obscurity and out of darkness," and the great and abominable church "shall war among themselves, and the sword of their own hands shall fall upon their own heads." Every nation that wars against Israel "shall fall into the pit which they digged." "All the proud and they who do wickedly shall be as stubble... must be burned," but "he will preserve the righteous by his power... even if it so be as by fire." The prophet "like unto" Moses (Deut 18) "was the Holy One of Israel." The kingdom of the devil is "all churches which are built up to get gain... power over the flesh... to become popular in the eyes of the world." And "because of the righteousness of his people, Satan has no power... he cannot be loosed for the space of many years; for he hath no power over the hearts of the people." "There shall be one fold and one shepherd." Nephi closes: "if ye shall be obedient to the commandments, and endure to the end, ye shall be saved at the last day."

**Thoughts:**
- *Isaiah is "both temporal and spiritual" (v1-3) — Nephi refuses the binary.* The brothers want a single key: literal history or spiritual allegory? Nephi will not choose: "both." The scattering and gathering happen in real geography *and* in the heart. This is the book's hermeneutic for prophecy — not either/or but both/and — and it models the likening: you read Isaiah for the nation *and* for yourself.
- *Nephi expounds the chapters he just read — modeling his own method.* Having read Isaiah 48-49 (chs 20-21), he now *applies* them: scatter → "nursed by the Gentiles" (49:23) → "a mighty nation among the Gentiles" → "a marvelous work" (the Restoration, Isa 29:14) → gather. He shows his brothers (and us) how likening works: take the prophet's words and trace them onto your own people's actual future. Ch 22 is the worked example of 19:23.
- *The church of the devil self-destructs (v13-14).* "They shall war among themselves, and the sword of their own hands shall fall upon their own heads." The great whore needs no external executioner; its own violence consumes it, and "every nation which shall war against thee... shall fall into the pit which they digged" (the pit-digger justice of 14:3). Evil is self-defeating — a recurring pattern (the Gadianton bands, the final wars) where wickedness is its own undoing.
- *The same fire — destruction for one, preservation for the other (v15-19).* "All the proud... shall be as stubble... must be burned" (Mal 4:1), yet "the righteous be preserved, even unto the destruction of their enemies by fire... they shall be saved, even if it so be as by fire." One blaze, opposite outcomes — the burning that consumes the wicked is the same that purifies the righteous (the marvelous-work-cuts-both-ways of 14:7, now eschatological).
- *Satan is bound by the people's righteousness (v15, 26).* The chapter's deepest doctrine, and easy to miss. The millennial binding of Satan is not (only) a chain dropped from heaven: "because of the righteousness of his people, Satan has no power... for he hath no power over the hearts of the people, for they dwell in righteousness." Satan is bound when hearts are too righteous for him to grip. The binding is, at least in part, *us* — a people whose hearts give him nothing to hold (cf Rev 20:2; D&C 43:31; 1 Ne 13:37). The Millennium is not Satan disabled by force but humanity not consenting.
- *The prophet like unto Moses is Christ (v20-21).* Nephi quotes Deuteronomy 18:15-19 — "A prophet shall the Lord your God raise up unto you, like unto me" — and names him: "this prophet... was the Holy One of Israel." Peter applies the same text to Jesus (Acts 3:22). The Mosaic prophecy points to the Messiah; the prophet-like-Moses is the premortal Christ who will "execute judgment in righteousness."
- *The kingdom of the devil is defined by motive, not name (v22-23).* Restating 14:10: who must fear? "All churches which are built up to get gain... to get power over the flesh... to become popular in the eyes of the world." The diagnostic is appetite — gain, power, popularity, lust — not a denominational label (priestcraft; Luke 6:26). The warning, again, is aimed at the motive in any heart.
- *The book closes on the invitation (v28-31).* "All nations, kindreds, tongues, and people shall dwell safely in the Holy One of Israel if it so be that they will repent." The plates "testify that a man must be obedient." And the humble close: "ye need not suppose that I and my father are the only ones that have testified." First Nephi ends where 6:4 said it would aim — persuading all to come unto Christ, repent, and endure to the end.
