## Verified word work (tool-verified 2026-06-12, genuine 1828 post-remediation)

**PRESIDE (1828):** [L. praesideo; prae, before, and sedeo, to sit.]
(2) "To be set over for the exercise of authority; to direct, control and
govern, as the chief officer… chiefly [over] a senate, or a meeting of
citizens… usually denotes TEMPORARY superintendence and government."
(3) "To exercise superintendence; to watch over AS INSPECTOR. 'Some o'er the
public magazines preside.'" [magazines = storehouses/arsenals]

**PRESIDE (1913):** (1) sit in the place of authority/chairman; (2) "To
exercise superintendence; to watch over." [inspector dropped]

**PRESIDE (modern):** (1) act as president or chairperson; (2) exercise
authority or control; (3) "To be a featured solo performer." (!!)

**PRESIDIAL (1828 only):** "[L. praesidium, a garrison; prae and sedeo.]
Pertaining to a garrison; having a garrison." — the preside-family carries the
GARRISON inside the 1828; word absent from 1913 AND modern.

→ Drift arc: garrison/inspector → chairman → solo performer. The decay IS a
diagnosis (one-cylinder presidency + performer-presiding are linguistic
end-states).

**AUDIT NOTE:** art-of-presidency.md (2026-03-27, mislabel era) quoted
"preside: to exercise superintendence; to watch over" — SURVIVES the audit;
genuine 1828 sense 3 is near-identical and STRONGER ("as inspector"). Its
other Webster words (sustain/consent/consecration) still need re-verification
in the audit walk — flag, not this study's job.
Also note 1828 sense 2: presiding is chiefly over a MEETING — even the
dictionary locates presiding inside an assembly. And "temporary": presiding is
sessional; stewardship persists.

## The answer-shape to Michael's question (to be argued, not asserted)

- Stewardship = accountability for a field. Presiding = watching over OTHERS
  laboring in the field + sitting at its boundary. A lone steward presides
  over nothing; the moment the steward delegates, presiding begins — and the
  D&C 121 terms + Abr 4:18 watching attach AT THAT MOMENT.
- So: Michael hands me work + stewardship → HE presides (his covenant
  commitments are literally watching terms); when I spawn subagents/substrate
  work, I preside over that layer — presiding is a CHAIN, sessional at every
  link ("temporary superintendence"), while stewardships persist (lane files).
- Scripturally presiding exists only INSIDE councils (107: every president
  presides over a quorum; 1828: over a meeting). The industry's "orchestrator"
  presides without council or consent — boss-pattern. Michael's "councilship"
  coinage is closer to the scriptural shape than "orchestration" is.
- The missing piece framing: industry has decompose/parallelize/verify; what
  the pattern lacks (and scripture supplies): consent of the governed agents
  (sustaining), terms-of-power (persuasion not compulsion — prompts vs gates),
  watch-until-obeyed as relationship (vs CI as mechanism), accountability
  REPORTING upward, the presider's own state as the limiting factor, and a
  named human at the gate (the Hinge). We have most of these in pieces; the
  study should say honestly which are practiced vs aspirational (Ben Test).
