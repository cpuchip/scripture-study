# Scratch: Nevertheless

## Binding Question

What does "nevertheless" actually mean, and why does scripture use it at every hinge point between ruin and rescue?

## Verified Quotes

### Webster 1828
**NEVERTHELESS** (adv.) — "Not the less; notwithstanding; in spite of that; yet."
Example given: "No chastening for the present seemeth to be joyous, but grievous; nevertheless, afterward it yieldeth the peaceable fruit of righteousness. Heb. xii. 11."

Etymology: NEVER + THE + LESS. Literally: "not ever to a lesser degree." The preceding condition subtracts nothing from what follows.

### D&C 19:18-19
"which suffering caused myself, even God, the greatest of all, to tremble because of pain, and to bleed at every pore, and to suffer both body and spirit — and would that I might not drink the bitter cup, and shrink — Nevertheless, glory be to the Father, and I partook and finished my preparations unto the children of men."
- The cost didn't make the choice lesser. "I would that I might not" → NEVERTHELESS → "I partook and finished."

### Luke 22:42
"Saying, Father, if thou be willing, remove this cup from me: nevertheless not my will, but thine, be done."
- Greek: πλήν (plēn). Same hinge. Desire for relief → NEVERTHELESS → submission.

### Alma 7:13
"Now the Spirit knoweth all things; nevertheless the Son of God suffereth according to the flesh"
- Omniscience → NEVERTHELESS → chosen experience. Knowledge didn't make suffering unnecessary. He could have succored from omniscience alone. He chose immersion.

### 2 Nephi 4:17
"Nevertheless, notwithstanding the great goodness of the Lord, in showing me his great and marvelous works, my heart exclaimeth: O wretched man that I am!"
- Gratitude → NEVERTHELESS → grief. Opposite direction from D&C 19. Here "nevertheless" carries the crash.

### 2 Nephi 4:19
"And when I desire to rejoice, my heart groaneth because of my sins; nevertheless, I know in whom I have trusted."
- Grief → NEVERTHELESS → trust. The second "nevertheless" overcomes the first. Two verses apart.

### Psalm 73:22-24
"So foolish was I, and ignorant: I was as a beast before thee. Nevertheless I am continually with thee: thou hast holden me by my right hand. Thou shalt guide me with thy counsel, and afterward receive me to glory."
- Self-assessment: foolish, ignorant, beast-like → NEVERTHELESS → continually held by God's right hand. Asaph's version of Nephi's Psalm. The psalmist's worst view of himself doesn't change God's grip.

### Psalm 89:30-34
"If his children forsake my law, and walk not in my judgments; If they break my statutes, and keep not my commandments; Then will I visit their transgression with the rod, and their iniquity with stripes. Nevertheless my lovingkindness will I not utterly take from him, nor suffer my faithfulness to fail. My covenant will I not break, nor alter the thing that is gone out of my lips."
- God speaking: Children rebel → discipline comes → NEVERTHELESS → lovingkindness remains. "I will not break my covenant." The consequence doesn't diminish the love. Not the less.

### Psalm 106:6-8, 43-45
"We have sinned with our fathers, we have committed iniquity, we have done wickedly. Our fathers understood not thy wonders in Egypt; they remembered not the multitude of thy mercies; but provoked him at the sea, even at the Red sea. Nevertheless he saved them for his name's sake."
"Many times did he deliver them; but they provoked him with their counsel, and were brought low for their iniquity. Nevertheless he regarded their affliction, when he heard their cry: And he remembered for them his covenant."
- The entire psalm is a cycle: Israel rebels → God delivers → Israel rebels again → NEVERTHELESS God hears their cry. The whole psalm is one long "nevertheless."

### Hebrews 12:11
"Now no chastening for the present seemeth to be joyous, but grievous: nevertheless afterward it yieldeth the peaceable fruit of righteousness unto them which are exercised thereby."
- The "nevertheless" of TIME. Present grief → NEVERTHELESS → future fruit. The present doesn't determine the end. Webster chose THIS verse as his example.

### Mosiah 23:9-11 (Alma speaking)
"I myself was caught in a snare, and did many things which were abominable in the sight of the Lord, which caused me sore repentance; Nevertheless, after much tribulation, the Lord did hear my cries, and did answer my prayers, and has made me an instrument in his hands... Nevertheless, in this I do not glory, for I am unworthy to glory of myself."
- Triple movement: sin → NEVERTHELESS → instrument of God → NEVERTHELESS → no personal glory. Alma's "nevertheless" keeps him honest in *both* directions — neither trapped in his past nor puffed up by his present.

### Mosiah 23:21-22
"Nevertheless the Lord seeth fit to chasten his people; yea, he trieth their patience and their faith. Nevertheless—whosoever putteth his trust in him the same shall be lifted up at the last day."
- DOUBLE NEVERTHELESS. First: prosperity → NEVERTHELESS → chastening comes. Second: chastening → NEVERTHELESS → trust leads to being lifted up. The two "neverthelessses" form a bridge over the valley. You enter through chastening and exit through trust.

### D&C 24:1-2
"I have lifted thee up out of thine afflictions, and have counseled thee, that thou hast been delivered from all thine enemies, and thou hast been delivered from the powers of Satan and from darkness! Nevertheless, thou art not excusable in thy transgressions; nevertheless, go thy way and sin no more."
- DOUBLE NEVERTHELESS to Joseph Smith. First: deliverance → NEVERTHELESS → accountability remains. Second: accountability → NEVERTHELESS → go forward. Honesty + grace in one sentence. God holds both truths at once.

### D&C 98:41-42
"And if he trespass against thee and repent not the first time, nevertheless thou shalt forgive him. And if he trespass against thee the second time, and repent not, nevertheless thou shalt forgive him."
- The nevertheless of COMMANDED PERSISTENCE in mercy. The offense repeats; the forgiveness repeats. "Nevertheless" becomes a refusal to let someone else's sin determine your response.

### D&C 75:8
"And he sinned; nevertheless, I forgive him and say unto him again, Go ye into the south countries."
- God to McLellin. The sin is named directly. The forgiveness is unconditional. And then: a recommission. "Go ye." The sin didn't revoke the calling.

### Judges 2:11-16
"And the children of Israel did evil in the sight of the Lord, and served Baalim... And the anger of the Lord was hot against Israel... Nevertheless the Lord raised up judges, which delivered them out of the hand of those that spoiled them."
- Israel at maximum rebellion → God angry → NEVERTHELESS → deliverance sent anyway. Not because they deserved it. Because He is who He is.

### Ezekiel 20:13-17, 21-22
"The house of Israel rebelled against me in the wilderness... then I said, I would pour out my fury upon them in the wilderness, to consume them. Nevertheless mine eye spared them from destroying them, neither did I make an end of them."
"Notwithstanding the children rebelled against me... then I said, I would pour out my fury upon them... Nevertheless I withdrew mine hand."
- Twice! Rebellion → fury intended → NEVERTHELESS → hand withdrawn. The anger is real. The restraint is also real. "Nevertheless" is the space between justice and mercy where God makes His choice.

### Jeremiah 5:18
"Nevertheless in those days, saith the Lord, I will not make a full end with you."
- Destruction is coming (vv.15-17) → NEVERTHELESS → not a full end. Even in judgment, preservation. The "not a full end" is the nevertheless of divine restraint.

### Proverbs 19:21
"There are many devices in a man's heart; nevertheless the counsel of the Lord, that shall stand."
- Human plans scatter → NEVERTHELESS → God's counsel stands. Almost meta-level: the word itself enacts the principle it describes.

### 2 Peter 3:13
"Nevertheless we, according to his promise, look for new heavens and a new earth, wherein dwelleth righteousness."
- Present world dissolving → NEVERTHELESS → promise of new creation. Forward-looking. The destruction doesn't diminish the hope.

### Alma 5:7
"They were in the midst of darkness; nevertheless, their souls were illuminated by the light of the everlasting word"
- Darkness → NEVERTHELESS → illumination. The darkness didn't diminish the light. Not the less.

## Observations

1. **Three voices use "nevertheless" in scripture:**
   - Christ's nevertheless (submission, chosen suffering)
   - God's nevertheless toward us (persistence in mercy despite rebellion)
   - Our nevertheless (trust despite pain, darkness, weakness)

2. **The double nevertheless** appears in at least three places (2 Nephi 4:17/19, Mosiah 23:21-22, D&C 24:2), each time creating a bridge: enter from one side, cross the valley, emerge on the other.

3. **Etymology is the meaning:** NEVER + THE + LESS = the preceding condition subtracts NOTHING from what follows. This isn't "but" or "however" — it's a stronger claim. Not diminished. Not even a little.

4. **The word works in both directions:** It can carry you from good to hard (2 Nephi 4:17 — gratitude → wretchedness) or from hard to good (2 Nephi 4:19 — grief → trust). It doesn't guarantee a happy ending. It guarantees that nothing is lost.

5. **Hebrews 12:11 adds TIME:** "nevertheless afterward." The nevertheless of patience. The present doesn't determine the end.

6. **D&C 98:41-42 makes it a command:** We are told to BE the nevertheless for others. Forgive nevertheless. The word becomes an ethic, not just an observation.
