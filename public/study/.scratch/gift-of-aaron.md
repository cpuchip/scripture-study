# Scratch — The Gift of Aaron (D&C 8:6)

> **Binding question:** What is "the gift of Aaron" in D&C 8:6, and does it relate to the Aaronic Priesthood?

## Status

Phase 2 in progress. Phase 1 outline + key historical anchor captured.

## Load-bearing facts (verified this session)

1. **Date of revelation:** April 1829. ([JSP, Revelation, April 1829–B (D&C 8), historical introduction](https://www.josephsmithpapers.org/paper-summary/revelation-april-1829-b-dc-8/1))
2. **Aaronic Priesthood restoration:** May 15, 1829, near Harmony, PA. (BD "Aaronic Priesthood", `[BD Aaronic Priesthood](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bd/aaronic-priesthood?lang=eng)`)
3. **Therefore: Oliver Cowdery did not hold the Aaronic Priesthood when D&C 8:6 was spoken.** The "gift of Aaron" in v.6 cannot be the priesthood office, because the office had not yet been conferred on earth in this dispensation.
4. **BYU Citation Index hit count on D&C 8:6, 8:7, 8:8, 8:9:** zero. The phrase has effectively dropped out of modern conference preaching. D&C 8:11 has 2 hits, both 19th-century (Woodruff JD 19:357b; Orson Pratt JD 19:218a). Exodus 4:14 (Aaron as Moses's mouth) zero. Numbers 17:8 (Aaron's rod budded) one (Pratt JD 16:55a).
5. **No Come Follow Me manual on D&C is present in the local gospel-library corpus.** Only the children's *Doctrine and Covenants Stories 2025* is present, and its Oliver Cowdery chapter does not mention D&C 8:6 or the gift of Aaron. The verse is genuinely under-treated in current curriculum.

## Historical wording (the central textual fact)

The 1835 published reading "gift of Aaron" is a **revision**. The original 1829 manuscript (Revelation Book 1, page 12), in Oliver Cowdery's own hand, reads:

> "now this is not all for thou hast another gift which is the gift of working with the **sprout** Behold it hath told you things Behold there is no other power save God that can cause this **thing of Nature** to work in your hands for it is the work of God"
> — [JSP, Revelation, April 1829–B (D&C 8)](https://www.josephsmithpapers.org/paper-summary/revelation-april-1829-b-dc-8/1)

Sidney Rigdon, preparing Revelation Book 1 for publication, replaced "sprout" with "rod" for the 1833 *Book of Commandments* (BoC 7:3):

> "Now this is not all, for you have another gift, which is the gift of working with the **rod**: behold it has told you things: behold there is no other power save God, that can cause this **rod of nature**, to work in your hands, for it is the work of God."

For the 1835 *Doctrine and Covenants* the wording was revised again to the current "gift of Aaron" / "this gift of Aaron."

**So the textual progression is: "sprout" → "rod" → "Aaron".** All three refer to the same gift. The 1835 revision is interpretive — it renames a tangible-object gift after a biblical figure who used a tangible object (a rod) at God's direction. (Source: bookofmormonevidence.org synthesis citing Revelation Book 1 p.13 footnotes + Cannon, "Oliver Cowdery's Gift," *Revelations in Context*, 2012.)

## Verified scripture quotes

### D&C 8:6-11 — the core passage (read in full this session)

> "6. Now this is not all thy gift; for you have another gift, which is the gift of Aaron; behold, it has told you many things;
> 7. Behold, there is no other power, save the power of God, that can cause this gift of Aaron to be with you.
> 8. Therefore, doubt not, for it is the gift of God; and you shall hold it in your hands, and do marvelous works; and no power shall be able to take it away out of your hands, for it is the work of God.
> 9. And, therefore, whatsoever you shall ask me to tell you by that means, that will I grant unto you, and you shall have knowledge concerning it.
> 10. Remember that without faith you can do nothing; therefore ask in faith. Trifle not with these things; do not ask for that which you ought not.
> 11. Ask that you may know the mysteries of God, and that you may translate and receive knowledge from all those ancient records which have been hid up, that are sacred; and according to your faith shall it be done unto you."
> — [D&C 8:6-11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/8?lang=eng&id=p6-p11#p6)

**Textual observations:**
- "another gift" — distinct from the gift of revelation/translation already named in vv.1-5
- "it has told you many things" — past tense; this gift was already operating in Cowdery's life before the revelation
- "you shall hold it in your hands" — a physical object is implied in the original; the 1835 revision retains the physicality ("in your hands")
- "no power shall be able to take it away out of your hands"
- "whatsoever you shall ask me to tell you by **that means**" (v.9) — a *medium* of revelation
- v.11 turns from the gift to its purpose ("Ask that you may know the mysteries of God, and that you may translate...") — the gift's *function* (revelation that enables translation) survives the revision intact

### D&C 6 — Oliver's prior revelation (footnote 6a target for D&C 8:6 "gift"), read in full

D&C 6 was given in early April 1829, just before D&C 8. Oliver had begun as scribe April 7, 1829. Key verses ([D&C 6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/6?lang=eng)):

> "10. Behold thou hast a gift, and blessed art thou because of thy gift. Remember it is sacred and cometh from above—
> 11. And if thou wilt inquire, thou shalt know mysteries which are great and marvelous; therefore thou shalt exercise thy gift, that thou mayest find out mysteries…
> 12. Make not thy gift known unto any save it be those who are of thy faith. Trifle not with sacred things.
> 13. … thou shalt be saved in the kingdom of God, which is the greatest of all the gifts of God; for there is no gift greater than the gift of salvation."

Then v.25-27 offers Oliver an *additional* gift — translation:

> "25. And, behold, I grant unto you a gift, if you desire of me, to translate, even as my servant Joseph.
> … 27. … then shall you assist in bringing to light, with your gift, those parts of my scriptures which have been hidden because of iniquity."

**D&C 6 establishes the architecture D&C 8 fills in.** Oliver already had a gift before he came to Joseph (D&C 6:10 — note past tense, "thou hast"). That gift is "sacred and cometh from above." It is a gift of inquiring and knowing mysteries. The same "trifle not" warning recurs in D&C 8:10. Then D&C 6:25 offers a *new* gift on top — translation — which is what D&C 9 will record Oliver failing at.

So the layering is: (1) Oliver's pre-existing revelatory gift, named in D&C 6:10 and specified in D&C 8:6-9 as "the gift of Aaron"; (2) the gift of translation, offered in D&C 6:25-27 and revoked in D&C 9:5.

### D&C 9 — the failed translation attempt (read in full)

> "1. Behold, I say unto you, my son, that because you did not translate according to that which you desired of me, and did commence again to write…
> 5. And, behold, it is because that you did not continue as you commenced, when you began to translate, that I have taken away this privilege from you."
> — [D&C 9:1, 5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/9?lang=eng&id=p1#p1)

**Crucial structural observation:** the translation gift was *taken away*. But D&C 8:8 had said of the gift of Aaron, "no power shall be able to take it away out of your hands." These are therefore two distinct gifts. The translation gift was conditional and revocable; the gift of Aaron was foundational and inalienable. Oliver kept the latter even after failing at the former.

### Exodus 4 — the call of Aaron (read in full)

The structure is decisive ([Ex. 4:10-17, 27-31](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/ex/4?lang=eng)):

- v.10: Moses says he is "slow of speech, and of a slow tongue."
- v.14: "the anger of the Lord was kindled against Moses" — but the solution is Aaron, not a replacement of Moses.
- v.15-16: "thou shalt speak unto him, and put words in his mouth: and I will be with thy mouth, and with his mouth, and will teach you what ye shall do. And he shall be thy spokesman unto the people: **and he shall be, even he shall be to thee instead of a mouth, and thou shalt be to him instead of God.**"
- v.16 footnote 16b: "A prophet is a spokesman; hence he speaks for, or instead of, God." Aaron is a *prophet to the prophet's mouth* — the visible mediator who delivers what cannot be heard direct.
- v.17: "And thou shalt take this rod in thine hand, wherewith thou shalt do signs." (Rod is given to Moses, not Aaron, at this point.)
- v.20: "Moses took the rod of God in his hand."
- v.27-31: Aaron meets Moses in the wilderness, "Moses told Aaron all the words of the Lord… And Aaron spake all the words which the Lord had spoken unto Moses, and did the signs in the sight of the people. And the people believed."

**What Aaron does:** he carries his brother-prophet's words into the people's hearing, and he performs the signs that authenticate them. The rod and the words alike originate with Moses (and behind Moses, with God). Aaron's gift is to *be the visible bearer* of what is properly his brother's.

This is the structural answer the 1835 revision is reaching for. "The gift of Aaron" is the gift of going forward as the spokesman, witness, and sign-bearer for the prophet who cannot do it alone. Oliver, like Aaron, was given to a prophet who was struggling and behind in the work.

### Numbers 17 — Aaron's rod buds (read in full)

Twelve tribal rods placed in the tabernacle overnight. Initially Aaron's rod is indistinguishable from the rest ([Num. 17:6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/num/17?lang=eng): "the rod of Aaron was among their rods"). In the morning ([v.8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/num/17?lang=eng&id=p8#p8)):

> "the rod of Aaron for the house of Levi was budded, and brought forth buds, and bloomed blossoms, and yielded almonds."

Then v.10: "Bring Aaron's rod again before the testimony, to be kept for a token against the rebels." The rod is preserved in the ark of the covenant.

**Aaron's authority is shown by what God causes to live in his rod.** Not by anything Aaron does to it. The instrument is ordinary; the life is divine. This is the typological mirror to D&C 8:8 — "no power shall be able to take it away out of your hands, for it is the work of God." The work is God's; the hand is Aaron's; the instrument is the witness between them.

## Apostolic/institutional readings of D&C 8:6

### Joseph Fielding Smith — *Church History and Modern Revelation* 1:52

> "There was another gift bestowed upon Oliver Cowdery, and that was the gift of Aaron. Like Aaron with his rod in his hand going before Moses as a spokesman, so Oliver Cowdery was to go before Joseph Smith. Whatever he should ask the Lord by power of this gift should be granted if asked in faith and in wisdom. Oliver was blessed with the great honor of holding the keys of this dispensation with Joseph Smith, and, like Aaron, did become a spokesman on numerous occasions. It was Oliver who delivered the first public discourse in this dispensation."

Quoted in the official LDS *D&C Student Manual* (sections 1–9, section 8). This is the canonical institutional reading: **Aaron-as-spokesman**. Pairs with the fact that Oliver, like Aaron, went out front and spoke for the prophet. Note this reading sidesteps the "rod / tangible object" question entirely.

### Smith and Sjodahl — *Commentary*, p. 44

> "Oliver Cowdery also had the 'gift of Aaron.' Aaron was the elder brother of Moses. Being prompted by the Spirit of the Lord, he met his younger brother in the wilderness and accompanied him to Egypt. He introduced him to the children of Israel in the land of Goshen. He was his spokesman before Pharaoh, and he assisted him in opening up the dispensation which Moses was commissioned to proclaim (Exodus 4:27–31). This was the gift of Aaron. In some respects Oliver Cowdery was the Aaron of the new and last dispensation."

Same Aaron-as-spokesman reading. Note also: "**assisted him in opening up the dispensation**" — this anticipates Oliver's role at the Aaronic priesthood restoration (he held the priesthood jointly), the founding of the Church, the Three Witnesses, the second elder office.

### Jeffrey G. Cannon — "Oliver Cowdery's Gift," *Revelations in Context* (2012)

Official Church publication. Frames the revelation as the Lord meeting Oliver where he already was — Oliver came to Joseph already practicing rod-revelation (a known frontier folk practice; many sincere Christians of that era believed God could speak through such instruments). The April 1829-B revelation legitimizes that gift ("there is no other power save God that can cause this thing of Nature to work in your hands") but immediately disciplines and redirects it: "trifle not with these things"; "ask that you may know the mysteries of God." Oliver is then offered a *larger* gift — translation — which he tries in D&C 9 and fails.

### Dallin H. Oaks — "Recent Events Involving Church History and Forged Documents," Ensign, October 1987

> "It should be recognized that such tools as the Urim and Thummim, the Liahona, seerstones, and other articles have been used appropriately in biblical, Book of Mormon, and modern times by those who have the gift and authority to obtain revelation from God in connection with their use."

Direct institutional acknowledgement that tangible objects can be lawful instruments of revelation **when held by those with the gift and authority**. The criterion is not the object; it is the person and their authority.

## Aaron as the bearer of his brother's rod

Three things to notice from the OT once those passages are read in Phase 2:

1. **Aaron is given a rod, but it is also "Moses's rod" and "the rod of God."** Exodus uses all three names for what appears to be the same instrument (e.g. Ex. 4:2 — Moses's rod; Ex. 4:20 — "the rod of God"; Ex. 7:9-10 — "Aaron took the rod"). The rod's power is not Aaron's; he carries his brother-prophet's rod and at God's command performs signs with it.
2. **Aaron is the spokesman, not the source.** "He shall be thy spokesman unto the people: and he shall be, even he shall be to thee instead of a mouth, and thou shalt be to him instead of God" (Ex. 4:16). The signs Aaron does are Moses's signs. The words Aaron speaks are Moses's words. Aaron's gift is to *carry* what is properly his brother's.
3. **Numbers 17: Aaron's rod is chosen because it lives.** Twelve rods in the tabernacle, one for each tribe, and Aaron's alone buds. The rod's authority is shown by life — by what God causes to grow in it. This is the typological mirror to D&C 8:8 ("it is the work of God").

This is the structural answer the 1835 revision is reaching for. Oliver, like Aaron, carries an instrument that is not his own (the gift comes only by God's power), and his calling is to go ahead of the prophet — to speak, witness, and assist in opening the dispensation.

## Gap & Critical Analysis

### Ring check on the binding question

The binding question was: *What is "the gift of Aaron" in D&C 8:6, and does it relate to the Aaronic Priesthood?*

Where the text led:
1. The gift in v.6 is a revelatory gift Oliver Cowdery already possessed when he came to Joseph (confirmed by D&C 6:10's past tense "thou hast"). The 1829 manuscript names it "the gift of working with the sprout"; the 1833 *Book of Commandments* renames it "the gift of working with the rod"; the 1835 D&C renames it "the gift of Aaron." All three name the same gift.
2. The 1835 revision is not erasure — it is interpretation. "Aaron" specifies what the rod *meant*: an instrument carried by the brother-spokesman of a prophet, used at God's command, never the source of its own power.
3. It is **not** the Aaronic Priesthood office. D&C 8 was given April 1829; the Aaronic Priesthood was restored May 15, 1829. Oliver did not yet hold the office.
4. But it is *related* to it by type. Oliver would, weeks later, kneel beside Joseph and receive together the priesthood that bears Aaron's name. He would later be ordained Second Elder, joint holder of the keys of this dispensation, the prophet's spokesman. The gift named in v.6 typifies the office he was about to be given.

So the answer is layered: not identity, but type. The verse names a revelatory gift; the office that comes weeks later is what the gift typifies.

### Posture check — am I reading to discover or confirm?

The cleanest version of the thesis is "the gift of Aaron is decisively NOT the Aaronic Priesthood." That is structurally true at the level of office. But if I stop there, I miss what Joseph Fielding Smith was reaching for and what the 1835 revision was doing. The text is reaching across the few weeks between April and May 1829 — between the personal gift Oliver brought and the priesthood he was about to receive — and naming both after the same biblical figure. That is not accidental. It is a typological hinge.

I have to write the study so that it surfaces the no (the office is not the gift) *and* the yes (the gift typifies the office), without collapsing them.

### What voices am I missing?

- **The "divining rod" reading.** I should name it plainly. Elder Oaks' 1987 framing is the right pastoral touch: tangible instruments can be lawful when held by those with the gift and authority. The criterion is not the object but the person.
- **The Joseph Fielding Smith reading.** I almost wrote past it because the Aaron-as-spokesman move felt obvious. It's the canonical institutional reading and I should foreground it, not skip past it.
- **Cannon's *Revelations in Context* essay.** Official Church publication, 2012/2016. Treats the same material I'm covering. I'm not departing from it; I'm leaning on it.

### Weakest link

The Aaronic Priesthood timing argument (April 1829 vs. May 15, 1829) is solid as a chronological fact, but it could be overplayed. A pushback would be: "the canonical text is the 1835 revision, and by the time the Saints first read 'gift of Aaron' in print, the Aaronic Priesthood had been restored. The verse always reaches across that hinge." That's right. The study should not read as "the priesthood reading is wrong" — it should read as "the priesthood reading by itself is incomplete, because v.6 is naming Oliver's existing personal gift, not the office he would shortly receive."
