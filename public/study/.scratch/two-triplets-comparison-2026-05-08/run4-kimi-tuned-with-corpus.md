# Bringeth Unto Me: The Two Triplets and the Door of Salvation

**Binding question:** How do the triplets Faith, Hope, and Charity and The Way, The Truth, The Life interrelate? Are they the same concepts viewed from different angles, or genuinely different? Is one human-centered and the other Christ-centered? Are they the same point seen from different vantage points?

> "Thomas saith unto him, Lord, we know not whither thou goest; and how can we know the way?"  
> — [John 14:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/14?lang=eng&id=p5#p5)

Thomas asked for directions. Jesus answered with Himself: "I am the way, the truth, and the life" ([John 14:6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/14?lang=eng&id=p6#p6)). Six centuries later and an ocean away, Moroni wrote: "Wherefore, there must be faith; and if there must be faith there must also be hope; and if there must be hope there must also be charity" ([Moroni 10:20](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/10?lang=eng&id=p20#p20)). One triplet is Christ's identity. The other is the soul's transformation. The binding question is whether they are two names for one thing, or two things that happen to converge.

---

## Thomas asked for directions, and Jesus gave Himself

The setting of John 14 matters. Jesus has just washed the disciples' feet, instituted the sacrament, and predicted His betrayal. He is about to enter Gethsemane and pass through the veil of death. Thomas wants a map. Jesus gives a person.

The [way-truth-life](way-truth-life.md) study traces how this triplet maps onto the tabernacle itself. The ancient house of God had three spaces: the outer court, the holy place, and the holy of holies. The author of Hebrews makes the connection explicit:

> "Having therefore, brethren, boldness to enter into the holiest by the blood of Jesus, By a new and living way, which he hath consecrated for us, through the veil, that is to say, his flesh." — [Hebrews 10:19-20](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/heb/10?lang=eng&id=p19-p20#p19)

Christ is the way *through the veil*. He is not a signpost pointing toward the veil; He is the veil's piercing. The way-truth-life study notes that this same progression is enacted in the modern temple endowment: creation, fall, law and covenants, passage through the veil into the celestial room. The initiate physically walks the way, receives the truth, and is brought to life. Christ does not merely show the path; He IS the path, the knowledge, and the arrival.

The faith-hope-charity triplet operates with a different grammar. Moroni calls it an inseparable chain:

> "Wherefore, there must be faith; and if there must be faith there must also be hope; and if there must be hope there must also be charity." — [Moroni 10:20](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/10?lang=eng&id=p20#p20)

> "And except ye have charity ye can in nowise be saved in the kingdom of God; neither can ye be saved in the kingdom of God if ye have not faith; neither can ye if ye have no hope." — [Moroni 10:21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/10?lang=eng&id=p21#p21)

Remove one link and the structure collapses. The [enoch-charity](enoch-charity.md) study presses the point: "They are like a three-legged stool." Faith is the foundation, hope is the endurance, charity is the expression. Ether compresses the whole architecture into a single clause:

> "Faith, hope and charity bringeth unto me — the fountain of all righteousness." — [Ether 12:28](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/ether/12?lang=eng&id=p28#p28)

On its own terms, each triplet carries a distinct direction. The way-truth-life triplet declares who Christ is, objectively, whether any human being responds or not. The faith-hope-charity triplet declares what the human soul must become, subjectively, and it collapses without human participation. "If ye have no hope ye must needs be in despair; and despair cometh because of iniquity" ([Moroni 10:22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/10?lang=eng&id=p22#p22)). Despair is a human state. "No man cometh unto the Father, but by me" is true regardless of human state. Before any synthesis is attempted, the two triplets speak different grammatical persons.

---

## Scripture builds in threes because the structure is real

The [hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs](hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs.md) study notices that the gospel arrives in pairs and triads constantly: sacrifice and obedience, laws and rights and ordinances, spirit and element. Most are structural, not decorative. The faith-hope-charity order is doctrinally established by three independent writers. Paul: "And now abideth faith, hope, charity, these three; but the greatest of these is charity" ([1 Corinthians 13:13](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/1-cor/13?lang=eng&id=p13#p13)). Moroni: "if there must be faith there must also be hope; and if there must be hope there must also be charity" ([Moroni 10:20](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/10?lang=eng&id=p20#p20)). Ether: "Faith, hope and charity bringeth unto me" ([Ether 12:28](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/ether/12?lang=eng&id=p28#p28)). Three writers, separated by oceans and centuries. The corpus treats this as an application of the witness law.

The order matters. Faith trusts the unseen Christ. Hope orients the soul toward what He has promised. Charity is what the soul becomes when that orientation has done its work. The [hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs](hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs.md) study states it plainly: "The triplet is a single transformation viewed at three stages." It is not a checklist of three separate virtues. It is one assimilation observed at three moments.

The way-truth-life triplet has its own internal progression. The [way-truth-life](way-truth-life.md) study shows that the three degrees of glory echo the same three-part movement: telestial, terrestrial, celestial. Christ as "the way" corresponds to entry and obedience; as "the truth" to knowledge and revelation; as "the life" to arrival in God's presence. The progression mirrors D&C 93:12-13: Christ "received not of the fulness at the first, but received grace for grace... continued from grace to grace, until he received a fulness."

Therefore both triplets are ordered sequences, not random clusters. But their sequences move differently. Faith-hope-charity describes the soul's maturation. Way-truth-life describes the temple's architecture, which is also the cosmos's architecture, which is also Christ's nature.

## The hinge is the Prototype

The two triplets meet at a specific doctrinal hinge. The [hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs](hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs.md) study draws on Lectures on Faith 7:

> "He proposed to make them like unto himself; and he was like the Father, the great prototype of all saved beings: And for any portion of the human family to be assimilated into their likeness is to be saved; and to be unlike them is to be destroyed: and on this hinge turns the door of salvation."

The hinge is assimilation. The human triplet is the mechanism of assimilation. The Christ triplet is the Prototype into whom the soul is assimilated. This is not a vague parallel. It is a stated causal relationship: Christ is the standard, salvation is becoming like Him, and the triplet is the process that produces the likeness.

John and Mormon both witness the same mechanics. John writes:

> "Beloved, now are we the sons of God, and it doth not yet appear what we shall be: but we know that, when he shall appear, we shall be like him; for we shall see him as he is. And every man that hath this hope in him purifieth himself, even as he is pure." — [1 John 3:2-3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/1-jn/3?lang=eng&id=p2-p3#p2)

Mormon gives the identical doctrine:

> "When he shall appear we shall be like him, for we shall see him as he is; that we may have this hope; that we may be purified even as he is pure." — [Moroni 7:48](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/7?lang=eng&id=p48#p48)

The [discernment-and-the-comprehending-eye](discernment-and-the-comprehending-eye.md) study reads this as progressive purification of the perceptual organ. Charity makes us like Christ; being like Christ allows us to see Him as He is. "Like cleaveth unto like." The same ontological law from D&C 88:40, applied to the deepest case. The eye that has been purified by charity finally sees "things as they are," which is exactly what Christ as "the truth" embodies. D&C 93:24 defines truth as "knowledge of things as they are, and as they were, and as they are to come." Charity, in Mormon's list, "rejoiceth not in iniquity but rejoiceth in the truth" ([Moroni 7:45](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/7?lang=eng&id=p45#p45)). The lens that loves as God loves is the same lens that sees as God sees.

Therefore the convergence is real, but it is a convergence of process and Person, not a convergence of identical concepts. The soul does not become "the way"; the soul becomes like the One who is the Way. The soul does not become "the truth"; the soul is purified until it can finally see the Truth as He is.

## They are not the same point from different vantage points

The binding question asks whether the two triplets are "the same concepts viewed from different angles." The corpus says no, with a qualification.

The [discernment-and-the-comprehending-eye](discernment-and-the-comprehending-eye.md) study calls faith, hope, and charity "the progressive purification of the perceiver" — a chain that changes what the soul is so the soul can finally comprehend "things as they are." The Christ triplet, by contrast, names the reality the perceiver is moving toward: the Way, the Truth, and the Life. The argument that they are the same point from different vantage points is strong enough to be tempting, but it requires a qualification: they are convergent complements, not identical concepts.

The [way-truth-life](way-truth-life.md) study emphasizes the exclusivity of the Christ-triplet. "No man cometh unto the Father, but by me." This is true whether anyone believes it. The [enoch-charity](enoch-charity.md) study shows the human triplet's dependence on human response: "If ye have no hope ye must needs be in despair." One is Christ's identity; the other is the soul's transformation. They converge because the transformation is into His likeness, but they are not interchangeable labels for the same thing.

The "us-centric vs. Christ-centered" frame is therefore partially true but incomplete. Better: one triplet names the vessel being prepared, and the other names the filling. One names the journey, and the other names the Destination who is also the Path. The frame that treats them as "two vantage points on the same point" misses the directionality. The human triplet is moving toward the Christ triplet. The Christ triplet is not moving toward the human triplet. The movement is one-way, which is why assimilation language is more accurate than symmetry language.

There is a real tension here, and the corpus does not resolve it by flattening. The [tree-of-life-and-the-chain](tree-of-life-and-the-chain.md) study maps the faith-hope-charity chain onto the tree of life and identifies the fruit as Christ Himself. The fruit is the love of God shed abroad, which is Christ in the flesh. The chain grows toward the fruit, but the chain is not the fruit. The tree is not the sun it turns toward.

---

## The temple is where the vessel meets the filling

The temple is the physical place where both triplets operate simultaneously. The [way-truth-life](way-truth-life.md) study shows that the endowment enacts the journey from Fall to Law to Celestial room, mirroring the way-truth-life progression. The initiate learns the way, receives truth, and is brought to life by passing through the veil. Hebrews 10:20 identifies that veil as Christ's flesh.

The [hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs](hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs.md) study adds the human triplet's temple dimension. Hope is an anchor "which entereth into that within the veil" ([Hebrews 6:19](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/heb/6?lang=eng&id=p19#p19)). The temple endowment ends with the initiate passing through a veil into a celestial room, "a room built to feel like the place hope is anchored to." The temple is "hope's anchor made visible."

The [discernment-and-the-comprehending-eye](discernment-and-the-comprehending-eye.md) study supplies the eye. D&C 88:67-68 promises that the single eye fills the body with light so it "comprehendeth all things," and then the Lord "will unveil his face unto you." The veil that hope enters is the veil that gets pulled back. The human triplet prepares the eye; the Christ triplet is what the eye finally sees when the veil opens.

Therefore the temple does not choose between the two triplets. It enacts both at once. The initiate walks by faith, is sustained by hope, and is purified by charity, all while physically moving along the way, through the truth, toward the life. The temple is where "us-centric" preparation meets Christ-centered reality without collapsing the two into each other.

## Becoming

The binding question lands here: what does this mean for how the soul lives this week?

First, stop treating faith, hope, and charity as a checklist of three virtues to work on separately. The [hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs](hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs.md) study calls the triplet "a single transformation viewed at three stages." Pray for the whole chain, not the individual links. "Pray unto the Father with all the energy of heart, that ye may be filled with this love" ([Moroni 7:48](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/7?lang=eng&id=p48#p48)), and trust that the love bestowed will pull faith and hope along with it.

Second, keep the directionality straight. The goal is not to become a more hopeful person or a more charitable person as an end in itself. The goal is to be assimilated into the likeness of the Prototype. When Moroni says "we shall see him as he is," he means that the eye is being tuned to a specific Object. Ask yourself this week: what is my eye actually fixed on? If it is fixed on my own improvement, I am looking at the wrong thing. If it is fixed on Christ, improvement is a byproduct.

Third, use the temple as the enacted check. The next time you attend, notice that you are physically walking both triplets. You are moving by covenant (faith), toward a promised presence (hope), through a veil that represents the flesh of Christ (charity's object). The temple does not let you separate the process from the Person. Neither should your daily prayer.

Fourth, where hope is dim, do not try to manufacture optimism. Hope is the orientation of the eye toward something promised but not yet visible. Repentance, in this frame, is re-aiming the eye. The [discernment-and-the-comprehending-eye](discernment-and-the-comprehending-eye.md) study puts it sharply: "The fight to keep hope is the fight to keep the eye where it must stay." This week, identify the specific distraction that has pulled your eye off the prototype. Name it. Then do the small act of turning back.

Fifth, charity is obtained, not achieved. The [charity](charity.md) study records a six-month prayer for charity that transformed into a prayer to see others as Christ sees them. That is the pattern. Ask for the gift. Expect it to change what you see before it changes what you do.
