# Two Triplets, One Road

> *"Jesus saith unto him, I am the way, the truth, and the life: no man cometh unto the Father, but by me."* — [John 14:6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/14?lang=eng&id=p6#p6)

Thomas had just asked a practical question. *"Lord, we know not whither thou goest; and how can we know the way?"* ([John 14:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/14?lang=eng&id=p5#p5)). He wanted directions. Jesus gave him a Person.

Six centuries later, on an American desert island, Moroni would write three words in the same sequence: *"Wherefore, there must be faith; and if there must be faith there must also be hope; and if there must be hope there must also be charity"* ([Moroni 10:20](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/10?lang=eng&id=p20#p20)).

Both triplets arrive in threes. Both point toward God. Both use the word *way* — one literally, the other in the walking language that frames them. They look like the same truth dressed differently. But are they?

---

## The two triplets describe the same motion from different standpoints

The substrate already holds deep treatment of each triplet on its own. The [faith-hope-charity chain](enoch-charity.md) is traced through Moroni 7, Ether 12, and the tree of life vision. The [way-truth-life declaration](way-truth-life.md) is unpacked through the tabernacle, the temple endowment, and Christ's "I am" statements. The [hope study](hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs.md) identifies the triplet as *"a single transformation viewed at three stages."*

The first thing that stands out is perspective. The faith-hope-charity triplet describes *what happens to the soul*. Faith is the trust we exercise. Hope is the orientation we hold. Charity is what we become. These are traveler's terms — they name the experience of the one who walks.

The way-truth-life triplet describes *what Christ IS*. He is the way. He is the truth. He is the life. These are road terms — they name the nature of the path itself.

The [way-truth-life study](way-truth-life.md) makes this plain: *"Jesus responds not with directions but with Himself — He IS the way."* Hebrews 10:20 calls Christ's flesh *"a new and living way, which he hath consecrated for us, through the veil."* The road is not parallel to Christ. The road *is* Christ.

So the structural difference is this: faith-hope-charity reports the journey from the traveler's eyes. Way-truth-life reports the same journey from the road's perspective. One is the view from the feet. One is the view from the ground beneath them.

---

## Therefore both triplets terminate at the same reality

The [hope study](hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs.md) already established that the faith-hope-charity chain ends at assimilation. Mormon's closing verse on charity is dense:

> *"When he shall appear we shall be like him, for we shall see him as he is; that we may have this hope; that we may be purified even as he is pure."* — [Moroni 7:48](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/7?lang=eng&id=p48#p48)

The grammar runs like this: we shall see Him as He is, AND we shall be like Him. That seeing — that future vision — is what Mormon calls *"this hope."* And holding this hope is what purifies us into His likeness. John says the same thing in 1 John 3:2-3. *Lectures on Faith* 7 builds the whole theology of salvation on it: Christ is the prototype, and *"on this hinge turns the door of salvation."*

The way-truth-life triplet terminates at the same place. The [truth-enjoy study](truth-enjoy.md) traces the chain from D&C 84:44-45: *"For the word of the Lord is truth, and whatsoever is truth is light, and whatsoever is light is Spirit, even the Spirit of Jesus Christ."* The words of eternal life are spirit and life ([John 6:63](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/6?lang=eng&id=p63#p63)) because the Word IS Christ ([John 1:1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/1?lang=eng&id=p1#p1)). D&C 93:8-9 restates this: *"the Word was, for he was the Word, even the messenger of salvation — the light and the Redeemer of the world; the Spirit of truth."*

To receive the words is to receive Christ's substance. To walk the way is to walk through Him. To arrive at the life is to arrive at Him.

The [tree-of-life study](tree-of-life-and-the-chain.md) puts it this way: the fruit of the tree is the love of God, and the love of God is Christ Himself. *"To partake of the fruit is to receive Christ."* The chain ends in charity, which is *"the pure love of Christ"* ([Moroni 7:47](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/7?lang=eng&id=p47#p47)) — Christ in you.

Both triplets are descriptions of the same assimilation. Faith-hope-charity names the stages of the soul's transformation. Way-truth-life names the substance through which the transformation happens. They converge on *Lectures on Faith* 7: salvation is being made like the prototype. The triplet of virtues is how you get there. The triplet of declarations is what you're made of when you arrive.

---

## But the temple is where the two triplets physically meet

The [way-truth-life study](way-truth-life.md) shows that the temple endowment enacts the way-truth-life progression: creation, fall, covenants, through the veil, into the celestial room. *"We learn THE WAY (the plan, the covenants), we receive THE TRUTH (instruction, knowledge, promises), and we are brought to THE LIFE (God's presence in the celestial room)."*

The [hope study](hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs.md) shows that the temple is also hope's anchor made visible. Hebrews 6:19 calls hope *"an anchor of the soul, both sure and steadfast, and which entereth into that within the veil."* The endowment ends with the initiate passing through a veil into a celestial room. *"We walk to where the anchor is, stand on the bottom for a few minutes, and walk back out."*

Moses 6:59-60 gives the three agents of rebirth — water, Spirit, blood — and the [truth-atonement study](truth-atonement.md) shows that the temple ordinances operate through these same three agents. The temple is not a metaphor for the triplets. It is their meeting place.

The triad of virtues qualifies you to make the journey. The way-truth-life progression is the journey itself. And the temple is the physical space where both triplets touch the earth.

---

## The triplet shape itself carries meaning

The [hope study](hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs.md) treats this question directly. The gospel arrives in pairs and triads constantly: sacrifice and obedience, laws-rights-ordinances, spirit and element, mercy and justice. *"Most are structural, not pedagogical."* When a truth carries weight that one term cannot bear, the gospel gives it as a pair or a triad.

The witness law is the foundation: *"at the mouth of two witnesses, or at the mouth of three witnesses, shall the matter be established"* ([Deuteronomy 19:15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/deut/19?lang=eng&id=p15#p15)). Christ applied this rule to His own teaching ([Matthew 18:16](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/matt/18?lang=eng&id=p16#p16)). Joseph received it as the standard for Restoration witnesses ([D&C 5:15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/5?lang=eng&id=p15#p15)). The Three Witnesses to the Book of Mormon were not chosen for drama. They were chosen because the witness law required at least that many.

When Paul, Moroni, and Mormon all give the faith-hope-charity triplet in the same order, the triplet is established as doctrine, not just a teacher's preference. When Christ declares Himself as the way, the truth, and the life — three terms in one breath — the declaration carries the evidentiary weight of three witnesses.

There is a deeper reason too. God Himself is a triplet: Father, Son, and Holy Ghost. *"The pattern of three witnessing as one is not just a courtroom rule we borrow. It is the structure of the Godhead applied to revealed truth."* When the gospel arrives in threes, it takes on the shape of its source.

The faith-hope-charity triplet covers three temporal dimensions: faith (trust in what was promised), hope (orientation toward what is promised), charity (the love that is the promise fulfilled). The way-truth-life triplet covers three spatial dimensions: way (the path), truth (the reality along the path), life (the destination). Together they form a coordinate system: time and space, traveler and road, process and substance.

---

## This is a reading, not a doctrine

No single verse states *"Faith-Hope-Charity and Way-Truth-Life are two perspectives on the same reality."* The synthesis is built by juxtaposition — placing the two triplets side by side and asking what pattern emerges. This is honest scholarship: the pieces are all canonical; the arrangement is interpretive.

The [hope study](hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs.md) already states: *"The three are not three options; they are three names for one assimilation, viewed at three stages."* The [truth-enjoy study](truth-enjoy.md) already states: *"The Word IS Christ... the words carry His substance because they proceed from His being."* The [tree-of-life study](tree-of-life-and-the-chain.md) already states: *"The fruit of the tree is Christ Himself."*

The contribution here is not new doctrine. It is the juxtaposition that makes visible what each study already implies. Faith-hope-charity answers *"What do I need to become?"* Way-truth-life answers *"Who do I become like, and through whom?"* They are two sides of one reality, and the temple is where the coin is minted.

## Becoming

**Questions to sit with:**
- When I think about my journey toward Christ, am I more aware of the traveler's experience (what I need to do) or the road's nature (who Christ is)? Is one shadowing the other?
- Where in my life am I trying to walk the way without trusting that the way IS the One who walks with me?
- When I enter the temple, do I feel the two triplets meeting — the virtues I carry and the Person I'm walking through?

**This week:**
- In scripture study, when I encounter a faith-hope-charity passage, pause and ask: *"What would this look like from the road's perspective? What is Christ being here?"* And when I encounter a way-truth-life passage, ask the reverse: *"What is happening in the soul of the one who walks this?"*
- Before a difficult conversation, pray: *"Lord, I am trying to walk the way. Help me remember that You ARE the way — not just a set of directions I'm trying to follow."*

**Connected to:** [becoming/charity.md](../becoming/charity.md) — This study reinforces that charity is not something we manufacture but something we receive. The way-truth-life triplet reminds us that the road is Christ Himself, and walking it means walking through Him, not alongside Him.
