# Scratch — The Stewardship Pattern: How God Delegates

## Binding Question
How does God delegate, and what does His pattern teach about presiding over human stewardships — especially when you're better at delegating to machines than to people?

## Observations

### Exodus 18
- Jethro is an outsider with priesthood authority who sees what Moses can't see from inside the work
- The diagnosis has TWO victims: "thou wilt surely wear away, **both thou, and this people**" — burnout hurts the leader AND the led
- The selection criteria for delegates (v21): able, God-fearing, truth-loving, covetousness-hating. Character first, competence implied.
- v19-20 preserves Moses' unique role: "be thou for the people to God-ward" and "teach them ordinances and laws." Moses doesn't delegate his prophetic function — only the judging.
- The structure is hierarchical: thousands, hundreds, fifties, tens. Escalation protocol: small matters handled locally, great matters go up.
- v23 makes delegation conditional: "If thou shalt do this thing, **and God command thee so**" — Jethro adds the caveat that God must confirm.
- v18a footnote to D&C 10:4 is stunning: "Do not run faster or labor more than you have strength" — the exact Mosiah 4:27 principle, cross-referenced right here in the OT delegation text.

## Observations (continued)

### Matthew 10
- Christ gives **power** first (v1), then **boundaries** (v5-7: go here, not there), then **tools** (v8: heal, cleanse, cast out), then **identity framing** (v16: sheep among wolves, wise as serpents).
- He warns of failure (v17-18: they WILL be persecuted, delivered up, hated) but promises provision (v19-20: the Spirit will speak through you). Delegation isn't protecting from hardship — it's equipping for it.
- v40: "He that receiveth you receiveth me" — the delegate carries the authority of the delegator. This is the same chain Luke 10:16 makes explicit.
- The footnote on v1a leading to TG "Delegation of Responsibility" — the Church's own cross-reference system recognizes this as a formalized concept.

### Luke 10
- The Seventy are sent "two and two" — partnership in delegation. Not solo operators.
- v2: "the harvest is great, but the labourers are few" — the problem isn't lack of work, it's lack of workers. Delegation isn't offloading, it's multiplying.
- v17: They come back successful AND joyful. Delegation creates capacity AND joy in the delegate.
- v20: Christ immediately reframes success. Don't rejoice in your performance ("spirits are subject unto you") — rejoice in your identity ("your names are written in heaven"). Performance-based delegation is fragile. Identity-based delegation is durable.

### Abraham 4-5
- The Gods **counsel** among themselves (v26) before acting. Delegation starts with shared deliberation, not unilateral assignment.
- "Watched those things which they had ordered until they obeyed" (v18) — oversight without micromanagement. They don't do the work themselves. They watch the work and verify obedience.
- "They would be obeyed, and that their plan was good" (v21) — evaluation is about obedience AND goodness. Not just compliance but direction.
- Abraham 5 is the execution phase: "the Gods came down and formed" (v4). They planned in 4, executed in 5. The spiritual creation (planning) precedes the physical creation (execution).
- v5: "According to all that which they had said concerning every plant of the field **before it was in the earth**" — execution follows the plan exactly. Specification before implementation.
- v20: Adam is given naming authority. The Gods don't name the animals — Adam does. "Whatsoever Adam called every living creature, that should be the name thereof." This is delegation of creative authority, not just task execution.

## Observations (additional sources)

### Mosiah 18
- One priest to every fifty (v18) — same ratio as Jethro's "rulers of fifties" in Exodus 18:21. Same delegation pattern, same number, different dispensation. Coincidence or revealed structure?
- Alma constrains what priests may teach (v19-20): only what Alma taught, only what the prophets spoke — "repentance and faith on the Lord." Delegation with doctrinal guardrails. Not creative freedom — faithful transmission.
- Priests labor with their own hands (v24) — they're not clergy class. They work. This prevents the delegation from creating a dependent hierarchy.
- v26: "for their labor they were to receive the grace of God" — the compensation for serving is spiritual growth, not temporal reward. Contrast with professional clergy models.
- v21: "hearts knit together in unity and in love" — the organizational structure is embedded in relational covenant, not bureaucratic hierarchy. The unity comes first, then the structure.
- v29: priests and teachers teach "according to the desires and wishes of the people." The delegates are responsive to those they serve. Delegation flows down AND accountability flows up AND responsiveness flows sideways.

### D&C 10:4
- "Do not run faster or labor more than you have strength **and means provided**" — not just your own strength, but the means God provides. If God hasn't provided the means, the labor isn't required yet.
- "But be diligent unto the end" — the correction isn't less work. It's paced work. Diligence without destruction.
- This verse was about translation (Joseph and the 116 pages aftermath), but the principle transcends the context. Cross-referenced in Mosiah 4:27 and Exodus 18:18.

### Numbers 11
- Moses' burnout is more severe than Exodus 18: "kill me, I pray thee" (v15). This is suicidal despair, not just fatigue. The weight of undelegated responsibility nearly destroys the prophet.
- God's solution is NOT organizational reorganization (that was Jethro's). God's solution is **spiritual transfer**: "I will take of the spirit which is upon thee, and will put it upon them" (v17). Delegation in God's pattern involves sharing the Spirit, not just distributing tasks.
- The seventy prophesied (v25) — they didn't just judge cases or handle logistics. They received the same spiritual gift Moses had. This is empowerment, not mere employment.
- Eldad and Medad (v26-29): They stayed in camp, didn't come to the tabernacle, but prophesied anyway. Joshua's instinct is institutional control — "forbid them." Moses' response is the ideal: "would God that ALL the Lord's people were prophets." The goal of delegation isn't managed control—it's universal empowerment.
- v29 is the vision statement: not a hierarchy of the capable managing the incapable, but a people who are ALL prophets. The delegation pattern works toward its own obsolescence.

### D&C 107
- "Sit in council with them" appears for EVERY quorum level (v72, 78, 85-89). The president doesn't direct from above — he councils from within. This is Webster's "counsel" (interchange of opinions), not command.
- v91: "to be like unto Moses" — the D&C explicitly connects modern Church presidency to the Moses pattern. Full circle: Exodus 18 → Numbers 11 → D&C 107.
- v99: "let every man learn his duty" — not "let the president do every man's duty." Each person is responsible for their own stewardship. The president's job is to teach the duty, not perform it.
- v100: consequences for slothfulness AND for not learning your duty. The system expects active participation from delegates, not passive waiting for instruction.
- The presidency ratios scale with quorum size: 12 deacons, 24 teachers, 48 priests, 96 elders. The ratio of president-to-members grows as the quorum requires more maturity. Deacons get closer supervision; elders operate with more independence.
- v78: The escalation protocol: "most important business of the church... shall be carried up unto the council" — same as Jethro's "every great matter they shall bring unto thee."

## Webster 1828

**Delegate** (1828): "Any one sent and empowered to act for another; one deputed to represent; a chosen deputy." Modern sense is similar. Key insight: *empowered* to act, not just *assigned* to act. A delegate carries authority, not just tasks.

**Steward** (1828): "A man employed in a large family, or on a large estate, to manage the domestic concerns, supervise other servants, collect the rents or income, keep accounts." A steward manages what belongs to someone else. The steward doesn't own — he manages FOR the owner. In gospel context: the calling, the ward, the quorum — none of it is ours.

**Preside** (1828): "To be set, or to sit, in the place of authority... to direct, control, and regulate, as chief officer." ALSO: "To exercise superintendence; to watch over." The second definition connects directly to Abraham 4:18 — "the Gods watched those things which they had ordered until they obeyed." Presiding IS watching. Not doing the work. Watching until it obeys.

**Counsel** (1828): "Interchange of opinions; mutual advising; consultation." Also: "Result of consultation; advice; instruction" and "Deliberate purpose; design; intent; scheme; plan." Key: counsel is *interchange* — bilateral, not unilateral. When D&C 107 says the president "sits in council," it means mutual advising, not one-directional instruction. This is what Abraham 4:26 describes: "the Gods took counsel among themselves."

**Ordain** (1828): "To set in order; to arrange according to rule; to regulate." ALSO: "To set apart for an office; to appoint" and "To invest with ministerial or sacerdotal functions." The first definition is fascinating — ordain means to ORDER, to ARRANGE. Ordination is organization. When Alma "ordained priests" in Mosiah 18, he was setting the church in order.

### Mosiah 25-26 — Scaling the Church

**25:19:** "King Mosiah granted unto Alma that he might establish churches throughout all the land of Zarahemla; and gave him power to ordain priests and teachers over every church."

**25:20:** "Now this was done because there were so many people that they could not all be governed by one teacher; neither could they all hear the word of God in one assembly."

**25:21:** "Therefore they did assemble themselves together in different bodies, being called churches; every church having their priests and their teachers, and every priest preaching the word according as it was delivered to him by the mouth of Alma."

**25:22:** "And thus, notwithstanding there being many churches they were all one church, yea, even the church of God; for there was nothing preached in all the churches except it were repentance and faith in God."

**26:7:** "And it came to pass that they were brought before the priests, and delivered up unto the priests by the teachers; and the priests brought them before Alma, who was the high priest."

**26:8 footnote:** 8a cross-refs TG "Delegation of Responsibility." The Topical Guide itself flags Mosiah's grant to Alma as a delegation text.

**26:10-12:** Alma faces an unprecedented case (sinners who won't repent), takes it to king Mosiah. Mosiah says: "I judge them not; therefore I deliver them into thy hands to be judged." The king recognizes the boundary between civil and ecclesiastical authority and delegates BACK.

**26:13-14:** Alma, still uncertain, "inquired of the Lord what he should do... for he feared that he should do wrong in the sight of God." The escalation chain: teachers → priests → Alma → king → back to Alma → God.

**26:29:** God's answer gives Alma the judging standard: "whosoever transgresseth against me, him shall ye judge according to the sins which he has committed; and if he confess his sins before thee and me, and repenteth in the sincerity of his heart, him shall ye forgive, and I will forgive him also."

**26:37-38:** "Alma did regulate all the affairs of the church... And now all these things did Alma and his fellow laborers do who were over the church, walking in all diligence."

### Observations — Mosiah 25-26

- v20 is the plainest rationale for delegation in all scripture: "there were so many people that they could not all be governed by one teacher." Scale demands delegation. One person cannot hold it all.
- v21-22: "many churches" but "all one church." Structural multiplicity doesn't break unity when they all teach the same doctrine — "nothing preached except repentance and faith in God." Same constraint as Mosiah 18:19-20.
- The escalation chain in Mosiah 26 is beautiful: teachers handle what they can → pass to priests what they can't → priests pass to Alma → Alma tries the king (secular authority) → king sends it back (wrong jurisdiction) → Alma takes it to God. Every level tries, and only escalates what exceeds its capacity. Same pattern as Exodus 18:22.
- v8 footnote to TG "Delegation of Responsibility" — the Church's own scripture apparatus recognizes this as a core delegation text.
- "Alma and his fellow laborers" (v38) — not Alma alone. The delegation worked. He has fellow laborers.
