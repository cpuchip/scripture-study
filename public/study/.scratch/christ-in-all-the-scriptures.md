## IV. The types and shadows (Agent 2, all verified) — and the prophecy/type distinction

**Webster 1828 — type:** sense 1 "The mark of something; an emblem; that which represents something else"; sense 2 **"A sign; a symbol; a figure of something to come; as, Abraham's sacrifice and the paschal lamb, were types of Christ. To this word is opposed antitype. Christ, in this case, is the antitype."** (1828 itself names Christ-types in the definition — directly supports the study. NOTE: the earlier quoted "A figure or representation of something to come... A type is no longer a type when the thing typified comes to be actually exhibited" was Webster *1913*, not 1828 — replaced. The type-ceases-at-fulfillment idea is still sound doctrine via 3 Ne 15:4, just not an 1828 quotation.)

THE [EXPLICIT] TYPES (a scripture itself names each):
1. **Passover lamb** — Ex 12:5,13,46 (without blemish; the blood; "neither shall ye break a bone thereof"). Named: **1 Cor 5:7** "Christ our passover is sacrificed for us"; unbroken bone fulfilled **John 19:36**; **John 1:29** "Behold the Lamb of God." Strongest physical-detail confirmation.
2. **Brazen serpent** — Num 21:8 → **John 3:14** + **Helaman 8:14–15** + **Alma 33:19** ("a type was raised up... many did look and live" — Alma uses the word *type*). Triple-witnessed; best-attested type in the canon.
3. **Isaac on Moriah** — Gen 22:7–8 ("God will provide himself a lamb"), v12 "thine only son," v14 Jehovah-jireh ("In the mount of the Lord it shall be seen"). Named: **Jacob 4:5** "a similitude of God and his Only Begotten Son"; **Heb 11:19** received him back "in a figure." SEAM worth naming: a *ram* (not a lamb) is substituted in v13 — the promised "lamb" points past Isaac to Christ.
4. **Melchizedek** — Gen 14:18 (bread and wine, priest of the most high God); **Ps 110:4** → **Heb 5:6; 7:1–3** ("made like unto the Son of God").
5. **Tabernacle / Day of Atonement / sacrifices** — Lev 16:15–16. Named the strongest "shadow" language in scripture: **Heb 8:5** "the example and shadow of heavenly things"; **Heb 10:1** "a shadow of good things to come"; **Col 2:17** "a shadow of things to come; but the body is of Christ." **Heb 9:11–12** Christ entered "by his own blood."
6. **Manna / the smitten Rock** — Num 20:8–11; **1 Cor 10:4** "that Rock was Christ" (Paul names it); **John 6:48–51** "I am that bread of life."
7. **Jonah** — Jonah 1:17 → **Matt 12:40** (the Lord names it; cleanest resurrection foreshadow).
8. **Moses / Deut 18:15,18** ("a Prophet... like unto me") → **Acts 3:22–23**; and **Moses 1:6** "thou art in the similitude of mine Only Begotten." (Category note: Deut 18 reads as much like prophecy as type; Moses 1:6 is the cleaner type.)
9. **Sacrifice "in similitude"** — **Moses 5:6–8** "this thing is a similitude of the sacrifice of the Only Begotten of the Father." [EXPLICIT] — the FIRST thing taught to Adam.

MESSIANIC PROPHECY (spoken foretelling, distinct from enacted type — all verified):
- **Gen 3:15 / Moses 4:21** (protoevangelium) — "it/he shall bruise thy head." Moses 4:21 sharpens KJV "it" → "he."
- **Isa 7:14** (virgin/Immanuel); **Isa 9:6** (Wonderful, Counsellor, mighty God, Prince of Peace); **Isa 53** (wounded for our transgressions v5; lamb to slaughter v7; with the rich in his death v9; soul an offering for sin v10); **Ps 22:16,18** (pierced hands/feet; parted garments); **Micah 5:2** (Bethlehem; goings forth from everlasting); **Zech 9:9** (king on an ass); **Zech 12:10** (look upon me whom they pierced); **Mal 3:1–3** (the Lord comes to his temple; refiner's fire).

THE HONEST [INTERPRETIVE] LINE (rich tradition, NOT named by scripture — must be held with a lighter hand):
- **Joseph of Egypt** — beloved son, sold for silver (Gen 37:28), suffers, exalted, saves his brethren (Gen 45:4). Acts 7 and 2 Ne 3 retell him but NEVER call him a type of Christ.
- **Adam — borderline:** **Rom 5:14** calls Adam "the figure of him that was to come"; **1 Cor 15:45** "the last Adam." But the typology is by CONTRAST (Adam → death, Christ → life), not likeness. Present precisely.
- **Scapegoat** (Lev 16:21–22) — the bearing-away reading is interpretive; note it's the SECOND goat (the slain goat of v15 carries the blood-atonement).
- **Noah's ark / flood** — **1 Pet 3:20–21** makes the flood an explicit "like figure" (Greek *antitypon*) — but of BAPTISM, not directly of Christ. Useful nuance.
- **Boaz the kinsman-redeemer** (Ruth) — traditional *gōʾēl* parallel; no confirming verse exists.

ETYMOLOGY (load-bearing, with caveat): **Messiah** (Heb. *māšîaḥ*) / **Christ** (Gk. *Christos*) both = "the anointed one." "Jesus Christ" literally = "Jesus the Anointed." CAVEAT (Agent 2): Webster 1828 defines Messiah as "the expected king and deliverer... the Savior; Christ" but does NOT give the "anointed" root; the *māšîaḥ*/*Christos* = "anointed" gloss is standard lexical/Bible-Dictionary knowledge, NOT corpus-verified — present as reference-grounded; lead to confirm via webster_define / BD before quoting as fact.

## V. The Book of Mormon makes it plain (Agent 3, all verified)

- **2 Nephi 25:26** — "we talk of Christ, we rejoice in Christ, we preach of Christ, we prophesy of Christ... that our children may know to what source they may look for a remission of their sins." [EXPLICIT] — **76 citations** (most-cited of the set). The thesis verse of the BoM as Christ-witness.
- **2 Nephi 25:24,27** — "we keep the law of Moses, and look forward with steadfastness unto Christ, until the law shall be fulfilled"; "may look forward unto that life which is in Christ, and know for what end the law was given."
- **1 Nephi 19:23** — "I did liken all scriptures unto us, that it might be for our profit and learning" (in order "to persuade them to believe in the Lord their Redeemer"). [EXPLICIT] — names the METHOD.
- **2 Nephi 25:1–5** — why Isaiah is hard ("the manner of prophesying among the Jews"); "plain unto all those that are filled with the spirit of prophecy"; "my soul delighteth in plainness." The bridge between BQ1 and BQ2: the difficulty is real; plainness + the Spirit is the cure.
- **Jacob 4:4–6** — "all the holy prophets which were before us" had the hope; "the law of Moses, it pointing our souls to him"; "we search the prophets." [EXPLICIT]
- **Alma 34:13–14** (Amulek) — "this is the whole meaning of the law, every whit pointing to that great and last sacrifice; and that great and last sacrifice will be the Son of God." [EXPLICIT] — the most totalizing single-verse type-statement.
- **Mosiah 16:14–15** (Abinadi) — "if ye teach the law of Moses, also teach that it is a shadow of those things which are to come— Teach them that redemption cometh through Christ the Lord." [EXPLICIT] — the teaching imperative.
- **Alma 37:43–45** — the Liahona as a type the BoM INVENTS and decodes in one breath: "is there not a type in this thing?... it is as easy to give heed to the word of Christ... as it was for our fathers to give heed to this compass." [EXPLICIT] — the BoM generates its own types, not only re-reads the Bible's.
- **Jarom 1:11** (sleeper find) — the priests taught "persuading them to look forward unto the Messiah, and believe in him to come as though he already was." [EXPLICIT] — the doctrine of likening compressed to a phrase.
- **2 Nephi 32:3** — "feast upon the words of Christ; for behold, the words of Christ will tell you all things what ye should do."
- **1 Nephi 13:28,40** — "many plain and precious things taken away from the book"; the latter-day records "make known... that the Lamb of God is the Son of the Eternal Father, and the Savior of the world." The diagnosis + the fix: Christ got hard to see because plainness was removed; the BoM restores it.
- **Ether 3:14** — "Behold, I am Jesus Christ. I am the Father and the Son." The premortal Christ APPEARS in the oldest BoM narrative — type collapses into presence. [EXPLICIT]
- **Book of Mormon Title Page** (read from `bofm/bofm-title.md`; not retrievable by gospel_get reference) — "to the convincing of the Jew and Gentile that Jesus is the Christ, the Eternal God, manifesting himself unto all nations." The book states BQ2's answer as its reason for existing.

**Agent 3 synthesis:** The Bible ENACTS Christ-in-all-scripture (Luke 24:27 reports the exposition happened but doesn't transcribe the method); the BoM NAMES the method ("liken," "type," "shadow"), STATES the warrant (Jacob 7:11; Alma 34:14), and EXPLAINS why Christ became hard to see (1 Ne 13 plainness removed; 2 Ne 25 manner of prophesying) — then restores the plainness. Same Christ, key handed over.

## Gap & Critical Analysis (Phase 3 + 3a)

**Coverage / missing voices:** All five standard works represented — OT (IV), NT (I, III), BoM (II, V), D&C (II, VI: D&C 20:26, 138, 18), PGP (Moses 1:6, 4:21, 5:7, 6:63). Modern prophets (VI). Webster (IV). No gap in volume coverage. Could still add: a Hebrew/Greek confirm of "anointed" (see below).

**Strongest claims (bedrock — STATED, not inferred):** Jacob 7:11; 2 Ne 11:4; John 5:39; Moses 6:63; Luke 24:27/44; Alma 34:14. The [EXPLICIT] types are airtight: serpent (triple), Passover (Paul + unbroken bone), Isaac ("similitude," Jacob 4:5), Rock (1 Cor 10:4), Jonah (the Lord himself). Build on these.

**Weakest links (handle with care):** (a) the INTERPRETIVE types (Joseph, Boaz, scapegoat) — present as devotional tradition, never "scripture says." (b) The cross-quotes (Ps 22, Ps 31) as FULL typologies — the quotation is explicit, the whole-psalm correspondence is reader-recognized. (c) The Messiah/"anointed" etymology — reference-grounded, NOT corpus-verified; verify via `webster_define`/BD or present as reference, do not assert as proven fact. (d) The citation counts — only state them as "in the BYU index, this session" (Agent 4 verified).

**Framing (speculation vs doctrine):** The [EXPLICIT]/[INTERPRETIVE] split IS the study's integrity mechanism — keep the labels visible in the prose (not as jargon, but in how confidently each is asserted).

**Ring check (does it answer the binding questions?):** BQ1 (what did Jesus expound) → I + III (Luke 24:44 names law/prophets/psalms; the suffer/rise template; the named instances; the verb "expound" recurring through 3 Ne + Acts 28). BQ2 (how do we see Christ in all scripture) → II (the doctrine) + IV (the types) + V (the BoM names it). YES, both answered. The text also led to a THIRD thing the questions didn't ask: **the method/discipline of reading** — which becomes VII and links to `abinadi-how-to-read.md` (the heart as hermeneutic instrument). Name that turn honestly.

**Posture check (discover vs confirm?) — the honest complications the study MUST keep:**
1. **Luke 24:27 is nearly uncited in modern conference** — complicates any "this is the church's central proof-text" framing. The anchor is honored in practice (John 5:39 / 2 Ne 25:26), not in direct citation. Keep this; don't hide it.
2. **The thesis is NOT "Christ is obvious everywhere."** 2 Ne 25:1–5 admits Christ is HARD to see in the prophets ("hard... because of the manner of prophesying"); 1 Ne 13 says plain things were REMOVED. The honest thesis: Christ is THERE, but seeing him takes the Spirit, plainness, and an applied heart — not cleverness. This is the non-triumphalist version.
3. **The real tension to name (Abinadi-style, don't soften):** the line between FINDING Christ where scripture puts him and IMPOSING Christ where we want him. If "all things bear record of me" (Moses 6:63), is ANY Christ-reading valid? No — the guardrail: the surest types are those scripture itself names; likening (1 Ne 19:23) is authorized but disciplined by the text, the Spirit, and the living prophets, not by our ingenuity. The [EXPLICIT] cases anchor the [INTERPRETIVE] ones; never the reverse.
