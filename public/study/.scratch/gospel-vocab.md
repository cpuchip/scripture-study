# Gospel Vocabulary — Source Log

*Working document. Kept as research provenance — traces how observations were reached.*
*Binding question: What theological patterns does a local LLM need in its context window to score typological depth — and how do we express them densely enough to fit in ~3,500 tokens without inflating scores?*

---

## Outline

1. Doctrine of Christ (3 Ne 11, 3 Ne 27, 2 Ne 31, AofF 1:4)
2. Tree of Life = Love of God = Christ (1 Ne 11)
3. The Word = Christ (John 1, Alma 32-33)
4. "All Things Testify of Christ" (Moses 6:63, 2 Ne 11:4)
5. Faith, Hope, Charity (Moroni 7, Ether 12, 1 Cor 13)
6. "Love Shed Abroad" (1 Ne 11:22, Rom 5:5, Moro 8:26)
7. Types and Shadows (brass serpent, paschal lamb, living water, bread of life)
8. First Principles and Ordinances (AofF 1:4, 2 Ne 31, 3 Ne 27)

## Verified Quotes

### [3 Nephi 11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/11?lang=eng) — 3 Nephi 11:31-40
> "And this is my doctrine, and it is the doctrine which the Father hath given unto me; and I bear record of the Father, and the Father beareth record of me, and the Holy Ghost beareth record of the Father and me; and I bear record that the Father commandeth all men, everywhere, to repent and believe in me." (v. 32)

> "And whoso believeth in me, and is baptized, the same shall be saved; and they are they who shall inherit the kingdom of God." (v. 33)

> "Verily, verily, I say unto you, that this is my doctrine, and I bear record of it from the Father; and whoso believeth in me believeth in the Father also; and unto him will the Father bear record of me, for he will visit him with fire and with the Holy Ghost." (v. 35)

> "Verily, verily, I say unto you, that this is my doctrine, and whoso buildeth upon this buildeth upon my rock, and the gates of hell shall not prevail against them." (v. 39)

> "And whoso shall declare more or less than this, and establish it for my doctrine, the same cometh of evil, and is not built upon my rock" (v. 40)

**Observation:** Christ declares "my doctrine" THREE TIMES in vv. 31-39. The doctrine is: repent, believe, be baptized → saved, inherit kingdom. The Father and Holy Ghost bear witness of this. Building on this = building on rock. Declaring more or less = evil. This is the most explicit, first-person doctrinal statement in all scripture. → Section 1
**Connects to:** v. 35 — "fire and the Holy Ghost" = the mechanism of bearing record. Connects to Pattern 6 (love shed abroad via Holy Ghost).

### [3 Nephi 27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/27?lang=eng) — 3 Nephi 27:13-21
> "Behold I have given unto you my gospel, and this is the gospel which I have given unto you—that I came into the world to do the will of my Father, because my Father sent me." (v. 13)

> "And my Father sent me that I might be lifted up upon the cross; and after that I had been lifted up upon the cross, that I might draw all men unto me" (v. 14)

> "And it shall come to pass, that whoso repenteth and is baptized in my name shall be filled; and if he endureth to the end, behold, him will I hold guiltless before my Father at that day when I shall stand to judge the world." (v. 16)

> "Now this is the commandment: Repent, all ye ends of the earth, and come unto me and be baptized in my name, that ye may be sanctified by the reception of the Holy Ghost, that ye may stand spotless before me at the last day." (v. 20)

> "Verily, verily, I say unto you, this is my gospel; and ye know the things that ye must do in my church; for the works which ye have seen me do that shall ye also do" (v. 21)

**Observation:** This is Christ's SECOND first-person gospel definition. Where 3 Ne 11 emphasizes the *doctrinal elements* (believe, repent, be baptized, Holy Ghost), 3 Ne 27 adds the *narrative arc*: Christ came to do the Father's will → was lifted up on the cross → draws all men → those who repent and are baptized are sanctified by the Holy Ghost → stand spotless. The gospel IS Christ's mission and atonement, not just a list of steps. v. 20 adds "sanctified by the reception of the Holy Ghost" — 3 Ne 11 says "fire and Holy Ghost" as witness, 3 Ne 27 says Holy Ghost as sanctifier. → Section 1
**Connects to:** v. 14 "lifted up" echoes Moses' serpent (Pattern 7, Types). v. 27 — "what manner of men ought ye to be? Even as I am" — connects to Pattern 5 (character trajectory).

### [2 Nephi 31](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/31?lang=eng) — 2 Nephi 31:2-21
> "Wherefore, the things which I have written sufficeth me, save it be a few words which I must speak concerning the doctrine of Christ" (v. 2)

> "the gate by which ye should enter is repentance and baptism by water; and then cometh a remission of your sins by fire and by the Holy Ghost." (v. 17)

> "And then are ye in this strait and narrow path which leads to eternal life; yea, ye have entered in by the gate; ye have done according to the commandments of the Father and the Son; and ye have received the Holy Ghost, which witnesses of the Father and the Son" (v. 18)

> "Wherefore, ye must press forward with a steadfastness in Christ, having a perfect brightness of hope, and a love of God and of all men. Wherefore, if ye shall press forward, feasting upon the word of Christ, and endure to the end, behold, thus saith the Father: Ye shall have eternal life." (v. 20)

> "And now, behold, my beloved brethren, this is the way; and there is none other way nor name given under heaven whereby man can be saved in the kingdom of God. And now, behold, this is the doctrine of Christ, and the only and true doctrine of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Ghost, which is one God, without end." (v. 21)

**Observation:** Nephi's formulation adds what 3 Ne 11/27 don't: the GATE metaphor (repentance + baptism = entering the gate), the PATH metaphor (press forward with steadfastness, hope, love), and the "feasting upon the word of Christ" language — which connects to Pattern 3 (the word = Christ). v. 20 gives the enduring formula: steadfastness + hope + love + feast on the word + endure = eternal life. This is the fullest single-verse description of the doctrine of Christ in practice. → Section 1
**Connects to:** "feasting upon the word of Christ" (v. 20) doubles: feasting on scripture AND feasting on Christ himself (Pattern 3). "Perfect brightness of hope, and a love of God" maps to Pattern 5 (faith, hope, charity). "The Holy Ghost, which witnesses of the Father and the Son" (v. 18) = Pattern 6 mechanism.

### [1 Nephi 11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/11?lang=eng) — 1 Nephi 11:21-25, 33
> "Behold the Lamb of God, yea, even the Son of the Eternal Father! Knowest thou the meaning of the tree which thy father saw?" (v. 21)

> "Yea, it is the love of God, which sheddeth itself abroad in the hearts of the children of men; wherefore, it is the most desirable above all things." (v. 22)

> "And he spake unto me, saying: Yea, and the most joyous to the soul." (v. 23)

> "I beheld that the rod of iron, which my father had seen, was the word of God, which led to the fountain of living waters, or to the tree of life; which waters are a representation of the love of God; and I also beheld that the tree of life was a representation of the love of God." (v. 25)

> "And I, Nephi, saw that he was lifted up upon the cross and slain for the sins of the world." (v. 33)

**Observation:** The interpretive key for the ENTIRE tree of life vision. The angel shows Nephi Christ's birth (v. 18-20), then asks "Knowest thou the meaning of the tree?" — Nephi answers "the love of God." The tree, the fruit, the living waters = the love of God = Christ. v. 25 explicitly identifies: rod of iron = word of God, waters = love of God, tree = love of God. The angel's response to showing the tree is to show Christ's life and death. The condescension of God IS the tree — the love of God made flesh. → Section 2
**Connects to:** v. 22 "sheddeth itself abroad" — exact phrase that connects to Rom 5:5 and Moro 8:26 (Pattern 6). v. 25 "the word of God" = the rod of iron — connects to Pattern 3 (word = Christ). v. 33 "lifted up upon the cross" = type language, connects to brass serpent (Pattern 7). v. 17 "I know that he loveth his children" — Kearon's talk builds on this (footnote 5 in the talk).

### [1 Nephi 8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/8?lang=eng) — 1 Nephi 8:10-12
> "And it came to pass that I beheld a tree, whose fruit was desirable to make one happy." (v. 10)

> "And it came to pass that I did go forth and partake of the fruit thereof; and I beheld that it was most sweet, above all that I ever before tasted. Yea, and I beheld that the fruit thereof was white, to exceed all the whiteness that I had ever seen." (v. 11)

> "And as I partook of the fruit thereof it filled my soul with exceedingly great joy; wherefore, I began to be desirous that my family should partake of it also; for I knew that it was desirable above all other fruit." (v. 12)

**Observation:** The key descriptors: "most sweet, above all," "white, to exceed all the whiteness," "filled my soul with exceedingly great joy," "desirable above all other fruit." These are the verbatim echoes that Alma 32:42 picks up: "most precious... sweet above all that is sweet, white above all that is white, pure above all that is pure." The near-identical language proves the texts are linked — Alma is invoking the tree of life vision. → Section 2
**Connects to:** The footnote 10a on "tree" points to 1 Ne 11:4, 8, 21-25 and 15:22 — confirming the interpretive chain.

### [John 1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/1?lang=eng) — John 1:1-4, 14
> "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God." (v. 1)

> "All things were made by him; and without him was not any thing made that was made." (v. 3)

> "In him was life; and the life was the light of men." (v. 4)

> "And the Word was made flesh, and dwelt among us, (and we beheld his glory, the glory as of the only begotten of the Father,) full of grace and truth." (v. 14)

**Observation:** The foundational text for Pattern 3 (Word = Christ). "The Word was God" and "the Word was made flesh" establish that "the word" in scripture can mean Christ himself, not just His message. This is what makes Alma 32:28 ("give place, that a seed may be planted in your heart... even if ye can no more than desire to believe, let this desire work in you, even until ye believe in a manner that ye can give place for a portion of my words") operate on two levels: receiving the word of the gospel AND receiving Christ. → Section 3
**Connects to:** v. 4 "the life was the light of men" echoes 3 Ne 11:11 "I am the light and the life of the world." v. 12 "as many as received him, to them gave he power to become the sons of God" — the receiving/becoming pattern that Kearon's talk builds on.

### [Alma 32](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/32?lang=eng) — Alma 32:28, 40-42
> "Now, we will compare the word unto a seed. Now, if ye give place, that a seed may be planted in your heart, behold, if it be a true seed, or a good seed, if ye do not cast it out by your unbelief, that ye will resist the Spirit of the Lord, behold, it will begin to swell within your breasts" (v. 28)

> "And thus, if ye will not nourish the word, looking forward with an eye of faith to the fruit thereof, ye can never pluck of the fruit of the tree of life." (v. 40)

> "And because of your diligence and your faith and your patience with the word in nourishing it, that it may take root in you, behold, by and by ye shall pluck the fruit thereof, which is most precious, which is sweet above all that is sweet, and which is white above all that is white, yea, and pure above all that is pure; and ye shall feast upon this fruit even until ye are filled, that ye hunger not, neither shall ye thirst." (v. 42)

**Observation:** v. 40 makes the tree of life connection explicit: "the fruit of the tree of life." The seed parable ISN'T just an analogy for faith — it's the tree of life vision in instructional form. v. 42's language mirrors 1 Ne 8:11 almost verbatim. v. 28 "give place, that a seed may be planted in your heart" = receive the word (gospel) = receive Christ (Pattern 3). → Section 2, 3
**Connects to:** Footnote 28b in original points to Luke 8:11 ("the seed is the word of God") — confirming the seed=word link. Footnote 42b points to 1 Ne 8:10 — confirming the tree of life echo.

### [Alma 33](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/33?lang=eng) — Alma 33:19-23
> "Behold, he was spoken of by Moses; yea, and behold a type was raised up in the wilderness, that whosoever would look upon it might live. And many did look and live." (v. 19)

> "begin to believe in the Son of God, that he will come to redeem his people, and that he shall suffer and die to atone for their sins; and that he shall rise again from the dead" (v. 22)

> "I desire that ye shall plant this word in your hearts, and as it beginneth to swell even so nourish it by your faith. And behold, it will become a tree, springing up in you unto everlasting life. And then may God grant unto you that your burdens may be light, through the joy of his Son." (v. 23)

**Observation:** v. 19 identifies the brass serpent as "a type" of Christ — Pattern 7. v. 22 explicitly identifies the seed AS the Son of God. v. 23 ties it together: "plant this word" → tree → everlasting life → "through the joy of his Son." The word planted IS the Son. The tree that grows IS Christ's love taking root. This is the passage that resolves all ambiguity about whether Alma 32 is "about Christ." → Section 3, 7
**Connects to:** Pattern 7 (brass serpent as type). John 3:14-15 — "as Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, even so must the Son of man be lifted up."

### [2 Nephi 11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/11?lang=eng) — 2 Nephi 11:4-6
> "Behold, my soul delighteth in proving unto my people the truth of the coming of Christ; for, for this end hath the law of Moses been given; and all things which have been given of God from the beginning of the world, unto man, are the typifying of him." (v. 4)

> "And also my soul delighteth in the covenants of the Lord which he hath made to our fathers; yea, my soul delighteth in his grace, and in his justice, and power, and mercy in the great and eternal plan of deliverance from death." (v. 5)

> "And my soul delighteth in proving unto my people that save Christ should come all men must perish." (v. 6)

**Observation:** Nephi's delight triplet: (1) all things given from the beginning are "the typifying of him," (2) the covenants and plan of deliverance, (3) without Christ all men perish. v. 4 is the most sweeping typological statement in the Book of Mormon — broadens from "some things are symbols" to "ALL things from the beginning are typifying." The law of Moses is named as the example. → Section 4
**Connects to:** Pattern 7 (types and shadows) — this verse provides the principle that types exist; Pattern 7 provides examples. Also connects to Pattern 2 via the concept that tree/fruit ARE Christ, not just symbols of Him.

### [Moses 6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/6?lang=eng) — Moses 6:63
> "And behold, all things have their likeness, and all things are created and made to bear record of me, both things which are temporal, and things which are spiritual; things which are in the heavens above, and things which are on the earth, and things which are in the earth, and things which are under the earth, both above and beneath: all things bear record of me." (v. 63)

**Observation:** God speaking to Adam directly. "All things have their likeness" and "all things are created and made to bear record of me." The scope is total: temporal and spiritual, heavens/earth/under earth. This is the ORIGIN of the typological principle — spoken to Adam at the very beginning. Where 2 Ne 11:4 says all things "are the typifying of him," Moses 6:63 gives God himself saying "all things... bear record of ME." First person. → Section 4
**Connects to:** The surrounding verses are remarkable: v. 59 teaches being born again "of water, and of the Spirit, and be cleansed by blood" — Pattern 1 (doctrine of Christ). v. 62 "this is the plan of salvation" — framing. v. 64-66 Adam is baptized immediately after receiving this teaching — the doctrine leads to covenant action (Pattern 8).

### [Moroni 7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/7?lang=eng) — Moroni 7:40-48
> "And again, my beloved brethren, I would speak unto you concerning hope. How is it that ye can attain unto faith, save ye shall have hope?" (v. 40)

> "And what is it that ye shall hope for? Behold I say unto you that ye shall have hope through the atonement of Christ and the power of his resurrection, to be raised unto life eternal, and this because of your faith in him according to the promise." (v. 41)

> "Wherefore, if a man have faith he must needs have hope; for without faith there cannot be any hope." (v. 42)

> "If so, his faith and hope is vain, for none is acceptable before God, save the meek and lowly in heart; and if a man be meek and lowly in heart, and confesses by the power of the Holy Ghost that Jesus is the Christ, he must needs have charity; for if he have not charity he is nothing; wherefore he must needs have charity." (v. 44)

> "But charity is the pure love of Christ, and it endureth forever; and whoso is found possessed of it at the last day, it shall be well with him." (v. 47)

> "Wherefore, my beloved brethren, pray unto the Father with all the energy of heart, that ye may be filled with this love, which he hath bestowed upon all who are true followers of his Son, Jesus Christ; that ye may become the sons of God; that when he shall appear we shall be like him, for we shall see him as he is; that we may have this hope; that we may be purified even as he is pure." (v. 48)

**Observation:** The faith-hope-charity progression: faith is prerequisite to hope (v. 42), hope comes through the atonement (v. 41), meekness and confession by the Holy Ghost lead to charity (v. 44). The chain: meekness → Holy Ghost → confession that Jesus is the Christ → charity. v. 47 defines charity as "the pure love of Christ" — ambiguous: Christ's love for us, AND our love empowered by Christ. v. 48 transforms it: "pray... that ye may be filled with this love... that ye may become the sons of God... that we shall be like him." Charity isn't just behavior — it's becoming. → Section 5
**Connects to:** v. 48 "filled with this love" echoes Pattern 6 (love shed abroad). "Become the sons of God" echoes John 1:12 (Pattern 3). v. 45 mirrors 1 Cor 13:4-7 nearly verbatim. "Purified even as he is pure" connects to Pattern 1 (sanctification via Holy Ghost).

### [Ether 12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/ether/12?lang=eng) — Ether 12:4, 27-28, 33-34
> "Wherefore, whoso believeth in God might with surety hope for a better world, yea, even a place at the right hand of God, which hope cometh of faith, maketh an anchor to the souls of men, which would make them sure and steadfast, always abounding in good works, being led to glorify God." (v. 4)

> "And if men come unto me I will show unto them their weakness. I give unto men weakness that they may be humble; and my grace is sufficient for all men that humble themselves before me; for if they humble themselves before me, and have faith in me, then will I make weak things become strong unto them." (v. 27)

> "And I, Moroni, having heard these words, was comforted, and said: O Lord, thy righteous will be done, for I know that thou workest unto the children of men according to their faith" — and v. 28: "faith, hope and charity bringeth unto me—the fountain of all righteousness." (vv. 29, 28)

> "And now I know that this love which thou hast had for the children of men is charity; wherefore, except men shall have charity they cannot inherit that place which thou hast prepared in the mansions of thy Father." (v. 34)

**Observation:** v. 4 gives faith→hope chain with the "anchor" metaphor. v. 27 — weakness→humility→grace→strength — is the transformation pattern. v. 28 names faith, hope and charity as leading to Christ ("me—the fountain of all righteousness"). v. 34 is striking: Christ's love for the world IS charity. Moroni identifies the love of God (Pattern 2/6) as the same thing as charity (Pattern 5). This means charity isn't just Pattern 5 — it's connected to the tree of life directly. → Section 5
**Connects to:** v. 34 bridges Patterns 2, 5, and 6: love of God (tree) = charity = Christ's love for the world. v. 11 "in the gift of his Son hath God prepared a more excellent way" — the law of Moses as type yielding to Christ (Pattern 4, 7).

### [1 Corinthians 13](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/1-cor/13?lang=eng) — 1 Corinthians 13:1-2, 4-8, 13
> "Though I speak with the tongues of men and of angels, and have not charity, I am become as sounding brass, or a tinkling cymbal." (v. 1)

> "And though I have the gift of prophecy, and understand all mysteries, and all knowledge; and though I have all faith, so that I could remove mountains, and have not charity, I am nothing." (v. 2)

> "Charity suffereth long, and is kind; charity envieth not; charity vaunteth not itself, is not puffed up, Doth not behave itself unseemly, seeketh not her own, is not easily provoked, thinketh no evil; Rejoiceth not in iniquity, but rejoiceth in the truth; Beareth all things, believeth all things, hopeth all things, endureth all things." (vv. 4-7)

> "Charity never faileth" (v. 8)

> "And now abideth faith, hope, charity, these three; but the greatest of these is charity." (v. 13)

**Observation:** Paul's attributes list (vv. 4-7) is reproduced nearly verbatim in Moroni 7:45 — evidence of shared doctrinal architecture across dispensations. v. 2 "faith so that I could remove mountains, and have not charity, I am nothing" — faith without charity is hollow. v. 13 "the greatest of these is charity" gives the hierarchy. Footnote 1a: GR "love" — confirming "charity" translates the Greek word for love (agape). → Section 5
**Connects to:** The GR footnote ("love") confirms the link between charity (Pattern 5) and love of God (Pattern 2/6). If charity = love = agape, and the tree of life = love of God, then charity IS the fruit of the tree.

### [Romans 5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/rom/5?lang=eng) — Romans 5:1-5
> "Therefore being justified by faith, we have peace with God through our Lord Jesus Christ" (v. 1)

> "And not only so, but we glory in tribulations also: knowing that tribulation worketh patience; And patience, experience; and experience, hope" (vv. 3-4)

> "And hope maketh not ashamed; because the love of God is shed abroad in our hearts by the Holy Ghost which is given unto us." (v. 5)

**Observation:** The Romans 5 chain: faith → peace → tribulation → patience → experience → hope → "love of God shed abroad in our hearts by the Holy Ghost." The mechanism: the Holy Ghost is the delivery vehicle for God's love entering human hearts. This is the New Testament anchor for 1 Ne 11:22 ("sheddeth itself abroad") and Moro 8:26 (Comforter "filleth with hope and perfect love"). Three different authors (Nephi, Paul, Mormon) using the same phrase for the same concept. → Section 6
**Connects to:** v. 5 creates the three-witness chain for Pattern 6: Nephi sees the tree (love of God sheds abroad), Paul receives the teaching (love shed abroad by Holy Ghost), Mormon states the mechanism (Comforter fills with hope and perfect love). Three independent witnesses = doctrinal bedrock.

### [Moroni 8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/8?lang=eng) — Moroni 8:25-26
> "And the first fruits of repentance is baptism; and baptism cometh by faith unto the fulfilling the commandments; and the fulfilling the commandments bringeth remission of sins" (v. 25)

> "And the remission of sins bringeth meekness, and lowliness of heart; and because of meekness and lowliness of heart cometh the visitation of the Holy Ghost, which Comforter filleth with hope and perfect love, which love endureth by diligence unto prayer, until the end shall come, when all the saints shall dwell with God." (v. 26)

**Observation:** Mormon gives the fullest sequential chain in scripture: repentance → baptism → faith → commandments → remission of sins → meekness → lowliness → Holy Ghost → hope and perfect love → diligence unto prayer → dwell with God. The chain runs from first principles (Pattern 1/8) through sanctification to the love of God (Pattern 6) sustained by practice. "Which love endureth by diligence unto prayer" — love isn't automatic or permanent; it requires active maintenance. → Section 6
**Connects to:** This IS the operational bridge between Pattern 1 (doctrine of Christ) and Pattern 6 (love shed abroad). The doctrine isn't abstract — it's the mechanism by which love enters and stays. Also connects to Pattern 5: "hope and perfect love" = hope + charity.

> "And Jesus said unto them, I am the bread of life: he that cometh to me shall never hunger; and he that believeth on me shall never thirst." (v. 35)
