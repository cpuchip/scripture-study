# Scratch — How Is It Done?

## Status: IN PROGRESS — Phase 2 (Source Gathering)

## Binding Question
**"Lord, how is it done?"** (Enos 1:7) — How does the Atonement work? How does one Being absorb the suffering of every soul? How does His pain become my healing? How does justice acknowledge a debt paid by someone else? And how does He know *my* name?

## Outline
1. Who Is Jesus? — identity that made the Atonement possible
2. What He Took Upon Himself — scope (not just sins)
3. The Mechanics — what scriptures reveal about the *how*
4. The Cost — what it cost Him
5. The Result — what changes in us
6. Nephi's Psalm — the personal witness model
7. Becoming

## Verified Quotes

### Enos 1 — The Anchor Chapter
Source: [Enos 1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/enos/1?lang=eng) (read in full)

**v.2** "And I will tell you of the wrestle which I had before God, before I received a remission of my sins."
- "wrestle" — this is physical, exhausting language. Compare Gen 32:24 (Jacob wrestling the angel). Footnote points there.

**v.3** "...the words which I had often heard my father speak concerning eternal life, and the joy of the saints, sunk deep into my heart."
- The words "sunk deep" — they went down before they came back up. The gospel entered as seed.

**v.4** "And my soul hungered; and I kneeled down before my Maker, and I cried unto him in mighty prayer and supplication for mine own soul; and all the day long did I cry unto him; yea, and when the night came I did still raise my voice high that it reached the heavens."
- All day. Into the night. This is not casual prayer.

**v.5** "And there came a voice unto me, saying: Enos, thy sins are forgiven thee, and thou shalt be blessed."

**v.6** "And I, Enos, knew that God could not lie; wherefore, my guilt was swept away."
- "swept away" — not argued away, not earned away. Swept. Like a flood taking something.

**v.7** "And I said: Lord, how is it done?"
- THE question. He doesn't ask "why?" or "what do I owe?" He asks HOW. The mechanics. The marvel.

**v.8** "And he said unto me: Because of thy faith in Christ, whom thou hast never before heard nor seen. And many years pass away before he shall manifest himself in the flesh; wherefore, go to, thy faith hath made thee whole."
- Faith in someone he's never seen = Atonement works retroactively. Enos is ~420 BC. Christ comes ~1 AD. The Atonement is already operational 400+ years before Gethsemane.
- "thy faith hath made thee whole" — not "my Atonement made thee whole." The mechanism is faith. The power is Christ. But faith is the conduit.

**v.9** "Now, it came to pass that when I had heard these words I began to feel a desire for the welfare of my brethren, the Nephites"
- IMMEDIATE outward turn after personal redemption. First himself (v.4), then his brethren (v.9), then the Lamanites (v.11), then the records for future generations (v.13). Concentric circles of love expanding from the personal encounter.

**v.27** "And I soon go to the place of my rest, which is with my Redeemer; for I know that in him I shall rest. And I rejoice in the day when my mortal shall put on immortality, and shall stand before him; then shall I see his face with pleasure, and he will say unto me: Come unto me, ye blessed, there is a place prepared for you in the mansions of my Father. Amen."
- Bookends the chapter. Opened with wrestle, closes with rest. The whole arc of the Atonement's effect on one soul.

### Alma 7:11-13 — The Scope Passage
Source: [Alma 7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/7?lang=eng) (read vv.1-24)

**v.11** "And he shall go forth, suffering pains and afflictions and temptations of every kind; and this that the word might be fulfilled which saith he will take upon him the pains and the sicknesses of his people."
- Not just sins. Pains. Afflictions. Temptations. Sicknesses. The scope goes far beyond moral debt.

**v.12** "And he will take upon him death, that he may loose the bands of death which bind his people; and he will take upon him their infirmities, that his bowels may be filled with mercy, according to the flesh, that he may know according to the flesh how to succor his people according to their infirmities."
- "according to the flesh" — He chose to know by experience, not by omniscience alone. He COULD know from heaven. He chose to know from inside the suffering.
- "succor" — Webster 1828 will be key here. The word means to run TO someone in distress.

**v.13** "Now the Spirit knoweth all things; nevertheless the Son of God suffereth according to the flesh that he might take upon him the sins of his people, that he might blot out their transgressions according to the power of his deliverance; and now behold, this is the testimony which is in me."
- "the Spirit knoweth all things; nevertheless" — He already knew. He suffered ANYWAY. Knowledge wasn't enough. He chose experience.

### 2 Nephi 9:5-26 — Jacob's Infinite Atonement Treatise
Source: [2 Nephi 9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/9?lang=eng) (read vv.1-31)

**v.5** "Yea, I know that ye know that in the body he shall show himself unto those at Jerusalem, from whence we came; for it is expedient that it should be among them; for it behooveth the great Creator that he suffereth himself to become subject unto man in the flesh, and die for all men, that all men might become subject unto him."
- "the great Creator... suffereth himself to become subject unto man" — the one with all power chose subjection. This is voluntary descent, not inevitable tragedy.

**v.7** "Wherefore, it must needs be an infinite atonement—save it should be an infinite atonement this corruption could not put on incorruption. Wherefore, the first judgment which came upon man must needs have remained to an endless duration. And if so, this flesh must have laid down to rot and to crumble to its mother earth, to rise no more."
- WHY infinite? Because the fall was infinite in scope — all humanity, all creation, forever. A finite fix can't repair an infinite break.

**v.8-9** "O the wisdom of God, his mercy and grace! For behold, if the flesh should rise no more our spirits must become subject to that angel who fell from before the presence of the Eternal God, and became the devil, to rise no more. And our spirits must have become like unto him, and we become devils, angels to a devil, to be shut out from the presence of our God, and to remain with the father of lies, in misery, like unto himself"
- The alternative to atonement: not just death, but spiritual enslavement forever. The stakes couldn't be higher.

**v.10** "O how great the goodness of our God, who prepareth a way for our escape from the grasp of this awful monster; yea, that monster, death and hell"

**v.21** "And he cometh into the world that he may save all men if they will hearken unto his voice; for behold, he suffereth the pains of all men, yea, the pains of every living creature, both men, women, and children, who belong to the family of Adam."
- "every living creature" — not just the faithful or the repentant. Everyone in Adam's family. The scope is total.

**v.25-26** "Wherefore, he has given a law; and where there is no law given there is no punishment; and where there is no punishment there is no condemnation... and the atonement satisfieth the demands of his justice upon all those who have not the law given to them"
- The Atonement covers those who never heard. Justice is satisfied for them already.

### D&C 19:15-19 — Christ's Own First-Person Description
Source: [D&C 19](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/19?lang=eng) (read vv.1-25)

**v.15** "Therefore I command you to repent—repent, lest I smite you by the rod of my mouth, and by my wrath, and by my anger, and your sufferings be sore—how sore you know not, how exquisite you know not, yea, how hard to bear you know not."
- He's speaking to Martin Harris. The intensity of "how sore you know not" — three times — is a warning from someone who DOES know.

**v.16** "For behold, I, God, have suffered these things for all, that they might not suffer if they would repent"
- The simplest statement of substitutionary atonement in all scripture. First person. No metaphor.

**v.17** "But if they would not repent they must suffer even as I"
- Refusal of the gift doesn't make the price disappear. Someone pays. Always.

**v.18** "Which suffering caused myself, even God, the greatest of all, to tremble because of pain, and to bleed at every pore, and to suffer both body and spirit—and would that I might not drink the bitter cup, and shrink—"
- "even God, the greatest of all, to tremble" — the emphasis on identity. The greatest. And He trembled. He shrank. He wanted it to stop. This is not stoic endurance, it's raw honesty about what it cost.
- "suffer both body and spirit" — the Atonement wasn't just physical. The spiritual suffering may have been worse.

**v.19** "Nevertheless, glory be to the Father, and I partook and finished my preparations unto the children of men."
- "Nevertheless" — the hinge word. From "Eternity Paused" study: this is the word that held eternity together. Everything before it is the cost. Everything after it is the choice.

### D&C 88:6 — The Comprehension Principle
Source: [D&C 88](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88?lang=eng) (read vv.1-10)

**v.6** "He that ascended up on high, as also he descended below all things, in that he comprehended all things, that he might be in all and through all things, the light of truth"
- "descended below all things" — below the worst any human has ever experienced. Below death. Below despair. Below abandonment. There is no depth He hasn't been.
- "in that he comprehended" — the descent IS the comprehension. He understands because He went there. Not observation but immersion.
- Connects to Alma 7:12 — "that he may know according to the flesh how to succor"

### Alma 34:8-16 — Amulek's Logic: Why Infinite?
Source: [Alma 34](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/34?lang=eng) (read vv.1-21)

**v.9** "For it is expedient that an atonement should be made; for according to the great plan of the Eternal God there must be an atonement made, or else all mankind must unavoidably perish; yea, all are hardened; yea, all are fallen and are lost, and must perish except it be through the atonement which it is expedient should be made."
- "unavoidably perish" — no other path. Not one of several options. THE option.

**v.10** "For it is expedient that there should be a great and last sacrifice; yea, not a sacrifice of man, neither of beast, neither of any manner of fowl; for it shall not be a human sacrifice; but it must be an infinite and eternal sacrifice."
- The logic chain: human sacrifice can't cover it. Animal sacrifice pointed to it. Only infinite covers infinite. The sacrifice must be qualitatively different from every prior offering.

**v.11-12** "Now there is not any man that can sacrifice his own blood which will atone for the sins of another... therefore there can be nothing which is short of an infinite atonement which will suffice for the sins of the world."
- Explicit: no mortal can pay for another mortal. The debt requires a qualitatively different kind of Being.

**v.14** "And behold, this is the whole meaning of the law, every whit pointing to that great and last sacrifice; and that great and last sacrifice will be the Son of God, yea, infinite and eternal."
- "every whit pointing" — every sacrifice, every burnt offering, every Passover lamb — all of it was a finger pointing at Gethsemane and Golgotha.

**v.15** "And thus he shall bring salvation to all those who shall believe on his name; this being the intent of this last sacrifice, to bring about the bowels of mercy, which overpowereth justice, and bringeth about means unto men that they may have faith unto repentance."
- "the bowels of mercy, which overpowereth justice" — mercy doesn't ignore justice or argue against it. It OVERpowers it. The sacrifice is so complete that justice has nothing left to demand.

### Mosiah 3:5-12 — King Benjamin's Angel: The Tabernacle of Clay
Source: [Mosiah 3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/3?lang=eng) (read vv.1-14)

**v.5** "For behold, the time cometh, and is not far distant, that with power, the Lord Omnipotent who reigneth, who was, and is from all eternity to all eternity, shall come down from heaven among the children of men, and shall dwell in a tabernacle of clay"
- "Lord Omnipotent... tabernacle of clay" — the contrast is breathtaking. Omnipotent = all-powerful. Clay = fragile, mortal, breakable. He chose the breakable container.

**v.7** "And lo, he shall suffer temptations, and pain of body, hunger, thirst, and fatigue, even more than man can suffer, except it be unto death; for behold, blood cometh from every pore, so great shall be his anguish for the wickedness and the abominations of his people."
- "even more than man can suffer, except it be unto death" — He went to the absolute edge of what a mortal body can survive. The point of death without dying (yet). 
- This is prophetic — King Benjamin (~124 BC) describing Gethsemane with clinical precision 150 years before it happens.

**v.8** "And he shall be called Jesus Christ, the Son of God, the Father of heaven and earth, the Creator of all things from the beginning"
- Same identity emphasis as D&C 88:6 and 2 Nephi 9:5 — the Creator is the Redeemer. The one who made us saves us.

**v.9** "And lo, he cometh unto his own, that salvation might come unto the children of men even through faith on his name; and even after all this they shall consider him a man, and say that he hath a devil, and shall scourge him, and shall crucify him."
- "even after all this" — after the miracles, the healing, the teaching. They still reject Him. The Atonement includes being rejected by the very people He came to save.

**v.11** "For behold, and also his blood atoneth for the sins of those who have fallen by the transgression of Adam, who have died not knowing the will of God concerning them, or who have ignorantly sinned."
- The Atonement covers those who never heard — same principle as 2 Nephi 9:25-26. Grace has wider scope than we usually imagine.

### Isaiah 53 — The Suffering Servant (The Oldest Portrait)
Source: [Isaiah 53](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/isa/53?lang=eng) (read in full)

**v.3** "He is despised and rejected of men; a man of sorrows, and acquainted with grief: and we hid as it were our faces from him; he was despised, and we esteemed him not."
- "acquainted with grief" — not that He observed grief from afar. He KNEW it. Intimately. As a companion knows a fellow traveler.

**v.4-5** "Surely he hath borne our griefs, and carried our sorrows: yet we did esteem him stricken, smitten of God, and afflicted. But he was wounded for our transgressions, he was bruised for our iniquities: the chastisement of our peace was upon him; and with his stripes we are healed."
- "the chastisement of our peace was upon him" — the punishment that BUYS our peace fell on Him. Peace costs something. He paid it.
- "with his stripes we are healed" — the mechanism: His wounding becomes our healing. This is the HOW Enos asks about.

**v.6** "All we like sheep have gone astray; we have turned every one to his own way; and the Lord hath laid on him the iniquity of us all."
- "the Lord hath laid on him" — the Father placed this burden on the Son. It wasn't just that Jesus volunteered — the Father actively participated in the arrangement.

**v.7** "He was oppressed, and he was afflicted, yet he opened not his mouth: he is brought as a lamb to the slaughter, and as a sheep before her shearers is dumb, so he openeth not his mouth."
- "opened not his mouth" — voluntary silence. He who could command legions of angels chose silence. See Matt 26:53.

**v.10** "Yet it pleased the Lord to bruise him; he hath put him to grief: when thou shalt make his soul an offering for sin, he shall see his seed"
- "he shall see his seed" — connects directly to Mosiah 15:10-12 and Abinadi's interpretation. Those who accept the gospel ARE His seed. We become His children through the Atonement.

**v.11** "He shall see of the travail of his soul, and shall be satisfied: by his knowledge shall my righteous servant justify many; for he shall bear their iniquities."
- "shall be satisfied" — after all the suffering, He looks at the result — at us, changed — and He is *satisfied*. The price was worth it to Him.
- "by his knowledge" — His experiential knowledge (the comprehension of D&C 88:6) is what enables justification.

**v.12** "Therefore will I divide him a portion with the great, and he shall divide the spoil with the strong; because he hath poured out his soul unto death: and he was numbered with the transgressors; and he bare the sin of many, and made intercession for the transgressors."
- "poured out his soul unto death" — not gave, poured. Total emptying. Nothing held back.

### 3 Nephi 17 — Bountiful: The Atonement Made Visible
Source: [3 Nephi 17](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/17?lang=eng) (read in full, 25 verses)

**v.5-6** "And it came to pass that when Jesus had thus spoken, he cast his eyes round about again on the multitude, and beheld they were in tears, and did look steadfastly upon him as if they would ask him to tarry a little longer with them. And he said unto them: Behold, my bowels are filled with compassion towards you."
- He was about to leave. He saw their tears and stayed. The Atonement isn't just a past event — it's a present posture. He stays with us.

**v.7** "Have ye any that are sick among you? Bring them hither. Have ye any that are lame, or blind, or halt, or maimed, or leprous, or that are withered, or that are deaf, or that are afflicted in any manner? Bring them hither and I will heal them, for I have compassion upon you; my bowels are filled with mercy."
- "afflicted in any manner" — the invitation is total. Every kind of suffering. He doesn't filter who qualifies.
- "my bowels are filled with mercy" — He says it twice (v.6 and v.7). He wants them to know it's not obligation. It's WANTING to help.

**v.9** "...and he did heal them every one as they were brought forth unto him."
- "every one" — not some. Not the deserving. Every single one.

**v.10** "And they did all, both they who had been healed and they who were whole, bow down at his feet, and did worship him; and as many as could come for the multitude did kiss his feet, insomuch that they did bathe his feet with their tears."
- The healed AND the whole worshipped. You don't have to be broken to be grateful. The Atonement addresses the whole person — not just the sickness.

**v.20-22** "...he said unto them: Blessed are ye because of your faith. And now behold, my joy is full. And when he had said these words, he wept, and the multitude bare record of it, and he took their little children, one by one, and blessed them, and prayed unto the Father for them. And when he had done this he wept again;"
- He wept TWICE. The first time because His joy was full. The second after blessing each child individually. This is the resurrected Lord — the Atonement is complete — and He is still moved to tears by individual children. The "how is it done?" includes: He knows your name because He cares about individual faces.

**v.23-24** "And he spake unto the multitude, and said unto them: Behold your little ones. And as they looked to behold they cast their eyes towards heaven, and they saw the heavens open, and they saw angels descending out of heaven as it were in the midst of fire; and they came down and encircled those little ones about, and they were encircled about with fire; and the angels did minister unto them."
- "Behold your little ones" — not "behold THESE children." YOUR little ones. He returns them to the parents with heaven attending them. The Atonement doesn't just save individuals — it sanctifies families.

### 2 Nephi 4:15-35 — Nephi's Psalm (The Personal Witness Model)
Source: [2 Nephi 4](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/4?lang=eng) (read in full)

**v.15-16** "And upon these I write the things of my soul, and many of the scriptures which are engraven upon the plates of brass. For my soul delighteth in the scriptures, and my heart pondereth them, and writeth them for the learning and the profit of my children. Behold, my soul delighteth in the things of the Lord; and my heart pondereth continually upon the things which I have seen and heard."
- "the things of my soul" — this is the plate Nephi reserves for what matters most. Not history, not geography. Soul-things.

**v.17-18** "Nevertheless, notwithstanding the great goodness of the Lord, in showing me his great and marvelous works, my heart exclaimeth: O wretched man that I am! Yea, my heart sorroweth because of my flesh; my soul grieveth because of mine iniquities. I am encompassed about, because of the temptations and the sins which do so easily beset me."
- "Nevertheless" — the hinge-word again, but going the OTHER direction from D&C 19:19. There it went from despair to choice. Here it goes from gratitude to grief. Same word, opposite movement. The Atonement holds both directions.

**v.19** "And when I desire to rejoice, my heart groaneth because of my sins; nevertheless, I know in whom I have trusted."
- THE verse Michael keeps circling back to. Two "neverthelessses" in three verses. v.17: nevertheless I am wretched. v.19: nevertheless I KNOW. The second "nevertheless" overcomes the first. This IS the Atonement working in real time in a real soul.

**v.20-21** "My God hath been my support; he hath led me through mine afflictions in the wilderness; and he hath preserved me upon the waters of the great deep. He hath filled me with his love, even unto the consuming of my flesh."
- "unto the consuming of my flesh" — love so intense it is physically overwhelming. Compare Mosiah 3:7 (blood from every pore) — the same intensity, but inverted. Christ's flesh consumed by suffering; Nephi's flesh consumed by love.

**v.26** "O then, if I have seen so great things, if the Lord in his condescension unto the children of men hath visited men in so much mercy, why should my heart weep and my soul linger in the valley of sorrow"
- The turning point. "Why?" becomes the engine of hope instead of despair. Because of what He's seen, grief loses its anchor.

**v.28-30** "Awake, my soul! No longer droop in sin. Rejoice, O my heart, and give place no more for the enemy of my soul... Rejoice, O my heart, and cry unto the Lord, and say: O Lord, I will praise thee forever; yea, my soul will rejoice in thee, my God, and the rock of my salvation."
- The psalm moves: grief → "nevertheless" → remembrance → command to self → praise. That's the Atonement's emotional architecture.

**v.34** "O Lord, I have trusted in thee, and I will trust in thee forever. I will not put my trust in the arm of flesh"
- Past tense PLUS future tense. "I HAVE trusted... I WILL trust." The Atonement works backward and forward — same as Enos's retroactive forgiveness.

**v.35** "Yea, I know that God will give liberally to him that asketh. Yea, my God will give me, if I ask not amiss; therefore I will lift up my voice unto thee; yea, I will cry unto thee, my God, the rock of my righteousness. Behold, my voice shall forever ascend up unto thee, my rock and mine everlasting God. Amen."
- Ends on bedrock. "My rock." Not "my idea" or "my effort." The Psalm begins in the valley and ends on the rock. That's the shape of every soul who encounters the Atonement.

### Alma 5:7-14, 26 — Song of Redeeming Love
Source: [Alma 5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/5?lang=eng) (read vv.1-24)

**v.7** "Behold, he changed their hearts; yea, he awakened them out of a deep sleep, and they awoke unto God. Behold, they were in the midst of darkness; nevertheless, their souls were illuminated by the light of the everlasting word"
- "nevertheless" again — darkness, BUT THEN illuminated. The word that keeps appearing at the hinge between ruin and rescue.

**v.9** "And again I ask, were the bands of death broken, and the chains of hell which encircled them about, were they loosed? I say unto you, Yea, they were loosed, and their souls did expand, and they did sing redeeming love."
- "their souls did expand" — the Atonement doesn't just remove chains. It EXPANDS the soul. Freedom isn't just absence of bondage; it's new capacity.
- "they did sing redeeming love" — present tense in the experience. Not past tense theology. Song.

**v.12-14** "And according to his faith there was a mighty change wrought in his heart... And now behold, I ask of you, my brethren of the church, have ye spiritually been born of God? Have ye received his image in your countenances? Have ye experienced this mighty change in your hearts?"
- "His image in your countenances" — the result of the Atonement is not just forgiveness but TRANSFORMATION. You look different. You carry His face on yours.

### Mosiah 15:1-9 — Abinadi: The Will Swallowed Up
Source: [Mosiah 15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng) (read vv.1-21)

**v.1** "And now Abinadi said unto them: I would that ye should understand that God himself shall come down among the children of men, and shall redeem his people."
- "God himself" — not a delegate, not an angel. GOD. The identity claim is total.

**v.5** "And thus the flesh becoming subject to the Spirit, or the Son to the Father, being one God, suffereth temptation, and yieldeth not to the temptation, but suffereth himself to be mocked, and scourged, and cast out, and disowned by his people."
- "suffereth himself" — voluntary again. He allowed it. He accepted it. The same language as 2 Nephi 9:5.

**v.7** "Yea, even so he shall be led, crucified, and slain, the flesh becoming subject even unto death, the will of the Son being swallowed up in the will of the Father."
- "the will of the Son being swallowed up" — from Eternity Paused study: this isn't loss of self. The Son's will doesn't disappear — it submits completely. Like a river being "swallowed" by the ocean — it doesn't cease; it joins something larger.

**v.8-9** "And thus God breaketh the bands of death, having gained the victory over death; giving the Son power to make intercession for the children of men—Having ascended into heaven, having the bowels of mercy; being filled with compassion towards the children of men; standing betwixt them and justice; having broken the bands of death, taken upon himself their iniquity and their transgressions, having redeemed them, and satisfied the demands of justice."
- "standing betwixt them and justice" — He is between us and what we deserve. Not arguing with justice. STANDING THERE. Physically interposed.
- "satisfied the demands of justice" — justice doesn't go away. It's satisfied. Fully paid. Alma 34:15 said mercy "overpowereth" justice; here justice is "satisfied." Both are true: mercy overpowers because justice has been fully met.

**v.10-12** "And now I say unto you, who shall declare his generation? Behold, I say unto you, that when his soul has been made an offering for sin he shall see his seed. And now what say ye? And who shall be his seed? ...For these are they whose sins he has borne; these are they for whom he has died, to redeem them from their transgressions. And now, are they not his seed?"
- Isaiah 53:10's answer: WE are His seed. The Atonement makes us His children. Not just beneficiaries — family.

### Luke 22:39-44 — Gethsemane
Source: [Luke 22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/luke/22?lang=eng) (read vv.1-46)

**v.41-42** "And he was withdrawn from them about a stone's cast, and kneeled down, and prayed, Saying, Father, if thou be willing, remove this cup from me: nevertheless not my will, but thine, be done."
- "about a stone's cast" — close enough to see, too far to hear. The disciples could watch but not share the burden.
- "nevertheless" — here it is again. Luke 22:42. The same hinge word. D&C 19:19. 2 Nephi 4:17, 19. Every time it appears near the Atonement, it marks the moment where submission overcomes resistance.

**v.43** "And there appeared an angel unto him from heaven, strengthening him."
- Even the Son of God receives ministering. The Father sends angelic support — but does NOT remove the cup. Strengthened to endure, not delivered from the task.

**v.44** "And being in an agony he prayed more earnestly: and his sweat was as it were great drops of blood falling down to the ground."
- "prayed MORE earnestly" — the agony intensified and so did the prayer. He didn't withdraw. He pressed in. The direction of His response to pain was toward the Father, not away.

### Matthew 27:46 — The Cry from the Cross
Source: [Matthew 27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/matt/27?lang=eng) (read vv.27-46)

**v.46** "And about the ninth hour Jesus cried with a loud voice, saying, Eli, Eli, lama sabachthani? that is to say, My God, my God, why hast thou forsaken me?"
- The only moment in all of eternity when the Son was separated from the Father's Spirit. Holland: "apparently He had not comprehended this." He anticipated losing mortal support; He had NOT anticipated the withdrawal of divine presence.
- This is Part 4's climax: the cost wasn't just physical pain (Gethsemane) but spiritual abandonment (Calvary). He felt what sin feels like — total separation from God — without ever having sinned.
- Quoting Psalm 22:1 — even in His darkest moment, He speaks scripture. The cry is both raw agony and theological declaration.

### Mosiah 5:1-7 — The Mighty Change
Source: [Mosiah 5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/5?lang=eng) (read vv.1-11)

**v.2** "And they all cried with one voice, saying: Yea, we believe all the words which thou hast spoken unto us; and also, we know of their surety and truth, because of the Spirit of the Lord Omnipotent, which has wrought a mighty change in us, or in our hearts, that we have no more disposition to do evil, but to do good continually."
- "no more disposition to do evil" — not just behavior modification. The DESIRE changes. The Atonement rewrites inclination, not just action.
- "one voice" — the community experience. The Atonement changes individuals, but the response is collective.

**v.5** "And we are willing to enter into a covenant with our God to do his will, and to be obedient to his commandments in all things that he shall command us, all the remainder of our days"
- Covenant as response to the Atonement. Changed hearts lead to willingness to bind themselves. Not forced — "willing."

**v.7** "And now, because of the covenant which ye have made ye shall be called the children of Christ, his sons, and his daughters; for behold, this day he hath spiritually begotten you; for ye say that your hearts are changed through faith on his name; therefore, ye are born of him and have become his sons and his daughters."
- "spiritually begotten" — the Isaiah 53/Mosiah 15 "seed" realized. Through covenant, we become His children. The Atonement creates family.

### Moroni 10:32-33 — The Final Invitation
Source: [Moroni 10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/10?lang=eng) (read vv.1-34)

**v.32** "Yea, come unto Christ, and be perfected in him, and deny yourselves of all ungodliness; and if ye shall deny yourselves of all ungodliness, and love God with all your might, mind and strength, then is his grace sufficient for you, that by his grace ye may be perfect in Christ"
- "be perfected IN him" — not BY yourself, not THROUGH effort alone. In Him. Perfection is a location, not an achievement.
- "his grace sufficient" — echoes 2 Corinthians 12:9. Sufficiency is HIS, not ours.

**v.33** "And again, if ye by the grace of God are perfect in Christ, and deny not his power, then are ye sanctified in Christ by the grace of God, through the shedding of the blood of Christ, which is in the covenant of the Father unto the remission of your sins, that ye become holy, without spot."
- "which is in the covenant of the Father" — the shedding of blood isn't random. It's covenantal. Planned. The blood, the covenant, the Father's plan — all one integrated system.
- "that ye become holy, without spot" — the end state. Moroni's last words about the Atonement: you become HOLY. Not just forgiven. Not just freed. Holy.

### John 1:1-14 — The Word Made Flesh
Source: [John 1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/1?lang=eng) (read vv.1-20)

**v.1-3** "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God. The same was in the beginning with God. All things were made by him; and without him was not any thing made that was made."
- "All things were made by him" — the Creator identity. Every atom, every star, every cell — His work. And then He chose to enter the creation as a creature. Mosiah 3:8 and 2 Nephi 9:5 echo this.

**v.4-5** "In him was life; and the life was the light of men. And the light shineth in darkness; and the darkness comprehended it not."
- "the darkness comprehended it not" — Webster 1828 "comprehend" includes "to contain, to embrace." The darkness could not CONTAIN the light. It could not hold it. D&C 88:6 reverses this: HE comprehended all things by descending below them. The darkness couldn't hold the light; but the Light held the darkness.

**v.10-11** "He was in the world, and the world was made by him, and the world knew him not. He came unto his own, and his own received him not."
- The Creator unrecognized by His creation. The Maker rejected by the made. Mosiah 3:9 says the same: "even after all this they shall consider him a man."

**v.12** "But as many as received him, to them gave he power to become the sons of God, even to them that believe on his name"
- "power to become" — the Atonement gives capacity. Not just forgiveness but operational capability. You can BECOME something you weren't.

**v.14** "And the Word was made flesh, and dwelt among us, (and we beheld his glory, the glory as of the only begotten of the Father,) full of grace and truth."
- "was made flesh" — from eternal Word to mortal flesh. Mosiah 3:5's "tabernacle of clay." The descent began here. The comprehension of D&C 88:6 required this incarnation.

### Holland, "None Were With Him" (April 2009)
Source: [None Were with Him (April 2009)](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2009/04/none-were-with-him?lang=eng) (read in full)

Holland on the loneliest journey:
"I have trodden the winepress alone; and of the people there was none with me. … I looked, and there was none to help; and I wondered that there was none to uphold [me]." (quoting Isaiah 63:3, 5)

On the narrowing circle:
"Thus, of divine necessity, the supporting circle around Jesus gets smaller and smaller and smaller, giving significance to Matthew's words: 'All the disciples [left] him, and fled.'"

On the Father's withdrawal — the most important passage:
"Had He not said to His disciples, 'Behold, the hour … is now come, that ye shall be scattered, every man to his own, and shall leave me alone: and yet I am not alone, because the Father is with me' and 'The Father hath not left me alone; for I do always those things that please him'?"

"With all the conviction of my soul I testify that He did please His Father perfectly and that a perfect Father did not forsake His Son in that hour. Indeed, it is my personal belief that in all of Christ's mortal ministry the Father may never have been closer to His Son than in these agonizing final moments of suffering. Nevertheless, that the supreme sacrifice of His Son might be as complete as it was voluntary and solitary, the Father briefly withdrew from Jesus the comfort of His Spirit, the support of His personal presence."

"It was required, indeed it was central to the significance of the Atonement, that this perfect Son who had never spoken ill nor done wrong nor touched an unclean thing had to know how the rest of humankind—us, all of us—would feel when we did commit such sins. For His Atonement to be infinite and eternal, He had to feel what it was like to die not only physically but spiritually, to sense what it was like to have the divine Spirit withdraw, leaving one feeling totally, abjectly, hopelessly alone."

- Holland uses "nevertheless" too — "Nevertheless, that the supreme sacrifice... might be as complete as it was voluntary and solitary." The word follows the Atonement everywhere.
- The key insight: the Father withdrew the Spirit so Christ would know what WE feel when we sin. The separation at Calvary wasn't punishment of the Son — it was *information*. He needed to know isolation from God in order to comprehend (D&C 88:6) the worst thing any soul can experience.
- "the Father may never have been closer to His Son" — proximity and withdrawal at the same time. Present but unfelt. This is pastorally crucial: God is near even when we can't feel Him.

On the consolation:
"Because Jesus walked such a long, lonely path utterly alone, we do not have to do so. His solitary journey brought great company for our little version of that path."

### Webster 1828 Definitions

**COMPREHEND** (D&C 88:6)
1828: "To contain; to embrace; to include" AND "To take into the mind; to grasp with the understanding"
- Both meanings apply to D&C 88:6. Christ CONTAINS all things (He includes all experience within Himself) AND He UNDERSTANDS all things (grasps them fully). The descent was both ontological and cognitive.

**SUCCOR** (Alma 7:12)
1828: "To run to, or run to support; hence, to help or relieve when in difficulty, want, or distress; to assist and deliver from suffering"
- "TO RUN TO" — not to wait for someone to come. He RUNS to us. The root meaning is movement toward the distressed. Active, urgent mercy.
- The quote in the 1828 dictionary is Hebrews 2:18: "He is able to succor them that are tempted." — the same Atonement principle.

**INFINITE** (Alma 34:10, 2 Nephi 9:7)
1828: "Unlimited or boundless, in time or space... Without limit in power, capacity, knowledge, or excellence; boundless; immeasurably or inconceivably great"
- "Immeasurably or inconceivably great" — Amulek's point exactly. Not just very large. Inconceivable. The Atonement exceeds human capacity to measure.
- The Psalm quote in the entry: "Great is our Lord, and of great power; his understanding is infinite." — understanding (comprehension) and infiniteness linked again.

**REDEEM** (throughout)
1828: "To purchase back; to regain possession of by payment of a stipulated price" AND "to ransom, liberate, or rescue from captivity or bondage"
- PURCHASE BACK — we were His to begin with. We didn't become His at redemption; we were returned to Him. "Redeem" implies prior ownership.
- The Galatians quote: "Christ hath redeemed us from the curse of the law, being made a curse for us." — He didn't just pay — He became the very thing that enslaved us.

## Observations
- **The comprehension principle** keeps surfacing: D&C 88:6 says He "comprehended all things" through descent; Alma 7:12 says He experienced "according to the flesh" so He could "succor"; Isaiah 53:11 says "by his knowledge" He justifies. The HOW of the Atonement is inseparable from His choosing to KNOW by experience.
- **"Every one" / "all" / "every living creature"** — the scope language is relentless across all sources. 2 Nephi 9:21, Alma 34:10, 3 Nephi 17:9. This isn't a limited atonement.
- **The weeping** — D&C 19 has Him trembling. 3 Nephi 17 has Him weeping twice. Isaiah 53 has Him silent. Mosiah 3 has blood from every pore. The emotional range is enormous. He's not a stoic savior.
- **Retroactivity** — Enos (420 BC) receives forgiveness through an Atonement that won't happen for 400 years. King Benjamin (124 BC) teaches it as settled fact. The Atonement operates outside time.
- **The voluntary nature** — 2 Nephi 9:5 ("suffereth himself to become subject"), D&C 19:19 ("nevertheless"), Isaiah 53:7 ("opened not his mouth"). Every source emphasizes choice, not compulsion.
- **Creator = Redeemer pattern** — Mosiah 3:8, 2 Nephi 9:5, D&C 88:6, John 1 (to be read). The one who made us saves us. This isn't coincidence — it's qualitative necessity (Alma 34).
