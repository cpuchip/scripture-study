# Scratch — What Was the Atonement For?

## Binding Question
**"What was the atonement for?"** (Morgan Philpot's closing question) — and what does 3 Nephi 6:18 teach about the stakes of having been *taught* the will of God?

Sub-questions:
- Why does the atonement specifically secure *freedom* (and what kind)?
- What is the structural logic of 3 Nephi 6:18 — taught → willful → destruction?
- Is "the atonement makes us free to choose God" the *whole* answer, or one face of a larger thing?
- Does the cite history bear out 3 Nephi 6:18 as a load-bearing verse, or is Philpot lifting a quiet one?

## Verified Quotes & Observations

### 3 Nephi 6 — the pericope (read in full)

> "Now they did not sin ignorantly, for they knew the will of God concerning them, for it had been taught unto them; therefore they did wilfully rebel against God."
> — 3 Nephi 6:18

The whole chapter is the setup. v.4-9 = peace, prosperity, laws "according to equity and justice." v.10-15 = pride, class distinctions ("distinguished by ranks, according to their riches and their chances for learning"), church broken up "save it were among a few of the Lamanites who were converted." v.16-17 = Satan leads the hearts; "in a state of awful wickedness." v.18 = the verdict.

v.20 — note what they were taught: "preaching and testifying boldly of the sins and iniquities of the people, and testifying unto them concerning the redemption which the Lord would make for his people, or in other words, the resurrection of Christ; and they did testify boldly of his death and sufferings." The taught content was *the atonement itself*. They were taught the atoning Christ and rebelled anyway.

v.21-25 — the lawyers and chief judges put the prophets to death secretly, contrary to the law of the land (no execution without the governor's signature). Lawful process becomes the cover.

v.28 — "they did enter into a covenant one with another... which covenant was given and administered by the devil, to combine against all righteousness." The anti-covenant.

v.30 — **the explicit goal: "destroy the governor, and to establish a king over the land, that the land should no more be at liberty but should be subject unto kings."** Liberty is the target. Footnote 30b → TG Liberty.

### 3 Nephi 7 — the result

> "Now behold, I will show unto you that they did not establish a king over the land; but in this same year, yea, the thirtieth year, they did destroy upon the judgment-seat, yea, did murder the chief judge of the land. And the people were divided one against another; and they did separate one from another into tribes... and thus they did destroy the government of the land."
> — 3 Nephi 7:1-2

> "Now all this was done... because they did yield themselves unto the power of Satan. And the regulations of the government were destroyed, because of the secret combination of the friends and kindreds of those who murdered the prophets."
> — 3 Nephi 7:5-6

So Philpot's claim "the government of the land was destroyed shortly thereafter" is textually exact. The plotters did not crown a king; they collapsed the republic into tribes. Worse, in some ways, than a king.

### Numbers 15:22-31 — the Mosaic structural backbone (3 Ne 6:18a footnote)

vv.22-29: sin by ignorance, whether congregational or individual → bring a sin offering → "the priest shall make an atonement for the soul that sinneth ignorantly... and it shall be forgiven him."

> "But the soul that doeth ought presumptuously, whether he be born in the land, or a stranger, the same reproacheth the Lord; and that soul shall be cut off from among his people. Because he hath despised the word of the Lord, and hath broken his commandment, that soul shall utterly be cut off; his iniquity shall be upon him."
> — Numbers 15:30-31

Hebrew for "presumptuously" = *be-yad ramah*, "with a high hand." Mosaic law literally had a category of sin for which atonement was unavailable. This is the OT shadow of 3 Ne 6:18. The footnote in our scripture chain is doing real work — 3 Ne 6:18 invokes the legal category of high-handed sin. "They did not sin ignorantly" = "they did not qualify for sin-offering atonement." "Therefore they did wilfully rebel" = the high-hand category.

### Mosiah 3:11 — the exact inverse phrasing (3 Ne 6:18a footnote)

> "For behold, and also his blood atoneth for the sins of those who have fallen by the transgression of Adam, who have died **not knowing the will of God concerning them, or who have ignorantly sinned**."
> — Mosiah 3:11

Compare:
- 3 Ne 6:18: "they **knew the will of God concerning them**, for it had been taught unto them"
- Mosiah 3:11: those whose blood Christ atones for include those who "died **not knowing the will of God concerning them**"

Same exact construction, opposite condition. Mosiah 3:11 is the mercy clause; 3 Ne 6:18 is the verse where the mercy clause does not apply. The atonement of Christ extends *automatically* to those who never knew. It must be *received* by those who did. The "without compulsion" Philpot quotes is the same doctrine in different vocabulary.

### Mosiah 16:12 (semantic search hit)

> "Having gone according to their own carnal wills and desires; having never called upon the Lord while the arms of mercy were extended towards them; for the arms of mercy were extended towards them, and they would not; they being warned of their iniquities and yet they would not depart from them."
> — Mosiah 16:12 (Abinadi)

"Warned of their iniquities" = same category as "taught." Abinadi names the same dynamic — mercy extended, ignored after warning, willful refusal. Note the verb: *would not*. Not *could not*.

### Joseph F. Smith — Teachings ch.30 (semantic search hit, worth reading in full)

> "[Our] obedience must be voluntary; it must not be forced, there must be no coercion. Men must not be constrained against their will to obey the will of God; they must obey it because they know it to be right, because they desire to do it, and because it is their pleasure to do it."
> — Joseph F. Smith, *Teachings* ch.30

This is the "without compulsion" doctrine in JFS's own voice. The Church's *Celebrating Freedom and Agency* statement (Philpot's other source) is restating Joseph F. Smith.

### 3 Nephi 6:18 — full footnote map

- **18a "ignorantly"** → Num 15:27 + Mosiah 3:11 + TG Ignorance — the Mosaic high-hand/low-hand distinction and Benjamin's mercy clause
- **18b "will"** → TG God, Will of
- **18c "rebel"** → TG Rebellion
- 3 Ne 6:30b "liberty" → TG Liberty

The 18a chain is the loaded one. The verse was deliberately written to invoke Numbers 15 and Mosiah 3.

### Moses 4:1-3 — the war in heaven, where agency was the target

> "That Satan, whom thou hast commanded in the name of mine Only Begotten, is the same which was from the beginning, and he came before me, saying—Behold, here am I, send me, I will be thy son, and I will redeem all mankind, that one soul shall not be lost, and surely I will do it; wherefore give me thine honor."
> — Moses 4:1

> "But, behold, my Beloved Son, which was my Beloved and Chosen from the beginning, said unto me—Father, thy will be done, and the glory be thine forever."
> — Moses 4:2

> "Wherefore, because that Satan rebelled against me, and **sought to destroy the agency of man**, which I, the Lord God, had given him, and also, that I should give unto him mine own power; by the power of mine Only Begotten, I caused that he should be cast down."
> — Moses 4:3

The war in heaven was over compulsion. Satan promised that *no soul would be lost* — the price was the destruction of agency. The Father chose Christ's plan because losing souls was the cost the Father was willing to pay to preserve choice. This is the pre-mortal "without compulsion" decision.

BYU Citation Index: Moses 4:3 = **42 conference citations**, including 13 by John Taylor in *Journal of Discourses* (Taylor preached on agency as the central battleground constantly), Robert D. Hales *Agency: Essential to the Plan of Life* (Oct 2010), Hales *To Act for Ourselves* (Apr 2006), Bednar 2025 (the same talk cited in `study/agency.md`).

### 2 Nephi 2 — Lehi's answer to the binding question

> "And the way is prepared from the fall of man, and **salvation is free**. And men are instructed sufficiently that they know good from evil. And the law is given unto men."
> — 2 Nephi 2:4-5

Salvation is free; men are *instructed sufficiently*. The "taught" condition that Mosiah 3:11 says ignorance shields against, and that 3 Ne 6:18 says removes the shield, is already universal per Lehi. "Sufficiently" is the bar. The light of Christ + the law given = enough to know good from evil.

> "Wherefore, the Lord God gave unto man that he should act for himself. Wherefore, man could not act for himself save it should be that he was enticed by the one or the other."
> — 2 Nephi 2:16

Acting requires opposition. Without enticement on both sides there is no acting-for-self, only being-acted-upon. This is the structural reason God refuses compulsion: forced obedience destroys the very thing obedience is supposed to produce.

> "And the Messiah cometh in the fulness of time, that he may redeem the children of men from the fall. And because that they are redeemed from the fall **they have become free forever**, knowing good from evil; **to act for themselves and not to be acted upon**, save it be by the punishment of the law at the great and last day, according to the commandments which God hath given."
> — 2 Nephi 2:26

**This is the answer to "what was the atonement for?"** The redemption from the fall MAKES the children of men free — restores them to the category of *acting* rather than *being acted upon*. The atonement secures agency itself.

> "Wherefore, men are free according to the flesh; and all things are given them which are expedient unto man. And they are **free to choose liberty and eternal life, through the great Mediator of all men, or to choose captivity and death**, according to the captivity and power of the devil; for he seeketh that all men might be miserable like unto himself."
> — 2 Nephi 2:27

Liberty is *through the Mediator*. The Mediator is what makes liberty even available as a choice. Without the atonement there is no liberty-pole on the choice; there is only captivity and death.

### D&C 88:32 — willingness all the way through

> "And they who remain shall also be quickened; nevertheless, they shall return again to their own place, **to enjoy that which they are willing to receive, because they were not willing to enjoy that which they might have received**."
> — D&C 88:32

Even at the final judgment, glory is structured by willingness. Not capacity, not deserving — willingness. The same anti-compulsion principle that fired the war in heaven runs all the way through to the kingdoms of glory. God will give us what we are willing to receive. He will not force a higher kingdom on a person whose will refused it.

BYU Citation Index: D&C 88:32 = **3 conference citations**. Another quiet but doctrinally massive verse. Elder Renlund is the modern voice on it (*Choose You This Day*, Oct 2018; *Your Divine Nature and Eternal Destiny*, Apr 2022).

### D&C 121:41 — even priesthood power cannot compel

> "No power or influence can or ought to be maintained by virtue of the priesthood, **only by persuasion, by long-suffering, by gentleness and meekness, and by love unfeigned**; by kindness, and pure knowledge..."
> — D&C 121:41-42

If even priesthood power — God's own delegated authority — operates only by persuasion, the no-compulsion principle is not a concession to human stubbornness. It is the structure of how God Himself works. Compulsion is, by definition, the devil's tool. The methodology of heaven is invitation.

### D&C 1:2-5 — voice of warning, by the mouth of His servants

> "For verily the voice of the Lord is unto all men, and **there is none to escape**; and there is no eye that shall not see, neither ear that shall not hear, neither heart that shall not be penetrated... And the voice of warning shall be unto all people, **by the mouths of my disciples**, whom I have chosen in these last days."
> — D&C 1:2,4

In the latter days the "voice of warning" goes to all people. The Lord's word here functions to *create* the 3 Nephi 6:18 condition: it teaches, so that none can later claim ignorance. The mercy of being unwarned (Mosiah 3:11) gives way, over time and at the Lord's pace, to the accountability of having been taught.

### Psalm 19:13 — the Psalmist's own fear of the high-hand category

> "Keep back thy servant also from presumptuous sins; let them not have dominion over me: then shall I be upright, and I shall be innocent from the great transgression."
> — Psalm 19:13

The Psalmist names exactly the Numbers 15:30 category — *presumptuous sins* — and prays not to commit it. "Then shall I be innocent from the great transgression." David knew that the high-hand category, once entered, was outside the sin-offering's reach. The whole prayer of the righteous includes the petition: *do not let me become the one who knows and rebels anyway*.

### Webster 1828 — the vocabulary

**COMPULSION** — *"The act of compelling, or the state of being compelled; the act of driving or urging by force or by physical or moral constraint; subjection to force."*
The Church's *Celebrating Freedom and Agency* statement uses this word precisely. "Without compulsion" = without subjection to force, including *moral* constraint.

**PRESUMPTUOUS** — Webster 1828 def 3: *"Done with bold design, rash confidence, or in violation of known duty; **willful**. 'Keep back the servant also from presumptuous sins.' Ps. xix. 13."*
The Hebrew *be-yad ramah* ("with a high hand", Num 15:30) → KJV *presumptuously* → Webster *"in violation of known duty; willful"* → 3 Ne 6:18 *"willfully rebel."* The vocabulary chain holds. Joseph Smith's translation uses the word ("wilfully") that Webster's dictionary defines under the older word ("presumptuous") that the KJV uses for the Mosaic category. Same doctrine, three vocabularies, one chain.

### Critical analysis

**Strongest claim to stress-test:** *3 Ne 6:18 invokes Numbers 15:30 deliberately.* Check: the 18a footnote in our cached scripture file points to Num 15:27 (not v.30). v.27 is the *forgiveness* clause for ignorance; the *high-hand* contrast is v.30. So the footnote points to the contrast Numbers makes, even though it lands on the mercy side. The full Numbers 15:22-31 pericope is one argument; pointing to v.27 invokes the whole pericope. Claim holds, but I should be precise: the footnote points to the Mosaic *category* of ignorance vs presumption, not to v.30 specifically.

**Weakest link:** the cite-count claim. I have BYU Citation Index data for the verses I asked about. I have not done a comprehensive sweep of *which* prophets have preached on the "taught therefore accountable" doctrine using *other* verses. The doctrine is clearly alive (Bednar 2025, Renlund 2018, Rasband 2020) — what's specifically under-preached is *this verse*. Frame carefully: the doctrine is central, the verse is quiet. Don't overclaim that the doctrine itself is under-developed.

**Missing voices:** All five standard works covered (OT Num/Ps, NT John ref, BoM 2 Ne/Mosiah/Alma/3 Ne, D&C 1/88/121, PGP Moses 4). Modern prophets: John Taylor, Joseph F. Smith, Maxwell, Benson, Hales, Renlund, Rasband, Bednar. Good spread across eras.

**Framing risks:**
- *Date-setting drift*: Philpot's post is timestamped (May 31, July 5). Our study must NOT cite specific dates as fulfillment. The pattern is permanent; the calendar is not ours. Per `morgan-philpot-revised/00_plan.md` — extract pattern, leave dates.
- *Political reading*: Philpot reads 3 Ne 6:30 ("destroy the governor... no more at liberty") as a US/Utah type. The text supports the *pattern* of a society moving from liberty to tyranny through willful rebellion after teaching. The text does not name a specific modern government. I'll let the pattern speak and refuse to map it.
- *Compulsion creep*: The "without compulsion" point can drift toward libertarian political theory. The Church's statement and the scriptural pattern is specifically about God's refusal to compel toward Him — not a general anti-government argument. Stay close to the spiritual question.

**Posture check:** Am I reading to discover or to confirm?
- Confirming risk: I came in suspecting the atonement-for-freedom answer was right. Did I look for what would disrupt it?
- Disruption found: the atonement is for *more* than freedom-to-choose. 2 Nephi 2:26 says we are "free forever, knowing good from evil; to act for themselves and not to be acted upon" — agency is one of multiple gifts. The resurrection (88:14), the redemption of the soul (88:16), the sanctification (88:18). Freedom-to-choose-God is one face of a larger jewel.
- The Philpot framing isn't wrong, but it's narrow. The atonement's full work includes overcoming death, overcoming sin, sanctification, and the restoration of agency. Saying "the atonement was for agency" is true but partial.
- The study should affirm Philpot's framing as far as it goes, then widen it.

**Ring check:** Does the study answer its binding question?
Binding question: *What was the atonement for, and what does 3 Ne 6:18 say about the stakes of being taught?*
The text answers both. The atonement makes us free to act, and being taught moves us from the mercy-clause category (Mosiah 3:11) into the accountability category (3 Ne 6:18 / Num 15:30). The two halves connect: the atonement provides the freedom; teaching activates the accountability that makes the freedom meaningful. The ring closes.

**Tension to name, not hide:**
- If teaching increases accountability, why does God keep teaching? The Mosiah 3:11 mercy clause looks safer than the 3 Ne 6:18 hammer. Why warn at all?
- Answer the text gives: D&C 88:32, willingness. God will not let us settle for what we never chose. Being taught is the gift of being given a higher choice, even though refusing it costs more. The Father wants children who *chose* Him, not children who escaped accountability by being unwarned. That is the meaning of "He wanted us to choose Him freely."
- This is the disruptive find. The "without compulsion" doctrine cuts BOTH ways: it justifies refusing to force us to obey, AND it justifies the increasing weight of teaching that makes refusal more dangerous. Not a softer love. A more serious one.
