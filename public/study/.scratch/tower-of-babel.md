## Verified Quotes

### Moses 6:5 (Adamic Language)

- Source: `[Moses 6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/6?lang=eng)` v.5
- Note: The language of Adam = the original language. It was "given" — divine origin. Writing was inspired, not merely invented.

### Moses 6:6
> "And by them their children were taught to read and write, having a language which was pure and undefiled"
- Source: `[Moses 6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/6?lang=eng)` v.6 (from chapter heading / TG Language)
- Note: "Pure and undefiled" — this language came from God. Same word "pure" appears in Zephaniah's restoration promise.

### Moses 6:57 (God's Name in Adamic)
> "for, in the language of Adam, Man of Holiness is his name, and the name of his Only Begotten is the Son of Man"
- Source: `[Moses 6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/6?lang=eng)` v.57
- Note: The Adamic language encoded theological truth in its very vocabulary. "Man of Holiness" = God. "Son of Man" = Son of God. The language itself was revelatory.

### Moses 7:13 (Enoch's Language Power)
> "so great was the faith of Enoch that he led the people of God, and their enemies came to battle against them; and he spake the word of the Lord, and the earth trembled, and the mountains fled, even according to his command; and the rivers of water were turned out of their course; and the roar of the lions was heard out of the wilderness; and all nations feared greatly, so powerful was the word of Enoch, and so great was the power of the language which God had given him."
- Source: `[Moses 7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/7?lang=eng)` v.13
- Note: "the language which God had given him" — the divine language wasn't just communication, it was power. It moved mountains. This is before Babel. The Adamic language was still intact in Enoch's time.

### Moses 7:18 (Zion — One Heart, One Mind)
> "And the Lord called his people Zion, because they were of one heart and one mind, and dwelt in righteousness; and there was no poor among them."
- Source: `[Moses 7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/7?lang=eng)` v.18
- Note: Zion = unity. One heart, one mind. Compare with "one language, one speech" at Babel (Gen 11:1). Zion is voluntary unity in righteousness; Babel is forced unity in pride. Both feature "oneness" but with opposite spirits.

### Genesis 10:5 (Tongues of Japheth's Descendants)
> "By these were the isles of the Gentiles divided in their lands; every one after his tongue, after their families, in their nations."
- Source: `[Genesis 10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/10?lang=eng)` v.5
- Note: This verse describes tongue-differentiation BEFORE Genesis 11. The Table of Nations is presented as a retrospective catalog, but the phrase "after his tongue" is notable.

### Genesis 10:8-10 (Nimrod and Babel)
> "And Cush begat Nimrod: he began to be a mighty one in the earth. He was a mighty hunter before the Lord: wherefore it is said, Even as Nimrod the mighty hunter before the Lord. And the beginning of his kingdom was Babel, and Erech, and Accad, and Calneh, in the land of Shinar."
- Source: `[Genesis 10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/10?lang=eng)` vv.8-10
- Note: Nimrod's kingdom begins at Babel in Shinar. Bible Dictionary confirms: "According to Gen. 10:8–10 it was founded by Nimrod and was one of the oldest cities of the land of Shinar." Nimrod as a "mighty hunter before the Lord" — is "before" a positive or defiant posture?

> "And the whole earth was of one language, and of one speech."
- Source: `[Genesis 11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/11?lang=eng)` v.1
- Note: "One language" AND "one speech" — two phrases. Language (śāpāh = lip, edge, border) and speech (dāḇār = word, matter). Both unified.

### Genesis 11:4 (The Motivation)
> "And they said, Go to, let us build us a city and a tower, whose top may reach unto heaven; and let us make us a name, lest we be scattered abroad upon the face of the whole earth."
- Source: `[Genesis 11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/11?lang=eng)` v.4
- Note: Their fear was being scattered. The tower was about preventing dispersion. Irony: the very thing they feared is what God decreed.

### Genesis 11:6-7 (The Confounding)
> "And the Lord said, Behold, the people is one, and they have all one language; and this they begin to do: and now nothing will be restrained from them, which they have imagined to do. Go to, let us go down, and there confound their language, that they may not understand one another's speech."
- Source: `[Genesis 11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/11?lang=eng)` vv.6-7
- Note: "Nothing will be restrained from them" — their unity gave them dangerous power. God's response: confound (Webster: "mingle and blend, so that different elements cannot be distinguished"). Not erase language — BLEND it. Mix it up.

### Genesis 11:9 (Babel Named)
> "Therefore is the name of it called Babel; because the Lord did there confound the language of all the earth: and from thence did the Lord scatter them abroad upon the face of all the earth."
- Source: `[Genesis 11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/11?lang=eng)` v.9
- Note: Confound + scatter = two actions. The language was mixed; the people were dispersed.

## Webster 1828: CONFOUND

1. **To mingle and blend, so that different elements can not be distinguished; to confuse.**
2. To mistake for another; to identify falsely.
3. To throw into confusion or disorder; to perplex; to strike with amazement; to dismay.
4. To destroy; to ruin; to waste. [Obs.]

**Key insight:** The primary 1828 meaning is "to mingle and blend so that different elements cannot be distinguished." This isn't just "confuse" in the modern lightweight sense. It's a mixing — taking something that was unified and blending it until the parts can't be separated back out. God didn't delete their language; He *mixed* it. The result: "they may not understand one another's speech" (Gen 11:7).

## Historical Context (Exa Search Results)

### Enmerkar and the Lord of Aratta (Sumerian parallel)
- A Sumerian account (ca. 21st century BC, Neo-Sumerian period) describes a "confusion of tongues"
- Enmerkar, king of Unug-Kulaba (Uruk), constructs temples at Eridu and Uruk
- Since Samuel Kramer's time, compared with the Genesis Tower of Babel narrative
- Wikipedia: "It gives a Sumerian account of the 'confusion of tongues'"

### Mesopotamian Echoes (Bible Odyssey)
- Gen 11:1-9 has strong Mesopotamian echoes: the plain of Shinar, building techniques (bricks + bitumen instead of stone + mortar), Babylon
- The most widely attributed inspiration for the Tower is Etemenanki, a ziggurat dedicated to Marduk in Babylon
- Jewish and Islamic lore connects Nimrod to the tower's construction
- Despite the "Mesopotamian touch," there aren't abundant direct Mesopotamian textual parallels

### Before and After Babel (ANE Today, August 2023)
- Marc Van De Mieroop: The parallel in ancient Near East wasn't in spoken language but in *written* language
- In the 3rd and 2nd millennia BCE, Sumerian served as a universal written language even as spoken languages diversified
- The transition from universal Sumerian literacy to multilingual writing systems mirrors the Babel narrative in an unexpected way

### Dating
- The Tower of Babel is traditionally placed c. 2242–2100 BC in various chronologies
- This overlaps with the late Early Bronze Age, the decline of the Akkadian Empire (c. 2154 BC), and the Ur III period
- The Akkadian Empire (c. 2334–2154 BC) unified Mesopotamia under one language (Akkadian) while preserving Sumerian — a bilingual reality that complicates "one language" narratives

### Location debate
- Traditional: Babylon in southern Mesopotamia
- Alternative (Habermehl): Shinar may be in northeastern Syria, near the Upper Khabur River triangle
- The Hebrew name "Babel" (bāḇel) was folk-etymologized as connected to Hebrew bālal ("to confuse/confound"), but the Akkadian Bāb-ilim means "Gate of God"

## Observations & Threads

1. **Genesis 10 vs. Genesis 11 tension:** Gen 10 repeatedly describes peoples "after their tongues" (vv. 5, 20, 31) — implying language diversity already exists. Then Gen 11 says "the whole earth was of one language." Resolution: Gen 10 is a retrospective genealogy (looking back from the writer's perspective), while Gen 11 narrates the event itself. The tongues in Gen 10 are the *result* of what Gen 11 describes.

2. **The Adamic language was more than vocabulary.** Moses 6:57 shows it encoded theology (Man of Holiness = God). Moses 7:13 shows it carried divine power. This wasn't just a communication system — it was a covenant medium.

3. **Nimrod connection.** Nimrod's kingdom begins at Babel (Gen 10:8-10). Bible Dictionary says "founded by Nimrod." He's described as "a mighty hunter before the Lord" — which reads as defiance in context. The post-Flood world's first kingdom-builder is the Babel-founder.

4. **The brother of Jared's prayer reveals what was at stake.** He didn't pray for "our language" in the abstract — he prayed "that we may not understand our words" (Ether 1:34). The fear was losing mutual comprehension. The confounding wasn't just about phonemes; it was about *understanding* — the ability to think together, to be "of one heart and one mind."

5. **Zion vs. Babel as inverse images.** Zion (Moses 7:18): "one heart and one mind," voluntary unity in righteousness. Babel (Gen 11:1-4): "one language, one speech," unity in pride to "make us a name." Both are stories of oneness — one preserved by God, the other shattered.

6. **Zephaniah's promise closes the arc.** The Babel confounding isn't permanent. Zephaniah 3:9 promises God will "turn to the people a pure language" — the same descriptor used for the Adamic language in Moses 6:6. The story arc: pure language → confounded language → restored pure language.

7. **"Confound" (Webster 1828) = mingle/blend.** Not erase. Not replace. *Mix* so thoroughly that the parts can't be distinguished. This implies the original elements are still present — just no longer separable. The Babel event didn't destroy the Adamic language; it fractured it into pieces distributed across all human tongues.

8. **Akkadian "Babel" = "Gate of God."** The Hebrew folk etymology connects it to bālal (confuse), but the original Akkadian means "Gate of God" — a tower to reach heaven. The irony is embedded in the name itself: they called it the gate of God; God called it confusion.
