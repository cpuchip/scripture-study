# Phase 5: Resurrection & Judgment

*Plan of Salvation Study — February 20, 2026*

## The Question That Opens Everything

Corianton is worried. His father Alma can see it: "I perceive that thy mind is worried concerning the resurrection of the dead" ([Alma 40:1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/40?lang=eng&id=p1#p1)). It's a question that rattles everyone eventually — what happens to this body? What happens after? Is there really a rising again?

What Alma unfolds in response is one of the most detailed resurrection teachings in all of scripture. And when we weave it together with Paul's letter to the Corinthians, Joseph and Sidney's vision in D&C 76, the cosmic law-glory framework of D&C 88, Jacob's thundering sermon in 2 Nephi 9, and Abinadi's teaching in Mosiah 15 — a picture emerges that is both more generous and more precise than most of us realize.

This phase is where the promise from Phase 2 gets fulfilled. Remember D&C 93:33-34: "Spirit and element, inseparably connected, receive a fulness of joy; and when separated, man cannot receive a fulness of joy" ([D&C 93:33-34](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93?lang=eng&id=p33-p34#p33)). Resurrection is the event that makes that inseparable connection permanent. It's the redemption of the whole soul.

## The Mechanics of Resurrection

### What Resurrection Actually Is

D&C 88 gives us the clearest definition in all of scripture:

> "The spirit and the body are the soul of man. And the resurrection from the dead is the redemption of the soul." ([D&C 88:15-16](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88?lang=eng&id=p15-p16#p15))

That word **redemption** here is stunning. The resurrection isn't just a reassembly — it's a *redemption*. The soul (spirit + body) was captive; now it's redeemed. Bought back. Freed.

Alma 40 lays out the mechanics:

- **A fixed time**: "There is a time appointed that all shall come forth from the dead" ([Alma 40:4](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/40?lang=eng&id=p4#p4)). God knows this time; we don't.
- **A necessary space**: "There must needs be a space betwixt the time of death and the time of the resurrection" ([Alma 40:6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/40?lang=eng&id=p6#p6)). This is the spirit world from Phase 3.
- **Reuniting, not reassembling**: "The first resurrection ... meaneth the reuniting of the soul with the body" ([Alma 40:18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/40?lang=eng&id=p18#p18)). The word is *reuniting* — these belong together. They were separated; they come back together.
- **Perfect restoration**: "The soul shall be restored to the body, and the body to the soul; yea, and every limb and joint shall be restored to its body; yea, even a hair of the head shall not be lost; but all things shall be restored to their proper and perfect frame" ([Alma 40:23](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/40?lang=eng&id=p23#p23)).

Webster 1828 defines **RESURRECTION** as "a rising again; the resumption of vigor." Even this first, general definition is instructive: it's not just coming back — it's a *resumption of vigor*. Re-empowerment. And secondly: "the rising again from the dead; the resumption of life by the dead."

### The Phase 3 Connection: Disembodiment as Bondage

Remember from Phase 3 that D&C 138:50 taught the dead "had looked upon the long absence of their spirits from their bodies as a bondage." Even the righteous in paradise felt this. Two kinds of bondage emerged in Phase 3:

1. **Bondage of disembodiment** — resolved by *resurrection* (this phase)
2. **Bondage of unrepented sin** — resolved by *gospel teaching + vicarious ordinances* (Phases 3-4)

Resurrection completes the liberation. The captive body is freed from the grave; the captive spirit, from hell or paradise, rejoins it. Jacob says it explicitly: "death and hell must deliver up their dead, and hell must deliver up its captive spirits, and the grave must deliver up its captive bodies, and the bodies and the spirits of men will be restored one to the other; and it is by the power of the resurrection of the Holy One of Israel" ([2 Nephi 9:12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/9?lang=eng&id=p12#p12)).

### Without the Atonement: The Terrifying Alternative

Jacob paints the starkest picture of what would happen without an infinite atonement:

> "If the flesh should rise no more our spirits must become subject to that angel who fell from before the presence of the Eternal God, and became the devil, to rise no more. And our spirits must have become like unto him, and we become devils, angels to a devil, to be shut out from the presence of our God." ([2 Nephi 9:8-9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/9?lang=eng&id=p8-p9#p8))

Without resurrection, we wouldn't just stay dead — we would become *captive to Satan*. Disembodied spirits under his dominion, forever. The Atonement doesn't just rescue us from death — it rescues us from absolute subjugation to the adversary. The stakes of resurrection are far higher than most of us realize.

## Paul's Resurrection Teaching: Celestial and Terrestrial Bodies

### The Seed Analogy

Paul addresses the inevitable question: "How are the dead raised up? and with what body do they come?" ([1 Corinthians 15:35](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/1-cor/15?lang=eng&id=p35#p35)). His answer uses an agricultural metaphor:

> "That which thou sowest is not quickened, except it die: And that which thou sowest, thou sowest not that body that shall be, but bare grain." ([1 Corinthians 15:36-37](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/1-cor/15?lang=eng&id=p36-p37#p36))

The seed must die before the plant rises. And the plant is not the same thing as the seed — it's something *more*. The mortal body goes into the ground; what comes up is related but transformed.

Webster 1828 defines **QUICKEN** as "to make alive; to vivify; to revive or resuscitate, as from death or an inanimate state." You can't be quickened unless you've first died. Death is the necessary precondition for the quickening. This mirrors Phase 2's insight about the Fall being a designed doorway: without the Fall (death), there's no resurrection (quickening).

### The Four Contrasts

Paul gives us a stunning four-fold contrast for what resurrection does:

| Sown (mortality) | Raised (resurrection) |
|---|---|
| In **corruption** | In **incorruption** |
| In **dishonour** | In **glory** |
| In **weakness** | In **power** |
| A **natural** body | A **spiritual** body |

([1 Corinthians 15:42-44](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/1-cor/15?lang=eng&id=p42-p44#p42))

Webster 1828 defines **INCORRUPTION** as "the condition or quality of being incorrupt or incorruptible; absence of, or exemption from, corruption." Not just "not corrupt" — *exempt from corruption.* Permanently beyond decay. The resurrected body doesn't just stop decaying; it becomes incapable of decay.

### Celestial, Terrestrial, and Telestial Bodies

Then Paul makes the connection that triggered Joseph Smith's vision:

> "There are also celestial bodies, and bodies terrestrial: but the glory of the celestial is one, and the glory of the terrestrial is another. There is one glory of the sun, and another glory of the moon, and another glory of the stars: for one star differeth from another star in glory." ([1 Corinthians 15:40-41](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/1-cor/15?lang=eng&id=p40-p41#p40))

The JST adds what Paul almost certainly taught but what was lost from his letter: "Also celestial bodies, and bodies terrestrial, *and bodies telestial;* but the glory of the celestial, one; and the terrestrial, another; *and the telestial, another*" (JST 1 Cor. 15:40, footnote a).

This is revolutionary. Paul isn't just saying bodies come back — he's saying they come back at different *levels of glory*. The resurrected body itself carries the law it lived, the glory it can abide.

## The Law-Glory Framework: D&C 88

### You Receive What You Can Abide

D&C 88 gives us the most precise framework for how resurrection connects to glory:

> "He who is not able to abide the law of a celestial kingdom cannot abide a celestial glory. And he who cannot abide the law of a terrestrial kingdom cannot abide a terrestrial glory. And he who cannot abide the law of a telestial kingdom cannot abide a telestial glory; therefore he is not meet for a kingdom of glory." ([D&C 88:22-24](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88?lang=eng&id=p22-p24#p22))

The word **abide** here is doing crucial work. President Oaks noted in "Kingdoms of Glory" that it means "a secure placement." You inherit the kingdom you can comfortably, securely live in. Not the one you're forced into — the one that *fits* you.

The resurrection itself is the mechanism:

> "They who are of a celestial spirit shall receive the same body which was a natural body; even ye shall receive your bodies, and your glory shall be that glory by which your bodies are quickened. Ye who are quickened by a portion of the celestial glory shall then receive of the same, even a fulness." ([D&C 88:28-29](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88?lang=eng&id=p28-p29#p28))

The *glory* that quickens you determines the *body* you receive. And the glory that quickens you corresponds to the *law* you lived. This is a breathtaking chain: **law → glory → quickening → body → kingdom**.

Webster 1828's fourth definition of **GLORY** illuminates this: "The presence of the Divine Being; the manifestations of the divine nature and favor to the blessed in heaven; celestial honor; heaven." Glory isn't just brightness — it's *presence*. The degree of glory you receive corresponds to how much of God's presence you can abide.

### Even Those Who Refuse

Even this framework has room for those who reject all law:

> "They who remain shall also be quickened; nevertheless, they shall return again to their own place, to enjoy that which they are willing to receive, because they were not willing to enjoy that which they might have received." ([D&C 88:32](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88?lang=eng&id=p32#p32))

The phrase "they were not willing to enjoy" is haunting. God offered more. They didn't want it. And God honors that choice.

### The Governing Principle

Then comes one of the most elegant verses in all of scripture:

> "That which is governed by law is also preserved by law and perfected and sanctified by the same." ([D&C 88:34](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88?lang=eng&id=p34#p34))

And its opposite: "That which breaketh a law, and abideth not by law, but seeketh to become a law unto itself, and willeth to abide in sin, and altogether abideth in sin, cannot be sanctified by law, neither by mercy, justice, nor judgment" ([D&C 88:35](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88?lang=eng&id=p35#p35)).

The law doesn't just restrict — it *preserves, perfects, and sanctifies*. Those who follow it are shaped by it. Those who reject it lose its protection. This connects directly to the restoration principle in Alma 41.

## The Resurrection Order: Trumpets and Timing

### D&C 88's Trumpet Sequence

D&C 88:95-102 reveals that resurrection happens in ordered waves, each announced by an angelic trump:

1. **First trump** — The celestial: "Christ's, the first fruits, they who shall descend with him first, and they who are on the earth and in their graves, who are first caught up to meet him" ([D&C 88:98](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88?lang=eng&id=p98#p98))
2. **Second trump** — Those "who have received their part in that prison which is prepared for them, that they might receive the gospel" ([D&C 88:99](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88?lang=eng&id=p99#p99)). These are the spirit world converts from Phase 3-4.
3. **Third trump** — "The spirits of men who are to be judged, and are found under condemnation" ([D&C 88:100](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88?lang=eng&id=p100#p100)). These "live not again until the thousand years are ended" ([D&C 88:101](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88?lang=eng&id=p101#p101)).
4. **Fourth trump** — "Those who are to remain ... who shall remain filthy still" ([D&C 88:102](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88?lang=eng&id=p102#p102)).

### Mosiah 15: Who Qualifies for the First Resurrection

Abinadi gives the most inclusive list:

- All the prophets ([Mosiah 15:22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p22#p22))
- All who believed in the prophets' words ([Mosiah 15:22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p22#p22))
- All who kept the commandments of God ([Mosiah 15:22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p22#p22))
- Those who died in ignorance, "not having salvation declared unto them" ([Mosiah 15:24](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p24#p24))
- Little children ([Mosiah 15:25](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p25#p25))

The excluded: "none such that rebel against him and die in their sins; yea, even all those that have perished in their sins ever since the world began, that have wilfully rebelled against God, that have known the commandments of God, and would not keep them" ([Mosiah 15:26](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p26#p26)). The key qualifier is *wilfully*. Those who never knew have a place. Those who knew and wilfully refused don't — at least not in the *first* resurrection.

### But All Are Resurrected

Paul's universal promise stands: "As in Adam all die, even so in Christ shall all be made alive. But every man in his own order" ([1 Corinthians 15:22-23](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/1-cor/15?lang=eng&id=p22-p23#p22)). The Greek behind "order" means *rank* (footnote 23a). Everyone rises, but in their own rank, in their own time, with their own glory.

D&C 76 confirms: "All the rest shall be brought forth by the resurrection of the dead, through the triumph and the glory of the Lamb" ([D&C 76:39](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/76?lang=eng&id=p39#p39)). Even the telestial kingdom — the abode of "liars, and sorcerers, and adulterers" ([D&C 76:103](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/76?lang=eng&id=p103#p103)) — receives bodies of glory, and that glory "surpasses all understanding" ([D&C 76:89](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/76?lang=eng&id=p89#p89)). The only exceptions are the sons of perdition, "the only ones on whom the second death shall have any power" ([D&C 76:37](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/76?lang=eng&id=p37#p37)).

## The Final Judgment: What We Have Become

### Webster 1828: Judgment as Discernment

**JUDGMENT** in Webster 1828 is first defined as "the operation of the mind, involving comparison and discrimination, by which a knowledge of the values and relations of things ... is obtained." God's judgment isn't arbitrary sentencing — it's perfect discernment. He sees the values and relations of everything we are. His eighth definition gets to the final scene: "the final award; the last sentence."

### The Judgment Scene

John sees it in vision:

> "I saw the dead, small and great, stand before God; and the books were opened: and another book was opened, which is the book of life: and the dead were judged out of those things which were written in the books, according to their works." ([Revelation 20:12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/rev/20?lang=eng&id=p12#p12))

Two sets of books. The books of works — what we've done. And the book of life — who we are. Both matter.

Then the triumph: "Death and hell were cast into the lake of fire. This is the second death" ([Revelation 20:14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/rev/20?lang=eng&id=p14#p14)). Death itself is destroyed. Paul had promised this: "The last enemy that shall be destroyed is death" ([1 Corinthians 15:26](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/1-cor/15?lang=eng&id=p26#p26)).

### 2 Nephi 9: Perfect Knowledge

Jacob adds a devastating and beautiful detail about judgment:

> "We shall have a perfect knowledge of all our guilt, and our uncleanness, and our nakedness; and the righteous shall have a perfect knowledge of their enjoyment, and their righteousness, being clothed with purity, yea, even with the robe of righteousness." ([2 Nephi 9:14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/9?lang=eng&id=p14#p14))

At judgment, the veil is gone. We see ourselves clearly. The guilty feel *naked*. The righteous are *clothed*. And notice — the righteous are clothed not with their own works but with the "robe of righteousness." That's a gift, not an achievement.

### Alma 41: The Restoration Principle — You Get Back What You Are

This is the heart of judgment:

> "It is requisite with the justice of God that men should be judged according to their works; and if their works were good in this life, and the desires of their hearts were good, that they should also, at the last day, be restored unto that which is good." ([Alma 41:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/41?lang=eng&id=p3#p3))

Notice: works AND desires. Not just behavior — *intent*.

Then Alma's devastating clarity: "Wickedness never was happiness" ([Alma 41:10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/41?lang=eng&id=p10#p10)). You can't be restored from sin *to* happiness, because sin was never happiness in the first place. You can only be restored to what you actually are.

The restoration principle explained:

> "The meaning of the word restoration is to bring back again evil for evil, or carnal for carnal, or devilish for devilish—good for that which is good; righteous for that which is righteous; just for that which is just; merciful for that which is merciful." ([Alma 41:13](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/41?lang=eng&id=p13#p13))

Webster 1828 on **RESTORE**: "To bring back to its former state; to bring back from a state of ruin, decay, disease, or the like; to repair; to renew; to recover." Restoration means you go back to what you actually are. If you've been cultivating mercy, you get mercy back. If you've been cultivating cruelty, you get cruelty back. The universe echoes your character.

And then the jaw-dropping implication: "They are their own judges" ([Alma 41:7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/41?lang=eng&id=p7#p7)). Cross-referenced to [2 Nephi 2:26](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/2?lang=eng&id=p26#p26) and [Helaman 14:30](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/hel/14?lang=eng&id=p30#p30). We literally judge ourselves — not by what we decide, but by what we've become. The judgment is the natural consequence of our becoming.

### The Cosmic Echo: D&C 88:40

This principle is woven into the fabric of reality itself:

> "Intelligence cleaveth unto intelligence; wisdom receiveth wisdom; truth embraceth truth; virtue loveth virtue; light cleaveth unto light; mercy hath compassion on mercy and claimeth her own; justice continueth its course and claimeth its own." ([D&C 88:40](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88?lang=eng&id=p40#p40))

Like attracts like. What you are gravitates to its match. This isn't arbitrary assignment — it's the natural order of things. The judgment is the moment when what you've become finds its proper place in the cosmos.

### Elder Oaks: The Challenge to Become

Elder Oaks pulled these threads together definitively in two landmark talks:

From "The Challenge to Become" (October 2000):

> "The Final Judgment is not just an evaluation of a sum total of good and evil acts—what we have *done.* It is an acknowledgment of the final effect of our acts and thoughts—what we have *become.* It is not enough for anyone just to go through the motions. The commandments, ordinances, and covenants of the gospel are not a list of deposits required to be made in some heavenly account. The gospel of Jesus Christ is a plan that shows us how to become what our Heavenly Father desires us to become." ([Elder Dallin H. Oaks, "The Challenge to Become," October 2000 General Conference](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2000/10/the-challenge-to-become?lang=eng))

His parable of the wealthy father is unforgettable: "That which I *have* I can easily give you, but that which I *am* you must obtain for yourself." Inheritance requires becoming. The Father can't just hand us exaltation — we have to *become* the kind of beings who can abide celestial glory.

And on charity: "Charity ... is not an *act* but a *condition* or state of being. Charity is attained through a succession of acts that result in a conversion. Charity is something one *becomes*."

From "Kingdoms of Glory" (October 2023):

> "The kingdom of glory we receive in the Final Judgment is determined by the laws we choose to follow in our mortal journey. Under that loving plan, there are multiple kingdoms—many mansions—so that all of God's children will inherit a kingdom of glory whose laws they can comfortably 'abide.'" ([President Dallin H. Oaks, "Kingdoms of Glory," October 2023 General Conference](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2023/10/17oaks?lang=eng))

And President Nelson's distillation, quoted by Oaks: "During this mortal life you get to choose which laws you are willing to obey—those of the celestial kingdom, or the terrestrial, or the telestial—and, therefore, in which kingdom of glory you will live forever. What a plan! It is a plan that completely honors *your* agency."

### Christ's Role in Judgment: The Keeper of the Gate

2 Nephi 9 gives us the most intimate portrait of Christ as Judge:

> "The keeper of the gate is the Holy One of Israel; and he employeth no servant there; and there is none other way save it be by the gate; for he cannot be deceived, for the Lord God is his name." ([2 Nephi 9:41](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/9?lang=eng&id=p41#p41))

Christ stands at the gate *personally*. He employs no servant. This means:
- No bureaucratic process — He meets each person directly
- No deception possible — He who descended below all things ([D&C 88:6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88?lang=eng&id=p6#p6)) cannot be fooled
- But also: the one who judges is the one who *suffered for us*. Mosiah 15:9 says He stands "betwixt them and justice; having broken the bands of death, taken upon himself their iniquity and their transgressions, having redeemed them, and satisfied the demands of justice" ([Mosiah 15:9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p9#p9))

The Judge is also the Advocate. The one who weighs our souls is the one who bore their weight of sin. That changes everything about how we should think about judgment.

## The Grand Architecture: Connecting the Phases

### Phase 1 → Phase 5: Agency Across the Veil

In the premortal council, we chose to come. We "shouted for joy" ([Job 38:7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/job/38?lang=eng&id=p7#p7)). That original agency is honored all the way through judgment. Alma 41:7 says "they are their own judges." We chose the plan; we choose our becoming; the judgment reflects our choice. Agency isn't just a mortal feature — it's the thread through every phase.

### Phase 2 → Phase 5: The Promise Fulfilled

Phase 2 revealed that "spirit and element, inseparably connected, receive a fulness of joy" ([D&C 93:33](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93?lang=eng&id=p33#p33)). Resurrection is where this happens. D&C 88:15-16 confirms: "the spirit and the body are the soul of man. And the resurrection from the dead is the redemption of the soul." The Phase 2 insight about the Fall requiring physicality for joy reaches its completion here: the body you get back is permanent. That joy is permanent.

### Phase 3-4 → Phase 5: The Liberation Sequence

Phase 3: Gospel taught to captive spirits.
Phase 4: Vicarious ordinances performed for those spirits.
Phase 5: Resurrection delivers the assembled soul — spirit and body reunited, ordinances in place, gospel received. The whole system — missionaries, temples, and resurrection — works together.

### The Generosity of the System

The Father "saves all the works of his hands" ([D&C 76:43](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/76?lang=eng&id=p43#p43)). Even the telestial kingdom's glory "surpasses all understanding" ([D&C 76:89](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/76?lang=eng&id=p89#p89)). Its inhabitants are "innumerable as the stars" ([D&C 76:109](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/76?lang=eng&id=p109#p109)). The system is overwhelmingly generous. Only a vanishingly small number (sons of perdition) are excluded from *all* kingdoms of glory. Everyone else — every sinner, every soul who struggled, every person who never heard the gospel — has a place in a kingdom of glory.

## Comfort Promises

For friends who are sick and facing mortality:

1. **Your body will be made whole.** "Every limb and joint shall be restored to its body; yea, even a hair of the head shall not be lost; but all things shall be restored to their proper and perfect frame" ([Alma 40:23](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/40?lang=eng&id=p23#p23)). Whatever disease or limitation defines the body now will be gone. The resurrected body is exempt from corruption forever (Webster 1828: "absence of, or exemption from, corruption").

2. **Resurrection is universal.** "As in Adam all die, even so in Christ shall all be made alive" ([1 Corinthians 15:22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/1-cor/15?lang=eng&id=p22#p22)). This is a gift, not a reward. Everyone receives it.

3. **Christ is the one who judges you, and He is the one who suffered for you.** "The keeper of the gate is the Holy One of Israel; and he employeth no servant there" ([2 Nephi 9:41](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/9?lang=eng&id=p41#p41)). The Judge knows your pain from the inside.

4. **Your desires matter as much as your actions.** "If their works were good in this life, and the desires of their hearts were good" ([Alma 41:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/41?lang=eng&id=p3#p3)). People who want to be good but are limited by mortality's constraints are *seen*.

5. **Your suffering is not wasted.** Elder Oaks: "Our needed conversions are often achieved more readily by suffering and adversity than by comfort and tranquillity. ... The refinement and sanctification possible through such experiences can help us achieve what God desires us to become" ([Elder Dallin H. Oaks, "The Challenge to Become"](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2000/10/the-challenge-to-become?lang=eng)).

6. **The system is overwhelmingly generous.** Even the least kingdom of glory "surpasses all understanding" ([D&C 76:89](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/76?lang=eng&id=p89#p89)). The Father "saves all the works of his hands" ([D&C 76:43](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/76?lang=eng&id=p43#p43)).

7. **Death itself will be destroyed.** "The last enemy that shall be destroyed is death" ([1 Corinthians 15:26](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/1-cor/15?lang=eng&id=p26#p26)). Death and hell are cast into the lake of fire ([Revelation 20:14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/rev/20?lang=eng&id=p14#p14)). Death doesn't win. It doesn't even survive.

8. **"O death, where is thy sting? O grave, where is thy victory?"** ([1 Corinthians 15:55](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/1-cor/15?lang=eng&id=p55#p55)). Paul's triumph song is the song of every believer who faces mortality with faith. The grave is not the end. It's a doorway to quickening.

## Questions That Press Forward into Phase 6

- D&C 76 describes who inhabits each kingdom. Phase 6 will explore what *life* in each kingdom looks like — what does it mean to "receive of the presence of the Son, but not of the fulness of the Father" (D&C 76:77)?
- D&C 131:1-4 adds levels *within* the celestial kingdom. What are the conditions for the highest degree?
- D&C 132 introduces the new and everlasting covenant of marriage as the gateway to exaltation. How does the family structure we've been building across all phases reach its completion?
- The comfort thread: how does knowing the kingdoms of glory change how we speak to friends about what comes next?
