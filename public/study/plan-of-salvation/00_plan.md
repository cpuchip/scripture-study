# Study Plan: The Plan of Salvation

*Created: 2026-02-19*

## Driving Question

**What is the Plan of Salvation — not as a Primary diagram, but as a living architecture?**

We want to trace the soul's journey from pre-mortal intelligence to eternal glory, connecting each stage with actual scriptural text, prophetic teaching, and the profound body of work we've already built (truth, intelligence, creation, atonement). Along the way, we want to answer a deeply personal question: *What comfort does this plan actually offer someone who is sick, dying, or grieving?*

### Sub-Questions

1. **The 30,000-foot view:** What are the stages, and how do they connect? What holds the whole thing together?
2. **Pre-mortal life:** What do we actually know? What was the council? What choices were made there?
3. **Mortality as school, not just probation:** Is "probationary state" the whole picture, or is mortality more like a divine education — designed to teach us *how* to live a Christlike life?
4. **The spirit world:** Where do we go after death? What is paradise vs. spirit prison? What happens there? What comfort can we offer friends who are facing death right now?
5. **Baptism for the dead:** What about those who didn't receive ordinances in this life? How does vicarious work function in the plan?
6. **Resurrection:** What are the mechanics? What do the scriptures actually say about resurrected bodies and timing?
7. **Judgment:** What is it? Is it a courtroom scene, or something else?
8. **Kingdoms of glory:** What distinguishes them? What does "salvation" really mean at each level?

## Reference: Preach My Gospel 2023

[PMG Lesson 2 — Heavenly Father's Plan of Salvation](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/preach-my-gospel-2023/04-chapter-3/09-chapter-3-lesson-2?lang=eng) covers the same stages we're exploring, but at a missionary-teaching level (15-25 minutes). It's useful as a structural check and a reminder of what the Church considers the essential framing:

- **Premortal life** → Creation → Fall → Mortality → Atonement → Spirit World → Resurrection → Judgment → Kingdoms of Glory
- Key repeated phrase: *"All that is unfair about life can be made right through the Atonement of Jesus Christ."*
- Good scripture references to cross-check: Ecclesiastes 12:7 (spirit returns to God), Abraham 3:25-26 (we *chose* to come), President Ballard on divine identity

Our study goes far deeper — into the mechanics (truth/intelligence studies), the temple's enactment of the plan (way-truth-life), the "probation vs. school" question, vicarious work, and the personal comfort thread. PMG is the syllabus; this is the semester.

## Phases

### Phase 1: The Bookends — Pre-Mortal Existence & The Council
**Skill:** `deep-reading`
**Focus:** Abraham 3, D&C 138:53-56, Moses 4:1-4, Job 38:4-7, D&C 93:29-30, Alma 13:3-5
**Output:** Intermediate findings in `notes-01-premortal.md`
**Questions to answer:**
- What do we know about pre-mortal life from actual scripture (not cultural assumptions)?
- What was the nature of the "council"? What was decided?
- How does Abraham 3's teaching on intelligences connect to our intelligence/truth studies?
- What does "noble and great ones" mean in context (Abraham 3:22-23)?
- What was foreordination, and how does it relate to agency?
- How does D&C 93:29-30 (intelligence uncreated, self-acting) change our understanding of pre-mortality?

### Phase 2: The Fall & Mortality as Divine Education
**Skill:** `deep-reading`
**Focus:** 2 Nephi 2 (Lehi's grand sermon), Alma 12:24-27, Alma 34:32-35, Alma 42, Moses 5-6, D&C 29:39-45
**Output:** Intermediate findings in `notes-02-mortality.md`
**Questions to answer:**
- Is mortality a "probation" (testing/proving) or a "school" (education/transformation) — or both?
- What does Webster 1828 say about "probation"? Does it carry educational connotations we've lost?
- Lehi's 2 Nephi 2 — the most complete single-chapter theology of the Fall. What does it teach about *why* the Fall was necessary?
- How do our truth/atonement studies illuminate mortality's purpose? (The spectrum, the circle of existence, refinement)
- What is the relationship between agency, opposition, and growth?
- How does Moses 6 (gospel taught to Adam) reframe mortality as designed for learning, not just enduring?

### Phase 3: The Spirit World — Where Are They Now?
**Skill:** `deep-reading`
**Focus:** D&C 138 (entire vision), Alma 40:11-14, 1 Peter 3:18-20 & 4:6, Luke 23:43 ("paradise"), D&C 131:7-8, Alma 34:34
**Output:** Intermediate findings in `notes-03-spirit-world.md`
**Questions to answer:**
- What is paradise? What is spirit prison? Are they separate or aspects of the same place?
- What happens in the spirit world? Is there progression? Learning? Missionary work?
- D&C 138 — Joseph F. Smith's vision. What did he actually see? How is missionary work organized there?
- What does "the same spirit which doth possess your bodies at the time that ye go out of this life" (Alma 34:34) actually mean? Is it about disposition, habits, or something deeper?
- **The comfort question:** For someone who is sick and facing death — what does the gospel *actually promise* about what comes next? Paradise is described as a state of rest and peace. What does that look like?
- What about those who weren't baptized? What hope does the spirit world offer?

### Phase 4: Baptism for the Dead & Vicarious Work
**Skill:** `deep-reading` + `wide-search`
**Focus:** D&C 128 (Joseph's letters on baptism for the dead), D&C 127, 1 Corinthians 15:29, D&C 124:28-36, D&C 137:7-10, Malachi 4:5-6
**Output:** Intermediate findings in `notes-04-vicarious-work.md`
**Questions to answer:**
- What is the scriptural basis for baptism for the dead? How did Paul reference it?
- What did Joseph Smith teach about it in D&C 127-128?
- D&C 137:7-10 — the vision of the celestial kingdom. How does it address those who died without the gospel?
- How does Malachi's promise about Elijah connect to this work?
- What does this say about God's fairness and the scope of the Atonement?
- How does this connect to the spirit world work from Phase 3?

### Phase 5: Resurrection & Judgment
**Skill:** `deep-reading`
**Focus:** Alma 40-41, 1 Corinthians 15, D&C 76, D&C 88:14-33, 2 Nephi 9:6-22, Mosiah 15:20-27, Revelation 20:12-15
**Output:** Intermediate findings in `notes-05-resurrection-judgment.md`
**Questions to answer:**
- What are the mechanics of resurrection? Alma 40's teaching on timing and order.
- What does Paul mean by "celestial bodies" and "terrestrial bodies" in 1 Corinthians 15?
- How does resurrection connect to the matter spectrum from our truth study? ("Spirit and element, inseparably connected, receive a fulness of joy" — D&C 93:33)
- What is the final judgment? Elder Oaks taught it's about what we've *become*, not just what we've done. What do the scriptures say?
- Alma 41's "restoration" — you get back what you *are*. How does that reframe judgment?
- What role does Christ play in judgment? (2 Nephi 9's teaching on the "Holy One of Israel")

### Phase 6: Kingdoms of Glory & Eternal Life
**Skill:** `deep-reading` + `wide-search`
**Focus:** D&C 76 (the Vision), D&C 131, D&C 132:19-24, D&C 137, D&C 88:17-33
**Output:** Intermediate findings in `notes-06-kingdoms.md`
**Questions to answer:**
- What distinguishes telestial, terrestrial, and celestial glory?
- What does our way-truth-life study add? (Temple as enacted plan, kingdoms mapped to tabernacle)
- What is "eternal life" vs. "immortality"? (Moses 1:39 distinguishes them)
- What is exaltation specifically? How does D&C 132 describe it?
- D&C 137 — Joseph's vision of the celestial kingdom. What surprised him?
- How does the know-god study illuminate this? (Eternal life = knowing God)
- What does "salvation" mean at each level? Is everyone "saved"?

### Phase 7: Synthesis — The 30,000-Foot View
**Output:** `plan-of-salvation.md` — the comprehensive study document
**Structure:**
1. **The Architecture** — A visual/narrative overview of the entire plan, stage by stage
2. **The Thread That Holds It Together** — Christ as the central figure at every stage (drawing from truth-atonement, way-truth-life)
3. **Mortality Reframed** — Not just probation, but divine education. The school metaphor grounded in scripture.
4. **The Comfort Section** — "Where Are They Now?" — a section specifically written for sharing with friends who are sick or grieving. Drawing from Phase 3 findings.
5. **What Existing Studies Reveal** — How our intelligence, truth, creation, atonement, agency, and covenant studies map onto the Plan
6. **Open Questions** — What we still don't fully understand

### Phase 8: Becoming
**Skill:** `becoming`
**Output:** Personal application section in the final doc + `becoming/` companion if warranted
**Questions:**
- How does understanding the plan change how I live *today*?
- What do I say to my friends who are sick? What words actually carry the Spirit?
- If mortality is a school, what am I currently learning? What's the curriculum?
- How does knowing the architecture of existence change my prayers, my service, my patience?
