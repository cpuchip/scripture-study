# The Father and the Son: Distinct Roles in the Plan of Salvation

> *"The term 'Father' as applied to Deity occurs in sacred writ with plainly different meanings."*
> — [The Father and the Son: A Doctrinal Exposition, 1916](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/teachings-joseph-f-smith/chapter-40?lang=eng)

---

## Why This Study Exists

During the synthesis of our [plan-of-salvation study](plan-of-salvation.md), the North Star section contained a sentence that read: *"It is a relationship with the Person who designed it, enacted it, suffered for it, and stands at every transition point within it."* The sentence points entirely to Christ — and rightly so for much of it. Christ *enacted* the plan. Christ *suffered* for it. Christ *stands at every transition point*.

But Christ did not *design* the plan. The Father did.

That single word — "designed" — conflates the roles of two distinct, glorified Beings. And if we're not careful about that distinction in our own study documents, we contribute to the very confusion the Restoration was sent to correct. The First Vision itself began by separating what centuries of creeds had fused together: two personages, standing above Joseph, distinct and separate, with the Father introducing the Son.

This study explores the doctrine of the Father and the Son — their distinct roles, the specific senses in which Christ bears the title "Father," and how to read scripture with the kind of precision that honors both Beings.

---

## Part I: The Distinct Roles

### God the Father — Elohim

The scriptures consistently identify the Father as the **author**, the **presiding authority**, and the **originator** of the plan of salvation. He is not a passive figure who delegated and stepped away. He is the Chief.

Joseph Smith taught this directly:

> *"Any person that had seen the heavens opened knows that there are three personages in the heavens who hold the keys of power, and one presides over all. … These personages … are called God the first, the Creator; God the second, the Redeemer; and God the third, the Witness or Testator. [It is] the province of the Father to preside as the Chief or President, Jesus as the Mediator, and the Holy Ghost as the Testator or Witness."*
> — cited in [The Godhead and the Plan of Salvation](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2017/04/the-godhead-and-the-plan-of-salvation?lang=eng), Elder Dallin H. Oaks

Elder Oaks declared plainly:

> *"God the Father is the Father of our spirits. We are His children. He loves us, and all that He does is for our eternal benefit. He is the **author** of the plan of salvation, and it is by His power that His plan achieves its purposes for the ultimate glory of His children."*
> — [The Godhead and the Plan of Salvation](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2017/04/the-godhead-and-the-plan-of-salvation?lang=eng)

The grand council scene in Abraham confirms this architecture. It is "one like unto God" — the Father — who presides, who announces the purpose ("We will go down … we will make an earth … we will prove them"), and who asks the pivotal question:

> *"Whom shall I send?"*
> — [Abraham 3:27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/abr/3?lang=eng&id=p27#p27)

The *I* in that question is not Christ. It is the Father, the one with the authority to send, looking for a volunteer to carry out **His** plan.

Moses 1 underscores this ownership. The Father speaks directly to Moses about the purpose of all creation — including the creation of worlds through the Son:

> *"By the **word** of my power, have I created them, which is mine Only Begotten Son, who is full of grace and truth."*
> — [Moses 1:32](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/1?lang=eng&id=p32#p32)

> *"And worlds without number have I created; and I also created them for **mine own purpose**; and **by the Son** I created them, which is mine Only Begotten."*
> — [Moses 1:33](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/1?lang=eng&id=p33#p33)

And then the defining statement of the entire plan:

> *"For behold, this is **my** work and **my** glory — to bring to pass the immortality and eternal life of man."*
> — [Moses 1:39](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/1?lang=eng&id=p39#p39)

This is the Father speaking. My work. My glory. The plan belongs to Him. He designed it. He ordained it. He presides over it.

The 1916 exposition affirmed: *"God the Eternal Father, whom we designate by the exalted name-title 'Elohim,' is the literal Parent of our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ, and of the spirits of the human race. Elohim is the Father in every sense in which Jesus Christ is so designated, and distinctively He is the Father of spirits."* — [Chapter 40](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/teachings-joseph-f-smith/chapter-40?lang=eng)

**Summary — The Father's Role:**
- Author and designer of the plan of salvation
- Father of all spirits — literal parent
- Presiding Chief of the Godhead
- The one who asks "Whom shall I send?" — the sender, not the sent one
- The one whose work and glory this is

### God the Son — Jehovah / Jesus Christ

Christ's role is not lesser — it is different. He is the **executor** of the Father's plan, chosen and foreordained, the one who said "Here am I, send me" ([Abraham 3:27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/abr/3?lang=eng&id=p27#p27)).

In the council, when Satan proposed to go and guarantee salvation by compulsion — demanding the Father's honor in return — Christ offered the opposite:

> *"Father, **thy** will be done, and the glory be **thine** forever."*
> — [Moses 4:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/4?lang=eng&id=p2#p2)

This is the heart of the Son's identity. He executes the Father's will. He carries out the Father's design. And He returns the glory to the Father. When He prays in the Garden of Gethsemane, He does not pray "My will be done" — He prays, *"Not my will, but thine, be done"* ([Luke 22:42](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/luke/22?lang=eng&id=p42#p42)).

The Intercessory Prayer in John 17 crystallizes this relationship:

> *"I have glorified **thee** on the earth: I have finished the work which **thou gavest me** to do."*
> — [John 17:4](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/17?lang=eng&id=p4#p4)

The work is the Father's. The doing is the Son's. Christ is consistently the one who *receives* the mission, *carries out* the mandate, *finishes* the work given Him:

> *"As **thou** hast given him power over all flesh, that he should give eternal life to as many as **thou** hast given him."*
> — [John 17:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/17?lang=eng&id=p2#p2)

> *"The Son, the greatest of all, was **chosen by the Father** to carry out **the Father's plan** — to exercise the Father's power to create worlds without number and to save the children of God from death by His Resurrection and from sin by His Atonement."*
> — [The Godhead and the Plan of Salvation](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2017/04/the-godhead-and-the-plan-of-salvation?lang=eng)

Even Christ's creative power is *derived* — authorized and delegated by the Father. Hebrews establishes this:

> *"[God] hath in these last days spoken unto us by his Son, whom he hath appointed heir of all things, **by whom also he made the worlds**."*
> — [Hebrews 1:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/heb/1?lang=eng&id=p2#p2)

> *"Who being the brightness of his glory, and **the express image of his person**."*
> — [Hebrews 1:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/heb/1?lang=eng&id=p3#p3)

And D&C 76:

> *"Those things which were from the beginning before the world was, which were **ordained of the Father**, through his Only Begotten Son."*
> — [D&C 76:13](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/76?lang=eng&id=p13#p13)

**Summary — The Son's Role:**
- Firstborn of all the Father's spirit children
- Chosen and foreordained to carry out the Father's plan
- Creator — as Executive, under the Father's direction and authority
- Redeemer — the one who suffered, bled, and died to complete the Atonement
- Mediator between Father and humankind
- The one who says "Here am I, send me" — the sent one, not the sender
- The one who finishes the work the Father gave Him to do

### The Relationship: Not Hierarchy of Worth, but Order of Operation

John 14 captures both the intimacy and the distinction:

> *"He that hath seen me hath seen the Father"* — [John 14:9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/14?lang=eng&id=p9#p9)

But in the same discourse:

> *"My Father is greater than I."* — [John 14:28](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/14?lang=eng&id=p28#p28)

These are not contradictory. Christ perfectly represents the Father — to see Christ *is* to see what the Father is like. But "greater" doesn't mean "better" in some competitive sense. It means the Father presides. He is the author. The Son carries the Father's authority, reflects the Father's character, executes the Father's will — and in that order of operation, the Father is "greater."

D&C 93 illuminates how Christ relates to the Father internally:

> *"I am in the Father, and the Father in me, and the Father and I are one — The Father because he **gave me of his fulness**, and the Son because I was in the world and made flesh my tabernacle."*
> — [D&C 93:3–4](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93?lang=eng&id=p3-p4#p3)

The oneness is real. It is a oneness of purpose, knowledge, power, and love — two separate, glorified Beings so perfectly aligned that to know one is to know the other. But they are still two.

President Joseph F. Smith: *"I do not apprehend that any intelligent person will construe these words to mean that Jesus and his Father are one person, but merely that they are one in knowledge, in truth, in wisdom, in understanding, and in purpose."* — [Chapter 40](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/teachings-joseph-f-smith/chapter-40?lang=eng)

## Part II: The Three Senses in Which Christ Is Called "Father"

This is where it gets hard — and where most conflation happens. The 1916 First Presidency and Quorum of the Twelve exposition identified three specific senses in which Christ bears the title "Father" in scripture. Understanding these is essential to reading the Book of Mormon, the Doctrine and Covenants, and the Pearl of Great Price with precision.

### 1. Father of Heaven and Earth — Creator

Christ is called "the very Eternal Father of heaven and of earth" because He is the *Creator* — the one who organized this earth and the heavens under the Father's direction.

Zeezrom asked Amulek directly:

> *"Is the Son of God the very Eternal Father?"*
> And Amulek said: *"Yea, he is the very Eternal Father of heaven and of earth, and **all things which in them are**; he is the beginning and the end, the first and the last."*
> — [Alma 11:38–39](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/11?lang=eng&id=p38-p39#p38)

Isaiah's messianic prophecy uses this same sense:

> *"For unto us a child is born, unto us a son is given; and the government shall be upon his shoulder; and his name shall be called, Wonderful, Counselor, The Mighty God, **The Everlasting Father**, The Prince of Peace."*
> — [2 Nephi 19:6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/19?lang=eng&id=p6#p6) (compare [Isaiah 9:6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/isa/9?lang=eng&id=p6#p6))

The 1916 exposition explains: *"Jesus Christ, whom we also know as Jehovah, was the executive of the Father, Elohim, in the work of creation. … Jesus Christ, being the Creator, is consistently called the Father of heaven and earth … ; and since His creations are of eternal quality He is very properly called the Eternal Father of heaven and earth."* — [Chapter 40](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/teachings-joseph-f-smith/chapter-40?lang=eng)

This sense is critical. Christ is "Father" of the creation because He *made* it. But He made it under the Father's direction, by the Father's authority, for the Father's purposes. Moses 1:32–33 makes this explicit — the Father says *"I created … by the Son."* The design is the Father's. The execution is the Son's.

### 2. Father of Those Who Abide in His Gospel — Spiritual Rebirth

This is the sense the user identified — and it is deeply personal. When we are baptized, when we take upon ourselves the name of Christ, we are *born again*. Christ becomes our spiritual Father through covenant.

King Benjamin's people experienced this:

> *"Because of the covenant which ye have made ye shall be called the **children of Christ**, his sons, and his daughters; for behold, this day he hath **spiritually begotten you**; for ye say that your hearts are changed through faith on his name; therefore, ye are born of him and have become his sons and his daughters."*
> — [Mosiah 5:7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/5?lang=eng&id=p7#p7)

The Lord said to the brother of Jared:

> *"Behold, I am Jesus Christ. … I am the Father and the Son. In me shall all mankind have life, and that eternally, even they who shall believe on my name; and **they shall become my sons and my daughters**."*
> — [Ether 3:14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/ether/3?lang=eng&id=p14#p14)

D&C 76:24 offers the definitive formulation of this process — the inhabitants of the worlds *"are begotten sons and daughters unto God"* — meaning *through Christ*, by the new birth, they become children of God.

D&C 93:21–22 confirms Christ's own experience of fathering the faithful: *"I am the Firstborn; And all those who are begotten through me are partakers of the glory of the same."*

Abinadi's great discourse in [Mosiah 15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng) wrestles with this directly. When he says Christ is "both the Father and the Son" (v. 3), he is describing how Christ, because He *subjects the flesh to the will of the Father* (v. 2), functions in both capacities — He is the Son because He took a mortal body, and the Father because in that body He did the Father's work and begat spiritual children through His atonement. Mosiah 15:10–13 makes the spiritual fatherhood explicit: Christ's "seed" are those "whosoever has heard the words of the prophets … and believed that the Lord would redeem his people."

The 1916 exposition: *"If it be proper to speak of those who accept and abide in the Gospel as Christ's sons and daughters — and upon this matter the scriptures are explicit and cannot be gainsaid nor denied — it is consistently proper to speak of Jesus Christ as the Father of the righteous, they having become His children and He having been made their Father through the second birth — the baptismal regeneration."* — [Chapter 40](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/teachings-joseph-f-smith/chapter-40?lang=eng)

**Important distinction:** Christ is our spiritual Father through covenant — through the new birth. God the Father (Elohim) is the Father of our **spirits** — our literal spirit parent. These are not the same fatherhood. One is ontological (Elohim begat our spirits). The other is covenantal (Christ spiritually begets us through the gospel).

### 3. Father by Divine Investiture of Authority

This is the most frequent source of confusion in scripture reading. When Christ speaks in the first person *as though He is the Father*, He does so because the Father has placed His name upon the Son. Christ is authorized — *invested* — to speak and act in the Father's name.

The 1916 exposition: *"In all His dealings with the human family Jesus the Son has represented and yet represents Elohim His Father in power and authority. This is true of Christ in His preexistent, antemortal, or unembodied state, in the which He was known as Jehovah; also during His embodiment in the flesh; and during His labors as a disembodied spirit in the realm of the dead; and since that period in His resurrected state."* — [Chapter 40](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/teachings-joseph-f-smith/chapter-40?lang=eng)

This explains some of the most puzzling passages in scripture:

**3 Nephi 9:15** — Christ declares to the Nephites: *"I am Jesus Christ the Son of God. **I created the heavens and the earth**, and all things that in them are. I was with the Father from the beginning. **I am in the Father, and the Father in me; and in me hath the Father glorified his name.**"* He declares His identity as the Son, then speaks of *creating* (as the Father's executive) and having the Father's name glorified *in Him*. He speaks with the Father's authority, but He names the source of that authority.

**Moses 1** — In the opening vision, the voice introducing itself to Moses says: *"I, the Almighty, have chosen thee"* (v. 25) and speaks of creating all things, using language that sounds like the Father. But the Father also says: *"mine Only Begotten … is and shall be the Savior"* (v. 6) and *"by the Son I created them"* (v. 33). The context shifts between the Father speaking directly and the Son speaking by investiture. The key hermeneutic tool is watching for clues — when the speaker says "mine Only Begotten," it is the Father. When the speaker identifies Himself as the one who will come into the world and atone, it is the Son.

The word **investiture** itself illuminates this. Webster 1828 defines it as *"the act or ceremony of investing … a giving possession"* — from *invest*, to clothe, to put upon. The Father *clothed* the Son with His own authority and name. When Christ speaks "as the Father," He is wearing the Father's mantle. He is not pretending to be the Father. He is authorized to carry out the Father's work *in the Father's name*, and when He speaks in that capacity, His words *are* the Father's words.

## Part III: How to Read the Hard Passages

With the three senses established, here is a practical framework for reading passages where Christ seems to be called "Father":

| Passage | What It Says | Which Sense? |
|---------|-------------|--------------|
| [Alma 11:38–39](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/11?lang=eng&id=p38-p39#p38) | "He is the very Eternal Father of heaven and of earth" | **Creator** — Father of creation |
| [2 Nephi 19:6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/19?lang=eng&id=p6#p6) / [Isaiah 9:6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/isa/9?lang=eng&id=p6#p6) | "The Everlasting Father" | **Creator** — eternal quality of His creative work |
| [Mosiah 15:1–5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p1-p5#p1) | "Being the Father and the Son" | **Spiritual rebirth + Creator** — He is Father of the faithful and Father of creation, Son because He took flesh |
| [Mosiah 5:7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/5?lang=eng&id=p7#p7) | "He hath spiritually begotten you" | **Spiritual rebirth** — covenant children of Christ |
| [Ether 3:14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/ether/3?lang=eng&id=p14#p14) | "I am the Father and the Son … they shall become my sons and my daughters" | **Spiritual rebirth + Creator** |
| [3 Nephi 9:15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/9?lang=eng&id=p15#p15) | "I created the heavens and the earth … I am in the Father, and the Father in me" | **Creator + Divine investiture** |
| [D&C 93:3–4](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93?lang=eng&id=p3-p4#p3) | "The Father because he gave me of his fulness" | **Divine investiture** — Christ received the Father's fulness |
| [Moses 1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/1?lang=eng) (shifting voice) | "I, the Almighty" / "mine Only Begotten" | **Divine investiture** — Father and Son speaking, sometimes interchangeably |
| [John 14:9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/14?lang=eng&id=p9#p9) | "He that hath seen me hath seen the Father" | **Oneness of character** — to know the Son is to know the Father |

Notice that Alma 11:44 — in the same chapter where Amulek calls Christ "the very Eternal Father" — draws the distinction back out: *"arraigned before the bar of **Christ the Son, and God the Father**, and the Holy Spirit, which is one Eternal God."* Three distinct personages. One God. The "one Eternal God" language refers to their unity in purpose and glory, not a Trinity of being.

## Part IV: Webster 1828 — Words That Matter

**FATHER** (1828): "One who has begotten a child" but also "one who, or that which, **gives origin**; an originator; a producer, **author**, or contriver" and "The Supreme Being and Creator; God; in theology, the first person in the Trinity."

Two layers. The Father is literally the parent of our spirits. But "father" also means originator and author. When we call Christ "Father" in the creative sense, we're using this secondary meaning — He originated the created world. When we speak of God the Father, we use *both* — He is the literal parent of spirits AND the author/originator of the plan.

**AUTHOR** (1828): "The **beginner**, former, or **first mover** of anything; hence, the efficient cause of a thing; **a creator**; an originator." The plan of salvation has an *author* — a first mover, a beginner. That is Elohim. Christ is the Redeemer within that plan, not its author.

**DESIGN** (1828): "To create or produce, as a work of art; to **form a plan or scheme of**; to form in idea; to **invent**; to project; to **lay out in the mind**." The Father *designed* the plan — formed it in idea, laid it out in mind. Christ *carried out* that design. Conflating these verbs conflates their roles.

**INVESTITURE** (1828): "The act or ceremony of investing … a **giving possession**." From *invest*: to clothe, to put upon. The Father clothed the Son with authority and name. Divine investiture is the Father dressing the Son in His own power to act.

**SON** (1828): "A male child; the male issue, or offspring, of a parent" and "Jesus Christ, the Savior; — called the Son of God, and the Son of man." The word inherently implies derivation *from* another. The Son proceeds from the Father — in spirit birth, in authority, in mission. The Son is never the source of Himself.

## Part V: What This Means for Our Documents

The specific conflation that prompted this study — *"the Person who designed it, enacted it, suffered for it"* — collapses the Father's authorship into the Son's execution. Here is what precision sounds like:

**Before:** *"It is a relationship with the Person who designed it, enacted it, suffered for it, and stands at every transition point within it."*

**After (one possibility):** *"It is a relationship — with the Father who designed the plan, and with the Son who enacted it, suffered for it, and stands at every transition point within it."*

Or perhaps: *"It is a relationship with the Beings who conceived it and completed it — the Father who authored the plan, and the Son who carries it out, who suffered for it, and who stands at every transition point within it."*

The plan of salvation is a collaboration between Father and Son. The North Star for us *is* Christ — He is the way, the truth, and the life, and no one comes to the Father except through Him ([John 14:6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/14?lang=eng&id=p6#p6)). But Christ Himself is pointing *to the Father*. His entire mission is to bring us *home* to the Father. Even the Intercessory Prayer is Christ praying to the Father on our behalf:

> *"Father, I will that they also, whom thou hast given me, be with me where I am; that they may behold **my** glory, which **thou** hast given me: for thou lovedst me before the foundation of the world."*
> — [John 17:24](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/17?lang=eng&id=p24#p24)

Christ does not hoard glory. He received it from the Father. He shares it with us. And the entire arc bends back to the Father's house — the many mansions ([John 14:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/14?lang=eng&id=p2#p2)) where the Father waits.

## Becoming

This isn't just about getting the theology right in a study document. It's about how we worship.

**When I pray:** I already pray to the Father, in the name of the Son, through the Spirit. But do I understand *why*? Not as formula but as relationship — I speak to the Being who designed my existence and loves me as His literal child. I close in the name of the Being who made it possible for that prayer to reach the Father at all.

**When I read scripture:** The next time I encounter a passage where Christ speaks "as the Father," I'll slow down and ask: Is this divine investiture? Is this Christ as Creator? Is this spiritual fatherhood? The answer changes what the verse is teaching me.

**When I teach:** The plan of salvation is not Christ's plan. It is the Father's plan, carried out by Christ. When I teach about the council in heaven, I should be clear: the Father presided, the Father asked "Whom shall I send?", the Son volunteered, and the Father chose Him. The design is the Father's. The execution — including the most painful execution in all of history — is the Son's.

**When I think about my own fatherhood:** There is something here about what it means to be a father. The Father designed, the Son executed. But the Father didn't just hand off a blueprint and walk away. He *watched*. He was present at the baptism: "This is my Beloved Son." He was present at the Transfiguration: "This is my Beloved Son; hear him." He was present when Christ prayed in Gethsemane — close enough that an angel was sent to strengthen. He introduced the Son to the Nephites personally. He introduced the Son to Joseph Smith personally. The Father is always present, always watching, always introducing, always saying *"Listen to Him."* That is a pattern for fatherhood.

**Questions to sit with:**
- Do I understand the difference between being a child of God (spirit child of Elohim) and being born again as a child of Christ (covenant rebirth)? Both are real. Both matter. They answer different questions about identity.
- When I read "I am the Father and the Son" in the Book of Mormon, do I know *which* of the three senses is operating? Or do I just gloss over it?
- Can I articulate — to a friend, to a child, to a missionary companion — why Latter-day Saints don't believe in the Trinity without oversimplifying? Two separate Beings. One perfectly united in purpose and love. The Son doing the Father's will, not His own. The Father's plan, not the Son's plan. But the Son's sacrifice, freely given.

---

## North Star Correction

The North Star is still Christ. Every study leads back to Him. But Christ Himself leads back to the Father. The plan is the Father's. The path home is through the Son. And the Son never lets us forget where the path ends — not at Him, but at the Father who sent Him.

> *"I am the way, the truth, and the life: no man cometh **unto the Father**, but by me."*
> — [John 14:6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/14?lang=eng&id=p6#p6)

Christ is not the destination. He is the way *to* the destination. The destination is the Father's presence. And Christ — the Firstborn, the Redeemer, the Creator, our covenant Father through rebirth — gladly points us there, because that's where He wants us to be. That's where the Father wants us to be. That's what the plan was *designed* for.

### Webster 1828
- **Father**: originator, author, begetter — both literal parent and figurative source
- **Author**: beginner, first mover, efficient cause — the one who starts something
- **Design**: to form a plan or scheme, to lay out in the mind — to conceive before executing
- **Investiture**: the act of investing, giving possession — to clothe with authority
- **Son**: offspring, one who proceeds from another — inherently derived, not self-originating
