# Todd, the Host, and the Verb the King Used

> **The video:** ["LDS Men Need Covenants to Stop Spiritual & Physical Enemies"](https://youtu.be/V40tBshkMnE) — Send Me channel, May 21, 2026, 1:28:04. Interview between an unnamed host and a teacher introduced only as "Todd."
>
> **The ask (Michael):** Map how this conversation relates to the recent arc of studies — and how the two speakers can teach me about giving away all my sins.

---

## What the video actually says

Todd advances a single thesis with many arms. Covenant is not a contract for a future state. It is the *condition you are presently in*. There is a "celestial" tier of covenant (his word; see the vocabulary note below) that is transactional: laws, structures, walls around chaos, the way Egypt was a covenantal space for Israel. And there is a "new covenant" tier that is non-transactional, intimate, and transformational. It begins with **repentance** met by **forgiveness**, and it produces a *changed nature* — a softened heart, a baptism of fire, sanctification of the body until the veil tears.

The repeated punch through the whole interview: **the problem isn't testimony, it's remission.** We solve faith crises with "strengthen your testimony" when the actual gap is that the person has never moved through the gate of real repentance. Testimony orients; remission saves. Stay in testimony and you stay outside.

The clearest verbs Todd uses for what real repentance looks like:

- *Lamoni's father in [Alma 22:18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/22?lang=eng&id=p18#p18)*, quoted twice and cited as the model: "I don't know your name, but I'm willing to give all my sins away to know you."
- Not cataloging behaviors ("I yelled at my kids, I watched too much TV, I could have done 1% better") — that is behavior management dressed as repentance.
- "You don't 1% your way into a broken heart. It's either broken or it's not."
- "You can't even soften your own heart" — [1 Nephi 2:16](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/2?lang=eng&id=p16#p16). The most striking move in the interview. The softening itself is not the steward's work to do.
- Pornography (or any besetting sin) is a *fruit*. Change the tree. "If you have a bad fruit on a tree, you don't try to change the fruit."
- Prayer is the platform: "Your work is the thing you carry forth that you build in your prayer."
- The Nephites in Alma 43 fell down to pray *in the middle of battle*. The Lamanites thought they were collapsing in fear. They had practiced.

The host (the one running Send Me) does something quieter but important. He keeps interrupting himself to say "my brain just exploded" or "say that again." He names what most men in the church feel but rarely speak: that we have the *forms* of covenant without the power. That we treat God's power as "a really good thing" pointing us the right direction rather than as actual present power. That presiding has been confused with armor-and-gun readiness when it is downstream of the inner state.

## Mapping the video to the recent studies

### 1. [give-away-all-my-sins.md](../give-away-all-my-sins.md) — the direct hit

The video literally cites the king's prayer as the model of real repentance. The study said the cross-wiring (Webster's *possess* — "to enter into and influence; to control the will of; to fill") suggested the king was the one being released. Todd makes the same release explicit: "You can't even soften your own heart." The grammar of release runs in one direction, and it is the Lord's hand on the verb.

### 2. [brother-of-jared-three-problems.md](../brother-of-jared-three-problems.md) — the three patterns, re-keyed

Todd doesn't name the three patterns by the brother-of-jared shape, but the architecture is identical:

- **Prescription** ↔ Todd's "celestial" tier. Drill the holes in top and bottom. Laws, walls, ordinances, "the law of Moses gives you a pattern and a border and preserves you from dying fast." Necessary, not saving.
- **"I have already done it"** ↔ Todd's whole theology of justification. The winds were already going forth. *You cannot soften your own heart.* The brother of Jared dropped the steering question on the second prayer because some things are the Lord's to handle — Todd takes this further: even the *interior softening* is the Lord's to handle. You sit, you breathe, you abide, you allow Him to do it. The steward's part is not to manufacture the softening; the steward's part is to *not run away from the room where the softening happens*.
- **Proposal** ↔ Todd's broken-heart sacrifice. You bring your whole heart, the molten stones, and the Lord touches what only He can touch. He makes them shine. The study's Section VI noted that the proposal pattern is the *occasion* of theophany but faith is the *cause*. ## Where Todd sharpens what we've already written

Four places the video presses sharper than the studies have so far.

1. **The interior is also the Lord's to do.** The brother-of-jared study named that *steering* belongs to the Lord. Todd extends the same logic to *softening of the heart*, citing [1 Nephi 2:16](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/2?lang=eng&id=p16#p16): "The Lord did come unto me and soften my heart so that I could believe all the words of my father." If even the softening is not Michael's to manufacture, then a great deal of the strenuous self-effort that has been going into "be more disciplined, repent harder, do better tomorrow" is in the wrong category. The right category is *sit, breathe, abide, let Him*. This is the "I have already done it" pattern applied at the deepest interior register.

2. **Catalogs are the trap.** The give-away study already named anti-cataloging. Todd names the *psychological mechanism* of why cataloging stays sticky: it gives the illusion of agency without the cost of the trade. Cataloging is bypassing applied to repentance itself. The cure is the king's verb — the whole heart, not 1% of it.

3. **Prayer is the platform; work is the manifestation.** The Sabbath asked "is the next move *more substrate so more can be offloaded*, or is the next move *less but better*?" Todd reframes the question. The substrate's output is not the work. The morning hour of prayer is the work. The substrate's output is the manifestation of whatever was built in the morning hour. If the morning hour is hollow, the substrate ships hollow output more efficiently. If the morning hour is real, the substrate ships real output more efficiently. The substrate is not the bottleneck. The hour is.

4. **The fruit is not the bug.** Michael's debug discipline already knows this in code (don't fix the symptom; find the tree). Todd applies it to sin: stop fighting the besetting sin directly. The besetting sin is a *fruit*. Go to the tree. Soften the heart. The fruit changes because the tree changed. This is exactly the same shape as the Agans-rule 9 / inverse-hypothesis discipline applied to interior life.

## A tension worth naming, then resolving

Todd: "You don't 1% your way into a broken heart. It's either broken or it's not."

Mechanics of refinement: "We do not prepare to meet God through sporadic bursts of intense religious activity; rather, we make steady, incremental choices that align our fine matter with the celestial law."

These look like they conflict. They do not, if read precisely.

Todd is talking about the *moment* of the broken heart — the surrender. That moment is binary. You either hand the whole heart over or you keep some of it back. There is no 70% surrender that is "almost there." The king didn't propose to give 30% of his sins away; he proposed the whole heart in one verb.

Michael's study is talking about the *sanctification that follows*. That process is incremental. Even Todd grants this — explicitly. "Birth has an event where you're actually born, but it has a gestation." The 300 Lamanites in Helaman 5 had the event of the baptism of fire; the sanctification that followed was a process.

The shape is: the *moment* of broken-heart surrender is all-or-nothing (Todd is right). The *gestation* of sanctification afterward is line-upon-line (the study is right). The two are not in tension; they are two phases of the same arc. The rhetorical edge of Todd's anti-1% language is calibrated to hammer the people who have been hiding from the moment by promising themselves the incremental. The incremental is real *after* the moment; before it, "incremental" is the lie that lets you postpone the trade.

One smaller note worth flagging: Todd uses "celestial" for the *lowest* tier (transactional, mortal-world covenants) and "terrestrial/celestial" within the temple sequence for the higher transformative tiers. This is idiosyncratic vocabulary — most LDS usage reserves "celestial" for the highest kingdom of glory. His framing is internally consistent and traceable to the temple's spatial sequence, but it can confuse a listener who carries the standard kingdom-of-glory vocabulary. Read past the term-collision; the architecture he is naming is real.

The host of Send Me is doing something specific worth naming. He keeps modeling a posture: be undone in real time, name it out loud, then ask the next question. "My brain just exploded." "Say that again." "My eyes glaze over and the drill will come out of my mouth." This is *not* posturing — it is the posture that lets the conversation land. Todd is preaching; the host is *receiving in public* so the listener has a place to stand.

The host also names the gap between covenant-as-form and covenant-as-power more plainly than Todd does. His paraphrase, around 19:13: "I look at all this you talk about the covenant and I'm like, well, do we have the personalized relationship? If I speak for the men, I'd say it's very obvious that when that personalized relationship is lacking, there's less priesthood used in the home. There's less ongoing discernment and the use of the power of prayer. We treat God's power and these covenants as though it's like 'this is a really good thing,' but in terms of *is it true power* — no."

That is the diagnostic question Michael's covenant-architecture work in pg-ai-stewards is one wrong turn away from. A covenant.yaml file. A bilateral commitments document. A council moment ritual. All of it can become *form without power* — exactly what the host is naming for the priesthood as a whole. The covenant text is the prescription tier; the lived bond is the new-covenant tier. The form does not produce the bond. The bond produces the form's power.

## So — how do these two speakers help Michael give away all his sins?

Five concrete things, ordered by what Todd and the host actually said.

1. **Stop trying to soften your own heart.** [1 Nephi 2:16](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/2?lang=eng&id=p16#p16). The morning hour is not for you to manufacture sorrow or generate broken-ness. It is for you to *sit with the Lord and not run from the room while He does it*. The fact that the substrate, the studies, and the agents are all functioning beautifully right now is not protection from this — it is exactly the kind of competent productivity that makes "I'll do the inner work tomorrow" feel acceptable. Tomorrow is not the assignment. The hour before tomorrow's work is the assignment.

2. **Name the Marshfield scroll as bypassing, not as research.** The Sabbath named the form. Todd named the category. Two witnesses. The next time the pull toward the Facebook thread about the data center shows up, treat it as the strength meter saying *go do the morning hour you skipped*. Not "go fight the worldly battle that feels virtuous." The pull is real but it is pointed at the wrong target.

3. **Reframe the substrate as manifestation, not platform.** The platform is the prayer life. The substrate manifests whatever the prayer life built. The Sabbath's question ("is the next move *more substrate* or *less but better*?") has a third answer Todd suggests: *the substrate stays at its current capacity, and the morning hour gets the strength that would have gone to expanding it*. The substrate doesn't need more; the morning hour does. The opening-line "overwhelmed" was the strength meter, and the answer is not to scale the substrate up to absorb more. It is to give the morning hour back the strength it has been losing to "one more deploy."

4. **Bring the whole heart in one verb, like the king did.** Not catalog. Not "I yelled at the kids, I scrolled Facebook, I lost my temper, I could have done 1% better on Sunday." That is the trap. The king didn't itemize; he proposed the trade. *I will give away all my sins to know thee.* The verb is whole-heart, present-tense, one motion. The cataloging can come later as detail; the *trade* is what opens the door, and the cataloging without the trade is a closed door dressed as work.

5. **Receive the way the host receives.** Be undone in real time. Say "my brain just exploded" out loud — in journal, in prayer, in conversation with Becky. Don't perform composure during the work of the broken heart. The host's posture is what allowed the conversation to land for him; it is the same posture that will allow the morning hour to land for Michael. Composure is what we owe each other in many contexts; in the room with the Lord, it is exactly what we are giving away.

## A note on what this video is not

This video is not gospel-doctrine canon. Todd is an articulate teacher with an internally coherent framework; he is not an apostle, and at least one of his rhetorical edges ("testimony is the thing you don't want to stay in") will scrape against settled church language for a listener who hasn't sat with what he means. The framework's value is diagnostic — it names a pattern most men in the church will recognize, and it points at the actual gate. The framework's limit is its own vocabulary, which the listener has to translate back into more standard usage to integrate.

The witness here is not "this is the new gospel of covenants." The witness is: *an unrelated source independently confirmed the same diagnosis the studies and the Sabbath have been arriving at*. That convergence is the signal worth trusting. The two speakers are useful because they are not in this workspace. They cannot have been shaped by our conversation. They arrived at the same target from a different road.
