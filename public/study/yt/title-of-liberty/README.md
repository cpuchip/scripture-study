## What We Learned From This Video

The video introduced the concept of "stripling warrior boot camps" — structured programs to raise covenant youth with Book of Mormon character. The evaluation ([evaluation.md](evaluation.md)) assessed the video at 7/10: strong scriptural foundation and genuine passion, but some claims went beyond what the text supports.

What the video *sparked* was more valuable than what it *said*. The idea of a family discipleship program grounded in the Book of Mormon led to:

1. **Deep research** into youth development programs (BSA, Pathfinders, Royal Rangers, Awana, Children & Youth)
2. **An initial design** modeled after BSA with ranks, merit badges, and ceremonies (v1 — heavy, structured)
3. **A reality check** from family conversation about weight, autonomy, and motivation
4. **Serious research** into motivation psychology (Self-Determination Theory, overjustification effect, gamification literature)
5. **Charlotte Mason alignment** — discovering that her "atmosphere, discipline, life" framework maps perfectly onto what we actually want
6. **A design pivot** toward a lightweight, autonomy-supportive system that trusts the person and the material

The project continues at [becoming/title-of-liberty/](../../../becoming/title-of-liberty/README.md).
