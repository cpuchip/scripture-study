# Title of Liberty — Guidance & Open Questions

*Questions that need answers before the program can move forward. These emerged during initial design (March 2025) and from real family conversation.*

*Answers recorded March 17, 2026.*

---

## Meta-Observation: Do Q1–Q6 Even Matter?

The motivation research (see [motivation-systems.md](motivation-systems.md)) shifted the entire design direction from structured/prescribed toward open/autonomy-supportive. Several of the original design questions were framed around a system that may no longer exist in its original form:

- **Q1 (late starters)** was about rank sequence. If ranks dissolve into recognized milestones rather than prescribed checkpoints, "starting late" becomes meaningless — you start where you are.
- **Q4 (pillar naming)** was about managing two parallel naming systems. If the system is open and self-directed, the pillar names become a lens for reflection, not a category system to manage.
- **Q6 (physical tokens)** was about BSA-style achievement markers. If the system moves away from badge earning, the nature of tokens changes — they become family artifacts, not rank insignia.

The questions that **still matter regardless of design direction**: Q2 (family involvement), Q3 (project location), Q5 (auth infrastructure), and all of Q7–Q10. These are about logistics, relationships, and philosophy — not system mechanics.

## From Initial Design Session

### Q1: Late Starters — Accelerated Track?

Should a 10-year-old who enters at Rank 1, 3rd Degree have an option to combine Ranks 1–2 into a condensed "fast start" track? Or does everyone walk the full path regardless of age? The concern: a teenager doing Rank 1 work that feels too basic. The counter-concern: skipping steps undermines the sequential character-building.

**Answer:** They should be able to start where they are based on life experience. It should be as easy as possible to see where you fit and step in. *(Note: in a more autonomy-supportive design, this question largely dissolves — there's no prescribed sequence to be "late" to.)*

**Status:** Answered — design should make entry frictionless at any point

---

### Q2: Adult Participation

Do parents participate in the ranks alongside their kids, or do they lead from above? If parents participate, do we need a 5th Degree (adults)? There's something powerful about a dad earning Rank 3 alongside his 11-year-old. But also something important about the parent being the guide, not the co-student.

**Answer:** The whole family should be involved. *(This aligns with Charlotte Mason's "atmosphere" — the parents' own practice IS 1/3 of the education. Parents doing the work alongside the kids models intrinsic motivation.)*

**Status:** Answered — parents participate, not just supervise

---

### Q3: Project Location

Where should this project live long-term? Currently at `study/yt/title-of-liberty/` because it grew from a YT video evaluation. Options:
- Keep it here (origin matters)
- Move to `becoming/title-of-liberty/` (since it integrates with the Becoming app)
- Move to top-level `title-of-liberty/` (it's its own thing)

**Answer:** Move to `./becoming/title-of-liberty/`. Leave a README in the YouTube study location that points to the new home and describes what we learned from the video.

**Status:** Answered — moved to `becoming/title-of-liberty/`

---

### Q4: Pillar Naming — Replace or Parallel?

The Becoming app has default pillars: Spiritual 🙏, Social 🤝, Intellectual 📚, Physical 💪. Title of Liberty has: Faith & Covenant ⚔️, Liberty & Service 🛡️, Knowledge & Skill 🏗️, Strength & Discipline 💪.

Do the Title of Liberty pillars *replace* the defaults when the program is active? Or do they run in parallel? The Title of Liberty names carry more weight and specificity, but confusion between two pillar systems is a risk.

**Answer:** Parallel the names. They are the same pillars — the Title of Liberty names just reflect them through a Book of Mormon lens. No replacement needed, just alignment.

**Status:** Answered — same pillars, different framing

---

### Q5: webeco.me & Auth Priority

The webeco.me community layer for Title of Liberty requires authentication (Plan 09, currently deferred). Does this project change the priority of auth? Or do we build the personal-tracker side first (ibeco.me, no auth needed) and defer the community layer?

**Answer:** Auth is probably already set up via Google — both ibeco.me and webeco.me point to the right place and use the same Google auth. Need to verify this is working.

**Status:** Answered — auth may already exist, verify before building more

---

### Q6: Physical Tokens

BSA has ranks on a sash, patches on a uniform, an Eagle medal. Children & Youth has a medallion. Physical tokens create a sense of tangible achievement. Options:
- Digital-only (badges in the app)
- Physical tokens (patches, coins, certificates)
- Both (app tracking + physical ceremony items)
- Family-defined (let each family choose their tradition)

**Answer:** Digital tokens for now. Real physical tokens in the future — probably commissioned through Fiverr. *(Note: if the system shifts from badge-earning to character-recognition, tokens become commemorative rather than achievement-based — more "family heirloom" than "merit badge.")*

**Status:** Answered — digital now, physical later via commission

## From Family Conversation (March 15, 2026)

### Q7: Wife's Concern — System Weight vs. Homeschool Load

Michael's wife didn't get excited about the system. She's already managing homeschool for 5 kids. This feels like adding another heavy curriculum on top. If only one parent is driving it, the program fails — it's supposed to be a *family* system.

**Key question:** How do we make this lightweight enough that a busy homeschool mom sees it as *helping carry the load* instead of *adding to it*? Could parts of Title of Liberty *replace* existing homeschool elements rather than stacking on top?

**Answer:** Don't know yet. This will take many iterations. But the direction is clear: move toward lightweight, move toward what the research says. This is the hardest question and we should expect to circle back to it repeatedly.

**Status:** Active — direction set, specifics need many iterations

---

### Q8: Wife's Concern — Open-Ended vs. Structured

The Church's Children & Youth program is intentionally open-ended to allow kids creative freedom in setting their own goals. Title of Liberty, as currently designed, is highly structured (specific badge requirements, specific rank criteria). 

**The tension:** Structure provides clarity and direction. Open-endedness enables ownership and creativity. The BSA model worked partly because the *kid* chose which merit badges to pursue. But the requirements within each badge were specific.

**Key question:** How much of the system should be "here's what you do" vs. "here's the space, you decide what to do in it"? Can we preserve the rank/badge framework while making the *content* of each badge more self-directed?

**Answer:** Most of it should be open-ended. Users pick their own path and options to fit their needs and desires. Whether ranks/badges even make sense is still an open question worth noodling on.

**Status:** Active — leaning strongly toward open-ended; badge/rank system is under reconsideration

---

### Q9: Wife's Concern — External vs. Internal Motivation

The badge/rank/ceremony system is fundamentally extrinsic motivation (visible rewards for achievement). The family goal is to raise internally motivated kids who do the right thing because it *is* the right thing, not because they earn a badge.

**The tension:** Research on motivation shows extrinsic rewards can undermine intrinsic motivation (overjustification effect). But research also shows that structured programs *can* scaffold intrinsic motivation when designed well. The key variables are autonomy, competence, and relatedness (Self-Determination Theory).

**Key question:** How do we design a badge/rank system that *scaffolds* intrinsic motivation instead of replacing it? What does the research say? What does the gospel say?

**Answer:** Maybe a choose-your-own-adventure model — let kids pick their learning and goals and support them in that. Not sure that's the right answer, but it's the direction. The gospel answer is clear: [D&C 58:26–29](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/58?lang=eng&id=p26-p29#p26) says agents acting of their own free will, not compelled in all things. See [motivation-systems.md](motivation-systems.md).

**Status:** Active — research done, gospel framework clear, design still forming

---

### Q10: Only Three Days Old

This entire concept is 3 days old. It emerged from a YouTube video evaluation and grew fast. The design energy is high but untested. No one in the family has tried any piece of it.

**Key question:** What would a minimum viable pilot look like? Something small enough to try for 2 weeks that doesn't require buy-in for the whole system?

**Answer:** Noodle on this for a few weeks. Continue pushing the theory along until we're happy with it, then implement. No rush to pilot — get the thinking right first.

**Status:** Active — theory-refining phase, not implementation phase
