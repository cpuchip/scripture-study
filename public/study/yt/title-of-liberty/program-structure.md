# Title of Liberty — Program Structure

Detailed rank requirements by degree. Each rank builds on the previous one. Degrees scale the difficulty to the participant's age and capability.

---

### Advancement Requirements Summary

To advance from one rank to the next, a participant must:

1. **Complete all required badges** for their current rank at their degree level
2. **Earn the minimum number of elective badges** for their rank (see [merit-badges.md](merit-badges.md))
3. **Demonstrate the rank's core character trait** — witnessed and affirmed by family council
4. **Pass a Family Council review** — the council (all family members) discusses whether the candidate has genuinely lived the rank's values
5. **Write (or tell) a personal reflection** on what they learned during this rank

Character demonstration is not a checklist. It's a judgment call made by the family. A child who completes every badge but doesn't show trustworthiness hasn't earned Warrior of Helaman. This keeps the program honest.

## Rank 1: Apprentice of Lehi 🌱

**Theme:** Willingness to begin  
**Scripture:** "I will go and do the things which the Lord hath commanded" — [1 Nephi 3:7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/3?lang=eng&id=p7#p7)  
**Character trait:** Willingness. Lehi left everything because the Lord told him to. He didn't have a plan. He had a promise.  
**Book of Mormon reading:** 1 Nephi 1–7

### Required Badges

| Badge | 1st Degree (4–6) | 2nd Degree (7–9) | 3rd Degree (10–12) | 4th Degree (13+) |
|-------|------------------|------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| **Scripture Mastery** | Memorize 1 scripture (with help) | Memorize 2 scriptures | Memorize 3 scriptures | Memorize 5 scriptures |
| **Book of Mormon Reading** | Be read to: 1 Nephi 1–7 | Read aloud together: 1 Ne 1–7. Independently read 2+ chapters | Read 1 Ne 1–7 independently. Journal 3 things learned | Read 1 Ne 1–7 independently. Write a 1-page summary of Lehi's choice |
| **Physical Challenge** | Complete 3 active play sessions (hiking, sport, etc.) | Run/walk 1 mile. 10 push-ups | Run 1 mile under a time goal. 20 push-ups | Run 1.5 miles. 30 push-ups. Hold plank 60 sec |
| **Title of Liberty** | Draw a picture of what you stand for | Write 3 sentences about what you stand for | Write a paragraph declaration | Write a full personal declaration (½ page+) |

### Elective Badges Required: 2 (from at least 2 pillars)

### Duration Estimate: 1–3 months

## Rank 2: Scout of Nephi 🏹

**Theme:** Doing hard things  
**Scripture:** "I will go and do the things which the Lord hath commanded, for I know that the Lord giveth no commandments unto the children of men, save he shall prepare a way for them that they may accomplish the thing which he commandeth them." — [1 Nephi 3:7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/3?lang=eng&id=p7#p7)  
**Character trait:** Obedience before understanding. Nephi acted when it didn't make sense. He went back for the plates. He built a ship.  
**Book of Mormon reading:** 1 Nephi 1–22

### Required Badges

| Badge | 1st Degree (4–6) | 2nd Degree (7–9) | 3rd Degree (10–12) | 4th Degree (13+) |
|-------|------------------|------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| **Scripture Mastery** | Memorize 2 scriptures (with help). Review Rank 1 scriptures | Memorize 3 new scriptures. Pass review of all prior | Memorize 5 new scriptures. Pass review of all prior | Memorize 7 new scriptures. Pass review of all prior |
| **Book of Mormon Reading** | Be read to: 1 Ne 8–22 (key chapters) | Read 1 Nephi 1–22 (full book). Journal 5 things | Read 1 Ne 1–22 in full. Write about Nephi's hardest moments | Read 1 Ne 1–22. Write essay: "What does Nephi teach about doing hard things?" |
| **Service Project** | Help with 3 acts of kindness (with family) | Plan and do 1 service activity for someone outside the family | Plan and execute a service project (4+ hours) | Plan, lead, and document a service project (8+ hours) |
| **Physical Challenge** | Complete 5 active sessions | Run/walk 2 miles. Fitness test improvement over Rank 1 | Run 2 miles. 30 push-ups. 50 sit-ups | Run 2 miles under target. 40 push-ups. Hold plank 90 sec. Complete a hard hike (5+ mi) |
| **Practical Skill** | Learn 1 new thing (tie shoes, set table, help cook) | Learn to cook 1 simple meal. OR learn a basic outdoor skill | Cook a full meal. OR demonstrate a certified skill (first aid, etc.) | Cook 3 different meals. AND learn 1 trade skill (basic woodworking, coding, gardening, etc.) |
| **Hard Thing Journal** | Tell about a time you did something hard | Write about 2 times you did something hard this rank | Journal weekly about challenges faced and overcome | Journal weekly. Write a final reflection: "My hardest thing and what I learned" |

### Elective Badges Required: 4 (from at least 3 pillars)

### Duration Estimate: 3–6 months

## Rank 3: Warrior of Helaman ⚔️

**Theme:** Trustworthy in all things  
**Scripture:** "They were men who were true at all times in whatsoever thing they were entrusted. Yea, they were men of truth and soberness, for they had been taught to keep the commandments of God and to walk uprightly before him." — [Alma 53:20–21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/53?lang=eng&id=p20-p21#p20)  
**Character trait:** Reliability. The stripling warriors' defining trait wasn't strength — it was that they could be trusted.  
**Book of Mormon reading:** Alma 53, 56–58. Plus Mosiah 18 (covenant of Alma at Waters of Mormon).

### Required Badges

| Badge | 1st Degree (4–6) | 2nd Degree (7–9) | 3rd Degree (10–12) | 4th Degree (13+) |
|-------|------------------|------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| **Scripture Mastery** | Memorize 3 total (review all prior) | Memorize 5 total. Begin learning Alma 53:20–21 | Memorize Alma 53:20–21 + 5 new. Pass all prior | Memorize Alma 53:20–21 + 7 new. Pass all prior. Study Hebrew/Greek of 1 word |
| **Book of Mormon Reading** | Be read to: Alma 53, 56 (key passages) | Read Alma 53, 56–58. Journal about the mothers' faith | Read Alma 53, 56–58, Mosiah 18. Write about: "What covenant did the stripling warriors keep?" | Read Alma 53, 56–58, Mosiah 18. Essay: "What does it mean to be true at all times?" |
| **Service Project** | Participate in family service (5 acts) | Plan and execute 1 project (4+ hours) | Lead a service project for the community (8+ hours) | Lead a sustained service effort (12+ hours, multi-day) |
| **Physical Challenge** | Complete 7 active sessions in 2 weeks | Run 2 miles. Beat Rank 2 fitness scores. Complete a family hike | Run 3 miles. Complete a 30-day fitness challenge. Family hike (8+ mi) | Run 3 miles under target. 30-day challenge in 2 fitness areas. Complete an endurance event |
| **Practical Skill** | Learn 2 new skills WITH a parent | Demonstrate 2 skills independently (cooking, cleaning, yard work, etc.) | Learn a skilled craft or practical trade (cooking series, coding, music, woodwork) | Demonstrate proficiency in a skilled area. Produce something of value |
| **Teaching** | Tell a scripture story to the family | Teach a 5-minute lesson from the Book of Mormon | Teach a 15-minute family lesson with scripture references | Teach a 20-minute lesson. Use footnotes and cross-references |
| **30-Day Challenge** | Do 1 good thing every day for 2 weeks (with chart) | Complete a 30-day streak in 1 pillar (prayer, exercise, reading, service) | Complete a 30-day streak in 2 pillars simultaneously | Complete a 30-day streak in all 4 pillars simultaneously |
| **Trustworthiness** | Complete 5 assignments without reminders | Complete 10 assignments/chores without reminders over 30 days | Complete all assigned responsibilities for 30 days without reminders. Zero missed | Same as 3rd Degree, for 60 days |

### Elective Badges Required: 6 (from all 4 pillars)

### Duration Estimate: 4–8 months

## Rank 4: Captain of Moroni 🗡️

**Theme:** Defending and leading  
**Scripture:** "Moroni was a strong and a mighty man; he was a man of a perfect understanding; yea, a man that did not delight in bloodshed; a man whose soul did joy in the liberty and the freedom of his country, and his brethren from bondage and slavery." — [Alma 48:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/48?lang=eng&id=p11#p11)  
**Character trait:** Prepared leadership. Moroni led because he had to, not because he wanted power. He fortified before the battle came. He delighted in doing good.  
**Book of Mormon reading:** Alma 43–62 (the war chapters).

### Required Badges

| Badge | 1st Degree (4–6) | 2nd Degree (7–9) | 3rd Degree (10–12) | 4th Degree (13+) |
|-------|------------------|------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| **Scripture Mastery** | Review all prior. Memorize 1 new | 8 total memorized. Begin Alma 48:11–13 | Memorize Alma 48:11–13 + 5 new. Study word origins of 2 words | Memorize 10+ scriptures total. Study 3+ word origins. Be able to teach from memory |
| **Book of Mormon Reading** | Be read to: Alma 46 (Title of Liberty), Alma 48 (Moroni's character) | Read Alma 43–50. Journal: "What makes a good leader?" | Read Alma 43–62. Write about Moroni's preparation strategy | Read Alma 43–62. Essay: "What kind of person do I want to be?" (Alma 48:11–17) |
| **Service Project** | 7 acts of service in 1 month | Lead a family service activity | Plan, lead, and execute a community project (15+ hrs) | Design, fund/resource, lead, and complete a major project (20+ hrs). Document it |
| **Physical Challenge** | Master 1 physical skill (bike, swim, etc.) | Run 3 miles. Sustained 60-day exercise habit | Run 4 miles. 60-day challenge. Complete a significant physical achievement | Run 5K race or equivalent. 60-day sustained fitness. Demonstrate strength/endurance gains over prior rank |
| **Practical Skill** | Learn 3 skills | Demonstrate 3 independent skills. Build or make 1 thing | Learn an advanced skill. Build a meaningful project | Demonstrate mastery-level skill in 1 area. Produce work of real value |
| **Teaching** | Lead a family prayer or song | Teach a 10-minute lesson. Help a younger sibling with a badge | Teach a 20-minute lesson with depth. Mentor a younger participant through 1 badge | Teach a 30-minute lesson using footnotes, cross-references, and original language. Mentor a younger participant through an entire rank |
| **Leadership** | Lead 1 family activity (game night, cleanup, etc.) | Plan and lead a family outing from start to finish | Plan and lead a sustained family project (multi-week) | Plan, lead, and complete a multi-person project that serves others. Demonstrate delegation, planning, and follow-through |
| **Study a Defender** | Learn about 1 person who helped others | Study a historical figure who defended liberty. Tell the family | Research a historical or modern figure who defended truth. Present a 10-minute report | Write a research report on a defender of liberty. Present 15+ min. Draw parallels to Book of Mormon |
| **60-Day Challenge** | Do 1 good thing daily for 30 days | 60-day streak in 1 pillar | 60-day streak in 3 pillars | 60-day streak in all 4 pillars |

### Elective Badges Required: 8 (from all 4 pillars, min 2 per pillar)

### Duration Estimate: 6–12 months

## Rank 5: Builder of Zion 👑

**Theme:** The full measure — building something that blesses others  
**Scripture:** "Finding there was greater happiness and peace and rest for me, I sought for the blessings of the fathers, and the right whereunto I should be ordained to administer the same" — [Abraham 1:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/abr/1?lang=eng&id=p2#p2)  
**Character trait:** The Josephite birthright. Receive blessings, then build a place where others can receive them too. Not just personal righteousness — but community-building righteousness.  
**Book of Mormon reading:** The entire Book of Mormon, cover to cover.

### Required Badges

| Badge | 1st Degree (4–6) | 2nd Degree (7–9) | 3rd Degree (10–12) | 4th Degree (13+) |
|-------|------------------|------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| **Scripture Mastery** | 5+ total memorized. Review all | 12+ total memorized across all pillars | 15+ memorized. Study word origins of 5+ words | 20+ memorized. Teach 5 scriptures from memory with context and cross-references |
| **Book of Mormon Reading** | Be read to: full abridgement (key stories from every book) | Read the Book of Mormon (parents supplement difficult sections) | Read the full Book of Mormon independently. Journal throughout | Read the full Book of Mormon. Write a testimony of the book. Teach a lesson from each major section |
| **Capstone Project** | Make something for someone (drawing, card, craft) | Plan and give a meaningful gift or service to someone in need | Execute a sustained project (30+ days) that serves the community | Design, plan, resource, execute, and document a major project (60+ days) that blesses others. Present to family council |
| **Physical Challenge** | Demonstrate physical growth from Rank 1 to now | Run 4 miles. Complete an endurance challenge (long hike, event) | Run 5 miles or equivalent. | **Leadership** | Help younger siblings with their challenges | Lead 2 family activities over the rank | Lead a multi-week project. | **Personal Declaration** | Draw and tell what you believe | Write a personal declaration of covenant identity (½ page) | Write a full declaration (1 page): who you are, what you stand for, what you will build | Write a comprehensive declaration (2+ pages). ### Elective Badges Required: 12 (from all 4 pillars, min 3 per pillar)

### Duration Estimate: 8–18 months

## Pacing Guidelines

### Realistic Multi-Year Timeline

The program is designed to take **3–5 years** from start (Rank 1) to completion (Rank 5) depending on age, degree, and pace. This is not a sprint — it's a discipleship lifestyle.

| Starting Age | Expected Completion Age | Notes |
|-------------|------------------------|-------|
| 4–6 (1st Degree) | 8–12 | Will progress through degrees as they age. May reach Rank 3–4 by age 10 |
| 7–9 (2nd Degree) | 11–14 | Core boot camp progression. 4–5 year journey to Builder of Zion |
| 10–12 (3rd Degree) | 14–17 | Deep engagement age. Can reach Builder of Zion before mission age |
| 13+ (4th Degree) | 16–18 | Accelerated but rigorous. Leadership-heavy. Powerful pre-mission preparation |

### Family Pacing

All family members work through ranks simultaneously but at their own degree level. A family might have:
- A 4-year-old at Rank 1, 1st Degree
- A 7-year-old at Rank 2, 2nd Degree
- A 10-year-old at Rank 3, 3rd Degree
- A 13-year-old at Rank 3, 4th Degree
- A 15-year-old at Rank 4, 4th Degree

Family Council discusses all participants' progress together. Older kids naturally help younger ones — this IS the leadership badge.

## Rank Advancement Ceremonies

Drawing from scouting tradition, rank advancement is a **family event**, not a checkbox:

### Ceremony Elements

1. **Review** — Family council reviews completed badges and discusses the candidate's character growth
2. **Testimony** — Candidate shares what they learned during this rank
3. **Question** — Family members ask one question each about what the candidate will carry forward
4. **Declaration** — Candidate reads or updates their Title of Liberty
5. **Presentation** — A physical token is presented (patch, pin, certificate, or mark on the family banner)
6. **Celebration** — Favorite meal, outing, or special activity chosen by the advancing member

### Physical Tokens

| Rank | Suggested Token |
|------|----------------|
| Apprentice of Lehi | A leather cord or simple bracelet |
| Scout of Nephi | A carved or engraved stone (Liahona symbol) |
| Warrior of Helaman | A shield patch or pin |
| Captain of Moroni | A sword/standard pin or engraved item |
| Builder of Zion | A family ring, plaque, or significant keepsake |

The **Family Title of Liberty** (a banner or display in the home) gets a new entry each time a family member advances. Over years, it becomes a record of the family's covenant journey.
