# Title of Liberty: A Book of Mormon Boot Camp for Families

> "We are a remnant of the seed of Jacob; yea, we are a remnant of the seed of Joseph" — [Alma 46:23](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/46?lang=eng&id=p23#p23)

---

## Vision

A family training program grounded in the Book of Mormon — not a curriculum to *study about* the scriptures, but a course to **become** the kind of people the scriptures describe. Challenges, quests, ranks, and goals that build the character profile of Helaman's stripling warriors and Captain Moroni's people.

**For:** The cpuchip family — 5 kids (ages 4–15), led by Dad (tribe of Ephraim, builder of Zion-building tools).

**Inspired by:** The "For Our Day" model — reading the Book of Mormon as a preparation manual for our time. Sparked by the [Send Me / Hannah Stoddard conversation](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pZljklzcdEI) on the prophetic destiny of the elders of Israel.

**The Core Principle:**
> "They had been taught by their mothers, that if they did not doubt, God would deliver them." — [Alma 56:47](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/56?lang=eng&id=p47#p47)

Faith is transmitted through parents who actually believe it. This boot camp is how we live it so our children catch it.

---

## The Four Pillars

These map to the Becoming app pillars and ground every rank, quest, and challenge. They emerge directly from scripture:

### 1. ⚔️ **Faith & Covenant** (Spiritual)
> "They were men of truth and soberness, for they had been taught to keep the commandments of God and to walk uprightly before him." — [Alma 53:21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/53?lang=eng&id=p21#p21)

**What it looks like:** Prayer, scripture study, ordinances, hearing the Lord's voice, keeping promises, personal revelation.

### 2. 🛡️ **Liberty & Service** (Social / Community)
> "In memory of our God, our religion, and freedom, and our peace, our wives, and our children" — [Alma 46:12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/46?lang=eng&id=p12#p12)

**What it looks like:** Serving others, defending what's right, building community, learning to lead, standing up for the vulnerable, preparing temporally.

### 3. 🏗️ **Knowledge & Skill** (Intellectual / Creative)
> "Finding there was greater happiness and peace and rest for me, I sought for the blessings of the fathers, and the right whereunto I should be ordained to administer the same; ... desiring also to be one who possessed great knowledge" — [Abraham 1:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/abr/1?lang=eng&id=p2#p2)

**What it looks like:** Learning real skills (building, growing, creating, coding, music), studying the scriptures deeply, understanding history, gaining wisdom through practice.

### 4. 💪 **Strength & Discipline** (Physical)
> "They were exceedingly valiant for courage, and also for strength and activity" — [Alma 53:20](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/53?lang=eng&id=p20#p20)

**What it looks like:** Physical fitness, hard work, endurance, healthy habits, self-discipline, going to bed and getting up on time, doing hard things without complaint.

## The Ranking System

Each rank is named for a figure or group from the Book of Mormon who embodies the next level of becoming. Ranks aren't "earned" by points alone — they require **demonstrated character** witnessed by the family.

### 🌱 Rank 1: **Apprentice of Lehi** (Starting Out)
*"Lehi was just a dad who said, 'We are in a mess,' and laid down his life to protect his people."*

**The character:** Willingness to begin. Lehi left everything because the Lord told him to. He didn't have it figured out. He just went.

**Requirements (all ages, adjusted for ability):**
- [ ] Read (or be read to) 1 Nephi 1–7 as a family
- [ ] Memorize one scripture (any from 1 Nephi)
- [ ] Complete 1 quest from each pillar
- [ ] Make a personal "Title of Liberty" — write down what you're willing to stand for

**Approximate age entry:** 4+ (with family support)

---

### 🏹 Rank 2: **Scout of Nephi** (Learning Obedience)
*"I will go and do the things which the Lord hath commanded"*

**The character:** Obedience before understanding. Nephi went back for the brass plates when it made no sense. He built a ship when nobody believed.

**Requirements:**
- [ ] Read (or listen to) 1 Nephi 1–22 (full book)
- [ ] Memorize [1 Nephi 3:7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/3?lang=eng&id=p7#p7)
- [ ] Complete a hard physical challenge (age-appropriate: hiking, running, pull-ups)
- [ ] Do something the Lord asked that didn't make sense at first — journal about it
- [ ] Serve someone outside the family without being asked
- [ ] Learn one new practical skill (cook a meal, start a fire, fix something, plant a garden)

---

### ⚔️ Rank 3: **Warrior of Helaman** (Building Character)
*"They were men who were true at all times in whatsoever thing they were entrusted."*

**The character:** Reliability. Trustworthiness. Doing what you said you'd do. The stripling warriors' defining trait wasn't fighting skill — it was that they could be trusted.

**Requirements:**
- [ ] Read Alma 53, 56–58 (the stripling warrior chapters)
- [ ] Memorize [Alma 53:20–21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/53?lang=eng&id=p20-p21#p20)
- [ ] Complete a 30-day challenge in one pillar (e.g., daily scripture study, daily exercise, daily service, daily skill practice)
- [ ] Demonstrate trustworthiness: complete 3 assignments/tasks without reminders
- [ ] Physical test: run/walk distance goal, strength goal (age-scaled)
- [ ] Teach a family lesson from the Book of Mormon
- [ ] Fast and pray about a specific question — journal the answer

---

### 🗡️ Rank 4: **Captain of Moroni** (Defending & Leading)
*"If all men had been, and were, and ever would be, like unto Moroni, behold, the very powers of hell would have been shaken forever."*

**The character:** Moroni was strong, understanding, joyful in freedom, faithful, reluctant to fight but willing when necessary. He didn't delight in bloodshed — he delighted in **doing good**. He prepared, fortified, and led.

**Requirements:**
- [ ] Read Alma 43–62 (the war chapters — yes, all of them)
- [ ] Read [Alma 48:11–17](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/48?lang=eng&id=p11-p17#p11) and write a personal response: "What kind of person do I want to be?"
- [ ] Lead a family activity from planning to execution
- [ ] Complete a 60-day sustained practice (any pillar)
- [ ] Build or create something meaningful (project, craft, code, garden)
- [ ] Study a historical figure who defended liberty — present to the family
- [ ] Mentor a younger sibling through a challenge
- [ ] Physical: sustained fitness habit, demonstrated improvement

---

### 👑 Rank 5: **Builder of Zion** (The Full Measure)
*"I sought for the blessings of the fathers, and the right whereunto I should be ordained to administer the same"*

**The character:** Abraham's pattern — receive for yourself, then administer to others. Not just personal righteousness but **building something that blesses others**. This is the Josephite birthright: the capacity to create refuge, to gather, to build.

**Requirements:**
- [ ] Read the Book of Mormon cover to cover
- [ ] Memorize 10+ scriptures across all four pillars
- [ ] Complete a sustained project that serves others (teach a class, build something for the community, create something that helps people learn the gospel)
- [ ] Write a personal declaration of covenant identity (who you are, what you stand for, what you will build)
- [ ] Demonstrate all four pillars in daily life for 90 days
- [ ] Lead a family scripture study session with real depth (footnotes, cross-references, Hebrew/Greek)
- [ ] Physical: complete an endurance challenge (long hike, race, physical accomplishment)
- [ ] Pass it on: help someone else begin their boot camp journey

## Age Adaptations

| Age Group | How It Works |
|-----------|-------------|
| **4–6** (youngest) | Everything is family-read, verbal, activity-based. "Challenges" are games. Scripture is through pictures, acting out, and simple memorization. Ranks are celebrated with the same gravity as the older kids — they are warriors too. |
| **7–10** | Reading independently (or shared), journals can be drawings or short sentences. Physical challenges are real but scaled. Skill-building is supervised. This is the **core boot camp age** — enough agency to choose, enough growth to shape. |
| **11–13** | Full participation. Reading, memorizing, writing, teaching, building. This is the age the 2,000 warriors would have been. The challenges should feel like they *matter*. |
| **14–15** | Leadership emphasis. They mentor younger siblings. Their projects should serve others. They're not just doing the boot camp — they're helping build it. The Captain of Moroni and Builder of Zion ranks become real aspirations. |

## Quest Examples by Pillar

### ⚔️ Faith & Covenant
- Pray morning and night for 7 consecutive days
- Read a chapter and find one thing the Spirit teaches you — journal it
- Memorize a new scripture
- Fast for a specific purpose and write what happened
- Give a blessing (for priesthood holders) or ask for one
- Bear testimony to a family member about something specific
- Read a conference talk and write one thing you'll do differently

### 🛡️ Liberty & Service
- Serve someone outside the family without being asked — 3 times this week
- Write a letter of gratitude to someone who has helped your family
- Learn about a historical figure who sacrificed for freedom
- Do extra chores without complaint for a week
- Plan and execute a service project (rake leaves, visit someone, make food)
- Stand up for someone at school or church
- Prepare an emergency kit with the family

### 🏗️ Knowledge & Skill
- Learn to cook a full meal for the family
- Read a non-fiction book and share one thing you learned
- Build something with your hands (woodworking, electronics, craft)
- Plant and tend a garden plot
- Write a poem, story, or song
- Learn basic first aid
- Study the Hebrew meaning of a Book of Mormon name
- Code a simple program or create a digital project

### 💪 Strength & Discipline
- Run/walk 1 mile (age-scaled distance)
- Do push-ups/sit-ups every morning for a week
- Complete a family hike (3+ miles)
- Wake up on time every day for 2 weeks without help
- No screens for a day — do something productive instead
- Cold shower challenge (one morning)
- Learn a martial art basic or self-defense move
- Help with heavy yard work or physical labor

## Family Council & Advancement

- **Weekly check-in:** Sunday family council reviews progress, celebrates wins, adjusts challenges
- **Rank advancement:** Voted by family council (including younger kids — they have a voice), confirmed by Dad
- **Ceremonies matter:** When a rank is earned, make it a family event. Moroni planted the standard on every tower. Make it visible.
- **Badges/tokens:** Create physical markers — patches, pins, a family banner, entries on a wall chart
- **The Title of Liberty:** Each family member's personal declaration hangs in the home. Updated as they grow.

## What Existing Programs Did

The exa search turned up:

1. **Nashua Stake "Stripling Warrior Camp" (2020)** — A 3-day virtual/in-person camp for Aaronic Priesthood youth. Theme: "Worthy to Be Called Sons" (Alma 56:9–10). Daily quorum devotionals, challenges, inspirational speakers. Focused on unifying quorums and building faith. Good model for a short-burst camp experience.

2. **Church Seminary Manual: "How can we become like the 2,000 stripling warriors?"** — Official church curriculum for military basic training members. Focuses on faith, courage, obedience, integrity. Lists key attributes from Alma 53:20–21. Provides the canonical character profile.

3. **Friend Magazine "Stripling Warrior Challenge" (2020)** — Simple physical + spiritual challenge for Primary-age kids. Push-ups, marching, saying what you believe. Shows this resonates even with the very young.

4. **Sister Craig's "Stripling Warrior Family" (Friend, 2019)** — A real family in Chile who adopted the stripling warrior identity after their father died. "We need to be brave like the stripling warriors. We have another battle to fight." Shows the family application.

**What's different here:** None of these are a sustained, ranked, family-wide training program with progression and skill-building. They're either short events, curriculum supplements, or inspirational stories. The boot camp fills a gap: a **long-form family discipleship program** grounded in Book of Mormon character development.
