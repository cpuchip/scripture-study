# Teaching the Savior's Way — Tad R. Callister

**Video:** [Teaching the Savior's Way — Elder Tad R. Callister](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GybaxBERWMk)
**Channel:** Inspiring · 2022-04-14 · 38:58
**Audience:** Military chaplain corps (interfaith), with multiple LDS teachers in the room
**Setting:** Discussion-based — Callister teaches by asking and letting attendees answer

## Summary

Callister, former Sunday School General President, walks the chaplain corps through several principles from the (then) new *Teaching in the Savior's Way* manual. He frames the first question of every teacher as: not "what should I teach?" or "how should I teach?" but "*how can I live more like the Savior?*" The talk is structured around four practical commitments — three for the teacher and one for the class — woven together with the master theme that revelation is available to every calling and every teacher.

The format is conversational. Callister asks a question, hands microphones around, and lets attendees answer in their own voice. He confirms their answers and adds the punctuation. The doctrine arrives through the room, not over the top of it. That alone is a craft worth noticing.

## In Line

### 1. Conversion = becoming like the Savior
> "Conversion to me is helping people convert to living a life like the Savior, acquiring his attributes. Every time we help those that we teach the more like the Savior, we're bringing about the conversion process." — [Callister, 4:46](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GybaxBERWMk&t=286)

This is exactly the move *Teaching in the Savior's Way* makes in Part 1 chapter 6, "[Help Learners Come unto Jesus Christ](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/teaching-in-the-saviors-way-2022/04-part-1/06-help-learners-come-unto-christ?lang=eng)" — "becoming like Him happens only as we act in faith… making intentional choices to follow His example and receive His grace." Callister reframes it crisply: the lesson is not the unit of conversion. The attribute-acquisition is.

### 2. Every calling carries the right to receive revelation
> "Inherent in every single calling in this church is the right to receive revelation. The Lord will never give us a responsibility in life — whether it's as a spouse or a parent or a chaplain or whatever it may be — without giving us the right to receive his revelation so we can magnify that calling." — [Callister, 6:30](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GybaxBERWMk&t=390)

He grounds this on Brigham Young's "we live far beneath our privileges" and on [D&C 42:61](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/42?lang=eng&id=p61#p61) (paraphrased: "if thou shalt ask, thou shalt receive revelation upon revelation, knowledge upon knowledge"). Joseph Smith was "the master receiver of revelation because he was the master asker." The point lands hard: revelation is generally predicated on us taking the initiative and asking.

This is the doctrinal anchor of the whole talk. Every other principle that follows is downstream of this one. If revelation is available to teachers, then the question for the teacher is not "did I cover the lesson?" but "did I ask?"

### 3. Read the scripture block first — before the manual
> "We read the assigned scripture block before reading the rest of the lesson. We read the assigned scripture block, we seek inspiration, we ponder what questions we might ask, what needs we might address." — [Callister, 14:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GybaxBERWMk&t=840)

His own practice illustrates the principle: when writing a book, he was tempted to consult commentaries first; when he went to the scriptures first instead, sometimes the answer matched the commentary "but now it was mine, not theirs," and sometimes a custom answer came that the commentary would have prejudiced him out of. This maps directly to TSW [Teach the Doctrine](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/teaching-in-the-saviors-way-2022/07-part-2/10-teach-the-doctrine?lang=eng): "Learn the doctrine of Jesus Christ for yourself."

A sister in the room added the right second move: "When I read the scripture block to me that's where you're getting the true doctrine and principles, and then when you read the lesson you're getting the inspiration on how to teach." Doctrine from the source. Method from the manual. The order matters.

### 4. Prepare well in advance — at least a week, ideally more
> "The Lord may want to give us revelation on Tuesday morning or Wednesday night or whatever, if we're thinking about it in advance." — [Callister, 16:50](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GybaxBERWMk&t=1010)

A Relief Society teacher in the room raised the bar: "If I read the lesson even a full month ahead of time, it allows the Spirit to tell me what my sisters need." A seminary teacher added that you can't prepare a whole week ahead for daily lessons, but you can read the scripture text of the week before you read the day-by-day breakdowns. Same principle, scaled to context.

The doctrine: revelation is line upon line ([2 Nephi 28:30](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/28?lang=eng&id=p30#p30), [D&C 98:12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/98?lang=eng&id=p12#p12)). Cramming compresses the line into a point and forfeits the upon.

### 5. Teachers AND learners record spiritual impressions
This is the one Callister calls a "cultural change" the Church is trying to make. He stacks four supports for it:

- **The teenage-son thought experiment:** Imagine your 14-year-old says, "Mom, hold on — that's really good counsel, I want to write it down." How would you feel about giving him more counsel? "We are all like spiritual teenagers."
- **Elder Scott:** "Knowledge carefully recorded is knowledge available in time of need… this practice enhances the likelihood of your receiving further light."
- **Joseph Smith** (a quote Callister had not seen until recently): "Here is another important item — if you proceed to discuss important questions and feel to write them down. Perhaps for neglecting to write those things when God had revealed them, not esteeming them of sufficient worth, the Spirit may withdraw."
- **Oliver Cowdery in [D&C 6:22-23](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/6?lang=eng&id=p22-p23#p22):** "Cast your mind upon the night that I cried unto your heart… did I not speak peace unto your mind?" Recorded impressions are the witness that survives the doubt.

He ties it back to [Helaman 11:23](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/hel/11?lang=eng&id=p23#p23): "they did receive many revelations daily." A verse he says he couldn't understand for years now reads obvious — *if you record the impressions, you can receive many in a day.*

This single section may be the most useful piece of the video for teacher councils. The "spiritual anchor" framing from Sister Phelps in the room (referencing Elder Renlund's talk on remembering) is worth lifting verbatim into a council.

### 6. Teach people, not lessons
> "Success isn't having covered the whole lesson. Success isn't having given a brilliant presentation. Success is — did we have the spirit to change the lives of those in our class to help them become more christ-like?" — [Callister, 9:35](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GybaxBERWMk&t=575)

The major (Beck, from Maxwell Air Force Base) testifies that being called as a Sunday School president in a brand-new ward forced him to teach two consecutive sessions completely differently. "I had no plan to go in that direction but clearly I felt led." Callister punctuates: "We're not teaching a given lesson, we're teaching people, and the lesson is a means."

This is TSW [Love Those You Teach](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/teaching-in-the-saviors-way-2022/07-part-2/08-love-those-you-teach?lang=eng) and [Teach by the Spirit](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/teaching-in-the-saviors-way-2022/07-part-2/09-teach-by-the-spirit?lang=eng) collapsed into one sentence.

### 7. The doctrine itself has converting power
> "Inherent in the doctrine is a power that changes life — that is inherent in the doctrine itself." — [Callister, 36:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GybaxBERWMk&t=2160)

Anchored on [Alma 31:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/31?lang=eng&id=p5#p5) ("the word… had a more powerful effect upon the minds of the people than the sword") and Boyd K. Packer's "true doctrine, understood, changes attitudes and behavior more quickly than a study of behavior will improve behavior."

The closing missionary anecdote — walking the discouraged elder through 10-12 Restoration doctrines he himself believed, then saying "this church has just ruined you for any other church, you know too much" — is a master illustration of the principle. The doctrine itself, once taken in honestly, does the work.

## Out of Line

Nothing significant. This is a faithful exposition of the manual.

## Missed the Mark

Two small things:

**1. The "Saturday 1-3 PM" framing was a useful joke but Callister never actually answered the second question that he raised:** the woman teaching gospel doctrine described her actual practice (rising at 5 AM, taking notes verse-by-verse, writing the lesson out by hand the second half of the week, color-coded pens) and Callister moved on rather than holding her up as the model. The room saw the model. He could have named it. Minor — but a teacher council where you're trying to spread practice would benefit from naming what good practice looks like, not just describing the shape of it.

**2. The audience is not exclusively LDS** (military chaplain corps including interfaith chaplains) — and the chaplain Emily's beautiful answer about ministering across faith traditions ("there has been a creative way to minister to them and to get their needs met without stepping outside the bounds of time and place for me") got a cordial confirmation but no real engagement. Callister's audience-handling is mostly excellent, but the moment when the doctrine he was teaching about revelation actually showed up in a chaplain ministering to dying babies of other faiths — that was the moment. He moved past it. A small miss given the otherwise warm reception of every voice in the room.

## Missed Opportunities

The one that matters for your teacher council:

**Callister did not connect "becoming like the Savior" to Moroni 7:48 or 1 John 3:2-3.** Conversion as attribute-acquisition is the foundation of his whole talk, and the canonical verse for that doctrine — "when he shall appear we shall be like him; for we shall see him as he is; and every man that hath this hope in him purifieth himself, even as he is pure" — was right there. He had the doctrine; he didn't pull the verse.

This is a clean place for you to extend his framework in your council. Mormon, John, and *Lectures on Faith* 7 each independently give the same doctrine: the seeing-ahead is what shapes the soul. That's exactly what Callister is asking teachers to do — fix the eye on the Savior so the soul (theirs and the class's) gets shaped by what it's looking at. The hope-study material gives you the textual backbone for what Callister stated as a principle.

Worth pairing in council: Callister's "first question is how can I live more like the Savior" + Moroni 7:48 / 1 John 3:2-3 / *Lectures on Faith* 7:16. The chain is: faith trusts the prototype is real → hope fixes the eye on the prototype → charity is what the soul becomes after long gazing. Your teachers can locate themselves in the chain *and* in their teaching practice using the same framework.

## Overall Assessment

This is one of the cleanest, most usable Sunday-School-president-level expositions of TSW you could find. Callister's experience as Sunday School General President shows in every move he makes: he asks rather than tells; he names the cultural change he's trying to produce; he gives concrete practices that teachers can implement Monday morning; he illustrates with stories that don't preach over the doctrine.

The video is approximately 80% Part 2 of TSW — Teach by the Spirit, Teach the Doctrine, Invite Diligent Learning — with the opening 4-5 minutes anchoring it in Part 1 (Help Learners Come unto Christ via attribute-acquisition). Almost nothing on Love Those You Teach as a standalone, though the way he runs the discussion *demonstrates* it.

For a teacher council, the video does the heavy lifting of an entire manual chapter in 38 minutes. Highly recommended.
