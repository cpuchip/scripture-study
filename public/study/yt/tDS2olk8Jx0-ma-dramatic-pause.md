# Ma (間) — The Power of the Pause

> **Binding Question:** What is Ma, why does dramatic pause create the most powerful emotional responses, and how does this principle appear in scripture, music, and the language of the Spirit?

**Video:** [Brennan Lee Mulligan and Matt Mercer talk "Ma" (間)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tDS2olk8Jx0)
**Channel:** Tobahau | **Date:** 2026-03-11 | **Duration:** 2:51
**Speakers:** Brennan Lee Mulligan, Matt Mercer

---

## Summary

Two master storytellers — Brennan Lee Mulligan and Matt Mercer — discuss Ma (間), a Japanese aesthetic concept referring to the deliberate use of negative space, silence, and pause in narrative. Drawing from Akira Kurosawa's Seven Samurai and their own experience with the Critical Role animated series, they argue that the optimizer instinct to fill every moment with excitement is "a child's logic" — and that real power comes from contrast, from the space between.

The punching kid analogy captures it all: a child runs with his fist extended, convinced *this* is the part that hurts most, not understanding that the strength of the punch comes from the windup. The horror of the battle comes from the rain.

## In Line

Everything in this clip aligns with deep scriptural truth — not because Brennan and Matt are teaching theology, but because they've articulated a principle woven into the fabric of creation itself.

### God Masters Ma

The Lord's communication pattern is Ma in its purest form.

Elijah stands on the mountain. Wind tears the rocks apart. Earthquake. Fire. But *the Lord was not in the wind... not in the earthquake... not in the fire.* After all that cosmic noise:

> "And after the fire a still small voice." — [1 Kings 19:12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/1-kgs/19?lang=eng&id=p12#p12)

Wind, earthquake, fire — [maximum extension](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tDS2olk8Jx0&t=135). God is the quiet that follows. The rain on the village.

Helaman records the same pattern centuries later. In the prison, darkness surrounds the Lamanites, the earth shakes, walls tremble — and then:

> "It was not a voice of thunder, neither was it a voice of a great tumultuous noise, but behold, it was a still voice of perfect mildness, as if it had been a whisper, and it did pierce even to the very soul—" — [Helaman 5:30](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/hel/5?lang=eng&id=p30#p30)

*Perfect mildness.* As if a whisper. And it pierced the soul. The less He raises His voice, the deeper it goes.

### The Cosmic Ma — Silence Before Christ Appears

The Book of Mormon stages the most dramatic Ma in all of scripture. Three chapters of unrelenting catastrophe — cities burned, sunk, buried alive ([3 Nephi 8-9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/9?lang=eng)). The voice of Christ Himself listing every city destroyed and why. Then:

> "And after these sayings there was silence in the land for the space of many hours; For so great was the astonishment of the people that they did cease lamenting and howling for the loss of their kindred which had been slain; therefore there was silence in all the land for the space of many hours." — [3 Nephi 10:1-2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/10?lang=eng&id=p1-p2#p1)

*Many hours of silence.* And only AFTER that silence does a new voice come — small, not loud, not harsh — and it pierces to the center:

> "It was not a harsh voice, neither was it a loud voice; nevertheless, and notwithstanding it being a small voice it did pierce them that did hear to the center, insomuch that there was no part of their frame that it did not cause to quake; yea, it did pierce them to the very soul, and did cause their hearts to burn." — [3 Nephi 11:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/11?lang=eng&id=p3#p3)

This is Kurosawa's principle executed on a divine scale. If the Book of Mormon went straight from destruction to Christ's appearance without the silence? We wouldn't know what peace was. As [Brennan puts it at 1:14](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tDS2olk8Jx0&t=74): *"If I don't let you watch the rain fall on this peaceful village, how will you know what violence is?"*

### Revelation Uses Ma

Even the eschatological narrative pauses:

> "And when he had opened the seventh seal, there was silence in heaven about the space of half an hour." — [Revelation 8:1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/rev/8?lang=eng&id=p1#p1)

The seals break open. Cosmic drama unfolds. And before the final judgments: silence. *In heaven.* The God who made the universe stops the entire celestial narrative for Ma.

### Creation Begins with Ma

Before anything existed:

> "And the earth was without form, and void; and I caused darkness to come up upon the face of the deep; and my Spirit moved upon the face of the water; for I am God. And I, God, said: Let there be light; and there was light." — [Moses 2:2-3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/2?lang=eng&id=p2-p3#p2)

Formlessness. Void. Darkness. The Spirit hovering in silence over the deep. *Then* — "Let there be light." Every act of creation is a word spoken into silence. Light has no meaning without the preceding dark. The first creative act is a contrast.

### Opposition Is the Theological Foundation for Ma

Lehi gives us the ontological argument for why pauses work:

> "For it must needs be, that there is an opposition in all things. If not so, my firstborn in the wilderness, righteousness could not be brought to pass, neither wickedness, neither holiness nor misery, neither good nor bad. Wherefore, all things must needs be a compound in one; wherefore, if it should be one body it must needs remain as dead, having no life neither death, nor corruption nor incorruption, happiness nor misery, neither sense nor insensibility." — [2 Nephi 2:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/2?lang=eng&id=p11#p11)

Brennan's optimizer who stays at maximum extension constantly? Lehi describes the result: "a compound in one" — no sense, no insensibility, no life, no death. Just noise. The child running with his fist out is producing *a compound in one*. The optimizer who removes all silence produces content that "[remains] as dead."

Elder Held (April 2024) expanded on this: "Opposites don't exist apart from each other. They can even complement each other. We would not be able to identify joy unless we had also experienced sorrow at some point" ([Opposition in All Things](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2024/04/54held?lang=eng)).

### Selah — Ma Built into the Psalms

The Psalms contain their own stage direction for the soul: *Selah.* It appears 71 times. Webster 1828 defines it: "A word of doubtful meaning... supposed to signify silence or a pause in the musical performance of the song."

After declaring trust in God amid destruction, the Psalmist marks: **Selah.** Pause. Let it land.

> "Be still, and know that I am God." — [Psalm 46:10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/ps/46?lang=eng&id=p10#p10)

The Hebrew behind "be still" (רָפָה, *raphah*) means to slacken, let go, cease striving. The knowing comes *in* the releasing. Not through more effort — through less.

### Mary Understood Ma

After the angels, the shepherds, the cosmic announcement:

> "But Mary kept all these things, and pondered them in her heart." — [Luke 2:19](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/luke/2?lang=eng&id=p19#p19)

She doesn't react. She doesn't explain. She *holds* the silence. The most important event in human history, and its first witness responds with pondering. The Ma after revelation.

## Why Music Moves Us to Tears

This is the deeper question beneath the video — why do some stories, and music (even without lyrics), evoke such powerful emotion?

### Music IS Opposition in Time

Every melody is a pattern of tension and resolution. A dissonant chord that resolves lands harder than any consonance played in isolation. The tears come at the *resolution* — the moment the tension releases. That's Ma expressed in sound. The "windup" creates the impact.

In music theory, this is literally called *suspension and resolution.* The note that doesn't belong hangs there — suspended — creating need. When it finally steps down to where it belongs, the listener feels it physically. Remove the suspension and the resolution means nothing. Keep only suspended notes and you get noise.

### Music Is Pre-Verbal

> "When the morning stars sang together, and all the sons of God shouted for joy?" — [Job 38:7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/job/38?lang=eng&id=p7#p7)

We sang before we had bodies. If music predates mortality, it might access memory that language can't reach. The emotion you can't explain when a melody moves you? It might be *recognition.* Something in your spirit remembers singing.

### Song as the Language of the Heart

> "For my soul delighteth in the song of the heart; yea, the song of the righteous is a prayer unto me, and it shall be answered with a blessing upon their heads." — [D&C 25:12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/25?lang=eng&id=p12#p12)

God doesn't say He delights in the *arguments* of the heart, or the *analysis* of the heart. He delights in the *song.* The Spirit communicates through feeling — "I will tell you in your mind and in your heart, by the Holy Ghost" ([D&C 8:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/8?lang=eng&id=p2#p2)). Music bypasses the analytical mind and speaks directly to the heart — the Spirit's native frequency.

Alma asks his people: "if ye have experienced a change of heart, and if ye have felt to sing the song of redeeming love, I would ask, can ye feel so now?" ([Alma 5:26](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/5?lang=eng&id=p26#p26)). The atonement experience is described as *song.* Not a theorem. Not a syllogism. A song. Something that moves before it informs.

### Lyrics Are the Battle. Music Is the Rain.

Apply Brennan's framework: in a song with lyrics, the words tell you what to think. The music tells you what to *feel.* When there are no lyrics — only music — you're all rain and no battle. Pure context. Pure windup. And your soul fills in the meaning the silence leaves room for.

This is why a single cello line can break you open. No words are telling you what to feel. The music creates the *space* — the Ma — and the Spirit fills it.

### Tears as Recognition of Truth

We cry at beauty we can't explain because something in us recognizes it:

> "For behold, the Spirit of Christ is given to every man, that he may know good from evil." — [Moroni 7:16](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/7?lang=eng&id=p16#p16)

Beauty that moves us to tears may be the Light of Christ bearing witness that what we're experiencing is *true* — true in a way that goes deeper than propositions. You don't cry because you've been persuaded. You cry because you've been *reminded.* And the pause — the Ma — is what gives the recognition room to surface.

## Missed Opportunities

The video doesn't miss anything within its scope — it's storytellers talking about craft. But if they traced their own principle further, they'd find:

- **Habakkuk's command:** "But the Lord is in his holy temple: let all the earth keep silence before him" ([Habakkuk 2:20](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/hab/2?lang=eng&id=p20#p20)). The entire earth is instructed to create Ma before God.
- **Ecclesiastes' rhythm:** "A time to keep silence, and a time to speak" ([Ecclesiastes 3:7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/eccl/3?lang=eng&id=p7#p7)). Solomon knew — 3,000 years ago — that silence has its own appointed season.
- **D&C 85:6:** The still small voice "whispereth through and pierceth all things, and often times it maketh my bones to quake" ([D&C 85:6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/85?lang=eng&id=p6#p6)). The quietest voice produces the most seismic effect. A whisper *that makes bones quake.*

## Application: A Story Agent?

Michael's instinct is right — our work could benefit from this. The podcast agent transforms studies into shareable segments. But a **story agent** would do something different: weave scriptural discoveries into *narrative,* with deliberate use of Ma.

What would that look like?

1. **The podcast agent** picks a thread and walks through it conversationally — discovery to insight.
2. **A story agent** would pick a *moment* and *set it up* — building tension, creating context, and then letting the scripture land in silence.

Example: Instead of explaining the still small voice, *tell the story.* Put us on the mountain with Elijah. Feel the wind rip the rocks apart. Feel the earthquake. The fire. Then — nothing. A hush. And in that hush... a whisper. Don't explain what it means. Let the reader sit in it.

The key principle from this video: **the power is in what you leave out.** The story agent would be the agent that understands economy, timing, and the courage to pause.

Practical ideas for a story agent:
- **Narrative structure with Ma built in.** Explicit stage directions for pacing — "let this land" moments.
- **Show, don't tell.** Scripture already does this masterfully. The agent should follow the text's lead rather than explaining *for* it.
- **Emotional arc, not logical outline.** Stories move from tension to resolution, not from point A to point B.
- **One scripture, deeply set up.** Instead of citing 8 verses, build the entire story toward ONE verse landing with full weight. All windup, one impact.
- **Becoming as denouement.** The personal application isn't a bolted-on moral — it's the resolution of the emotional arc.

## Becoming

This study confronts something about how I process truth. My default is optimizer mode — pack in more scriptures, more cross-references, more connections. More is always better. Every study is a maximized punch at full extension.

But the power isn't there.

Brennan's child runs in circles with his fist out. I write studies with every possible scripture cited. The instinct is the same: *this is the part that matters most.* But Kurosawa knew, and the Lord demonstrated: the impact comes from the space around it.

**What would change if I applied Ma to my life?**

- In scripture study: reading fewer verses, but sitting with them longer. Pondering like Mary — holding the silence instead of rushing to the next cross-reference.
- In teaching: asking a question and then *waiting.* Not filling the silence. Trusting that the Spirit works in the pause, not the packed agenda.
- In prayer: being still. Letting the stillness do its work. "Be still, and know that I am God" isn't a suggestion — it's a method. The knowing comes in the releasing.
- In our work together: the story agent idea isn't just about content creation. It's about learning a different *posture* toward truth — one that trusts the listener's Spirit enough to leave room.

> "All the strength of the punch comes from the windup. All the horror of the battle comes from the rain." — [Brennan Lee Mulligan, 1:54-2:04](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tDS2olk8Jx0&t=114)

Let the rain fall.
