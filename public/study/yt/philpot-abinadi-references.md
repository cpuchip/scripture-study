## Philpot's Core Abinadi Theology

Across 10+ presentations, Philpot returns to the same handful of points. His reading is typological — the Abinadi story isn't just history, it's a template for our day.

### 1. The Blasphemy Charge: Christ Coming Down *Literally*

Philpot's signature move. Abinadi was killed because he taught that Christ would literally come down among the children of men, and the priests called it blasphemy. Philpot then turns the question on the audience: *Do we take the scriptures literally?*

**Key scripture:** [Mosiah 17:7-8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/17?lang=eng&id=p7-p8#p7) — "He has said that God himself should come down among the children of men... and for this thou shalt be put to death."

**Clearest statement:**
> "The reason Abinadi was burned at the stake is because he said God would literally come down among the people. They said this is blasphemy and they burned him. So do we take the scriptures literally?"
>
> — [Highlighting Philpot Interview, 7:21](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sKsv8WOfQtk&t=441)

**Extended treatment:**
> "For that statement they accuse him of blasphemy and they burn him at the stake... it's the blasphemy that Christ will come down literally that gets him killed. So are we the same here?"
>
> — [Identifying and Leaving Babylon, 42:31](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1zFa8qmQjBk&t=2551)

### 2. King Noah's Court as Modern Type

Philpot reads King Noah's court as a template for modern religious-political corruption. Noah's priests are the corrupted leaders. The harlots are idolaters and lobbyists. The tower is institutional power used to survey and control.

**Key scripture:** [Mosiah 11:1-15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/11?lang=eng&id=p1-p15#p1) — Noah's wickedness, heavy taxation, harlots, and building projects.

**References:**
> "King Noah you're surrounded by harlots, lobbyists, people who are idolatrous — it's a court dedicated to idolatry."
>
> — [Manti Presentation, 1:20:29](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sBiT8AYHUTo&t=4829)

> "Utah, King Noah's new organization with his 12 high priests who meet with their harlots in Davos Switzerland."
>
> — [Provo Presentation, 30:29](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_GtrcdavjXY&t=1829)

> "Remember King Noah was a monger, guilty of whoredoms, because he and his priests had many harlots."
>
> — [Provo Presentation, 1:58:19](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_GtrcdavjXY&t=7099)

### 3. "What Teach Ye This People?"

Abinadi's confrontation with Noah's priests about teaching the Law of Moses without teaching the coming of Christ. Philpot uses this as a lens for modern religious leaders who teach programs and procedures without teaching Christ's literal return.

**Key scripture:** [Mosiah 12:25-27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/12?lang=eng&id=p25-p27#p25) — Abinadi challenges the priests who claim to teach but don't understand.

**References:**
> "Remember Abinadi, where he says 'what teach ye this people?' and they say 'we teach the law of Moses.' He says... why don't you teach the people that?"
>
> — [Signs of the Second Coming, 1:32:17](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_OwSK0GPhRs&t=5537)

> "Think of Abinadi — 'what teach ye this people?' — and one of the priests of Noah says 'the Ten Commandments'... how many LDS politicians [do the same]?"
>
> — [Cardston Alberta Part 2, 2:57:45](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zj_mcanOUM0&t=10665)

### 4. Written for Our Day

Philpot explicitly argues that Mormon included the Abinadi narrative as an editorial choice aimed at modern readers — specifically latter-day covenant people who might find themselves in Noah's position without realizing it.

**Key scripture:** [Mormon 8:35](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/morm/8?lang=eng&id=p35#p35) — "Behold, I speak unto you as if ye were present, and yet ye are not. But behold, Jesus Christ hath shown you unto me, and I know your doing."

> "The story of Abinadi was literally written by Mormon, or editorialized into the Book of Mormon by Mormon, for the state of Utah in our day today."
>
> — [Identifying and Leaving Babylon, 47:35](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1zFa8qmQjBk&t=2855)

### 5. Mosiah 16 — Persisting in Carnal Nature

Philpot quotes Abinadi's words directly, applying them to modern complacency.

**Key scripture:** [Mosiah 16:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/16?lang=eng&id=p5#p5) — "He that persists in his own carnal nature, and goes on in the ways of sin and rebellion against God, remaineth in his fallen state."

> Quotes Mosiah 16 directly — "He that persists in his own carnal nature."
>
> — [Manti Presentation, 9:40](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sBiT8AYHUTo&t=580)

## Connection to the Abinadi Hermeneutic Study

Philpot's reading converges with our [Abinadi hermeneutic study](../abinadi-how-to-read.md) on the central question: **the charge of blasphemy hinges on whether Christ's coming is literal or figurative, and that question is as live now as it was in Noah's court.**

Where Philpot goes further — and where caution is warranted — is in the one-to-one mapping of ancient figures to modern entities. The typological pattern is real (Mormon tells us he wrote for our day in [Mormon 8:35](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/morm/8?lang=eng&id=p35#p35)). But the specific political identifications are Philpot's interpretation, not scripture's claim.

The enduring lesson: Abinadi's audience *thought* they believed. They professed faith in Christ's coming. What they couldn't tolerate was the claim that it would be *literal*. That's the question each generation has to answer for itself.
