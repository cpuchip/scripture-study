# Enjoy the Words of Eternal Life

**Source:** [Moses 6:59](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/6?lang=eng&id=p59#p59)
**Date:** February 15, 2026

---

## The Phrase

> That by reason of transgression cometh the fall, which fall bringeth death, and inasmuch as ye were born into the world by water, and blood, and the spirit, which I have made, and so became of dust a living soul, even so ye must be born again into the kingdom of heaven, of water, and of the Spirit, and be cleansed by blood, even the blood of mine Only Begotten; that ye might be sanctified from all sin, and **enjoy the words of eternal life in this world**, and eternal life in the world to come, even immortal glory.
> — [Moses 6:59](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/6?lang=eng&id=p59#p59)

This is God speaking to Adam directly — the first teaching of the plan of salvation. The phrase sits at the summit of a carefully structured statement: fall → death → rebirth → sanctification → and then this. The *purpose* of all of it. Not just survival. Not just forgiveness. But to **enjoy the words of eternal life in this world**.

Two promises, parallel:
- **In this world:** enjoy the words of eternal life
- **In the world to come:** eternal life itself, even immortal glory

The words come *first*. Before we receive the thing itself, we receive the *words* of that thing — and we are meant to enjoy them.

## Footnote Trail

The footnotes on verse 59 are unusually precise. Two of them point directly to the heart of this phrase:

### 59e — "enjoy" → [2 Nephi 4:15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/4?lang=eng&id=p15#p15)

> For my soul **delighteth** in the scriptures, and my heart **pondereth** them, and writeth them for the learning and the profit of my children.
> — [2 Nephi 4:15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/4?lang=eng&id=p15#p15)

The footnote doesn't link "enjoy" to a Topical Guide entry. It links to *Nephi* — to a man who actually did it. His soul *delighteth*. His heart *pondereth*. He *writeth* them. This is what enjoying the words of eternal life looks like in practice: delight, pondering, and writing.

And this isn't Nephi at peace. This is Nephi in the middle of his psalm of anguish — surrounded by enemies, weeping over his sins, exhausted by his weaknesses. In the middle of *that*, his anchor is the scriptures. He delights in them not because life is easy, but because they are true.

> Behold, my soul delighteth in the things of the Lord; and my heart pondereth continually upon the things which I have seen and heard. ([2 Nephi 4:16](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/4?lang=eng&id=p16#p16))

> He hath filled me with his love, even unto the consuming of my flesh. ([2 Nephi 4:21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/4?lang=eng&id=p21#p21))

### 59f — "words" → [John 6:68](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/6?lang=eng&id=p68#p68)

> Then Simon Peter answered him, Lord, to whom shall we go? **thou hast the words of eternal life.**
> — [John 6:68](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/6?lang=eng&id=p68#p68)

Peter's declaration comes after the Bread of Life discourse — after Jesus has said things so hard that "many of his disciples went back, and walked no more with him" ([John 6:66](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/6?lang=eng&id=p66#p66)). Jesus turns to the Twelve and asks: "Will ye also go away?"

Peter doesn't say "I understand everything you just said." He says: *Where else would we go? You have the words of eternal life.*

Peter had already recognized — the words themselves are the draw. Not the miracles (thousands had seen those and left). Not complete understanding (Peter clearly didn't fully understand the eat-my-flesh discourse). The **words**. Something about what Jesus spoke carried life in it. Peter could feel it even when he couldn't fully parse it.

And this connects back to what Jesus said just five verses earlier:

> **The words that I speak unto you, they are spirit, and they are life.**
> — [John 6:63](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/6?lang=eng&id=p63#p63)

The words aren't *about* spirit and life. They *are* spirit. They *are* life. This is a metaphysical claim, not a metaphor.

## Webster 1828: "Enjoy"

The modern ear hears "enjoy" as something light — *enjoy your meal, enjoy the show*. Webster 1828 hears it differently:

> **ENJOY** (v.)
> 1. To take pleasure or satisfaction in the possession or experience of.
> 2. **To have, possess, and use with satisfaction; to occupy or have the benefit of**, as a good or profitable thing, or as something desirable. *"That the children of Israel may enjoy every man the inheritance of his fathers."* — Num. 36:8. *"To enjoy the pleasures of sin for a season."* — Heb. 11:25.

Definition 2 is the one that matters here. "Enjoy" in 1828 doesn't just mean *take pleasure in*. It means **to have, possess, and use**. There is ownership in this word. Occupation. Active benefit.

And the scriptural example Webster chose is striking: Hebrews 11:25 — Moses choosing *not* to enjoy the pleasures of sin for a season, "choosing rather to suffer affliction with the people of God." The word "enjoy" there carries the full weight of *having and possessing* something. Moses could have *had* the pleasures of Egypt. He chose differently.

So when Moses 6:59 says we may "enjoy the words of eternal life in this world," the 1828 meaning says: you may **have them, possess them, use them, and take satisfaction in them**. These words are not distant. They are not just promises about the future. They are something you can *hold* now.

## Three Layers of "The Words of Eternal Life"

Traced through the footnotes and cross-references, there are at least three layers to what constitutes the "words of eternal life":

### Layer 1: The Scriptures

The footnote to 2 Nephi 4:15 makes this the most immediate meaning. The scriptures are words of eternal life — they record God's dealings, His covenants, His instructions. Nephi delights in them because they contain the truths of eternity in written form.

But this is the starting layer, not the final one. The scriptures are the words of eternal life *written down*. They point toward something living.

### Layer 2: The Living Words of Christ

John 6:63 — "the words that I speak unto you, they are spirit, and they are life." These are not recorded words. These are spoken words, present-tense, alive. Christ's words carry His life in them. When He speaks, something is transmitted that goes beyond information.

Peter recognized this at John 6:68. He couldn't have fully articulated what was happening, but he knew: the words Jesus spoke were different from anyone else's words. They carried eternal life *in them*.

This is why the Bread of Life discourse uses language of consumption — eating His flesh, drinking His blood. You don't just intellectually engage with these words. You take them in. They become part of you.

### Layer 3: The Ongoing Communication of the Holy Ghost

This is where Moses 6:59 goes in the verses that follow. After saying we may enjoy the words of eternal life, God explains what it means to be born of the Spirit:

> Therefore it is given to abide in you; the **record of heaven**; the **Comforter**; the **peaceable things of immortal glory**; the **truth of all things**; that which quickeneth all things, which maketh alive all things; that which knoweth all things, and hath all power according to wisdom, mercy, truth, justice, and judgment.
> — [Moses 6:61](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/6?lang=eng&id=p61#p61)

What *abides in you* after the rebirth? The record of heaven. The Comforter. The peaceable things of immortal glory. The truth of all things. These are the words of eternal life at their deepest — not written on plates or spoken by mortal lips, but living inside you. The Holy Ghost transmitting the knowledge and peace and truth of God directly into your soul.

The words of eternal life are not just words *about* eternal life. They are the medium through which eternal life reaches you *now*, in this world.

## The Structure of Moses 6:58–62

The passage has a deliberate architecture:

| Verse | Content |
|-------|---------|
| 58 | **Command:** "Teach these things freely unto your children" |
| 59 | **The Problem & The Solution:** Fall → death → born again → sanctified → enjoy the words of eternal life now, eternal life later |
| 60 | **The Mechanism:** Water (commandment), Spirit (justification), Blood (sanctification) |
| 61 | **What Abides:** The record of heaven, the Comforter, peaceable things, truth, quickening power |
| 62 | **The Name:** "This is the plan of salvation unto all men, through the blood of mine Only Begotten" |

Verse 59 states the promise. Verse 60 explains *how*. Verse 61 explains *what you receive*. And verse 61 is extraordinary — it describes the Holy Ghost not as a visitor but as a permanent resident: "it is given **to abide in you**."

## What This Means for Study

If "enjoy" means to *have, possess, and use with satisfaction*, then scripture study isn't just learning — it's ownership. We don't study the words of eternal life to extract information. We study them to *possess* them. To carry them. To have them abide in us, the way Moses 6:61 describes.

Nephi's model from 2 Nephi 4:15 shows the process:
- **Delight** — your soul responds to them with pleasure
- **Ponder** — your heart turns them over, examines them, goes deeper
- **Write** — you record them for the learning and profit of others

Peter's model from John 6:68 shows the commitment:
- You don't have to understand everything
- You need to recognize where the *life* is
- "To whom shall we go?" is not a question of logic — it's a question of where life resides

And Moses 6:59 shows the scope:
- This isn't just about the next life
- The words of eternal life are meant to be enjoyed **in this world**
- The promise of eternal life in the world to come is *preceded by* the gift of its words now
