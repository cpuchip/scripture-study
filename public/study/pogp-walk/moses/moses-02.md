# Moses 2 — the creation account, told in the first person by God

**Binding idea:** Genesis 1 retold from inside — "And **I, God**, said… created… saw." The Restoration's two great additions to the creation account land here: the work is done "by mine Only Begotten" (v1), and the ancient crux of "Let *us* make man" (Gen 1:26) is resolved — God is speaking to the Only Begotten (v26-27).

**Read:** Moses 2 covers the six creative periods of Genesis 1, but the voice is changed. Where Genesis narrates God in the third person ("And God said… and God saw"), Moses gives God's own first-person account to Moses: "I am the Beginning and the End, the Almighty God; by mine Only Begotten I created these things" (v1). Light, firmament, dry land, lights in the firmament, living creatures, and man follow the Genesis order, each period closing "I, God, saw that all things which I had made were good." At the creation of man: "I, God, said unto mine Only Begotten, which was with me from the beginning: Let us make man in our image, after our likeness" (v26); "I, God, created man in mine own image, in the image of mine Only Begotten created I him; male and female created I them" (v27). Man is blessed, given dominion and "every herb bearing seed… for meat" (v28-29), and the chapter ends "all things which I had made were very good… the sixth day" (v31).

**Thoughts:**
- *The "I, God" refrain is the whole point of the retelling.* Twenty-plus times the chapter says "I, God" — and that single change converts a report *about* God into testimony *from* God, given to Moses to write (v1). Genesis tells you what happened; Moses 2 has the Creator telling you He did it. The creation account becomes a first-person witness, which is exactly what Moses 1 set up (the vision in which this is spoken).
- *"By mine Only Begotten I created" (v1) carries Moses 1 straight into Genesis.* Moses 1:32-33 established Christ as the Creator-agent of worlds without number; Moses 2:1 applies it to *this* world. So the chapter opens by naming what Genesis 1:1 leaves unnamed: the *how* and the *by-whom*. The footnote chains v1 to Abr 4:1 — the third creation account (Abraham's) will pluralize it further ("the Gods").
- *Moses 2:26 resolves the oldest puzzle in Genesis.* "Let us make man in our image" (Gen 1:26) has driven interpreters for millennia — royal plural? angels? the Trinity read backward? Moses 2:26 answers it cleanly and without strain: "I, God, said unto mine Only Begotten… Let us make man." The "us" is the Father speaking to the Son. The footnotes make it explicit — v26's "us" → TG "Jesus Christ, Creator," "make" → "Man, Physical Creation of." This is the Restoration doing what it does best: not adding mystery but removing it.
- *Man is in the image of Christ specifically (v27).* Genesis says "in the image of God created he him." Moses sharpens it: "in the image of mine Only Begotten created I him." The image we bear is the image of the Son — which is why Moses, in chapter 1, could be "in the similitude of mine Only Begotten" (1:6). The imago Dei is, more precisely, an imago Christi. (Abr 3:14 / Ether 3:15-16 — the brother of Jared sees the premortal Christ's *body* and learns all men are created after it — is the same doctrine from the other end.)
- *The goodness refrain is doctrine, not decoration.* Seven times "good," climaxing in "very good" (v31). The footnote sends to Moroni 7:12 ("all things which are good cometh of God") and D&C 59:17-20. Creation is pronounced good *before* the Fall — the material world is not a prison or an accident but a "very good" work. This anchors the matter-spectrum reading: element is not the enemy of spirit; both are God's, both are good.

**Critical analysis / tensions named:**
- *This is the first telling, and it's about to be reframed.* Moses 2 reads as a straightforward physical creation. But Moses 3:5 will reveal that all of it was created "spiritually, before… naturally upon the earth" — the two-creations doctrine. So Moses 2 should be read as the *account* whose nature (spiritual-first) Moses 3 then discloses. I'll carry that forward rather than resolve it here.
- *The dating frame:* heading says June–October 1830. Moses 2 is the JST of Genesis 1 — Joseph's revelatory revision, not a translation from an ancient manuscript. The textual changes (the "I, God" voice, the Only Begotten) are the *doctrinal restorations* the JST work surfaced.
- *Don't over-read the parallel.* Most of Moses 2 *is* Genesis 1 verbatim. The walk's job is to mark the *deltas* (first-person voice, Only Begotten, image-of-the-Son) — the deep creation theology already lives in our corpus (`creation.md`, `word`, `doc-01_planning-then-create`), so link, don't re-derive.
