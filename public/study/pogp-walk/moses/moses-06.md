# Moses 6 — the language of Adam, Enoch's call, and "in the language of Adam, Man of Holiness is his name"

**Binding idea:** The righteous line (Seth → Enoch) keeps a record "in a language… pure and undefiled" (6:6) against Cain's spreading darkness, and Enoch — "but a lad… slow of speech" (6:31) — is called to preach the *whole* gospel that was given to Adam. Its summit is a name: "in the language of Adam, Man of Holiness is his name, and the name of his Only Begotten is the Son of Man" (6:57). The title Jesus wears all through the Gospels is, underneath, a divine patronymic.

> **Note:** the full gospel-to-Adam exposition (vv48-68 — the Fall, original guilt, born of water/Spirit/blood, "the plan of salvation unto all men," Adam's baptism) is already worked in our study **`moses-6-gospel-to-adam`**. This note digests the chapter and records the *walk's distinct observations*; it does not re-derive that study.

**Read:** Adam calls his sons to repent; Seth is born "instead of Abel" (6:2-3); the righteous line begins to "call upon the name of the Lord" (6:4), keeping "a book of remembrance… in the language of Adam… pure and undefiled" (6:5-6), passing the priesthood and the record down through Enos, Cainan, Mahalaleel, Jared, to Enoch — while "Satan had great dominion among men… wars and bloodshed… because of secret works, seeking for power" (6:15). Enoch is called: the Spirit descends, a voice sends him to a people whose "hearts have waxed hard" (6:27); he protests, "I am but a lad, and all the people hate me; for I am slow of speech" (6:31); God answers, "Open thy mouth, and it shall be filled… therefore walk with me" (6:32-34), and "anoint thine eyes with clay… and thou shalt see" (6:35). Enoch becomes a seer, preaches from the high places, and the people tremble (6:47). He then recounts the gospel taught to Adam: "because that Adam fell, we are" (6:48); the Lord called Adam to "be baptized… in the name of mine Only Begotten Son… which is Jesus Christ" (6:52); "the Son of God hath atoned for original guilt, wherein the sins of the parents cannot be answered upon the heads of the children, for they are whole" (6:54); men "taste the bitter, that they may know to prize the good… they are agents unto themselves" (6:55-56); "no unclean thing can dwell… in his presence; for, in the language of Adam, Man of Holiness is his name, and the name of his Only Begotten is the Son of Man" (6:57); be "born again… of water, and of the Spirit, and… cleansed by blood" (6:59); "This is the plan of salvation unto all men" (6:62); "all things are created and made to bear record of me… all things bear record of me" (6:63); and Adam is baptized, "born of the Spirit," and told, "thou art one in me, a son of God; and thus may all become my sons" (6:64-68).

**Thoughts:**
- *"Man of Holiness is his name… the Son of Man" (6:57) reframes the most-used title in the Gospels.* Jesus calls Himself "the Son of Man" constantly — and it's usually read as a marker of His humanity/lowliness (the Son of [a mere] man). Moses 6:57 turns it over: in Adam's language the *Father's* name is "Man of Holiness," so "Son of Man" is "Son of [the] Man [of Holiness]" — a **divine patronymic**. When Jesus says "the Son of Man hath power on earth to forgive sins" or "shall come in the glory of his Father," He is naming His descent from the Holy One, not apologizing for the flesh. (The footnote sends to Matt 20:28 and John 3:13 — both "Son of Man" sayings about His mission and His heavenly origin.) The title that sounds humblest is, at root, the highest claim of sonship.
- *"All things bear record of me" (6:63) is the broadest Christological claim in the early chapters.* Not just scripture, not just prophets — "things which are in the heavens above, and things which are on the earth, and things which are in the earth, and things which are under the earth… all things bear record of me." The whole created order is a testimony of Christ. The footnote pairs it with Alma 30:44 (Korihor: "all things denote there is a God… the planets… witness that there is a Supreme Creator") and D&C 88:47. This is the seed of "Christ in all the scriptures / all the creation" — the creation isn't neutral matter but a standing witness. (And it closes the loop with Moses 3:9: every created thing was a spirit-being first; here every created thing is *also* a witness.)
- *Enoch is the reluctant prophet, and the pattern is deliberate (6:31-34).* "I am but a lad… slow of speech." The footnotes chain him to Moses ("who am I," Ex 3:11; "slow of speech," Ex 4:10) and Jeremiah ("I am a child," Jer 1:6) — and, strikingly, the "all the people hate me" footnote (6:31) points to **JS—H 1:20** (Joseph hated after the First Vision). The Lord's answer is the same answer given to all of them: "Open thy mouth, and it shall be filled" (6:32). The qualification for a prophet isn't eloquence or standing — it's a mouth God can fill. And the call ends not with a task but with companionship: "walk with me" (6:34), the very phrase Genesis uses of Enoch ("Enoch walked with God," Gen 5:24) and that this book will spend chapter 7 unfolding.
- *Anoint the eyes with clay (6:35) → John 9.* The Lord tells Enoch to "anoint thine eyes with clay, and wash them, and thou shalt see," and the footnote sends to John 9:6 — Jesus making clay to heal the man born blind. The same gesture, four thousand years apart: clay on the eyes to grant sight. The footnote editors handed us the parallel; the seer's spiritual sight and the blind man's restored sight are the same act of the same Lord. (This is one of the footnote-chains we've named as "insights on a silver platter.")
- *The two streams from chapter 5 keep running.* The righteous line keeps "a book of remembrance" in a "pure and undefiled" language (6:5-6, 46) — a covenant of *record and memory*; meanwhile Satan's "secret works, seeking for power" spread "wars and bloodshed" (6:15, footnote → Ether 8:23). Light keeps a record; darkness keeps a secret. The chapter literally contrasts a book written "by the spirit of inspiration" against oaths sworn "in the dark." Memory vs. secrecy is the two-streams antithesis in its sharpest form.

**Critical analysis / tensions named:**
- *"Conceived in sin" (6:55) is not original-sin-as-guilt — the same verse refutes it.* 6:54 says children "are whole from the foundation of the world" and "the sins of the parents cannot be answered upon the heads of the children." So 6:55's "conceived in sin" cannot mean inherited guilt; the footnote (TG "Conceived in Sin") and the surrounding text point to the *condition* of being born into a fallen, sin-surrounded world, not a stain of guilt. Honest to flag: this verse has been misread toward Augustinian original sin, and the chapter itself forecloses that reading (it's the Moroni 8 doctrine — children are whole — stated to Adam). 
- *The Enoch material is uniquely Restoration.* Genesis gives Enoch four words ("Enoch walked with God… God took him," Gen 5:24). Moses 6–7 gives him two chapters of vision and a translated city. This is the largest single expansion in the Book of Moses, and worth naming as such — the PoGP doesn't just clarify Genesis here, it *restores a prophet the Bible reduced to a genealogical footnote*.
- *"Slow of speech" and the Joseph parallel.* The footnote ties Enoch's reluctance to JS—H 1:20. The walk should hold this lightly — it's a thematic resonance the footnote draws (the hated young prophet), not a claim that Enoch's call and Joseph's are identical events. Calibrated: pattern, not equation.
