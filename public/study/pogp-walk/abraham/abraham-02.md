# Abraham 2 — the covenant that adopts: "as many as receive this Gospel… shall be accounted thy seed"

**Binding idea:** The Abrahamic covenant is given in full (seed · land · priesthood · the gospel to all nations) — and its defining clause is the one that breaks lineage open. "As many as receive this Gospel shall be called after thy name, and shall be accounted thy seed" (2:10). One chapter after the hard lineage passage of Abraham 1, the covenant is declared *trans-lineage*: you become Abraham's seed by **receiving the gospel**, not by blood. The covenant's content is "the blessings of salvation, even of life eternal" (2:11) — Genesis 12 read as the gospel itself.

**Read:** The famine kills Abraham's brother Haran; Terah stays in Ur and "turned again unto his idolatry" (2:5). Abraham and Lot pray, and the Lord appears: "I have purposed to take thee away out of Haran, and to make of thee a minister to bear my name in a strange land which I will give unto thy seed… for an everlasting possession, when they hearken to my voice" (2:6). The Lord declares Himself — "My name is Jehovah, and I know the end from the beginning" (2:8) — and gives the covenant: "I will make of thee a great nation… make thy name great among all nations… that in their hands they shall bear this ministry and Priesthood unto all nations; and… for as many as receive this Gospel shall be called after thy name, and shall be accounted thy seed, and shall rise up and bless thee, as their father… and in thee (that is, in thy Priesthood) and in thy seed… shall all the families of the earth be blessed, even with the blessings of the Gospel, which are the blessings of salvation, even of life eternal" (2:9-11). Abraham answers, "Thy servant has sought thee earnestly; now I have found thee" (2:12), and departs at sixty-two with Sarai, Lot, "and the souls that we had won in Haran" (2:15) — "eternity was our covering and our rock and our salvation" (2:16). He builds altars at Jershon, Sechem, and Bethel; the Lord promises "Unto thy seed will I give this land" (2:19). The famine drives them toward Egypt, and the Lord instructs: "when the Egyptians shall see her, they will say—She is his wife; and they will kill you… Let her say unto the Egyptians, she is thy sister, and thy soul shall live" (2:23-24).

**Thoughts:**
- *2:10 is the covenant's hinge, and it answers Abraham 1's hard verses (2:10).* "As many as receive this Gospel shall be called after thy name, and shall be accounted thy seed." The covenant is extended to whoever *receives the gospel* — they are adopted, "accounted" Abraham's seed, and "rise up and bless [him] as their father." The footnotes make the doctrine explicit: Gal 3:29 ("if ye be Christ's, then are ye Abraham's seed, and heirs according to the promise") and Rom 4:11 (Abraham "the father of all them that believe"). So the very next chapter after the priesthood-lineage passage of Abraham 1 declares the covenant *open by reception, not closed by blood*. The walk's strongest observation here: **Abraham 2:10 is a textual counterweight to a blood-determinist reading of Abraham 1:27.** The covenant Abraham carries is, by its own terms, trans-lineage — its membership is defined by who receives the gospel. (This is the same principle the BoM walk found dissolving the lineage-as-worth reading of 2 Ne 5:21 — covenant tracks belief, not ancestry. I log it as a counterweight datapoint for Michael's study.)
- *The covenant's content is the gospel, and its endpoint is eternal life (2:11).* Genesis 12 reads as land + descendants + national blessing. Abraham 2:11 reads it as "the blessings of the Gospel, which are the blessings of salvation, even of life eternal." The Restoration doesn't add a separate "gospel" alongside the Abrahamic covenant — it identifies them. The promise to Abraham *is* the plan of salvation extended through a covenant lineage-and-priesthood to all families of the earth. The triple gloss in 2:11 ("in thee — that is, in thy Priesthood — and in thy seed — that is, thy Priesthood") makes the point almost insistently: the blessing carried to all nations is the *priesthood/gospel*, not merely genealogy.
- *Abraham and Sarai are the first missionaries (2:15).* "The souls that we had won in Haran." Genesis 12:5 says "the souls that they had gotten in Haran" — easily read as servants/possessions; the Book of Abraham (and the footnote → TG "Conversion") reads it as *converts*. So the "gospel to all nations" of the covenant (2:9) isn't only a future promise about descendants — Abraham is already doing it, carrying souls out of Haran. The covenant to bless all families is enacted from the first journey.
- *"Eternity was our covering and our rock and our salvation" (2:16).* A pilgrim line. Abraham leaves a settled house for tents, and names *eternity* his shelter — not a roof but the eternal God and the eternal covenant. (Heb 11:9-10 reads Abraham the same way: he "dwelt in tabernacles… for he looked for a city… whose builder and maker is God.") The man who just received a promise of land lives, for now, with eternity for a covering. The covenant is held by faith before it is held by deed.
- *"Now I have found thee" (2:12) closes the seeking of chapter 1.* Abraham 1 opened with *desiring* and *seeking*; 2:12 closes the arc — "Thy servant has sought thee earnestly; now I have found thee." The covenant is the answer to the seeking. (Jer 29:13 in the footnote — "ye shall seek me, and find me, when ye shall search for me with all your heart.") Desire → seeking → finding → covenant.

**Critical analysis / tensions named:**
- *The "say she is thy sister" passage is harder in Abraham than in Genesis, not softer (2:22-25).* In Genesis 12, Abraham appears to devise the sister-ruse himself ("Say, I pray thee, thou art my sister," Gen 12:13). The Book of Abraham *intensifies* the difficulty: here **the Lord instructs it** — "the Lord said unto me… Let her say… she is thy sister, and thy soul shall live" (2:22-24). So the Restoration text doesn't excuse Abraham by putting the scheme on him; it puts the instruction in God's mouth. Two honest things to hold: (1) it is a *half-truth*, not a fabrication — Sarai was Abraham's close kin (Gen 20:12, "she is the daughter of my father, but not… of my mother," footnoted at 2:25); (2) the frame is explicitly *preservation of life under lethal threat* ("they will kill you, but they will save her alive," 2:23). The text presents a God-directed, life-preserving half-truth told to a murderous regime. Whether that is deception-sanctioned or prudence-under-tyranny is a real ethical seam — I name it rather than resolve it; the chapter offers the life-preservation rationale and the kinship fact, and stops there.
- *The covenant is conditional ("when they hearken to my voice," 2:6).* The "everlasting possession" of the land is given "when they hearken." The Abrahamic covenant, like all covenants (D&C 130:21), carries a condition — the blessing flows on obedience. Worth naming against any reading of the covenant as unconditional ethnic entitlement: even the land-promise is hearken-gated.
