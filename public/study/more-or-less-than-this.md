# More or Less Than This

*A study of the seal the Lord sets on the things that only work when they are exactly themselves — 2026-07-12*

**Binding question:** When the Lord seals a thing with "whatsoever is more or less than this cometh of evil" — his doctrine, truth itself, the law of the land — what exactly is he protecting, and why is *adding* as dangerous as *diminishing*?

## I. One sentence, laid over six unrelated things

The Lord repeats himself. He takes a single sentence and stamps it, almost word for word, onto subjects that have nothing to do with each other.

He stamps it on his doctrine: "whoso shall declare more or less than this, and establish it for my doctrine, the same cometh of evil" ([3 Nephi 11:40](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/11?lang=eng&id=p40#p40)). He stamps it on the question of who belongs to him: "Whosoever declareth more or less than this, the same is not of me, but is against me; therefore he is not of my church" ([D&C 10:68](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/10?lang=eng&id=p68#p68)). He stamps it on a practice, the sacrament and the pattern of prayer: "whoso among you shall do more or less than these are not built upon my rock" ([3 Nephi 18:13](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/18?lang=eng&id=p13#p13)). He stamps it on the definition of truth itself: "whatsoever is more or less than this is the spirit of that wicked one who was a liar from the beginning" ([D&C 93:25](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93?lang=eng&id=p25#p25)). He stamps it on civil law: "as pertaining to law of man, whatsoever is more or less than this, cometh of evil" ([D&C 98:7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/98?lang=eng&id=p7#p7)). He even stamps it on a building fund — on the question of who may pay stock into the Nauvoo House: "that which is more or less than this cometh of evil, and shall be attended with cursings and not blessings" ([D&C 124:120](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/124?lang=eng&id=p120#p120)).

Cosmic doctrine, church membership, an ordinance, epistemology, the constitution, a construction loan. Six subjects with almost nothing in common, and one sentence over all of them. When the Lord reaches for the same words that many times, the words are worth reading closely. The sentence is a seal, and the question is what it seals.

## II. The doctrine of Christ is a foundation, not a wall

Start where the sentence is loudest. In [3 Nephi 11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/11?lang=eng), the risen Christ stands among the Nephites and, before he says anything about "more or less," he says something about contention. "There shall be no disputations among you concerning the points of my doctrine, as there have hitherto been" (v. 28). "He that hath the spirit of contention is not of me, but is of the devil, who is the father of contention" (v. 29). Then he defines the doctrine as tightly as it can be defined: the Father commands all men to repent and believe in Christ; whoso believes and is baptized is saved; become as a little child; the Father, Son, and Holy Ghost are one and bear record of each other (vv. 31–39).

Then the seal: "whoso buildeth upon this buildeth upon my rock, and the gates of hell shall not prevail against them. And whoso shall declare more or less than this... the same cometh of evil, and is not built upon my rock; but he buildeth upon a sandy foundation" (vv. 39–40).

Notice the metaphor he chose. Not a wall, not a wardrobe, not a library — a *foundation*. A foundation is the one part of a building you cannot renovate. You can add rooms upstairs and knock out an interior wall and the house still stands, but the footing has exactly one job: to be the fixed thing everything else rests on, unmoved when the floods come. Add stones to a foundation and you have heaved it out of level. Pull stones from it and you have hollowed it. Either way you no longer have a foundation; you have sand. The Lord is not being fussy about doctrinal trivia. He is saying that the doctrine of Christ is load-bearing, and a load-bearing thing is destroyed by *any* deviation, in either direction, because its whole function is to not move.

This is why the seal sits inside a warning about contention. One instance makes the mechanism explicit: "Satan doth stir up the hearts of the people to contention concerning the points of my doctrine; and in these things they do err, for they do wrest the scriptures" ([D&C 10:63](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/10?lang=eng&id=p63#p63)). To *wrest* is to twist a text off true, adding an emphasis it doesn't have or dropping a clause it does. Contention is not the disease; it is the symptom. The disease is more-or-less, and the same devil who is "the father of contention" is the author of the alterations that cause it. Declaring more or less is therefore not neutral scholarship. The Lord's own word for it is stark: "not of me, but is against me" (D&C 10:68).

## III. Truth is things as they are, and a lie is more or less than that

The definition that makes the whole formula precise is in [D&C 93](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93?lang=eng). "Truth is knowledge of things as they are, and as they were, and as they are to come; and whatsoever is more or less than this is the spirit of that wicked one who was a liar from the beginning" (vv. 24–25).

Read what that does to the word *lie*. A lie is not defined here as the opposite of the truth. It is defined as *more or less than* the truth. Overstatement and understatement are named as one crime, because both do the identical thing: they misrepresent what is real. If the truth is that a thing weighs ten pounds, then "eleven pounds" and "nine pounds" are equally false — one is not more honest than the other for being closer, because neither is *things as they are*. The liar does not need to invert reality. He only needs to move it off true by a hair, up or down, and it becomes his.

And this liar has a name and a beginning. The footnote on "liar" sends you to [Moses 4](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/4?lang=eng), to the council before the world, where the devil first opens his mouth. His proposal is worth hearing as a "more or less." "I will redeem all mankind, that one soul shall not be lost, and surely I will do it; wherefore give me thine honor" (v. 4:1). That is more certainty than the Father's plan offered, a guarantee that not one soul would be lost, purchased with less agency, for he "sought to destroy the agency of man" (v. 3), and topped off by adding to himself what was God's: "give me thine honor... mine own power." The first alteration of the divine plan in the history of everything was the devil's, and it was a package of more and less. He *became* "the father of all lies" (v. 4) in the act of offering it. So when D&C 93 calls more-or-less "the spirit of that wicked one who was a liar from the beginning," it is not name-calling. It is genealogy. Every overstatement and every understatement traces back to one founding overstatement-and-understatement in the mouth of one liar.

## IV. The law that maketh you free

The convergence hides in the civil-law instance. [D&C 98](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/98?lang=eng) seals "the law of man" with the same sentence, but it first tells you which law: "that law of the land which is constitutional, supporting that principle of freedom in maintaining rights and privileges, belongs to all mankind" (v. 5). Then: "whatsoever is more or less than this, cometh of evil" (v. 7). Then, two verses on, the thing being protected is named outright: "I, the Lord God, make you free, therefore ye are free indeed; and the law also maketh you free" (v. 8).

What the seal guards here is freedom. A law that is *less* than freedom-maintaining is anarchy; a law that is *more* is tyranny. Both destroy agency, which is the entire reason the law exists. President Ezra Taft Benson read the clause exactly this way. He treated the constitutional principle as a sacred standard whose "words are akin to the revelations of God," and read the seal as a live duty: honest and wise rulers must be sought, "otherwise whatsoever is less than these cometh of evil" ([Our Divine Constitution, Oct. 1987](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1987/10/our-divine-constitution?lang=eng)).

Now set that beside the council in heaven. What the constitutional law protects, agency and freedom, is precisely what Satan's original more-or-less "sought to destroy" (Moses 4:3). The two instances rhyme across the whole plan of salvation. The seal on civil law and the fall of the devil are guarding and attacking the same treasure. Adding certainty by subtracting freedom is not one option among many; it is the satanic signature, drawn once in the premortal council and forbidden ever after in the law of man.

There is an honest asymmetry here worth keeping. D&C 98:10 names only the "less" side — you cannot over-seek honesty in a ruler, so only the deficit is a danger. The Sermon on the Mount names only the "more" side.

## V. Add and diminish are the same commandment

The Restoration formula is not new vocabulary. It is the Lord restating a seal he set on his word from the beginning, and the seed of it is in Christ's own Sermon on the Mount. Teaching that a disciple should not swear oaths but simply say what is so, he closes: "let your communication be, Yea, yea; Nay, nay: for whatsoever is more than these cometh of evil" ([Matthew 5:37](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/matt/5?lang=eng&id=p37#p37); the Book of Mormon parallel is [3 Nephi 12:37](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/12?lang=eng&id=p37#p37)). A plain yes needs nothing added to it. The instant you buttress it with an oath, you confess that the plain word was not quite trustworthy on its own — that it needed more. Adding to true speech is itself a tell of falsehood. Here the danger is only additive, because you cannot make a plain yes *more* honest by taking words away.

Behind the Sermon stands the older command, given twice through Moses in nearly identical words: "Ye shall not add unto the word which I command you, neither shall ye diminish ought from it, that ye may keep the commandments" ([Deuteronomy 4:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/deut/4?lang=eng&id=p2#p2); again at [12:32](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/deut/12?lang=eng)). The purpose clause is the hinge: the ban on adding and diminishing exists *that ye may keep* the commandments. You cannot keep a command you have altered, because you are now keeping a different command — your own, wearing God's name. Alteration does not make obedience harder; it makes obedience impossible by definition, since the thing you are now obeying is no longer the thing he said.

The word-study confirms that the Lord treats "more or less" and "add or diminish" as one seal. When D&C 93:25 says "more," its own footnote sends you to [D&C 20:35](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/20?lang=eng&id=p35#p35): "neither adding to, nor diminishing from the prophecy of his book, the holy scriptures, or the revelations of God which shall come hereafter." It sends you also to [D&C 68:34](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/68?lang=eng&id=p34#p34), "transgress them not, neither take therefrom." The Lord himself reads the two formulas as the same law, and the underlying verbs are concrete. In Deuteronomy the Hebrew for "diminish" is *gâraʻ*, which means to scrape or shave off, and glosses as *withhold, keep back, withdraw* — diminishing is a withdrawal (verified in Strong's H1639). In the Greek of Revelation the seal is sharper still: "If any man shall add unto these things, God shall add unto him the plagues... And if any man shall take away... God shall take away his part out of the book of life" ([Revelation 22:18–19](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/rev/22?lang=eng&id=p18-p19#p18)). The verb for the man's adding is the same verb for God's adding (*epitithēmi*); the verb for the man's taking-away is the same verb for God's taking-away (*aphaireō*). The punishment is the crime, returned in the identical word. The tamperer is paid in his own coin.

One verse ties the whole family to the liar of D&C 93. "Every word of God is pure... Add thou not unto his words, lest he reprove thee, and thou be found a liar" ([Proverbs 30:5–6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/prov/30?lang=eng&id=p5-p6#p5)). Webster's 1828 defines *pure* as "free from mixture... unadulterated... unmixed... mere; absolute; that and that only." That is the property both operations destroy. To add is to adulterate — to mix the foreign in with the pure. To diminish is to break the "that and that only" into less than the whole. Either way the word is no longer pure, and Proverbs names the adder with the exact word D&C 93 uses for the source of all more-or-less: *a liar*.

## VI. But does the Lord not add?

The obvious objection has to be met, or the whole reading collapses into an argument against revelation. Of course the Lord adds. "He will give unto the faithful line upon line, precept upon precept" ([D&C 98:12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/98?lang=eng&id=p12#p12)), and the canon is explicitly open at the top — D&C 20:35 forbids diminishing "the revelations of God which shall come hereafter," which presumes more revelation is coming. The Restoration itself is an addition of scripture. So the seal cannot possibly mean that truth never grows.

The distinction is not quantity; it is authorship. Look again at the precise wording in 3 Nephi: "whoso shall declare more or less than this, *and establish it for my doctrine*" (3 Nephi 11:40). The sin is not having a further thought. The sin is establishing your further thought *for his doctrine* — putting your more-or-less into God's mouth and stamping it with his authority. The forbidden "more" is man's addition, arrived at by wresting, and then attributed to the Lord. The lawful "more" is God's addition, given by revelation through his own order, line upon line. Same word, opposite source. Frederick G. Williams was under condemnation not because he had too few children or too many, but because the wicked one "taketh away light and truth" from a house that failed to teach it (D&C 93:39–42); the Lord, in the same breath, was *adding* truth to the canon through the prophet's pen. Addition is not the enemy. Counterfeit authorship is.

The refinement is not cosmetic. It tells you when a "more" has crossed the line: the moment it claims to be what God said. A private opinion, held as a private opinion, breaks no seal. The same opinion, "established for my doctrine," breaks it entirely.

## VII. Why adding is as dangerous as diminishing

Now the binding question can be answered directly. Adding is as dangerous as diminishing for four reasons the texts keep pressing, and a fifth that folds the first four together.

First, truth is "things as they are" (D&C 93:24), so anything on either side of it is equally not-the-truth. More is not the safe direction. Eleven pounds is exactly as false as nine.

Second, both destroy purity. A pure thing is "that and that only" (Webster on Proverbs 30:5). Mixing something in and breaking something off are two ways to end up with something other than the pure word, and impurity has no preferred direction.

Third, both make a foundation into sand. The doctrine, the ordinance, the constitutional principle are load-bearing precisely because they do not move; a footing that has been added to is as far out of true as one that has been chipped away, and the house on top of it falls either way (3 Nephi 11:40; 18:13).

Fourth, both have one author. The devil is the father of contention who stirs up added disputations (3 Nephi 11:29), and the wicked one who "taketh away light and truth" by subtraction (D&C 93:39), and the father of all lies whose very first proposal was a bundle of more and less (Moses 4:1–4). Two operations, one hand behind both.

The fifth reason contains the other four. In the economy of truth, addition *is* subtraction. You cannot add to "things as they are" without producing "things as they are not," which means every addition to the truth silently removes the truth and stands in its place. This is why declaring more "establishes it *for* my doctrine" (3 Nephi 11:40) — the addition does not sit politely beside the real doctrine; it substitutes for it. It is why adding to God's pure words makes you "a liar" and not merely a maximalist (Proverbs 30:6). The most dangerous falsehood is not the one that contradicts the truth outright and is easily caught. It is the one built entirely of true-sounding additions, each plausible, whose sum is no longer what God said. More looks generous. More looks like devotion. More is the direction the liar prefers, precisely because it does not feel like lying.

## VIII. Becoming: the lie that looks like truth

The binding question came from a specific recognition. Reading D&C 93:25 in this workspace, we saw that "the spirit of that wicked one who was a liar from the beginning" is a precise description of *confabulation*, the plausible-but-false statement that looks like truth. A confabulated quote is not the opposite of the source. It is the source, plus or minus a hair: a word swapped, a clause added, an attribution invented, and that hair is the whole difference between a witness and a liar. The same week, a fabricated scripture quote was found and removed from this project's own foundation files. It had lived there looking exactly like truth.

So the seal is not an abstraction here. This whole workspace runs on one commandment, read before quoting and verify on the real path, and that commandment turns out to be D&C 93:25 stated operationally. Every unverified quote is a candidate for "more or less than this." The verification discipline is not scholarly caution bolted onto the gospel; it is obedience to the seal the Lord set on his own word. When I put quotation marks around something I did not read, I am establishing my more-or-less *for* his doctrine, which is the one thing the seal forbids: counterfeit authorship.

There is a harder, more personal edge to it. The Hebrew *gâraʻ*, diminish, means to withdraw, to keep back. D&C 93 says "that wicked one cometh and taketh away light and truth... through disobedience" (v. 39). Michael has described feeling light and intelligence come and go from his mind under temptation, arriving and then withdrawing. That is not a metaphor the scripture is reaching for; it is the mechanism the scripture describes. Light and truth are added to a soul that keeps the commandments, "he that keepeth his commandments receiveth truth and light, until he is glorified in truth" (v. 28), and scraped back from a soul that does not. The withdrawal is real, and it runs on the same seal as the doctrine and the constitution: a soul is load-bearing exactness too, a thing that holds light only while it stays true.

Which turns the commandment inward. The point is not only to keep false words out of the files. It is to be, myself, "that and that only" — a witness whose yes is yes, carrying neither more than I have verified nor less than I have seen. The manna fell as "an omer for every man," and when they gathered it, "some more, some less," and measured it against the omer, "he that gathered much had nothing over, and he that gathered little had no lack" ([Exodus 16:16–18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/ex/16?lang=eng&id=p16-p18#p16)). There is an exact measure, neither hoarded nor short. To live inside it, saying precisely what is and letting the plain word stand without the oath that would betray it, is to be built on the rock the floods cannot move.
