# Beguiled: The Serpent's Craft

A follow-up to the [priestcraft study](priestcraft.md), tracing the word chain that connects "subtle," "cunning," "craft," "guile," "beguile," and "wile" — revealing them as **one semantic family** in the language of the Restoration. The serpent in the Garden was the first practitioner of *craft*, and the scriptures never let us forget it.

> **Related studies:** [Priestcraft: Satan's Counterfeit of Priesthood](priestcraft.md) · [The Oath and Covenant of the Priesthood](priesthood-oath-and-covenant.md) · [Obtaining vs. Ordaining](priesthood-obtaining-exploration.md)

---

## The Word Chain: One Family

Webster's 1828 dictionary — the dictionary of the Restoration — reveals something striking. These six words all define each other in a closed loop:

| Word | Webster 1828 | Points To |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| **SUBTLE** | "Sly in design; artful; **cunning**; insinuating" | → cunning, crafty |
| **CUNNING** | "**Crafty**; sly; artful; designed; deceitful" | → crafty |
| **CRAFT** | "Cunning, art, or skill, in a bad sense… artifice; **guile**" | → guile |
| **GUILE** | "**Craft**; deceitful cunning; artifice; duplicity; **wile**" | → craft, wile |
| **BEGUILE** | "To delude **by guile**, artifice, or **craft**" | → guile, craft |
| **WILE** | "A sly, insidious artifice; a **beguilement**" | → beguile |

This is not a list of loosely related synonyms. This is a **closed semantic loop** — each word directs you to the others. They are one concept with six faces.

And right at the center of the loop sits **CRAFT**.

> Webster even provides the scriptural anchor for "beguile": *"The serpent **beguiled** me, and I did eat."* — [Genesis 3:13](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/3?lang=eng&id=p13#p13)
>
> And for "wile": *"the **wiles** of the devil"* — [Ephesians 6:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/eph/6?lang=eng&id=p11#p11)

### What This Means for "Priestcraft"

If guile = craft and beguile = "to delude by craft," then:

- **Priestcraft** literally = **priest-guile**
- **D&C 121:42** says priesthood operates "without hypocrisy, and without **guile**" — literally "without craft"
- The suffix *-craft* in "priestcraft" IS the theology. It doesn't just mean "the trade of a priest" — it means **the guile of a priest**

The [priestcraft study](priestcraft.md) explored the suffix distinction (*-hood* = identity vs. *-craft* = skill/cunning). Now we see: **craft IS guile**. They're the same word.

## The Garden: Where Craft Entered the World

### Genesis vs. Moses: What the Restoration Adds

The Genesis account gives us the frame. Moses fills in *motivation* — and that motivation is the DNA of priestcraft.

**Genesis 3:1** — "Now the serpent was more **subtil** than any beast of the field" ([Genesis 3:1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/3?lang=eng&id=p1#p1))

**Moses 4:5–6** — "the serpent was more **subtle** than any beast of the field… And **Satan** put it into the heart of the serpent, (for he had drawn away many after him,) and he sought also to **beguile** Eve, for he knew not the mind of God, wherefore he sought to **destroy the world**" ([Moses 4:5–6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/4?lang=eng&id=p5-p6#p5))

Genesis tells us the serpent was "subtil." Moses tells us *who was behind the serpent and why*.

Now apply Webster 1828. The serpent was "subtle" — which Webster defines as "sly in design; artful; **cunning**; insinuating." Synonyms: "**crafty**; cunning; shrewd; sly; wily."

**The serpent was crafty. He beguiled — deluded by craft. The serpent was the first practitioner of *craft*.**

### Satan's Original Pitch: The Priestcraft Blueprint

Before the Garden, Satan made his proposal in the council:

> "I will be thy son, and I will redeem all mankind, that one soul shall not be lost, and surely I will do it; wherefore **give me thine honor**." — [Moses 4:1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/4?lang=eng&id=p1#p1)

Look at this through the priestcraft lens from [2 Nephi 26:29](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/26?lang=eng&id=p29#p29):

| Priestcraft Element | Satan's Pitch |
|---------------------|---------------|
| "set themselves up for a light" | "I will redeem all mankind" — *I* will do it |
| "get gain" | "give me thine honor" |
| "seek not the welfare of Zion" | "he sought to destroy the agency of man" ([Moses 4:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/4?lang=eng&id=p3#p3)) |

Satan proposed the first priestcraft before the world was: a religious program that substitutes compulsion for agency, performed to acquire honor and power. He then executed it in the Garden through *craft* — through subtlety, guile, beguiling.

### How the Beguiling Worked

Watch Satan's technique in the Garden, step by step:

1. **Question what God said** — "Yea, hath God said—Ye shall not eat of every tree of the garden?" ([Moses 4:7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/4?lang=eng&id=p7#p7)) — not a frontal attack, but a *question*. Subtle. Insinuating.

2. **Contradict God directly** — "Ye shall not surely die" ([Moses 4:10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/4?lang=eng&id=p10#p10)) — the flat lie, but only after trust has been established

3. **Offer godlike knowledge** — "ye shall be as gods, knowing good and evil" ([Moses 4:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/4?lang=eng&id=p11#p11)) — mixing truth with lie. They *would* gain knowledge. But the promise of becoming "as gods" through disobedience is the counterfeit of a real doctrine.

4. **Make it look desirable** — "the woman saw that the tree was good for food, and that it became pleasant to the eyes" ([Moses 4:12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/4?lang=eng&id=p12#p12)) — the fruit appealed to legitimate desires

This sequence — question, contradict, promise, beautify — is the anatomy of all beguiling.

### What Moses 4:4 Tells Us About the Result

> "He became Satan, yea, even the devil, the **father of all lies**, to **deceive** and to **blind** men, and to lead them **captive** at his will" — [Moses 4:4](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/4?lang=eng&id=p4#p4)

Four verbs: **lie**, **deceive**, **blind**, **capture**. This is the progression. Craft doesn't stop at deception — it moves toward captivity. The endgame of guile is always to "lead captive at his will" — the erasure of agency that Satan proposed from the beginning.

## Christ: The Guileless One

If the serpent's defining characteristic is **guile**, then Christ's defining characteristic is its total absence. The scriptures draw this contrast with unmistakable precision:

> "Who did no sin, neither was **guile** found in his mouth" — [1 Peter 2:22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/1-pet/2?lang=eng&id=p22#p22)

> "In their mouth was found **no guile**: for they are without fault before the throne of God" — [Revelation 14:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/rev/14?lang=eng&id=p5#p5) (of the 144,000)

> "Behold an Israelite indeed, in whom is **no guile**!" — [John 1:47](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/1?lang=eng&id=p47#p47) (Jesus of Nathanael — His highest compliment)

> "Blessed is the man unto whom the Lord imputeth not iniquity, and in whose spirit there is **no guile**" — [Psalm 32:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/ps/32?lang=eng&id=p2#p2)

### What "No Guile" Means in Webster 1828

Since guile = craft, "no guile" literally means **no craft**. No cunning. No artifice. No ulterior motive. No hidden agenda. Complete transparency of intent.

When Jesus looked at Nathanael and said "in whom is no guile" — He was saying: *Here is a man with no craft in him. What you see is what there is. He has no subtlety, no hidden design.*

That's the highest thing the Lord says about a person in all of John's Gospel. Not "in whom is great faith" or "great knowledge" — but "**no guile**."

### The Lord Uses This Same Marker in the Doctrine and Covenants

> "His heart is pure before me, for he is like unto **Nathanael** of old, in whom there is **no guile**" — [D&C 41:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/41?lang=eng&id=p11#p11) (of Edward Partridge)

> "My servant George Miller is **without guile**; he may be trusted because of the **integrity** of his heart" — [D&C 124:20](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/124?lang=eng&id=p20#p20)

Notice: guilelessness is linked to **trust**. "He may be trusted *because of* the integrity of his heart." Where there is no craft, there is trust. Where there is craft, trust erodes.

### The Priesthood Standard

> "No power or influence can or ought to be maintained by virtue of the priesthood, only by persuasion, by long-suffering, by gentleness and meekness, and by love unfeigned; by kindness, and pure knowledge, which shall greatly enlarge the soul **without hypocrisy, and without guile**" — [D&C 121:41–42](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/121?lang=eng&id=p41-p42#p41)

"Without guile" — without *craft*. The priesthood standard is the anti-serpent standard. Every quality listed here (persuasion, long-suffering, gentleness, meekness, love unfeigned, kindness, pure knowledge) is the opposite of what the serpent used in the Garden.

| The Serpent's Method | The Priesthood Standard |
|---------------------|------------------------|
| Subtle / insinuating | Persuasion / direct |
| Contradicted God | Pure knowledge |
| Made promises to get compliance | Love unfeigned |
| Motivated by honor and power | Without hypocrisy |
| Captured agency | Without compulsory means |
| Operated through guile | **Without guile** |

D&C 121 is *literally written as the mirror image of the serpent's method*.

## The Serpent Pattern Continues: Paul's Warning

Paul saw the Garden pattern replaying in real time among the churches:

> "But I fear, lest by any means, as the serpent **beguiled** Eve through his **subtilty**, so your minds should be **corrupted** from the **simplicity** that is in Christ" — [2 Corinthians 11:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/2-cor/11?lang=eng&id=p3#p3)

Three things to notice:

1. **Paul directly connects the Garden to his present day.** The serpent's beguiling is not just a historical event — it's a *pattern*.

2. **"Subtilty" = craftiness = guile.** The serpent's subtlety is the same word family. The technique hasn't changed.

3. **The target is "simplicity."** Christ's gospel is simple. Beguiling **corrupts from simplicity** — it complicates, adds layers, introduces sophistication that pulls away from the plain truth. Craft makes simple things complex.

Then Paul identifies the modern serpents:

> "For such are **false apostles**, deceitful workers, **transforming themselves** into the apostles of Christ. And no marvel; for **Satan himself is transformed into an angel of light**. Therefore it is no great thing if his ministers also be transformed as the ministers of righteousness" — [2 Corinthians 11:13–15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/2-cor/11?lang=eng&id=p13-p15#p13)

The key word is **transformed** — disguised. Satan and his ministers don't appear as what they are. They appear as ministers of righteousness. As angels of light. The craft is in the *costume*.

### An Ironic Echo

Paul, defending himself against accusations of manipulation, says sarcastically:

> "Being **crafty**, I caught you with **guile**" — [2 Corinthians 12:16](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/2-cor/12?lang=eng&id=p16#p16)

He's quoting his accusers back at them. They called *Paul* crafty. But Paul preached "the gospel of God freely" ([2 Corinthians 11:7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/2-cor/11?lang=eng&id=p7#p7)) — he specifically refused payment. The real "craft" was coming from the false apostles who were charging for their ministry. Priestcraft accuses faithfulness of being crafty.

## Jacob's Extension: Beguiling → Angel of Light → Secret Combinations

Jacob takes Paul's insight and extends the chain further:

> "to that being who **beguiled** our first parents, who **transformeth** himself nigh unto an **angel of light**, and stirreth up the children of men unto **secret combinations** of murder and all manner of secret works of darkness" — [2 Nephi 9:9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/9?lang=eng&id=p9#p9)

Three stages: **beguile** → **transform** → **organize**. From individual deception to collective corruption. From craft to combination.

This is the same progression Moroni sees:

> "whoso buildeth it up seeketh to overthrow the freedom of all lands, nations, and countries; and it bringeth to pass the destruction of all people, for it is built up by the devil, who is the father of all lies; even that same liar who **beguiled** our first parents" — [Ether 8:25](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/ether/8?lang=eng&id=p25#p25)

Moroni traces the thread all the way from the Garden to the Jaredite destruction. Same word: **beguiled**. Same person: "that same liar." Same method: craft. But now it's *institutionalized*: "built up to get **power and gain**" ([Ether 8:22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/ether/8?lang=eng&id=p22#p22)). Secret combinations are **organized guile** — priestcraft at scale.

## Nephi's Taxonomy: Three Methods of the Craftsman

In [2 Nephi 28](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/28?lang=eng), Nephi gives us Satan's three operational modes — three faces of craft:

### 1. RAGE (v. 20)
> "he shall **rage** in the hearts of the children of men, and stir them up to anger against that which is good"

Direct, hot, obvious. Anger. Opposition. Persecution. This is the least subtle form of guile — and the one most people recognize.

### 2. LULL (v. 21)
> "others will he **pacify**, and **lull** them away into carnal security, that they will say: All is well in Zion… thus the devil **cheateth** their souls, and leadeth them away **carefully** down to hell"

The word **carefully** is devastating. This isn't a fall — it's a *guided descent*. Comfortable. Gradual. The craft here is in the pacing. No alarm bells. No sense of danger. Just slow, comfortable movement in the wrong direction.

President Faust described exactly this: "Much of what comes from the devil is **alluring** and **enticing**. It glitters and is appealing… His message sounds so **reasonable** and easy to justify. His voice is usually **smooth** and **intriguing**." ([President Faust, "The Devil's Throat," April 2003](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2003/04/the-devils-throat?lang=eng))

### 3. FLATTER (v. 22)
> "others he **flattereth** away, and telleth them there is **no hell**; and he saith: **I am no devil**, for there is none"

The deepest craft: **deny the existence of craft**. Tell them there is no devil. Tell them there is no danger. If you can convince someone the craftsman doesn't exist, the craft becomes invisible.

Elder Oaks warned: "Many in the world deny the need for a Savior. Others deny that there is any right or wrong, and they scoff at the idea of sin or a devil." ([Elder Oaks, "Be Not Deceived," October 2004](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2004/10/be-not-deceived?lang=eng))

## D&C 10: Satan's "Cunning Plan"

In [D&C 10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/10?lang=eng), the Lord uses the exact word family — **cunning** — to describe Satan's attempt to destroy the Book of Mormon translation:

> "the devil has sought to lay a **cunning** plan, that he may destroy this work" (v. 12)

> "he has laid a **cunning** plan, thinking to destroy the work of God" (v. 23)

> "he saith unto them: **Deceive** and lie in wait to catch… this is no harm. And thus he **flattereth** them" (v. 25)

Webster 1828: **CUNNING** — "crafty; sly; artful; designed; deceitful."

Cunning = crafty = guile. Same word. Same spirit. Applied here to a very specific, deliberate plan to undermine the work of God through people who would "alter the words" of revelation. The method? Flattery: "this is no harm."

## The Armor Against Wiles

> "Put on the whole armour of God, that ye may be able to stand against the **wiles** of the devil" — [Ephesians 6:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/eph/6?lang=eng&id=p11#p11)

Webster 1828: **WILE** — "A trick or stratagem practiced for ensnaring or deception; a sly, insidious artifice; a **beguilement**; an allurement."

A wile is a *beguilement*. The armor of God is armor against *beguiling*. Every piece of the armor Paul describes — truth, righteousness, the gospel of peace, faith, salvation, the word of God — is a defense against the specific qualities of the serpent's craft.

And notice: the solution is not cleverness or counter-craft. It's armor. You don't out-guile the guileful. You put on truth, righteousness, faith. You become guileless, and that *is* the defense.

## The Ammon Question: Is All Guile Evil?

One passage creates a fascinating tension:

> "Now Ammon being **wise**, yet **harmless**, he said unto Lamoni: Wilt thou hearken unto my words, if I tell thee by what power I do these things? And the king answered him, and said: Yea, I will believe all thy words. And thus he was **caught with guile**." — [Alma 18:22–23](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/18?lang=eng&id=p22-p23#p22)

Lamoni was "caught with guile" — but this is clearly a *good* thing. Ammon wasn't deceiving Lamoni. He was using strategic wisdom to open a door for truth. This seems to stand in tension with the scriptures that consistently condemn guile.

But look at the descriptors: "**wise**, yet **harmless**." Ammon's wisdom had no harmful intent. His "guile" wasn't deception — it was *skillful* communication that met Lamoni where he was. He served first (the flocks), earned trust through action, then asked a question instead of launching into a sermon.

Compare to the serpent:
- Serpent: subtle, seeking to destroy → **harmful guile**
- Ammon: wise, yet harmless, seeking to save → **harmless wisdom**

The difference isn't technique — it's *motive and direction*. Are you pointing toward the Savior or toward yourself? Are you seeking to liberate or to capture? The serpent beguiled Eve to destroy; Ammon "caught" Lamoni to save. Same word, opposite spirit.

This echoes what Paul told the Corinthians sarcastically ([2 Corinthians 12:16](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/2-cor/12?lang=eng&id=p16#p16)) — his opponents called him "crafty," but his craft was preaching the gospel *for free*. Being wise in how you share truth is not the same as being guileful in how you hide lies.

### The Diagnostic

The priestcraft study offered a diagnostic for when something becomes priestcraft. Here's the parallel diagnostic for guile — because they're the same thing:

**Am I operating with craft or without it?**

- Am I being direct, or strategic in a way that serves my interests?
- Am I presenting things as they are, or beautifying them for an effect?
- Am I pointing toward the Savior, or toward my own cleverness?
- Could I do this with complete transparency and still feel good about it?
- Would Jesus call me a person "in whom there is no guile"?

## The Thread That Runs Through Everything

What started with a word study reveals a theological architecture:

1. **The serpent was "subtle"** (crafty) — [Genesis 3:1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/3?lang=eng&id=p1#p1), [Moses 4:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/4?lang=eng&id=p5#p5)
2. **He "beguiled" Eve** (deluded by craft/guile) — [Genesis 3:13](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/3?lang=eng&id=p13#p13), [Moses 4:6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/4?lang=eng&id=p6#p6)
3. **His method became a pattern** — Paul saw it in Corinth ([2 Corinthians 11:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/2-cor/11?lang=eng&id=p3#p3))
4. **The pattern institutionalized** into secret combinations — Moroni traced it from the Garden ([Ether 8:25](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/ether/8?lang=eng&id=p25#p25))
5. **It operates today** through rage, lulling, and flattery — Nephi mapped the three modes ([2 Nephi 28:20–22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/28?lang=eng&id=p20-p22#p20))
6. **Christ is its total opposite** — no guile, no craft, no subtlety, no hidden design ([1 Peter 2:22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/1-pet/2?lang=eng&id=p22#p22))
7. **His priesthood operates the same way** — "without guile" = literally "without craft" ([D&C 121:42](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/121?lang=eng&id=p42#p42))
8. **His highest compliment** for a mortal is guilelessness — "in whom is no guile" ([John 1:47](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/1?lang=eng&id=p47#p47))

The suffix in "priestcraft" is not an accident. It is a warning. Craft is guile is beguiling is the serpent. To practice priestcraft is to practice the serpent's method in priest's clothing — which is exactly what Paul saw in Corinth: Satan "transformed into an angel of light" ([2 Corinthians 11:14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/2-cor/11?lang=eng&id=p14#p14)).

And the antidote is not counter-craft or cleverness. It's what Peter, quoting Isaiah, says we should do:

> "Laying aside all malice, and all **guile**, and hypocrisies" — [1 Peter 2:1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/1-pet/2?lang=eng&id=p1#p1)

Lay it aside. Put on the armor. Become guileless. Point to the Savior.

> *"Behold an Israelite indeed, in whom is no guile!"* — The Lord

## Becoming

The diagnostic above asks the questions. Here's where they land:

**Questions to sit with:**

- The guile spectrum runs from subtle to beguile. Where do I live on it? Not in big dramatic deceptions — in the small ones. The email I wordsmith to get a particular reaction. The story I tell with just enough spin. The omission that serves my interests.
- Nathanael's highest commendation was guilelessness. If Jesus summarized me in one sentence, would "without guile" be in it?
- Nephi's three modes — rage, lull, flatter ([2 Nephi 28:20–22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/28?lang=eng&id=p20-p22#p20)) — which one am I most susceptible to? Not which one I *use*, but which one *works on me*?

**This week:**

- Notice one moment each day where I'm tempted toward craft — strategic framing, beautifying for effect, positioning myself. When I catch it, choose directness instead. Not bluntness — Ammon-style wisdom paired with harmlessness.
