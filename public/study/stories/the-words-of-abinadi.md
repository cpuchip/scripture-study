# The Words of Abinadi

*A story from [Mosiah 11–18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/11?lang=eng) and [Alma 36](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/36?lang=eng)*
*Born from: [abinadi-how-to-read.md](../abinadi-how-to-read.md)*

---

The seats were gold.

King Noah had replaced every priest his father consecrated with men "lifted up in the pride of their hearts" ([Mosiah 11:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/11?lang=eng&id=p5#p5)). He built them elevated seats ornamented with pure gold and a breastwork to lean against while they spoke ([Mosiah 11:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/11?lang=eng&id=p11#p11)). He planted vineyards and built wine-presses until he became "a wine-bibber, and also his people" ([Mosiah 11:15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/11?lang=eng&id=p15#p15)). He taxed every family a fifth of everything they had to pay for it ([Mosiah 11:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/11?lang=eng&id=p3#p3)).

So the people labored to support iniquity. And because they labored to support it, they believed it. "The eyes of the people were blinded; therefore they hardened their hearts against the words of Abinadi" ([Mosiah 11:29](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/11?lang=eng&id=p29#p29)).

Blinded first. Hardened second. That sequence matters.

---

A man named Abinadi came among them and prophesied: repent, or the Lord will deliver you into bondage ([Mosiah 11:20-21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/11?lang=eng&id=p20-p21#p20)).

They tried to kill him. The Lord delivered him out of their hands.

But God sent him back.

Two years later, Abinadi returned in disguise. "They knew him not" ([Mosiah 12:1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/12?lang=eng&id=p1#p1)). His message was worse this time. Bondage. Pestilence. Famine. Vultures devouring their flesh. Noah's life "valued even as a garment in a hot furnace" ([Mosiah 12:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/12?lang=eng&id=p3#p3)).

So they seized him. Carried him bound before the king.

---

The priests gathered to interrogate him. Not to understand. To accuse. They "began to question him, that they might cross him, that thereby they might have wherewith to accuse him" ([Mosiah 12:19](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/12?lang=eng&id=p19#p19)).

The question they chose was Isaiah 52:7: "How beautiful upon the mountains are the feet of him that bringeth good tidings; that publisheth peace" ([Mosiah 12:21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/12?lang=eng&id=p21#p21)).

They quoted it from memory. Every word perfect. Men who spoke "lying and vain words" ([Mosiah 11:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/11?lang=eng&id=p11#p11)) from gold seats, asking a bound prisoner to explain the passage about publishing truth.

Abinadi turned it back on them.

"Are you priests, and pretend to teach this people, and to understand the spirit of prophesying, and yet desire to know of me what these things mean?" ([Mosiah 12:25](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/12?lang=eng&id=p25#p25))

"Ye have not applied your hearts to understanding; therefore, ye have not been wise." ([Mosiah 12:27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/12?lang=eng&id=p27#p27))

Not your *minds*. Your *hearts*.

---

The king ordered him killed. "Away with this fellow, and slay him; for he is mad" ([Mosiah 13:1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p1#p1)).

They stood up to lay hands on him.

But they couldn't.

"Touch me not, for God shall smite you if ye lay your hands upon me, for I have not delivered the message which the Lord sent me to deliver." ([Mosiah 13:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p3#p3))

His face shone "with exceeding luster, even as Moses' did while in the mount of Sinai, while speaking with the Lord" ([Mosiah 13:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p5#p5)).

Moses' face shone when he *received* the law. Abinadi's face shone when he *read* it.

So he read.

He recited the Ten Commandments to the priests who were supposed to teach them. "I perceive that they are not written in your hearts; I perceive that ye have studied and taught iniquity the most part of your lives" ([Mosiah 13:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p11#p11)).

He told them the law was "types of things to come" ([Mosiah 13:31](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p31#p31)). But they couldn't see it, "because of the hardness of their hearts; for they understood not that there could not any man be saved except it were through the redemption of God" ([Mosiah 13:32](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p32#p32)).

So he showed them.

He read Isaiah 53 in its entirety. The suffering servant. God himself, coming down, taking on flesh, bearing their sins. He walked through the whole passage until the question the priests had asked back in chapter 12 finally got its answer.

The prophets who publish peace? They are Christ's seed. "These are they who have published peace, who have brought good tidings of good, who have published salvation; and said unto Zion: Thy God reigneth!" ([Mosiah 15:14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p14#p14))

Past prophets ([Mosiah 15:15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p15#p15)). Present believers ([Mosiah 15:16](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p16#p16)). Future publishers ([Mosiah 15:17](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p17#p17)). And Christ himself, "the founder of peace" ([Mosiah 15:18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p18#p18)). One Isaiah passage, read through every era at once. Their test question, answered with the entire gospel.

And then this:

"But this much I tell you, what you do with me, after this, shall be as a type and a shadow of things which are to come." ([Mosiah 13:10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p10#p10))

He was reading his own death. The same way he read Isaiah. The man with the shining face, standing bound in front of men who would kill him, told them the killing itself would shadow Christ.

"And then it matters not whither I go, if it so be that I am saved." ([Mosiah 13:9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p9#p9))

---

King Noah was about to release him.

"He feared his word; for he feared that the judgments of God would come upon him." ([Mosiah 17:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/17?lang=eng&id=p11#p11))

But the priests would not allow it.

"The priests lifted up their voices against him, and began to accuse him." ([Mosiah 17:12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/17?lang=eng&id=p12#p12))

Their accusation: "Thou hast said that God himself should come down among the children of men." ([Mosiah 17:8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/17?lang=eng&id=p8#p8))

The truth he taught became the charge against him.

Three days in prison. Then the offer: take it back, and live.

"I will not recall the words which I have spoken unto you concerning this people, for they are true; and that ye may know of their surety I have suffered myself that I have fallen into your hands." ([Mosiah 17:9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/17?lang=eng&id=p9#p9))

So they bound him. They scourged his skin with faggots until the flames began to scorch him. From inside the fire, he prophesied that their own seed would suffer the same pains ([Mosiah 17:15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/17?lang=eng&id=p15#p15)).

Then five words.

"O God, receive my soul." ([Mosiah 17:19](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/17?lang=eng&id=p19#p19))

He fell.

"Having sealed the truth of his words by his death." ([Mosiah 17:20](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/17?lang=eng&id=p20#p20))

---

But one man had believed him.

Before the fire. Before the sentencing. While Abinadi was still speaking, "there was one among them whose name was Alma" ([Mosiah 17:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/17?lang=eng&id=p2#p2)). He was young. He believed not because he was smarter than the other priests but because "he knew concerning the iniquity which Abinadi had testified against them" ([Mosiah 17:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/17?lang=eng&id=p2#p2)). He was honest about what he already knew.

So he pleaded with Noah to let Abinadi go.

But Noah tried to kill him instead.

So Alma ran. And because he ran, he lived. And because he lived, he did something that changed everything after.

"He being concealed for many days did write all the words which Abinadi had spoken." ([Mosiah 17:4](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/17?lang=eng&id=p4#p4))

Alone. Hiding in the hill country. Writing by whatever light he could find. The words of a man now dead. Every argument, every reading, every line of Isaiah, the shining face, the typological reading of his own execution. All of it, in a fugitive's handwriting.

Without those pages, the reading dies with the reader. Everything that follows next never happens.

---

But the pages survived. So Alma taught.

He found a fountain of pure water, near a thicket of small trees ([Mosiah 18:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/18?lang=eng&id=p5#p5)). He taught "the words of Abinadi" ([Mosiah 18:1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/18?lang=eng&id=p1#p1)). Not his own words. A dead man's. People came.

And when enough had gathered, Alma offered them something that looked nothing like what they had left behind. Not gold seats. Not taxation. Not flattery. A covenant:

"As ye are desirous to come into the fold of God, and to be called his people, and are willing to bear one another's burdens, that they may be light; yea, and are willing to mourn with those that mourn; yea, and comfort those that stand in need of comfort, and to stand as witnesses of God at all times and in all things, and in all places that ye may be in, even until death." ([Mosiah 18:8-9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/18?lang=eng&id=p8-p9#p8))

They clapped their hands for joy ([Mosiah 18:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/18?lang=eng&id=p11#p11)).

Two hundred and four souls. Pure water. Small trees. Priests who labored with their own hands and received "the grace of God, that they might wax strong in the Spirit" ([Mosiah 18:26](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/18?lang=eng&id=p26#p26)) instead of a salary.

"The place of Mormon, the waters of Mormon, the forest of Mormon, how beautiful are they to the eyes of them who there came to the knowledge of their Redeemer; yea, and how blessed are they, for they shall sing to his praise forever." ([Mosiah 18:30](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/18?lang=eng&id=p30#p30))

---

Seventy-four years passed.

A young man named for his father lay on the ground. He couldn't move. He couldn't speak. For three days and three nights he was "racked, even with the pains of a damned soul" ([Alma 36:16](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/36?lang=eng&id=p16#p16)).

He had been trying to destroy the church his father built from Abinadi's words. An angel stopped him. But the angel did not save him. It left him broken, remembering every person he had led away from God ([Alma 36:14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/36?lang=eng&id=p14#p14)).

Nothing could reach him.

But something did.

"I remembered also to have heard my father prophesy unto the people concerning the coming of one Jesus Christ, a Son of God, to atone for the sins of the world." ([Alma 36:17](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/36?lang=eng&id=p17#p17))

His father's voice. Teaching what Abinadi taught. About a God who would come down.

"Now, as my mind caught hold upon this thought, I cried within my heart: O Jesus, thou Son of God, have mercy on me." ([Alma 36:18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/36?lang=eng&id=p18#p18))

The pain stopped. The joy that replaced it was "as exceeding as was my pain" ([Alma 36:21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/36?lang=eng&id=p21#p21)).

---

Abinadi read Isaiah in a room of men who wanted him dead. He told them the killing itself would shadow Christ. They killed him.

But one man wrote it down.

So the words survived. So Alma could teach them at a fountain. So two hundred and four people could enter a covenant. So a church could form. So that church could survive bondage and exile and a wilderness crossing back to Zarahemla.

And when Alma's son was drowning in three days of darkness, the one thing that reached him was a voice he had once heard. His father. Teaching what a dead man said. About a God who would come down and bear what they bore. Whose soul would be made an offering.

One reading of Isaiah. One fire. One man who wrote it down. One fountain.

And then, three generations later, one cry: *O Jesus, thou Son of God, have mercy on me.*

"How beautiful upon the mountains are the feet of those who shall hereafter publish peace, yea, from this time henceforth and forever!" ([Mosiah 15:17](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p17#p17))
