# The Words of Abinadi

*A story from [Mosiah 11–18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/11?lang=eng) and [Alma 36](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/36?lang=eng)*
*Born from: [abinadi-how-to-read.md](../abinadi-how-to-read.md)*

---

The seats were gold.

Not gilded — ornamented with pure gold, elevated above the rest of the room, with a breastwork built in front so a man could rest his arms while he spoke. The architects had thought of everything. You could lean against that breastwork and talk for hours without tiring. You could say anything from those seats and the gold would make it sound authoritative. King Noah had commissioned them himself when he replaced his father's priests with men more to his liking — men "lifted up in the pride of their hearts" ([Mosiah 11:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/11?lang=eng&id=p5#p5)).

The wine helped, too. Noah had planted vineyards across the land and built wine-presses, and he had become, the record says without flinching, "a wine-bibber, and also his people" ([Mosiah 11:15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/11?lang=eng&id=p15#p15)). His priests spent their time with harlots ([Mosiah 11:14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/11?lang=eng&id=p14#p14)). A fifth of everything the people earned went to support this arrangement ([Mosiah 11:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/11?lang=eng&id=p3#p3)). Comfortable seats. Good wine. Someone else paying for it. The people labored exceedingly to support iniquity, and the priests spoke "lying and vain words" from behind that golden breastwork ([Mosiah 11:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/11?lang=eng&id=p11#p11)).

This is the room a man walked into, alone, to read Isaiah.

---

He had tried once before. Two years earlier, Abinadi had come among them and prophesied plainly: repent, or the Lord will deliver you into bondage ([Mosiah 11:20-21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/11?lang=eng&id=p20-p21#p20)). They tried to kill him. The Lord delivered him out of their hands, and he vanished.

Two years.

Then he came back in disguise. The text says "they knew him not" ([Mosiah 12:1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/12?lang=eng&id=p1#p1)). Whatever he looked like when he left, he looked different now. Maybe older. Maybe thinner. Maybe just harder in the way a man gets when he's spent two years knowing he has to go back to the place that wants him dead.

His message was worse this time. Not "repent or bondage" — this time: bondage, pestilence, famine, hail, insects, destruction. Their flesh devoured by vultures. Noah's life "valued even as a garment in a hot furnace" ([Mosiah 12:2-3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/12?lang=eng&id=p2-p3#p2)). They seized him. Carried him bound before the king.

The priests gathered to interrogate him.

Their goal was accusation, not understanding. They "began to question him, that they might cross him, that thereby they might have wherewith to accuse him" ([Mosiah 12:19](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/12?lang=eng&id=p19#p19)). And the question they chose was Isaiah 52:7 — "How beautiful upon the mountains are the feet of him that bringeth good tidings; that publisheth peace" ([Mosiah 12:21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/12?lang=eng&id=p21#p21)). They quoted it from memory, every word perfect.

The irony is devastating. Men who spoke "lying and vain words" from gold seats asked a bound prisoner to explain the passage about publishing truth.

Abinadi turned the question back on them:

"Are you priests, and pretend to teach this people, and to understand the spirit of prophesying, and yet desire to know of me what these things mean?" ([Mosiah 12:25](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/12?lang=eng&id=p25#p25))

Then the indictment:

"Ye have not applied your hearts to understanding; therefore, ye have not been wise." ([Mosiah 12:27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/12?lang=eng&id=p27#p27))

Not your *minds* — your *hearts*. They knew the words. They could quote Isaiah cold. But the knowing was in the wrong organ.

---

What happened next should have been impossible.

The king said: "Away with this fellow, and slay him; for he is mad" ([Mosiah 13:1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p1#p1)). They stood up to lay hands on him.

He stopped them.

"Touch me not, for God shall smite you if ye lay your hands upon me, for I have not delivered the message which the Lord sent me to deliver" ([Mosiah 13:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p3#p3)).

They didn't touch him. The Spirit of the Lord was on him, and "his face shone with exceeding luster, even as Moses' did while in the mount of Sinai, while speaking with the Lord" ([Mosiah 13:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p5#p5)).

Moses' face shone when he received the law. Abinadi's face shone when he *read* it. The parallel says something about what scripture study actually is, when done with the right organ.

And from that shining face, he recited the Ten Commandments — to priests. Men who were supposed to teach them. "I perceive that they are not written in your hearts; I perceive that ye have studied and taught iniquity the most part of your lives" ([Mosiah 13:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p11#p11)).

Then he read Isaiah 53 in its entirety. The suffering servant. God himself, coming down among men, taking on flesh, bearing their transgressions. He walked through the whole passage and came out the other side with a reading so layered that the passage the priests had quoted back in chapter 12 — "How beautiful upon the mountains" — finally got its answer.

The prophets who publish peace? They are Christ's seed. "These are they who have published peace, who have brought good tidings of good, who have published salvation; and said unto Zion: Thy God reigneth!" ([Mosiah 15:14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p14#p14))

Past prophets. Present believers. Future publishers. And Christ himself, "the founder of peace" ([Mosiah 15:18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p18#p18)). One Isaiah passage, read through every era at once. He answered their test question with the entire gospel.

And somewhere in the middle of all this, a line the tradition doesn't talk about:

"But this much I tell you, what you do with me, after this, shall be as a type and a shadow of things which are to come." ([Mosiah 13:10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p10#p10))

He read his own death typologically. Applied the same hermeneutic to his life that he applied to Isaiah. The man standing in that room, face shining, bound, already knew what they would do to him. And he told them the killing itself would shadow Christ.

Then he finished.

"And then it matters not whither I go, if it so be that I am saved." ([Mosiah 13:9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p9#p9))

---

King Noah was about to let him go.

This detail is easy to miss. "King Noah was about to release him, for he feared his word; for he feared that the judgments of God would come upon him" ([Mosiah 17:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/17?lang=eng&id=p11#p11)). The king *heard* something. Whatever Abinadi said, it got past the wine and the gold and the pride and landed somewhere Noah didn't want it to land. For a moment, fear outweighed power.

But the priests.

"The priests lifted up their voices against him, and began to accuse him" ([Mosiah 17:12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/17?lang=eng&id=p12#p12)). And their accusation was the doctrine itself: "Thou hast said that God himself should come down among the children of men" ([Mosiah 17:8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/17?lang=eng&id=p8#p8)). The truth he taught became the legal basis for his execution.

Three days in prison. Then the offer: recant, and live.

"I will not recall the words which I have spoken unto you concerning this people, for they are true; and that ye may know of their surety I have suffered myself that I have fallen into your hands." ([Mosiah 17:9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/17?lang=eng&id=p9#p9))

They bound him. They scourged his skin with faggots. When the flames began to scorch him, he prophesied one last time — their seed would suffer as he suffered, and they would be hunted and driven and scattered ([Mosiah 17:15-18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/17?lang=eng&id=p15-p18#p15)).

Then five words:

"O God, receive my soul." ([Mosiah 17:19](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/17?lang=eng&id=p19#p19))

And he fell. "Having sealed the truth of his words by his death" ([Mosiah 17:20](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/17?lang=eng&id=p20#p20)).

---

One man ran.

Before the fire. Before the sentence. Before the priests lifted up their voices and forced the execution. When Abinadi was still speaking, "there was one among them whose name was Alma" ([Mosiah 17:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/17?lang=eng&id=p2#p2)).

He was young. The record says so. And he believed — not because he was smarter than the other priests, but because "he knew concerning the iniquity which Abinadi had testified against them" ([Mosiah 17:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/17?lang=eng&id=p2#p2)). He was honest about what he already knew. His heart was in the right place, so the reading worked.

He pleaded with Noah to let Abinadi go. Noah tried to kill him instead. Alma fled.

And then this:

"He being concealed for many days did write all the words which Abinadi had spoken." ([Mosiah 17:4](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/17?lang=eng&id=p4#p4))

Imagine him. Hiding somewhere in the hill country north of Shilom, alone, writing by whatever light he could manage. The words of a dead man. Every argument, every reading, every line of Isaiah, the shining face, the typological reading of his own death — all of it, preserved by a fugitive's hand on whatever surface he could find.

Without this act, Abinadi's hermeneutic dies with him. The reading disappears into a room of men who didn't want to hear it. The whole subsequent history — the waters of Mormon, the church of Christ, the conversion of Alma's son, the entire second half of the Book of Mormon — none of it happens if this young man doesn't sit in the wilderness and write.

---

He found a place. A fountain of pure water, near a thicket of small trees ([Mosiah 18:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/18?lang=eng&id=p5#p5)). He hid in the daytime and taught privately to anyone who would listen. He taught "the words of Abinadi" ([Mosiah 18:1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/18?lang=eng&id=p1#p1)). Not his own words yet. He was still passing on what he had received.

People came. And when enough had gathered at the waters of Mormon, Alma offered a covenant — not a system, not gold seats, not taxation. Something entirely different:

"As ye are desirous to come into the fold of God, and to be called his people, and are willing to bear one another's burdens, that they may be light; yea, and are willing to mourn with those that mourn; yea, and comfort those that stand in need of comfort, and to stand as witnesses of God at all times and in all things, and in all places that ye may be in, even until death" ([Mosiah 18:8-9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/18?lang=eng&id=p8-p9#p8))

They clapped their hands for joy ([Mosiah 18:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/18?lang=eng&id=p11#p11)). Two hundred and four souls, baptized in pure water, under small trees, in the borders of the land. And the priests Alma ordained were not to depend on the people for support — they were to labor with their own hands, and "for their labor they were to receive the grace of God" ([Mosiah 18:26](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/18?lang=eng&id=p26#p26)).

Gold seats traded for a thicket of small trees. Taxation for voluntary generosity. Flattery for mourning with those who mourn. Everything inverted. Everything Abinadi's reading demanded, made concrete.

The record pauses here. Almost tenderly:

"The place of Mormon, the waters of Mormon, the forest of Mormon, how beautiful are they to the eyes of them who there came to the knowledge of their Redeemer; yea, and how blessed are they, for they shall sing to his praise forever." ([Mosiah 18:30](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/18?lang=eng&id=p30#p30))

---

Seventy-four years later.

A young man named for his father — Alma — lay on the ground. He couldn't move. He couldn't speak. For three days and three nights he was "racked, even with the pains of a damned soul" ([Alma 36:16](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/36?lang=eng&id=p16#p16)). He had been trying to destroy the very church his father built from Abinadi's words. An angel had stopped him, and now nothing could reach him. Every sin, every person he had led away, every commandment broken — all of it present, all at once.

And then something did reach him.

"I remembered also to have heard my father prophesy unto the people concerning the coming of one Jesus Christ, a Son of God, to atone for the sins of the world." ([Alma 36:17](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/36?lang=eng&id=p17#p17))

His father. Prophesying to the people. Teaching what Abinadi taught.

"Now, as my mind caught hold upon this thought, I cried within my heart: O Jesus, thou Son of God, have mercy on me." ([Alma 36:18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/36?lang=eng&id=p18#p18))

And the pain stopped. The joy that replaced it was "as exceeding as was my pain" ([Alma 36:21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/36?lang=eng&id=p21#p21)).

---

Trace the chain.

Abinadi read Isaiah 53 in a room of men who wanted him dead. He read it with his heart applied to understanding, and with his face shining like Moses', and he told them every prophet since the world began had spoken of Christ. He read his own death as a type and shadow. He died in that reading. His words, sealed by fire, would have vanished from the earth.

Except one young man wrote them down.

Alma taught the words of Abinadi at the waters of Mormon. Those words baptized two hundred and four people. Those people became a church. That church survived exile, bondage, and a wilderness journey back to Zarahemla.

And when Alma's son was racked with the pains of hell, drowning in the memory of everything he had destroyed — the one thing that reached him, across three days of silence and darkness, was a voice he had once heard. His father's voice. Teaching what a dead man said. About a God who would come down. Who would bear what they bore. Whose soul would be made an offering.

One reading of Isaiah. One death. One man who wrote it down. One fountain of pure water. And then, generations later, a young man on the ground, remembering — and crying out the same name Abinadi cried from the flames.

"How beautiful upon the mountains are the feet of those who shall hereafter publish peace, yea, from this time henceforth and forever!" ([Mosiah 15:17](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p17#p17))
