# Mormon 8 — The Book That Speaks of Its Own Coming Forth

*A verse-by-verse study of Moroni's first words, and how they interlock with
Isaiah's voice from the dust, Nephi's handoff to John, and the 1828–29
translation history.*

## The binding question

Mormon 8 is hard to follow because Moroni's referents keep moving. "They,"
"it," "he," "ye" each change antecedent two or three times, and the chapter
swings from a lonely man burying plates to a thunderous indictment of people not
yet born. Once you can track who and what each pronoun points to, the chapter
resolves into something specific: **this is the Book of Mormon prophesying its
own arrival, written by the man who buried it.** Everything else — the dust, the
witnesses, the wicked churches, the reference to John's revelations — hangs on
that frame.

## Who is speaking, and when

Chapter 8 is the seam where the book changes hands. Mormon, the great
abridger, has just finished his own farewell in chapter 7, addressed to the
latter-day remnant: lay hold on the gospel "not only in this record but also in
the record which shall come unto the Gentiles from the Jews" — the Bible — "and
if ye believe that ye will believe this also" ([Mormon 7:8–9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/morm/7?lang=eng&id=p8-p9#p8)).
That is the hinge the whole rest of the book turns on: the two records
witnessing for each other.

Then Mormon dies, and his son Moroni picks up the stylus. "Behold I, Moroni, do
finish the record of my father, Mormon" ([Mormon 8:1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/morm/8?lang=eng&id=p1#p1)).
These are the first words of the man who will spend the next thirty-plus years
alone, add the book of Ether and his own book of Moroni, and finally bury the
plates in the hill where Joseph Smith will dig them up. He does not know yet
that he has decades left. He thinks he has "but few things to write" (v. 1). The
loneliness in these verses is not literary; it is a man writing because there is
no one left to talk to.

## The referent map (read this first)

The single most useful thing for following the chapter is a map of what each
pronoun points to. The chapter moves through five referents:

| Verses | "it / they / he / ye" refers to |
|--------|-------------------------------|
| 1–9 | The destroyed **Nephites** and the **Lamanites** hunting the survivors |
| 10–11 | The **Three Nephite disciples** (3 Nephi 28) |
| 12–22 | The **record itself** coming forth, the **translator** who brings it, and the **enemies** who attack it |
| 23–25 | The **dead Nephites** crying from the dust, and again the **translator** |
| 26–41 | **Our day** — the day the book emerges — and **us**, its latter-day readers |

Hold that table next to the text and the fog lifts. Now walk it.

## Movement I — The last man (vv. 1–9)

Moroni records the end with terrible economy. After "the great and tremendous
battle at Cumorah," the surviving Nephites "were hunted by the Lamanites, until
they were all destroyed" (v. 2). "My father also was killed by them, and I even
remain alone to write the sad tale of the destruction of my people… And whether
they will slay me, I know not" (v. 3). He has "not friends nor whither to go"
(v. 5).

Two details matter for the rest of the chapter. First, his plan: "I will write
and hide up the records in the earth" (v. 4). The burial is deliberate, a
commandment — and it is the literal mechanism by which the book will later become
a *voice from the dust* (hold that for v. 23). Second, his attribution of the
destruction: "it is the hand of the Lord which hath done it" (v. 8). This is not
a random tribal collapse; it is covenant judgment. The same point the Lord makes
to Joseph Smith in [D&C 3:18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/3?lang=eng&id=p18#p18),
naming "the Lamanites… whom the Lord has suffered to destroy their brethren the
Nephites." The footnote on Mormon 8:2 sends you straight to that verse — the
record and the modern revelation describe the same event, fourteen centuries
apart.

## Movement II — The witnesses who remain (vv. 10–11)

A brief, easily-missed referent shift. "There are none that do know the true God
save it be the disciples of Jesus, who did tarry in the land" (v. 10). These are
the Three Nephites of [3 Nephi 28](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/28?lang=eng),
the translated disciples. Whether they are still on the land "no man knoweth" —
but Moroni adds the personal note that anchors his whole testimony: "my father
and I have seen them, and they have ministered unto us" (v. 11). The last
honest men on the continent were not, in fact, without heavenly company. The
footnote tags v. 11 with *Translated Beings*.

## Movement III — The record's coming forth (vv. 12–22)

Here the chapter pivots from past to future, and "this record" becomes the
subject. This is the heart of the chapter.

**The reader's posture (v. 12).** "Whoso receiveth this record, and shall not
condemn it because of the imperfections which are in it, the same shall know of
greater things than these." Moroni sets the terms of engagement before the book
is even out of the ground: receive it without condemning its human faults, and
more is promised. The "greater things" footnote points to the sealed portion and
to [3 Nephi 26:9–11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/26?lang=eng&id=p9-p11#p9).

**The translator (vv. 14–16).** "I am the same who hideth up this record unto
the Lord… whoso shall bring it to light, him will the Lord bless. For none can
have power to bring it to light save it be given him of God." This is Joseph
Smith, and the apparatus says so: the footnote on "no one shall have them to get
gain" routes to [Joseph Smith—History 1:53](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/js-h/1?lang=eng&id=p53#p53),
where the angel Moroni tells Joseph the plates must not be obtained for gain.
The man burying the book in 421 A.D. is describing, by the Spirit, the man who
will unbury it. "It shall be brought out of darkness unto light… it shall be
brought out of the earth, and it shall shine forth out of darkness… and it shall
be done by the power of God" (v. 16). *Out of the earth* — again the burial is
load-bearing.

**The honest disclaimer (v. 17).** "If there be faults they be the faults of a
man." Moroni anticipates the critic and answers in advance: the message is
God's, the imperfections are human, "and he that condemneth, let him be aware
lest he shall be in danger of hell fire." This is the same posture as the title
page of the book.

**The sign-seeker and the destroyer (vv. 18–22).** Two kinds of opposition. The
first: "he that saith: Show unto me, or ye shall be smitten — let him beware"
(v. 18). The footnote sends you to [JS—H 1:60–61](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/js-h/1?lang=eng&id=p60-p61#p60),
the people who schemed to seize the plates and demanded to see them. The second,
graver one: "he that shall breathe out wrath and strifes against the work of the
Lord… and shall say: We will destroy the work of the Lord, and the Lord will not
remember his covenant" (v. 21). To which Moroni answers with the line that ties
the whole chapter to the Restoration: **"For the eternal purposes of the Lord
shall roll on, until all his promises shall be fulfilled"** (v. 22).

That verse is not a generic comfort. Its footnote points to [D&C 3:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/3?lang=eng&id=p3#p3),
and D&C 3:3's footnote points *back* to Mormon 8:22. The cross-reference is
reciprocal — the editors saw the seam. (More on this below; it is the church-
history key.)

## Movement IV — The voice from the dust (vv. 23–26)

"Search the prophecies of Isaiah. Behold, I cannot write them" (v. 23). Moroni,
running out of plates and ore, cannot copy Isaiah — so he points you to him. And
the specific Isaiah he means is unmistakable from what he says next: "those
saints who have gone before me, who have possessed this land, shall cry, yea,
even from the dust will they cry unto the Lord."

That is [Isaiah 29:4](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/isa/29?lang=eng&id=p4#p4): "thou
shalt be brought down, and shalt speak out of the ground, and thy speech shall
be low out of the dust… and thy speech shall whisper out of the dust." Isaiah's
chapter header in our edition names the referent outright: "A people (the
Nephites) will speak as a voice from the dust." Nephi had already applied it to
his own people in [2 Nephi 26:16](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/26?lang=eng&id=p16#p16):
"those who shall be destroyed shall speak unto them out of the ground… as one
that hath a familiar spirit… whisper." So when Moroni buries the plates (v. 4)
and then says the dead "from the dust will they cry" (v. 23), he is consciously
becoming the fulfillment of Isaiah's prophecy. The book is the voice. The grave
is the dust. Moroni knows it as he writes.

Verse 25 closes a loop most readers miss: the ancient prayers "were also in
behalf of him that the Lord should suffer to bring these things forth" — the
prophets prayed for the future translator. And [D&C 10:46](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/10?lang=eng&id=p46#p46)
confirms it from the other side: the record contains the gospel "which my holy
prophets, yea, and also my disciples, desired in their prayers should come forth
unto this people." The footnote on Mormon 8:24 points to that very D&C passage.
Moroni says the old prophets prayed for Joseph; the Lord, in 1829, tells Joseph
He granted those prayers. The two ends of the same prayer are recorded in two
different books.

Then verse 26, the thesis sentence: "out of the earth shall they come, by the
hand of the Lord, and none can stay it; and it shall come in a day when it shall
be said that miracles are done away; and it shall come even as if one should
speak from the dead." A buried book, unearthable by no human power, arriving in
an age that has decided miracles are finished — and the arrival is itself the
miracle, the dead speaking.

## Movement V — "It shall come in a day when…" (vv. 26–32)

Now Moroni does something formally beautiful. Five times he repeats "it shall
come in a day when," and each clause paints the spiritual weather of the day the
book emerges. The anaphora is the structure; "it" is always the coming-forth of
the record. He is describing **our day** by describing the conditions into which
the book would drop:

- "when it shall be said that miracles are done away" (v. 26)
- "when the blood of saints shall cry unto the Lord, because of secret
  combinations and the works of darkness" (v. 27) — the footnote pairs this with
  [Revelation 6:10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/rev/6?lang=eng&id=p10#p10), the souls
  under the altar crying for justice
- "when the power of God shall be denied, and churches become defiled and be
  lifted up in the pride of their hearts… leaders of churches and teachers shall
  rise in the pride of their hearts" (v. 28)
- "when there shall be heard of fires, and tempests, and vapors of smoke in
  foreign lands… wars, rumors of wars, and earthquakes in divers places"
  (vv. 29–30)
- "when there shall be great pollutions upon the face of the earth" — and a
  moral relativism named precisely: "many who will say, Do this, or do that, and
  it mattereth not, for the Lord will uphold such at the last day" (v. 31)
- "when there shall be churches built up that shall say: Come unto me, and for
  your money you shall be forgiven of your sins" (v. 32)

Verse 28's defiled churches carry a footnote to [1 Nephi 14:9–10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/14?lang=eng&id=p9-p10#p9)
— Nephi's vision of the two churches. Moroni is not inventing his list; he is
reading our day off the same vision Nephi saw. And verse 32's "for your money
you shall be forgiven" is the exact twin of [1 Nephi 22:23](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/22?lang=eng&id=p23#p23),
"all churches which are built up to get gain," and of the Lord's own words in
[D&C 10:56](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/10?lang=eng&id=p56#p56), "build up
churches unto themselves to get gain." Three prophets across a thousand years,
the same indictment.

## Movement VI — "Jesus Christ hath shown you unto me" (vv. 33–41)

The final referent shift, and the most personal. Moroni stops describing the day
and turns to address its people directly: "O ye wicked and perverse and
stiffnecked people, why have ye built up churches unto yourselves to get gain?"
(v. 33). The word *stiffnecked* recurs, deliberately, in [D&C 5:8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/5?lang=eng&id=p8#p8)
— "this unbelieving and stiffnecked generation" — and the footnote links them.

Then verse 33 ends with the line that answers one of your questions: **"look ye
unto the revelations of God; for behold, the time cometh at that day when all
these things must be fulfilled."** (We will take up the John/Revelation thread
in its own section below — the footnote here is the key that unlocks it.)

Verses 34–35 are the chapter's most astonishing claim. "The Lord hath shown unto
me great and marvelous things concerning that which must shortly come, at that
day when these things shall come forth among you. Behold, I speak unto you as if
ye were present, and yet ye are not. But behold, **Jesus Christ hath shown you
unto me, and I know your doing.**" Moroni is telling the latter-day reader that
he has *seen us*. The indictment that follows is not a guess about a future
generation; it is testimony about people he was shown.

And the indictment is specific, and it is about the poor. "Ye do love money, and
your substance, and your fine apparel, and the adorning of your churches, more
than ye love the poor and the needy, the sick and the afflicted" (v. 37). "Why
do ye adorn yourselves with that which hath no life, and yet suffer the hungry,
and the needy, and the naked, and the sick and the afflicted to pass by you, and
notice them not?" (v. 39). After everything — the destruction of a civilization,
the loneliness, the prophetic sweep — the thing Moroni saw in us and could not
let pass was decorated churches next to ignored poor. The chapter ends with the
blood of widows and orphans crying from the ground (v. 40) and the sword of
vengeance hanging over those who built their wealth on it (v. 41).

## Deep dive 1 — Does verse 34 point to John's Revelation?

Your instinct is right, and the route is worth getting exactly right.

Verse 34 says the Lord showed Moroni "great and marvelous things concerning that
which **must shortly come**." That phrase is the verbal twin of [Revelation
1:1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/rev/1?lang=eng&id=p1#p1): "The Revelation of Jesus
Christ… to shew unto his servants things which **must shortly come to pass**."
The echo is real. But the chapter's *footnoted* link to Revelation does not sit
on v. 34 — it sits on the line just before it, v. 33: "look ye unto the
revelations of God." That footnote points to two places: [1 Nephi 14:18–27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/14?lang=eng&id=p18-p27#p18)
and [Ether 4:16](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/ether/4?lang=eng&id=p16#p16).

The decisive evidence is Ether 4:16 — because Moroni wrote it. The same man,
later in the same plates, says: "then shall **my revelations which I have caused
to be written by my servant John** be unfolded in the eyes of all the people.
Remember, when ye see these things, ye shall know that the time is at hand." Its
footnote points to Revelation 1:1 and 1 Nephi 14. So when Moroni says in Mormon
8:33 "look ye unto the revelations of God," we are not guessing what he means by
cross-referencing a stranger — we have the same author, elsewhere, naming John's
Revelation by name as part of what God has revealed and will unfold.

The honest precision: "the revelations of God" in v. 33 is plural and broad. It
means the whole body of what God has revealed, not *only* the book of
Revelation. But John's apocalypse is unmistakably inside that body for Moroni,
and v. 34's "must shortly come" leans the same direction. So — yes, it points
there, with Ether 4:16 as the anchor, rather than v. 34 being a direct citation
of Revelation.

## Deep dive 2 — Nephi's vision, and the handoff to John

This is the connection you sensed from the tree-of-life vision, and it is
structural, not incidental.

Nephi's great vision ([1 Nephi 11–14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/14?lang=eng))
begins with the tree of life and widens until, at its climax in chapter 14, the
angel shows Nephi "one of the twelve apostles of the Lamb… he shall see and
write the remainder of these things… And he shall also write concerning the end
of the world" (14:20–22). Nephi is then told: "the things which thou shalt see
hereafter thou shalt not write; for the Lord God hath ordained the apostle of
the Lamb of God that he should write them" (14:25). And: "I, Nephi, am forbidden
that I should write the remainder of the things which I saw" (14:28). The
apostle's name, Nephi records, "was John" (14:27).

So Nephi and John saw *the same vision*. Nephi was shown the latter days, the
two churches, the wars and the wrath — and then told to stop, because that
portion belonged to John to write. The Book of Mormon and the book of Revelation
are deliberately interlocked: two prophets, one vision, the writing assignment
split between them. Revelation is, in a real sense, the part of Nephi's vision
Nephi was not allowed to publish.

That is why Moroni, at the very end of the record, points the reader back to it.
Mormon 8 follows the identical shape as Nephi's vision: a prophet is shown the
latter days, sees the defiled churches (8:28, footnoted to Nephi's two-church
vision in 14:9–10), describes the wickedness, and then points to John for the
full unsealing (8:33 → Ether 4:16 → Revelation). The first major vision of the
Book of Mormon and its last prophet's farewell both hand you off to the same
apostle. The book opens and closes by saying: now go read John.

## Deep dive 3 — The sealed book and the voice from the dust

Mormon 8:23–26 is the short version of a prophecy the Book of Mormon tells three
times. Isaiah gives the seed in [Isaiah 29](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/isa/29?lang=eng):
a people speaking "out of the dust" (29:4) and "the words of a book that is
sealed, which men deliver to one that is learned, saying, Read this, I pray thee:
and he saith, I cannot; for it is sealed" (29:11). Nephi expands it into a full
chapter in [2 Nephi 27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/27?lang=eng): the
sealed book (27:7–8), the three witnesses who alone shall behold it (27:12), the
words that "speak as if it were from the dead" (27:13), and the famous exchange
where the learned man says "I cannot read it" (27:15–20) — the Anthon episode
prefigured. Moroni then compresses the whole thing back into two verses in
Mormon 8:23–26, trusting you to "search the prophecies of Isaiah" for the rest.

The chain is tight and entirely footnote-verified: Mormon 8:23 → Isaiah 29:4 → 2
Nephi 26:16 / 27. The same imagery — buried, whispering, sealed, brought forth by
power, witnessed — runs through all of it. Even the hymns we sing carry it: "An
Angel from on High" ("A sacred record lies concealed") and "A Voice Hath Spoken
from the Dust" are both built directly on this passage.

## Deep dive 4 — Lined up with the translation, 1828–29

This is where the study turns from beautiful to almost eerie, and it answers
your question about church history during the translation.

Set the timeline (dated from the Doctrine and Covenants section headings):

- **July 1828** — [D&C 3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/3?lang=eng).
  Martin Harris has lost the 116 manuscript pages. The first great attempt to
  derail the work. The Lord's answer opens: "The works, and the designs, and the
  purposes of God cannot be frustrated" (3:1), and "Nevertheless, my work shall
  go forth" (3:16).
- **Summer 1828 → April 1829** — [D&C 10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/10?lang=eng).
  The Lord reveals the actual conspiracy: wicked men have *altered* the stolen
  pages, planning to wait for a retranslation and then publish the discrepancies
  to prove Joseph a fraud (header; 10:10–19). The countermove is the small
  plates: don't retranslate the lost book of Lehi; translate the plates of Nephi
  instead (10:38–45). "I will not suffer that they shall destroy my work; yea, I
  will show unto them that my wisdom is greater than the cunning of the devil"
  (10:43).
- **March 1829** — [D&C 5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/5?lang=eng).
  The promise of three witnesses (5:11–15); the rebuke of "this unbelieving and
  stiffnecked generation" (5:8); "there are many that lie in wait to destroy
  thee" (5:33).
- **April–June 1829** — Oliver Cowdery scribing, the bulk of our present text is
  rendered (Mosiah through Moroni, then back to 1 Nephi). **Mormon 8 was
  translated in this same spring-1829 window.**
- **June 1829** — the Three Witnesses; translation finished; copyright filed
  June 11.
- **March 1830** — the book is published.

Now read Mormon 8 against that. Moroni warns of one "that shall breathe out
wrath and strifes against the work of the Lord… and shall say: We will destroy
the work of the Lord" (8:21). While Joseph was dictating that verse, he had
*personally just survived* an attempt to do exactly that — and he was holding the
revelations (D&C 3 and 10) in which the Lord names the conspiracy and defeats it.
The prophecy and its first fulfillment sat on the same desk. Moroni's answer in
8:22 — "the eternal purposes of the Lord shall roll on" — is, word for theme, the
Lord's answer in D&C 3:1 — "the purposes of God cannot be frustrated." The
footnotes cross-link them in both directions.

And the small-plates miracle closes the loop backward in time. Mormon, around
A.D. 385, was moved to include Nephi's small plates in his abridgment "for a
wise purpose… which I do not know" ([Words of Mormon 1:7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/w-of-m/1?lang=eng&id=p7#p7)
— the very passage D&C 10's heading cites). Fourteen hundred years later that
unexplained inclusion turns out to be the precise material that lets Joseph route
around the altered pages. The defense against the 1828 attack was engraved into
the plates in the fourth century by a man who admitted he didn't know why he was
doing it. Then Moroni, a generation after his father, writes the warning about
the attack itself, and prays in 8:24–25 that the saints' prayers "in behalf of
him that the Lord should suffer to bring these things forth" — the translator —
be answered. D&C 10:46 tells Joseph they were.

That is the alignment in one sentence: **the same book that prophesies its own
enemies also carries, by a provision its own authors didn't understand, the
defense against them — and the man translating it was living the prophecy as he
wrote it down.**

## What it asks of us

Moroni saw us, and the thing he flagged was not doctrine or persecution — it was
the gap between our adorned churches and our ignored poor (8:37–39). For a study
that traces such a high arc — Isaiah, John, the witnesses, the translation — the
landing is deliberately low and near: the needy who "pass by you," and whether
we "notice them not." The chapter that is most about the book coming forth ends
by asking whether the people who receive the book will love money more than the
hungry.

Two honest commitments fall out of this study:

1. **Receive the record without condemning its human faults (8:12, 17).** Moroni
   asked exactly one thing of the reader before the book was even unearthed. The
   modern application is to read the Book of Mormon as a believer reads it —
   willing to be taught — rather than as a critic hunting for the "faults of a
   man."
2. **Read the gap Moroni named in 8:37–39 as a personal audit, not a cultural
   one.** It is easy to read "your churches" as someone else's churches. The
   honest reading is to ask where my own substance and my own attention actually
   go, against the needy I could notice and don't.

And one connection to carry forward: the Book of Mormon opens (Nephi's vision)
and closes (Moroni's farewell) by pointing to John. The next study almost writes
itself — read [1 Nephi 14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/14?lang=eng) and
[Revelation 1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/rev/1?lang=eng) and
[Ether 4](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/ether/4?lang=eng) together, as the
three corners of one vision split across two testaments.
