## The Purpose of This Study

In [Part 1](./order-of-god.md), we telescoped *in* — into Alma 13, D&C 84, D&C 107, Hebrews 7, and the scriptural architecture of the holy order. We found that the order is the eternal pattern by which intelligence progresses from agency through sanctification to fulness. We found it connected to truth, intelligence, and "one eternal round."

Now we telescope *out*. What have modern prophets and apostles taught about this order? How do they expand our understanding? Where do they press on things we might have missed?

## Part 1: "The Doctrine of the Priesthood" — McConkie's Masterwork (1982)

This talk is the most deliberate modern treatment of the holy order. McConkie opens with a startling claim:

> "The doctrine of the priesthood ... cannot be learned out of the scriptures alone. It is not set forth in the sermons and teachings of the prophets and Apostles, except in small measure. The doctrine of the priesthood is known only by personal revelation."
> — [Elder Bruce R. McConkie, April 1982](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1982/04/the-doctrine-of-the-priesthood?lang=eng)

That's a remarkable admission from a man famous for his scriptural commentary. He's saying the priesthood's doctrine is *too deep to be fully captured in words.* It must be experienced. It distils "as the dews from heaven" ([D&C 121:45](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/121?lang=eng&id=p45#p45)) on those whose souls are full of charity and virtue.

### Priesthood Is the Power of God — Not a Subset of It

McConkie then defines priesthood in breathtaking terms:

> "Priesthood is power like none other on earth or in heaven. It is the very power of God himself, the power by which the worlds were made, the power by which all things are regulated, upheld, and preserved."
> — [McConkie, 1982](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1982/04/the-doctrine-of-the-priesthood?lang=eng)

This maps directly onto what we found in the [truth study](./truth.md): the light that "is in all things, which giveth life to all things, which is the law by which all things are governed" ([D&C 88:13](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88?lang=eng&id=p13#p13)). McConkie is saying: priesthood = the power behind D&C 88:6-13. It's not a *subset* of God's power — it *is* God's power.

### Faith and Priesthood Are the Same Thing

Then comes the connection that blew this study open:

> "It is the power of faith, the faith by which the Father creates and governs. God is God because he is the embodiment of all faith and all power and all priesthood. ... Faith and priesthood go hand in hand. Faith is power and power is priesthood."
> — [McConkie, 1982](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1982/04/the-doctrine-of-the-priesthood?lang=eng)

Faith = power = priesthood. These are not three things that cooperate — they are three descriptions of the same reality. This radically reframes Alma 13:3, where those called to the holy order were selected "on account of their exceeding faith." They weren't chosen because they had faith AND were good priesthood candidates. Their faith *was* their qualification for the priesthood, because faith and priesthood are one.

### The Order of Enoch

McConkie traces the priesthood through its historical names:

> "The holy priesthood did more to perfect men in the days of Enoch than at any other time. Known then as the order of Enoch, it was the power by which he and his people were translated."
> — [McConkie, 1982](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1982/04/the-doctrine-of-the-priesthood?lang=eng)

The order of Enoch, the order of Melchizedek, the order of the Son — all one order. And in Enoch's time, it did something we haven't seen since: *it translated an entire people*. That's the order operating at maximum capacity. An entire community entering into the rest of the Lord.

This connects to our Part 1 question for further study: "Is Zion what it looks like when a whole community enters the holy order?" McConkie confirms it — yes.

### The Oath and the Prototype

McConkie then makes the connection to the oath:

> "God swore with an oath that Christ would be exalted, and he swears anew, at the time each of us receives the Melchizedek Priesthood, that we will have a like exaltation if we are true and faithful in all things."
> — [McConkie, 1982](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1982/04/the-doctrine-of-the-priesthood?lang=eng)

The oath is individualized. God doesn't swear it once and let it stand — He swears it *anew* for each person. This is a deeply personal covenant. And the measure is Christ himself:

> "Christ is the prototype; he is the Son; he is the Heir of the Father. But we, as joint-heirs, inherit equally with him because we also abide as priests forever."
> — [McConkie, 1982](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1982/04/the-doctrine-of-the-priesthood?lang=eng)

"Made like unto the Son of God, abiding a priest continually" ([JST Hebrews 7:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/jst/jst-heb/7?lang=eng&id=p3#p3)). The order doesn't elevate you *next to* Christ — it makes you *like* Him.

### The Doctrine Summarized

McConkie closes with the full doctrine stated plainly:

> "It is that priesthood is the very name of the power of God, and that if we are to become like him, we must receive and exercise his priesthood or power as he exercises it. ... It is that we have power, by faith, to govern and control all things, both temporal and spiritual; to work miracles and perfect lives; to stand in the presence of God and be like him because we have gained his faith, his perfections, and his power, or in other words the fulness of his priesthood."
> — [McConkie, 1982](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1982/04/the-doctrine-of-the-priesthood?lang=eng)

This is the rest from Alma 13:12, stated in modern language. "The fulness of his priesthood" = exaltation = godhood. The order leads here.

## Part 2: The Ten Blessings — A Map of the Order's Fruits (1977)

Five years before "The Doctrine of the Priesthood," McConkie gave an equally profound talk: ["The Ten Blessings of the Priesthood"](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1977/10/the-ten-blessings-of-the-priesthood?lang=eng). He lays out the blessings that flow from the holy order — and when read alongside Alma 13, they trace the very progression we mapped in Part 1.

### The Ten Blessings, Mapped to the Order

| Blessing | What McConkie Says | Connection to the Order |
|----------|-------------------|------------------------|
| 1. Fulness of the gospel | "This greater priesthood administereth the gospel" | The order *is* the gospel in its administered form — both are the plan of salvation |
| 2. Gift of the Holy Ghost & spiritual gifts | "The key of the mysteries of the kingdom, even the key of the knowledge of God" | [D&C 84:19](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/84?lang=eng&id=p19#p19) — the order holds this key |
| 3. **Sanctification** by the Spirit | "Have dross and evil burned out of us as though by fire" | Alma 13:11-12 — "sanctified ... garments washed white" |
| 4. Standing in Christ's place | "He preached the gospel; so can we" | The order points people to the Son (Alma 13:2) |
| 5. Becoming **sons of God** | "Thou art one in me, a son of God; and thus may all become my sons" ([Moses 6:67-68](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/6?lang=eng&id=p67-p68#p67)) | Adam's entry into the order — the adoption |
| 6. **Patriarchal order** / eternal marriage | "The new and everlasting covenant of marriage" | The family is a subset of the holy order |
| 7. Power to **govern all things** | [JST Genesis 14:30-31](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/jst/jst-gen/14?lang=eng&id=p30-p31#p30) — break mountains, divide seas, stand in God's presence | The operative power of the order — faith exercised to its fulness |
| 8. **Eternal life** | "All that my Father hath" ([D&C 84:38](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/84?lang=eng&id=p38#p38)) | The rest of the Lord = the fulness of His glory = eternal life |
| 9. **Calling and election sure** | "Sealed up unto eternal life, by revelation and the spirit of prophecy" ([D&C 131:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/131?lang=eng&id=p5#p5)) | The order's guarantee — God's oath fulfilled |
| 10. **Seeing the face of God** | [D&C 93:1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93?lang=eng&id=p1#p1) — "every soul who forsaketh his sins and cometh unto me ... shall see my face" | The culmination — "no man can see the face of God ... and live" without the priesthood ([D&C 84:22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/84?lang=eng&id=p22#p22)) |

Notice the progression: gospel → spiritual gifts → sanctification → transformation → fulness → seeing God. This is the same trajectory as Alma 13. McConkie is essentially giving us a modern *commentary* on the Alma 13 progression.

### Blessing 3: The Key Passage on Sanctification

McConkie's description of the third blessing is a direct quotation of Alma 13:11-12:

> "Of such faithful persons among the ancients, Alma says: 'They were called after this holy order' — that is, they held the Melchizedek Priesthood — 'and [they] were sanctified, and their garments were washed white through the blood of the Lamb. Now they, after being sanctified by the Holy Ghost, having their garments made white, being pure and spotless before God, could not look upon sin save it were with abhorrence; and there were many, exceeding great many, who were made pure and entered into the rest of the Lord their God.'"
> — [McConkie, October 1977](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1977/10/the-ten-blessings-of-the-priesthood?lang=eng)

He identifies the Alma 13 progression — called → sanctified → entered rest — as the *third* of ten blessings, placing sanctification squarely in the early stages of what the order can accomplish. In other words: if you've been sanctified and entered His rest, there's still more. Seven more blessings follow. The order keeps unfolding.

### The JST Genesis Connection

McConkie concludes by reading extensively from JST Genesis 14 — the expanded Melchizedek account. This is the text our Part 1 flagged as a question for further study:

> "Now Melchizedek was a man of faith, who wrought righteousness; and when a child he feared God, and stopped the mouths of lions, and quenched the violence of fire. And thus, having been approved of God, he was ordained an high priest after the order of the covenant which God made with Enoch."
> — [JST Genesis 14:26-27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/jst/jst-gen/14?lang=eng&id=p26-p27#p26), quoted by [McConkie](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1977/10/the-ten-blessings-of-the-priesthood?lang=eng)

Several things stand out:
- Melchizedek was a "man of faith" — faith is the qualifying attribute
- He was ordained after "the order of the covenant which God made with Enoch" — confirming the order is one continuous chain
- His people "obtained heaven, and sought for the city of Enoch" — they were reaching back toward the translated community
- God "sworn with an oath, that the heavens and the earth should come together" — the reunion of Enoch's Zion with the latter-day Saints is *sworn* in the order's covenant

## Part 3: Walking with Him — Eyring's Pastoral Vision (2017)

President Eyring's ["Walk with Me"](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2017/04/walk-with-me?lang=eng) takes an entirely different approach. Where McConkie is doctrinal and sweeping, Eyring is personal and pastoral. He translates the order's language into a single invitation:

> "Our ordination to the priesthood is an invitation from the Lord to walk with Him, to do what He does, to serve the way He serves."
> — [President Henry B. Eyring, April 2017](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2017/04/walk-with-me?lang=eng)

This is the Enoch pattern. The Lord said to Enoch: "Walk with me" ([Moses 6:34](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/6?lang=eng&id=p34#p34)). And Eyring points out that this is what priesthood ordination *is* — an invitation to walk with God. Not an administrative appointment. An invitation to companionship.

### He Quotes Alma 13:7 Directly

Eyring grounds his talk in the holy order texts:

> "'This high priesthood [is] after the order of [God's] Son, which order was from the foundation of the world; or in other words, being without beginning of days or end of years, being prepared from eternity to all eternity, according to his foreknowledge of all things.'"
> — Eyring quoting [Alma 13:7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/13?lang=eng&id=p7#p7)

He also reads JST Genesis 14:30-31, and then asks the searingly personal questions:

> "Have I ever felt that the heavens have been opened to me? Would anyone use the phrase 'ministering of angels' to describe my priesthood service? Do I bring the 'power of godliness' into the lives of those I serve?"
> — [Eyring, April 2017](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2017/04/walk-with-me?lang=eng)

This is Alma 13 applied as self-examination. The order isn't just theology — it's a mirror.

### Three Lessons from Walking with the Lord

Eyring draws three lessons from his decades of priesthood service:

1. **God notices even the newest deacon.** "You need never feel that you are too small or too insignificant for Him to take notice of you." — The order is available to all, not reserved for the spiritual elite. This echoes Alma 13:4-5: "in the first place they were on the same standing with their brethren."

2. **The Lord's work is to build people, not to solve problems.** "God's work and glory is not simply to run an effective organization; it is 'to bring to pass the immortality and eternal life of man.'" — The order is about *transformation*, not administration. Our progress IS His work.

3. **Walking with the Savior changes how you see others.** Eyring describes a moment when God let him see a struggling ward member as God sees him — "a bright, noble son of God." — The sanctification of the order doesn't just purify your behavior; it transforms your *perception*. You begin to see as God sees.

This third lesson connects directly to D&C 93:1 — "every soul who forsaketh his sins and cometh unto me, and calleth on my name, and obeyeth my voice, and keepeth my commandments, shall see my face and know that I am." McConkie listed this as blessing ten. Eyring shows it beginning to happen in small ways for the newest priesthood holder.

## Part 4: Faith and the Oath — Eyring on Alma 13 (2008)

In his 2008 talk ["Faith and the Oath and Covenant of the Priesthood,"](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2008/04/faith-and-the-oath-and-covenant-of-the-priesthood?lang=eng) President Eyring does something remarkable: he reads the oath and covenant from D&C 84:33-43 and then turns to Alma 13 to explain what it means in practice:

> "The Lord describes that wonderful blessing in Alma 13:6: 'And thus being called by this holy calling, and ordained unto the high priesthood of the holy order of God, to teach his commandments unto the children of men, that they also might enter into his rest.'"
> — [President Henry B. Eyring, April 2008](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2008/04/faith-and-the-oath-and-covenant-of-the-priesthood?lang=eng)

Then he reads Alma 13:12-13 — the sanctification passage — and applies it as a *promise of success*. If you walk in this order faithfully, you *will* be sanctified. You will enter His rest. This is not aspirational language — it is covenantal certainty, backed by God's oath.

### Foreknowledge and Confidence

Eyring makes a pastoral move that reframes foreordination:

> "The very fact that you have been offered the oath and covenant is evidence that God has chosen you, knowing your power and capacity. He has known you since you were with Him in the spirit world. With His foreknowledge of your strength, He has allowed you to find the true Church of Jesus Christ and to be offered the priesthood."
> — [Eyring, 2008](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2008/04/faith-and-the-oath-and-covenant-of-the-priesthood?lang=eng)

This is Alma 13:3 — "called and prepared from the foundation of the world according to the foreknowledge of God" — turned into *personal encouragement*. God foreknew your capacity and called you anyway. The foreordination isn't just a doctrinal fact; it's evidence of God's confidence in *you*.

### Sanctification as Renewal

One detail Eyring adds that isn't explicit in Alma 13:

> "For whoso is faithful unto the obtaining these two priesthoods of which I have spoken, and the magnifying their calling, are sanctified by the Spirit unto the renewing of their bodies."
> — [D&C 84:33](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/84?lang=eng&id=p33#p33), quoted by [Eyring](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2008/04/faith-and-the-oath-and-covenant-of-the-priesthood?lang=eng)

He testifies to having seen this fulfilled — aged prophets being *renewed* each time they stood to testify. The sanctification of the order isn't only spiritual; it's physical. The Spirit renews the body. This connects to the [truth-atonement study](./truth-atonement.md) — the refinement of matter, the spectrum from coarse to pure, is happening *in mortality* through the power of the priesthood.

## Part 5: The Order Is for Everyone — Nelson, Oaks, and Renlund

Here's where modern prophets press on something the ancient texts leave ambiguous. Alma 13 speaks of "priests" and uses masculine language. But modern revelation expands the frame dramatically.

### Nelson: "Priesthood Power Is Not Limited to Men" (2019)

President Nelson's ["Spiritual Treasures"](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2019/10/36nelson?lang=eng) talk to women is a landmark:

> "The heavens are just as open to *women* who are endowed with God's power flowing from their priesthood covenants as they are to men who bear the priesthood. I pray that truth will register upon each of your hearts because I believe it will change your life. Sisters, you have the right to draw liberally upon the Savior's power to help your family and others you love."
> — [President Russell M. Nelson, October 2019](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2019/10/36nelson?lang=eng)

And:

> "Every woman and every man who makes covenants with God and keeps those covenants, and who participates worthily in priesthood ordinances, has direct access to the power of God. Those who are endowed in the house of the Lord receive a gift of God's priesthood power by virtue of their covenant."
> — [Nelson, 2019](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2019/10/36nelson?lang=eng)

This is the "thus may *all* become my sons" of Moses 6:68, expanded. The order of God is not gender-limited in its *blessings*, even if ordination to specific offices is. The power of the priesthood — the very power McConkie described as "the power by which the worlds were made" — is accessible to every covenant-keeper.

### Oaks: "Priesthood Power Blesses All" (2014)

Elder Oaks pressed this point even more directly in ["The Keys and Authority of the Priesthood"](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2014/04/the-keys-and-authority-of-the-priesthood?lang=eng):

> "When men and women go to the temple, they are both endowed with the same power, which is priesthood power. ... Access to the power and the blessings of the priesthood is available to all of God's children."
> — [Elder Dallin H. Oaks, April 2014](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2014/04/the-keys-and-authority-of-the-priesthood?lang=eng)

And:

> "Unlike priesthood keys and priesthood ordinations, the blessings of the priesthood are available to women and to men on the same terms."
> — [Oaks, 2014](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2014/04/the-keys-and-authority-of-the-priesthood?lang=eng)

This is critical for understanding the *scope* of the holy order. If the order is the eternal pattern by which intelligence progresses toward fulness — as we argued in Part 1 — then it cannot be limited to half the human family. The sanctification progression of Alma 13 (called → ordained → sanctified → entered rest) applies to *all* who walk the covenant path, regardless of ordination to specific priesthood offices.

### Renlund: Covenants as the Connecting Mechanism (2023)

Elder Renlund's ["Accessing God's Power through Covenants"](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2023/04/23renlund?lang=eng) provides the mechanism:

> "Before the earth was created, God established covenants as the mechanism by which we, His children, could unite ourselves to Him. Based on eternal, unchanging law, He specified the nonnegotiable conditions whereby we are transformed, saved, and exalted."
> — [Elder Dale G. Renlund, April 2023](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2023/04/23renlund?lang=eng)

Covenants are the *mechanism*. This echoes what we found in Part 1: the order makes truth *operational*. Without the order, truth exists but "the power of godliness is not manifest" ([D&C 84:21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/84?lang=eng&id=p21#p21)). Renlund adds that covenants are the *connecting mechanism* — the means by which finite beings attach themselves to infinite power.

And his closing:

> "As you come to Christ and are connected to Him and our Heavenly Father by covenant, something seemingly unnatural happens. You are transformed and become perfected in Jesus Christ. You become a covenant child of God and an inheritor in His kingdom."
> — [Renlund, 2023](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2023/04/23renlund?lang=eng)

"Something seemingly unnatural happens." You go against the flow. The fallen trajectory is *reversed*. In the language of our [truth-atonement study](./truth-atonement.md): you move upward on the spectrum, from coarse toward pure, from fallen toward glory. The order, through covenant, does what law alone cannot do — it carries you back up.

## Part 6: Foreordination and the Premortal Council

Several talks address the foreordination of Alma 13:3 in expanded terms.

### McConkie: "God Foreordains His Prophets and His People" (1974)

> "All those who receive the Melchizedek Priesthood in this life were, as Alma teaches, 'called and prepared from the foundation of the world according to the foreknowledge of God,' because they were among the noble and great in that premortal sphere."
> — [Elder Bruce R. McConkie, April 1974](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1974/04/god-foreordains-his-prophets-and-his-people?lang=eng)

McConkie here reads Alma 13 as applying to *all* who receive the Melchizedek Priesthood — not just ancient high priests. Every elder, every high priest who has been ordained was called from the foundation of the world. The foreordination is not rare; it is *standard procedure* for the order.

### McConkie: "The Doctrine of the Priesthood" (1982) — Again

> "We received the priesthood first in the premortal existence and then again as mortals. Adam held the keys and used the priesthood when he participated in the creation of the earth."
> — [McConkie, 1982](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1982/04/the-doctrine-of-the-priesthood?lang=eng)

Two priesthood receptions: once in the premortal world, once in mortality. The order spans both sides of the veil. It was "without beginning of days" because it predates earth life. We received it *there first*. What we receive in mortality is a *renewal* of something already ours.

This reframes the whole concept of ordination. You're not receiving something new — you're reclaiming something eternal.

### Eyring: Foreknowledge as Confidence (2008)

As noted above, Eyring reframes foreordination as *evidence of God's confidence* in each individual priesthood holder. This pastoral application turns a doctrinal abstraction into a personal assurance: God knew you could do this. That's why you're here.

## Part 7: The Patriarchal Order and Eternal Family

McConkie's sixth blessing — the patriarchal order — points to a dimension of the holy order we didn't fully develop in Part 1.

> "To gain the highest heaven and to enjoy the fulness of that light and glory which comprise eternal life, we must 'enter into' that 'order of the priesthood' which bears the name 'the new and everlasting covenant of marriage.'"
> — [McConkie, October 1977](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1977/10/the-ten-blessings-of-the-priesthood?lang=eng), referencing [D&C 131:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/131?lang=eng&id=p2#p2)

And from the 1982 talk:

> "It is that we can enter an order of the priesthood named the new and everlasting covenant of marriage, named also the patriarchal order, because of which order we can create for ourselves eternal family units of our own, patterned after the family of God our Heavenly Father."
> — [McConkie, 1982](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1982/04/the-doctrine-of-the-priesthood?lang=eng)

The patriarchal order is *within* the holy order. Marriage is not an addendum to the priesthood — it is "an order of the priesthood." The family unit is the *structure* in which the sanctification journey of Alma 13 is lived out. You don't walk the order alone.

This is why Oaks and Nelson emphasize that priesthood blessings are available equally to women and men. The patriarchal order requires *both*. As President Spencer W. Kimball said, quoted by Oaks: "We do not want our LDS women to be silent partners or limited partners in that eternal assignment! Please be a contributing and full partner."

## Synthesis: What the Modern Lens Adds

The scriptural study of Part 1 gave us the *architecture*. The prophetic voices of Part 2 give us the *experience*:

| Part 1 Found | Part 2 Adds |
|-------------|-------------|
| The order is the pattern of eternal progression | **Faith and priesthood are the same thing** — the pattern runs on faith |
| Alma 13 traces the progression: called → sanctified → rest | McConkie maps **ten blessings**, showing the order unfolds far beyond even "rest" |
| The order is eternal — without beginning or end | **We received the priesthood first in the premortal world** — ordination in mortality is a renewal |
| The order leads to "the rest of the Lord" | The rest is **eternal life itself**, and the progression includes calling and election sure, seeing God's face, governing all things |
| The order is connected to one eternal round | **The order is one eternal round** — the same process everywhere, always, for everyone |
| Those called "were on the same standing" | **The order's blessings are for all** — women and men, through covenant, have access to the same priesthood power |
| The order points people to the Son | **Walking with Christ is the order** — Eyring turns doctrine into daily invitation |
| The order is governed by oath and covenant | **God swears the oath anew for each person** — it's individualized and personal |
| The order is connected to truth and intelligence | **Priesthood IS the power behind truth and intelligence** — it's the very power by which worlds are created and governed |

### The Deepest Insight

McConkie gives us the single most important sentence for connecting our truth, intelligence, and order studies:

> "God is God because he is the embodiment of all faith and all power and all priesthood."
> — [McConkie, 1982](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1982/04/the-doctrine-of-the-priesthood?lang=eng)

God is God because of *priesthood*. Not: God *has* priesthood. God *is* the embodiment of priesthood. In our truth study, we said "the glory of God is intelligence" ([D&C 93:36](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93?lang=eng&id=p36#p36)). In our intelligence study, we explored how intelligence cleaveth unto intelligence. Now McConkie is saying: priesthood is the *power expression* of what intelligence *is*. Intelligence is the substance. Priesthood is the power. Faith is the mechanism. They are three faces of one reality.

The holy order of God is the pattern by which that reality is *entered by covenant*. It is truth made operational. It is intelligence made accessible through ordinance. It is faith formalized into oath.

And it is one eternal round — the same always, for everyone, from eternity to eternity.

---

## Become

1. **"The doctrine of the priesthood is known only by personal revelation."** Don't stop at studying it. Ask God to show you. Let it distil upon your soul. The dews come to those whose hearts are full of charity and virtue ([D&C 121:45](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/121?lang=eng&id=p45#p45)).

2. **Faith and priesthood are the same.** If you want more priesthood power, exercise more faith. If you want more faith, serve in your priesthood. They grow together. This is why Alma 13 says those called had "exceeding faith" — faith is not a *prerequisite* to the order; it is the very *substance* of the order.

3. **Your ordination was a renewal, not an introduction.** You received the priesthood before this world. What you've been given in mortality is a reconnection to something eternal. Let that reframe how you see your calling — you're not learning something foreign. You're *remembering* something native.

4. **The order includes your spouse.** The patriarchal order is the family dimension of the holy order. Your wife has access to the same priesthood power through covenant. Study Nelson's 2019 talk together. The order is not a solo journey.

5. **Walk with Him.** Eyring's three lessons are the daily practice of the order: (a) God notices you, (b) the work is about transformation not efficiency, and (c) serving in the order changes how you see people. If you're walking with Christ, the order is already working in you.

6. **Let McConkie's ten blessings be your checklist.** Not a checklist of accomplishment, but of *expectation*. These blessings are *promised*. Which ones have you experienced? Which ones are you reaching toward? The order doesn't promise them in the next life alone — McConkie presents them as present-tense realities for those who exercise faith.

## Questions for Further Study

1. McConkie says God is God "because he is the embodiment of all faith and all power and all priesthood." Does this mean that without exercising priesthood, God would cease to be God? How does this connect to the "if it were not so, God would cease to be God" language of Alma 42?
2. If we received the priesthood "first in the premortal existence," what was the nature of that reception? Was there an ordinance? Or is it more like the "calling and preparing" of Alma 13:3?
3. Enoch's order translated an entire city. What conditions enabled that level of collective sanctification? What would that look like in our day?
4. How does the temple endowment function as the *ordinance* by which we enter the holy order? McConkie lists it implicitly through the patriarchal order (blessing 6), but how do the specific covenants of the endowment map onto the Alma 13 progression?
5. Nelson and Oaks expand priesthood blessings to include women through covenant. Does this suggest that the *order* of Alma 13 — not just its blessings — extends to all covenant-keepers? What would "walking after the holy order" look like for women who don't hold priesthood office?
