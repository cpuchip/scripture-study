## [ESTABLISHED] — facts (official/primary sources)

- **16th Amendment (federal income tax).** Passed Congress July 2, 1909; ratified **Feb 3, 1913**; certified Feb 25, 1913. Text: "The Congress shall have power to lay and collect taxes on incomes, from whatever source derived, without apportionment among the several States, and without regard to any census or enumeration." It overrode *Pollock v. Farmers' Loan & Trust Co.* (1895), which had struck down a federal income tax. Source: [National Archives — 16th Amendment](https://www.archives.gov/milestone-documents/16th-amendment).
- **17th Amendment (direct election of senators).** Passed Congress May 13, 1912; ratified **Apr 8, 1913**. Removed state legislatures as the body selecting senators, transferring it to statewide electorates. Source: [National Archives — 17th Amendment](https://www.archives.gov/milestone-documents/17th-amendment).
  - **→ CORRECTION of Michael's recollection:** income tax = 16th, senators = 17th, **both 1913** (not "16th / 1916–17"). The numbers are adjacent and easy to swap.
- ***Lochner v. New York***, 198 U.S. 45 (Apr 17, 1905) — struck a NY maximum-hours law; epitomized the "liberty of contract" era of judicial review of economic legislation. [Justia](https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/198/45/).
- ***A.L.A. Schechter Poultry Corp. v. United States***, 295 U.S. 495 (May 27, 1935) — unanimous; struck the National Industrial Recovery Act as an unconstitutional delegation of legislative power. "Extraordinary conditions... cannot create or enlarge constitutional power." [Justia](https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/295/495/).
- ***NLRB v. Jones & Laughlin Steel Corp.***, 301 U.S. 1 (Apr 12, 1937) — upheld the Wagner Act; broad Commerce-Clause reading permitting federal regulation of activities with substantial effects on interstate commerce. The doctrinal pivot popularly called the "switch in time that saved nine" (phrase from journalists, NOT any Court opinion). [Justia](https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/301/1/).
- **Brownlow Committee** (President's Committee on Administrative Management), report Jan 1937: the executive branch "has thus grown up without plan or design like the barns, shacks, silos, tool sheds, and garages of an old farm"; recommended consolidation + a strengthened presidency → the modern Executive Office of the President / administrative state. [Teaching American History](https://teachingamericanhistory.org/document/report-of-the-presidents-committee-on-administrative-management/).

## [INTERPRETIVE — contested scholarship; BOTH views, non-partisan]

- **Originalist / federalist "loss of liberty" view** — Randy E. Barnett, "Three Federalisms," 39 Loy. U. Chi. L.J. 285 (2008): the New Deal "eviscerated altogether" Enumerated Powers Federalism. On this reading: the 16th enabled limitless federal fiscal extraction; the 17th severed the Senate's structural dependence on state governments; the 1937 Commerce-Clause pivot ended judicial enforcement of enumerated powers; Brownlow centralized administration in the presidency. [Loyola](https://lawecommons.luc.edu/luclj/vol39/iss2/2/).
- **Mainstream / progressive counter-view** — "Countering the Majoritarian Difficulty," *Constitutional Commentary* (2017): the pre-1937 *Lochner* Court protected powerful economic interests under the guise of principle; the 1937 settlement properly *restored* deference to democratic majorities, not a constitutional rupture. [link](https://constitutionalcommentary.lib.umn.edu/article/countering-the-majoritarian-difficulty/).
