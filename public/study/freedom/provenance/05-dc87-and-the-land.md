# Provenance — D&C 87, the Doctrine of This Land, and the Date-Stamp Discipline

*Source: background research subagent C, 2026-05-31. All 15 passages retrieved this session via `mcp__gospel-engine-v2__gospel_get` and reproduced exactly (no memory). Labels per 00-frame. Governing finding: the text frames national "end" as **CONDITIONAL, not inevitable**, and is **emphatically not** a date-setting text.*

## (a) The conditional promise of the land — the center

**Ether 2:8–12 [ESTABLISHED]** — the grammar IS the doctrine:
> (8) "...whoso should possess this land of promise... should serve him, the true and only God, **or** they should be swept off..." (9) "...shall serve God, **or** they shall be swept off... And the fulness of his wrath cometh upon them **when they are ripened in iniquity**." (10) "...**it is not until** the fulness of iniquity among the children of the land, that they are swept off." (11) "...that ye may know the decrees of God—**that ye may repent**, and not continue in your iniquities until the fulness come..." (12) "...whatsoever nation shall possess it shall be free from bondage... **if they will but serve the God of the land, who is Jesus Christ**."

Four conditional hinges: the disjunction ("or"), the moral trigger not a date ("when... ripened"), the explicit delay ("not until the fulness"), the redemptive purpose ("that ye may repent"). The agent of judgment is the **nation/children of the land** collectively; the remedy is **serving God / repenting** — not a party, policy, or leader. **The center of the whole study.**

**1 Nephi 2:20 [ESTABLISHED]** — "**inasmuch as** ye shall keep my commandments, ye shall prosper... a land which is choice above all other lands." The prosperity edge of the same covenant.

**2 Nephi 1:5–11 [ESTABLISHED]** — Lehi's cleanest statement (v.7): "**if** it so be that they shall serve him according to the commandments... it shall be a land of liberty... they shall never be brought down into captivity; **if so, it shall be because of iniquity**." v.10: judgment as a *when/if* clause ("when the time cometh that they shall dwindle in unbelief... if the day shall come that they will reject the Holy One of Israel").

**[INTERPRETIVE — faithful]:** Jaredites (Ether 2), Lehites (2 Ne 1), and latter-day Gentiles (Ether 2:11 "O ye Gentiles") are placed under ONE land-covenant — which is why the study can responsibly apply it to the present nation. Faithful reading, labeled as such.

## (b) The date-stamp doctrine — established firmly

- **Matthew 24:36 [ESTABLISHED]** — "of that day and hour knoweth no man, no, not the angels of heaven, but my Father only."
- **D&C 49:7 [ESTABLISHED]** — "the hour and the day no man knoweth, neither the angels in heaven, **nor shall they know until he comes**." (Stronger: withheld until the event.)
- **D&C 39:21 [ESTABLISHED]** — "the time is at hand; the day or the hour no man knoweth; **but it surely shall come**." (Holds all three poles: nearness + unknowable timing + certainty — the exact non-fatalist, non-date-setting posture.)
- **Matthew 24:42–44 [ESTABLISHED]** — *because* you know not the hour (v.42), *therefore* watch and be ready (v.44). Unknowability is the reason for readiness, not anxiety.
- **D&C 130:12–17 [ESTABLISHED] — THE KEYSTONE.** D&C 87's footnote on "South Carolina" points here: 130:12–13 is Joseph's own return to the 1832 South Carolina war prophecy. But note the dating — **D&C 130 was given at Ramus, Illinois, April 2, 1843** (section heading), about a decade after D&C 87 (Kirtland, Dec 25, 1832). So 130:12–13 (the war prophecy revisited) and 130:14–17 (the date-setting rebuke) are the same prophet returning to the signs and being refused the time — **not the same day as D&C 87.** Joseph "was once praying very earnestly **to know the time of the coming of the Son of Man**" and was told: "Joseph, my son, if thou livest until thou art eighty-five years old, thou shalt see the face of the Son of Man; **therefore let this suffice, and trouble me no more on this matter**." And v.16: he "was left thus, **without being able to decide**" what it even meant. → The prophet who prophesied the war-signs (1832) asked for the date a decade later (1843) and was told to stop asking. **Decisive: the same prophet given the signs was refused the date — D&C 87 is not a date-setting text.**

- **D&C 45:26 [ESTABLISHED]** — names the *opposite* error: men "shall say that Christ **delayeth** his coming." Brackets both failures: neither "I computed the date" nor "it is indefinitely postponed." Also names the emotional failure to prevent: "men's hearts shall fail them" (fear).

## (c) The hope — the response the texts command

- **D&C 87:8 [ESTABLISHED]** — the war prophecy ENDS in: "**stand ye in holy places, and be not moved**, until the day of the Lord come." (Footnote links "holy places" to Matt 24:15, D&C 45:32, 101:22 — a defined covenant concept.)
- **D&C 45:32 [ESTABLISHED]** — "my disciples shall stand in holy places, and shall not be moved."
- **D&C 45:35 [ESTABLISHED]** — "**Be not troubled**, for, when all these things shall come to pass, ye may know that the promises which have been made unto you shall be fulfilled." (The signs are evidence the promises are KEPT — the explicit antidote to fatalism.)
- **D&C 38:30 [ESTABLISHED] — likely the application/Becoming verse** — "**if ye are prepared ye shall not fear.**" Preparation removes fear; conditional structure mirrors the land doctrine (service → freedom; preparation → no fear).

## (d) D&C 87 — what it actually claims, and does NOT

Given Dec 25, 1832 (slavery + S. Carolina nullification disputes). Text [ESTABLISHED]: war "beginning at the rebellion of South Carolina" (v.1) → South vs North, South calls on Great Britain (v.3) → "war... poured out upon all nations" (v.2) → sword, famine, plague, earthquake = the "chastening hand of an Almighty God, **until the consumption decreed hath made a full end of all nations**" (v.6) → "stand ye in holy places" (v.8).
- **DOES claim:** a specific, partly-fulfilled prophecy (S. Carolina 1832 → Fort Sumter 1861); escalation to all nations; a decreed "end of all nations"; the command to stand.
- **Does NOT claim:** any dates ("shortly," "after many days," "quickly" — all relative); that the end is unconditional or scheduled (v.6 ties it to *chastening* — corrective, repentance-aimed).
- **[INTERPRETIVE — faithful]:** read alone, v.6 sounds fatalistic; but its footnotes wire it to the hope/refuge doctrine, its terminal command is hope (v.8), and its own day produced the date-rebuke (D&C 130). So: **87 supplies the *signs*; Ether 2 supplies the *condition*; D&C 130 supplies the *timing discipline*; D&C 38:30/45:35/87:8 supply the *response*.**
- **[SPECULATIVE — exclude]:** mapping v.4–6 (slave uprising, remnants vexing the Gentiles, "full end of all nations") onto specific modern nations / a timeline is beyond the text and collides with the same-day date-rebuke. Resist it.

## (e) The inspired-Constitution doctrine

**D&C 101:77–80 [ESTABLISHED]** — the Constitution's stated *purpose* is **moral agency**: (78) "that every man may act... according to the **moral agency** which I have given unto him, that every man may be accountable"; (79) "it is not right that any man should be in bondage one to another"; (80) "I established the Constitution of this land, by the hands of wise men whom I raised up unto this very purpose, **and redeemed the land by the shedding of blood**." → ties directly to the "whose blood?" thread (topic 06) AND grounds freedom as freedom-*for*-agency.
**D&C 109:54 [ESTABLISHED]** — Kirtland dedicatory *prayer*: "have mercy upon **all the nations of the earth**; have mercy upon the rulers of our land..." Non-triumphalist, non-partisan, global — a counterweight to any America-only reading.

## One-line answer to the governing question
National "end" is **CONDITIONAL, not inevitable** — the study can say so with full textual warrant (Ether 2's disjunction + ripeness + "not until" + "that ye may repent"; Lehi's "if/inasmuch"); D&C 87 ends in "stand in holy places," not dread; the date-stamp is airtight (Matt 24:36; D&C 49:7; 39:21; and decisively 130:14–17, same day as 87); the hope is explicit (45:35 "be not troubled"; 38:30 "if ye are prepared ye shall not fear").
