# D&C 48 — share the land, and wait for the place

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, March 10, 1831.** Saints were migrating from the eastern states to Ohio (per §37, §45:64), and Joseph asked the Lord *how* to procure land for them. Six verses of practical counsel — and a quiet exercise in the discipline of the withheld: the *site* of the city of Zion is not yet to be revealed.

**Binding idea:** Two instructions hold the migration together: *share* — those who have lands "impart to the eastern brethren" (48:2); and *wait* — "the place [of the city] is not yet to be revealed; but… there are to be certain men appointed, and to them it shall be given to know the place" (48:5). Consecration in practice (share the land you have), and patience with the withheld (the place of Zion comes in God's time, through the order).

**Read:** "Remain for the present time in your places of abode… And inasmuch as ye have lands, ye shall impart to the eastern brethren" (48:1-2); "inasmuch as ye have not lands, let them buy for the present time in those regions round about" (48:3). "Save all the money that ye can… that in time ye may be enabled to purchase land for an inheritance, even the city" (48:4). "The place is not yet to be revealed; but after your brethren come from the east there are to be certain men appointed, and to them it shall be given to know the place, or to them it shall be revealed" (48:5). The gathering with families "every man according to his family… as is appointed to him by the presidency and the bishop" (48:6).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "Impart to the eastern brethren" (48:2) — consecration as the first instinct of the gathering.* When the migrants arrive landless, the Ohio Saints' first instruction is to *share their lands*. This is the §42 law of consecration enacted *practically* at the moment of need: those who have, impart to those who have not. The gathering doesn't begin with a building program; it begins with *sharing what's already there* (48:2-3). (The "be one" economy [§38:27] lived out at the wagon's arrival: the eastern brethren show up with nothing, and the law is *give them part of your land*. Zion is built, first, by hospitality — the haves imparting to the have-nots before any city is purchased. The §44:6 care-for-the-poor and the §42:30 consecration are not abstractions; they are *the doorstep response to a landless arriving brother*.)
- *★ "The place is not yet to be revealed" (48:5) — the discipline of the withheld, applied to Zion's site.* The Saints are gathering *toward* a city whose *location they do not yet know* — "the place is not yet to be revealed… to certain men appointed… it shall be revealed." They must *move and prepare* in faith, without the destination disclosed. (This is §25:4's "murmur not… they are withheld… wisdom in me in a time to come" applied to the *most consequential* unknown — *where Zion is*. Emma was told to trust withheld knowledge about doctrine; here a whole people is told to gather toward a place God hasn't named. Faith includes *acting on a withheld destination* — you save your money, buy temporary land, and trust that the place "shall be revealed" through the order when the time comes [§57 reveals it: Independence, Missouri]. The withheld is not absence; it's *not-yet*, and the discipline is to prepare faithfully in the meantime.) And the revealing will come *through the order* — "certain men appointed… it shall be revealed" (48:5), not to any freelance claimant (§28).
- *Ordered, family-by-family gathering (48:6).* Even the gathering is *ordered*: "every man according to his family… as is appointed to him by the presidency and the bishop." Not a chaotic rush but a *structured* migration, each family placed "according to his circumstances" by the leaders. (The §42/§44 order extended to the logistics of gathering — the presidency and bishop direct the settlement, so the gathering is the "be one" people *organized*, not a stampede. Consecration [share the land] + order [appointed settlement] + patience [the withheld place] together make the migration a building-up rather than a scramble.)
