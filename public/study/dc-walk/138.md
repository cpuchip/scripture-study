# D&C 138 — ★★ the redemption of the dead: he organized his forces

**Setting:** **Salt Lake City, October 3, 1918** — a vision given to President Joseph F. Smith, the last section added to the Doctrine and Covenants and **the latest-dated revelation in the book** (received the day before the 89th Semiannual General Conference; canonized in 1976, accepted unanimously). Pondering 1 Peter 3–4 — Christ preaching "to the spirits in prison" — JFS sees the spirit world: the righteous dead rejoicing, the Savior's visit, and *how* the gospel was carried to all the dead. It is the temple/genealogy taproot's final bloom, and the book closes — fittingly — on the redemption of the dead, the doctrine the Malachi bracket opened at §2.

**Binding idea:** **"The Lord went not in person among the wicked… But behold, from among the righteous, he organized his forces and appointed messengers, clothed with power and authority, and commissioned them to go forth and carry the light of the gospel to them that were in darkness… and thus was the gospel preached to the dead" (138:29-30).** Even the redemption of the dead the Lord accomplishes by *organizing and delegating* — He does not do it all in person; He commissions His servants. And the §137 law (judged by the heart's desire) is here given its *machinery*: the dead are *taught*, so they can choose.

**Read:**
- Revelation by pondering: "I sat in my room pondering over the scriptures… As I pondered over these things which are written, the eyes of my understanding were opened, and the Spirit of the Lord rested upon me, and I saw the hosts of the dead, both small and great" (138:1,11).
- The righteous rejoicing: "there were gathered together in one place an innumerable company of the spirits of the just… I beheld that they were filled with joy and gladness, and were rejoicing together because the day of their deliverance was at hand" (138:12,15). "the Son of God appeared, declaring liberty to the captives who had been faithful" (138:18).
- The delegation: "I perceived that the Lord went not in person among the wicked and the disobedient who had rejected the truth, to teach them; But behold, from among the righteous, he organized his forces and appointed messengers, clothed with power and authority, and commissioned them to go forth and carry the light of the gospel to them that were in darkness, even to all the spirits of men; and thus was the gospel preached to the dead" (138:29-30). "...unto whom he could not go personally, because of their rebellion and transgression, that they through the ministration of his servants might also hear his words" (138:37).
- The machinery of mercy: "Thus was the gospel preached to those who had died in their sins, without a knowledge of the truth… These were taught faith in God, repentance from sin, vicarious baptism for the remission of sins… that they might be judged according to men in the flesh, but live according to God in the spirit" (138:32-34).
- Eve and the daughters: "And our glorious Mother Eve, with many of her faithful daughters who had lived through the ages and worshiped the true and living God" (138:39).
- The Malachi bracket, once more: "The Prophet Elijah was to plant in the hearts of the children the promises made to their fathers, Foreshadowing the great work to be done in the temples of the Lord in the dispensation of the fulness of times, for the redemption of the dead, and the sealing of the children to their parents, lest the whole earth be smitten with a curse and utterly wasted at his coming" (138:47-48).
- The body and the continuing labor: "For the dead had looked upon the long absence of their spirits from their bodies as a bondage" (138:50). "I beheld that the faithful elders of this dispensation, when they depart from mortal life, continue their labors in the preaching of the gospel… in the great world of the spirits of the dead" (138:57). "The dead who repent will be redeemed, through obedience to the ordinances of the house of God… for they are heirs of salvation" (138:58-59).

**Thoughts:**

- *★★ Even redeeming the dead, the Lord works by delegation (138:29-37).* The vision's central surprise answers JFS's own wonder — how could Christ, in the brief time between His death and resurrection, possibly reach *all* the dead? The answer is not that He did it faster, but that He did *not do it Himself*: "the Lord went not in person among the wicked… But behold, from among the righteous, he **organized his forces** and **appointed messengers**, clothed with power and authority, and **commissioned them** to go forth and carry the light of the gospel… and thus was the gospel preached to the dead" (138:29-30). (The presiding/delegation pattern at the *cosmic* scale: the Lord of all, with every power, accomplishes the greatest mercy-work in the universe — the redemption of the entire human dead — by *organizing and delegating to His servants*, not by doing it all in person. The §1:38 voice-of-servants ["by mine own voice or by the voice of my servants… it is the same"] and the art-of-delegation given their *highest warrant*: if even *Christ* redeems the dead by commissioning messengers rather than going personally to each, then delegation is not a concession to weakness but the *very pattern of divine work*. And the manner matters — He "clothed [them] with power and authority" and "commissioned them" [138:30, the §121:41-46 presiding terms: the delegate is equipped and sent, not coerced]; the §107 council-order and the §132:7 "never but one… at a time" sealing-order applied to the spirit world. For the project's whole delegation/presiding/substrate-dispatch frame: the deepest model is the Lord who *organizes forces and appoints messengers* — the work scales by faithful delegation under authority, even at the scale of redeeming all the dead. "Unto whom he could not go personally… through the ministration of his servants might also hear his words" [138:37]: the servants reach what the principal, by design, delegates.)

- *★★ §137's law given its machinery — the dead are taught (138:31-34).* §137 gave the *law* — God judges "according to the desire of their hearts," so those who "would have received" are heirs. §138 shows the *machinery* that makes the law operable: the dead are *actually taught* — "faith in God, repentance from sin, vicarious baptism for the remission of sins, the gift of the Holy Ghost" (138:33) — "that they might be judged according to men in the flesh, but live according to God in the spirit" (138:34, 1 Pet 4:6). (The §137 mercy is not a *waiver* but a *real offer*: those who never heard are given the gospel *in fact*, taught and invited, so their choice is genuine. The §128 welding-link [the living perform the vicarious ordinances] and the §137 desire-of-the-heart [God counts the one who would receive] *fuse* here: the dead are taught [§138], they choose [§137's heart], and the living seal it on earth [§128's record that binds in heaven]. The three sections complete one doctrine — the redemption of the dead — and the temple/genealogy taproot the Malachi bracket opened at §2 reaches full bloom. The mercy is *just*: "after they have paid the penalty… and are washed clean, shall receive a reward according to their works" [138:59] — not cheap, but real.)

- *★ The Malachi bracket's final echo — the book closes where it opened (138:46-48).* The walk's central cross-walk thread — Malachi/Elijah, opened at §2 — sounds one last time in the *last* section. JFS sees Malachi himself, "who testified of the coming of Elijah," and names Elijah's mission: "to plant in the hearts of the children the promises made to their fathers, **foreshadowing the great work to be done in the temples**… for the redemption of the dead, and the sealing of the children to their parents, **lest the whole earth be smitten with a curse**" (138:47-48). (The bracket's full arc, now complete across the whole book: §2 [Moroni's 1823 Malachi quote] → §13 [the sons of Levi] → §27:9 [Elijah's keys named] → §110 [Elijah appears] → §128 [the welding-link doctrine, both halves closed] → §138 [the work itself, seen in vision]. The promise that *opened* the canon — that Elijah would turn the hearts lest the earth be wasted — is shown in the *final* section as the redemption of the dead in operation. The book begins and ends on the same taproot.)

- *★ The body is not the prison — its absence is (138:50).* A striking inversion: "the dead had looked upon the long absence of their spirits from their bodies as a **bondage**" (138:50). (Where Platonism treats the body as the soul's prison, §138 says the *opposite* — the disembodied spirit experiences the *lack* of a body as the bondage, and longs for the resurrection. The §93:33-34 "spirit and element, inseparably connected, receive a fulness of joy" / §131:7-8 spirit-is-matter given their *affective* form: even the righteous dead, in paradise, await the body as *deliverance*. The matter-spectrum's body-affirmation reaching its furthest point — the body is so essential to joy that its absence is felt as captivity. For the walk's matter-spectrum thread: this is the final word against any spirit-good/matter-bad dualism — the redeemed *want their bodies back*.)

- *Revelation by pondering scripture (138:1-11).* The vision itself models how revelation comes: "I sat in my room **pondering over the scriptures**… As I pondered… the eyes of my understanding were opened" (138:1,11). (The §9:7-8 "study it out in your mind" and the §76:19 "as we pondered… the Lord touched the eyes of our understandings" — the great visions come to those *already at work in the word*. JFS was reading 1 Peter when the heavens opened. For the project's study discipline: revelation rewards the ponderer; the deepest light comes *through* the patient reading, not around it. The last section of the D&C was born from a man pondering scripture — a fitting close for a book of revelations.)
