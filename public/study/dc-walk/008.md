# D&C 8 — "I will tell you in your mind and in your heart"

**Setting:** Harmony, Pennsylvania, **April 1829.** Oliver, watching the translation flow through Joseph day after day, wanted the gift himself — to translate, not just transcribe. The Lord answers his supplication. This is the section *before* the famous correction in §9; here the Lord says *yes, you can have the gift* — and defines what the gift actually is. (★ Project-source-text: **8:2** is the workspace's own "revelation by the Spirit" anchor, the mind-and-heart pole of the how-revelation-feels triad.)

**Binding idea:** Revelation is not information delivered to the eyes; it is the Holy Ghost speaking *in your mind and in your heart* (8:2). The gift Oliver asks for is real — but it works by the same quiet inward channel as every other gift of the Spirit, and it runs on faith, not on technique (8:10-11).

**Read:** "As surely as the Lord liveth… so surely shall you receive a knowledge of whatsoever things you shall ask in faith, with an honest heart, believing that you shall receive a knowledge concerning the engravings of old records… spoken by the manifestation of my Spirit" (8:1). Then the anchor: **"Yea, behold, I will tell you in your mind and in your heart, by the Holy Ghost, which shall come upon you and which shall dwell in your heart"** (8:2). "Now, behold, this is the spirit of revelation; behold, this is the spirit by which Moses brought the children of Israel through the Red Sea on dry ground" (8:3). "Therefore this is thy gift; apply unto it" (8:4); "remember these words, and keep my commandments. Remember, this is your gift" (8:5). Then a second gift: "you have another gift, which is the gift of Aaron; behold, it has told you many things" (8:6); "there is no other power, save the power of God, that can cause this gift of Aaron to be with you" (8:7); "doubt not, for it is the gift of God; and you shall hold it in your hands, and do marvelous works… for it is the work of God" (8:8). "Remember that without faith you can do nothing; therefore ask in faith. Trifle not with these things; do not ask for that which you ought not" (8:10). "Ask that you may know the mysteries of God, and that you may translate… according to your faith shall it be done unto you" (8:11).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "In your mind and in your heart" (8:2) — the project-source definition of revelation.* This is the verse the workspace leans on for *how* the Spirit speaks, and walking it in context sharpens it. Revelation is dual-channel — *mind* (thought, idea, understanding) AND *heart* (feeling, peace, desire). Not one or the other: the Holy Ghost "shall come upon you" (a visitation) "and which shall dwell in your heart" (a residence). It's both an event and an indwelling. And critically, it is **interior** — God's normal mode is not the brass plates appearing in the air but a knowing that rises *inside* you, indistinguishable in its felt-texture from your own thought, except that it carries light it didn't have before. (This is precisely the seam Michael named in the period-language/Spirit-distillation principle: true distillation feels like your own mind, which is exactly why discernment — is this God or is this me? — is the human's irreducible work by the Spirit. The verse that grounds the principle also grounds the difficulty.)
- *The Red Sea comparison (8:3) raises the stakes enormously.* "This is the spirit by which Moses brought the children of Israel through the Red Sea." The same inward voice that will whisper Oliver a translation is the power that *parted an ocean.* The D&C deliberately refuses to make personal revelation small. The quiet thought in your heart and the wall of water standing up are the same Spirit operating at two scales. (And it reframes the Exodus: Moses didn't part the sea by a wand — he parted it by *revelation*, by knowing-from-God what to do and when.)
- *The gift of Aaron (8:6-8) — the honest historical seam.* The 1833 Book of Commandments printed this as "the gift of working with the rod" (later "the gift of Aaron"). Oliver, by the culture of his time and place, used a divining rod — a folk practice then common in New England. The Lord doesn't mock it or forbid it; He *claims* it: "there is no other power, save the power of God, that can cause this gift of Aaron to be with you" (8:7). This is the **period-language principle as the Lord's own practice** — He meets Oliver inside the frame Oliver actually lives in (rod-and-revelation) and redirects it toward Himself, the way 6:24's witness met Oliver inside his own private memory. God condescends to the seeker's world. (I flag the rod's folk-practice background as the historical fact it is, labeled — not to embarrass it but because the honest setting *is* the point: revelation arrives through culturally-shaped instruments, and the Lord works with the instrument the person brings. The Aaron reference also dignifies it — Aaron's rod budded and swallowed the magicians' serpents; a rod in a true prophet's hand is a sign-instrument of God.)
- *"Without faith you can do nothing… according to your faith shall it be done" (8:10-11) — the gift runs on faith, not mechanism.* Oliver will discover in §9 that he assumed the gift was *automatic* — ask and the words appear. §8 already warned otherwise: the gift is bounded by faith and by *what you ought to ask* ("do not ask for that which you ought not," 8:10). The setup for §9's correction is right here — the Lord grants the gift *and* names its conditions in the same breath; Oliver heard the grant and missed the conditions.
