# D&C 110 — the Lord appears, and Elijah comes: the keys committed

**Setting:** **The Kirtland Temple, April 3, 1836 — Easter Sunday.** A week after the dedication (§109). Joseph had assisted in administering the Lord's Supper; then, "the veils being dropped," he and Oliver Cowdery "bowed… in solemn and silent prayer." What followed answered the dedicatory prayer in the most literal way: the Lord *appeared* and *accepted* the house — and then Moses, Elias, and **Elijah** came, committing their keys. On the day of the Resurrection, the heavens opened in the house built for that purpose.

**Binding idea:** "The time has fully come, which was spoken of by the mouth of Malachi… Therefore, the keys of this dispensation are committed into your hands; and by this ye may know that the great and dreadful day of the Lord is near, even at the doors" (110:14-16). The Elijah of Malachi *came* — and with him, the sealing keys: the prophecy that opened this whole walk (§2) is, here, *fulfilled*.

**Read:** "The veil was taken from our minds, and the eyes of our understanding were opened. We saw the Lord standing upon the breastwork of the pulpit… his eyes were as a flame of fire; the hair of his head was white like the pure snow; his countenance shone above the brightness of the sun; and his voice was as the sound of the rushing of great waters, even the voice of Jehovah" (110:1-3). "I am the first and the last; I am he who liveth, I am he who was slain; I am your advocate with the Father" (110:4). "Your sins are forgiven you; you are clean before me… I have accepted this house, and my name shall be here; and I will manifest myself to my people in mercy in this house. Yea, I will appear unto my servants… if my people will keep my commandments, and do not pollute this holy house" (110:5-8). "Moses appeared before us, and committed unto us the keys of the gathering of Israel from the four parts of the earth, and the leading of the ten tribes from the land of the north" (110:11). "Elias appeared, and committed the dispensation of the gospel of Abraham, saying that in us and our seed all generations after us should be blessed" (110:12). "Elijah the prophet, who was taken to heaven without tasting death, stood before us, and said: Behold, the time has fully come, which was spoken of by the mouth of Malachi… to turn the hearts of the fathers to the children, and the children to the fathers, lest the whole earth be smitten with a curse— Therefore, the keys of this dispensation are committed into your hands" (110:13-16).

**Thoughts:**
- *★★ Elijah comes — the Malachi bracket fulfilled (110:13-16).* The climax not only of this section but of a thread running the *entire walk*. At §2 — the *second* section of the Doctrine and Covenants, Moroni's 1823 visit — the Lord promised through Malachi that *Elijah would come*, "before the coming of the great and dreadful day of the Lord," to "plant in the hearts of the children the promises made to the fathers… lest the whole earth be utterly wasted." And *here* — §110 — Elijah *actually appears* and *commits the keys*: "the time has fully come, which was spoken of by the mouth of Malachi… therefore, the keys of this dispensation are committed into your hands" (110:14-16). (The walk's central cross-walk thread — the **Malachi bracket** [§2 ↔ §13 ↔ §27:9 ↔ §110] — reaches its *fulfillment*. The promise that *opened* the canon is *kept* at section 110: the sealing/temple/genealogy taproot, planted at §2, *blooms*. "Turn the hearts of the fathers to the children, and the children to the fathers" — the sealing power that binds families across generations [§128, the work for the dead], the reason "the whole earth" would otherwise "be smitten with a curse." - *★ The risen Lord on Easter — "I am he who liveth, I am he who was slain" (110:1-8).* The timing is not incidental: it is *Easter Sunday*, the day of the Resurrection, and the *risen* Lord appears. "I am the first and the last; **I am he who liveth, I am he who was slain**; I am your advocate with the Father" (110:4). (The §76:22 "that he lives! And the conditional from §94/§97/§109 is restated: "I will appear unto my servants… *if* my people will keep my commandments, and do not pollute this holy house" [110:8] — the presence given, the holiness still required.)
- *★ Moses and Elias — the gathering and the Abrahamic keys (110:11-12).* Before Elijah, two more: *Moses* committed "the keys of the gathering of Israel from the four parts of the earth, and the leading of the ten tribes from the land of the north" [110:11] — the *gathering* keys [§29/§45/§101; the §110 fulfillment of the gathering thread]. And *Elias* committed "the dispensation of the gospel of Abraham, saying that in us and our seed all generations after us should be blessed" [110:12, Abr 2:11/Gen 12:3] — the *Abrahamic covenant* keys, the §86:8 "lawful heirs" thread [blessed to bless "all generations"]. (The §27:5-13 "gathering of all keys" reaching its *temple* fulfillment: the keys named at §27 are *delivered* at §110, in the house built for it. Three messengers, three keys: Moses [gathering], Elias [the Abrahamic blessing], Elijah [sealing] — the full apparatus of the latter-day work *committed* in one Easter afternoon. The Restoration's "convergence of every dispensation" [§27:13] localized to a pulpit. The endowment §95/§105 prayed at §109 *is* this — the elders endowed with the keys of heaven.)
