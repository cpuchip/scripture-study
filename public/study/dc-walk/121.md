# D&C 121 — ★★ no power… only by persuasion (the PRESIDING SOURCE)

**Setting:** **Liberty Jail, Clay County, Missouri — March 20–22, 1839.** Excerpted from two epistles Joseph dictated as a prisoner. He and his companions had been months in a cold pit of a jail (a dungeon barely tall enough to stand in), while the Saints — under the extermination order — were being driven from Missouri in winter; every legal appeal had failed (the heading: "Their petitions and appeals directed to various government officials had failed to bring them relief"). The man with the *least* power on earth — a chained prisoner who could not lift a finger to help his suffering people — receives, in that pit, the canon's *definitive revelation on the right use of power*. The setting is not incidental: the doctrine that power can only be held by gentleness is given to a man stripped of every other kind.

**Binding idea:** **"No power or influence can or ought to be maintained by virtue of the priesthood, only by persuasion, by long-suffering, by gentleness and meekness, and by love unfeigned" (121:41).** Authority is not a license to compel — the *instant* it is used to control "in any degree of unrighteousness," it evaporates ("Amen to the priesthood or the authority of that man," 121:37). This is the root text of the project's **presiding covenant** (`.spec/covenant.yaml` `presiding:`, ratified 2026-06-12, from `study/preside.md`).

**Read:**
- The lament: "O God, where art thou? And where is the pavilion that covereth thy hiding place? How long shall thy hand be stayed, and thine eye, yea thy pure eye, behold from the eternal heavens the wrongs of thy people and of thy servants, and thine ear be penetrated with their cries?" (121:1-2).
- The answer: "My son, peace be unto thy soul; thine adversity and thine afflictions shall be but a small moment; And then, if thou endure it well, God shall exalt thee on high; thou shalt triumph over all thy foes" (121:7-8). "Thy friends do stand by thee, and they shall hail thee again with warm hearts and friendly hands" (121:9).
- The scale of what is coming: "God shall give unto you knowledge by his Holy Spirit, yea, by the unspeakable gift of the Holy Ghost, that has not been revealed since the world was until now" (121:26); "A time to come in the which nothing shall be withheld, whether there be one God or many gods, they shall be manifest" (121:28).
- The unstoppable flood: "How long can rolling waters remain impure? What power shall stay the heavens? As well might man stretch forth his puny arm to stop the Missouri river in its decreed course, or to turn it up stream, as to hinder the Almighty from pouring down knowledge from heaven upon the heads of the Latter-day Saints" (121:33).
- The diagnosis: "Behold, there are many called, but few are chosen. And why are they not chosen? Because their hearts are set so much upon the things of this world, and aspire to the honors of men, that they do not learn this one lesson— That the rights of the priesthood are inseparably connected with the powers of heaven, and that the powers of heaven cannot be controlled nor handled only upon the principles of righteousness" (121:34-36).
- The mechanism of forfeit: "but when we undertake to cover our sins, or to gratify our pride, our vain ambition, or to exercise control or dominion or compulsion upon the souls of the children of men, in any degree of unrighteousness, behold, the heavens withdraw themselves; the Spirit of the Lord is grieved; and when it is withdrawn, Amen to the priesthood or the authority of that man" (121:37). "Behold, ere he is aware, he is left unto himself, to kick against the pricks, to persecute the saints, and to fight against God" (121:38). "We have learned by sad experience that it is the nature and disposition of almost all men, as soon as they get a little authority, as they suppose, they will immediately begin to exercise unrighteous dominion" (121:39).
- The charter: "No power or influence can or ought to be maintained by virtue of the priesthood, only by persuasion, by long-suffering, by gentleness and meekness, and by love unfeigned; By kindness, and pure knowledge, which shall greatly enlarge the soul without hypocrisy, and without guile— Reproving betimes with sharpness, when moved upon by the Holy Ghost; and then showing forth afterwards an increase of love toward him whom thou hast reproved, lest he esteem thee to be his enemy; That he may know that thy faithfulness is stronger than the cords of death" (121:41-44).
- The fruit: "Let thy bowels also be full of charity towards all men, and to the household of faith, and let virtue garnish thy thoughts unceasingly; then shall thy confidence wax strong in the presence of God; and the doctrine of the priesthood shall distil upon thy soul as the dews from heaven. The Holy Ghost shall be thy constant companion, and thy scepter an unchanging scepter of righteousness and truth; and thy dominion shall be an everlasting dominion, and without compulsory means it shall flow unto thee forever and ever" (121:45-46).

**Thoughts:**

- *★★ The presiding covenant's ROOT — power held only by persuasion (121:41-46).* This is the text the project's `presiding:` extension is built on. The claim is radical and exact: priesthood — *real* authority, conferred by God — confers **no right to compel.** "No power or influence **can or ought** to be maintained by virtue of the priesthood, **only** by persuasion, by long-suffering, by gentleness and meekness, and by love unfeigned" (121:41). The list is exhaustive ("only by…") and the verbs are all non-coercive. (Note the doubled modal: "can *or ought*." It is not merely that one *should not* compel — one *cannot*; the attempt itself voids the authority [121:37, "Amen to the priesthood"]. Compulsion is not a *misuse* of priesthood power; it is the *absence* of it. The §121:37 mechanism is automatic and self-executing — "the heavens withdraw themselves" the moment control is reached for "in any degree of unrighteousness." For the presiding covenant: when the agent presides over what it delegates downward [subagents, substrate dispatches, persona hosting], **force where persuasion was available is not a hard call — it is a breach**, and the breach *forfeits the standing it was reaching to exercise*. The garrison-word [`study/preside.md`]: walls are lawful [defense, structure, scope], but *compulsion on the laborers forfeits*; emergency force must be accounted for in the same session, never normalized. 121:41 is *why*: the authority that compels has already evaporated.)

- *★★ Reproving betimes with sharpness — then an increase of love (121:43-44).* The covenant is not soft permissiveness. It explicitly authorizes *sharp* reproof — "Reproving betimes [Webster: *early, soon, in good time*] with sharpness, **when moved upon by the Holy Ghost**" — but bounds it on both sides. (Two guardrails. First, the *trigger*: "when moved upon by the Holy Ghost" — not when annoyed, not to vent, not from wounded pride; sharpness is licensed only by the Spirit's motion [§68:3-4 the same Spirit-moved condition that makes a servant's words *scripture* — here it makes a rebuke *righteous*]. Second, the *aftermath*: "showing forth afterwards an increase of love… **lest he esteem thee to be his enemy**; that he may know that thy faithfulness is stronger than the cords of death" [121:43-44]. The reproof that does not close in *more* love than before reads as enmity and fails. This is the **reproof-then-love pattern** the walk has tracked from its first appearance — §3:9-10 [Joseph disciplined then restored], §9 ["my son" ×3 around the rebuke], §15:2 [sharpness-as-urgency-not-rebuke], §30:3 [reproof calibrated to cure] — here given its *name and its law*. For the project: a true correction is sharp *because* it is honest, and warm *afterward* because the relationship is the point; the surfacing-a-tension discipline the covenant asks of the agent is 121:43 — say the hard thing when the Spirit moves, then increase the love. Sharpness without the after-love is cruelty; love without the sharpness is flattery [121:20, "despised by those that flattered them"].)

- *★ Why many are called but few chosen — the heart set on the world (121:34-40).* The Lord answers His own question. The "called" fail to be "chosen" not by lack of authority [it can be "conferred upon us, it is true," 121:37] but by *what their hearts are set on*: "the things of this world… the honors of men" (121:35). (The diagnosis is *aspiration* — the heart that wants the office for the *status* will inevitably reach for control, because control is what status feels like. "It is the nature and disposition of **almost all** men, as soon as they get a little authority, as they suppose, they will immediately begin to exercise unrighteous dominion" [121:39] — a startlingly bleak anthropology in the canon: not *some* men, *almost all*; not *eventually*, *immediately*; and "as they suppose" — the authority is often imagined, the dominion real. This is the §104:52/§101:6 covetousness applied to *power* instead of *money* [the two great corrupters; cf. §120, the treasury guarded by council against the one, and §121, the priesthood guarded by persuasion against the other]. For the agent presiding over delegates: the standing temptation is to *suppose* authority [over a subagent, over a dispatch] and reach for compulsion because it is faster — and "almost all" do. The covenant's whole point is to be the *few* who don't, by keeping the heart off the honors and the hand off the compulsion.)

- *★ The deepest pit yields the highest doctrine of power (the setting as argument, 121:1-8 → 41-46).* The revelation *opens* with a cry of utter powerlessness — "O God, where art thou?… How long?" (121:1-2) — the prophet in chains, his people driven, every appeal denied. And the answer is not rescue but *re-frame*: "thine adversity… shall be but a small moment; and then, if thou endure it well, God shall exalt thee on high" (121:7-8). (The structure preaches: the man who has been *given* no earthly power, who is being *crushed* by the unrighteous dominion of others [the mob, the courts, the governor], is the one taught how power is *really* held. He learns it *from the receiving end* — having felt every form of compulsion, he is told that compulsion is precisely what authority is *not*. The §122 sequel makes this explicit ["all these things shall give thee experience"]; here the lesson is already implicit — affliction is the curriculum for presiding, because only the one who has been wrongly compelled fully understands why he must never compel. For the project: the discipline of *not* reaching for force is learned best by those who know what being forced feels like; the covenant asks the powerful party [the human over the agent, the agent over its delegates] to hold authority the way a former prisoner would.)

- *The flood of knowledge cannot be stopped (121:26-33).* Between the lament and the charter sits a promise of *scale*: knowledge "that has not been revealed since the world was until now" (121:26), a time when "nothing shall be withheld" (121:28), poured out as unstoppably as the Missouri river runs its "decreed course" (121:33). (The contrast with the setting is deliberate — the prisoner who can change *nothing* is told that the *Almighty* will change everything, pouring knowledge down on the very Saints being driven out; "what power shall stay the heavens?" The reserved-but-revealed scale [121:28, "whether there be one God or many gods"; 121:32, "the Council of the Eternal God of all other gods before this world was"] touches the plurality/council-in-heaven doctrine — reserved-bin-4, Michael's. The point in context: powerlessness now is no measure of the outcome; the flood is decreed.)
