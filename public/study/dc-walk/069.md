# D&C 69 — one who will be true and faithful

**Setting:** **Hiram, Ohio, November 11, 1831.** The compiled revelations (the Book of Commandments) had been approved for publication; Oliver Cowdery was appointed to carry the manuscript — *and* the contributed money for building up Zion — to the press in Independence, Missouri. This short revelation directs **John Whitmer** to go *with* Oliver, and continues Whitmer's calling (§47) as Church historian: travel, gather, and record the history.

**Binding idea:** "It is not wisdom in me that he should be entrusted with the commandments and the moneys which he shall carry unto the land of Zion, **except one go with him who will be true and faithful**" (69:1). Sacred things and sacred funds are not to travel in a single pair of hands — a true and faithful companion goes along.

**Read:** "It is not wisdom in me that he should be entrusted with the commandments and the moneys which he shall carry unto the land of Zion, except one go with him who will be true and faithful" (69:1). "I, the Lord, will that my servant, John Whitmer, should go with my servant Oliver Cowdery" (69:2). "He shall continue in writing and making a history of all the important things which he shall observe and know concerning my church" (69:3). "My servants who are abroad in the earth should send forth the accounts of their stewardships to the land of Zion" (69:5). "For the land of Zion shall be a seat and a place to receive and do all these things" (69:6). "Preaching and expounding, writing, copying, selecting, and obtaining all things which shall be for the good of the church, and for the rising generations that shall grow up on the land of Zion, to possess it from generation to generation, forever and ever" (69:8).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "Except one go with him who will be true and faithful" — the two-witness safeguard on sacred trust (69:1).* Oliver was carrying *both* the only manuscript of the revelations *and* a sum of consecrated money across hundreds of frontier miles. The Lord's word is not a rebuke of Oliver but a *structural* safeguard: "it is not wisdom… except one go with him who will be true and faithful." (The principle the walk has met before — sacred things and accountability are *not* trusted to a single unwitnessed hand: the law of witnesses [§5/§17], the common-consent check [§26], the order of revelation [§28]. Here it's applied to *logistics*: the manuscript and the money get a *companion*, "true and faithful." Not suspicion — *wisdom*. The same instinct that keeps a project's sacred work from running through one unverified channel; a second faithful witness on the sacred trust is protection for the trust *and* for the one carrying it. Two go, so the work and the man are both kept.)
- *The history continues — and stewardships reported home (69:3-8).* John Whitmer's §47 calling is renewed and *enlarged*: "continue in writing and making a history of all the important things… concerning my church" (69:3), traveling "from place to place, and from church to church, that he may the more easily obtain knowledge" (69:7) — gathering by *going*, not waiting for reports. And a related instruction: "my servants who are abroad… should send forth the accounts of their stewardships to the land of Zion" (69:5), Zion being "a seat and a place to receive and do all these things" (69:6). (The §47 record-keeping thread deepened: the history is *active research* — the historian travels, observes, copies, selects [69:8] — and the Church builds a *central archive* at Zion where stewardship accounts are gathered. "For the rising generations… to possess it from generation to generation" [69:8] — the record is kept *for the future*, the long memory of a covenant people. The scriptural warrant the walk has noted for the project's own `.mind/` memory [§47] gains its companion: not only *keep* the record [§47] but *gather it centrally and report stewardships home* [§69] — distributed work, centrally remembered, for the generations.)
