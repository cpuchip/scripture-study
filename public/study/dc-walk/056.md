# D&C 56 — wo to the hoarding rich, and wo to the covetous poor

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, June 15, 1831.** **Ezra Thayre** had not obeyed an earlier revelation about his duties on the farm where he lived, and was now to travel to Missouri (§52:22). This revelation *revokes* that call (Thayre's pride and selfishness disqualified him for now) and, widening out, delivers a stinging two-edged warning about wealth and poverty — the most balanced word in the early D&C on *the heart behind the money*.

**Binding idea:** Greed is condemned at *both ends*. "Wo unto you rich men, that will not give your substance to the poor… your riches will canker your souls" (56:16) — and equally, "Wo unto you poor men, whose hearts are not broken… whose eyes are full of greediness, and who will not labor with your own hands!" (56:17). The sin isn't being rich or being poor; it's the *covetous, unbroken heart* — which the rich express by hoarding and the poor by coveting and idleness. The blessing is for "the poor **who are pure in heart**" (56:18).

**Read:** "Mine anger is kindled against the rebellious" (56:1); "he that will not take up his cross and follow me… shall not be saved" (56:2). "I, the Lord, command; and he that will not obey shall be cut off… after I have commanded and the commandment is broken" (56:3); "I, the Lord, command and revoke, as it seemeth me good; and all this to be answered upon the heads of the rebellious" (56:4). Thayre's call is revoked; he "must repent of his pride, and of his selfishness" (56:8). "Your sins… are not pardoned, because you seek to counsel in your own ways" (56:14). "Wo unto you rich men, that will not give your substance to the poor, for your riches will canker your souls… The harvest is past, the summer is ended, and my soul is not saved!" (56:16). "Wo unto you poor men, whose hearts are not broken, whose spirits are not contrite, and whose bellies are not satisfied, and whose hands are not stayed from laying hold upon other men's goods, whose eyes are full of greediness, and who will not labor with your own hands!" (56:17). "But blessed are the poor who are pure in heart, whose hearts are broken, and whose spirits are contrite, for they shall see the kingdom of God coming in power and great glory unto their deliverance; for the fatness of the earth shall be theirs" (56:18); "their generations shall inherit the earth from generation to generation, forever and ever" (56:20).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ The reciprocal wo — greed at both ends (56:16-17).* This is the section's great contribution and a crucial *correction* to any flat reading of the consecration/equality thread. The Lord does *not* say "wo to the rich, blessed are the poor." He says **wo to the hoarding rich AND wo to the covetous, idle poor**. The rich man's sin is *refusing to give* ("will not give your substance to the poor," 56:16); the poor man's sin is *coveting and refusing to work* ("hands… not stayed from laying hold upon other men's goods… will not labor with your own hands," 56:17). The *same* sin — greed, the unbroken covetous heart — wears two costumes: in the rich it hoards; in the poor it covets and idles. (This is essential for the "be one" economy [§38:27, §49:20]: equality is *not* achieved by demonizing wealth and sanctifying poverty. The poor are *not automatically righteous* — a poor man "full of greediness" who "will not labor" is condemned alongside the hoarding rich. Consecration requires *broken hearts* on both sides — the rich willing to give, the poor willing to *work* and not covet. The §49:20 "the world lieth in sin" because "one man possesses above another" is here balanced: the *poor* also sin if their hearts are covetous and idle. The Zion economy isn't class warfare; it's the conversion of the *heart* — at every income level.)
- *★ "Blessed are the poor who are *pure in heart*" (56:18) — not the poor as such.* The beatitude is *qualified*: "blessed are the poor **who are pure in heart, whose hearts are broken, and whose spirits are contrite**" (Matt 5:3, "poor in *spirit*"). It's not poverty that's blessed — it's the *pure, broken, contrite heart*, which the faithful poor often have but the covetous poor (56:17) lack. To *them* — the poor-and-pure — "the fatness of the earth shall be theirs" (56:18), "their generations shall inherit the earth" (56:20). (The inheritance goes to the *heart*, not the bracket: the pure-in-heart poor inherit the earth, the covetous poor get the same wo as the hoarding rich. The §52 discernment pattern [contrite + broken] applied to *economic* righteousness: the test is the heart's condition, not the bank balance.)
- *★ God "commands and revokes, as it seemeth me good… answered upon the heads of the rebellious" (56:3-4).* Revelation can be *withdrawn*. Thayre's call to Missouri is *revoked* because of his pride and selfishness (56:4-8) — and the revoking is "answered upon the heads of the rebellious," i.e., *their fault*, not God's caprice. (The §3 / §10:2 pattern — Joseph's lost gift — generalized: a commandment given can be *taken back* when the one commanded won't obey it. God's commands are not unconditional entitlements; persistent disobedience forfeits them. And it's *exactly just*: "all this to be answered upon the heads of the rebellious" — when God revokes a call, the loss is the rebel's doing. The §53:6 "the residue according to your labor" from the dark side: as faithfulness unfolds more, rebellion *retracts* what was given.)
- *"Not pardoned, because you seek to counsel in your own ways" (56:14).* The un-pardon's root: "ye seek to counsel in your own ways" — the §22:4 "seek not to counsel your God" again. The Thompson Saints (and Thayre) weren't forgiven because they kept trying to *run the consecration their own way* rather than receive God's terms. (The recurring sin of the willful heart: *counseling* God instead of *taking counsel from* Him [Jacob 4:10]. The whole §54-56 Thompson crisis — Copley's breach, Thayre's disobedience, the divisions over property — traces to people *counseling in their own ways* about consecration rather than submitting to the law as given. Self-will is the engine under both the hoarding and the coveting.)

**Notable / flag:** No spin-off. **Walk's contribution:** **§56 corrects the consecration/equality thread with the reciprocal wo (56:16-17)** — greed is condemned at *both ends*: the hoarding rich ("will not give… riches will canker your souls") *and* the covetous, idle poor ("full of greediness… will not labor with your own hands"). The *same* sin — the unbroken covetous heart — hoards in the rich and covets-and-idles in the poor. So the "be one" economy is *not* class warfare and the poor are *not* automatically righteous; equality is the conversion of the *heart* at every income level. The blessing is reserved for **"the poor who are pure in heart… broken… contrite"** (56:18, Matt 5:3's "poor in spirit") — the inheritance goes to the *heart*, not the bracket. Plus **God "commands and revokes"** (56:3-4) — a call can be *withdrawn* when the one commanded won't obey, the loss "answered upon the heads of the rebellious"; and the root of the whole Thompson crisis is **"counsel in your own ways"** (56:14 = §22:4), self-will running consecration its own way instead of receiving God's terms.
