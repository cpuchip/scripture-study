# D&C 99 — until your children are provided for

**Setting:** **Hiram, Ohio, August 29, 1832** (out of chronological order in the book) — to **John Murdock**, a widower. His wife, Julia Clapp, had died in April 1831, and for over a year he had preached while his "motherless" children lived with other families. The Lord calls him to the eastern mission — but *with a condition about his children*.

**Binding idea:** "It is not expedient that you should go until your children are provided for, and sent up kindly unto the bishop of Zion" (99:6). The mission is real, and so is the duty to the children — and the duty comes *first*: provide for them, *then* go.

**Read:** "Thou art called to go into the eastern countries… to proclaim mine everlasting gospel… in the midst of persecution and wickedness" (99:1). "Who receiveth you receiveth me; and you shall have power to declare my word in the demonstration of my Holy Spirit" (99:2). "Who receiveth you as a little child, receiveth my kingdom; and blessed are they, for they shall obtain mercy" (99:3). "It is not expedient that you should go until your children are provided for, and sent up kindly unto the bishop of Zion" (99:6). "After a few years, if thou desirest of me, thou mayest go up also unto the goodly land, to possess thine inheritance" (99:7).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ The children provided for, before the mission (99:6).* The Lord calls Murdock to preach — and then *holds him back* until the children are cared for: "it is not expedient that you should go until your children are provided for, and sent up kindly unto the bishop of Zion." (The §75:24-28 "in nowise lose his crown" / the family cared for, applied to a *widowed father*: the missionary call does *not* override the duty to the children — "provided for, and sent up *kindly*" [the tenderness in *kindly*]. A man does not abandon his motherless children to "serve the Lord"; the serving *includes* providing for them first, then the children pass to the bishop of Zion's care [§83:5-6, the storehouse for the orphan]. The §1 Tim 5:8 "provide for your own" and the §83 church-as-net working together: Murdock provides, *and* the church receives the children "kindly." For the project's stewardship pattern: the nearest duty [the children] is *not* in competition with the higher call [the mission] — it is the *precondition* of it; you do not sacrifice the children to the cause. The Lord Himself sequences it: family first, then go.)
- *"Who receiveth you as a little child… shall obtain mercy" (99:2-3).* The missionary commission with its dignity: "who receiveth you receiveth me" [Matt 10:40]; and "who receiveth you *as a little child*, receiveth my kingdom" [99:3, Matt 18:4 — receive the messenger with a child's humility]. (The §75:21 witness-establishes-accountability paired with its *positive* side: to receive the servant *humbly* [as a child] is to receive the kingdom and "obtain mercy." And the "demonstration of my Holy Spirit" [99:2, 1 Cor 2:4] — the power to declare is the Spirit's *demonstration*, not the preacher's eloquence [§67/§88 — the substance, not the polish]. Even for a grieving widower, the call carries power: the Spirit demonstrates through him.)
