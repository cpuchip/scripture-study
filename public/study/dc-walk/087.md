# D&C 87 — the prophecy on war, and stand ye in holy places

**Setting:** **At or near Kirtland, Ohio, December 25, 1832.** Disputes over slavery and South Carolina's nullification of federal tariffs were boiling; Joseph's history notes that "appearances of troubles among the nations" had become "more visible" than at any time since the Church began. On Christmas Day, the Lord gave "a revelation and prophecy on war" — naming **South Carolina** as the starting point of wars that would spread "upon all nations." (South Carolina seceded on December 20, 1860; the Civil War began at Fort Sumter, South Carolina, in April 1861 — twenty-eight years after this dated prophecy.)

**Binding idea:** "Thus saith the Lord concerning the wars that will shortly come to pass, **beginning at the rebellion of South Carolina**, which will eventually terminate in the death and misery of many souls" (87:1). A specific, dated, falsifiable prophecy — and the disciple's response to a world heading into war: "**stand ye in holy places, and be not moved**" (87:8).

**Read:** "Verily, thus saith the Lord concerning the wars that will shortly come to pass, beginning at the rebellion of South Carolina, which will eventually terminate in the death and misery of many souls" (87:1). "The time will come that war will be poured out upon all nations, beginning at this place" (87:2). "The Southern States shall be divided against the Northern States, and the Southern States will call on other nations, even the nation of Great Britain… and then war shall be poured out upon all nations" (87:3). "After many days, slaves shall rise up against their masters, who shall be marshaled and disciplined for war" (87:4). "With the sword and by bloodshed the inhabitants of the earth shall mourn; and with famine, and plague, and earthquake, and the thunder of heaven… shall the inhabitants of the earth be made to feel the wrath… of an Almighty God, until the consumption decreed hath made a full end of all nations" (87:6). "Wherefore, stand ye in holy places, and be not moved, until the day of the Lord come; for behold, it cometh quickly" (87:8).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ A specific, dated, fulfilled prophecy — South Carolina and the Civil War (87:1-4).* The remarkable thing about §87 is its *specificity*. Not "there will be wars" [vague, unfalsifiable] but "the wars… **beginning at the rebellion of South Carolina**" (87:1), with the South "divided against" the North and calling on "Great Britain" (87:3), and "slaves shall rise up against their masters" (87:4). (Given in 1832 — *twenty-eight years before* South Carolina's secession [Dec 1860] and the firing on Fort Sumter [April 1861]. The prophecy names the *state* where it would begin, the *sectional* division [South vs. North], the *foreign appeal* [the South did seek British recognition and aid], and the *slaves rising* [emancipation and Black troops]. This is the kind of concrete, checkable prediction that the faith-and-evidence thread of the walk has tracked: §5 [proof can't *compel* faith] and §63 [signs come *by* faith, not faith by signs] — and yet here is a *sign given in advance*, dated and named, that *did* come to pass. The point is not that the prophecy *proves* the work to a skeptic [it can't, §5/§63 — the sign-seeker would explain it away], but that to the *believer* it is a confirming witness: God *knows the end from the beginning*, and a prophet spoke it plainly decades early. For a project concerned with honest, verifiable claims: §87 is a falsifiable prophecy that was *not* falsified — the rare scriptural prediction with a date, a place, and a documented fulfillment.)
- *★ "War poured out upon all nations… a full end of all nations" (87:2-6).* The prophecy does *not* stop at the Civil War — it widens: "war will be poured out upon **all nations**, beginning at this place" (87:2), with "famine, and plague, and earthquake… until the consumption decreed hath made a full end of all nations" (87:6). (The Civil War is the *beginning*, not the whole — the §45/§29 last-days desolation. The wars that *began* at South Carolina become a pattern of escalating global conflict [the twentieth century's world wars and the convulsions since fit the shape, though the walk does not pin specific later events the text doesn't name]. The arc is toward "a full end of all nations" — not annihilation of *people* but the end of the *present world order* before "the day of the Lord" [87:8]. The point [87:7]: this continues "that the cry… of the blood of the saints, shall cease to come up into the ears of the Lord of Sabaoth" — the judgments are, in part, the *avenging* of the persecuted [Rev 6:10]. The God who restrained the angels for the wheat's sake [§86:6] does, in the end, answer the blood of the saints.)
- *★ "Stand ye in holy places, and be not moved" (87:8).* After the catalogue of war and calamity, the disciple's whole instruction: not flee, not fight, not predict — *stand in holy places*. (The §45:32 / §101:22 refuge thread: amid the world's convulsions, the faithful are not promised *exemption* but a *place to stand* — "holy places," "and be not moved." The holy place is wherever the covenant is kept [the temple, the gathered Zion, the consecrated home, the steadfast heart]; standing there, the disciple is *not moved* by the surrounding upheaval. This is the §61 "the faithful shall not perish by the waters" / §71 "no weapon shall prosper" posture toward the *largest* scale of trouble — global war: don't be moved. The response to a prophecy of universal calamity is not panic or survivalism but *steadfastness in a holy place*. "It cometh quickly" [87:8] — the urgency is real, and the instruction is *stand*, not run.)
