# D&C 95 — whom I love I also chasten

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, June 1, 1833** — a continuation of the §88 command to build the house of the Lord. Months had passed; the Saints had *not* built it. This revelation is a *chastening* for the delay — and, characteristically, the chastening opens with *love*.

**Binding idea:** "Verily, thus saith the Lord unto you whom I love, and whom I love I also chasten that their sins may be forgiven; for with the chastisement I prepare a way for their deliverance… and I have loved you" (95:1). The chastening is not the withdrawal of love but its *operation* — God chastens *because* He loves, to forgive and to deliver.

**Read:** "Whom I love I also chasten that their sins may be forgiven; for with the chastisement I prepare a way for their deliverance in all things out of temptation, and I have loved you" (95:1). "Ye must needs be chastened and stand rebuked before my face; for ye have sinned against me a very grievous sin, in that ye have not considered the great commandment… concerning the building of mine house" (95:2-3). "I design to endow those whom I have chosen with power from on high; for this is the promise of the Father unto you; therefore I command you to tarry, even as mine apostles at Jerusalem" (95:8-9). "There are many who have been ordained among you, whom I have called but few of them are chosen… they who are not chosen have sinned a very grievous sin, in that they are walking in darkness at noon-day" (95:5-6). "It is my will that you should build a house. If you keep my commandments you shall have power to build it. If you keep not my commandments, the love of the Father shall not continue with you, therefore you shall walk in darkness" (95:11-12). "Let the house be built, not after the manner of the world… let it be built after the manner which I shall show unto three of you" (95:13-14).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "Whom I love I also chasten" (95:1) — chastening as the operation of love.* The section's first word about a *rebuke* is *love*: "unto you whom I love, and whom I love I also chasten that their sins may be forgiven; for with the chastisement I prepare a way for their deliverance" (95:1). (Hebrews 12:6 ["whom the Lord loveth he chasteneth"] given a *purpose-clause*: the chastening is *for forgiveness* and *for deliverance* — "with the chastisement I prepare a way for their deliverance… out of temptation." The reprove-then-love thread [§3:9-10 / §95 / §121:43] reaches its clearest statement: chastening is not the *opposite* of love but its *instrument* — the Father corrects the child He loves, to forgive and free him. "And I have loved you" closes the verse [95:1] — the chastening is bracketed by love on both sides. For anyone under God's correction: the rebuke is the love, not its withdrawal; "with the chastisement I prepare a way for their deliverance." The §90:36 "chasten her until she overcomes and is clean" and the §1:27 nevertheless — God's severity is the severity of a Father who intends to *deliver*.)
- *★ Rebuked for delaying the house — the endowment of power (95:3,8-9).* The "very grievous sin" is *not building the temple* — "ye have not considered the great commandment… concerning the building of mine house" (95:3). And the reason it matters: "I design to **endow those whom I have chosen with power from on high**… therefore I command you to tarry, even as mine apostles at Jerusalem" (95:8-9, Luke 24:49). (The temple exists *for the endowment* — the §38:32 "endowed with power from on high" thread reaching its building: the house is where God will "endow" His chosen with power, as the apostles waited at Jerusalem for Pentecost. The delay isn't mere slowness in construction; it *withholds the endowment* the house was to deliver. I flag the endowment-of-power thread [95:8; §38:32; §105:11; §110] as the temple-reserved thread, Michael's — but the structural point is clear: the house is *for* power-from-on-high, and to delay it is to delay the gift it carries.)
- *★ "Many called, but few chosen… walking in darkness at noon-day" (95:5-6).* A sobering line: "many… have been ordained among you, whom I have called but **few of them are chosen**… they who are not chosen have sinned… in that they are walking in **darkness at noon-day**" (95:5-6). (The many-called-few-chosen [Matt 20:16, fully unfolded at §121:34-40 — "why are they not chosen? Because their hearts are set… upon the things of this world"]: *ordination* is not *election* — to be *called* is not yet to be *chosen*; the choosing depends on faithfulness. And "darkness at noon-day" is the sharpest image — the light is *fully present* [noon], and they walk in darkness anyway, by their own refusal [§88:67 the single eye admits the light; these shut it out at the brightest hour]. The §84:54 treating-lightly and the §95:12 "if you keep not my commandments… you shall walk in darkness": darkness is not the absence of light but the *refusal* of it. For the project: the called are not automatically the chosen; the choosing is in the keeping, and one can walk in darkness *at noonday* if the heart won't receive the light that's fully there.)
- *The house by revealed pattern, the power-to-build by obedience (95:11-14).* "If you keep my commandments you shall have power to build it" [95:11] — the *power* to build comes with *obedience*; and "let the house be built, not after the manner of the world… after the manner which I shall show unto three of you" [95:13-14] — the temple *by revelation*, not worldly design. (Two §94 echoes: the pattern *shown* [§94:2], and the work *enabled by obedience*. "Not after the manner of the world" — the house of God is *unlike* the world's building, in pattern and in spirit; "I give not unto you that ye shall live after the manner of the world" [95:13]. The temple is a *revealed* thing, and the capacity to raise it is a *gift of obedience*, not merely of labor or funds.)
