# D&C 21 — receive his word in all patience and faith

**Setting:** **Fayette, New York, April 6, 1830** — the organizing meeting itself, in Peter Whitmer Sr.'s home. Six men, previously baptized, voted by common consent to organize and to sustain Joseph and Oliver as presiding officers. Joseph ordained Oliver an elder; Oliver ordained Joseph; they administered the sacrament and confirmed each one. This revelation was given *at* that meeting — the first revelation received by the Church *as* a Church. It defines the prophet's role and the people's relationship to his word.

**Binding idea:** "Thou shalt give heed unto all his words and commandments… for his word ye shall receive, as if from mine own mouth, **in all patience and faith**" (21:4-5). The prophet's word carries the Lord's authority — *and* the qualifier "in all patience and faith" is the whole nuance: the Saints follow a man who is still receiving line upon line, so following him requires patience with a *learning* prophet, not just obedience to a finished oracle.

**Read:** "There shall be a record kept among you; and in it thou shalt be called a seer, a translator, a prophet, an apostle of Jesus Christ, an elder of the church" (21:1), "inspired of the Holy Ghost to lay the foundation thereof, and to build it up unto the most holy faith" (21:2). "Thou shalt give heed unto all his words and commandments which he shall give unto you as he receiveth them, walking in all holiness before me; for his word ye shall receive, as if from mine own mouth, in all patience and faith" (21:4-5). "By doing these things the gates of hell shall not prevail against you… and cause the heavens to shake for your good" (21:6). "Him have I inspired to move the cause of Zion… and his diligence I know, and his prayers I have heard. Yea, his weeping for Zion I have seen, and I will cause that he shall mourn for her no longer" (21:7-8). "All those who labor in my vineyard… shall believe on his words, which are given him through me by the Comforter" (21:9). Then the ordination: "it behooveth me that he should be ordained by you, Oliver Cowdery mine apostle" (21:10), "he being the first unto you" (21:11).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "As if from mine own mouth" — the voice-of-servants principle, now the living prophet (21:5).* §1:38 stated the doctrine ("by mine own voice or by the voice of my servants… it is the same"); §21:5 *installs* it in the living prophet at the moment the Church is born. The prophet's word is to be received "as if from mine own mouth." This is the delegation/stewardship principle (covenant.yaml — the agent acting in the principal's name) at the Church's foundation: the Lord governs through a *servant's voice*, and to honor the servant's word is to honor the Lord's. (The same structure the project runs on — intent flowing through a steward — is the structure of prophetic authority. To hear the prophet rightly is the §1:38 / honor-the-delegation discipline.)
- *★ "In all patience and faith" — the load-bearing qualifier (21:5).* This is the verse's genius and its mercy. The prophet's word is the Lord's — *but* it is received "as he receiveth them" (21:4, progressively, line upon line) and so it must be received "in all patience and faith." The Saints are not following a finished, infallible oracle who downloads complete truth; they're following a man who is *learning the will of God in real time* (the §9 study-it-out prophet, who relapsed in §20:5). "Patience" is required precisely because the prophet is *en route* — corrections come, understanding grows, the §1:25-28 "line upon line" applies to him too. This is the honest middle path between "the prophet is infallible" (no — §3, §20:5 name his failures) and "the prophet is just a man" (no — his word is "as if from mine own mouth"). The resolution is *patience and faith*: trust the office and the man, and be patient as both unfold. (This rhymes hard with the project's covenant: the human "reads fully and flags when wrong" [9:8] — covenant partnership with a learning steward, not blind compliance — *and* receives the steward's word in good faith. Patience-and-faith is exactly the posture of a covenant partner toward someone still receiving.)
- *The prophet's weeping is seen (21:7-8).* Tucked into the founding revelation is something tender and personal: "his weeping for Zion I have seen, and I will cause that he shall mourn for her no longer; for his days of rejoicing are come." On the day Joseph is given the most authority — seer, prophet, apostle — the Lord speaks to his *grief*, not his power. He has wept for Zion; now come his days of rejoicing. The office is granted to a man the Lord has *watched weep*. (The §6:16/22 private-knowledge intimacy again — the Lord names what He has seen in secret: Joseph's tears. Authority is given to the one whose hidden sorrow God has witnessed.)
- *The mutual ordination (21:10-11).* "It behooveth me that he should be ordained by you, Oliver Cowdery." The prophet of the Restoration is ordained *by* Oliver — and Oliver, in the same act, becomes an elder "under his hand" (21:11). Neither man self-ordains; each receives the office through the other, by the keys already conferred (§13). Even the prophet does not lay hands on himself. (Authority is always *received*, never seized — the §13 "I confer" principle holds even at the top: Joseph's own elder's office comes through Oliver's hands, by common consent of the six.)
