# D&C 66 — thou art clean, but not all

**Setting:** **Hiram, Ohio, October 29, 1831.** William E. McLellin — a schoolteacher, recently baptized, intellectually able and proud of it — "had petitioned the Lord in secret to make known through the Prophet the answer to **five questions, which were unknown to Joseph Smith**." At McLellin's request, Joseph inquired and received this revelation. (McLellin later testified that all five secret questions were answered to his satisfaction — a private *test* of the gift, of a piece with §67's public challenge two weeks later.) The revelation is a personal call-and-warning: preach from land to land, and forsake the named private sin.

**Binding idea:** "Verily I say unto you, my servant William, that you are **clean, but not all**; repent, therefore, of those things which are not pleasing in my sight, saith the Lord, for the Lord will show them unto you" (66:3). A partial cleanness — most of the way, but not all the way — and the honesty to name it, so it can be repented of.

**Read:** "Blessed are you, inasmuch as you have turned away from your iniquities, and have received my truths" (66:1). "Blessed are you for receiving mine everlasting covenant, even the fulness of my gospel… that they might have life and be made partakers of the glories which are to be revealed in the last days" (66:2). "You are clean, but not all; repent, therefore, of those things which are not pleasing in my sight" (66:3). "It is my will that you should proclaim my gospel from land to land, and from city to city… where it has not been proclaimed" (66:5). "Bear testimony in every place, unto every people and in their synagogues, reasoning with the people" (66:7). "He that is faithful shall be made strong in every place; and I, the Lord, will go with you" (66:8). "Lay your hands upon the sick, and they shall recover… Ask, and ye shall receive; knock, and it shall be opened unto you" (66:9). "Seek not to be cumbered. Forsake all unrighteousness. **Commit not adultery—a temptation with which thou hast been troubled**" (66:10). "Keep these sayings, for they are true and faithful; and thou shalt magnify thine office, and push many people to Zion with songs of everlasting joy upon their heads" (66:11). "Continue in these things even unto the end, and you shall have a crown of eternal life at the right hand of my Father, who is full of grace and truth" (66:12).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "Clean, but not all" — the precision of God's honesty (66:3,10).* The Lord blesses McLellin warmly ("you have turned away from your iniquities… received my truths," 66:1) and *in the same breath* tells him the truth about himself: "clean, but **not all**." And He gets *specific*: "Commit not adultery—a temptation with which **thou hast been troubled**" (66:10). (This is the §6:22 / §62 intimacy thread at its most searching: God names the *private* struggle no one else knew McLellin carried — exactly as He named Oliver's secret night-prayer back to him [§6:22-24]. But where §6 named a hidden *faith*, §66 names a hidden *temptation*. The same omniscience that comforts ["whose hearts I know," §67:1] also *exposes* — and the exposure is mercy: a sin named is a sin that can be repented of, while a sin God left politely unmentioned would stay hidden and fester. "The Lord will show them unto you" [66:3] — He shows you your faults *so you can fix them*, not to shame you. The honesty is the kindness. For anyone who'd rather God not see the thing they're troubled by: He sees it, and He names it, and the naming is the first move of the rescue.)
- *The warning that named the fault line (66:3,10).* McLellin would not endure. He left the Church repeatedly, was excommunicated in 1838, and became a bitter, lifelong opponent — and his falling was bound up with the very things named here (pride of intellect, and moral failure). (The §23/§31 *warning-given-early* pattern, again: the Lord names the precise weakness at the moment of the call. McLellin was *told* — "clean, but not all," "the temptation with which thou hast been troubled," "continue… even unto the end" [66:12] — and the warning was not heeded. The canon keeps a record of warnings unkept [§3/§20:5/§40], not to condemn but because the warnings *were given*; agency is real, and so is the cost. bin-4-adj biography — McLellin's later course is his, not mine to litigate; what the walk notes is that the revelation *named the danger in advance*.)
- *"I, the Lord, will go with you" — the with-ness, and the missionary commission (66:5-9).* Under the warning is a full, warm commission: preach "from land to land… reasoning with the people" (66:5,7), heal the sick (66:9), "ask, and ye shall receive" (66:9, Matt 7:7) — and the constant of these Hiram sections, "I, the Lord, will go with you" (66:8; §31:13/§32:3/§62:9). (The pattern holds: the call comes *with* the presence. McLellin is sent to hard places but not alone; "he that is faithful shall be made strong in every place" [66:8] — strength supplied to the faithful, §24:9. The warning and the commission are one revelation: God names the weakness *and* promises to go with him into the work despite it. The honesty doesn't withdraw the call; it equips it.)

**Notable / flag:** No spin-off. **Walk's contribution:** **§66 is the revelation that answered McLellin's five secret questions (unknown to Joseph) — and named his hidden temptation (66:3,10).** "You are **clean, but not all**" (John 13:10's words to the disciples): the Lord blesses McLellin and in the same breath tells him the precise truth about himself, getting specific — "Commit not adultery, a temptation with which **thou hast been troubled**." The §6:22 intimacy thread at its most searching: where §6 named a hidden *faith* back to Oliver, §66 names a hidden *temptation* back to William — the same omniscience that comforts also *exposes*, and the exposure is *mercy* (a sin named is a sin that can be repented; "the Lord will show them unto you," Jacob 4:7 — He shows you your weakness so you can mend it, not to shame you). It's also the **warning-given-early** pattern (§23/§31): McLellin was told the danger at the call, did not endure, and his later opposition was bound up with the very things named (bin-4-adj; his course is his). Under the warning is a full warm commission — preach "from land to land," heal the sick, "ask and ye shall receive" — with the constant of these sections: "**I, the Lord, will go with you**" (66:8). The honesty doesn't withdraw the call; it equips it. For Michael: the section where the Lord proves the gift by answering questions Joseph never heard, and proves His love by naming the private temptation no one else knew — because a fault God names is a fault you can finally fix.
