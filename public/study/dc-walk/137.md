# D&C 137 — ★★ judged according to the desire of their hearts

**Setting:** **The Kirtland Temple, January 21, 1836** — a vision given during the administration of ordinances preparing for the temple's dedication (ten weeks before §110's Easter visitation). Chronologically it belongs in 1836, but it is placed near the *end* of the book, paired with §138 (1918) as the two **visions of the redemption of the dead**. Joseph sees the celestial kingdom — and his brother Alvin there, though Alvin died before the Restoration and was never baptized. The marvel becomes the doctrine.

**Binding idea:** **"For I, the Lord, will judge all men according to their works, according to the desire of their hearts" (137:9).** Alvin is an heir of the celestial kingdom because he *would have received* the gospel had he lived to hear it: "All who have died without a knowledge of this gospel, who would have received it if they had been permitted to tarry, shall be heirs of the celestial kingdom of God" (137:7). God judges the *heart's desire*, not merely the achieved outcome.

**Read:** "The heavens were opened upon us, and I beheld the celestial kingdom of God, and the glory thereof, whether in the body or out I cannot tell" (137:1). "Also the blazing throne of God, whereon was seated the Father and the Son" (137:3). "I saw Father Adam and Abraham; and my father and my mother; my brother Alvin, that has long since slept; And marveled how it was that he had obtained an inheritance in that kingdom, seeing that he had departed this life before the Lord had set his hand to gather Israel the second time, and had not been baptized for the remission of sins" (137:5-6). "Thus came the voice of the Lord unto me, saying: All who have died without a knowledge of this gospel, who would have received it if they had been permitted to tarry, shall be heirs of the celestial kingdom of God; Also all that shall die henceforth without a knowledge of it, who would have received it with all their hearts, shall be heirs of that kingdom; For I, the Lord, will judge all men according to their works, according to the desire of their hearts" (137:7-9). "And I also beheld that all children who die before they arrive at the years of accountability are saved in the celestial kingdom of heaven" (137:10).

**Thoughts:**

- *★★ Judged by the desire of the heart — the accountability-mercy law (137:7-9).* The vision resolves the cruelest version of the salvation question: *what about the one who never had the chance?* Alvin died in 1823, before there was a restored gospel to receive or an authority to baptize him — yet Joseph sees him in the celestial kingdom. The answer is a law of *mercy keyed to intent*: "All who have died without a knowledge of this gospel, **who would have received it if they had been permitted to tarry**, shall be heirs of the celestial kingdom… For I, the Lord, will judge all men according to their works, **according to the desire of their hearts**" (137:7,9). (God does not condemn for an opportunity never given. He judges the *heart's desire* — what the person *would* have done with the light, had they had it. This is the §128 welding-link's *mercy-engine*: baptism for the dead works because God already counts the one "who would have received it" as an heir, and the ordinance ratifies what the heart had already chosen. And it is the walk's **intent-over-outcome** thread reaching the level of *salvation itself*: just as §124:49 said God "accepts the offering" of the one hindered from finishing the work, §137:9 says God judges "the desire of their hearts" — the achieved outcome [baptism, hearing the gospel] is not the measure when circumstance, not the will, prevented it. The §64:34 "the Lord requireth the heart" given its eternal weight: at the judgment, what is weighed is *what you wanted to be*, what you "would have received… with all [your] heart" [137:8]. For the project's whole intent-over-outcome theology: this is its deepest form — God reads the desire beneath the prevented act, and judges *that*. The mercy is exact, not loose: it is for the one who *would* have received "with all their hearts," not a blanket amnesty — but it means no honest seeker is lost for want of a chance.)

- *★ All children saved (137:10).* "All children who die before they arrive at the years of accountability are saved in the celestial kingdom of heaven." (The §29:46-47 / §74:7 children-redeemed doctrine confirmed at the highest court: not merely *saved* but saved *in the celestial kingdom* — the §19 dissolution of infant-damnation reaching its positive form. The same accountability-mercy logic [137:9]: a child who never reached accountability is judged by what is true of them, which is innocence. For the walk's mercy thread [§19 hell redefined, §29 children redeemed, §137 the heart's desire]: God's justice is structured *toward* mercy — it asks what the person would have chosen, and it does not charge anyone for a chance they never had.)

- *The vision predates its doctrine's full bloom (the 1836→1918 pairing).* §137 is the *seed*, §138 the *flower*. (In 1836 Joseph sees Alvin and is given the principle [the heart's desire is judged]; in 1918 Joseph F. Smith sees the *whole spirit world* and how the principle is *operationalized* — the Savior organizing the righteous dead to carry the gospel to those who "would have received it." The book places the two visions side by side at its close, 82 years apart, because they are one doctrine: §137 the *law* [judged by the heart's desire], §138 the *machinery* [the dead taught, so the desire can be answered]. Together with §128 [the welding-link and the binding record], they complete the temple/genealogy taproot the Malachi bracket opened at §2.)
