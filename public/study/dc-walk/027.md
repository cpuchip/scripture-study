# D&C 27 — the gathering of the keys, and the whole armor of God

**Setting:** **Harmony, Pennsylvania, August 1830.** Joseph set out to buy wine for a sacrament service and was met by "a heavenly messenger," who gave this revelation (part then, the rest that September). The occasion — a practical errand for sacrament wine — opens into one of the grandest passages in the D&C: a roll call of the key-holders of every dispensation, and the whole armor of God. (Because of 27:3-4, the Church has used **water** for the sacrament ever since.)

**Binding idea:** What matters in the sacrament is *not the substance* but the *remembering* — "it mattereth not what ye shall eat or what ye shall drink… if it so be that ye do it with an eye single to my glory" (27:2). And the Restoration is revealed as the *gathering-place* of all prior dispensations: Christ will yet drink "the fruit of the vine" with Moroni, Elias, John, Elijah, Abraham, Adam, Peter-James-John — every key-holder of every age — because in "the dispensation of the fulness of times" He will "gather together in one all things" (27:13).

**Read:** "It mattereth not what ye shall eat or what ye shall drink when ye partake of the sacrament, if it so be that ye do it with an eye single to my glory—remembering unto the Father my body which was laid down for you, and my blood which was shed for the remission of your sins" (27:2). Don't buy wine "of your enemies" (27:3); use what "is made new among you" (27:4). "The hour cometh that I will drink of the fruit of the vine with you on the earth, and with Moroni… to whom I have committed the keys of the record of the stick of Ephraim" (27:5); "and also with Elias, to whom I have committed the keys of bringing to pass the restoration of all things" (27:6); "and also John… filled with the spirit of Elias" who ordained Joseph and Oliver "unto the first priesthood… even as Aaron" (27:7-8); "and also Elijah, unto whom I have committed the keys of the power of turning the hearts of the fathers to the children, and the hearts of the children to the fathers, that the whole earth may not be smitten with a curse" (27:9); "and also with Joseph and Jacob, and Isaac, and Abraham, your fathers, by whom the promises remain" (27:10); "and also with Michael, or Adam, the father of all… the ancient of days" (27:11); "and also with Peter, and James, and John, whom I have sent unto you, by whom I have ordained you… apostles, and especial witnesses of my name… unto whom I have committed the keys of my kingdom, and a dispensation of the gospel for the last times; and for the fulness of times, in the which I will gather together in one all things, both which are in heaven, and which are on earth" (27:12-13). Then: "gird up your loins, and take upon you my whole armor… having your loins girt about with truth, having on the breastplate of righteousness, and your feet shod with the preparation of the gospel of peace… Taking the shield of faith wherewith ye shall be able to quench all the fiery darts of the wicked; and take the helmet of salvation, and the sword of my Spirit… and be agreed as touching all things… and be faithful until I come" (27:15-18).

**Thoughts:**
- *The sacrament is about remembering, not the elements (27:2).* The whole revelation begins because Joseph worried about the *wine* — and the Lord's first word dissolves the worry: "it mattereth not what ye shall eat or what ye shall drink… if it so be that ye do it with an eye single to my glory—remembering." The emblem is a vehicle for *remembrance*, not a magic substance; bread and water serve as well as bread and wine because the ordinance lives in the *eye single to His glory* (the §4:5 / 88:67 single-eye again — the matter-spectrum's measure of pure intent) and in the *remembering* (the §20:77 covenant: "always remember him"). (A quiet anti-superstition: the power is not in the thing consumed but in the heart that remembers. The practical accommodation — water for wine — *is* the doctrine: the substance is adiaphora; the remembering is everything.)
- *★ The roll call of keys — the priesthood-line thread fully gathered (27:5-13).* This is the section's grandeur. Every prior visitation is named as a *committing of keys*: Moroni (the record / the stick of Ephraim, Ezek 37), Elias (the restoration of all things), John the Baptist (the Aaronic, §13), **Elijah** (the turning of hearts, §2's Malachi), the patriarchs Abraham-Isaac-Jacob (the promises), Adam/Michael (the Ancient of Days), Peter-James-John (the Melchizedek, the apostleship, §7). The Restoration is not one new revelation; it is the *convergence* of all of them — "a dispensation of the gospel for the last times… in the which I will gather together in one all things" (27:13, Eph 1:10). The priesthood-line thread from the PoGP walk (Moses 6:7 / Abr 1:3, the unbroken line) doesn't just get re-spliced (§13) — here every segment of the line, from Adam forward, is *gathered into one dispensation*. (This is the answer to "why a Restoration and not just a revival": the last dispensation is the *gathering-up* of all the keys ever given, so that nothing is lost — the §10 wise-purpose redundancy at the scale of all sacred history.)
- *★ Elijah and the turning of hearts (27:9) — the sealing/temple thread matures.* "Elijah, unto whom I have committed the keys of the power of turning the hearts of the fathers to the children, and the hearts of the children to the fathers, that the whole earth may not be smitten with a curse." This is §2's Malachi (Mal 4:5-6) now named as a *key* — the sealing power that binds generations, the taproot of temple/genealogy work. The Malachi bracket (§2 ↔ §13) gets its keystone here: Elijah's keys are the turning-of-hearts, and they exist so "the whole earth may not be smitten with a curse" — i.e., so the human family is *sealed together* rather than left as disconnected individuals. (The temple thread: Moses 7's Zion, §2's promises-to-the-fathers, §13's sons-of-Levi, all converge on Elijah's turning-of-hearts keys. I keep flagging this as the reserved temple territory — its full sense is Michael's + the Spirit's; here I mark that 27:9 is where the *keys* of it are named.)
- *★ The whole armor of God — Ephesians 6 in the Restoration's voice (27:15-18).* Paul's armor (Eph 6:11-17) is given again, almost verbatim, but with Restoration inflections: the sword is "the sword of my Spirit, which I will pour out upon you, and my word which I reveal unto you" (27:18 — the armor is *revelation itself*, the §8:2 mind-and-heart Spirit as a weapon); and the charge adds "be agreed as touching all things" (27:18 — unity, the §26 common-consent body) and "be faithful until I come, and ye shall be caught up, that where I am ye shall be also." The armor is *defensive and standing* — "that ye may be able to **stand**" (27:15, repeated) — not for conquest but for *withstanding the evil day*. (The pieces map to the walk's threads: loins girt with *truth* [truth.md], breastplate of *righteousness*, feet shod with the *gospel of peace*, shield of *faith* [§17 faith-precedes-sight], helmet of *salvation*, sword of the *Spirit/word* [§8-9]. The whole epistemology and ethic of the early sections, assembled as armor.)
- *Christ will yet drink with them "on the earth" (27:5).* The framing is a future *messianic supper* — Christ promising to keep the sacrament again, in person, "with you on the earth," gathered with every dispensation's prophets (Matt 26:29, "I will not drink… until that day when I drink it new with you"). The sacrament Joseph was fretting the wine for is a *type* of a literal future feast where the whole sealed family of all ages sits together. The small ordinance points to the great gathering. (The §20:75 "meet together often" sacrament is rehearsal for 27:5's consummation.)
