# D&C 10 — wisdom greater than the cunning of the devil

**Setting:** Harmony, Pennsylvania, **likely around April 1829** (parts perhaps as early as summer 1828). The companion to §3: this is the *resolution* of the lost-116-pages crisis. The pages Martin Harris lost were the translation of the **book of Lehi**; wicked men had them and a plan — wait for Joseph to retranslate, then publish their *altered* copy to "prove" he can't reproduce his own words. The Lord names the trap and disarms it. This section closes the arc §3 opened.

**Binding idea:** Satan laid "a cunning plan… thinking to destroy the work of God" (10:23) — and the Lord answers not by undoing the loss but by revealing that He prepared for it **two thousand years in advance.** Nephi was commanded to keep a *second*, more spiritual record (the small plates) for "a wise purpose" no one then understood (W of M 1:7). The redundancy was built into the Book of Mormon a millennium before the theft. "I will show unto them that my wisdom is greater than the cunning of the devil" (10:43).

**Read:** Because Joseph delivered the writings "into the hands of a wicked man, you have lost them. And you also lost your gift… and your mind became darkened" (10:1-2) — "Nevertheless, it is now restored unto you again" (10:3). Then the counsel: **"Do not run faster or labor more than you have strength and means provided to enable you to translate; but be diligent unto the end"** (10:4). "Pray always, that you may come off conqueror" (10:5). Satan "hath put it into their hearts to alter the words which you have caused to be written" (10:10); the plan is to get Joseph to "translate it over again" (10:15), produce the same words, and then "we have the same with us, and we have altered them; therefore they will not agree, and we will say that he has lied" (10:17-18). "I will not suffer that Satan shall accomplish his evil design" (10:14). "You shall not translate again those words which have gone forth out of your hands" (10:30). And the prepared answer: "an account of those things… is engraven upon the plates of Nephi" (10:38); "you shall translate the engravings which are on the plates of Nephi… you shall publish it as the record of Nephi; and thus I will confound those who have altered my words" (10:41-42). "They have only got a part, or an abridgment of the account of Nephi" (10:44); "there are many things engraven upon the plates of Nephi which do throw greater views upon my gospel" (10:45). The ancient prophets prayed in faith that the gospel would reach their brethren (10:46-50). Then the close — Christ in His own voice (10:57-58, echoing §6), "other sheep have I which are not of this fold" (10:59-60), and the doctrine: "whosoever repenteth and cometh unto me, the same is my church. Whosoever declareth more or less than this, the same is not of me" (10:67-68); "whosoever… endureth of my church to the end, him will I establish upon my rock, and the gates of hell shall not prevail against them" (10:69).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "Do not run faster… than you have strength" (10:4) — a project-source text lands.* This verse is cited in `covenant.yaml` (`human_commits_to.not_bypass_process`: "The process IS the pacing mechanism"). Walked in context, it's even sharper than the covenant quotes it: it comes *immediately after* the catastrophe. Joseph lost the pages partly by *over-reaching* — he pestered the Lord three times for permission to lend them to Martin (the §3 backstory), running ahead of the answer he'd already been given. So the pacing command isn't generic prudence; it's the **direct lesson of the failure**. Run faster than your strength and means, and you hand the sacred to wickedness. The covenant's "do not bypass the process for a quick fix" is this verse's exact shape: the corners Joseph cut cost him the book of Lehi. (And note the realism — "strength *and means provided*." The limit isn't only the body's energy; it's the resources actually given. You labor at the rate the means allow, not the rate the ambition wants.)
- *★ The wise-purpose redundancy (10:38-45) — God's foresight as the real miracle.* The astonishing thing isn't that Joseph recovered; it's *when the recovery was prepared.* Around 600 BC, Nephi was commanded to engrave a second, smaller, more spiritual set of plates *covering the same period* as Lehi's record — and he wrote that he did it for a purpose he didn't know: "the Lord knoweth all things… wherefore, he worketh in me to do according to his will" (W of M 1:7, the footnote's anchor). For two and a half millennia those small plates sat at the front of the stack as a sealed insurance policy against a theft that hadn't happened yet. Mormon, abridging a thousand years later, felt prompted to include them "for a wise purpose" he also didn't fully understand. The providence runs across two ancient authors who each obeyed a prompting whose reason was hidden — and it cashed out in 1829. This is the answer to §3's "God's work cannot be frustrated, only men's": not because God reacts fast, but because He *pre-acted slow*, building the redundancy into the foundation. (It reframes the whole crisis: the 116 pages were never the irreplaceable thing; the small plates — 1 Nephi through Omni, the most beloved doctrinal opening of the Book of Mormon — are what we got *instead*, and they "throw greater views upon my gospel," 10:45. The loss yielded a better book.)
- *"I will not suffer that they shall destroy my work… my wisdom is greater than the cunning of the devil" (10:43).* The contest is framed as *wisdom vs. cunning.* Satan's plan is genuinely clever — a falsification trap, internally airtight (retranslate → mismatch → "liar"). The Lord doesn't call it stupid; He out-*wisdoms* it. And He does it not by force (He could have struck the conspirators) but by **not playing their game**: "you shall not translate again those words" (10:30). The counter to a trap is often refusing the move it depends on. (There's a presiding echo here — the Lord declines the compulsory route, the dramatic confrontation, and wins by patience and prepared provision. Wisdom-greater-than-cunning is the long game, not the strong arm.)
- *"As you cannot always judge the righteous… hold your peace" (10:37).* A small, honest epistemic humility: even Joseph can't reliably tell the wicked from the righteous, so the safe rule is restraint about sacred things "until I shall see fit to make all things known." This rhymes with §6:12 ("make not thy gift known unto any save… those who are of thy faith") and forward with the temple-reserve thread — the discipline of *holding* the sacred when you cannot vet the hearts around you. Not paranoia: a frank admission that human discernment of others is limited, so guard accordingly.
- *The doctrine-of-Christ minimal core (10:67-68).* "Whosoever repenteth and cometh unto me, the same is my church. Whosoever declareth more or less than this… is not of me." The same radically simple definition as 3 Ne 11:39-40 (the BoM walk) and ahead in D&C 20. The church is not first an institution; it is *the repentant who come to Christ.* And the error runs *both* directions — "more or less than this." Adding to the doctrine is as much a departure as subtracting. The minimal core guards against both the reducer and the embellisher.
