# D&C 68 — when moved upon by the Holy Ghost, it shall be scripture

**Setting:** **Hiram, Ohio, November 1, 1831** — in the same conference-cluster as §67, in answer to prayer about four men (Orson Hyde, Luke and Lyman Johnson, William McLellin) who wanted to know the Lord's will concerning them. But "much of the content pertains to the whole Church": the revelation opens with a definition of *scripture itself*, then turns to the Presiding Bishopric, the *age of accountability*, and a rebuke of idleness in Zion. (Expanded under Joseph's direction for the 1835 edition.)

**Binding idea:** "Whatsoever they shall speak when moved upon by the Holy Ghost shall be scripture, shall be the will of the Lord, shall be the mind of the Lord, shall be the word of the Lord, shall be the voice of the Lord, and the power of God unto salvation" (68:3-4). Scripture is *not* a closed book — it is *Spirit-moved speech*. When the Holy Ghost moves on a servant, what is spoken *is* the word of the Lord.

**Read:** "Orson Hyde was called by his ordination to proclaim the everlasting gospel, by the Spirit of the living God… reasoning with and expounding all scriptures unto them" (68:1). "This is the ensample unto them, that they shall speak as they are moved upon by the Holy Ghost" (68:3). "Whatsoever they shall speak when moved upon by the Holy Ghost shall be scripture, shall be the will of the Lord, shall be the mind of the Lord, shall be the word of the Lord, shall be the voice of the Lord, and the power of God unto salvation" (68:4). "Be of good cheer, and do not fear, for I the Lord am with you, and will stand by you" (68:6). "Go ye into all the world, preach the gospel to every creature, acting in the authority which I have given you" (68:8). "Inasmuch as parents have children in Zion… that teach them not to understand the doctrine of repentance, faith in Christ the Son of the living God, and of baptism and the gift of the Holy Ghost by the laying on of the hands, when eight years old, **the sin be upon the heads of the parents**" (68:25). "Their children shall be baptized for the remission of their sins when eight years old, and receive the laying on of the hands" (68:27). "They shall also teach their children to pray, and to walk uprightly before the Lord" (68:28). "The inhabitants of Zion shall also observe the Sabbath day to keep it holy" (68:29). "I, the Lord, am not well pleased with the inhabitants of Zion, for there are idlers among them; and their children are also growing up in wickedness; they also seek not earnestly the riches of eternity, but their eyes are full of greediness" (68:31). "Behold, I am Alpha and Omega, and I come quickly. Amen" (68:35).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "When moved upon by the Holy Ghost… shall be scripture" (68:3-4) — the definition of living scripture.* The most quoted line in the section, and a doctrine that reorders what "scripture" *means*. Scripture is not defined by being *old*, or *written*, or *canonized* — it is defined by its *source*: speech given "when moved upon by the Holy Ghost." When the Spirit moves on a servant, what comes out "shall be scripture… the will… the mind… the word… the voice of the Lord, and the power of God unto salvation." (Five names for the same thing, ascending — will, mind, word, voice, power. This is §1:38 ["by mine own voice or by the voice of my servants… it is the same"] given its mechanism: *what makes* the servant's voice the Lord's voice is the *Holy Ghost moving*. And it pairs *exactly* with §67, two days prior: §67 said you cannot *manufacture* a revelation — "make one like unto it" and the wisest man fails, because the substance descends "from the Father of lights"; §68 says when the Spirit *does* move, the speech *is* scripture. Together they are the whole doctrine of revelation's *authentication*: not the eloquence of the words [§67 — rough language carries the light], but the *moving of the Spirit* [§68] makes it the word of God. And the condition is load-bearing — "*when* moved upon by the Holy Ghost," not whenever a servant opens his mouth [§28's order, §50/§52's discernment, govern]. The teaching covenant the walk has tracked [§11:21 obtain-before-declare; §42:14 "if ye receive not the Spirit ye shall not teach"] is the *other side* of this promise: receive the Spirit and your words become scripture; teach without it and you have nothing. For the project: the same principle under §67 — words carry the Lord's authority not by their polish but by the Spirit that moved them; the test of any word is its source, not its style.)
- *★ The age of accountability — eight years, and the sin on the parents (68:25-28).* A landmark of doctrine and of *parental duty*. Children are to be taught "the doctrine of repentance, faith in Christ… baptism and the gift of the Holy Ghost… when eight years old" — and if the parents in Zion *fail* to teach it, "the sin be upon the heads of the parents" (68:25). The children are baptized "for the remission of their sins when eight years old" (68:27). (Two doctrines fused. First, the *age of accountability*: eight is the threshold where a child becomes accountable — the completion of §29:46-47 [little children "cannot sin, for power is not given unto Satan to tempt little children, until they begin to become accountable"] and §20:71. Before eight, redeemed by Christ's grace with no need of baptism [the §19 mercy, infant-damnation dissolved]; at eight, accountable, and baptized. Second, and sharper, the *parental teaching duty* with *teeth*: the teaching covenant [§11:21 / §42:14 — "if ye receive not the Spirit ye shall not teach"] applied to the *family*, and the failure to teach is not merely negligence — "the sin be upon the heads of the parents." Untaught children's sin is *charged to the parents* [Ezek 33:6, the unwarned watchman's blood-guilt]. This is the §1:38/§68:4 scripture-doctrine domesticated: the parent is the first teacher, and the home the first place the gospel is "moved upon by the Holy Ghost" into a child. "Teach their children to pray, and to walk uprightly" [68:28] — the curriculum is prayer and integrity, taught early. For a parent: the teaching is not optional enrichment; it is a *covenant duty* whose neglect God charges to you. The §64:34 "willing mind" and the §59 gratitude raised at the family hearth.)
- *The idlers and the greedy in Zion — the §56 reciprocal-wo continues (68:30-31).* The Lord is "not well pleased with the inhabitants of Zion, for there are idlers among them… they seek not earnestly the riches of eternity, but their eyes are full of greediness" (68:31). (The §56:16-17 reciprocal-wo's *poor*-end, recurring: idleness and greed condemned in Zion itself. "The idler shall be had in remembrance before the Lord" [68:30] — even in the gathered city, the covetous-and-idle heart [§56] is the standing danger; equality is not automatic righteousness. And "their children are also growing up in wickedness" — ties straight back to 68:25: the idle, greedy parents are *not teaching* their children, and the wickedness propagates. The economic sin [§56] and the teaching-neglect [68:25] are one failure: parents whose "eyes are full of greediness" raise children "growing up in wickedness." Zion is undone not by its enemies here but by idleness and ungoverned want *within*.)
- *The Presiding Bishopric and the lineage of Aaron (68:14-24) — noted.* The section also gives the order of the bishopric: bishops are high priests "appointed by the First Presidency… except they be literal descendants of Aaron" — "the firstborn… of Aaron" has "a legal right to the bishopric" (68:16-18), else a high priest of the Melchizedek Priesthood officiates "when no literal descendant of Aaron can be found" (68:19). (The Aaronic-lineage office — the §13 "sons of Levi" line surfacing as church *structure*. The literal-descendant-of-Aaron right connects to the priesthood-lineage thread [§2/§13/§27, the Malachi bracket]; I note it as the bishopric's structural form and flag the lineage clause as part of the reserved priesthood-genealogy material, Michael's. The practical doctrine for the Church: the office goes to the worthy high priest when no Aaronic descendant is identified — authority by *appointment and the keys*, not merely by blood.)
