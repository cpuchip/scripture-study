# D&C 15 — the thing of most worth

**Setting:** **Fayette, New York, June 1829** — to **John Whitmer**, another of the Whitmer sons at whose family farm the translation was being finished. Like §14 (David) and §16 (Peter Jr.), it answers a young man's question about his duty. Six verses, "intimately and impressively personal" (the heading): the Lord tells John something "known only to John Whitmer and Himself." John later became one of the **Eight Witnesses**.

**Binding idea:** John had "many times… desired of me to know that which would be of the most worth unto you" (15:4). The Lord finally answers the most-worth question — and the answer is not a gift, an office, or a sight, but a *labor*: "the thing which will be of the most worth unto you will be to declare repentance unto this people, that you may bring souls unto me" (15:6). What's worth most is what saves others.

**Read:** "Hearken, my servant John, and listen to the words of Jesus Christ, your Lord and your Redeemer" (15:1). "I speak unto you with sharpness and with power, for mine arm is over all the earth" (15:2). "I will tell you that which no man knoweth save me and thee alone" (15:3) — "for many times you have desired of me to know that which would be of the most worth unto you" (15:4). "Blessed are you for this thing" (15:5). "The thing which will be of the most worth unto you will be to declare repentance unto this people, that you may bring souls unto me, that you may rest with them in the kingdom of my Father" (15:6).

**Thoughts:**
- *The most-worth question, answered (15:4-6).* John's recurring private prayer was essentially "what should I do with my life that matters most?" — and the Lord's answer reframes "worth to *you*" as worth *through* you: declaring repentance, bringing souls. The most valuable thing John can do for *himself* is the thing that saves *others* — and the two are joined at the end: "that you may **rest with them** in the kingdom" (15:6). You don't bring souls to Christ and then receive a separate reward; the souls you bring *are* the company you rest with. The worth and the reward are the same people. (This is the worth-of-souls doctrine — D&C 18:15-16, two sections ahead — in seed form: the joy is the souls.)
- *"Sharpness" in a call revelation (15:2).* Striking word for a commissioning. The Lord speaks to John "with sharpness and with power" — not rebuke (John's done nothing wrong; he's blessed, 15:5), but *intensity*, edge, the §6:2 "sharper than a two-edged sword" word cutting to the point. The footnote ties it to Hel 3:29 (the word of God "sharper than a two-edged sword"). Sometimes the Lord's love is *pointed* — He cuts straight to what matters most rather than cushioning it. (And it answers the §3/§121:43 "reprove with sharpness" pattern from the gentle side: sharpness isn't only for correction; it's the register of *urgency*. The work is real, time is short, here is the thing that matters — no padding.)
- *The private-knowledge witness, again (15:3).* "That which no man knoweth save me and thee alone" — the exact §6:16/24 move (the secret prayer the Lord names back). The Restoration's revelations keep authenticating themselves the same way: not by spectacle but by *the Lord knowing the disciple's hidden interior.* John, like Oliver, is convinced by being *known.* (The how-revelation-feels thread has a sibling: the *that-it's-real* signal is consistently "He told me what only I knew.")
