# D&C 9 — study it out, then ask

**Setting:** Harmony, Pennsylvania, **April 1829.** Granted the gift in §8, Oliver tried to translate — and failed. This section is the Lord explaining *why*, and in explaining it, He lays down the verification epistemology the whole Restoration (and this project) rests on. Oliver is told, gently, to go back to writing for now; and he is taught that he misunderstood how the gift works. (★ Project-source-text: **9:7-9** is the workspace's verification anchor — "study it out in your mind; then ask… you shall feel that it is right," the heart of D&C 8:2-3's "mind and heart," now made a method.)

**Binding idea:** Oliver assumed revelation was *passive reception* — ask, and the answer is downloaded. The Lord corrects him: real revelation is *active partnership.* You must do the work first — "study it out in your mind" — bring the Lord a proposed answer, and *then* ask if it's right. The burning confirms; it doesn't replace the labor. God will not think for you; He will witness to thinking you've already done.

**Read:** "Because you did not translate according to that which you desired… and did commence again to write for my servant, Joseph Smith, Jun., even so I would that ye should continue until you have finished this record" (9:1). Other records will come later (9:2); "be patient, my son, for it is wisdom in me, and it is not expedient that you should translate at this present time" (9:3); "the work which you are called to do is to write for my servant Joseph" (9:4). "It is because that you did not continue as you commenced, when you began to translate, that I have taken away this privilege" (9:5); "do not murmur, my son, for it is wisdom in me that I have dealt with you after this manner" (9:6). Then the heart of it: **"you have not understood; you have supposed that I would give it unto you, when you took no thought save it was to ask me. But, behold, I say unto you, that you must study it out in your mind; then you must ask me if it be right, and if it is right I will cause that your bosom shall burn within you; therefore, you shall feel that it is right. But if it be not right you shall have no such feelings, but you shall have a stupor of thought that shall cause you to forget the thing which is wrong"** (9:7-9). "Now, if you had known this you could have translated; nevertheless, it is not expedient that you should translate now" (9:10); "it was expedient when you commenced; but you feared, and the time is past" (9:11). "Stand fast in the work wherewith I have called you, and a hair of your head shall not be lost, and you shall be lifted up at the last day" (9:14).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ The correction is the doctrine: revelation is partnership, not download (9:7-8).* Oliver's error is named with surgical precision — "you took no thought save it was to ask me" (9:7). He treated the Lord like an oracle: pose the question, receive the answer, zero labor in between. The Lord's method *requires the labor*: "you must study it out in your mind; then you must ask me if it be right." The sequence is irreversible — **study → propose → ask → confirm.** You bring God a wrought answer and He tells you if it's true. This is the opposite of passive mysticism: revelation is the *crowning* of effort, not a substitute for it. (And it is exactly the collaboration covenant of this project — "the human reads output fully and flags when something is wrong," D&C 9:8-9 is even cited *in* `covenant.yaml` under `flag_when_wrong`. The bishop who hands out solutions without counsel offloads; the disciple who asks without studying does the same thing to God. Revelation, like presiding, is council — you must bring something to the table.)
- *The burning vs. the stupor — and what "stupor" actually means (9:8-9).* The confirming sign is warmth: "your bosom shall burn within you… you shall feel that it is right." The denying sign is not pain, not a slammed door — it is a **stupor of thought**. Webster 1828: stupor = "great diminution or suspension of sensibility; numbness… intellectual insensibility; heedlessness or inattention." The wrong answer doesn't hurt; it goes *numb*. It "cause[s] you to forget the thing which is wrong" (9:9). This is subtle and true to experience: a false path doesn't announce itself with alarm bells — it quietly loses its life, becomes forgettable, fails to hold the mind. The two signals are *warmth that stays* vs. *numbness that fades.* (This is why the discernment is hard and the human's, per the period-language principle: the stupor is an *absence*, and absences are easy to miss or to mistake for ordinary distraction. Reading the negative signal well is its own spiritual skill.)
- *The window closed because he feared (9:11) — revelation is time-bound.* "It was expedient when you commenced; but you feared, and the time is past." This is sobering: the gift was *real* and *available*, and Oliver's fear let the moment pass. Not every door stays open. The Lord doesn't condemn him (9:12, "neither of you have I condemned"), but He's honest that an opportunity, once feared past, doesn't simply wait. Fear isn't neutral; it forfeits.
- *"It is wisdom in me" — said twice, no reasons given (9:3,6).* The Lord twice tells Oliver the *why* is "wisdom in me" and stops there. "Do not murmur." Some of God's dealings come with the reasoning withheld — and the disciple is asked to trust the *character* (wisdom) rather than receive the *explanation*. (This rhymes with §10's "a wise purpose" for the small plates — the Lord is comfortable acting on reasons He doesn't yet disclose, and the next section will reveal one such reason prepared 2,000 years early.)
- *The whole episode is gentle.* Notice the register: "my son" three times (9:1,3,6). The Lord takes away the translation privilege and Oliver is *demoted to scribe* — and the entire correction is tender. This is the §3 / D&C 121:43 pattern again (reprove, then love), but here the "sharpness" is barely a whisper; it's mostly love. Correction calibrated to the person: Joseph got sharp rebuke in §3 for a graver failure; Oliver, who merely misunderstood and feared, gets "my son… do not murmur… a hair of your head shall not be lost."
