# D&C 17 — by faith you shall see

**Setting:** **Fayette, New York, June 1829.** The translation was nearly done. From the plates themselves (Ether 5:2-4; 2 Ne 27:12) Joseph and Oliver had learned that *three special witnesses* would be granted a view of the record. **Oliver Cowdery, David Whitmer, and Martin Harris** — each "moved upon by an inspired desire" — asked to be those three. This revelation is the Lord's answer, given *before* the experience in the Fayette woods where an angel showed them the plates and Christ's voice declared the translation true. It fulfills the promise of §5 (to Martin) and §14 (to David).

**Binding idea:** The order is faith *then* sight, never the reverse: "you must rely upon my word, which if you do with full purpose of heart, you shall have a view of the plates… it is by your faith that you shall obtain a view of them" (17:1-2). Even the men granted the most direct physical evidence in the Restoration had to exercise *faith first* — the sight was the *fruit* of belief, not its cause. The greatest evidentiary witness still runs through the §5 door: faith comes first.

**Read:** "You must rely upon my word, which if you do with full purpose of heart, you shall have a view of the plates, and also of the breastplate, the sword of Laban, the Urim and Thummim… given to the brother of Jared upon the mount… and the miraculous directors which were given to Lehi… on the borders of the Red Sea" (17:1). "It is by your faith that you shall obtain a view of them, even by that faith which was had by the prophets of old" (17:2). "After that you have obtained faith, and have seen them with your eyes, you shall testify of them, by the power of God; and this you shall do that my servant Joseph Smith, Jun., may not be destroyed" (17:3-4). "You have received the same power, and the same faith, and the same gift like unto him" (17:7). "If you do these last commandments of mine… the gates of hell shall not prevail against you; for my grace is sufficient for you, and you shall be lifted up at the last day" (17:8). "I, Jesus Christ, your Lord and your God, have spoken it" (17:9).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ Faith precedes sight — even here (17:1-2).* This is the section's spine and the resolution of the whole §5-6-9 epistemology arc. Martin Harris asked for *proof* (§5) and was told proof can't compel faith. Now Martin is granted the most concrete evidence imaginable — an angel, the plates, the relics held in his eyes — and *still* the condition is faith-first: "by your faith… you shall obtain a view." The witnesses don't believe *because* they saw; they saw *because* they believed, "with full purpose of heart" (17:1). Sight is the *reward* of faith, not its substitute. (This is why the Three Witnesses' testimony has the structure it does — it is not "we examined the evidence and concluded"; it is "an angel of God came down and showed them unto us… by the power of God." Their witness is itself a witness *of faith*, not of forensics. The §5 teaching holds even at the moment of maximum evidence.)
- *The relics anchor the Book of Mormon to a whole sacred history (17:1).* The list is remarkable: the plates, the breastplate, **the sword of Laban** (1 Ne 4 — Nephi's, then Nephite kingship's regalia), **the Urim and Thummim / interpreters given to the brother of Jared** (Ether 3, on the mount, face to face with the premortal Christ), and **the Liahona** ("the miraculous directors… given to Lehi… on the borders of the Red Sea," 1 Ne 16). The Three Witnesses don't just see *a* relic; they see the *physical continuity* of the entire Book of Mormon narrative — Jaredite, Lehite, Nephite — assembled in one place. The artifacts make the book's thousand-year span *tangible*: these objects were carried, used, buried by the people whose record this is. (It's a deliberate evidentiary density — not one proof but the convergence of a whole material history.)
- *The witnesses exist to protect Joseph (17:4).* "This you shall do that my servant Joseph Smith, Jun., may not be destroyed." The function of the Three is partly to *take the weight off Joseph* — to distribute the testimony so it does not rest on one man's word (the law-of-witnesses, Deut 19:15, §5:11-15). It also disarms the §10 trap: with three independent witnesses by the power of God, the "he made it up" charge loses its single target. The witnesses are a *shield* — the same logic as §10's wise-purpose redundancy, now in human form: God protects the work by not letting it stand on one point of failure.
- *"The same power, the same faith, the same gift like unto him" (17:7).* The witnesses receive what *Joseph* received — not a lesser, second-hand version. For the moment of the vision, they are raised to the prophet's own footing: same power, same faith, same gift. The witness is not a spectator's glimpse but a *participation* in Joseph's own seeric experience. (And it dignifies them permanently — whatever later estrangement came, all three saw "even as my servant Joseph Smith, Jun., has seen them," 17:5, by the same power.)
- *"By that faith which was had by the prophets of old" (17:2).* Their faith is placed in a lineage — the same faith as Moses, the brother of Jared, Nephi. The Restoration's witnesses are joined to the ancient seers by a *shared faith*, not just a shared doctrine. (Ether 12, the BoM walk's faith chapter, is the canon's theology of exactly this — "it was by faith that... the brother of Jared saw the finger of the Lord," Ether 12:20-21; the Three Witnesses see by the same faith.)
