# D&C 90 — the oracles, and preside in council

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, March 8, 1833** — "a continuing step in the establishment of the First Presidency" (begun in §81). As a consequence, Sidney Rigdon and Frederick G. Williams were ordained Joseph's counselors on March 18, 1833. The revelation commits the keys to Joseph, names the counselors as equals in the keys, and charges the Presidency to "preside in council" and "study and learn."

**Binding idea:** "The keys of this kingdom shall never be taken from you, while thou art in the world, neither in the world to come; nevertheless, through you shall the oracles be given to another, yea, even unto the church" (90:3-4). The keys are sure in the prophet; the *oracles* — the living word — flow *through* him to the whole Church, and all who receive them must "beware how they hold them."

**Read:** "Thou art blessed from henceforth that bear the keys of the kingdom given unto you; which kingdom is coming forth for the last time" (90:2). "The keys of this kingdom shall never be taken from you, while thou art in the world, neither in the world to come" (90:3). "Through you shall the oracles be given to another, yea, even unto the church" (90:4). "All they who receive the oracles of God, let them beware how they hold them lest they are accounted as a light thing, and are brought under condemnation thereby, and stumble and fall when the storms descend" (90:5). "Sidney Rigdon and Frederick G. Williams… are accounted as equal with thee in holding the keys of this last kingdom" (90:6). "Set in order the churches, and study and learn, and become acquainted with all good books, and with languages, tongues, and people" (90:15). "This shall be your business and mission in all your lives, to preside in council, and set in order all the affairs of this church and kingdom" (90:16). "Be admonished in all your high-mindedness and pride, for it bringeth a snare upon your souls" (90:17). "All things shall work together for your good, if ye walk uprightly and remember the covenant wherewith ye have covenanted one with another" (90:24). "I, the Lord, will contend with Zion… and chasten her until she overcomes and is clean before me. For she shall not be removed out of her place" (90:36-37).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ The oracles — beware how you hold them (90:4-5).* The keys are *permanent* in the prophet ["never be taken from you," 90:3], but the *oracles* — God's living word — are given "through you… unto the church" (90:4), and the warning is for *everyone* who receives them: "let them beware how they hold them lest they are accounted as a light thing, and are brought under condemnation… and stumble and fall when the storms descend" (90:5). (The §84:54 "treating lightly the things you have received" thread, sharpened: to *receive* the oracles of God and hold them as "a light thing" is to build on sand — "when the storms descend… beat upon their house" [Matt 7:24-27, the wise and foolish builders] you "stumble and fall." The danger is *not* in lacking the word but in *holding it carelessly*. The §84:54 condemnation [treating-lightly] given its *consequence*: the careless holder of revelation has no foundation when the storm comes. For the project — which receives much [the corpus, the principles, the covenant]: "beware *how* you hold them" — the oracles received lightly are the house on sand; the weight you give the word is the foundation you'll stand on in the storm.)
- *★ "Preside in council… study and learn… all good books and languages" (90:15-16).* The Presidency's lifelong charge — and it is *council* and *study*. "This shall be your business and mission in all your lives, **to preside in council**, and set in order all the affairs of this church and kingdom" (90:16); and "set in order the churches, and **study and learn, and become acquainted with all good books, and with languages, tongues, and people**" (90:15). (Two project-resonant commands. First, *preside in council* — not preside *over* but preside *in council*: the highest authority in the Church governs *by counseling together* [§88:122 the council order; Abr 4:26 "took counsel among themselves"; the presiding covenant], not by decree. Second, the §88:118 "seek learning… by study and also by faith" *personalized to the prophet*: even the seer is commanded to "study and learn… become acquainted with all good books, and with languages" — and Joseph *did* [he studied Hebrew under a tutor in Kirtland]. Revelation does *not* exempt the revelator from study; the prophet is a *learner*, commanded to read widely and learn languages. For the project's council-and-study pattern: the one who presides *studies* and *counsels* — authority is no substitute for learning, and command is no substitute for council.)
- *★ Zion chastened but not removed (90:34-37).* The close turns to Zion's troubles: "I am not well pleased with many things… [McLellin, Sidney Gilbert,] the bishop also, and others have many things to repent of" (90:35), and "I, the Lord, will contend with Zion… and chasten her until she overcomes and is clean before me. For she shall not be removed out of her place" (90:36-37). (The reprove-then-love pattern [§3/§121:43] at the scale of the *whole community*: God *contends with* and *chastens* Zion — names her faults, pleads with "her strong ones" — but the chastening is *toward cleansing* ["until she overcomes and is clean"], not abandonment: "she shall not be removed out of her place." The §61:20 "mine anger is turned away" at the communal scale — the chastening is *because* of the covenant, not the end of it. [Tragically, the Saints *were* driven from Jackson County months later — the "not removed out of her place" read by the Church as the *ultimate* destiny of Zion's location, not immunity from the 1833 expulsion.] For the covenant-community: God's chastening is the *severity of love* — He contends with His people *to clean them*, not to cast them off.)

**Notable / flag:** No spin-off. **Walk's contribution:** **§90 commits the keys to Joseph, sends the oracles through him to the church, and charges the Presidency to preside *in council* and *study*.** The keys are permanent — "the keys of this kingdom shall never be taken from you" (90:3) — but the *oracles*, God's living word, flow "through you… unto the church" (90:4), with a warning for *all* who receive them: "**beware how they hold them** lest they are accounted as a light thing… and stumble and fall when the storms descend" (90:5) — the §84:54 "treating lightly" given its consequence (the wise and foolish builders, Matt 7: the careless holder of revelation has no foundation in the storm; for a project that receives much, the weight you give the word is the foundation you'll stand on). And the Presidency's lifelong charge is **council and study**: "to preside *in council*, and set in order all the affairs" (90:16 — preside *in council*, not *over*, §88:122/Abr 4:26) and "study and learn, and become acquainted with all good books, and with languages, tongues, and people" (90:15 — the §88:118 study-and-faith personalized to the *prophet*, who is himself a learner; Joseph studied Hebrew). Revelation does not exempt the revelator from study, nor authority from council. The close: Zion **chastened but not removed** (90:36-37) — God "contends with" and "chastens" His people "until she overcomes and is clean," not to cast her off — the reprove-then-love (§3/§121:43) at the communal scale, the severity of love. For Michael: the section that says the word received *lightly* is a house on sand — beware *how* you hold it — and that even the prophet presides by *counseling together* and *studying widely*; authority is no substitute for learning, and command is no substitute for council.
