# D&C 53 — calling and election, beginning with forsaking the world

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, June 8, 1831** — to **Algernon Sidney Gilbert**, a merchant who asked the Lord, through Joseph, "concerning his calling and election in the church." Seven verses: his ordination as an elder, his appointment as a bishop's agent, and a sober closing word. (Gilbert would run the Church store in Independence; he died of cholera in 1834.)

**Binding idea:** Gilbert asked about his "calling and election" — and the answer *begins* with renunciation: "forsake the world" (53:2), *then* take ordination and a stewardship. The path to one's place in the kingdom starts not with the office but with letting go of the world; and "he only is saved who endureth unto the end" (53:7).

**Read:** "I have heard your prayers… concerning your calling and election in the church" (53:1). "I, the Lord, who was crucified for the sins of the world, give unto you a commandment that you shall forsake the world" (53:2). "Take upon you mine ordination, even that of an elder, to preach faith and repentance and remission of sins… and the reception of the Holy Spirit by the laying on of hands" (53:3); "and also to be an agent unto this church in the place which shall be appointed by the bishop" (53:4). "These are the first ordinances which you shall receive; and the residue shall be made known in a time to come, according to your labor in my vineyard" (53:6). "He only is saved who endureth unto the end" (53:7).

**Thoughts:**
- *"Forsake the world" comes first (53:2).* Gilbert, a businessman, asks about his standing in the church; the Lord's *first* word is "forsake the world." Before the office, before the agency, before the calling-and-election — *let go of the world*. (The order is the lesson: you cannot receive a kingdom-stewardship while still clutching the world. The §49:20 "the world lieth in sin" and the §53:2 "forsake the world" frame the merchant's call — he's to handle the Church's *temporal* business [the store, the agency, 53:4] precisely *after* forsaking the *worldliness* of it. You can administer money for the kingdom only once you've stopped serving it. The §41:11 guileless bishop and the §53:2 world-forsaking agent are the same safeguard: those who handle the kingdom's wealth must have let go of the world first.)
- *"The residue shall be made known… according to your labor" (53:6) — calling unfolds with faithfulness.* Gilbert receives "the *first* ordinances"; "the residue shall be made known in a time to come, according to your labor in my vineyard." Your full calling-and-election isn't handed over at once; it *unfolds* as you labor. (The discipline of the withheld [§25:4] applied to one's *own calling*: you get the next step, not the whole map, and the rest is *earned forward* by faithful labor. The §1:25-28 line-upon-line for a personal vocation — labor opens the next disclosure. A merchant asking "what is my place?" is told: here's the first part; the rest comes as you work.)
- *"He only is saved who endureth unto the end" (53:7) — the sober close.* The revelation ends not on the office but on *endurance*: the calling, the ordination, the agency mean nothing without enduring to the end. (And, poignantly, Gilbert *did* labor faithfully and died in the work — cholera, 1834, in the Missouri he'd helped settle. The closing word stood: he endured to *his* end. The §5:22/§9:14 "lifted up at the last day" promise made the condition of salvation — not the calling, but the *enduring* in it. A fitting word for a man whose faithful labor would be cut short: salvation is in the enduring, however long the labor lasts.)

**Notable / flag:** No spin-off. **Walk's contribution:** **§53 orders the path to one's place in the kingdom — forsake the world *first* (53:2)**, then receive the office and stewardship; you cannot administer the kingdom's temporal business while still clutching the world (the §41:11 guileless wealth-handler, the §49:20 world-that-lieth-in-sin). And **calling-and-election unfolds with labor (53:6)** — "the residue shall be made known… according to your labor": the discipline of the withheld applied to one's *own* vocation, the next step given and the rest earned forward by faithful work. It closes sober — "he only is saved who endureth unto the end" (53:7), the calling worth nothing without the enduring (and Gilbert endured to his own end in the work, 1834). For Michael: the merchant's call that puts renunciation before office, lets the full calling unfold only with labor, and stakes salvation not on the assignment but on enduring in it.
