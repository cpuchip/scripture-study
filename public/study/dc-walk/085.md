# D&C 85 — the still small voice that pierceth, and one mighty and strong

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, November 27, 1832** — "an extract from a letter of the Prophet to William W. Phelps," living in Independence. It answers a real problem: some Saints had moved to Zion but had *not* consecrated their properties, and so had not received inheritances "according to the established order." (Notable: a private *letter* that carries revelation — the §68:4 principle in action, a servant's words "moved upon by the Holy Ghost" becoming scripture.)

**Binding idea:** "It is contrary to the will and commandment of God that those who receive not their inheritance by consecration… should have their names enrolled with the people of God… Their names shall not be found… written in the book of the law of God" (85:3-5). The record of Zion is the *book of the law of God*; a name is enrolled there by *faithfulness* — by consecration, by enduring — and the unfaithful name is *not found*.

**Read:** "It is the duty of the Lord's clerk… to keep a history, and a general church record of all things that transpire in Zion, and of all those who consecrate properties, and receive inheritances legally from the bishop; and also their manner of life, their faith, and works; and also of the apostates who apostatize after receiving their inheritances" (85:1-2). "Their names shall not be found, neither the names of the fathers, nor the names of the children written in the book of the law of God" (85:5). "Thus saith the still small voice, which whispereth through and pierceth all things, and often times it maketh my bones to quake while it maketh manifest" (85:6). "I, the Lord God, will send one mighty and strong, holding the scepter of power in his hand, clothed with light for a covering… to set in order the house of God, and to arrange by lot the inheritances of the saints whose names are found… in the book of the law of God" (85:7). "That man, who was called of God and appointed, that putteth forth his hand to steady the ark of God, shall fall by the shaft of death, like as a tree that is smitten by the vivid shaft of lightning" (85:8). "These things I say not of myself; therefore, as the Lord speaketh, he will also fulfil" (85:10).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ The book of the law of God — enrolled by faithfulness (85:1-5).* The record-keeping thread [§47/§69/§72] reaches the *book of life*. The clerk keeps not only the history but the *register of the faithful* — "all those who consecrate properties, and receive inheritances… their manner of life, their faith, and works; and also of the apostates" (85:1-2). And the enrollment is *conditional*: the name of one who "receive[s] not their inheritance by consecration" is "not found… written in the book of the law of God" (85:5). (The earthly record mirrors the heavenly: the §47 "history written by the Comforter" and the §72 certificate of standing converge here into "the book of the law of God" — the register of who *kept the covenant*. Enrollment among "the people of God" is by *faithfulness*, not mere membership; the apostate's name, even after receiving an inheritance, can be *un-enrolled* [§82:7 "the former sins return"]. Malachi 3:16's "book of remembrance" for those who feared the Lord — names written *because* they were faithful. For the project's own memory/record discipline: the record is not neutral bookkeeping; it *registers fidelity*, and the standing it records is earned and kept, not assumed.)
- *★ "One mighty and strong… to set in order the house of God" (85:7).* A striking and much-discussed prophecy: "I, the Lord God, will send one mighty and strong, holding the scepter of power… clothed with light for a covering, whose mouth shall utter… eternal words… to set in order the house of God, and to arrange by lot the inheritances of the saints" (85:7). (A figure sent to *set the house in order* — to right what disorder had crept into the consecration system, distributing inheritances correctly to "the saints whose names are found… in the book of the law of God." The phrase has invited much speculation across Church history [claimants have arisen]; the walk notes it as a *promised setting-in-order*, not resolving the identity — that's beyond the text and the walk's scope. What the section *teaches* is clear: God will not leave His house in disorder; one mighty and strong will come to *order* it. The "scepter… clothed with light" echoes the §84:45 light-and-truth — the one who sets the house in order does so by *light* and "eternal words," not force.)
- *★ Steadying the ark — Uzzah's sin (85:8).* Paired with the one-mighty-and-strong is a warning: "that man… that putteth forth his hand to steady the ark of God, shall fall by the shaft of death" (85:8). (The Uzzah story [2 Sam 6:6-7; 1 Chr 13:9-10 — Uzzah, "called of God and appointed," reached out to steady the tipping ark and was struck dead]: the sin of *steadying the ark* is *presumptuous interference* — taking into one's own hands the correcting of God's work, as though God needed *us* to keep His ark from falling. The §28 order again: a man who oversteps his stewardship to "fix" the Lord's house — even with good intent, even when "called of God and appointed" — falls. The contrast is exact: the *one mighty and strong* is *sent* to set the house in order [authorized]; the ark-steadier *presumes* to do it himself [unauthorized] and falls. For the presiding covenant and any steward: the line between authorized action and presumptuous interference is *whether you were sent* — Uzzah's hand was sincere and fatal. Don't steady the ark; trust the One whose ark it is.)
- *★ The still small voice that pierces, and "not of myself" (85:6,10).* Framing the prophecy: "thus saith the still small voice, which whispereth through and pierceth all things, and often times it maketh my bones to quake while it maketh manifest" (85:6), and "these things I say not of myself" (85:10). (The revelation-by-the-Spirit thread [§8:2 mind-and-heart; §6:23 peace; §9:8 burning] given a new register: the still small voice [1 Kgs 19:12] that is *quiet* yet "pierceth all things" and "maketh my bones to quake." The softest voice is the most *penetrating* — it whispers, and it shakes the bones. And "not of myself" [§1:38] — even in a *letter*, Joseph distinguishes his own words from the Lord's, the §74:5 honesty about source. The deepest revelation comes not in thunder but in the piercing whisper; and the honest servant marks which words are the Lord's.)

**Notable / flag:** No spin-off. **Walk's contribution:** **§85 — an extract from a private letter that became scripture — gives the book of the law, the one mighty and strong, and Uzzah's warning.** The record of Zion is "the book of the law of God," in which a name is enrolled by *faithfulness* — by consecration and endurance — and the apostate's name, even after receiving an inheritance, is "not found" (85:1-5): the §47/§69/§72 record-keeping reaching the book of life, the record that *registers fidelity*, not neutral bookkeeping. The Lord promises "**one mighty and strong**… clothed with light for a covering… to set in order the house of God" (85:7) — an ordering accomplished by light and eternal words (§84:45), its identity beyond the text. And paired with it, the warning of **steadying the ark** (85:8): the man who "putteth forth his hand to steady the ark of God" falls (Uzzah, 2 Sam 6:6-7) — the sin of *presumptuous interference*, taking into one's own hands the correcting of God's work; the contrast exact, the one mighty and strong is *sent* (authorized) while the ark-steadier *presumes* (unauthorized) — the line, for any steward, is *whether you were sent*. All framed by "the still small voice, which… pierceth all things… maketh my bones to quake" (85:6) — the softest voice the most penetrating — and Joseph's "these things I say not of myself" (the source honestly marked, even in a letter). For Michael: the section that says the record registers fidelity, that God will set His own house in order by light, and that the sincere hand reaching to steady the ark falls — don't steady the ark; trust the One whose ark it is.
