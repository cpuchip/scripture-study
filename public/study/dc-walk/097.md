# D&C 97 — this is Zion: the pure in heart

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, August 2, 1833** — about the Saints in Zion (Jackson County, Missouri), in answer to Joseph's inquiry about a school of the prophets there. But by the time the revelation came, Missouri was in crisis: the Saints "were at this time subjected to severe persecution and, on July 23, 1833, had been forced to sign an agreement to leave Jackson County." Into that crisis the Lord gives the canon's definition of Zion — and the *condition* of her survival.

**Binding idea:** "This is Zion—**the pure in heart**" (97:21). Zion is not first a *place* but a *people*: the pure in heart. And "all the pure in heart that shall come into [the house] shall see God" (97:16). The city is defined by the hearts in it.

**Read:** "Many of whom are truly humble and are seeking diligently to learn wisdom and to find truth… blessed are such, for they shall obtain" (97:1-2). "The ax is laid at the root of the trees; and every tree that bringeth not forth good fruit shall be hewn down" (97:7). "All among them who know their hearts are honest, and are broken, and their spirits contrite, and are willing to observe their covenants by sacrifice… they are accepted of me" (97:8). "A house should be built unto me in the land of Zion, like unto the pattern… let it be built speedily, by the tithing of my people" (97:10-11). "A place of thanksgiving for all saints, and… a place of instruction… that they may be perfected in the understanding of their ministry, in theory, in principle, and in doctrine" (97:13-14). "Inasmuch as my people build a house unto me… and do not suffer any unclean thing to come into it… my glory shall rest upon it; yea, and my presence shall be there… and all the pure in heart that shall come into it shall see God" (97:15-16). "If it be defiled I will not come into it… for I will not come into unholy temples" (97:17). "This is Zion—the pure in heart" (97:21). "Zion shall escape if she observe to do all things whatsoever I have commanded her. But if she observe not… I will visit her according to all her works, with sore affliction" (97:25-26). "If she sin no more none of these things shall come upon her" (97:27).

**Thoughts:**
- *★★ "This is Zion—the pure in heart" (97:21).* The canon's *definition of Zion*, and the resolution of a thread running the whole walk. Through dozens of sections Zion has been a *place* — gathered to (§37), located at Independence (§57), built up, defended, wept over. Here it is defined by its *essence*: "this is Zion—**the pure in heart**" (Moses 7:18, Enoch's Zion, "of one heart and one mind… and there was no poor among them"). (Zion is not *primarily* a geography but a *purity of heart* — a people "of one heart" [§38:27 be-one] whose defining mark is *cleanness within*. This reframes everything: the *place* matters because of the *people*, and the people are Zion by being *pure in heart*. The §45:66 refuge, the §57 location, the §82:14 "beauty and holiness" all resolve here — Zion is wherever the pure in heart are gathered. And it is *portable* in a way a city is not: even as the Saints were being *expelled* from the Jackson County "Zion" [the very week of this revelation], the Lord defines Zion as something the expulsion could not take — *the pure in heart* carry it. For the project and for anyone: Zion is not a address you reach but a *condition of heart* you become; "the pure in heart" is the only city that cannot be driven out.)
- *★ "All the pure in heart… shall see God" (97:15-16).* The temple promise, conditioned on purity. The house built in Zion, kept undefiled — "all the pure in heart that shall come into it shall see God" (97:16). (The §67:11 / §88:68 / §93:1 see-God thread given its *temple* and its *condition*: Matthew 5:8 ["blessed are the pure in heart, for they shall see God"] located in a *house*. To *see God* requires *purity* — not the natural eye but the cleansed heart [§67:11 the natural man can't abide; the cleansing is the qualification]. And the §94:8-9 holiness-conditions-the-presence repeated: "if it be defiled I will not come into it… I will not come into unholy temples" [97:17]. The temple is where the pure in heart see God — and *both* must be pure: the house *and* the heart. I flag the see-God-in-the-temple thread as temple-reserved, Michael's. The doctrine plain enough: purity of heart is the organ of seeing God.)
- *★ Zion's survival is conditional — and the condition was being broken (97:25-27).* The most poignant verses, given *as Jackson County fell*: "Zion shall escape if she observe to do all things whatsoever I have commanded her. But if she observe not… I will visit her according to all her works, with sore affliction" (97:25-26). (The conditional [§82:10 "bound when ye do what I say"; §90:36 "chasten her until she… is clean"] at the most tragic moment: Zion's *protection* was never unconditional — "if she observe to do all things." And the Saints, divided and not fully keeping the law of consecration [§85 the un-consecrated; §101 will name the causes], did *not* meet the condition — and were driven out within months. The Lord even offers the door: "if she sin no more none of these things shall come upon her" [97:27]. The walk has tracked Zion's promise *and* the realism that the promise is *conditional*: the New Jerusalem will be built [§84:4], but *this* generation's Zion was lost because the condition wasn't kept. For the covenant-community: the promise is sure, but its *application to you, now* is conditioned on *your observing to do all things* — and the loss of Jackson County is the canon's sober proof that the condition is real. Zion the *pure in heart* endures; Zion the *unkept place* was driven out.)
- *The contrite accepted, the school, the house of instruction (97:7-14).* Threads woven in: "the ax is laid at the root of the trees… every tree that bringeth not forth good fruit shall be hewn down" [97:7, Matt 3:10 — the fruit test]; "all… whose hearts are honest, and are broken, and their spirits contrite, and are willing to observe their covenants by sacrifice… are accepted" [97:8, the §59:8/§64:34 contrite-and-willing]; and the house as "a place of thanksgiving… a place of instruction… that they may be perfected in the understanding of their ministry, in theory, in principle, and in doctrine" [97:13-14, the §88:119 house of learning]. (The temple is a *house of instruction* — perfecting the ministry "in theory, in principle, and in doctrine" — built "by the tithing of my people" [97:11]. The acceptance is by the *contrite and willing* heart, tested by *fruit* [the ax at the root]. The strands all serve the binding idea: Zion is the pure in heart, and the house is where the pure are taught and see God.)
