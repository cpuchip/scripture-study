# D&C 136 — the Word and Will of the Lord: if thou art merry, dance

**Setting:** **Winter Quarters, on the west bank of the Missouri River, January 14, 1847** — the Saints, driven from Nauvoo after the martyrdom, are encamped in misery on the Nebraska/Iowa plains, preparing the exodus to the Rocky Mountains. This is **the only canonized revelation given through Brigham Young** — proof in itself that the kingdom did not die with Joseph (§118/§107:99: the office outlasts the man; "you have received my kingdom," 136:41). It organizes the Camp of Israel for the trek and gives the standards by which they are to travel.

**Binding idea:** **"The Word and Will of the Lord concerning the Camp of Israel in their journeyings to the West… Let all the people… be organized into companies, with a covenant and promise to keep all the commandments" (136:1-2).** The exodus is a *covenant* journey, ordered like Moses's Israel (captains of hundreds, fifties, tens) — and traveled with the poor carried, the trials borne, and joy commanded even on the trail.

**Read:**
- The organization: "Let all the people… be organized into companies, with a covenant and promise to keep all the commandments… Let the companies be organized with captains of hundreds, captains of fifties, and captains of tens… And this shall be our covenant—that we will walk in all the ordinances of the Lord" (136:2-4).
- Carry the poor: "Let each company bear an equal proportion, according to the dividend of their property, in taking the poor, the widows, the fatherless, and the families of those who have gone into the army, that the cries of the widow and the fatherless come not up into the ears of the Lord against this people" (136:8).
- The wise steward: "Thou shalt be diligent in preserving what thou hast, that thou mayest be a wise steward; for it is the free gift of the Lord thy God, and thou art his steward" (136:27).
- Joy on the trail: "If thou art merry, praise the Lord with singing, with music, with dancing, and with a prayer of praise and thanksgiving. If thou art sorrowful, call on the Lord thy God with supplication, that your souls may be joyful" (136:28-29).
- Trial as preparation: "My people must be tried in all things, that they may be prepared to receive the glory that I have for them, even the glory of Zion; and he that will not bear chastisement is not worthy of my kingdom" (136:31).
- The martyrdom needful: "Many have marveled because of his death; but it was needful that he should seal his testimony with his blood, that he might be honored and the wicked might be condemned" (136:39). "Have I not delivered you from your enemies, only in that I have left a witness of my name?" (136:40).

**Thoughts:**

- *★ Joy commanded on the hardest road (136:28-29).* The most striking command in a revelation about a winter exodus is to *celebrate*: "If thou art merry, praise the Lord with **singing**, with music, with **dancing**, and with a prayer of praise and thanksgiving" (136:28). (On the bleakest journey in the Saints' history — driven from their homes, burying their dead on the plains — the Lord commands not grim endurance but *joy*: sing, dance, give thanks. The §59:15-19 goodness-of-the-earth [pleasure rightly ordered is holy] and the §128:22 sacred-ends-in-joy given their hardest-case form: joy is not a reward that waits for better circumstances but a *discipline practiced in the worst of them*. And the balance — "if thou art sorrowful, call on the Lord… that your souls may be **joyful**" (136:29) — names sorrow honestly *and* points it toward joy. For the project's theology of trial: the §122 affliction-curriculum is not joyless; the command on the trail of tears is to dance. Joy is commanded *in* the affliction, not after it — the Saints who danced at Winter Quarters were obeying.)

- *★ Consecration on the move — carry the poor (136:8).* The exodus is not every-family-for-itself: "Let each company bear an equal proportion… in taking the poor, the widows, the fatherless… that the cries of the widow and the fatherless come not up… against this people" (136:8). (The §42/§104/§119 consecration thread applied to a migration: the company carries those who cannot carry themselves, *equally proportioned* — the §38:27 "be one" / §104:17 "enough and to spare" enacted on the trail. The cry of the unprovided poor "comes up… against this people" [the §104:18 hoarder's judgment]; a Zion-people does not leave its weak behind. For the project's stewardship: the measure of a community on the move is whether it carries its poor — "an equal proportion… that the cries… come not up against this people." And the stewardship hinge restated, 136:27: "thou art his steward… it is the free gift of the Lord" [§104:55-56 received-not-owned, even on the road].)

- *★ Trial as preparation, and the martyrdom "needful" (136:31,39).* Two confirmations of threads the walk has carried. The trial: "My people must be **tried in all things, that they may be prepared** to receive the glory… even the glory of Zion; and he that will not bear chastisement is not worthy of my kingdom" (136:31) — the §122:7 "experience" / §105:6 "learn obedience by suffering" restated under Brigham, now framing the exodus itself as the curriculum. And the martyrdom: "it was **needful** that he should seal his testimony with his blood, that he might be honored and the wicked might be condemned" (136:39). (The §135 "the death seals the testimony" *confirmed by revelation* — not a tragedy that befell the work but something "needful," the testator's death putting the testament in force [§135:5, Heb 9:16-17]. For the walk: §135 announced the seal; §136:39 ratifies it as the Lord's own word — the martyrdom was *needful*, and the kingdom continued [a Brigham-given revelation is itself the proof].)
