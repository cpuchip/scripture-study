# Official Declaration 2 — the priesthood extended to all (1978)

**Setting:** **The revelation received by President Spencer W. Kimball in the Salt Lake Temple, June 1, 1978; announced June 8; sustained by the Church September 30, 1978.** The official declaration extending the priesthood and temple blessings to *all* worthy male members "without regard for race or color." The header is honest about the history it closes: a few black men were ordained in Joseph Smith's day; "early in its history, Church leaders stopped conferring the priesthood on black males of African descent. **Church records offer no clear insights into the origins of this practice.**" The restriction ended by revelation. This is the **last unit of the Doctrine and Covenants — and the close of the bin-4 priesthood-lineage cross-walk thread that has run through all three canon walks.**

**Binding idea:** **"He has heard our prayers, and by revelation has confirmed that the long-promised day has come when every faithful, worthy man in the Church may receive the holy priesthood… all worthy male members of the Church may be ordained to the priesthood without regard for race or color."** The priesthood is universalized — and the book that opened with the Lord's voice "unto all men" (§1:2) ends with the priesthood extended to all.

**Read:**
- The standard the header invokes: "The Book of Mormon teaches that 'all are alike unto God,' including 'black and white, bond and free, male and female' (2 Nephi 26:33)."
- The revelation by supplication: "this revelation, which came to him after extended meditation and prayer in the sacred rooms of the holy temple." "...we have pleaded long and earnestly in behalf of these, our faithful brethren, spending many hours in the Upper Room of the Temple supplicating the Lord for divine guidance."
- The declaration: "He has heard our prayers, and by revelation has confirmed that the long-promised day has come when every faithful, worthy man in the Church may receive the holy priesthood, with power to exercise its divine authority, and enjoy with his loved ones every blessing that flows therefrom, including the blessings of the temple. Accordingly, all worthy male members of the Church may be ordained to the priesthood without regard for race or color."
- The reach: "the Lord has now made known his will for the blessing of all his children throughout the earth who will hearken to the voice of his authorized servants." "The vote to sustain the foregoing motion was unanimous in the affirmative."
