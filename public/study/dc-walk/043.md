# D&C 43 — all the day long, but ye would not

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, February 1831** — "some members of the Church were disturbed by people making false claims as revelators" (the Hiram Page problem, §28, recurring with new claimants in Kirtland; one woman, "Mrs. Hubble," was reportedly producing revelations). The Lord reaffirms the order of revelation as a *standing law*, then turns to the elders' charge and to a sweeping warning to the nations — including one of the most moving passages in the D&C on how relentlessly God calls.

**Binding idea:** "There is none other appointed unto you to receive commandments and revelations until he be taken… receive not the teachings of any that shall come before you as revelations or commandments… that you may not be deceived" (43:3-6). The order of revelation (§28) is now a *law* against deception. And the Lord's own voice models the alternative to deception — His *persistent, multi-modal calling* of a people who "would not" (43:25).

**Read:** "Ye have received a commandment for a law [§42]… through him whom I have appointed" (43:2); "there is none other appointed unto you to receive commandments and revelations until he be taken, if he abide in me… none else shall be appointed unto this gift except it be through him" (43:3-4). "This shall be a law unto you, that ye receive not the teachings of any that shall come before you as revelations or commandments; and this I give unto you that you may not be deceived, that you may know they are not of me" (43:5-6). "He that is ordained of me shall come in at the gate" (43:7). When assembled, "ye shall instruct and edify each other… and be sanctified" (43:8-9). "Ye are not sent forth to be taught, but to teach… and ye are to be taught from on high. Sanctify yourselves and ye shall be endowed with power" (43:15-16). The great day is nigh (43:17); the trump shall say "Ye saints arise and live; ye sinners stay and sleep until I shall call again" (43:18). Then God's voice to the nations: "How often would I have gathered you together as a hen gathereth her chickens under her wings, but ye would not! How oft have I called upon you by the mouth of my servants, and by the ministering of angels, and by mine own voice, and by the voice of thunderings, and… lightnings… tempests… earthquakes… famines and pestilences… and by the voice of judgment, and by the voice of mercy all the day long, and by the voice of glory and honor and the riches of eternal life, and would have saved you with an everlasting salvation, but ye would not!" (43:24-25). "Let the solemnities of eternity rest upon your minds. Be sober" (43:34-35).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ The order of revelation made standing law against deception (43:5-6).* §28 corrected the Hiram Page episode; §43 generalizes it into *law* because the problem *recurred* — new false revelators in Kirtland. "This shall be a law unto you, that ye receive not the teachings of any that shall come before you as revelations or commandments… that you may not be deceived" (43:5-6). The structural discernment cross-check (§28: is this *in order*?) is now the Church's permanent immune system against deception. (And note the *why*: "that you may not be **deceived**." The order isn't about control; it's *protection from being fooled* — a single appointed channel means a counterfeit revelator is exposed by *position*, not by having to out-argue them. The same guardrail on the period-language principle [§28], now codified: the felt-spiritual claim of a false revelator is defeated not by debating its feel but by the fact that *they aren't appointed*. Deception's defense is order.)
- *★ "All the day long… but ye would not" (43:24-25) — divine persistence meets human agency.* This is the section's emotional center and a theological masterpiece. God catalogs *how* He has called — prophets, angels, His own voice, thunder, lightning, tempests, earthquakes, famines, pestilences, judgment, *mercy*, "glory and honor and the riches of eternal life" — every register from the catastrophic to the tender, "**all the day long**." And the refrain, twice: "**but ye would not**" (Matt 23:37, Christ weeping over Jerusalem). The picture is of a God who *will not stop calling* and a people who *will not* answer. (This is the agency doctrine [§29:39] at its most poignant: God's persistence is *total* — He exhausts every voice — and it *still* cannot override the will. "Ye would not" is the two-word monument to agency: even infinite, multi-modal divine love does not compel. The bitter truth beneath "agency requires opposition" — the same freedom that lets a soul choose God lets it refuse Him through *every* voice He has. And the grief in it: the calling is *love* ["would have saved you… but ye would not"], so the refusal is a wound to God, not just a loss to the refuser. The §6:22 God who remembers a private prayer is the same God who calls "all the day long" and is *refused*.)
- *★ "Not sent to be taught, but to teach… taught from on high… endowed with power" (43:15-16).* The elders' authority to teach rests on being *taught from on high* first — and the promise is again the *endowment*: "sanctify yourselves and ye shall be endowed with power" (38:32, the second sounding). The teaching law of §42:14 ("if ye receive not the Spirit ye shall not teach") given its source: you teach from what heaven taught *you*, and the capacity to teach is *endowed*, not self-generated. (Teach-from-what-you-received [§11:21, §35:20 the word as God holds it] — the elders are conduits of a word taught them from above, which is why sanctification precedes the endowment precedes the teaching.)
- *"Let the solemnities of eternity rest upon your minds. Be sober" (43:34-35).* The closing posture: *solemnity*. Not grim, but *weighty* — the great realities (the coming, the two resurrections, the relentless calling) are to *rest upon the mind* as a settled seriousness. (Against levity [the footnote → D&C 88:121] — the right response to "the solemnities of eternity" is sobriety, a mind that holds the weight of what's coming. It pairs with the §19:39 "canst thou read this without rejoicing?" — joy and solemnity together, not flippancy: the eternal things are *glad* and *grave* at once.)

And the **endowment** sounding again (43:16) with the teaching law's source (teach from what heaven taught you). For Michael: the section where God shows the alternative to the false revelator — not deception but a God who calls through every voice He has, all the day long, and *still* will not force a soul that "would not." The order protects from being fooled; the agency means even infinite love doesn't compel.
