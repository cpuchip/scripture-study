# D&C 52 — a pattern in all things, that ye may not be deceived

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, June 6, 1831**, closing a four-day conference at which "the first distinctive ordinations to the office of high priest were made, and certain manifestations of false and deceiving spirits were discerned and rebuked." This revelation sends Joseph, Sidney, and a long roster of elders to **Missouri** for the next conference — toward the land of their inheritance — and, in the middle of the travel assignments, gives a *pattern* by which "ye shall know the spirits in all cases under the whole heavens" (52:19): the concrete, checkable continuation of §50's discernment capstone.

**Binding idea:** "I will give unto you a pattern in all things, that ye may not be deceived; for Satan is abroad in the land" (52:14). The pattern is a *rubric* for the true spirit: contrite, meek, *edifying*, bringing forth *fruits* — "he that is overcome and bringeth not forth fruits, even according to this pattern, is not of me" (52:18). And the destination is named: Missouri, "the land of your inheritance, which is now the land of your enemies" (52:42).

**Read:** "The next conference… shall be held in Missouri, upon the land which I will consecrate unto my people, which are a remnant of Jacob" (52:2). Joseph and Sidney journey to Missouri; "inasmuch as they are faithful… it shall be made known unto them the land of your inheritance" (52:5). The elders preach by the way, "saying none other things than that which the prophets and apostles have written, and that which is taught them by the Comforter" (52:9), "two by two" (52:10). Then the pattern: "I will give unto you a pattern in all things, that ye may not be deceived; for Satan is abroad in the land" (52:14): "he that prayeth, whose spirit is contrite, the same is accepted of me if he obey mine ordinances" (52:15); "he that speaketh, whose spirit is contrite, whose language is meek and edifieth, the same is of God if he obey mine ordinances" (52:16); "he that trembleth under my power shall be made strong, and shall bring forth fruits of praise and wisdom" (52:17); "he that is overcome and bringeth not forth fruits, even according to this pattern, is not of me" (52:18). "By this pattern ye shall know the spirits in all cases under the whole heavens" (52:19). The roster of elders is assigned, each by name, to travel to Missouri preaching (52:22-33); "one man shall not build upon another's foundation, neither journey in another's track" (52:33). "Remember in all things the poor and the needy, the sick and the afflicted, for he that doeth not these things, the same is not my disciple" (52:40). "The land of your inheritance, which is now the land of your enemies" (52:42); "I, the Lord, will hasten the city in its time" (52:43).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ The pattern — §50's capstone made a checkable rubric (52:14-19).* §50 gave the discernment *principle* ("that which is of God is light… edifies"); §52 gives the *pattern* — a usable checklist for the true spirit: **contrite** (the heart's posture, 52:15-16), **meek** (the manner, 52:16), **edifieth** (the fruit in the body, 52:16, straight from §50:23), brings forth **fruits of praise and wisdom** (52:17), and **obeys the ordinances** (52:15-16, anchored in the covenant order). And the negative is sharp: "he that is overcome and bringeth not forth fruits… is **not of me**" (52:18). "By this pattern ye shall know the spirits **in all cases under the whole heavens**" (52:19) — a *universal* discernment tool. (This completes the discernment thread's progression: §28 structural [order], §43 standing-law, §45:57 the wise are undeceived, §46 the gift, §50 the principle [light/edifies], §52 the *pattern* [contrite + meek + edifying + fruitful + ordinance-obedient]. The fruit-test [Matt 7:16; Moroni 7:14-19] turned into a five-point rubric. Notice the *humility markers* in it: a true spirit is *contrite* and *meek* — it doesn't aggrandize, it lowers; the false spirit, by contrast, was the trance-and-spectacle of §50's setting. A genuine manifestation makes a person *more contrite and meek and fruitful*; one that makes a person dramatic, proud, or barren is "not of me." The discernment isn't esoteric — it's *check the fruit and the posture*.)
- *★ "Build not on another's foundation, neither journey in another's track" (52:33) — the fan-out principle.* The dozens of elders are sent to Missouri "in their several courses," each commanded *not* to retrace another's path: "one man shall not build upon another's foundation, neither journey in another's track" (Rom 15:20, Paul's "not where Christ was named"). Each missionary breaks *new* ground; no two overlap. (A genuinely useful organizing principle — the work is *parallelized*, each laborer covering territory no one else covers, so the whole field is reached without duplication. The same instinct as the workspace's fan-out skill: when a task is many independent units, spread distinct laborers across distinct ground rather than piling onto the same patch. The Lord's missionary deployment is *coverage by non-overlap* — a fresh-ground-per-laborer pattern, each "kept and blessed with much fruit" [52:34] on his own track.)
- *Care for the poor = the mark of discipleship (52:40).* Tucked into the assignments: "remember in all things the poor and the needy, the sick and the afflicted, for he that doeth not these things, **the same is not my disciple**." The same absolute test as §41:5 (the disciple is the doer) and §38:27 (be one) — *discipleship is disqualified by neglect of the poor*. Not "a good disciple cares for the poor" but "he that doeth not… is *not my disciple*." (The §44:6 poor-as-the-condition-of-the-law and §42:38 the-poor-are-Christ, stated as the line between disciple and not. Whatever else an elder does on the road, if he forgets the poor he forfeits the name.)
- *"The land of your inheritance, which is now the land of your enemies" (52:42).* The destination is named with sober honesty: Missouri is *the* land of inheritance — *and* "now the land of your enemies." The promised place is not yet a safe place; the inheritance is held in faith against present hostility. ("I will hasten the city in its time," 52:43 — the city is coming, but the time is the Lord's, and the present is *enemy ground*. The same realism as §38:28 [the enemy at home] — Zion's location is given, but it must be *won* through opposition, not received as a finished gift. The land of inheritance and the land of enemies are, for now, the same land.)
