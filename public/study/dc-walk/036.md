# D&C 36 — every man who embraces it, sent forth

**Setting:** **Near Fayette, New York, December 9, 1830** — two days after §35 — to **Edward Partridge**, who had come east with Sidney Rigdon. Joseph's history calls Partridge "a pattern of piety, and one of the Lord's great men"; he would become the **first bishop** of the Church (§41). Eight verses: his personal call, and through him a *general* commandment about who may be ordained and sent.

**Binding idea:** From one man's call the Lord opens a *universal* door: "every man which will embrace it with singleness of heart may be ordained and sent forth" (36:7). The call to preach is not reserved to a chosen few — *anyone* who embraces the gospel with a single heart may be ordained to carry it.

**Read:** "My servant Edward… you are blessed, and your sins are forgiven you, and you are called to preach my gospel as with the voice of a trump" (36:1); "I will lay my hand upon you by the hand of my servant Sidney Rigdon, and you shall receive my Spirit, the Holy Ghost, even the Comforter, which shall teach you the peaceable things of the kingdom" (36:2). "This calling and commandment give I unto you concerning all men—that as many as shall come before my servants Sidney Rigdon and Joseph Smith, Jun., embracing this calling… shall be ordained and sent forth to preach the everlasting gospel among the nations" (36:4-5); "every man which will embrace it with singleness of heart may be ordained and sent forth" (36:7). "Gird up your loins and I will suddenly come to my temple" (36:8).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "The peaceable things of the kingdom" (36:2) — what the Spirit teaches.* The Comforter's curriculum is named with a memorable phrase: not the spectacular or the speculative, but *the peaceable things of the kingdom*. The Holy Ghost teaches *peace* — the quiet, settling, kingdom-building truths, not signs and wonders (cf. §63's coming rebuke of sign-seeking). (This pairs with the §6:23 "peace to your mind" and §19:23 "you shall have peace in me": the consistent texture of the Spirit's teaching is *peace*. A prompting toward agitation, spectacle, or contention bears a different signature — another quiet datum for the discernment thread: the Spirit's things are *peaceable*.)
- *★ The open door — "every man… with singleness of heart" (36:7).* Edward's personal call becomes a *general law*: the ministry is open to *anyone* who embraces the gospel "with singleness of heart." The only gate is *singleness* (sincerity, undivided motive — the §4:5 "eye single") — not learning, status, or special selection. This is the §35:13 "weak things" doctrine institutionalized: the Restoration deliberately makes its ministry *broad and lay*, ordaining ordinary believers, because God works through the willing-and-single-hearted, not the credentialed. (The democratization of the ministry — every worthy member a potential elder — is built in here, two sections after the constitution. It's the opposite of priestcraft [§33:4]: not a professional clergy preaching for gain, but a lay priesthood of the single-hearted.)
- *"Hating even the garments spotted with the flesh" (36:6) — the urgency of repentance.* The call to "save yourselves from this untoward generation" (Acts 2:40) is stark (Jude 23): flee corruption so completely as to *hate even the garment* contaminated by it. (Strong language, in the early-Restoration register [§24's hard missionary law] — the point is the *thoroughness* of the break from "the fire," not a counsel of contempt.) And it closes on Malachi: "I will suddenly come to my temple" (36:8, Mal 3:1) — the Lord's coming to His temple, the §27 Elijah/temple thread sounding again.
