# D&C 72 — render an account of his stewardship

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, December 4, 1831** — three days after §71, with Joseph back in Kirtland where elders and members "had assembled to learn their duty." Three revelations of the same day, compiled: verses 1-8 call **Newel K. Whitney** as a bishop (the *second* in the Church, after Edward Partridge in Missouri — Whitney to serve in Kirtland); verses 9-23 detail a bishop's duties; verses 24-26 govern the gathering to Zion. The thread through all three is *stewardship rendered and certified*.

**Binding idea:** "It is required of the Lord, at the hand of every steward, to **render an account of his stewardship, both in time and in eternity**" (72:3). Every steward accounts — now and forever; the bishop is the appointed office that *receives* the account and keeps it on record.

**Read:** "It is required of the Lord, at the hand of every steward, to render an account of his stewardship, both in time and in eternity" (72:3). "He who is faithful and wise in time is accounted worthy to inherit the mansions prepared for him of my Father" (72:4). "The elders of the church in this part of my vineyard shall render an account of their stewardship unto the bishop" (72:5); "these things shall be had on record, to be handed over unto the bishop in Zion" (72:6). "My servant Newel K. Whitney is the man who shall be appointed and ordained unto this power" (72:8). The bishop's duty: "to keep the Lord's storehouse; to receive the funds of the church… to take an account of the elders… and to administer to their wants" (72:10-11), "that this also may be consecrated to the good of the church, to the poor and needy" (72:12). "The labors of the faithful who labor in spiritual things… shall answer the debt unto the bishop in Zion" (72:14). "A certificate from the judge or bishop… unto the bishop in Zion, rendereth every man acceptable, and answereth all things, for an inheritance, and to be received as a wise steward and as a faithful laborer; otherwise he shall not be accepted" (72:17-18). "Let my servants who are appointed as stewards over the literary concerns of my church have claim for assistance upon the bishop… that the revelations may be published, and go forth unto the ends of the earth" (72:20-21). "This shall be an ensample for all the extensive branches of my church, in whatsoever land they shall be established" (72:23). "Let them carry up unto the bishop a certificate from three elders of the church… otherwise he who shall go up unto the land of Zion shall not be accounted as a wise steward" (72:25-26).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "Render an account… both in time and in eternity" (72:3-4).* The stewardship-accounting of §70:4 ("an account… in the day of judgment") given its *ongoing earthly mechanism*. It is not only at the *final* judgment that the steward accounts — it is "both in time and in eternity," and the *bishop* is the office that receives the temporal account now (72:5). "He who is faithful and wise in time is accounted worthy to inherit the mansions" (72:4, Matt 24:45 / 25:21 — the faithful and wise steward). (The consecration system the walk has tracked [§42 the law → §51 in practice → §70 over the revelations] gets its *accountability office*: the bishop. The account is rendered *to a person, on a record* [72:6], not left to private conscience or the distant judgment alone — accountability is *structural and present*, a regular reckoning to an appointed steward-of-stewards. This is the §47/§69 record-keeping fused with the §42 consecration: the Church *writes down* who rendered what account, and the temporal record feeds "the bishop in Zion." Stewardship without an accounting office drifts; §72 builds the office. For the project's own stewardship pattern: the account is not only owed at the end — it's rendered *in time*, on a record, to a real steward who keeps the watch.)
- *★ The certificate system — verified standing, not self-asserted (72:17-26).* A striking piece of Church order: a steward is rendered "acceptable" to the bishop in Zion *by a certificate* — "a certificate from the judge or bishop… rendereth every man acceptable, and answereth all things… otherwise he shall not be accepted" (72:17-18); and one who would gather to Zion must "carry up… a certificate from three elders… otherwise he… shall not be accounted as a wise steward" (72:25-26). (Standing in the kingdom is *conferred and certified*, not *self-claimed*. The §20:64 / §52:41 recommend thread becomes a formal system: your good standing is *witnessed* by those who know your labor [72:19 — "recommended by the church… in which he labors"] and *certified* by the appointed office. This is the §28 order-of-things again — authority and standing run through the channel, witnessed and recorded, never merely asserted by the individual. The forerunner of the temple recommend and the ministerial certificate: you don't certify *yourself*; the order certifies you, on the testimony of those who've watched your work. It guards against both the imposter [who claims standing he hasn't earned] and the self-deceived [who thinks better of his standing than his labor warrants] — a second witness on every steward's standing, exactly the §69:1 wisdom applied to *persons*.)
- *The bishop cares for the poor; the literary stewards funded (72:9-23).* The bishop's concrete duties: keep the storehouse, receive funds, "administer to their wants," and consecrate the surplus "to the good of the church, to the poor and needy" (72:10-12) — and a specific provision that the literary stewards [the §70 Book-of-Commandments firm] "have claim for assistance upon the bishop… that the revelations may be published" (72:20-21). (The consecration machinery made concrete: the bishop is the hub where funds come in and flow out to the poor [§42:34] and to the work [publishing the revelations, §70/§72]. "The labors of the faithful who labor in spiritual things… shall answer the debt" [72:14] — the §70:12 laborer-worthy-of-hire operationalized: spiritual labor *counts* in the storehouse's accounting, settling the laborer's debt. And "this shall be an ensample for all the extensive branches… in whatsoever land" [72:23] — Kirtland's pattern is the *template* for the whole Church, then and now: every unit has a bishop, a storehouse function, an accounting, a certified standing.)
