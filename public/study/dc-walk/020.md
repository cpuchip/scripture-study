# D&C 20 — the constitution of the Church

**Setting:** At or near **Fayette, New York**; parts perhaps as early as summer 1829, the whole "recorded soon after **April 6, 1830** — the day the Church was organized." Known then as **the Articles and Covenants**, this is the Church's founding document — its constitution. Joseph wrote that the revelation "pointed out to us the precise day upon which… we should proceed to organize His Church once more here upon the earth." Eighty-four verses: a history, a creed, the offices and their duties, the ordinances, and the sacrament prayers still used word-for-word today.

**Binding idea:** Before a single congregation gathered, the Lord gave the *order* — who may baptize, how, what the sacrament prayers say, how members are received and disciplined, how records are kept. The Church was not improvised and later codified; it was *organized* by revelation from day one. "All things may be done in order" (20:68). This is "organize yourselves; prepare every needful thing" (88:119) enacted at the founding.

**Read:** "The rise of the Church of Christ in these last days… it being regularly organized and established… on the sixth day of the month which is called April" (20:1), through Joseph, "the first elder," and Oliver, "the second elder" (20:2-3). Joseph's own story, *including the failure*: after his remission of sins "he was entangled again in the vanities of the world; but after repenting… God ministered unto him by an holy angel" (20:5-6) and gave power to translate (20:8). The Book of Mormon proves "the holy scriptures are true, and that God does inspire men… as well as in generations of old; thereby showing that he is the same God yesterday, today, and forever" (20:11-12). Then the creed (20:17-28): "there is a God in heaven, who is infinite and eternal… the framer of heaven and earth" (20:17); "he created man, male and female, after his own image" (20:18); the Fall (20:20); the Only Begotten given (20:21), "crucified, died, and rose again the third day" (20:23); salvation for "all those from the beginning… as well as those who should come after" (20:26-27); "justification through the grace of our Lord… is just and true" (20:30) and "sanctification through the grace… is just and true" (20:31) — yet "there is a possibility that man may fall from grace" (20:32), so "let the church take heed and pray always" (20:33). "These things are true… neither adding to, nor diminishing from" the scriptures (20:35). The baptismal prayer (20:73), the offices and duties (20:38-67), the blessing of children (20:70), and the **sacrament prayers**: "O God, the Eternal Father, we ask thee in the name of thy Son, Jesus Christ, to bless and sanctify this bread to the souls of all those who partake of it… that they are willing to take upon them the name of thy Son, and always remember him and keep his commandments… that they may always have his Spirit to be with them" (20:77); and over the wine, "that they do always remember him, that they may have his Spirit to be with them" (20:79).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ Organize-before-building, enacted (20:68 "all things in order").* This whole section is the project's "Organize Before Building" principle (88:119) at the literal founding. The Lord doesn't hand the new Church a vision and let structure emerge; He gives the *structure first* — offices, ordinances, the exact words of the prayers, the record-keeping, the discipline. Order is not bureaucracy here; it is *protection* — it keeps the ordinances uniform (anyone baptized says the same words, 20:73), the doctrine fixed ("neither adding to nor diminishing," 20:35), and the body knit ("that all things may be done in order"). The Restoration's first act after authority (§13) is *specification* (§20). (This is the creation-cycle's Step 4 — Specification — in the Church's own birth: intent → covenant → stewardship → *the spec*. The Articles and Covenants are the spec.)
- *★ The canon includes the prophet's relapse (20:5).* Right in the founding constitution, in the official history of the Church's own prophet: after his first forgiveness "he was entangled again in the vanities of the world." Not buried, not softened — *in the charter.* The Church's founding document records that its founding prophet backslid and had to repent again (the §3 / §10:1-2 lost-pages failure named once more). This is `include_failures` at the most public possible level — the constitution itself refuses a flawless founder. (The honesty is doctrinally load-bearing: a Church that admits its prophet's sins in its charter is making a claim about *grace*, not about Joseph — "there is a possibility that man may fall from grace" applies to the first elder too, 20:32.)
- *The compact creed (20:17-28) is deliberately ecumenical-sounding — then quietly distinct.* Verses 17-28 read, at first, like a standard Christian creed: infinite eternal God, creation, Fall, the Only Begotten, crucified and risen, salvation by grace, "Father, Son, and Holy Ghost are one God." But woven in are the Restoration's distinctives: man created "after his own image" (20:18 — the footnote points to God's *corporeal* nature, Ether 3:15); salvation reaches "all those from the beginning… who believed in the words of the holy prophets" (20:26 — the pre-Christian faithful saved by the *same* gospel, not a different covenant); and the real possibility of *falling from grace* (20:32, against once-saved-always-saved). The creed meets historic Christianity on shared ground and then, without polemic, states what's different. (It's §18:20's "contend against no church" as a *drafting* posture — affirm the common faith, mark the distinction, don't argue.)
- *★ The sacrament prayers (20:77,79) — the covenant renewed weekly.* These two prayers, given here, are recited unchanged in every sacrament meeting today — among the most-repeated words in the Restoration. And they gather the cluster's threads: to partake is to *witness* willingness "to take upon them the name of thy Son" (the §18 name-theology), to "always remember him and keep his commandments," with the promise "that they may always have his Spirit to be with them." The sacrament is the *standing renewal* of the §18 covenant — taking the name, week by week — and its promise is the §8:2 indwelling Spirit. (The structure: *we witness* / *that we may have His Spirit.* The covenant is bilateral, like every covenant in this project — we commit to remember and keep; He gives the Spirit. Covenant.yaml's "I, the Lord, am bound when ye do what I say" [82:10] is the same shape: the sacrament is D&C 82:10 administered.)
- *The offices defined by duty, not rank (20:38-59).* Elder, priest, teacher, deacon — each is given a *job*, and the jobs overlap and assist ("the priest is to assist the elder if occasion requires," 20:52; deacons assist the teacher, 20:57). The teacher's charge is striking: "watch over the church always… see that there is no iniquity… neither hardness with each other, neither lying, backbiting, nor evil speaking" (20:53-54) — the office is *pastoral watchcare*, the Abr 4:18 "watch over" given a church office. (The presiding/watching thread from the PoGP walk lands in the Church's structure: an office whose whole duty is to *watch over* the body — and to do it by being "with and strengthen them," not by force, 20:53.)
- *Common consent built in from day one (20:63-67).* "No person is to be ordained… without the vote of that church" (20:65). The Restoration's hierarchy is *not* top-down imposition — every officer is sustained by the vote of the people. Authority comes from God by conferral (§13), but it is *exercised* only with the consent of the governed (20:65; D&C 26:2). (This is the presiding covenant's "persuasion, not compulsion" [121:41] structurally encoded: even rightful priesthood authority operates by common consent, not command.)
