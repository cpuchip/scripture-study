# D&C 46 — to every man is given a gift

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, March 8, 1831.** The young Church had no fixed pattern for its meetings, and a custom had grown of admitting only members to the sacrament and assemblies. This revelation corrects that (don't exclude the earnest seeker), then opens into the doctrine of **spiritual gifts** — how to seek them, what they are, and how the gifts (especially *discernment*) are the Church's protection against deception.

**Binding idea:** "To every man is given a gift by the Spirit of God. To some is given one, and to some is given another, **that all may be profited thereby**" (46:11-12). Everyone has a gift; the *variety* is the point — the gifts are distributed across the body so that each member needs and serves the others. And the gifts are sought "that ye may not be deceived" (46:8): the Spirit's gifts, rightly sought, are the immune system against the counterfeit.

**Read:** The elders conduct meetings "as they are directed and guided by the Holy Spirit" (46:2). "Never cast any one out from your public meetings" (46:3); don't cast out members (except trespassers don't partake "until he makes reconciliation," 46:4) nor non-members "who are earnestly seeking the kingdom" (46:5). "Ask of God, who giveth liberally… that ye may not be seduced by evil spirits, or doctrines of devils, or the commandments of men; for some are of men, and others of devils" (46:7). "Beware lest ye are deceived; and that ye may not be deceived seek ye earnestly the best gifts, always remembering for what they are given" (46:8) — "not for a sign that they may consume it upon their lusts" (46:9). "All have not every gift… for there are many gifts, and to every man is given a gift by the Spirit of God" (46:11). The enumeration: to "know that Jesus Christ is the Son of God" (46:13); to "believe on their words" (46:14); to "know the differences of administration" (46:15); to "know the diversities of operations, whether they be of God" (46:16); "the word of wisdom" (46:17); "the word of knowledge" (46:18); "faith to be healed" (46:19); "faith to heal" (46:20); "the working of miracles" (46:21); "to prophesy" (46:22); "the discerning of spirits" (46:23); "to speak with tongues" (46:24); "the interpretation of tongues" (46:25). "Unto the bishop… and… elders… are to have it given unto them to discern all those gifts lest there shall be any among you professing and yet be not of God" (46:27). "He that asketh in the Spirit asketh according to the will of God; wherefore it is done even as he asketh" (46:30).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "To every man is given a gift" (46:11-12) — universal gifting, distributed on purpose.* The headline doctrine: *no one is giftless*. "To every man is given a gift by the Spirit of God" — and "all have not every gift" (46:11), so the gifts are *spread out* across the body, "that all may be profited thereby" (46:12). The design is *interdependence*: God deliberately gives no one *all* the gifts (except, 46:29, the "head" — the prophet — that the body have a center), so that every member *needs* the others and every member has something to *give*. (This is 1 Corinthians 12's body-of-Christ as a *gift economy* — the same logic as the §42 law of consecration applied to *spiritual* goods: each receives a portion sufficient and distinct, and the portions serve the whole. The §38:27 "be one" extended to gifts: oneness is not uniformity but *complementarity* — a body whose members differ precisely so they can serve one another. And it dignifies every member — the one with only "faith to believe on their words" [46:14] is as truly gifted as the prophet; there is no giftless disciple.)
- *★ The gifts are protection against deception (46:7-8) — and the discernment gift is named (46:16,23,27).* The frame around the gifts is *anti-deception*: "that ye may not be seduced by evil spirits, or doctrines of devils, or the commandments of men… that ye may not be deceived seek ye earnestly the best gifts" (46:7-8). And three of the enumerated gifts are *discernment* gifts: "to know the differences of administration" (46:15), "to know the diversities of operations, **whether they be of God**" (46:16), and "the discerning of spirits" (46:23). The Lord names *three sources* of what claims to be revelation — God, men, and devils (46:7) — and the gift of discernment is the capacity to *tell them apart*. (This is the walk's discernment thread given its *positive* form: §28/§43 named the *structural* guard [the order — only the appointed channel]; §45:57 named the *eschatological* stakes [the wise are undeceived]; §46 names the *gift* — the Holy Ghost actually grants the capacity to discern "whether they be of God." The period-language/Spirit-distillation discipline is not only effort and order; it is a *gift to be sought*, "that ye may not be deceived." And it has a *leadership* form, 46:27: the bishop and elders are given to "discern all those gifts lest there shall be any among you professing and yet be not of God" — the watch [Abr 4:18] given the gift of seeing counterfeits.)
- *★ "He that asketh in the Spirit asketh according to the will of God" (46:30).* A profound resolution of the prayer problem: how do you ask "in faith, believing that you shall receive" (§42:14) without presuming on God? Answer: *ask in the Spirit*, and your ask is *aligned with His will* — "wherefore it is done even as he asketh." The Spirit doesn't just deliver answers; it *shapes the question* so that what you ask is what God wills. (This completes the §9 study-it-out epistemology: you study, you ask — but asking *in the Spirit* means the Spirit has already conformed your desire to God's, so the asking-and-receiving is not you bending God to your will but the Spirit bending your will to His. The §42:43-52 healing "if not appointed unto death" caveat resolved: asking-in-the-Spirit asks *for what God wills*, so the answer is sure because the request was Spirit-shaped. Prayer at its height is not getting God to want what you want; it's wanting what God wants, by the Spirit.)
- *Don't cast out the earnest seeker (46:3-6).* The Church is commanded *not* to exclude the sincere seeker — even non-members "earnestly seeking the kingdom" stay in the sacrament and confirmation meetings (46:5-6). Only the *trespasser* is asked to refrain from the emblems "until he makes reconciliation" (46:4 — and even he isn't cast out, just held from partaking). The boundary is drawn around *unrepented trespass*, not around *non-membership*. (Inclusion of the seeker is commanded — the opposite of a closed, defensive sect. The §44:6 care for the poor and the §46:3 welcome of the seeker are the same open posture: Zion gathers and welcomes, it doesn't wall people out. The only exclusion is self-imposed by unrepentance.)
- *The Spirit governs the meeting (46:2).* Before fixing any pattern of worship, the Lord establishes the *governing principle*: "conduct all meetings as they are directed and guided by the Holy Spirit." The form serves the Spirit, not the reverse. (This is the §43:8 council pattern again — the assembly is led by the Spirit in real time, not by rote. A meeting is a place for the Spirit to direct, which is why the gifts [given "to profit withal," 46:16] operate *in* the gathered body. The §44:2 "Spirit poured out when they assemble" finds its purpose here: the gifts manifest in the Spirit-led meeting.)
