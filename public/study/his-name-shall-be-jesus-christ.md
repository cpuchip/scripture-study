# His Name Shall Be Jesus Christ

> *"according to the words of the prophets, and also the word of the angel of God, his name shall be Jesus Christ, the Son of God."*
> — [2 Nephi 25:19](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/25?lang=eng&id=p19#p19)

**Catalyst:** A question Michael raised — the Book of Mormon names him "Jesus Christ" six hundred years before Bethlehem, yet the Old Testament never does. Christ is a title; isn't Jesus one too? What is going on with the name across time?

**Binding question:** Why does the Book of Mormon call him Jesus Christ in BC times when the Old Testament does not — and what does the name itself teach when you take it apart?

A companion to [christ-in-all-the-scriptures.md](christ-in-all-the-scriptures.md), which traces how every scripture testifies of him. This one stays on the name.

---

## I. The puzzle, stated plainly

Around 559 BC, Nephi writes a name onto metal plates: "his name shall be Jesus Christ, the Son of God" ([2 Nephi 25:19](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/25?lang=eng&id=p19#p19)). His brother Jacob had it first, and says where it came from: "Christ — for in the last night the angel spake unto me that this should be his name" ([2 Nephi 10:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/10?lang=eng&id=p3#p3)). Four centuries later King Benjamin hears it from an angel in even fuller form: "he shall be called Jesus Christ, the Son of God, the Father of heaven and earth, the Creator of all things from the beginning; and his mother shall be called Mary" ([Mosiah 3:8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/3?lang=eng&id=p8#p8)).

Then open Isaiah, the Psalms, Daniel. The Messiah is everywhere in those pages, but the words "Jesus Christ" are not. Two records, the same Lord, and the name printed on one and not the other. The honest question underneath Michael's is whether the Book of Mormon is being anachronistic — putting a Greek-and-English name into the mouth of a man who lived before Greek was the language of scripture. The answer turns out to be no, and the reason is worth the whole study.

## II. Two words, two languages, one person

Start by taking the name apart, because half the puzzle dissolves the moment you do.

**Christ is a title.** The English word comes from Greek *Christos*, "anointed" (Strong's G5547, from *chriō*, "to anoint"). Its exact Hebrew twin is *mashiach* — Messiah — also "anointed" (Strong's H4899). The Bible Dictionary states it flatly: "The *anointed* (Greek) or *Messiah* (Hebrew)" ([BD Christ](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bd/christ?lang=eng)), and the entry on the Anointed One adds that *Christ* "is the equivalent of *Messiah*, which is from a Hebrew and Aramaic term meaning 'anointed'" ([BD Anointed One](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bd/anointed-one?lang=eng)). To anoint with oil was to consecrate a prophet, priest, or king. So "Christ" names an office: the one consecrated to be Prophet, Priest, and King at once. "Jesus Christ" was never a first and last name. It reads "Jesus the Anointed."

**Jesus is a name — but a name that is a sentence.** Greek *Iēsous* (Strong's G2424) is simply the Greek spelling of a Hebrew name the lexicon names outright: "Jesus (i.e. Jehoshua) … of Hebrew origin." That Hebrew name is *Yehoshua* / *Yeshua* — the very name English elsewhere spells **Joshua**. And its construction is the heart of the matter. Strong's H3091 derives *Yehoshua* "from H3068 and H3467" — from **Jehovah** and the verb **yasha, to save** — and glosses it "Jehovah is salvation." The angel hands Joseph that meaning along with the name: "thou shalt call his name Jesus: for he shall save his people from their sins" ([Matthew 1:21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/matt/1?lang=eng&id=p21#p21)).

So Michael's instinct is right in the best way. Christ is a title. Jesus is a name, but not a neutral one. It is a theophoric name, a name with God folded inside it, and what it declares is the gospel in two syllables: *the LORD saves.* The Bible Dictionary glosses it "God is help" or "Savior" and notes it "was not an uncommon one among the Jews" ([BD Jesus](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bd/jesus?lang=eng)) — an ordinary Israelite name carrying an extraordinary claim, made literal in one Child.

## III. The Old Testament names him constantly — in Hebrew dress

Once you can read the Hebrew clothing, the claim that the Old Testament "doesn't name him" falls apart. It names him three ways.

It carries the **title**. "The kings of the earth set themselves … against the Lord, and against his anointed" ([Psalm 2:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/ps/2?lang=eng&id=p2#p2)). "Unto the Messiah the Prince" ([Daniel 9:25](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/dan/9?lang=eng&id=p25#p25)). That word — *anointed*, *Messiah* — is *mashiach*, which is *Christos*, which is Christ. The title is on nearly every prophetic page; the King James translators simply rendered it "anointed" or transliterated it "Messiah" instead of reaching for the Greek.

It carries the **name's root**. *Yehoshua* walks through the Old Testament as Joshua son of Nun and as Jeshua the high priest of the return. The New Testament proves they are one word: when the KJV's Greek source mentions Joshua, it calls him "Jesus" ([Acts 7:45](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/acts/7?lang=eng&id=p45#p45); [Hebrews 4:8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/heb/4?lang=eng&id=p8#p8)). Joshua and Jesus are the same name in two transliterations.

And it carries the **deepest form of all: Jehovah is Jesus.** The Bible Dictionary is unambiguous — "Jehovah is the premortal Jesus Christ and came to earth being born of Mary" ([BD Jehovah](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bd/jehovah?lang=eng)). The covenant name of the God of Israel, "the eternal I Am," is the name of the Being who would be born in Bethlehem. He told Abraham so: "My name is Jehovah, and I know the end from the beginning" ([Abraham 2:8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/abr/2?lang=eng&id=p8#p8)). He told Moses from the bush: "I Am That I Am … I Am hath sent me unto you" ([Exodus 3:14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/ex/3?lang=eng&id=p14#p14)). And standing in the temple courts, he claimed that exact name as his own: "Before Abraham was, I am" ([John 8:58](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/8?lang=eng&id=p58#p58)). To the Nephites he closed the loop: "I am he that gave the law, and I am he who covenanted with my people Israel" ([3 Nephi 15:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/15?lang=eng&id=p5#p5)).

Here is the quiet wonder in it. The name *Yeshua* is built from *Yah* — the short form of Jehovah — joined to "save." So every time the Old Testament says "the LORD" (printed in small capitals for YHWH), it is already, in hidden form, saying the first half of *Jesus*. The name was never absent from those pages. It was waiting under the most common word in them.

## IV. The name was revealed from the beginning — to Adam

Now the BC question can be answered directly, and the answer reaches back further than Nephi. The name "Jesus Christ" was not first disclosed in the meridian of time. It was given to Adam.

In the Book of Moses, the Lord teaches the first man the gospel and names the Savior in the plainest words the scriptures contain: turn, believe, repent, and "be baptized, even in water, in the name of mine Only Begotten Son … which is **Jesus Christ**, the only name which shall be given under heaven, whereby salvation shall come unto the children of men" ([Moses 6:52](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/6?lang=eng&id=p52#p52)). A few verses later he says it again, tying it to Adam's own tongue: "in the language of Adam, Man of Holiness is his name, and the name of his Only Begotten is the Son of Man, even **Jesus Christ**, a righteous Judge, who shall come in the meridian of time" ([Moses 6:57](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/6?lang=eng&id=p57#p57)).

That reframes everything. The name did not begin with Matthew; it ran like a buried river from Adam forward, surfacing wherever the Lord revealed it. The Nephite prophets are not inventing it — they are receiving it again from the same source Adam did, by the same means: an angel told Jacob ([2 Nephi 10:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/10?lang=eng&id=p3#p3)), an angel told Nephi ([2 Nephi 25:19](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/25?lang=eng&id=p19#p19)), an angel told Benjamin ([Mosiah 3:8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/3?lang=eng&id=p8#p8)). The Book of Mormon names him in BC times for the simplest possible reason: the ancients were told, just as the first family was told.

What differs is not knowledge but preservation. The Hebrew record that came down to us renders him by title (Messiah) and by covenant name (Jehovah, "the LORD"); the Nephite record, kept by prophets who saw further and lost less, preserved the angelic name-revelations intact.

## V. The translation seam

One mechanical piece remains: why the same Bible looks so different on either side of Matthew, and why Joseph Smith's translation reads "Jesus Christ" on plates engraved in a Semitic tongue.

It is a transliteration boundary, not a contradiction. The King James Old Testament is rendered from **Hebrew**, so the same person arrives in Hebrew dress — *Messiah*, *anointed*, *Joshua*, *the LORD*. The King James New Testament is rendered from **Greek**, so he arrives in Greek dress — *Christ*, *Jesus*. The Bible Dictionary on Messiah spells out the handoff: "In the New Testament the deliverer is called the Christ, which is the Greek equivalent of Messiah, and Jesus the Messiah is called Jesus the Christ, Jesus Christ, or Christ Jesus" ([BD Messiah](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bd/messiah?lang=eng)). Same Lord, different alphabet.

Nephi engraved the name in his own language — a Hebrew or reformed-Egyptian form, presumably the equivalent of *Yeshua ha-Mashiach*. Joseph Smith translated those plates in 1829 for a people who know the Lord by his English name, so it comes into our hands as "Jesus Christ," exactly the way *Yehoshua* comes into our Old Testament as "Joshua." The English on the page is a faithful translation of a revealed name, not a slip in the timeline.

## VI. The name is the gospel

Put the pieces back together and the name reads as a one-line creed. *Jesus* — Jehovah is salvation. *Christ* — the Anointed One. Joined: **the covenant God of the Old Testament, Jehovah the I Am, came to save, anointed for the work as Prophet, Priest, and King.** The Old Testament half of the name (Jehovah) and the New Testament half (saves, anointed) were always the same sentence; the Restoration scriptures simply let us read it whole.

And the name is not ornamental. It is the one name that does the work. Peter told the Sanhedrin there is "none other name under heaven given among men, whereby we must be saved" ([Acts 4:12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/acts/4?lang=eng&id=p12#p12)) — the very thing the Lord had told Adam, that *Jesus Christ* is "the only name … whereby salvation shall come" ([Moses 6:52](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/6?lang=eng&id=p52#p52)). Knowing the etymology is a delight. Knowing whose name it is, is salvation.

## VII. Becoming

The reason to learn the name is to bear it. Adam was taught not only the name but the practice that goes with it — to ask "all things in his name" ([Moses 6:52](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/6?lang=eng&id=p52#p52)). At baptism and at the sacrament table we covenant to take that name upon us. The Old Testament saints called him Jehovah and waited for him; the Nephites called him Jesus Christ and waited for him; we are given the name and the covenant both, and asked to live under it now.

To take upon ourselves the name that means "Jehovah is salvation" is to let him actually save us — and then to become the kind of people the name describes, whose lives quietly declare that the Lord saves. That is the whole arc of the name handed back as a calling. He carried a name that was a promise. We carry a name that is a witness.
