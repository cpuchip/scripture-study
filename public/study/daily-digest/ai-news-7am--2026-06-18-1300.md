# AI Daily — June 27, 2024

## Headlines

**Google ships Gemma 2 (9B and 27B)** — Google released Gemma 2, a second-generation family of open-weight models in 9B and 27B parameter sizes, claiming the 27B variant "offers competitive alternatives to models more than twice its size" and can run at full precision on a single H100 or TPU host [Google Blog](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/developers-tools/google-gemma-2/). The 9B model is positioned to outperform Llama 3 8B. A smaller 2.6B variant is teased for a future release. Worth watching: whether the benchmark claims hold up under independent evaluation — Google's own benchmarks are a known weak point for skepticism.

**Gemini 1.5 Pro opens 2M context window to all developers** — Google removed the waitlist for the 2 million token context window on Gemini 1.5 Pro and added native code execution to the Gemini API and AI Studio, targeting the model's historical weakness in math and data reasoning [Google AI](https://goo.gle/4c6ML9D). This is a meaningful accessibility shift — the 2M window was previously gated behind a whitelist.

**OpenAI partners with TIME** — OpenAI and TIME announced a multi-year content deal granting OpenAI access to TIME's 101-year archive for model training, with citations and links back to Time.com in ChatGPT responses [OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/strategic-content-partnership-with-time/). This is the first major legacy media archive deal of this kind. The framing ("setting new standards for veracity, for virtue and for value") is aspirational; the substance is a licensing arrangement that benefits both parties financially. Worth noting: this doesn't solve hallucination or sourcing problems more broadly — it just means TIME content is now in the training mix with attribution.

## Notable

- **Meta LLM Compiler** — Meta released an open-source suite of models (7B and 13B, built on Code Llama) trained on 546B tokens of LLVM-IR and assembly code, designed for compiler optimization tasks [Meta Research](https://ai.meta.com/research/publications/meta-large-language-model-compiler-foundation-models-of-compiler-optimization/). Claimed to reach 77% of autotuning search optimization potential. Niche but significant for the systems/compilers crowd.
- **OpenAI CriticGPT** — OpenAI researchers published a model trained to find bugs in LLM-generated code, finding errors in 24% of ChatGPT outputs that human annotators had previously rated as flawless [Ars Technica](https://arstechnica.com/information-technology/2024/06/openais-criticgpt-outperforms-humans-in-catching-ai-generated-code-bugs/). The model is most effective at pinpointing single-location errors; scattered bugs remain a challenge.

## Skeptical takes

No credible dissenting voices surfaced today on these specific releases. The usual caution applies: Google's self-reported benchmarks for Gemma 2 should be treated as claims until independently verified, and the TIME partnership's "veracity" framing is marketing language, not a technical guarantee.

## Carry-forward

- **Deep-research candidate:** The Gemma 2 vs. Llama 3 8B performance comparison is worth an independent benchmark survey — Google's claims are bold and the open-source community will be testing this aggressively.
- **Watch for:** Whether the TIME partnership becomes a template for other legacy media outlets licensing archives to AI companies. If this scales, it could reshape how journalism funds itself.
