**Headlines**

**Google ships Gemini 3.5 Flash as its new default model.** Google released Gemini 3.5 Flash, the first public entry in a new model family built for agentic workflows and coding, and made it the default in the Gemini app and Search AI Mode globally. The company describes the family as "a major leap forward in building more capable, intelligent agents" [Gemini 3.5: frontier intelligence with action](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/models-and-research/gemini-models/gemini-3-5/). GitHub also began rolling out 3.5 Flash as a selectable model for Copilot Pro, Business, and Enterprise users, with tentative pricing set at a 14X premium-request multiplier [Gemini 3.5 Flash is generally available for GitHub Copilot](https://github.blog/changelog/2026-05-19-gemini-3-5-flash-is-generally-available-for-github-copilot/). Google's benchmark claims (Terminal-Bench, GDPval-AA, MCP Atlas) are self-reported; independent verification is pending.

**Figma launches a native canvas-embedded design agent.** Figma shipped a design agent that reads design systems, components, and tokens to perform bulk edits, generate explorations, and synthesize feedback. The company says its goal was to create an agent "fluent in Figma and native to the way teams work," with deep context on internal components and standards that third-party tools cannot replicate [The Figma design agent is here](https://www.figma.com/blog/the-figma-agent-is-here/). It is entering a gradual beta that consumes no credits during the beta period.

**Stability AI releases Stable Audio 3.0 with open weights and licensed training data.** Stability AI launched a family of four audio-generation models, three of which ship as open weights on Hugging Face [Stability AI launches Stable Audio 3.0 with up to six-minute tracks and open weights](https://the-decoder.com/stability-ai-launches-stable-audio-3-0-with-up-to-six-minute-tracks-and-open-weights/). The models generate tracks up to 6:20 minutes long and were trained entirely on licensed data, with commercial use permitted for users under $1M revenue. A fourth model is reserved for API and enterprise customers.

**Notable**

- **Corti Symphony for Speech-to-Text:** Corti claims a 1.4% word-error rate in English medical terminology, versus 17–19% for generalist APIs, via a clinical-grade STT model now available in production [Corti launches Symphony for Speech-to-Text](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/corti-launches-symphony-for-speech-to-text-302776808.html). The comparison is specialized-vs-generalist from a vendor press release; independent auditing has not been cited.
- **Alibaba Zhenwu M890 AI chip:** Alibaba unveiled a 144GB AI chip for training and inference, a 128-chip server, and the Qwen3.7-Max LLM at its Cloud Summit [Alibaba Unveils In-House AI Chip 'Zhenwu M890'](https://www.ajupress.com/view/20260521121870797). Performance claims originate from summit presentations and are reported through a translated secondary source without independent verification.

**Skeptical takes**

No independent rebuttals have been published, but methodological caution is warranted: Google's benchmark numbers for 3.5 Flash are self-reported; Corti's dramatic error-rate reduction compares a specialized clinical model against general-purpose APIs rather than like-for-like competitors; and the Alibaba item relies on unverified summit claims via a translated secondary source.

**Carry-forward**

Watch for independent benchmarks on Gemini 3.5 Flash to test its agentic-task performance against Google's self-reported scores. Figma's beta is credit-free, so adoption metrics may be worth tracking. Nothing today warrants an immediate deep-research run.
