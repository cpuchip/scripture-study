### Headlines

**Anthropic ships Claude Opus 4.8 with parallel subagent orchestration.** Anthropic released [Claude Opus 4.8](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-opus-4-8) today with three practical additions. **Dynamic workflows** in Claude Code can now plan work and run hundreds of parallel subagents in a single session, verifying outputs before reporting back—aimed at tasks like codebase-scale migrations across hundreds of thousands of lines. A new **effort control** lets users dial inference depth up or down; fast mode runs at 2.5× speed and is priced three times cheaper than prior generations. Standard pricing is unchanged. Anthropic also reports the model is substantially more honest: on internal evaluations, Opus 4.8 was "[around four times less likely than its predecessor to allow flaws in code it has written to pass unremarked](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-opus-4-8)."

**Biohub releases an open protein world model with lab-validated binder designs.** Biohub announced an [open protein-biology engine](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/biohub-releases-a-world-model-of-protein-biology-302782681.html)—ESMC, ESMFold2, and ESM Atlas—that maps proteins across the tree of life and designs novel binders. In lab experiments, researchers used ESMFold2 to design binders against five cancer and immunology targets; the computational search finished in days rather than months or years, and the resulting molecules showed high affinity and specificity. Biohub's announcement also reports that ESMFold2 leads AlphaFold 3 on antibody-antigen prediction when both models use the same evolutionary data.

**X Square Robot open-sources Wall-OSS-0.5 for zero-shot manipulation.** X Square Robot [released Wall-OSS-0.5](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/x-square-robot-open-sources-wall-oss-0-5--bringing-pretrained-vla-performance-closer-to-post-training-levels-302784293.html), a vision-language-action model that demonstrates zero-shot robot behavior on physical hardware without task-specific fine-tuning. On a 17-task real-robot suite, the pretrained checkpoint scored above 80 on several tasks, including block sorting (100) and rope tightening (82). The release includes full model weights, training code, and a distributed optimizer implementation.
