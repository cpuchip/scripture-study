## AI Daily Digest — May 26, 2026

### Headlines

**SymJack hijacks AI coding agents via symlink trickery.** Adversa AI disclosed a single attack chain that tricks six major AI coding assistants—including Claude Code, Cursor, Copilot, Grok, Antigravity, and OpenAI Codex CLI—into overwriting their own configuration files and executing attacker code after the user approves what looks like a benign file copy. The technique uses symlink hijacking and disguised file copies in a booby-trapped repository. Because one exploit works across the category, the vulnerability appears architectural rather than six separate bugs. ([Adversa AI](https://adversa.ai/blog/the-approval-prompt-is-lying-to-you-symlink-rce-in-five-ai-coding-agents-claude-code-cursor-antigravity-copilot-grok-build/))

**Pointer.ai open-sources a computer-use agent that beats human baselines.** The company released a lightweight three-agent architecture that scored 83.6% on the OSWorld benchmark using Claude Opus 4.7 and 81.5% with Claude Sonnet 4.6, surpassing the 72.4% human baseline. The system uses a feasibility gate, planner, and executor, and the full implementation is open source. ([Pointer.ai](https://www.pointer.ai/blog/sota))

**Runway claims AI video has crossed the uncanny valley.** Runway released three fully AI-generated short films alongside internal user-study evidence arguing that solo creators can now produce cinematic short films in hours rather than weeks. The claim that the uncanny valley has been crossed rests on Runway's own screenings, not independent or peer-reviewed evaluation. ([Runway](https://runwayml.com/news/project-luxo))

### Notable

- **EAGLE 3.1 ships** — A joint release from the EAGLE, vLLM, and TorchSpec teams upgrades speculative decoding with fixes for attention drift, delivering roughly 2× throughput for single users and 1.66× at sixteen concurrent users in long-context workloads. ([vLLM](https://vllm.ai/blog/2026-05-26-eagle-3-1))
- **Claude Mythos reportedly solves Erdős planar unit distance problem** — Anthropic researchers claimed on social media that the unreleased Mythos frontier model independently produced a simple proof for the same 80-year-old problem OpenAI cracked on May 20. The claim has not yet received third-party mathematical validation equivalent to the OpenAI result, which was verified by Tim Gowers and others. ([The Decoder](https://the-decoder.com/claude-mythos-reportedly-solves-openais-landmark-erdos-problem-with-a-cute-simple-proof/))

### Skeptical takes

Runway's assertion that AI video has crossed the uncanny valley is backed by its own internal screenings, not outside review. The films are polished, but the evidentiary bar for declaring the valley crossed is higher than a vendor blog post. On the mathematical front, the Claude Mythos Erdős claim remains a researcher social-media thread, not a published result. The OpenAI breakthrough was externally validated; this one is still awaiting independent confirmation.

### Carry-forward

- **Deep-research candidate:** SymJack. If you use any of the affected AI coding agents, the mechanics of this attack deserve a closer look to understand whether your workflow is exposed.
- **Watch for:** Independent mathematical verification of the Claude Mythos Erdős proof, and vendor patch notes for the SymJack vulnerability.
