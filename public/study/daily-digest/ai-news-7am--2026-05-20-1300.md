**Headlines**

**Gemini 3.5 Flash.** Google claims its new model outperforms Gemini 3.1 Pro on coding and agentic benchmarks—Terminal-Bench 2.1 (76.2%), MCP Atlas (83.6%)—while running 4× faster than comparable frontier models. Independent verification was not available at scan time. [Gemini 3.5: frontier intelligence with action](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/models-and-research/gemini-3-5/)

**Gemini goes agentic.** Google launched Spark, a cloud-based 24/7 personal agent that keeps working in the background even when devices are locked; Omni, a text/image/video-to-video world model; and Daily Brief, a proactive morning digest. The app also got a "Neural Expressive" redesign. [The Gemini app becomes more agentic](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/products/gemini-app/next-evolution-gemini-app/)

**Skeptical takes**

All benchmark numbers for Gemini 3.5 Flash are self-reported by Google. Third-party tests had not appeared at scan time, so the "4× faster" and superiority claims over 3.1 Pro should be treated as marketing until independently verified. No releases from OpenAI, Anthropic, or Meta surfaced in the last 24 hours.

**Carry-forward**

Watch for independent benchmark runs on Gemini 3.5 Flash. The deeper pattern today is infrastructure: five vendors (Redis, LaunchDarkly, Confluent, IBM, Dell) shipped production layers for running agents, suggesting the industry is shifting from model competition to production plumbing. Deep-research candidate: whether Dell's on-prem TCO claims hold up against current cloud API pricing.
