**AI Daily Digest — June 3, 2026**

**Headlines**

Microsoft released seven in-house models under its MAI brand—including MAI-Thinking-1, MAI-Code-1-Flash, MAI-Image-2.5, MAI-Transcribe-1.5, and MAI-Voice-2—alongside a healthcare model built with Mayo Clinic. The company stressed that the models were trained without distillation from third-party labs and framed the release as part of a new "superintelligence lab" effort. [Building a hill-climbing machine: Launching seven new MAI models](https://microsoft.ai/news/building-a-hillclimbing-machine-launching-seven-new-mai-models/)

OpenAI pushed Codex into non-developer roles with plugins for corporate finance, strategy, and legal workflows, added in-place annotations for refining outputs without leaving the interface, and previewed "Sites," which lets enterprise users generate interactive web apps shareable by URL inside a workspace. The Sites preview is limited to business and enterprise tiers for now. [Codex for every role, tool, and workflow](https://openai.com/index/codex-for-every-role-tool-workflow/)

Anthropic scaled its Project Glasswing cybersecurity partnership to roughly 150 new organizations across 15-plus countries, extending access to its Claude Mythos Preview vulnerability-hunting model to critical infrastructure operators in power, water, healthcare, and communications. Each new partner must clear Anthropic's security requirements before gaining access. [Expanding Project Glasswing](https://www.anthropic.com/news/expanding-project-glasswing?s=)
