# What Was the Atonement For?

*A study of 3 Nephi 6:18, the doctrine that God refuses to compel, and what Christ's atonement actually purchased.*

> "Now they did not sin ignorantly, for they knew the will of God concerning them, for it had been taught unto them; therefore they did wilfully rebel against God."
> — [3 Nephi 6:18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/6?lang=eng&id=p18#p18)

---

**Binding question:** *"What was the atonement for?"* — and what does 3 Nephi 6:18 teach about the stakes of having been taught the will of God?

---

## I. The verse and its setting

3 Nephi 6:18 sits at the end of a chapter that began in peace. The Nephites had returned to their lands; the laws were administered "according to equity and justice" ([3 Nephi 6:4](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/6?lang=eng&id=p4#p4)). Then prosperity did the work prosperity always does. Within a few verses the people were "distinguished by ranks, according to their riches and their chances for learning" ([3 Nephi 6:12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/6?lang=eng&id=p12#p12)), the church was broken up "save it were among a few of the Lamanites who were converted" ([3 Nephi 6:14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/6?lang=eng&id=p14#p14)), and Mormon writes that "Satan had great power, unto the stirring up of the people to do all manner of iniquity" ([3 Nephi 6:15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/6?lang=eng&id=p15#p15)). *(Requoted 2026-06-12 — an earlier version quoted Helaman 16:23's "great hold upon the hearts" wording here.)*

Mormon does not say the people were deceived. He does not say they were ignorant of their state. He says the opposite.

> "Now they did not sin ignorantly, for they knew the will of God concerning them, for it had been taught unto them; therefore they did wilfully rebel against God."
> — [3 Nephi 6:18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/6?lang=eng&id=p18#p18)

Mormon also tells us what they had been taught. Two verses earlier he describes the prophets sent to them: they came "preaching and testifying boldly of the sins and iniquities of the people, and testifying unto them concerning the redemption which the Lord would make for his people, or in other words, the resurrection of Christ; and they did testify boldly of his death and sufferings" ([3 Nephi 6:20](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/6?lang=eng&id=p20#p20)). The content of the teaching was the atoning Christ. The people were taught the atonement, and Mormon's verb for what they did with it is *willfully rebel*.

The chapter closes with what the rebels were actually trying to do. The lawyers and chief judges entered into "a covenant one with another... administered by the devil, to combine against all righteousness" ([3 Nephi 6:28](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/6?lang=eng&id=p28#p28)), and the goal of the covenant was specific: "to destroy the governor, and to establish a king over the land, that the land should no more be at liberty but should be subject unto kings" ([3 Nephi 6:30](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/6?lang=eng&id=p30#p30)). The footnote on *liberty* points to the Topical Guide entry for Liberty. The narrator wants us to see what was actually at stake.

The next chapter shows what happened. The plotters did not crown a king. They overshot. "In this same year... they did murder the chief judge of the land. And the people were divided one against another; and they did separate one from another into tribes... and thus they did destroy the government of the land" ([3 Nephi 7:1-2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/7?lang=eng&id=p1-p2#p1)). The republic did not become a kingdom. It became fragments. Mormon's gloss: "all this was done... because they did yield themselves unto the power of Satan. And the regulations of the government were destroyed, because of the secret combination" ([3 Nephi 7:5-6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/7?lang=eng&id=p5-p6#p5)).

The arc, then, is six verses long. Peace, prosperity, pride, the teaching of Christ, willful rebellion against what was taught, the destruction of the governing structures that had held liberty. 3 Nephi 6:18 is the hinge. Without it, the collapse looks like ordinary politics. With it, the collapse is a verdict on a people who heard the truth and refused it on purpose.

## II. Taught therefore accountable

The footnote on *ignorantly* in 3 Nephi 6:18 points two places: to Numbers 15:27 and to Mosiah 3:11. Following the footnote reveals that Mormon's sentence is doing exact legal work. He is invoking a category of sin that the Mosaic law had built into Israel from the beginning, and that King Benjamin had explained again in the new world.

Numbers 15 sets up the contrast. Verses 22 through 29 cover sin "through ignorance" — a sin offering is brought, "and the priest shall make an atonement for the soul that sinneth ignorantly... and it shall be forgiven him" ([Numbers 15:28](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/num/15?lang=eng&id=p28#p28)). Then comes a different category entirely:

> "But the soul that doeth ought presumptuously, whether he be born in the land, or a stranger, the same reproacheth the Lord; and that soul shall be cut off from among his people. Because he hath despised the word of the Lord, and hath broken his commandment, that soul shall utterly be cut off; his iniquity shall be upon him."
> — [Numbers 15:30-31](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/num/15?lang=eng&id=p30-p31#p30)

The Hebrew underneath *presumptuously* is *be-yad ramah* — "with a high hand." Mosaic law contained a category of sin for which atonement was not available. The sin offering covered the unknowing. The high-handed sinner — the one who knew and did it anyway — bore his own iniquity. The Psalmist understood the stakes exactly. "Keep back thy servant also from presumptuous sins; let them not have dominion over me: then shall I be upright, and I shall be innocent from the great transgression" ([Psalm 19:13](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/ps/19?lang=eng&id=p13#p13)). David is praying not to enter the high-hand category. *The great transgression* is the one outside the sin offering's reach.

Webster 1828 holds the vocabulary chain together. His fifth sense of *presumptuous* leads with the word itself: "Willful; done with bold design, rash confidence or in violation of known duty; as a presumptuous sin." *(Requoted 2026-06-12 — the earlier ordering was the 1913 edition's; the genuine entry puts "willful" first and its own example is "a presumptuous sin.")* The KJV translators rendered the Hebrew high-hand idiom as *presumptuously*; Webster, working from the same English usage Joseph Smith inherited, defined *presumptuous* with the word *willful*; and Mormon translates 3 Nephi 6:18 as "they did wilfully rebel." Three vocabularies, one doctrine.

Now follow the second footnote, to Mosiah 3:11. King Benjamin is explaining the reach of the atonement, and he says:

> "For behold, and also his blood atoneth for the sins of those who have fallen by the transgression of Adam, who have died not knowing the will of God concerning them, or who have ignorantly sinned."
> — [Mosiah 3:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/3?lang=eng&id=p11#p11)

The construction is unmistakable. Benjamin: those whose blood Christ atones for include those who *died not knowing the will of God concerning them*. Mormon: these people *knew the will of God concerning them, for it had been taught unto them*. Same construction, opposite condition. Mosiah 3:11 is the mercy clause. 3 Nephi 6:18 is the verse where the mercy clause no longer applies.

This does not mean repentance is unavailable to the willful sinner; the New Testament makes clear that it is. What it does mean is that the *category* into which a person has placed himself has changed. The unknowing sinner is covered automatically by the atonement of Christ. The taught sinner must *receive* what was taught. The atonement is just as real, and just as offered, but its application is no longer passive. Abinadi names this dynamic precisely: "having gone according to their own carnal wills and desires; having never called upon the Lord while the arms of mercy were extended towards them; for the arms of mercy were extended towards them, and they would not; they being warned of their iniquities and yet they would not depart from them" ([Mosiah 16:12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/16?lang=eng&id=p12#p12)). The arms are still extended. The verb that fails is *call upon*.

A dispensational note is worth making here. The Lord's preface to the Doctrine and Covenants is a deliberate move to bring all people into the taught category: "For verily the voice of the Lord is unto all men, and there is none to escape; and there is no eye that shall not see, neither ear that shall not hear, neither heart that shall not be penetrated... And the voice of warning shall be unto all people, by the mouths of my disciples, whom I have chosen in these last days" ([D&C 1:2-4](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/1?lang=eng&id=p2-p4#p2)). The mercy clause of Mosiah 3:11 covers ignorance. But ignorance is not a stable condition in a world where the Lord has committed to penetrating every heart. He is, over time and at His pace, moving humanity into 3 Nephi 6:18's category, because the work of the dispensation is the giving of the choice.

And the giving of the choice is the work itself. The Lord told Moses the mission of His own labor in one verse: "For behold, this is my work and my glory — to bring to pass the immortality and eternal life of man" ([Moses 1:39](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/1?lang=eng&id=p39#p39)). Eternal life is His life, and His life is voluntary love all the way down. So D&C 1's voice of warning is the *mechanism*, and Moses 1:39 is the *why*. The dispensation-wide move into the taught category is not a side effect of the Father's work, picked up along the way as collateral damage of His preaching. It is what He is *doing*. The teaching that raises the stakes of refusal is the same teaching that opens the door to eternal life, because eternal life cannot be entered any other way than by knowing love freely given.

## III. The compulsion question

The reason God will not save a willful rebel against his will is the same reason God will not save anyone against his will. The principle predates the Fall.

> "Wherefore, because that Satan rebelled against me, and sought to destroy the agency of man, which I, the Lord God, had given him, and also, that I should give unto him mine own power; by the power of mine Only Begotten, I caused that he should be cast down."
> — [Moses 4:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/4?lang=eng&id=p3#p3)

The war in heaven was about compulsion. The plan was the Father's; the question He asked was "Whom shall I send?" ([Abraham 3:27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/abr/3?lang=eng&id=p27#p27)). Satan's response was not a competing plan but a rebellion against the Father's — his promise to "redeem all mankind, that one soul shall not be lost" ([Moses 4:1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/4?lang=eng&id=p1#p1)) was the kind of promise that could only be kept by removing the thing that made losing a soul possible. The Father kept His own plan, with its guaranteed losses, because losses were the cost of choice; and He chose the Son who said "Father, thy will be done, and the glory be thine forever" ([Moses 4:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/4?lang=eng&id=p2#p2)) to carry it out. Compulsion was, from the beginning, the devil's offer — not the Father's design, and not the Son's role to overturn.

That choice from before the world holds all the way through the Restoration. The most famous statement of it is the Lord's correction to a priesthood that had begun to think coercion was a tool of God's authority:

> "No power or influence can or ought to be maintained by virtue of the priesthood, only by persuasion, by long-suffering, by gentleness and meekness, and by love unfeigned; by kindness, and pure knowledge..."
> — [D&C 121:41-42](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/121?lang=eng&id=p41-p42#p41)

Even God's delegated authority cannot operate by compulsion. If priesthood power itself works only by persuasion, the no-compulsion principle is not a concession God makes to human stubbornness. It is a description of how God works.

Joseph F. Smith said it as plainly as anyone has:

> "[Our] obedience must be voluntary; it must not be forced, there must be no coercion. Men must not be constrained against their will to obey the will of God; they must obey it because they know it to be right, because they desire to do it, and because it is their pleasure to do it."
> — Joseph F. Smith, [*Teachings of Presidents of the Church: Joseph F. Smith*, ch. 30](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/teachings-joseph-f-smith/chapter-30?lang=eng)

The Church echoes the doctrine in its [*Celebrating the Founding of the United States and the U.S. Constitution*](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/celebrating-freedom-and-agency/01?lang=eng) materials when it says, "He wanted us to choose Him freely, to choose light, truth, and goodness **without compulsion**." The "without compulsion" is the load-bearing phrase, and it is older than the materials. It is older than the priesthood restoration. It is the position the Son took in the council in heaven, against the position the adversary took. To say that God refuses to compel is to name the structural fact of how heaven works.

This is what makes 3 Nephi 6:18 so grave. The Nephites had been taught. They were not deceived. They had heard the testimony of Christ's death and sufferings. Because God will not compel even those who know better, they were left to do what they had freely chosen, which turned out to be the destruction of the very government that held their liberty in place. The "without compulsion" doctrine does not protect a society from its own willful rebellion. It is the doctrine that *allows* the rebellion to run its course.

## IV. What the atonement was for

Lehi gave the load-bearing answer to the binding question. Speaking to his son Jacob, he laid out the meaning of the Fall and the redemption:

> "And the Messiah cometh in the fulness of time, that he may redeem the children of men from the fall. And because that they are redeemed from the fall they have become free forever, knowing good from evil; to act for themselves and not to be acted upon, save it be by the punishment of the law at the great and last day, according to the commandments which God hath given."
> — [2 Nephi 2:26](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/2?lang=eng&id=p26#p26)

The atonement, in Lehi's vocabulary, moves human beings from the category of *being acted upon* into the category of *acting for themselves*. Without the redemption from the Fall, agency would not function meaningfully — we would be subject to death, to the natural man, to the captivity of the devil, with no countervailing freedom-pole to choose against. The Mediator is what gives the choice its second option.

> "Wherefore, men are free according to the flesh; and all things are given them which are expedient unto man. And they are free to choose liberty and eternal life, through the great Mediator of all men, or to choose captivity and death, according to the captivity and power of the devil; for he seeketh that all men might be miserable like unto himself."
> — [2 Nephi 2:27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/2?lang=eng&id=p27#p27)

Liberty and eternal life are available *through* the Mediator. Captivity and death are available *through* the devil. Both are choices, and both are available, because the atonement has secured the conditions under which choosing is possible. This is the answer the binding question is reaching for, at least in part: the atonement is for *freedom* — not as a Western political abstraction, but as the metaphysical capacity to choose God or refuse Him with one's whole self, and have that choice be honored.

The honoring runs all the way through to the end. The Lord told Joseph Smith that even at the resurrection, the assignment to glory will be a function of willingness:

> "And they who remain shall also be quickened; nevertheless, they shall return again to their own place, to enjoy that which they are willing to receive, because they were not willing to enjoy that which they might have received."
> — [D&C 88:32](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88?lang=eng&id=p32#p32)

The verb that determines the kingdom is *willing*. The Lord does not say *deserving*, or *capable*, or *worked hardest*; He says *willing to receive*. The same anti-compulsion principle that fired the war in heaven runs all the way through to the kingdoms of glory. God will give us what we are *willing* to receive. He will not coerce a higher kingdom on a person whose will refused it.

It needs to be said clearly that the atonement was for more than this. Lehi himself names other things — redemption from the Fall, knowledge of good from evil, the resurrection, the answering of the ends of the law ([2 Nephi 2:4-10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/2?lang=eng&id=p4-p10#p4)). The Doctrine and Covenants frames the atonement as the redemption of the soul, the resurrection of the body, the sanctification that prepares the body for celestial glory ([D&C 88:14-18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88?lang=eng&id=p14-p18#p14)). Saying the atonement was for *agency* is true; it is also only one face of the work. The atonement was for overcoming death, for overcoming sin, for sanctifying flesh, for restoring the choice that the Fall had threatened to collapse. The choice-restoration is the face the binding question is asking about. But the gem has more than one face, and a study of one of them is not a denial of the others.

What this study claims, then, is narrower: the atonement secured the conditions under which agency could operate meaningfully, and 3 Nephi 6:18 is the verse where a people who had been taught the conditions and given the choice refused it. The atonement did not fail them. Their will did. And because God refuses to compel — because the entire economy of heaven, from the council before the world to the assignment of kingdoms at the judgment, runs on willingness — they were allowed to refuse, and the consequences of refusing were allowed to unfold.

## V. The harder mercy of being taught

There is a tension in this that should not be smoothed over. If being taught moves a person from the mercy-clause category of Mosiah 3:11 into the willful-rebellion category of 3 Nephi 6:18, why does God keep teaching? Why warn at all? The unwarned soul looks safer. Ignorance has Benjamin's mercy clause. Knowledge has Mormon's verdict.

The text gives a direct answer. *D&C 88:32 again.* The Lord will not let us settle for what we never chose. The kingdoms of glory are structured by *willing to receive*, not by *unable to refuse*. A soul who never knew is covered by the atonement automatically; that soul will inherit a glory. But a soul who *chose* God — who heard the doctrine of Christ and received it, who could have walked away and did not — that soul will inherit something greater. The whole point of being taught is being given the higher choice. The Father wants children who chose Him, not children who escaped the question by being unwarned.

This is a harder mercy than the unwarned one. It is also a fuller one. Joseph F. Smith's phrasing is what is actually wanted: "they must obey it because they know it to be right, because they desire to do it, and because it is their pleasure to do it." Compliance under threat is not it. Ignorance under shelter is not it. Knowing love, freely given by a soul who could have walked away and did not — that is what the atonement is for, in the end. To make the freely-given love possible.

3 Nephi 6:18 names the cost. When the freely-given love is offered and refused, the refusal is allowed to be real. The Lord does not pretend it did not happen. He does not undo the willing the way He would not undo the willing of Satan in the council. He lets the refusal stand, and He lets the consequences of refusal — the destruction of the governor, the collapse into tribes, the loss of liberty — stand with it. The atonement made the freedom possible. The freedom made the refusal possible. The refusal made the collapse possible. Each step honors the last.

The Nephite arc in 3 Nephi 6-7 is not, finally, a political story about how republics fail. It is a doctrinal story about what happens when a people who have been taught the atoning Christ refuse Him on purpose. The political collapse is the visible shape of the spiritual one. The governor was destroyed because the Governor was rejected. The land was no more at liberty because the people were no more at liberty — they had become "servants of sin, and... children of disobedience" ([D&C 121:17](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/121?lang=eng&id=p17#p17)), and a society made of those people cannot sustain a free government no matter what laws it writes.

The pattern is permanent. It does not belong to one continent or one century. Every dispensation has its 3 Nephi 6 moment, when a people have been sufficiently taught and the consequences of refusal become available to them. Whether ours is one of those moments is a question the Lord has not given any of us authority to date-stamp. What He has done is leave us the verse, and the chapter that surrounds it, and the footnote chain back to Numbers 15 and Mosiah 3, so that when we see the pattern we know what we are looking at.

## VI. Becoming

The pull of this study is outward — toward societies, prophets, political collapse, conference statistics. The text pulls back inward.

I have been taught. That is the first fact. I have read the death and sufferings of Christ in the words of Nephi and Mormon and Paul. I have testified of His resurrection in fast meetings and to my children. I know the will of God concerning me in the matters that He has actually given me clear instruction about — daily prayer, the sacrament, honesty, the way I treat my wife, the use of my time, the work I do under covenant. These are not gray areas. These are the verses that have been taught.

That puts me, in those matters, in the category of 3 Nephi 6:18. The Mosiah 3:11 shelter does not cover them. I will not be judged for whether I knew. I will be judged for what I did with the knowing.

This is not a reason to want to know less. It is a reason to want to *receive* what I have been taught — to move it from the "I know I should" column into the "this is what I do" column. The doctrine of 3 Nephi 6:18 says willful disobedience after teaching is the worst category. The doctrine of D&C 88:32 says willing reception after teaching is the highest one. They are the same axis, in opposite directions. The teaching is the gift that puts me on the axis. What I do with it determines which end I am moving toward.

The specific commitment, then, has to be at the level of *receiving*, not *learning*. I will pick one thing I have been taught this season that I am still treating as optional, and I will move it into the not-optional column this week. Not because I am afraid of the 3 Nephi 6:18 verdict. Because the Joseph F. Smith line is what I actually want to be true of me: *they obey it because they know it to be right, because they desire to do it, and because it is their pleasure to do it*. The atonement is for that. So am I.
