## The Word Study: -Hood vs. -Craft

The English language encodes the difference between priesthood and priestcraft right in the suffixes. Noah Webster's 1828 dictionary — the dictionary of the Restoration — makes the distinction unmistakable.

### -Hood: State of Being (Identity)

> **HOOD** — "State; condition." — Webster 1828

The suffix *-hood* denotes **who you are.** It describes identity, nature, a state of being:

| Word | Webster 1828 Definition |
|------|------------------------|
| **Priesthood** | "The office or character of a priest; the priestly function" |
| **Fatherhood** | "The state of being a father; the character or authority of a father" |
| **Brotherhood** | "The state of being brothers or a brother" |
| **Manhood** | "The state of being man as a human being" |
| **Godhood** | "Divine nature or essence; deity; godhead" |
| **Falsehood** | "Want of truth or accuracy" — the *state* of being false |
| **Neighborhood** | "The quality or condition of being a neighbor" |

Priesthood, then, is not something you *do*. It's something you *are*. A state of being. An identity received, not a skill acquired.

### -Craft: Skill, Trade, and Cunning

> **CRAFT** — (def. 2) "Art or skill; dexterity." (def. 4) "Cunning, art, or skill, in a bad sense, or applied to bad purposes; artifice; guile." — Webster 1828

Webster even quotes scripture for the dark connotation: *"Take him by craft"* — [Mark 14:1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/mark/14?lang=eng&id=p1#p1).

The suffix *-craft* denotes **what you do** — and often with connotations of manipulation:

| Word | Webster 1828 Definition |
|------|------------------------|
| **Priestcraft** | "Fraud or imposition in religious concerns; management by priests to gain wealth and power" |
| **Witchcraft** | "Practices or art of witches; sorcery" |
| **Kingcraft** | "The craft of kings; the art of governing as a sovereign" |
| **Statecraft** | "The art of conducting state affairs" |
| **Handicraft** | "A trade requiring skill of hand" |

### The Pattern

**-Hood = who you ARE** (identity, nature, state of being).
**-Craft = what you DO** (skill, trade — and often with guile).

Priesthood is the *sacred state of office*. Priestcraft is the *cunning manipulation of that office for gain*.

Note what's absent from Webster 1828: **mothercraft** and **fathercraft** don't exist in the Restoration-era dictionary. The language itself resists reducing parenting to a technique or trade. Motherhood and fatherhood are states of being — identities — not crafts to be performed. There is something quietly profound in that silence.

## The Scriptural Definition: 2 Nephi 26:29

Nephi gives us the clearest definition of priestcraft in all of scripture:

> **29.** He commandeth that there shall be no priestcrafts; for, behold, priestcrafts are that men preach and set themselves up for a light unto the world, that they may get gain and praise of the world; but they seek not the welfare of Zion.

— [2 Nephi 26:29](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/26?lang=eng&id=p29#p29)

This is a three-element test. All three are present:

1. **Preach and set themselves up for a light** — they position *themselves* as the source of truth, rather than pointing to God
2. **Get gain and praise of the world** — the motive is money, status, or social currency
3. **Seek not the welfare of Zion** — the sheep are not the point; the shepherd is

But look what Nephi does *before* defining priestcraft. In verses 24-28, he describes what God does:

> **24.** He doeth not anything save it be for the benefit of the world; for he loveth the world, even that he layeth down his own life. ...
>
> **25.** ... he commandeth none that they shall not partake of his salvation. ...
>
> **27.** ... have I commanded any that they should depart out of the synagogues, or out of the houses of worship? ...
>
> **28.** ... hath he commanded any that they should not partake of his salvation? Behold I say unto you, Nay; but he hath given it free for all men. ... Come unto me all ye ends of the earth, buy milk and honey, without money and without price.

— [2 Nephi 26:24-28](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/26?lang=eng&id=p24-p28#p24)

The structure is devastating: God gives everything **freely, for the benefit of the world**. Then Nephi immediately defines priestcraft as doing the exact opposite — preaching for **gain and praise, not the welfare of Zion.**

Priestcraft is the precise inversion of God's nature.

### The Antidote (vv. 30-31)

Nephi doesn't just define the disease. He prescribes the cure:

> **30.** Behold, the Lord hath forbidden this thing; wherefore, the Lord God hath given a commandment that all men should have charity, which charity is love. And except they should have charity they were nothing. ...
>
> **31.** But the laborer in Zion shall labor for Zion; for if they labor for money they shall perish.

— [2 Nephi 26:30-31](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/26?lang=eng&id=p30-p31#p30)

The antidote to priestcraft is **charity**. Not just avoiding getting paid — charity. You can labor without money and still commit priestcraft if you're doing it for praise. The heart of the issue is the heart.

## The Case Study: Nehor (Alma 1)

If 2 Nephi 26 is the *definition*, Alma 1 is the *case study*. Nehor is the first practitioner of priestcraft among the Nephites, and Alma names him as such:

> **3.** And he had gone about among the people, preaching to them that which he termed to be the word of God... declaring unto the people that every priest and teacher ought to become popular; and they ought not to labor with their hands, but that they ought to be supported by the people.
>
> **4.** And he also testified unto the people that all mankind should be saved at the last day, and that they need not fear nor tremble... for the Lord had created all men, and had also redeemed all men; and, in the end, all men should have eternal life.

— [Alma 1:3-4](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/1?lang=eng&id=p3-p4#p3)

Nehor's doctrine has three pillars:
1. **Popularity** — priests should be popular (set themselves up for a light)
2. **Support without labor** — no need to work with their hands (get gain)
3. **Universal salvation without repentance** — a theology designed to please, not to save (seek not the welfare of Zion)

Alma's judgment is sharp:

> **12.** ...this is the first time that priestcraft has been introduced among this people. And behold, thou art not only guilty of priestcraft, but hast endeavored to enforce it by the sword; and were priestcraft to be enforced among this people it would prove their entire destruction.

— [Alma 1:12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/1?lang=eng&id=p12#p12)

"their entire destruction." Not a slap on the wrist. Priestcraft — if enforced — destroys peoples. And the history of the Book of Mormon bears this out.

### The True Priest Contrast

Mormon, narrating Alma 1, gives us the anti-Nehor:

> **20.** ...the priests... did impart the word of God, one with another, without money and without price.
>
> **26.** ...the priest, not esteeming himself above his hearers, for the preacher was no better than the hearer, neither was the teacher any better than the learner; and thus they were all equal, and they did all labor, every man according to his strength.

— [Alma 1:20, 26](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/1?lang=eng&id=p20#p20)

The true priest: shares freely, considers himself equal, labors with his own hands.

## The Mirror: D&C 121 — Priesthood and Priestcraft as Opposites

The footnote chain from 2 Nephi 26:29d leads directly to [D&C 121:35](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/121?lang=eng&id=p35#p35), and this connection is the study's center of gravity. The same passage that defines how priesthood **works** also describes exactly when it becomes priestcraft:

### When Priesthood Becomes Priestcraft (vv. 34-39)

> **34.** Behold, there are many called, but few are chosen. And why are they not chosen?
>
> **35.** Because their hearts are set so much upon the things of this world, and aspire to the honors of men, that they do not learn this one lesson—
>
> **36.** That the rights of the priesthood are inseparably connected with the powers of heaven, and that the powers of heaven cannot be controlled nor handled only upon the principles of righteousness.
>
> **37.** That they may be conferred upon us, it is true; but when we undertake to cover our sins, or to gratify our pride, our vain ambition, or to exercise control or dominion or compulsion upon the souls of the children of men, in any degree of unrighteousness, behold, the heavens withdraw themselves; the Spirit of the Lord is grieved; and when it is withdrawn, Amen to the priesthood or the authority of that man.
>
> **39.** We have learned by sad experience that it is the nature and disposition of almost all men, as soon as they get a little authority, as they suppose, they will immediately begin to exercise unrighteous dominion.

— [D&C 121:34-39](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/121?lang=eng&id=p34-p39#p34)

Compare the language:

| 2 Nephi 26:29 (Priestcraft) | D&C 121:35-37 (Lost Priesthood) |
|-----|-----|
| "get gain and **praise of the world**" | "aspire to the **honors of men**" |
| "set themselves up for a **light**" | "**gratify** our pride, our vain ambition" |
| "seek not the **welfare of Zion**" | "exercise **control or dominion or compulsion**" |

These aren't similar ideas. They're the *same sin* described from two angles: one from the Book of Mormon, one from Liberty Jail. Priestcraft is simply what it looks like from the outside when someone has lost their priesthood power on the inside.

### What True Priesthood Looks Like (vv. 41-46)

> **41.** No power or influence can or ought to be maintained by virtue of the priesthood, only by persuasion, by long-suffering, by gentleness and meekness, and by love unfeigned;
>
> **42.** By kindness, and pure knowledge, which shall greatly enlarge the soul without hypocrisy, and without guile—
>
> **46.** ...thy scepter an unchanging scepter of righteousness and truth; and thy dominion shall be an everlasting dominion, and without compulsory means it shall flow unto thee forever and ever.

— [D&C 121:41-42, 46](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/121?lang=eng&id=p41-p42#p41)

"Without guile." Remember that *craft* in Webster 1828 means "artifice; guile." Priesthood operates **without** craft. The very suffix is the inverse.

And "without compulsory means it shall flow unto thee" — real influence doesn't need marketing. It flows. People come because the Spirit draws them, not because the preacher positioned himself as the light.

## The Old Testament Roots: Ezekiel's Wolves and Shepherds

Two passages from Ezekiel are footnoted from 2 Nephi 26:29, and they describe the ancient pattern of priestcraft among Israel's leaders:

### Ezekiel 22:25-28 — Priests Who Violate the Law

> **25.** There is a conspiracy of her prophets in the midst thereof, like a roaring lion ravening the prey; they have devoured souls; they have taken the treasure and precious things...
>
> **26.** Her priests have violated my law, and have profaned mine holy things: they have put no difference between the holy and profane...
>
> **27.** Her princes in the midst thereof are like wolves ravening the prey, to shed blood, and to destroy souls, to get dishonest gain.
>
> **28.** And her prophets have daubed them with untempered mortar, seeing vanity, and divining lies unto them, saying, Thus saith the Lord God, when the Lord hath not spoken.

— [Ezekiel 22:25-28](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/ezek/22?lang=eng&id=p25-p28#p25)

"To get dishonest gain" — the same language as 2 Nephi 26:29. And notice the prophets' role: they **daub with untempered mortar** — a gloss of respectability over rotten structures. They tell people what they want to hear and claim the Lord said it.

### Ezekiel 34:2-10 — Shepherds Who Feed Themselves

> **2.** ...Woe be to the shepherds of Israel that do feed themselves! should not the shepherds feed the flocks?
>
> **3.** Ye eat the fat, and ye clothe you with the wool, ye kill them that are fed: but ye feed not the flock.
>
> **4.** The diseased have ye not strengthened, neither have ye healed that which was sick, neither have ye bound up that which was broken, neither have ye brought again that which was driven away, neither have ye sought that which was lost; but with force and with cruelty have ye ruled them.
>
> **10.** ...I will require my flock at their hand, and cause them to cease from feeding the flock; neither shall the shepherds feed themselves any more...

— [Ezekiel 34:2-4, 10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/ezek/34?lang=eng&id=p2-p4#p2)

This is the priestcraft pattern laid bare: **shepherds who feed themselves instead of the flock.** The sheep are a resource, not a responsibility.

After the indictment, what does God say? "I, even I, will both search my sheep, and seek them out" (v. 11). When the paid shepherds fail, [the Good Shepherd](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/10?lang=eng) comes himself.

## The New Testament Pattern: Simon Magus (Acts 8)

The footnote from 2 Nephi 26:29a leads to the New Testament's defining example of priestcraft — Simon the Sorcerer:

> **9.** But there was a certain man, called Simon, which beforetime in the same city used sorcery, and bewitched the people of Samaria, giving out that himself was some great one:
>
> **10.** To whom they all gave heed, from the least to the greatest, saying, This man is the great power of God.

— [Acts 8:9-10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/acts/8?lang=eng&id=p9-p10#p9)

Simon "set himself up for a light" and the people "gave heed" to him. Classic priestcraft. But then Philip came preaching Christ, and even Simon himself believed and was baptized.

Here's where it turns:

> **18.** And when Simon saw that through laying on of the apostles' hands the Holy Ghost was given, he offered them money,
>
> **19.** Saying, Give me also this power, that on whomsoever I lay hands, he may receive the Holy Ghost.
>
> **20.** But Peter said unto him, Thy money perish with thee, because thou hast thought that the gift of God may be purchased with money.
>
> **23.** For I perceive that thou art in the gall of bitterness, and in the bond of iniquity.

— [Acts 8:18-20, 23](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/acts/8?lang=eng&id=p18-p20#p18)

"The gift of God" — that's the key phrase. Priesthood power is a **gift**. It cannot be bought, sold, marketed, or traded. The moment you try to purchase it — or to monetize it — you've shifted from *-hood* to *-craft*. From gift to transaction.

The church fathers coined a word for this sin: **simony** — named after Simon. The buying and selling of sacred things. It became the dominant corruption of medieval Christianity.

> **33.** O ye wicked and perverse and stiffnecked people, why have ye built up churches unto yourselves to get gain? Why have ye transfigured the holy word of God, that ye might bring damnation upon your souls?

>
> **38.** O ye pollutions, ye hypocrites, ye teachers, who sell yourselves for that which will canker, why have ye polluted the holy church of God? ... because of the praise of the world?

## The Alma Model: What True Service Looks Like

If priestcraft is the disease, what's the healthy pattern?

### Mosiah 18:24-26 — Alma's Priests

> **24.** And he also commanded them that the priests whom he had ordained should labor with their own hands for their support.
>
> **26.** And the priests were not to depend upon the people for their support; but for their labor they were to receive the grace of God, that they might wax strong in the Spirit, having the knowledge of God, that they might teach with power and authority from God.

— [Mosiah 18:24, 26](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/18?lang=eng&id=p24#p24)

Notice the exchange: they didn't receive money from the people. They received **the grace of God**. Their compensation was spiritual power — "that they might teach with power and authority from God." The payment for free service is the ability to serve more effectively.

### Elder McConkie's Summary

> "In the true church we neither preach for hire nor divine for money. We follow the pattern of Paul and make the gospel of Christ without charge, lest we abuse or misuse the power the Lord has given us. Freely we have received and freely we give, for salvation is free."

— Elder Bruce R. McConkie, ["Obedience, Consecration, and Sacrifice"](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1975/04/obedience-consecration-and-sacrifice?lang=eng), April 1975

And then he adds the Nephi verse as the underlying law:

> "All our service in God's kingdom is predicated on his eternal law which states: 'The laborer in Zion shall labor for Zion, for if they labor for money they shall perish.' ([2 Ne. 26:31](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/26?lang=eng).)"

## When Does Something Become Priestcraft?

This is the hardest question and the most honest one. Based on everything the scriptures lay out, here's the three-element test from 2 Nephi 26:29, rendered as diagnostic questions:

### 1. Who is the light?

Are you pointing people to Christ, or positioning yourself as the source of insight? Do people come away thinking about the Savior, or about how brilliant you are?

*"Set themselves up for a light unto the world"*

### 2. What is the motive?

Is this driven by love for the people, or by the dopamine of views, likes, subscribers, and revenue? Would you still do this if no one paid attention?

*"That they may get gain and praise of the world"*

### 3. Who benefits?

Does this serve the welfare of Zion — meaning, does it build the kingdom and bless the people? Or does it primarily build your brand, your audience, your revenue stream?

*"But they seek not the welfare of Zion"*

### The D&C 121 Diagnostic

D&C 121 adds depth to the test:

- **Hearts set on things of this world** — Is the content shaped by what the algorithm rewards, or by what the Spirit directs?
- **Aspire to the honors of men** — Am I seeking a following, or seeking to serve?
- **Cover our sins / gratify our pride** — Does this make me look good at the expense of honesty?
- **Exercise control or dominion** — Am I creating dependence (you *need* me to understand the scriptures) rather than empowerment?

### The Profit Question

McConkie acknowledged the nuance: "We know full well that the laborer is worthy of his hire, and that those who devote all their time to the building up of the kingdom must be provided with food, clothing, shelter, and the necessaries of life."

The line isn't "anyone who receives money for gospel-adjacent work is committing priestcraft." The line is **motive and direction.** CES teachers are paid. Seminary teachers are paid. Authors of gospel books receive royalties. Temple architects charge fees.

The question isn't whether money changes hands. The question is: **which direction is the service flowing?** Is the money enabling the service to the flock? Or is the flock being used to generate the money?

Ezekiel 34 gives the clearest picture: the shepherds who eat the fat and clothe themselves with the wool and *do not feed the flock*. The flock exists to serve *them*.

## Synthesis: The Counterfeit Pattern

Satan doesn't invent. He counterfeits. Every element of priestcraft has its genuine counterpart in priesthood:

| Priesthood (-hood: who you are) | Priestcraft (-craft: what you do) |
|------|------|
| Identity received from God | Persona crafted for an audience |
| Authority flows from righteousness | Influence bought through marketing |
| "Without compulsory means it shall flow" | SEO, monetization, growth hacking |
| Power through persuasion, long-suffering, love | Power through charisma, controversy, clicks |
| "Without hypocrisy, and without guile" | Craft = "artifice; guile" |
| Laborer receives grace of God | Laborer receives Patreon income |
| Teacher "not esteeming himself above his hearers" | Influencer cultivating a following |
| Freely give, "without money and without price" | "For your money you shall be forgiven" |
| Service empowers independence | Priestcraft creates dependence |

The pattern is consistent across every dispensation, from Ezekiel's wolves to Nehor to Simon Magus to Moroni's vision of our day. The faces change. The counterfeit doesn't.
