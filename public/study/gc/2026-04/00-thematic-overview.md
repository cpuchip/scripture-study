# April 2026 General Conference — Thematic Overview

**Binding Question:** What are the major themes running through the April 2026 General Conference, and how do the talks connect to each other?

This was a solemn assembly conference — the first under President Oaks — and it fell on Easter Sunday. Those two facts shape everything. The solemn assembly grounds the conference in covenant, priesthood authority, and the organized Church. Easter grounds it in Resurrection, hope, and the living Christ. Between those two anchors, 37 talks unfold a conference that keeps circling back to one question: what does it mean to abide in Christ — not as a slogan, but as a way of living?

The theological backbone is [John 14–15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/14?lang=eng). At least three major talks draw directly from these chapters — Soares on the vine, Gong on the Comforters and Emmaus, Eyring on the peace Christ promises. This was the Last Supper discourse. In an Easter conference, the speakers returned to what Christ said the night before His death: *abide in me.*

---

## The Overarching Pattern

Before the individual clusters, a structural observation: this conference moves from **covenant authority** (solemn assembly, Saturday morning) through **transformation** (charity, character, conversion — Saturday and Sunday) to **eternal belonging** (families, sealing, no one walks alone — Sunday afternoon closing). The arc mirrors the covenant path itself: enter → become → endure → belong forever.

Three talks form the conference's doctrinal spine and should be read in sequence:

1. [Elder Bednar, "All Who Have Endured Valiantly"](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/16bednar?lang=eng) — redefines "endure to the end" as *being possessed of charity*
2. [President Christofferson, "The Character of Christ"](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/51christofferson?lang=eng) — anatomizes Christ's motivations (love, lifting, will of God) and names the "mighty change of heart" as prerequisite
3. [President Oaks, "Alive in Christ"](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/49oaks?lang=eng) — applies it: love your enemies, be peacemakers, work one by one

Christofferson even cites Bednar's 2003 BYU-Idaho devotional by the same title, "The Character of Christ." These are companion pieces — one redefines the destination, the other maps the interior landscape of how we get there.

## Theme Clusters

### 1. Charity as Transformation — "The End We're Enduring Toward"

| Talk | Speaker | Key Contribution |
|------|---------|------------------|
| [All Who Have Endured Valiantly](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/16bednar?lang=eng) | Bednar | Charity = the "end" in enduring. Not grit — becoming. |
| [The Character of Christ](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/51christofferson?lang=eng) | Christofferson | Three motivations: pure love, lifting others, devotion to God's will |
| [Alive in Christ](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/49oaks?lang=eng) | Oaks | Love your enemies. Nurse holding a dying man she despised. |
| [Ministering](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/14yee?lang=eng) | Yee | Charity in practice — covenant-based service |
| [Closing Remarks](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/59oaks?lang=eng) | Oaks | Christlike ministry is serving one by one |

Bednar's insight is the anchor: the word *endure* appears in the scriptural *definition* of charity ("charity endureth all things," "endureth forever"). He substitutes "is possessed of the pure love of Christ" into three "endure to the end" scriptures and the result reads true. Enduring to the end *is* being possessed of charity. Not pushing through; being transformed.

Christofferson complements this with the internal mechanics. Character comes from motivations, not just behavior. "Christlike character grows out of a Christlike heart." The mighty change of heart is what produces the attributes, not the other way around.

Oaks makes it concrete. A nurse feels "an almost electric current of love from our Heavenly Father" for the most despised patient in her ward. He dies in her arms saying, "I want to go home. I just want to go home." This is what "possessed of charity" looks like in a hospital room at midnight.

The thread running through: charity is not an action we perform but a nature that possesses us. Bednar gives the doctrine, Christofferson gives the anatomy, Oaks gives the lived example.

### 2. Abiding in Christ — The True Vine and the Emmaus Pattern

| Talk | Speaker | Key Contribution |
|------|---------|------------------|
| [Jesus Christ—the True Vine](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/210soares?lang=eng) | Soares | Discernment — true vine vs. false vines in a noisy world |
| ['Tis Eastertide: No One Walks Alone](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/58gong?lang=eng) | Gong | Emmaus Road as covenant pattern — scripture + sacrament + walking |
| [Jesus Christ Is Not Our Burden](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/26wunderli?lang=eng) | Wunderli | Daily abiding — Christ as relief, not weight |
| [I Feel My Savior's Love](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/43larreal?lang=eng) | Larreal | The sacrament as weekly encounter with Christ's love |

Soares and Gong both draw from [John 14–15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/14?lang=eng) but from entirely different angles. Soares takes the vine metaphor and turns it into a discernment teaching: "The word *true* suggests that there are, in the world, other vines that present themselves as legitimate, even appearing to represent the loving voice and word of the Savior ... while subtly and deviously leading minds and hearts elsewhere." His central warning: "Abiding in Christ is not an occasional or casual act; it is a constant, conscious, and sacred choice."

Gong takes the same chapters and builds a covenant pattern from the Emmaus Road. Christ walks with us before we recognize Him. Scripture causes hearts to burn. Breaking bread opens eyes. The sacrament *is* the weekly Emmaus encounter. His invitation lands personally: "If you see someone walking alone and sad, will you please walk with them?"

Together they answer two questions: *what* are we connecting to (the True Vine, not the counterfeits — Soares), and *how* do we stay connected (scripture + sacrament + walking together — Gong).

### 3. Coming Home — Conversion and Return

| Talk | Speaker | Key Contribution |
|------|---------|------------------|
| [Come Home](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/15gilbert?lang=eng) | Gilbert | Invitation to return — repentance, families |
| [I Will Give Away All My Sins](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/25wu?lang=eng) | Wu | Total surrender — Lamoni's father's pattern |
| [Best Days and Worst Days](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/42freeman?lang=eng) | Freeman | Peter's arc from faith to denial to restoration |
| [Lost Luggage, Redeemed Souls](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/23stevenson?lang=eng) | Stevenson | Being found — redemption through ministering |

Four conversion arcs. Gilbert issues the direct invitation: come home. Wu models the cost: "I will give away all my sins to know thee" ([Alma 22:18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/22?lang=eng&id=p18#p18)). Freeman tells Peter's story — worst days serve eternal purposes — so failure isn't final. Stevenson adds the ministering angle: sometimes we're the ones who go find the lost.

These talks answer Bednar's framework from a different direction. If charity is the "end" we're enduring toward, these talks describe the *beginning* — the turning, the returning, the deciding to come home.

### 4. Guidance and Spiritual Safety

| Talk | Speaker | Key Contribution |
|------|---------|------------------|
| [Follow the Prophet](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/17teh?lang=eng) | Teh | Prophetic guidance as spiritual safety |
| [Choose Jesus Christ as Your Guide](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/44rowe?lang=eng) | Rowe | Deminer metaphor — following His footsteps through danger |
| [Jesus Christ Is the Way](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/28holmes?lang=eng) | Holmes | Jungfrau Marathon — mountains are dangerous without guides |
| [Prayers for Peace](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/19eyring?lang=eng) | Eyring | Continual prayer produces the spirit of prophecy and revelation |

This cluster is about navigation in dangerous terrain. Teh, Rowe, and Holmes all use guide metaphors (prophet, deminer, marathon guide). But Eyring provides the mechanism: continual prayer is what produces spiritual discernment. The sons of Mosiah "had given themselves to much prayer, and fasting; therefore they had the spirit of prophecy, and the spirit of revelation" ([Alma 17:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/17?lang=eng&id=p3#p3)). Their strength came from *continued* prayer, not crisis prayer.

Soares' vine/discernment talk (from Cluster 2) naturally overlaps here. The guidance cluster provides the *practical mechanisms* — follow prophets, pray constantly, follow Christ's footsteps — for what Soares describes as the *spiritual necessity*: distinguishing the True Vine from the counterfeits.

Eyring's personal testimony grounds the whole cluster: at his wife's funeral, "I was surprised at the feeling of peace and almost joy. The people at the funeral must have wondered why I was smiling." A lifetime of continual prayer produced a Spirit-given peace that death itself couldn't shake.

### 5. Resurrection and Easter Hope

| Talk | Speaker | Key Contribution |
|------|---------|------------------|
| [Encounter at the Empty Tomb](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/41uchtdorf?lang=eng) | Uchtdorf | Resurrection reality — women as first witnesses |
| [He Is Risen](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/45rasband?lang=eng) | Rasband | Easter joy — weeping endures but joy comes |
| [Keys, Covenants, and Easter](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/56cook?lang=eng) | Cook | Easter 2026 = Kirtland Temple anniversary. Resurrection makes sealing possible. |
| [Because of Jesus Christ](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/46renlund?lang=eng) | Renlund | Atonement as the simplifying answer to overwhelm |
| [The Joy of a Covenant Relationship](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/47mutombo?lang=eng) | Mutombo | Joy persists through devastating loss — because of covenants |

This being an Easter conference, the Resurrection pervades nearly everything. But these talks make it their primary subject. Cook's observation is the doctrinal linchpin: Easter 2026 falls on the 190th anniversary of Christ's appearance in the Kirtland Temple, where Elijah restored the sealing keys. Resurrection and sealing are not separate doctrines — the first makes the second possible. Without a risen Christ, there are no eternal families.

Oaks' "Alive in Christ" (from Cluster 1) also belongs here for its opening section on the Resurrection: "The conviction that death is not the conclusion of our identity changes the whole perspective of our mortal life."

### 6. Eternal Families and Sealing

| Talk | Speaker | Key Contribution |
|------|---------|------------------|
| [Keys, Covenants, and Easter](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/56cook?lang=eng) | Cook | Elijah, sealing keys, "without these keys, there are no eternal families" |
| [Eternal Marriage Is an Eternal Journey](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/55andersen?lang=eng) | Andersen | Marriage as character refinement, not just destination |
| [Come unto Christ—Together](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/57wakolo?lang=eng) | Wakolo | Part-member families — patience, divine timing, agency |

Cook establishes the priesthood keys. Andersen describes what happens *within* the sealed relationship: marriage refines character, requiring sacrifice, charity, patience. Wakolo addresses the hardest case: families where not everyone is on the covenant path. His four stories testify that "God honors patient faith, respects agency, and fulfills promises in His timing."

The connection to Cluster 1 is direct: if charity is the nature we're developing (Bednar), marriage is one of the primary crucibles where that development happens (Andersen).

### 7. God Knows You Personally

| Talk | Speaker | Key Contribution |
|------|---------|------------------|
| [Love All; Love Each](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/27causse?lang=eng) | Caussé | God's love is simultaneously universal AND individual |
| [He Knows You by Name](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/29matswagothata?lang=eng) | Matswagothata | A child's prayer reminds a stake president that God sees the invisible |
| [I Glory in My Jesus](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/53hall?lang=eng) | Hall | 60+ names for Christ in 1–2 Nephi — each a facet of relationship |
| ["Here Am I, Send Me"](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/54porter?lang=eng) | Porter | Children have divine purpose — five loaves and two fishes |

Caussé's framing — "Love All; Love Each" — captures the paradox of divine love. It's not either universal or personal; it's both simultaneously. Matswagothata gives the lived experience: a stake president in a dark season, comforted by a child's prayer. Hall gives the scriptural depth: Nephi's 60+ names for Christ aren't decoration but theology — each title describes a different way Christ relates to us.

### 8. Remembrance and Foundation

| Talk | Speaker | Key Contribution |
|------|---------|------------------|
| [Remember, Remember](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/52wong?lang=eng) | Wong | Five "remember, remember" Book of Mormon verses — a structural motif |

The Book of Mormon's own internal refrain.

## Recommended Study Pairings

These are talks that illuminate each other when read together. Each pairing could sustain a full study session.

### Pairing 1: Charity as the End — Bednar + Christofferson
[16bednar.md](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/16bednar?lang=eng) + [51christofferson.md](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/51christofferson?lang=eng)

**Why:** Together they redefine "endure to the end" from perseverance to transformation. Bednar gives the doctrinal reframe (charity = endurance = becoming); Christofferson gives the interior anatomy (three motivations, mighty change of heart). Christofferson explicitly cites Bednar's earlier work. Read Bednar first, then Christofferson — the second builds on the first.

**Study angle:** Trace every scripture Bednar cites. Then ask: does Christofferson's "mighty change of heart" framework explain *how* the charity Bednar describes develops? What is the mechanism?

---

### Pairing 2: Abide in Me — Soares + Gong
[210soares.md](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/210soares?lang=eng) + [58gong.md](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/58gong?lang=eng)

**Why:** Both draw from John 14–15 but from complementary angles. Soares = *discernment* (recognize the True Vine among counterfeits). Gong = *covenant pattern* (Emmaus Road as template for weekly discipleship — scripture burns, bread reveals). Together they answer both "what are we connecting to?" and "how?"

**Study angle:** Read John 14–15 in full first. Then read these two talks in sequence. Map out: what does Soares see in the vine that Gong doesn't emphasize, and vice versa? How does the Emmaus pattern relate to the sacrament?

---

### Pairing 3: Coming Home — Gilbert + Wu + Freeman
[15gilbert.md](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/15gilbert?lang=eng) + [25wu.md](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/25wu?lang=eng) + [42freeman.md](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/42freeman?lang=eng)

**Why:** Three conversion arcs at different scales. Gilbert = the invitation (come home). Wu = the cost (give away all sins). Freeman = the reassurance (Peter's worst day wasn't final). Together they address: what does it take to come home, what does it cost, and what if I've already failed?

### Pairing 4: Easter + Kirtland — Cook + Uchtdorf
[56cook.md](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/56cook?lang=eng) + [41uchtdorf.md](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/41uchtdorf?lang=eng)

**Why:** Easter 2026 on the 190th Kirtland Temple anniversary is not coincidence — it's context. Uchtdorf testifies of the Resurrection; Cook explains what the Resurrection enables: sealing keys, eternal families, the whole point. Read together, they complete the Easter story: He rose → He appeared in Kirtland → He restored the keys → families are forever.

**Study angle:** Read [D&C 110](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/110?lang=eng) alongside these talks. What happens when Elijah's keys meet Easter? What did Cook say about Jewish leaders recognizing the sealing room's significance?

### Pairing 5: God Sees You — Caussé + Matswagothata + Oaks ("Alive in Christ")
[27causse.md](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/27causse?lang=eng) + [29matswagothata.md](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/29matswagothata?lang=eng) + [49oaks.md](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/49oaks?lang=eng)

**Why:** Three angles on God's personal knowledge. Caussé = the theological claim (God loves all universally AND each individually). Matswagothata = the experience of being seen (a stake president comforted by a child's prayer). Oaks = the hardest application (seeing a despised enemy as a child of God). Together: God sees you, and when you see others the way God sees them, everything changes.

### Pairing 6: Marriage and Family — Andersen + Wakolo
[55andersen.md](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/55andersen?lang=eng) + [57wakolo.md](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/57wakolo?lang=eng)

**Why:** Andersen describes the journey *within* a sealed marriage — character refinement, trials, covenant love through long illness. Wakolo describes the journey *toward* a sealed family — part-member families, patience, respecting agency, God's timing. Together they address both those whose families are sealed and those still waiting with faith.

### Pairing 7: Navigation — Eyring + Soares
[19eyring.md](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/19eyring?lang=eng) + [210soares.md](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2026/04/210soares?lang=eng)

**Why:** Eyring provides the mechanism (continual prayer → spirit of prophecy and revelation → peace). **Study angle:** Trace the sons of Mosiah in [Alma 17](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/17?lang=eng). What did continual prayer produce in them? How does that connect to distinguishing true vine from false?

## Cross-Cluster Connections

The clusters aren't silos. The conference's coherence comes from how they overlap:

- **Charity (Cluster 1) → Abiding (Cluster 2):** Being possessed of charity IS abiding in the vine. Bednar's "endure" and Soares' "abide" are the same verb applied to the same relationship.
- **Abiding (Cluster 2) → Guidance (Cluster 4):** The True Vine IS the guide. Soares' discernment teaching and the guidance talks describe the same need from different angles — one theological, one practical.
- **Conversion (Cluster 3) → Charity (Cluster 1):** Coming home is the beginning; charity is where we're heading. These clusters are the same journey at different stages.
- **Resurrection (Cluster 5) → Families (Cluster 6):** Cook's Kirtland/Easter connection is the explicit bridge. Resurrection makes sealing possible. Without a risen Christ, family sealings are sentiment.
- **God Knows You (Cluster 7) → Charity (Cluster 1):** Oaks' nurse story sits at the intersection — she felt God's love for a despised man. Seeing as God sees IS being possessed of charity.

## What This Conference Is About

If I had to name it in a sentence: **Christ is the True Vine — abide in Him, be transformed by His charity, and the connection outlasts death.**

Every cluster feeds that central claim. The vine requires abiding (Soares, Gong). Abiding produces transformation (Bednar, Christofferson). Transformation looks like loving even enemies (Oaks). The Resurrection guarantees the connection survives death (Uchtdorf, Rasband). The sealing keys make the connection eternal for families (Cook). And God knows every branch on the vine by name (Caussé, Matswagothata).

Oaks' closing remarks distill it: "Jesus Christ is the way to peace in this life and eternal life in the world to come."

---

## Where to Start

If you're choosing one pairing for your first deep study, start with **Bednar + Christofferson** (Pairing 1). Bednar's reframe of "endure to the end" is the single most illuminating doctrinal move of the conference, and Christofferson builds directly on it. Everything else in the conference makes more sense through that lens.

If the discernment/Spirit angle draws you, start with **Soares + Gong** (Pairing 2) and add **Eyring** (Pairing 7). The John 14–15 thread is this conference's theological backbone.

If Easter and the Restoration anchor you, start with **Cook + Uchtdorf** (Pairing 4). The Kirtland/Easter convergence is a once-in-a-generation teaching moment.
