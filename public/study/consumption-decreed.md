# The Consumption Decreed

*A deep study on D&C 87:6 — what it means, where it comes from, and why it matters now*

## The Spark

On February 28, 2026, the United States and Israel launched a joint military operation — "Operation Epic Fury" (US) and "Operation Roaring Lion" (Israel) — that killed Iranian Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei and 47 senior military and intelligence officials. Celebrations broke out in parts of Iran. In Beijing, the response was measured and sympathetic to the regime.

Watching this unfold, a phrase that has lived in the back of my mind for years came flooding forward:

> "And thus, with the sword and by bloodshed the inhabitants of the earth shall mourn; and with famine, and plague, and earthquake, and the thunder of heaven, and the fierce and vivid lightning also, shall the inhabitants of the earth be made to feel the wrath, and indignation, and chastening hand of an Almighty God, until the consumption decreed hath made a full end of all nations"
> — [D&C 87:6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/87?lang=eng&id=p6#p6)

This is the verse the Spirit used years ago to whisper that it was time to move my family. Everything fell into place over six months. But I've never fully understood what "the consumption decreed" actually *means*. Time to find out.

## The Context: D&C 87

Joseph Smith received this revelation on Christmas Day, 1832, as he watched the South Carolina nullification crisis unfold. The revelation traces a chain of escalating conflict:

1. **Civil War** begins at "the rebellion of South Carolina" (v. 1) — *fulfilled, 1861*
2. **War poured out upon all nations**, beginning at that place (v. 2-3) — *in progress*
3. **Slaves rise** against their masters (v. 4) — *patterns visible throughout history*
4. **Remnants** marshal and "vex the Gentiles with a sore vexation" (v. 5) — *in progress*
5. Sword, famine, plague, earthquake, thunder, lightning — **"until the consumption decreed hath made a full end of all nations"** (v. 6)
6. **Purpose**: "That the cry of the saints, and of the blood of the saints, shall cease to come up into the ears of the Lord of Sabaoth" (v. 7)
7. **Instruction**: "Stand ye in holy places, and be not moved, until the day of the Lord come" (v. 8)

The consumption decreed is the *culmination* of this chain. It's where all the conflict leads. And it ends not one nation, but *all* nations.

## Tracing the Phrase: Isaiah's Prophecy

Joseph didn't invent this phrase. It comes straight from Isaiah, and the footnotes hand it to us on a silver platter.

### Isaiah 10:22-23 (and 2 Nephi 20:22-23)

> "For though thy people Israel be as the sand of the sea, yet a remnant of them shall return: **the consumption decreed shall overflow with righteousness.**"
> — [Isaiah 10:22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/isa/10?lang=eng&id=p22#p22)

> "For the Lord God of hosts **shall make a consumption, even determined**, in the midst of all the land."
> — [Isaiah 10:23](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/isa/10?lang=eng&id=p23#p23)

Nephi quotes this identically:

> "the **consumption** decreed shall overflow with righteousness."
> — [2 Nephi 20:22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/20?lang=eng&id=p22#p22)

### Isaiah 28:22

> "Now therefore be ye not mockers, lest your bands be made strong: for I have heard from the Lord God of hosts **a consumption, even determined upon the whole earth.**"
> — [Isaiah 28:22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/isa/28?lang=eng&id=p22#p22)

### Daniel 9:27

> "even until **the consummation**, and that determined shall be poured upon the desolate."
> — [Daniel 9:27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/dan/9?lang=eng&id=p27#p27)

The 2 Nephi 20:23 footnote cross-references this Daniel passage directly. Same concept: a *determined ending* that is poured out.

### Paul's Greek Rendering — Romans 9:27-28

Paul quotes Isaiah 10:22 but the Greek translation shifts the language:

> "Though the number of the children of Israel be as the sand of the sea, a remnant shall be saved: For he will **finish the work, and cut it short in righteousness**: because **a short work** will the Lord make upon the earth."
> — [Romans 9:27-28](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/rom/9?lang=eng&id=p27-p28#p27)

Paul renders "consumption decreed" as "finish the work, and cut it short." The Greek understood the Hebrew: this isn't a slow burn — it's a *determined finishing*. God will not let it drag on forever.

## The Hebrew: כָּלָה (Kālāh)

The word behind "consumption" in Isaiah 10:22-23 and 28:22 is **כָּלָה** (*kālāh*), from the root **כלה** (*kalah*) — meaning **to be complete, to come to an end, to be finished, to be consumed, to be spent.**

This is *not* the Hebrew word for eating or drinking. It's the word for *finishing*. For bringing something to its absolute conclusion. The related noun **כְּלָיָה** (*kelayah*) means **complete destruction, annihilation**.

Key nuance: *kalah* carries a sense of **completeness** — not random destruction, but a *finishing* that leaves nothing undone. When God makes a *kālāh*, He isn't raging. He is *completing* something.

This matters because Isaiah pairs "consumption" with "decreed" — Hebrew **חָרַץ** (*charats*), meaning **to cut, to decide, to determine.** A judicial decision. Something settled in advance. Something cut into stone.

So "the consumption decreed" is literally: **the determined completion. The finishing that was decided.**

---

## Webster 1828: Consumption

Noah Webster, writing just two years before the Church was organized, defines it precisely:

> **CONSUMPTION** (n.) — "The act or process of consuming by use, waste, etc.; **decay; destruction.**"
> — Webster 1828

And **CONSUME**:
> "To **destroy**, as by decomposition, dissipation, waste, or fire; to use up; to expend; to waste; to burn up; to eat up; to **devour.**"

The 1828 meaning captures three dimensions:
1. **Destruction** — burning, devouring
2. **Waste** — progressive decay, things spending themselves
3. **Disease** — "pulmonary consumption" was tuberculosis, a disease that *wastes the body from within*

That third meaning is haunting. Consumption as a disease that eats you from the inside. Nations wasting away from internal decay — not conquered from without, but consumed from within.

And **DECREE**:
> "An order from one having authority, deciding what is to be done... a determination by one having power, deciding what is to be done or to take place; **edict, law; authoritative rule.**"

And **CONSUMMATION** (the form used in Daniel 9:27):
> "**Completion; perfection; termination; end** (as of the world or of life)."

Completion. Perfection. End. The consumption decreed is the **decreed completion** — the divinely authorized finishing of something.

## The Paradox: Destruction That Overflows with Righteousness

Here's where Isaiah does something remarkable. Look at the full context of Isaiah 10:22:

> "For though thy people Israel be as the sand of the sea, yet a remnant of them shall return: **the consumption decreed shall overflow with righteousness.**"

The consumption *overflows with righteousness*. This isn't wanton destruction. It's *righteous completion*. God is not destroying for the sake of destroying — He is *finishing* what wickedness began.

Isaiah 10's context is crucial. Assyria is God's instrument — "the rod of mine anger" (v. 5). But Assyria doesn't know that. Assyria thinks it's conquering by its own power: "By the strength of my hand I have done it, and by my wisdom" ([Isa 10:13](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/isa/10?lang=eng&id=p13#p13)). God uses the proud to chasten the wicked, then holds the proud accountable for their pride.

The Isaiah 10:22 footnote in the LDS edition says it plainly: **"IE even when punishment comes, mercy is available."**

Isaiah 10:25 promises: "For yet a very little while, and the indignation shall cease."

And verse 27: "the yoke shall be destroyed because of the **anointing**" — footnoted to [TG Jesus Christ, Messiah](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/tg/jesus-christ-messiah?lang=eng). The yoke breaks because of Christ. The consumption ends at redemption.

## Jeremiah's Clarification: Full End, But Not of You

Jeremiah uses the same language twice, and adds a critical qualifier:

> "For I am with thee, saith the Lord, to save thee: though I make a **full end of all nations** whither I have scattered thee, **yet will I not make a full end of thee**: but I will correct thee in measure, and will not leave thee altogether unpunished."
> — [Jeremiah 30:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/jer/30?lang=eng&id=p11#p11)

> "I will make a **full end of all the nations** whither I have driven thee: but I will **not make a full end of thee**, but correct thee in measure"
> — [Jeremiah 46:28](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/jer/46?lang=eng&id=p28#p28)

There it is. The consumption decreed makes a full end of **nations** — but not of God's covenant people. Nations end. The covenant endures. Empires fall. Israel remains — corrected, chastened, but *not consumed*.

This is exactly what D&C 87:7 says: the consumption continues **until** "the cry of the saints... shall cease." The whole point is that the saints' suffering ends. The nations that caused the suffering? They end.

## Your Intuition: Consumption as Anti-Zion

You asked: *"Does it mean man's desires will grow so big we consume all things, like the opposite of Zion?"*

This is actually a profound reading, and it's more right than it might seem at first.

### Zion

> "And the Lord called his people **Zion**, because they were of **one heart and one mind**, and dwelt in righteousness; and there was **no poor** among them."
> — [Moses 7:18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/7?lang=eng&id=p18#p18)

Zion is *unity*, *righteousness*, and *sufficiency*. No one lacking. No one hoarding. One heart.

### Babylon

What is the opposite? **Consumption.** The insatiable desire for more. Power. Gain. Control. Taking for self what was meant to be shared.

Look at how 1 Nephi 22:23 describes what will be "consumed":

> "all churches which are built up to **get gain**, and all those who are built up to **get power** over the flesh, and those who are built up to **become popular** in the eyes of the world, and those who seek **the lusts of the flesh and the things of the world**, and to do all manner of iniquity... they are those who must be **consumed** as stubble"
> — [1 Nephi 22:23](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/22?lang=eng&id=p23#p23)

Get gain. Get power. Become popular. Lusts of the flesh. Things of the world. This is the vocabulary of **consumption** — of *taking*, *devouring*, *wanting more*.

The [Serpent and Dragon](serpent-and-dragon.md) study identified the beast's values as "pride, **consumption**, control." That word wasn't accidental. It's the serpent's nature to *devour*.

And here's the devastating insight: **nations built on consumption consume themselves.** They don't need God to destroy them. They do it to themselves. The sword of their own hands falls upon their own heads ([1 Ne 22:13](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/22?lang=eng&id=p13#p13)).

So yes — in a very real sense, the "consumption decreed" is the inevitable consequence of human appetite run unchecked. When a civilization's fundamental orientation is *consuming* rather than *building Zion*, the end is written into the beginning. The decree isn't arbitrary. It's natural law. It's the harvest of what was planted.

Zephaniah 1:2 puts it in God's own voice:

> "I will utterly **consume** all things from off the land, saith the Lord."
> — [Zephaniah 1:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/zeph/1?lang=eng&id=p2#p2)

And verse 18: "the whole land shall be devoured by the fire of his jealousy: for he shall make even a **speedy riddance** of all them that dwell in the land."

The earth itself is consumed by fire — and then made new:

> "every corruptible thing, both of man, or of the beasts of the field... shall be **consumed**; And also that of element shall **melt** with fervent heat; and all things shall become **new**, that my knowledge and glory may dwell upon all the earth."
> — [D&C 101:24-25](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/101?lang=eng&id=p24-p25#p24)

The consumption *makes way for something new*. It's not nihilistic — it's preparatory. The old must be consumed so the new can emerge.

## The Three Layers of Meaning

Pulling it all together, the "consumption decreed" operates on at least three levels:

### 1. The Hebrew Layer: Determined Completion
God has *decided* to finish something. The word is *kālāh* — complete, bring to an end. Paired with *charats* — cut, determined, decreed. This isn't reactive anger. It's a judicial sentence passed in advance. The verdict was rendered before the trial even began, because God sees the end from the beginning.

### 2. The Prophetic Layer: Nations Self-Destruct
The consumption isn't primarily God smiting nations from the outside. It's nations *consuming themselves* through pride, greed, war, and insatiable appetite. Assyria thought it was conquering by its own wisdom — "by the strength of my hand I have done it" ([Isa 10:13](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/isa/10?lang=eng&id=p13#p13)). But Assyria was just the rod. And rods get burned when the work is done ([Isa 10:17-19](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/isa/10?lang=eng&id=p17-p19#p17)). Every empire carries the seed of its own destruction.

### 3. The Spiritual Layer: Anti-Zion Reaches Its Conclusion
When a civilization chooses *consumption* over *consecration*, *getting* over *giving*, *devouring* over *building* — the end is inevitable. The consumption decreed is the logical terminus of a world that chose Babylon over Zion. It overflows "with righteousness" because it's *right*. It's the natural consequence. It's the harvest.

## What About Now?

The chain in D&C 87 is not over. War poured out upon all nations continues to escalate. The strikes on Iran — killing the supreme leader and the senior leadership of a major nation — are exactly the kind of domino the revelation describes: one nation calling on another, war spreading from place to place.

D&C 45 describes the same period:

> "in that day shall be heard of wars and rumors of wars, and the whole earth shall be in commotion, and men's hearts shall fail them, and they shall say that Christ delayeth his coming"
> — [D&C 45:26](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/45?lang=eng&id=p26#p26)

The consumption is the *end* of this process — not a single event, but the cumulative result of all the wars, famines, plagues, and earthquakes reaching their conclusion. The determined finishing.

But notice Jeremiah's promise: "I will make a full end of all nations... **yet will I not make a full end of thee**" ([Jer 30:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/jer/30?lang=eng&id=p11#p11)). Covenant people endure. Not comfortably. Not without correction. But they *endure*.

## The Answer: Stand

After the most terrifying prophecy in all of modern revelation, the Lord gives the simplest possible instruction:

> "Wherefore, **stand** ye in **holy places**, and **be not moved**, until the day of the Lord come; for behold, it cometh quickly, saith the Lord."
> — [D&C 87:8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/87?lang=eng&id=p8#p8)

Three verbs: **Stand. Be not moved. Come.**

You don't stop the consumption. You don't fix the nations. You don't need to understand every geopolitical chess move. You *stand*. In a holy place. And you do not move.

D&C 45:32 echoes this:

> "But my disciples shall **stand in holy places**, and shall not be moved; but among the wicked, men shall lift up their voices and curse God and die."
> — [D&C 45:32](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/45?lang=eng&id=p32#p32)

D&C 101:22 expands it:

> "all they who call on my name, and worship me according to mine everlasting gospel, should **gather together, and stand in holy places**"
> — [D&C 101:22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/101?lang=eng&id=p22#p22)

Elder Bednar connected this directly in his October 2020 talk:

> We will be enabled to "gather together, and stand in holy places" and "be not moved, until the day of the Lord come."
> — [David A. Bednar, "We Will Prove Them Herewith"](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2020/10/12bednar?lang=eng)

Holy places: temples, homes consecrated by covenant living, stakes of Zion. The gathering isn't just spiritual — it's physical. You moved your family because the Spirit said *move*. That's what standing in holy places looks like. Sometimes standing starts with moving.

## Becoming

**What this study revealed:**

The consumption decreed is not a punishment imposed from outside. It's the *determined completion* of what Babylon started. Nations that consume, get consumed. Civilizations built on appetite devour themselves. The decree was written into the choice.

But the consumption "overflows with righteousness." It's not nihilism — it's justice making way for renewal. Every corruptible thing consumed, and then "all things shall become new" ([D&C 101:25](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/101?lang=eng&id=p25#p25)).

**Questions to sit with:**

- What am I consuming? Not just physically — what am I devouring with my time, attention, energy? Am I building Zion or feeding Babylon?
- The Spirit whispered *move*. And you did. Where is the Spirit whispering now? What holy place is He pointing to?
- Jeremiah says God will "correct thee in measure." What correction am I resisting that would actually save me?
- If the answer to the most terrifying prophecy in scripture is simply "stand and be not moved" — why do I keep trying to *do more* instead of *standing firmer*?

**This week:**

- When the news cycle pulls me into anxiety about nations and conflict, return to D&C 87:8. Stand in a holy place. Be not moved. The consumption is decreed — it will finish itself. My job is not to stop it. My job is to stand.
