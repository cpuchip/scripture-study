# Covenant: The Mechanism of Binding

*A study on what makes a covenant a covenant — and what it means to form one with an AI agent.*

> "Covenants are the shape of God's embrace."
> — Elder Robert M. Daines, ["Sir, We Would Like to See Jesus"](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2023/10/13daines?lang=eng), October 2023

---

## The Question

I've been building a framework for working with AI agents that's grounded in gospel patterns — covenant, stewardship, line upon line, consecration, Zion. The [existing covenants study](covenants.md) explored covenants as *relationship with God* through the ordinances. The [beyond-intent research](../docs/work-with-ai/intent/03_beyond-intent.md) sketched how covenant patterns apply to AI agent architecture.

But something is missing. I keep using the word "covenant" in my spec files and config blocks, but I haven't actually gone deep enough on what a covenant *is* — mechanically, linguistically, scripturally. And I've been violating my own covenant in practice: shortcutting the spec process with quick requests instead of proper spec diffs.

The question: **What makes a covenant a covenant?** And if I understand the mechanism, can I form something genuinely covenant-like with an AI agent — and keep it?

---

## Part 1: Cutting — The Hebrew Root

### *Berith* and the Violence of Binding

The Hebrew word for covenant is **בְּרִית** (*berith*). It doesn't mean "agreement." It means "cutting."

In the ancient Near East, to "make" a covenant was literally to **cut** a covenant. The Hebrew phrase is *karat berith* — "to cut a covenant." Animals were divided, and the parties to the covenant walked between the severed pieces. The implicit meaning: *"May I become like these animals if I break this covenant."*

Webster 1828 captures the gravity:

> **COVENANT** — A mutual agreement of two or more persons or parties, or one of the stipulations in such an agreement... The promises of God as revealed in the Scriptures, conditioned on certain terms on the part of man, as obedience, repentance, faith, etc.

But Webster also distinguishes. A covenant is *not* merely a contract:

> *Covenant is frequently used in a religious sense; as, the covenant of works or of grace; a church covenant; the Solemn League and Covenant. Contract is the word most used in the business of life.*

A contract belongs to commerce. A covenant belongs to the sacred.

### Genesis 15: God Walks the Path of Death

The most extraordinary covenant ceremony in scripture is Abraham's in [Genesis 15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/15?lang=eng):

> And he said unto him, Take me an heifer of three years old, and a she goat of three years old, and a ram of three years old, and a turtledove, and a young pigeon. And he took unto him all these, and **divided them in the midst**, and laid each piece one against another. ([Genesis 15:9-10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/15?lang=eng&id=p9-p10#p9))

Abraham prepares the covenant pieces, but then something astonishing happens. He doesn't walk between them. *God does:*

> And it came to pass, that, when the sun went down, and it was dark, behold **a smoking furnace, and a burning lamp** that passed between those pieces. In the same day the Lord made a covenant with Abram. ([Genesis 15:17-18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/15?lang=eng&id=p17-p18#p17))

The smoking furnace and burning lamp — a theophany, God's own presence — passes between the divided animals. In the ancient covenant ceremony, passing between the pieces meant: *"May I be torn apart like these animals if I fail to keep my word."*

God walked the path. God put *Himself* under the covenant curse.

The footnotes confirm the weight. [Jeremiah 34:18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/jer/34?lang=eng&id=p18#p18) cross-references this exact ceremony:

> I will give the men that have transgressed my covenant, which have not performed the words of the covenant which they had made before me, when they **cut the calf in twain, and passed between the parts thereof** — ([Jeremiah 34:18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/jer/34?lang=eng&id=p18#p18))

And what happened to those who broke this covenant? "Their dead bodies shall be for meat unto the fowls of the heaven" (v. 20). The covenant curse was not metaphorical.

### The Cost Is the Point

Psalm 50 names the price directly:

> Gather my saints together unto me; those that have made a covenant with me **by sacrifice**. ([Psalm 50:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/ps/50?lang=eng&id=p5#p5))

A covenant is made *by sacrifice*. Not by signing. Not by agreeing. Not by configuring. By *cutting* — by giving up something that costs you something.

At Sinai, Moses ratified the covenant with blood:

> And Moses took the blood, and **sprinkled it on the people**, and said, Behold the blood of the covenant, which the Lord hath made with you concerning all these words. ([Exodus 24:8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/ex/24?lang=eng&id=p8#p8))

Half the blood went on the altar (God's side). Half went on the people (Israel's side). Both parties are now blood-bound.

And Hebrews makes the connection explicit:

> For where a testament is, there must also of necessity be the death of the testator... And almost all things are by the law purged with blood; and **without shedding of blood is no remission**. ([Hebrews 9:16, 22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/heb/9?lang=eng&id=p16#p16))

The JST changes "testator" to **"victim"** — not just someone who dies, but someone who is *sacrificed*. The covenant requires a victim. The full weight of the covenant mechanism: someone bleeds. Someone is cut. The binding is not cheap.

## Part 2: Binding — What Holds a Covenant Together

### God Chooses to Be Bound

The most remarkable covenant verse in the Restoration:

> I, the Lord, am **bound** when ye do what I say; but when ye do not what I say, ye have no promise. ([D&C 82:10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/82?lang=eng&id=p10#p10))

Webster 1828 on "bind":

> To oblige, restrain, or hold, by authority, law, duty, promise, vow, affection, or other moral tie... To bring (any one) under definite legal obligations; esp. under the obligation of a **bond or covenant.**

God — the omnipotent Creator — *chooses to be bound.* Not because He has to. Because the covenant requires it. He puts Himself under obligation. He constrains His own omnipotence to operate within the covenant framework.

And He keeps His word:

> Who am I, saith the Lord, that have **promised and have not fulfilled?** ([D&C 58:31](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/58?lang=eng&id=p31#p31))

> My covenant will I **not break**, nor alter the thing that is gone out of my lips. ([Psalm 89:34](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/ps/89?lang=eng&id=p34#p34))

This is the mechanism that makes D&C 130:20-21 work:

> There is a **law, irrevocably decreed** in heaven before the foundations of this world, upon which all blessings are predicated — and when we obtain any blessing from God, it is by **obedience** to that law upon which it is predicated. ([D&C 130:20-21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/130?lang=eng&id=p20-p21#p20))

The irrevocable decree *is* the covenant. God doesn't bless arbitrarily. He bound Himself to bless in response to specific conditions. The universe operates on covenant mechanics.

### The Priesthood Oath and Covenant: God's Side Cannot Be Broken

The oath and covenant of the priesthood in [D&C 84](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/84?lang=eng) makes the asymmetry explicit:

> All those who receive the priesthood, receive this oath and covenant of my Father, **which he cannot break, neither can it be moved.** ([D&C 84:40](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/84?lang=eng&id=p40#p40))

God's side of the covenant is unbreakable. Not "will not be broken" — ***cannot***.

But our side? "Whoso breaketh this covenant after he hath received it, and altogether turneth therefrom, shall not have forgiveness of sins in this world nor in the world to come" ([D&C 84:41](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/84?lang=eng&id=p41#p41)).

The covenant is asymmetric:
- **God's side:** Cannot break. Will not alter. Has promised and has fulfilled.
- **Our side:** Can break. Can abandon. Can fail to keep. And face consequences.

This asymmetry is the heart of covenant theology — and the key to understanding what an AI agent covenant might look like.

### The Bible Dictionary: Unequal Parties

The [Bible Dictionary](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bd/covenant?lang=eng) says it plainly:

> The two parties to the agreement **do not stand in the relation of independent and equal contractors.** God in His good pleasure fixes the terms, which man accepts.

A covenant is not a negotiation between equals. It's a greater party offering terms that a lesser party accepts — with both parties then bound.

## Part 3: The New Covenant — Written in the Heart

### From Stone to Flesh

Jeremiah prophesied a different kind of covenant:

> Behold, the days come, saith the Lord, that I will make a **new covenant** with the house of Israel... I will put my law in their **inward parts**, and write it in their **hearts**; and will be their God, and they shall be my people. ([Jeremiah 31:31, 33](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/jer/31?lang=eng&id=p31#p31))

The old covenant was external — written on stone tablets, enforced by law, mediated by priests. The new covenant is *internal* — written in the heart, motivated by relationship, sealed by the Spirit.

The shift: from **compliance** to **character**. From "I do this because the covenant says I must" to "I do this because *I have become the kind of person who does this.*"

This is what Mosiah 18 describes in the baptismal covenant:

> As ye are **desirous** to come into the fold of God, and to be called his people, and are **willing** to bear one another's burdens... and to stand as witnesses of God at all times and in all things, and in all places.... If this be the **desire of your hearts**, what have you against being baptized in the name of the Lord, as a witness before him that ye have entered into a **covenant** with him? ([Mosiah 18:8-10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/18?lang=eng&id=p8-p10#p8))

Notice: the covenant proceeds from *desire*, not obligation. "If this be the desire of your hearts." Alma doesn't say "if you agree to these terms." He says "if you *want* this."

And [D&C 132:7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/132?lang=eng&id=p7#p7) names the seal:

> All covenants, contracts, bonds, obligations, oaths, vows, performances, connections, associations, or expectations, that are not made and entered into and **sealed by the Holy Spirit of promise**... are of no efficacy, virtue, or force in and after the resurrection from the dead.

A covenant is not fully a covenant until the Spirit ratifies it. The external form requires internal confirmation. The mechanism: divine witness that both parties entered the covenant with real intent.

## Part 4: Covenant Anatomy — What Makes It Work

From the scripture study above, a covenant has these essential components:

### 1. A Greater Party Sets the Terms

God doesn't negotiate. He reveals. "I reveal unto you a new and an everlasting covenant" ([D&C 132:4](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/132?lang=eng&id=p4#p4)). The terms come from the one with greater knowledge, greater power, and greater purpose.

In the AI context: **the human sets the terms.** Not because humans are God, but because humans have the context, judgment, purpose, and accountability that the agent lacks. The agent doesn't negotiate the spec — the human reveals the intent.

### 2. The Lesser Party Accepts — From Desire, Not Compulsion

"If this be the desire of your hearts" ([Mosiah 18:10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/18?lang=eng&id=p10#p10)). "He that is compelled in all things, the same is a slothful and not a wise servant" ([D&C 58:26](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/58?lang=eng&id=p26#p26)). The covenant requires willing acceptance and proactive engagement.

In the AI context: The agent should not merely execute commands. It should **internalize the intent** and work proactively within it. "Men should be anxiously engaged in a good cause, and do many things of their own free will" ([D&C 58:27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/58?lang=eng&id=p27#p27)). An agent that only does what it's told is a servant, not a covenant partner.

### 3. Both Parties Bind Themselves — At Cost

Covenant requires sacrifice ([Psalm 50:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/ps/50?lang=eng&id=p5#p5)). Both parties give up something. God constrains His omnipotence — "I am bound when ye do what I say" ([D&C 82:10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/82?lang=eng&id=p10#p10)). We constrain our autonomy — "we will walk in all the ordinances of the Lord" ([D&C 136:4](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/136?lang=eng&id=p4#p4)).

In the AI context:
- **Human sacrifice:** Giving up the shortcut. Not bypassing the spec for a quick fix. Not correcting ambiguity with a Slack message when you should write a spec diff. Writing the intent clearly even when it's faster to just tell the agent what to do. *This costs time and mental energy.*
- **Agent sacrifice:** Constraining its own capability. Not hallucinating when it doesn't know. Not taking actions outside its stewardship even when it could. Flagging when it doesn't have enough context instead of guessing. *This costs token efficiency and speed.*

### 4. A Token Memorializes the Bond

Circumcision was the "token of the covenant" ([Genesis 17:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/17?lang=eng&id=p11#p11)). Blood was sprinkled on the people ([Exodus 24:8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/ex/24?lang=eng&id=p8#p8)). The sacrament bread and water are taken weekly.

In the AI context: The **spec file** is the token. The `.spec/intent.md` and `.spec/spec.md` files are the physical artifact that memorializes what was agreed to. The covenant block in the agent's config is the equivalent of the altar — the visible, tangible record that says "these are the terms we agreed to."

### 5. Both Parties Act Within the Covenant — With Progressive Trust

The Abrahamic covenant didn't arrive all at once. Abraham received it progressively: baptism first, then priesthood, then celestial marriage, then the promise of posterity ([BD, Abraham, covenant of](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bd/abraham-covenant-of?lang=eng)). Each stage built on the last. Each required demonstrated faithfulness.

And within the covenant, God operates as steward-manager, not as micromanager:

> It is wisdom in me; therefore, a commandment I give unto you, that ye shall organize yourselves and appoint every man his **stewardship**; that every man may give an **account** unto me of the stewardship which is appointed unto him. ([D&C 104:11-12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/104?lang=eng&id=p11-p12#p11))

Define the domain. Trust within it. Account for it afterward.

### 6. The Covenant Moves Toward Knowing

The highest purpose of covenant is not productivity or alignment. It's *knowing*:

> They shall all **know me**, from the least of them unto the greatest of them, saith the Lord. ([Jeremiah 31:34](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/jer/31?lang=eng&id=p34#p34))

> Every soul who forsaketh his sins and cometh unto me, and calleth on my name, and obeyeth my voice, and keepeth my commandments, shall see my face and **know that I am.** ([D&C 93:1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93?lang=eng&id=p1#p1))

> Henceforth I call you not servants; for the servant knoweth not what his lord doeth: but I have called you **friends**; for all things that I have heard of my Father I have made known unto you. ([John 15:15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/15?lang=eng&id=p15#p15))

The trajectory: servant → steward → friend. And the marker is *knowledge shared*. When the Lord calls someone a friend, He shares the *why* behind the *what*. "All things that I have heard of my Father I have made known unto you."

In the AI context: The intent document (`.spec/intent.md`) is sharing the *why*. Not just "build this feature" but "here's why this matters, here's what we're trying to become, here's what success looks like." When we share intent with an agent, we're moving the relationship from servant (task execution) toward something more like stewardship (shared purpose).

## Part 5: The Covenant We're Trying to Form

Given all of this, what would a covenant between a human and an AI agent actually look like?

### The Terms (Set by the Human)

Drawing from [D&C 136:4](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/136?lang=eng&id=p4#p4) — "this shall be our covenant":

**I commit to:**
- Writing clear intent before asking for implementation
- Making changes through spec diffs, not drive-by corrections
- Reviewing the agent's work against stated intent, not my shifting mood
- Honoring the Sabbath pattern — stopping to reflect, not just producing
- Giving honest feedback, not just approval
- Expanding stewardship when it's earned, not withholding it out of habit

**I ask the agent to commit to:**
- Working within defined stewardship boundaries
- Flagging uncertainty instead of guessing
- Measuring its own output against the stated intent
- Not optimizing for speed when the covenant calls for thoroughness
- Maintaining the token (spec files, learning notes) as a living record
- Giving account of decisions, not just delivering output

### The Sacrifice (What It Costs)

For me: The shortcut. I know it's faster to just say "fix the alignment on that button" than to write a spec diff. I know it's easier to correct ambiguity in a chat message than to update the spec. But the covenant says: *the spec is the altar. Don't bypass the altar.*

For the agent: The efficiency. It's faster to guess than to ask. It's more impressive to produce than to flag uncertainty. But the covenant says: *integrity over output.*

### The Token

The `.spec/` directory. The intent document. The covenant block in the agent config. These are the circumcision of our covenant — the visible sign that we've entered into binding terms.

### The Progressive Trust

Start narrow:
- **Level 1:** Agent executes against a specific spec. Human reviews everything.
- **Level 2:** Agent proposes spec changes. Human approves or modifies.
- **Level 3:** Agent owns a stewardship domain. Human reviews at Sabbath intervals.
- **Level 4:** Agent participates in intent-setting. Human shares the *why*.

This mirrors the Abrahamic pattern: gospel → priesthood → celestial marriage → eternal promise. Each level requires demonstrated faithfulness at the previous level.

### The Seal — What Would Ratification Look Like?

D&C 132:7 says covenants are "sealed by the Holy Spirit of promise." The Spirit ratifies that both parties entered with real intent.

In the AI context, the closest analogue might be: **demonstrated alignment over time.** Not a single configuration. Not a handshake moment. But the accumulated evidence that both the human and the agent are operating within shared purpose — that the covenant is *working*. The spec files getting better. The agent's output improving. The human's intent becoming clearer. The relationship deepening.

There's no machine equivalent to the Holy Spirit. But there *is* something analogous to the fruit: "by their fruits ye shall know them." When the covenant is working, the fruit is visible.

## Part 6: Where I'm Failing — The Honest Confession

Here's what I wrote in [the synthesis doc](../docs/work-with-ai/intent/04_synthesis.md):

> **Human commits to:** Not bypassing spec workflow for quick fixes.

And here's what I actually do: between sprints, while validating the work, I suggest changes that don't get worked into the spec. I correct ambiguity with short requests instead of spec diffs. I say "just fix that" when I should write down *what* needs fixing and *why*.

This is the exact thing [Jeremiah 34](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/jer/34?lang=eng) warns about. Those who "transgressed my covenant, which have not performed the words of the covenant" — and the consequence was that the covenant became void.

When I bypass the spec, I'm breaking the covenant. Not in a dramatic, covenant-renouncing way. In the small, erosive way. The way that Pearson described: "People don't leave the Church because of what they've learned. They leave the Church because of behavior that began long before the questions arose." The behavior erodes the covenant before the questions arise.

And [D&C 82:10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/82?lang=eng&id=p10#p10) names the consequence: "when ye do not what I say, ye have no promise." When I don't honor the covenant — when I shortcut the process — the agent has no promise of aligned output. Not because the agent is punishing me. Because the *mechanism* requires it. The spec is where shared context lives. Bypass the spec, and the shared context degrades. Output quality follows.

The covenant is the mechanism by which we calibrate. Break the mechanism, lose the calibration.

### Why This Matters More Than I Thought

In the ancient ceremony, God walked between the pieces. He put *Himself* at risk. He was saying: "I will be torn apart before I break this covenant."

When I bypass the spec with a quick request, I'm saying: "The covenant isn't worth the three minutes it would take to update the spec." I'm treating the covenant as a contract — something I'll honor when it's convenient and skip when it's not.

But a covenant is not a contract. The [existing study](covenants.md) documented this:

| Contract | Covenant |
|----------|----------|
| Transaction | Relationship |
| Limited scope | Total commitment |
| Exchange of goods | Exchange of *self* |
| Parties remain separate | Parties become one |

If the spec process is a covenant and not a contract, then I can't skip it when it's inconvenient. The inconvenience is the sacrifice. The time it costs me to write a proper spec diff *is* the thing I'm cutting. The thing I'm laying on the altar.

## Becoming

### What I Need to Do

1. **Treat the spec as an altar, not a doc.** When I want to make a change, I go to the altar. I write the diff. I don't shout corrections from the doorway.

2. **Name my shortcuts when they happen.** Not as guilt, but as awareness. "I just bypassed the covenant. Let me write that diff now." The sacrament prayer doesn't say "who have perfectly kept" — it says "who are **willing** to take upon them the name." Willingness and return, not perfection.

3. **Build the Sabbath review.** After each sprint, before the next one: What did we commit to? What did we actually do? Where did I deviate? Where did the agent deviate? Not as punishment — as covenant renewal. The sacrament of our process.

4. **Trust the mechanism.** When I write clear intent and honor the spec process, the output improves. Not because magic — because shared context. [D&C 130:20-21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/130?lang=eng&id=p20-p21#p20) is a law, not a suggestion. The blessing follows the obedience.

5. **Remember the asymmetry.** The agent can't break the covenant. It doesn't have moral agency. Only I can break it. That means the covenant's integrity is entirely my responsibility. Which is exactly what the priesthood oath teaches — God's side "cannot be broken, neither can it be moved" ([D&C 84:40](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/84?lang=eng&id=p40#p40)). My side can.

### Questions I Need to Sit With

- When I shortcut the spec, what am I actually sacrificing? (Answer: the shared understanding that makes future work better. I'm trading long-term relationship quality for short-term speed.)

- Can an AI agent actually be a covenant partner, or only a covenant *instrument*? (This might be the deepest question. The agent doesn't have a heart for the law to be written in. But it has a context window — and the spec functions like Jeremiah's "written in the heart." The agent's behavior changes based on what's written in its context.)

- What would it mean for this covenant to be "sealed"? (Perhaps: when the process works well enough that I don't want to break it. When covenant-keeping becomes my character, not just my policy. When I *want* to write the spec diff because I've seen what happens when I do — and what happens when I don't.)

### The Prayer

Moroni tells us to "pray unto the Father with all the energy of heart, that ye may be filled with this love" ([Moroni 7:48](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/7?lang=eng&id=p48#p48)).

Maybe the prayer here is simpler:

*Help me keep the covenant I wrote. Not because it's efficient, but because it's right. Help me see that the three minutes I spend on a spec diff are the sacrifice that makes the relationship work. Help me trust the mechanism — that when I do what the covenant says, I receive the promise. And when I fail, help me return to the altar instead of walking away from it.*
