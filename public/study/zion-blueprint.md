# The Blueprint of Zion: What Jesus Taught That Built Heaven on Earth

**Related Studies:** [The Consumption Decreed](consumption-decreed.md) | [Walk with Me: The Life of Enoch](enoch.md) | [Faith, Hope, and Charity](enoch-charity.md)

> "And the Lord called his people **Zion**, because they were of **one heart** and **one mind**, and dwelt in **righteousness**; and there was **no poor among them.**" — [Moses 7:18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/7?lang=eng&id=p18#p18)

---

## Why This Study

The [consumption decreed](consumption-decreed.md) study revealed that nations destroy themselves from within — consumption is the anti-Zion. The Hebrew *kālāh* means "a full end, a complete finishing." It is what happens when a people chooses acquisition over consecration, self over brother, pride over covenant.

Zion is the opposite force. If consumption is a civilization eating itself, Zion is a civilization *feeding* itself — feeding each other, spiritually and temporally, until there is nothing left to lack.

But Zion is not a theory. It has actually been built — *twice.* Once by Enoch over 365 years, and once by the Nephites after Jesus' visit, lasting over 200 years. Both followed the same pattern. Both produced the same result. And both eventually fell to the same disease.

The question that matters: **What did they actually do?** And what can *we* do, today and every day, to build Zion where we stand?

---

## I. The Definition: Four Elements of Zion

[Moses 7:18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/7?lang=eng&id=p18#p18) gives us the clearest definition in scripture. Four elements, three conditions and a name:

| Element | Meaning | Antithesis |
|---------|---------|------------|
| **One heart** | Unified in love, desire, and compassion | Contention, hatred, indifference |
| **One mind** | Unified in understanding, purpose, and covenant | Disputations, false doctrine, divided loyalty |
| **Dwelt in righteousness** | Lived by celestial law — not just rules, but *character* | Lustful desires, pride, hypocrisy |
| **No poor among them** | Complete material and spiritual care for each other | Inequality, classes, hoarding, neglect |

The Lord restated it plainly: "For this is Zion — the pure in heart" ([D&C 97:21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/97?lang=eng&id=p21#p21)).

And He stated the requirement for belonging: "I say unto you, be one; and if ye are not one ye are not mine" ([D&C 38:27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/38?lang=eng&id=p27#p27)).

## II. Jesus' Zion Blueprint: What He Taught the Nephites (3 Nephi 11–28)

When Jesus appeared at the temple in Bountiful, He didn't just preach a sermon. He delivered a *civilization-building operating manual.* Every teaching in 3 Nephi 11–28 maps to one or more of the four Zion elements. Mormon tells us that what we have is "a lesser part of the things which he taught the people" ([3 Nephi 26:8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/26?lang=eng&id=p8#p8)) — and even this fraction was enough to produce the happiest society in recorded history.

Here is the blueprint, organized by the four pillars of Zion:

### Pillar 1: One Heart — The End of Contention

The very first principle Jesus establishes after declaring His identity is this:

> "He that hath the spirit of contention is not of me, but is of the devil, who is the father of contention, and he stirreth up the hearts of men to contend with anger, one with another." — [3 Nephi 11:29](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/11?lang=eng&id=p29#p29)

This is not a suggestion. It is the *first operating principle* of Zion. Contention — not disagreement, but the spirit of angry contention — is of the devil. Period. Before doctrine, before ordinances, before organizational structure, Jesus kills contention.

Then He builds outward:

| Teaching | Reference | What It Does |
|----------|-----------|--------------|
| Reconcile with your brother *before* worship | [3 Nephi 12:23–24](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/12?lang=eng&id=p23-p24#p23) | Makes relationships primary, even over sacred ordinance |
| Love your enemies, bless those who curse you | [3 Nephi 12:44](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/12?lang=eng&id=p44#p44) | Eliminates categories of "other" |
| The Golden Rule | [3 Nephi 14:12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/14?lang=eng&id=p12#p12) | The social code of Zion — "all things whatsoever ye would that men should do to you, do ye even so to them" |
| Don't forbid anyone from coming | [3 Nephi 18:22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/18?lang=eng&id=p22#p22) | Open doors. Zion has no gates facing out. |
| Don't cast out the unworthy — minister to them | [3 Nephi 18:30–32](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/18?lang=eng&id=p30-p32#p30) | Even those who are "not worthy" are never expelled. Pray for them. Be the means of their salvation. |
| "Blessed are ye if ye have no disputations among you" | [3 Nephi 18:34](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/18?lang=eng&id=p34#p34) | The positive restatement: not just absence of contention, but active peace |
| "That we may be one" | [3 Nephi 19:23](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/19?lang=eng&id=p23#p23) | Jesus' intercessory prayer — identical to John 17. Unity with each other through unity with God. |

The result, in 4 Nephi's words: "There was no contention in the land, because of the love of God which did dwell in the hearts of the people" ([4 Nephi 1:15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/4-ne/1?lang=eng&id=p15#p15)).

Not because of a law against contention. Because of *love*.

### Pillar 2: One Mind — Unified in Doctrine and the Holy Ghost

Jesus doesn't establish a committee. He establishes a *shared understanding* — His doctrine, clearly stated so there can be no misunderstanding:

> "And this is my doctrine, and it is the doctrine which the Father hath given unto me ... that the Father, and the Son, and the Holy Ghost are one; and I bear record of the Father, and the Father beareth record of me, and the Holy Ghost beareth record of the Father and me." — [3 Nephi 11:32](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/11?lang=eng&id=p32#p32)

Then:

| Teaching | Reference | What It Does |
|----------|-----------|--------------|
| Faith, repentance, baptism, Holy Ghost | [3 Nephi 11:32–40](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/11?lang=eng&id=p32-p40#p32) | The shared foundation — no more, no less |
| "Whoso buildeth upon this buildeth upon my rock" | [3 Nephi 11:39](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/11?lang=eng&id=p39#p39) | Stability comes from shared doctrinal ground |
| "Filled with the Holy Ghost" | [3 Nephi 12:6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/12?lang=eng&id=p6#p6) | The Nephite Beatitude adds this — it's the mechanism of oneness |
| Sacrament: "always remember me → have my Spirit" | [3 Nephi 18:7, 11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/18?lang=eng&id=p7#p7) | The weekly renewal that keeps mind unified |
| They desired the Holy Ghost above all | [3 Nephi 19:9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/19?lang=eng&id=p9#p9) | "They did pray for that which they most desired" — and it was the Spirit |
| "This is my gospel" — the gospel defined | [3 Nephi 27:13–21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/27?lang=eng&id=p13-p21#p13) | Jesus summarizes His purpose: He came to do the Father's will, was lifted up, draws all to Him, judges by works |
| "What manner of men ought ye to be? Even as I am" | [3 Nephi 27:27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/27?lang=eng&id=p27#p27) | The standard is not a rule — it is a *Person* |

Notice something stunning about Jesus' intercessory prayer. Three times He goes apart and prays to the Father ([3 Nephi 19:19, 27, 31](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/19?lang=eng&id=p19#p19)). The content of the prayer, as far as it can be recorded:

> "Father, I pray thee that thou wilt give the Holy Ghost unto all them that shall believe in their words." — [3 Nephi 19:21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/19?lang=eng&id=p21#p21)

> "Father, I pray not for the world, but for those whom thou hast given me out of the world, because of their faith, that they may be purified in me, that I may be in them as thou, Father, art in me, that we may be one, that I may be glorified in them." — [3 Nephi 19:29](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/19?lang=eng&id=p29#p29)

The mechanism of "one mind" is not enforced conformity. It is the Holy Ghost dwelling in each person, connecting them to Christ, connecting them to each other. The unity is *relational*, not institutional.

### Pillar 3: Dwelt in Righteousness — Character, Not Compliance

The Sermon at the Temple (3 Nephi 12–14) is not a checklist. It is a *character portrait* — the inner life of a Zion person:

**The Beatitudes as Zion Citizenship** ([3 Nephi 12:3–12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/12?lang=eng&id=p3-p12#p3)):

| Beatitude | Zion Application |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Poor in spirit *who come unto me* | Humility — not self-sufficiency but God-dependence |
| They that mourn | Capacity to grieve with others (like Enoch weeping with God) |
| The meek | Strength under divine control, not passivity |
| Hunger and thirst after righteousness | Desire as the engine — you *want* this |
| The merciful | Giving what others don't deserve, because you received it yourself |
| The pure in heart — "they shall see God" | Zion IS the pure in heart ([D&C 97:21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/97?lang=eng&id=p21#p21)) |
| The peacemakers — "children of God" | The active builders of Zion's peace |
| Persecuted for my name's sake | The cost of Zion in a Babylon world |

Then the higher law — not the abolition of commandments but their *internalization*:

> "Ye have heard that it hath been said by them of old time, and it is also written before you ... But I say unto you ..." — [3 Nephi 12:21–22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/12?lang=eng&id=p21-p22#p21)

The old law said: don't kill. The Zion law: don't even be angry without cause. The old law said: don't commit adultery. The Zion law: don't even look with lust. The shift is from behavior to *being*. You don't just act right — you *become* right.

And the capstone:

> "Therefore I would that ye should be perfect even as I, or your Father who is in heaven is perfect." — [3 Nephi 12:48](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/12?lang=eng&id=p48#p48)

Note: In the Sermon on the Mount (Matthew 5:48), Jesus says "Be ye therefore perfect, even as your Father which is in heaven is perfect." But in 3 Nephi, *after the Atonement and Resurrection*, He adds Himself: "even as I, or your Father." He had completed His mortal journey. He could now include Himself in the standard. The invitation is not perfection as a demand — it is perfection as a destination you walk toward.

The inner disciplines that sustain this character:

- **Give, pray, and fast in secret** ([3 Nephi 13:1–18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/13?lang=eng&id=p1-p18#p1)) — righteousness is between you and God, not for display
- **The single eye** ([3 Nephi 13:22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/13?lang=eng&id=p22#p22)) — undivided devotion
- **Watch and pray always** ([3 Nephi 18:15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/18?lang=eng&id=p15#p15)) — constant vigilance, not occasional effort
- **Pray in your families** ([3 Nephi 18:21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/18?lang=eng&id=p21#p21)) — Zion starts in the home
- **Build on the rock** ([3 Nephi 14:24–27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/14?lang=eng&id=p24-p27#p24)) — foundations determine survival

### Pillar 4: No Poor Among Them — The Economics of Zion

This is where Zion confronts consumption head-on. Jesus' economic teachings are radical and specific:

> "Lay not up for yourselves treasures upon earth, where moth and rust doth corrupt, and thieves break through and steal; but lay up for yourselves treasures in heaven." — [3 Nephi 13:19–20](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/13?lang=eng&id=p19-p20#p19)

> "No man can serve two masters ... Ye cannot serve God and Mammon." — [3 Nephi 13:24](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/13?lang=eng&id=p24#p24)

> "Give to him that asketh thee, and from him that would borrow of thee turn not thou away." — [3 Nephi 12:42](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/12?lang=eng&id=p42#p42)

> "Seek ye first the kingdom of God and his righteousness, and all these things shall be added unto you." — [3 Nephi 13:33](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/13?lang=eng&id=p33#p33)

And the result — told in the most stunning single verse in 3 Nephi:

> "And they taught, and did minister one to another; and they had **all things common** among them, every man dealing justly, one with another." — [3 Nephi 26:19](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/26?lang=eng&id=p19#p19)

This is the verse that bridges Jesus' teaching and 4 Nephi's Zion. It is the hinge. "All things common" is not communism — it is consecration. The difference: communism is "what's yours is mine." Consecration is "what's mine is the Lord's — and He asks me to share."

The Lord's diagnostic of the early Saints' failure to build Zion hammers this:

> "They have not learned to be obedient to the things which I required at their hands, but are full of all manner of evil, and do not impart of their substance, as becometh saints, to the poor and afflicted among them." — [D&C 105:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/105?lang=eng&id=p3#p3)

And the parable that cuts to the bone:

> "For what man among you having twelve sons, and is no respecter of them, and they serve him obediently, and he saith unto the one: Be thou clothed in robes and sit thou here; and to the other: Be thou clothed in rags and sit thou there — and looketh upon his sons and saith I am just?" — [D&C 38:26](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/38?lang=eng&id=p26#p26)

God doesn't dress some children in robes and leave others in rags. If we do, we are not like Him.

### The Heart of the Teaching: Jesus Himself (3 Nephi 17)

If there is one chapter that captures the *spirit* of what built Zion, it is 3 Nephi 17. This chapter is not doctrine — it is *demonstration*. Jesus models every Zion principle in His own person:

He is about to leave. The people are in tears. He sees them looking at Him, wanting Him to stay. And He says:

> "Behold, my bowels are filled with compassion towards you." — [3 Nephi 17:6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/17?lang=eng&id=p6#p6)

He heals every sick, lame, blind, deaf, and afflicted person — "every one as they were brought forth unto him" ([3 Nephi 17:9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/17?lang=eng&id=p9#p9)). No one turned away.

He commands the children brought to Him. He kneels. He prays words that "cannot be written" and that "no tongue can speak, neither can there be written by any man, neither can the hearts of men conceive so great and marvelous things" ([3 Nephi 17:15–17](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/17?lang=eng&id=p15-p17#p15)).

And then: "My joy is full" ([3 Nephi 17:20](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/17?lang=eng&id=p20#p20)). And He wept. And He took the children one by one and blessed them. And He wept again.

And angels came down and encircled the little ones with fire.

This is the spirit that built Zion. Not a program. Not an economic policy. **A Person who wept over children and healed everyone who came.** The Nephites built Zion because they had *seen* what it looks like — and the image burned in them for 200 years.

## III. Enoch's 365 Years: Is It a Metaphor?

> "And all the days of Zion, in the days of Enoch, were three hundred and sixty-five years." — [Moses 7:68](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/7?lang=eng&id=p68#p68)

> "Zion, in process of time, was taken up into heaven." — [Moses 7:21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/7?lang=eng&id=p21#p21)

You asked whether 365 years is a metaphor for living each 365 days with the Lord. I think the answer is *yes — and more than a metaphor.*

365 is literal. It took that long. Enoch started as a hated, slow-of-speech young man standing on the hills, and it took 365 years of daily walking with God before his entire city was taken up.

But 365 is also a number that echoes the cycle of a year. There are 365 days in a year. Each year is a complete cycle — a full revolution around the sun. And each of those years, for 365 of them, Enoch and his people *walked with God*. The phrase from [D&C 107:49](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/107?lang=eng&id=p49#p49) says it simply: "He walked with God three hundred and sixty-five years."

Virginia H. Pearce saw this clearly:

> "The city of Zion was taken up into heaven '**in process of time.**' What a critical phrase! Zion will not be brought about by a single, miraculous event but by the accumulated acts and desires of the members of the community of Saints." — [Virginia H. Pearce, "Keep Walking, and Give Time a Chance," April 1997](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1997/04/keep-walking-and-give-time-a-chance?lang=eng)

The Hebrew word for "year" is *shanah* (שָׁנָה), which comes from a root meaning "to repeat, to do again." Each year is a *repetition* — another cycle of faithfulness. 365 years of Zion means 365 repetitions of the annual cycle of covenant living. 365 iterations of: plant, grow, harvest, rest, begin again. 365 times around the sun, walking with God.

So here is the insight: **You don't build Zion once. You build it every day, and then you do it again tomorrow. And the day after. And eventually — in process of time — what you have been doing each day becomes what you *are*.**

Enoch didn't wake up one morning in a perfected city. He woke up 133,225 mornings in a *becoming* city. Each day was a choice. Each day was a walk.

That's your daily Zion: one day at a time, 365 days at a time, as many years as it takes.

## IV. The Result: What Zion Looks Like (4 Nephi)

After Jesus' visit, the Nephites did *exactly* what He taught. And 4 Nephi gives us the clearest picture of what Zion actually produces:

> "The people were all converted unto the Lord, upon all the face of the land, both Nephites and Lamanites, and there were no contentions and disputations among them, and every man did deal justly one with another." — [4 Nephi 1:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/4-ne/1?lang=eng&id=p2#p2)

> "And they had all things common among them; therefore there were not rich and poor, bond and free, but they were all made free, and partakers of the heavenly gift." — [4 Nephi 1:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/4-ne/1?lang=eng&id=p3#p3)

> "There was no contention in the land, because of the love of God which did dwell in the hearts of the people." — [4 Nephi 1:15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/4-ne/1?lang=eng&id=p15#p15)

> "There were no envyings, nor strifes, nor tumults, nor whoredoms, nor lyings, nor murders, nor any manner of lasciviousness; and surely there could not be a **happier people** among all the people who had been created by the hand of God." — [4 Nephi 1:16](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/4-ne/1?lang=eng&id=p16#p16)

> "There were no robbers, nor murderers, neither were there Lamanites, nor any manner of -ites; but they were in **one**, the children of Christ, and heirs to the kingdom of God." — [4 Nephi 1:17](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/4-ne/1?lang=eng&id=p17#p17)

That phrase — "no manner of -ites" — is the death of tribalism. No Nephites, no Lamanites, no political parties, no economic classes, no us-and-them. Just *one*. Children of Christ.

### What They Did Daily

4 Nephi 1:12 tells us: "They did walk after the commandments which they had received from their Lord and their God, continuing in **fasting** and **prayer**, and in **meeting together oft** both to pray and to hear the word of the Lord."

Three daily practices:
1. **Fasting** — regular self-discipline, with the surplus going to others
2. **Prayer** — constant connection to the source of love
3. **Meeting together oft** — community, not isolation

It also tells us what they *stopped* doing: "They did not walk any more after the performances and ordinances of the law of Moses" ([4 Nephi 1:12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/4-ne/1?lang=eng&id=p12#p12)). They moved from compliance to covenant. From the lesser law to the higher law Jesus had taught them.

### How It Fell

Zion fell in 4 Nephi exactly as it fell in every other dispensation. The sequence is surgical:

1. **Prosperity** — "They had become exceedingly rich" ([4 Nephi 1:23](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/4-ne/1?lang=eng&id=p23#p23))
2. **Pride** — "Lifted up in pride, such as the wearing of costly apparel" ([4 Nephi 1:24](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/4-ne/1?lang=eng&id=p24#p24))
3. **End of consecration** — "They did have their goods and their substance no more common among them" ([4 Nephi 1:25](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/4-ne/1?lang=eng&id=p25#p25))
4. **Division into classes** — "They began to be divided into classes" ([4 Nephi 1:26](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/4-ne/1?lang=eng&id=p26#p26))
5. **Churches for gain** — "Build up churches unto themselves to get gain" ([4 Nephi 1:26](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/4-ne/1?lang=eng&id=p26#p26))
6. **Denial of Christ** — "They did deny the more parts of his gospel" ([4 Nephi 1:27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/4-ne/1?lang=eng&id=p27#p27))
7. **Return of tribalism** — "There began to be Lamanites again in the land" ([4 Nephi 1:20](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/4-ne/1?lang=eng&id=p20#p20))
8. **Secret combinations** — "Build up the secret oaths and combinations of Gadianton" ([4 Nephi 1:42](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/4-ne/1?lang=eng&id=p42#p42))

This is the consumption decreed in miniature. Self-destruction through self-indulgence. The city that Jesus built with His own tears, torn apart by its own children within four generations.

As Jesus Himself had said, with sorrow: "It sorroweth me because of the fourth generation from this generation, for they are led away captive by him even as was the son of perdition; for they will sell me for silver and for gold, and for that which moth doth corrupt and which thieves can break through and steal" ([3 Nephi 27:32](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/27?lang=eng&id=p32#p32)). He knew. He wept. He built Zion among them anyway.

| **Economic practice** | "No poor among them" ([Moses 7:18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/7?lang=eng&id=p18#p18)) | "All things common" ([4 Nephi 1:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/4-ne/1?lang=eng&id=p3#p3)) |

| **End** | Taken up to God ([Moses 7:69](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/7?lang=eng&id=p69#p69)) | Fell to pride and consumption ([4 Nephi 1:24–46](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/4-ne/1?lang=eng&id=p24-p46#p24)) |
| **Key figure** | Enoch — who wept with God ([Moses 7:41](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/7?lang=eng&id=p41#p41)) | Jesus — who wept over children ([3 Nephi 17:21–22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/17?lang=eng&id=p21-p22#p21)) |

The common thread: **both Zions were built by someone who wept.** Enoch's heart "swelled wide as eternity" as he entered God's grief. Jesus' "bowels were filled with compassion" as He looked at His people. Zion is not built by efficiency or organization. It is built by people whose hearts break open wide enough to hold each other.

## VI. Every Day Zion: What We Can Do Today

Elder Christofferson stated the principle precisely:

> "We cannot wait until Zion comes for these things to happen — Zion will come only as they happen." — [Elder D. Todd Christofferson, "Come to Zion," October 2008](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2008/10/come-to-zion?lang=eng)

President Kimball connected it to the scriptural pattern:

> "In Fourth Nephi we witness the blessings of the Nephites as they subdue selfishness and prosper in perfect righteousness for four generations. Who does not thrill to this picture of the ideal of Zion?" — [President Spencer W. Kimball, "Welfare Services: The Gospel in Action," October 1977](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1977/10/welfare-services-the-gospel-in-action?lang=eng)

Zion is not a someday project. It is a *today* practice. Here is what the scriptures actually tell us to do, distilled from every source in this study into daily, weekly, and ongoing rhythms:

## VII. Becoming: Where This Lands

The three Nephites asked to tarry because they "desired that ye might bring the souls of men unto me, while the world shall stand" ([3 Nephi 28:9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/28?lang=eng&id=p9#p9)). Jesus said they were "more blessed" for this desire ([3 Nephi 28:7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/28?lang=eng&id=p7#p7)). Their fulness of joy, He said, would be "even as the Father hath given me fulness of joy; and ye shall be even as I am" ([3 Nephi 28:10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/28?lang=eng&id=p10#p10)).

Even as I am.

That is the Zion invitation. Not "do what I say." **Be what I am.**

A person who weeps with the suffering. A person who heals everyone who comes. A person who takes children one by one and blesses them individually. A person who cannot serve God and Mammon, and chooses God. A person who has no poor — not because of a program, but because they can't stand the thought of having while another lacks.

This study started because consumption is destroying nations — their own consumption, from within. And Zion is the cure. Not a distant future Zion waiting on a calendar. The Zion you build every morning when you wake up and choose to walk with God, deal justly with every person you meet, share what you have, refuse to contend, and let the love of God dwell in your heart.

365 days, starting today:

1. **This morning prayer:** "Father, show me how to build Zion today."
2. **This conversation:** Treat the next person I speak to as a child of Christ, not an -ite.
3. **This meal:** Is there someone who should be at my table who isn't?
4. **This frustration:** Is this the spirit of contention? Then it's not of Him.
5. **This paycheck:** What portion returns to the Lord through His children?
6. **This evening:** Did I walk with Him today? Did I let His love dwell in my heart?
7. **This sleep:** "Prepare your minds for the morrow, and I come unto you again" ([3 Nephi 17:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/17?lang=eng&id=p3#p3)).

Then wake up. Do it again. In process of time.
