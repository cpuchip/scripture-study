# Abinadi — How to Read Scripture

*Study date: March 3, 2026*
*Born from: the endtimes-servant studies, where Abinadi's method in Mosiah 15 kept surfacing as the key to multi-referent reading*
*Study mode: study-exp1 (phased writing with externalized memory)*

---

## The Question

Abinadi walks into a room of priests who know Isaiah by heart — they *quote* it at him ([Mosiah 12:21-24](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/12?lang=eng&id=p21-p24#p21)) — and he tells them they don't understand it. Then he demonstrates what understanding looks like.

But what interests me isn't just *what* Abinadi teaches. It's **how he reads**. His method itself is a model for scripture study:
- He confronts people who have the text but miss the meaning
- He holds multiple referents simultaneously (Father and Son, past and future, Christ and his seed)
- He reads backward (prophecy through fulfillment) and forward (type through antitype)
- He connects disparate passages into a unified theological argument
- He dies for the reading — and the single convert he produces (Alma) changes everything

**The questions this study asks:**
1. What is Abinadi's reading method? Can we articulate it as a hermeneutic?
2. Why couldn't the priests see what he saw? What blocked their reading?
3. How does Abinadi's method compare to how Christ reads scripture?
4. What does Abinadi's method teach us about reading scripture today?

---

## I. The Setting — Who Is Abinadi Reading To?

To understand Abinadi's hermeneutic, you have to understand his audience. He isn't teaching a class. He's speaking to men who were *chosen for their inability to see*.

King Noah didn't merely corrupt the existing priesthood — he **replaced** it:

> "For he put down all the priests that had been consecrated by his father, and consecrated new ones in their stead, such as were lifted up in the pride of their hearts." ([Mosiah 11:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/11?lang=eng&id=p5#p5))

He selected specifically for pride. Then he built a physical environment that reinforced the spiritual architecture:

> "The seats which were set apart for the high priests, which were above all the other seats, he did ornament with pure gold; and he caused a breastwork to be built before them, that they might rest their bodies and their arms upon while they should speak lying and vain words to his people." ([Mosiah 11:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/11?lang=eng&id=p11#p11))

Gold seats. Elevated position. A breastwork to lean against — comfortable while lying. Even the furniture enables priestcraft.

The economic structure is equally revealing. Noah taxed the people at a fifth of all they possessed to support the priests' lifestyle ([Mosiah 11:3-6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/11?lang=eng&id=p3-p6#p3)). This isn't metaphorical corruption — it's a taxation system designed to fund interpretive blindness. When your salary depends on people accepting your reading, you cannot afford to see what the text actually says.

And the fruit? The text diagnoses it precisely:

> "The eyes of the people were blinded; therefore they hardened their hearts against the words of Abinadi." ([Mosiah 11:29](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/11?lang=eng&id=p29#p29))

Note the sequence: **blinded → hardened**. The blindness came first from flattering words ([Mosiah 11:7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/11?lang=eng&id=p7#p7)). Then the hardening was their own response when truth arrived. The priests created an environment where truth became impossible to receive.

This is the room Abinadi walks into. Not a room of ignorance — a room of *curated inability*.

## II. The Challenge — "What Teach Ye This People?"

When the priests have Abinadi before them, they don't ask him to teach. They interrogate:

> "They began to question him, that they might cross him, that thereby they might have wherewith to accuse him; but he answered them boldly, and withstood all their questions, yea, to their astonishment." ([Mosiah 12:19](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/12?lang=eng&id=p19#p19))

Their purpose is explicitly accusation, not understanding. They use scripture as a test question, not as a mirror. And the passage they choose is telling — Isaiah 52:7-10, the passage about publishing peace:

> "How beautiful upon the mountains are the feet of him that bringeth good tidings; that publisheth peace; that bringeth good tidings of good; that publisheth salvation; that saith unto Zion, Thy God reigneth." ([Mosiah 12:21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/12?lang=eng&id=p21#p21), quoting [Isaiah 52:7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/isa/52?lang=eng&id=p7#p7))

They know the words perfectly. They can quote them from memory. But they can't connect the passage to Christ, or to their own failure to publish peace. The irony is devastating — men who speak "lying and vain words" ([Mosiah 11:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/11?lang=eng&id=p11#p11)) ask about the passage that describes speaking truth.

Abinadi's response cuts to the heart of the problem:

> "Are you priests, and pretend to teach this people, and to understand the spirit of prophesying, and yet desire to know of me what these things mean?" ([Mosiah 12:25](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/12?lang=eng&id=p25#p25))

And then the hermeneutic indictment that frames everything that follows:

> "Ye have not applied your hearts to understanding; therefore, ye have not been wise. Therefore, what teach ye this people?" ([Mosiah 12:27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/12?lang=eng&id=p27#p27))

Not applied your **minds** — your **hearts**. Understanding, for Abinadi, is not intellectual. It's dispositional. The heart that is set on riches and whoredoms cannot read scripture. The moral condition of the reader determines what the text reveals.

This is Abinadi's first principle: **the reader's heart is a hermeneutic instrument.** A corrupt heart produces a corrupt reading, no matter how well you know the words.

## III. The Foundation — The Law Points to Christ

Before interpreting Isaiah, Abinadi lays a foundation. And his method of doing so is itself revealing.

His face shines:

> "His face shone with exceeding luster, even as Moses' did while in the mount of Sinai, while speaking with the Lord." ([Mosiah 13:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p5#p5))

Moses' face shone when *receiving* the law. Abinadi's face shines when *interpreting* the law. The parallel implies something startling: correct interpretation of scripture is a revelatory act on the same order as receiving it.

Then he does something that seems like a detour but isn't — he recites the Ten Commandments to priests who supposedly teach them. Why? Because:

> "I perceive that they are not written in your hearts; I perceive that ye have studied and taught iniquity the most part of your lives." ([Mosiah 13:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p11#p11))

They teach what they haven't internalized. The irony of reading the commandments aloud to professional religion teachers is devastating. And it proves his hermeneutic point: knowing the words is not the same as having them written in your heart.

Then the typological principle, stated explicitly:

> "All these things were types of things to come." ([Mosiah 13:31](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p31#p31))

This is Abinadi's second principle: **the law is a type pointing to Christ.** Every performance, every ordinance, every commandment — a shadow of things to come. A reader who doesn't see Christ in the law hasn't understood the law. The priests said "we teach the law of Moses" and "salvation cometh by the law of Moses" ([Mosiah 12:28, 32](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/12?lang=eng&id=p28#p28)). They treated the shadow as the substance.

But why couldn't they see it? Here the text is direct:

> "Did they understand the law? I say unto you, Nay, they did not all understand the law; and this because of the hardness of their hearts; for they understood not that there could not any man be saved except it were through the redemption of God." ([Mosiah 13:32](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p32#p32))

Hardness of hearts → lack of understanding. The interpretive failure is moral, not intellectual.

And then the scope claim:

> "Even all the prophets who have prophesied ever since the world began — have they not spoken more or less concerning these things?" ([Mosiah 13:33](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p33#p33))

ALL prophets. Since the world BEGAN. Not progressive revelation building toward Christ — testimony of Christ from the very first moment. This connects to the [gospel taught to Adam](moses-6-gospel-to-adam.md) in Moses 6 — the same principle, the same scope.

And woven through the foundation is a line that stops me cold every time I read it:

> "But this much I tell you, what you do with me, after this, shall be as a type and a shadow of things which are to come." ([Mosiah 13:10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p10#p10))

Abinadi reads his **own death** typologically. He applies the same hermeneutic to his life that he applies to Isaiah. His execution will shadow Christ's. He isn't just teaching the method — he's **living inside it**. (This verse, remarkably, has zero citations in the BYU Scripture Citation Index. The moment where Abinadi turns the lens on himself is the moment the scholarly tradition doesn't talk about.)

## IV. The Reading — Isaiah 53 Through Abinadi's Eyes

Now the reading itself. Abinadi quotes Isaiah 53 in its entirety ([Mosiah 14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/14?lang=eng)) and then interprets it ([Mosiah 15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng)). The structure matters: **full text first, then interpretation**. He doesn't cherry-pick verses. He presents the whole passage, then demonstrates what reading it actually demands.

### The First Move: Who Is the Servant?

> "God himself shall come down among the children of men, and shall redeem his people." ([Mosiah 15:1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p1#p1))

Isaiah's suffering servant IS God himself. Not a prophet, not a representative — God. This is the starting point. Everything else follows from this identification.

### The Father-Son Christology

What follows is the most complex Christological passage in the Book of Mormon:

> "Because he dwelleth in flesh he shall be called the Son of God, and having subjected the flesh to the will of the Father, being the Father and the Son — The Father, because he was conceived by the power of God; and the Son, because of the flesh; thus becoming the Father and Son — And they are one God, yea, the very Eternal Father of heaven and of earth." ([Mosiah 15:2-4](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p2-p4#p2))

Father and Son simultaneously, one and distinct. One by nature (both God), distinct by function (Father = divine power, Son = mortal flesh). This isn't modalism or trinitarianism — it's a formulation that requires holding two truths at once. And it IS the multi-referent reading applied to Christology itself: the same being is both Father and Son depending on which aspect you're reading.

### The Hinge: "Who Shall Declare His Generation?"

> "And now I say unto you, who shall declare his generation? Behold, I say unto you, that when his soul has been made an offering for sin he shall see his seed. And now what say ye? And who shall be his seed?" ([Mosiah 15:10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p10#p10))

This is the hinge of the entire argument. Isaiah 53:8 asks "who shall declare his generation?" and Isaiah 53:10 says "he shall see his seed." In Webster's 1828 dictionary, "declare" means "to make clear; to free from obscurity." Abinadi is asking: *who will make clear the meaning of his offspring?* And then he answers his own question. He **declares** it — he makes it clear.

### The Seed Redefined

> "Whosoever has heard the words of the prophets, yea, all the holy prophets who have prophesied concerning the coming of the Lord — I say unto you, that all those who have hearkened unto their words, and believed that the Lord would redeem his people, and have looked forward to that day for a remission of their sins, I say unto you, that these are his seed, or they are the heirs of the kingdom of God." ([Mosiah 15:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p11#p11))

Christ's "seed" = those who hear the prophets AND believe AND look forward for remission. Three conditions. Not biological descent. Not national identity. Covenantal belonging through faith. Webster's 1828 defines "seed" both as "progeny/offspring" and as "that from which anything springs; first principle; original." Abinadi redefines seed from genealogy to origin — Christ's seed are anyone in whom his Atonement takes root and grows.

The logic is beautifully circular:

> "For these are they whose sins he has borne; these are they for whom he has died, to redeem them from their transgressions. And now, are they not his seed?" ([Mosiah 15:12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p12#p12))

He bore THEIR sins → they are HIS seed. The Atonement creates the family. You become Christ's child by being the one he redeemed. And the prophets themselves are included:

> "Yea, and are not the prophets, every one that has opened his mouth to prophesy, that has not fallen into transgression, I mean all the holy prophets ever since the world began? I say unto you that they are his seed." ([Mosiah 15:13](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p13#p13))

The people who *declare* Christ *belong* to Christ. The readers become the reading.

### The Ring Closes

And then comes the moment that reveals the architectural genius of the entire speech:

> "And these are they who have published peace, who have brought good tidings of good, who have published salvation; and said unto Zion: Thy God reigneth!" ([Mosiah 15:14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p14#p14))

**He answers the priests' original question.** They quoted Isaiah 52:7-10 in [Mosiah 12:21-24](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/12?lang=eng&id=p21-p24#p21) — "How beautiful upon the mountains are the feet of him that bringeth good tidings..." Now Abinadi tells them who those people ARE: the prophets who declare Christ. The whole speech — from the hermeneutic indictment in chapter 12 through the typology of chapter 13, the full quotation of Isaiah 53 in chapter 14, and the Christological reading of chapter 15 — is one unified answer to their test question.

The speech is a **ring composition**: question → foundation → text → reading → answer to the original question.

And Abinadi doesn't stop at one referent. He layers the "beautiful feet" across time:

> "And again, how beautiful upon the mountains are the feet of those that are still publishing peace!" ([Mosiah 15:16](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p16#p16))

> "And again, how beautiful upon the mountains are the feet of those who shall hereafter publish peace, yea, from this time henceforth and forever!" ([Mosiah 15:17](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p17#p17))

> "For O how beautiful upon the mountains are the feet of him that bringeth good tidings, that is the founder of peace, yea, even the Lord, who has redeemed his people." ([Mosiah 15:18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p18#p18))

Past prophets (v.15). Present publishers (v.16). Future publishers (v.17). And Christ himself, the founder of peace (v.18). One Isaiah passage. Multiple fulfillments across all time. This is the multi-referent reading at its most explicit — not one person, not one era, but everyone who publishes peace IS the fulfillment.

(Remarkably, [Mosiah 15:14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p14#p14) — the structural keystone that completes the ring — has **zero** citations in the BYU Scripture Citation Index. The tradition largely misses the architectural brilliance of this moment.)

## V. The Method — Abinadi's Hermeneutic Articulated

Drawing from the speech, Abinadi's reading method rests on these principles:

**1. The reader's heart is a hermeneutic instrument.** "Ye have not applied your hearts to understanding" ([Mosiah 12:27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/12?lang=eng&id=p27#p27)). Understanding is dispositional, not merely intellectual. Hardness of heart → blindness to meaning ([Mosiah 13:32](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p32#p32)). The moral condition of the reader determines what the text reveals.

**2. Everything is a type of Christ.** "All these things were types of things to come" ([Mosiah 13:31](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p31#p31)). The law, the ordinances, the prophets — all point forward to Christ. A reading that doesn't find Christ hasn't finished reading.

**3. Multi-referent reading is the text's own method.** Isaiah 52:7-10 refers simultaneously to past prophets, present believers, future publishers, and Christ himself ([Mosiah 15:14-18](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p14-p18#p14)). The text is not a single-referent puzzle to be decoded. It is a layered witness that speaks to every era.

**4. Correct interpretation is a revelatory act.** Abinadi's face shone like Moses' ([Mosiah 13:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p5#p5)). Moses received the law on Sinai; Abinadi interprets it before the priests. The implication: the Spirit of revelation operates in reading, not only in receiving.

**5. The reader must be transformed by the reading.** Abinadi reads his own death typologically ([Mosiah 13:10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p10#p10)). He doesn't just interpret the text — he *lives* inside the interpretation. The goal is not knowledge but transformation. Scripture reading that produces information without conversion is the priests' method, not Abinadi's.

Compare these principles with what the priests practice. They know the words. They can quote Isaiah. But they read for **vindication** — to support a system that pays their salary — rather than for **transformation**. Their hermeneutic is self-serving; Abinadi's is self-sacrificing. The text goes dark for readers whose interest is in keeping it comfortable.

## VI. "What Teach Ye?" — The Anti-Hermeneutic of Priestcraft

If Abinadi's method is a hermeneutic, Noah's court practices an anti-hermeneutic. Understanding *why* the priests couldn't see illuminates what it takes to see.

The architecture of their blindness:

1. **Selection for pride** — Noah chose priests who were "lifted up in the pride of their hearts" ([Mosiah 11:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/11?lang=eng&id=p5#p5)). The interpretive community was curated for inability.

2. **Economic dependency** — The priests depended on the treasury, which depended on the people's labor, which depended on the people's acceptance of the system ([Mosiah 11:6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/11?lang=eng&id=p6#p6)). When your livelihood depends on a particular reading, you literally cannot afford a different one.

3. **Physical reinforcement** — Gold seats, elevated position, a comfortable breastwork ([Mosiah 11:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/11?lang=eng&id=p11#p11)). The environment itself announced that these men were above the people, not among them.

4. **Flattery over truth** — "They were lifted up in the pride of their hearts... speaking lying and vain words to his people" ([Mosiah 11:7, 11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/11?lang=eng&id=p7#p7)). The words they spoke blinded the people, who then hardened their hearts against truth when it arrived.

5. **Accusation instead of inquiry** — When Abinadi spoke, they "began to question him, that they might cross him, that thereby they might have wherewith to accuse him" ([Mosiah 12:19](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/12?lang=eng&id=p19#p19)). They used scripture as a weapon, not a mirror.

The result? They said: "We teach the law of Moses... salvation cometh by the law of Moses" ([Mosiah 12:28, 32](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/12?lang=eng&id=p28#p28)). The law was the end, not the means. They taught the shadow as the substance because the substance — Christ — would have demanded they change.

And when the hermeneutic they couldn't tolerate was delivered anyway, the priests responded with violence. Noah was ready to release Abinadi ([Mosiah 17:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/17?lang=eng&id=p11#p11)). It was the **priests** who forced the execution:

> "But the priests lifted up their voices against him, and began to accuse him." ([Mosiah 17:12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/17?lang=eng&id=p12#p12))

The accusation IS the doctrine:

> "Abinadi, we have found an accusation against thee, and thou art worthy of death. For thou hast said that God himself should come down among the children of men." ([Mosiah 17:7-8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/17?lang=eng&id=p7-p8#p7))

They condemned him for saying "God himself shall come down." The very truth he taught became the legal basis for his execution. Challenged reading leads to violence when the reader has power and pride. The hermeneutic that saves is the hermeneutic that kills the one who delivers it.

This connects to our [earlier study on priestcraft](priestcraft.md) — the pattern of teaching for riches and popular acclaim rather than for the welfare of Zion. Noah's court is the defining case.

## VII. The Fruit — One Convert, Everything Changes

Abinadi's speech produces exactly one convert:

> "There was one among them whose name was Alma, he also being a descendant of Nephi. And he was a young man, and he believed the words which Abinadi had spoken, for he knew concerning the iniquity which Abinadi had testified against them." ([Mosiah 17:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/17?lang=eng&id=p2#p2))

Alma believed because he **already knew** the iniquity was real. He wasn't more intelligent than the other priests — he was more honest. The prerequisite for receiving Abinadi's reading was moral honesty about what he already knew. His heart was applied to understanding because it wasn't invested in the lie.

Alma's first act is preservation:

> "He being concealed for many days did write all the words which Abinadi had spoken." ([Mosiah 17:4](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/17?lang=eng&id=p4#p4))

Without this, Abinadi's hermeneutic disappears with his death. The writing is what makes everything after possible — an act of textual preservation that parallels how the scripture tradition itself works. Someone receives, someone writes it down, someone teaches from those words.

And what does Alma do with what he received?

> "Alma, who had fled from the servants of king Noah, repented of his sins and iniquities, and went about privately among the people, and began to teach the words of Abinadi." ([Mosiah 18:1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/18?lang=eng&id=p1#p1))

"The words of Abinadi." Not his own words. Alma's first ministry is pure transmission — passing on another man's reading. The hermeneutic is reproducible.

And the community it produces is the structural opposite of Noah's court. The baptismal covenant born from Abinadi's teaching is entirely relational:

> "Willing to bear one another's burdens, that they may be light; yea, and are willing to mourn with those that mourn; yea, and comfort those that stand in need of comfort, and to stand as witnesses of God at all times." ([Mosiah 18:8-9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/18?lang=eng&id=p8-p9#p8))

Bearing. Mourning. Comforting. Witnessing. Not doctrinal performance. Not law-keeping as an end. The fruit of correct reading is covenantal relationship — people who carry each other, not people who win arguments.

And the economics flip completely:

> "The priests were not to depend upon the people for their support; but for their labor they were to receive the grace of God, that they might wax strong in the Spirit, having the knowledge of God, that they might teach with power and authority from God." ([Mosiah 18:26](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/18?lang=eng&id=p26#p26))

Noah's priests ate from taxation. Alma's priests labor with their own hands ([Mosiah 18:24](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/18?lang=eng&id=p24#p24)). The reward isn't salary — it's "the grace of God" and "power and authority from God." Abinadi's critique of priestcraft produces a concrete alternative structure.

The chain doesn't stop with Alma. Seventy-four years later, Alma the Younger — in the crisis of his conversion — is saved by a memory:

> "I remembered also to have heard my father prophesy unto the people concerning the coming of one Jesus Christ, a Son of God, to atone for the sins of the world." ([Alma 36:17](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/36?lang=eng&id=p17#p17))

His father. Who taught the words of Abinadi. Who read Isaiah 53 in a courtroom of hostile priests. The chain: **Abinadi reads Isaiah → preaches Christ → Alma writes it down → Alma teaches it → Alma the Younger remembers it when nothing else can reach him.** One reading of Isaiah 53, delivered to men who wanted him dead, saves a man three generations later. And that man — Alma the Younger — becomes himself one of the "beautiful feet" who publish peace ([Alma 36:24](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/36?lang=eng&id=p24#p24)), fulfilling Abinadi's own interpretation of the passage.

## VIII. Abinadi and Christ — Parallel Readers

Abinadi isn't the only one who reads this way. The resurrected Christ uses the same method.

In 3 Nephi, Christ commands the Nephites:

> "Ye ought to search these things. Yea, a commandment I give unto you that ye search these things diligently; for great are the words of Isaiah." ([3 Nephi 23:1](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/23?lang=eng&id=p1#p1))

Search. Not read — **search**. Diligently. And what does the searching look like?

> "When Jesus had expounded all the scriptures in one, which they had written, he commanded them that they should teach the things which he had expounded unto them." ([3 Nephi 23:14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/23?lang=eng&id=p14#p14))

"Expounded all the scriptures **in one**." Webster's 1828 defines "expound" as "to lay open the meaning of; to clear of obscurity." Christ reads ALL the scriptures as a unified whole pointing to himself. This is exactly Abinadi's method — "even all the prophets who have prophesied ever since the world began — have they not spoken more or less concerning these things?" ([Mosiah 13:33](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p33#p33)). Christ unifies; the priests fragment.

On the Emmaus road in Luke, the same method appears:

> "O fools, and slow of heart to believe all that the prophets have spoken: Ought not Christ to have suffered these things, and to enter into his glory? And beginning at Moses and all the prophets, he expounded unto them in all the scriptures the things concerning himself." ([Luke 24:25-27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/luke/24?lang=eng&id=p25-p27#p25))

"Slow of **heart**" — not slow of mind. The same diagnosis Abinadi gives. And the same method: starting with Moses, moving through all the prophets, clearing the obscurity. Abinadi and Christ use identically structured approaches: begin with the law, encompass all the prophets, show Christ in everything.

And the response?

> "Did not our heart burn within us, while he talked with us by the way, and while he opened to us the scriptures?" ([Luke 24:32](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/luke/24?lang=eng&id=p32#p32))

The heart **burned**. The same organ that must be "applied" to understanding ([Mosiah 12:27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/12?lang=eng&id=p27#p27)), that must not be "hardened" ([Mosiah 13:32](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p32#p32)), is the organ that responds when scripture is faithfully read. Correct reading produces a felt response. The priests' hearts didn't burn because they were set on riches rather than applied to understanding.

Later, to the gathered disciples:

> "Then opened he their understanding, that they might understand the scriptures." ([Luke 24:45](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/luke/24?lang=eng&id=p45#p45))

Christ **opens** understanding. The verb is active — he does something TO them so they can see what was always there. This is what the shining face means in Mosiah 13:5. The Spirit that opens understanding was operating through Abinadi just as it operates through Christ. The method is the same because the Spirit is the same.

The parallel is not accidental. Abinadi is doing what Christ does because Abinadi is reading BY the Spirit of Christ. Which makes him, in the language of [Mosiah 15:13](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p13#p13), one of Christ's own seed — a prophet who declares his generation.

## IX. Reading With Abinadi Today

What does this mean for how we read?

**The tradition remembers Abinadi as a martyr.** A search of General Conference talks that reference Abinadi finds talks on his courage (Hales 2013, 2008), his willingness to testify under threat (Zwick 2008, Rasband 2019), his example of standing alone (Petersen 1972). These are true. But the tradition largely misses what may be his most enduring contribution: **his reading method**. We remember the man who died for truth more readily than we study HOW he found that truth in the text.

The BYU Scripture Citation Index reveals the same pattern. [Mosiah 15:11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p11#p11) — about Christ's seed — has five citations from conference talks (including Elder Andersen in 2022 and President Nelson in 2020). [Mosiah 15:14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15?lang=eng&id=p14#p14) — the structural keystone where Abinadi answers the priests' original question — has zero. The verse that proves the ring composition, that reveals the architectural genius of the speech, is uncited. [Mosiah 13:10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p10#p10) — where Abinadi reads his own death typologically — is also zero. The moments where the method is most visible are the moments the tradition doesn't talk about.

This isn't a criticism. It's a discovery. It means there's something here for us to find.

**Abinadi's principles for any reader:**

1. *Check your heart before you check the text.* Are you reading to find truth, or to confirm something? The priests' question was designed to accuse, not to learn. Our questions shape our reading.

2. *Look for Christ in everything.* Not as a forced spiritual overlay, but as Abinadi does — recognizing that the law, the prophets, the ordinances are all "types of things to come" ([Mosiah 13:31](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/13?lang=eng&id=p31#p31)). When a passage seems disconnected from Christ, the problem may be in the reading, not the text.

3. *Hold multiple referents.* Isaiah 52:7 isn't about one person in one era. Abinadi reads it as applying to past prophets, present believers, future generations, and Christ himself. Many of the deepest passages work the same way — they have a historical referent, a typological referent, and a personal referent simultaneously. (This is the principle we explored in the [endtimes-servant studies](endtimes-servant.md), where Isaiah's servant is simultaneously historical Israel, a future figure, and Christ.)

4. *Let the reading change you.* Abinadi didn't produce information — he produced a community ([Mosiah 18:8-9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/18?lang=eng&id=p8-p9#p8)) and a chain of salvation ([Alma 36:17](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/36?lang=eng&id=p17#p17)). If a reading doesn't land somewhere personal — if it produces knowledge without direction — it's the priests' method, not Abinadi's.

5. *Follow the footnotes.* In our endtimes-servant work, we discovered that scripture footnotes function as hyperlinks — they're connections handed to us by editors who've already done the cross-referencing. Abinadi himself follows the connections: Isaiah 52 leads to Isaiah 53 which leads to the Mosaic law which leads back to Isaiah 52. The reading is a web, not a line.

**A note on reading with AI.** There's an honest tension here. This study admires a method rooted in the reader's *heart* — and it's partly written by an AI that doesn't have one. The processing capacity, the cross-referencing, the ability to hold Mosiah 12 and Mosiah 15 side by side and see the ring composition — that's what the AI contributes. The heart-application — the willingness to be changed by what the text says, the moral honesty that Alma had and the priests lacked — that's the human reader's alone. The tool can surface the patterns. Only the person can let those patterns rewrite their life.

Abinadi would probably say that's fine. He never objected to tools. He objected to hearts that wouldn't yield.

## X. Becoming

Abinadi's hermeneutic asks a question I need to sit with: **Am I reading scripture to find Christ, or to find support for what I already think?**

The distance between Noah's priests and me is smaller than I'd like to admit. They knew the text. They could quote it. They taught it professionally. But their hearts were applied to other things — comfort, status, the approval of a corrupt system. When Abinadi showed them what the text actually demanded, they killed him rather than change.

I don't kill prophets. But I do sometimes skim past the demanding parts. I notice the clever cross-reference. I enjoy the intellectual architecture. And I move on without letting it rewrite anything.

Abinadi's example says: the reading isn't done until it costs you something. His face shone because the Spirit was in the interpretation. His death sealed it because his life was already inside it. He lived typologically — reading himself as part of the same story he was reading in Isaiah.

What would it look like to read that way?

It would look like Alma at the waters of Mormon — taking a reading and turning it into a covenant. Bearing burdens, mourning with mourners, comforting those who need comfort. Not just understanding the text, but becoming the kind of person the text describes.

The one thing I take from this: **Applied hearts. Not applied minds — applied hearts.** ([Mosiah 12:27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/12?lang=eng&id=p27#p27))

The next time I open a chapter, I want to ask Abinadi's question first: Is my heart in this? Or just my curiosity?
