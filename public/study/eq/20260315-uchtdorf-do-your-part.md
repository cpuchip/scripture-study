# Student Prep: "Do Your Part with All Your Heart"

**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Speaker:** Elder Dieter F. Uchtdorf, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles
**Talk:** [Do Your Part with All Your Heart](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2025/10/16uchtdorf?lang=eng) — October 2025 General Conference

---

## Talk Summary

Elder Uchtdorf uses a visit to a Lufthansa 747 flight simulator — decades after his career as a captain — to teach that **discipleship, like any skill, atrophies without practice**. The flight went fine, but what once felt like second nature now required total concentration.

He builds the talk around three movements:
1. **Discipleship takes discipline** — faith must be nourished daily or it fades
2. **You are gifted** — everyone has spiritual gifts, even if they aren't flashy
3. **Do your little part** — consistent small steps, not heroic leaps

## Key Scriptures to Be Ready to Discuss

### The Parable of the Talents — [Matthew 25:14–30](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/matt/25?lang=eng&id=p14-p30#p14)
The master gives talents "to every man according to his several ability" (v. 15). The two who used theirs doubled them. The one who buried his out of **fear** (v. 25) lost even what he had. Uchtdorf's reading: God gives gifts and expects us to *use and amplify* them. They don't multiply on a shelf.

Worth noting: **the servant who buried his talent didn't do anything wrong — he just didn't do anything.** The accusation wasn't wickedness, it was slothfulness (v. 26). That's the spiritual danger Uchtdorf is pointing at.

### Spiritual Gifts — [D&C 46:11–12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/46?lang=eng&id=p11-p12#p11)
> "There are many gifts, and to every man is given a gift by the Spirit of God. To some is given one, and to some is given another, that all may be profited thereby."

This is the direct quote Uchtdorf uses. The "that all may be profited thereby" is key — gifts aren't trophies, they're tools for blessing others.

### Moroni on Gifts — [Moroni 10:8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/10?lang=eng&id=p8#p8)
> "Deny not the gifts of God, for they are many; and they come from the same God."

Moroni echoes the same principle with urgency: the gifts come "different ways" but from the same God, and "they are given by the manifestations of the Spirit of God unto men, to profit them" (v. 8).

### The Seed of Faith — [Alma 32:37–43](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/32?lang=eng&id=p37-p43#p37)
Uchtdorf references this without quoting directly. Alma's message: if you nourish the seed "with great diligence, and with patience," it takes root and springs up to everlasting life (v. 41). But if you **neglect** it, "it will not get any root" — and the failure isn't the seed's fault, "it is because your ground is barren, and ye will not nourish the tree" (v. 39).

This connects to Uchtdorf's flight-simulator insight: the skill was real, but years of not practicing eroded it.

### The Light That Grows — [D&C 50:24](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/50?lang=eng&id=p24#p24)
> "That which is of God is light; and he that receiveth light, and continueth in God, receiveth more light; and that light groweth brighter and brighter until the perfect day."

Uchtdorf paraphrases this as the promise of becoming "refined, until that perfect day when we will be perfected in Christ." The engine is **continueth** — ongoing, not one-time.

### The Oath and Covenant — [D&C 84:38](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/84?lang=eng&id=p38#p38)
> "And he that receiveth my Father receiveth my Father's kingdom; therefore all that my Father hath shall be given unto him."

This is the closing promise of the talk. The blessings are within reach — but only if we do our part.

### Hidden Talent Warning — [D&C 60:2–3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/60?lang=eng&id=p2-p3#p2)
Uchtdorf cites this as a Restoration-era echo of the parable. The Lord chastises elders who "will not open their mouths, but they hide the talent which I have given unto them, because of the fear of man" (v. 2). The talent here is specifically the gospel and their testimony — hiding it is not neutral.

### By Small and Simple Things — [Alma 37:6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/37?lang=eng&id=p6#p6)
> "By small and simple things are great things brought to pass."

The capstone of the "do your little part" section. Don't wait for the grand gesture.

## Uchtdorf's List of Quiet Gifts

These are the gifts he names that you won't see at a ward talent show — worth scanning before class so you can recognize which ones resonate with you:

- Showing compassion
- Noticing people who are overlooked
- Finding reasons to be joyful
- Being a peacemaker
- Noticing small miracles
- Giving sincere compliments
- Forgiving
- Repenting
- Enduring
- Explaining things simply
- Connecting with children
- Sustaining Church leaders
- Helping others know that they belong

## Questions That Might Come Up in Class

These are the kind of questions a good teacher would ask from this talk. Thinking through them now means you'll be ready to contribute instead of processing on the spot.

**On discipline:**
- *What spiritual practice has been hardest for you to maintain consistently?*
- *When has something you once felt sure about started to fade — and what brought it back?*

**On gifts:**
- *Which of the "quiet gifts" listed in the talk do you see in the people around you?*
- *How do you discover gifts you didn't know you had?*
- *What's the difference between burying a talent out of fear vs. genuine humility?*

**On doing your part:**
- *What does "doing your part with all your heart" look like on a Tuesday?*
- *How do you balance knowing you have a long way to go with not being discouraged by the distance?*

## Threads Worth Thinking About Before Class

### The fear problem
The one-talent servant's issue was **fear** — "I was afraid, and went and hid thy talent in the earth" ([Matt 25:25](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/matt/25?lang=eng&id=p25#p25)). The D&C 60 elders hid their talent "because of the fear of man" ([D&C 60:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/60?lang=eng&id=p2#p2)). Uchtdorf doesn't dwell on this, but it's worth sitting with: where does fear keep you from using what God gave you?

### "Second nature" is fragile
Uchtdorf's flight-simulator story isn't about someone who never learned to fly. He was a *chief pilot*. Mastery itself isn't permanent. This maps directly to testimony. You can't coast on last year's spiritual experiences. In Alma's language: even after the seed sprouts, if you "neglect the tree, and take no thought for its nourishment," it withers — "not because the seed was not good" ([Alma 32:38–39](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/32?lang=eng&id=p38-p39#p38)).

### Christ did the superhuman part
Uchtdorf's most pointed theological move: "Remember, it is Jesus Christ who already did the superhuman part when He conquered death and sin. Our part is to follow the Christ." This reframes the whole talk from performance pressure to responsive devotion. We're not generating our own salvation. We're walking toward the One who already secured it.

### The "no-win scenario" line
"Because of our beloved Savior, there is no such thing as a no-win scenario." That's a Star Trek reference from a former airline pilot — and it's also genuine theology. In a universe where the Atonement is real, every faithful effort counts. [D&C 84:38](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/84?lang=eng&id=p38#p38) backs it: the covenant promises everything the Father has.

## A Personal Angle

If you want to contribute something in class, the richest territory from this talk is probably the intersection of **Uchtdorf's "quiet gifts" list** and your own experience. Think about a time you saw one of those gifts in action — someone who noticed you were overlooked, someone who explained something simply, someone who helped you feel you belonged. That kind of story ministers.

The other angle: **what spiritual skill have you let atrophy?** Not as a guilt thing — Uchtdorf's tone is warm, not scolding. More like: what's the thing you used to do that made your faith feel alive, and what would it look like to pick it back up?
