# Data Center Research

Research thread on the economics, community impacts, and policy landscape of data center development — anchored in the live Marshfield, MO case but built to generalize.

## Why this exists

A ~10MW AI-ready data center is being built by Lumon Solutions on Rifle Range Road outside Marshfield, in Webster County. The community is largely opposed; the county has minimal zoning authority; a community letter has been circulating. Michael lives in this community.

The work here aims to be useful for:
- Understanding what data centers actually deliver economically (vs. what marketing claims)
- Evaluating community letters and proposals critically and fairly
- Building a defensible negotiating position grounded in verified facts and the Governor's own framework
- Cross-referencing the Marshfield case against comparable Missouri cases (Liberty/Metrobloks, Camdenton, St. Louis)

## Key findings to date

1. **The "based on the servers it hosts" angle (Tier 4) is the one most analyses miss.** Enabled-by-compute jobs (SaaS, AI labs, enterprise users) are real but geographically untethered from the host community — they accrue wherever the tenants are, not where the building sits.

2. **State-published economic multipliers (2.58–7.5x) hide that the host community gets very little.** Most of the multiplier accrues at state/national scale through supply chain (Tier 2) and induced spending (Tier 3) far from the rural site.

3. **Missouri EO 26-02 (Kehoe, Jan 13, 2026) has substantial ratepayer-protection language** requiring data centers to "cover their full cost of electricity and infrastructure service" so residential rates don't rise. This is a community lever, not just industry recruitment — and the Marshfield letter omits it.

4. **The Liberty/Metrobloks case included a 25-year, $27.75M Community Benefits Agreement** for the Liberty Institute for Science and Ethics. This is the strongest in-state model for what Webster County could ask for.

5. **One quote in the circulating Marshfield letter appears fabricated** ("AI is the space race of our time and we must win" attributed to Kehoe via KOMU — the real quote and the "space race" phrase come from two different sources). Likely AI confabulation.
