# Verification Scratch — Marshfield Letter

## EO 26-02 (Kehoe, Jan 13, 2026) — REAL but MISCHARACTERIZED

- Confirmed: Executive Order 26-02 signed Jan 13, 2026
- Source: https://www.sos.mo.gov/library/reference/orders/2026/eo2 (Missouri Secretary of State, full text)
- Letter claims: directs agencies "to align with President Trump's national AI initiative and accelerate data center development across the state"
- **Actual EO content includes substantial RATEPAYER PROTECTION language:**
  > "Ratepayer Protection: The Director shall coordinate the review of current state regulations and utility practices to ensure that the construction and operation of data centers and other large power users do not result in higher energy rates for residential and small business Missourians, in accordance with the principle that large users should cover their full cost of electricity and infrastructure service…"
- NASEO summary: "The Executive Order also emphasizes protecting Missourians from potential high energy rates and grid impacts from energy intensive AI data centers."
- **The letter omits this entirely.** This is material — the EO actually contains language a community could USE to push back, not just industry recruitment.
- Reports due to Governor Nov 30, 2026

## Kehoe "AI is the space race of our time and we must win" quote — **APPEARS FABRICATED**

Searched KOMU directly. The actual Kehoe statement to KOMU 8 (Jan 22, 2026, Nathan Lee):

> "Missouri stands ready to welcome business investments that bring economic growth and opportunity to our communities. Local support and collaboration are critical to the success of data center development, and these types of investments will support high-wage jobs, generate millions in construction-related spending, and strengthen the economic foundation of local communities for long-term success. Many leading companies are looking at Missouri for these projects because they have confidence in our people, our infrastructure, and our pro-business environment."

Source: https://www.komu.com/news/midmissourinews/missouri-leadership-pushes-data-center-development-despite-electricity-environmental-fears/article_73d1d49a-b2d2-4d9d-8187-0e891f613f52.html

The "space race" framing comes from a DIFFERENT person in a DIFFERENT article:
- West News Magazine (Oct 29, 2025) titled "The new space race? Data centers eye Missouri…"
- Quote from Janelle Higgins (VP Marketing, Missouri Partnership): "Some people have even compared the boom in building data centers to the space race"

**The letter attributes "AI is the space race of our time and we must win" to Kehoe as a "direct quote." I cannot find this quote in any verified source. It appears to be an AI confabulation conflating Higgins's statement and the article title with Kehoe's actual KOMU statement.**

The "Missouri stands ready to welcome business investments…" line in the letter IS real. The "space race" line is not.

Bonus context the letter omits: Kehoe in Joplin (Feb 10, 2026, KOAM):
> "Many of these data centers use less water in an entire year than a fast-food restaurant"
> "consumer protections are in place to prevent energy costs from being passed on to residents"

## Camdenton example — PARTIALLY ACCURATE

- Letter claim: "The Lake of the Ozarks community had municipal zoning authority. They packed city hall and turned the data center away at the door."
- Actual events (May 5–6, 2026):
  - Camdenton Board of Aldermen passed a **12-month MORATORIUM** on data center approvals — not a permanent rejection
  - Also RESCINDED prior support for an Opportunity Zone that turned out to include a data center
  - Camden County Commission and Rep. Jeff Vernetti also dropped Opportunity Zone support
  - Project: MO Lake Development LLC, Blake Hodits, ~1,045 acres near Walmart between Hwy 54 and Hwy 5
- Letter's framing is "Camdenton turned them away" — closer to "Camdenton hit pause for 12 months while drafting zoning rules"
- BUT the underlying point — that Camdenton's municipal authority gave them a tool Webster County lacks — is correct
- Sources:
  - https://www.ozarksfirst.com/news/camdenton-data-center-regulation/
  - https://www.krmsradio.com/camdenton-says-no-to-opportunity-zone-support-issues-12-month-moratorium-on-data-centers/
  - https://krcgtv.com/news/local/camdenton-aldermen-pass-data-center-moratorium-rescind-opportunity-zone-support
