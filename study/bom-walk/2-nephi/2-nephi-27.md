# 2 Nephi 27 — The Sealed Book: The Marvelous Work Confounds the Learned

*(Nephi's prophetic **expansion** of Isaiah 29 — the coming forth of the Book of Mormon. Mini-study. Not a verbatim Isaiah block: vv6-23 are a BoM expansion with no Isaiah-29 parallel.)*

**Binding idea:** God brings forth a sealed book through an *unlearned* man because the learned reject it. Why does the "marvelous work and a wonder" run precisely through the one who says "I am not learned"?

**Read:** In the last days the nations are "drunken with iniquity" (v1) and given "the spirit of deep sleep... ye have closed your eyes... rejected the prophets" (v5, weaving Isa 29:9-10). Then the expansion: "the Lord God shall bring forth unto you the words of a book... and behold the book shall be sealed; and in the book shall be a revelation from God, from the beginning of the world to the ending thereof" (vv6-7). The sealed part is withheld; the book is delivered to a man who delivers its words to another (vv9-11), and "three witnesses shall behold it, by the power of God" (v12). Then the famous scene: the Lord says, deliver the unsealed words "unto the learned, saying: Read this, I pray thee. And the learned shall say: Bring hither the book, and I will read them" — "because of the glory of the world and to get gain will they say this" (vv15-16). "And the man shall say: I cannot bring the book, for it is sealed. Then shall the learned say: **I cannot read it**" (vv17-18). So "the Lord God will deliver again the book... to him that is not learned; and the man that is not learned shall say: I am not learned" (v19). "Then shall the Lord God say unto him: **The learned shall not read them, for they have rejected them, and I am able to do mine own work**" (v20). "I am God; and I am a God of miracles... I work not among the children of men **save it be according to their faith**" (v23). Then Isaiah 29 resumes: "this people draw near unto me with their mouth... but have removed their hearts far from me, and their fear towards me is taught by the precepts of men" (v25); "I will proceed to do **a marvelous work and a wonder, for the wisdom of their wise and learned shall perish**" (v26); "shall the work say of him that made it, he made me not?" (v27, the potter/clay); "in that day shall the deaf hear the words of the book, and the eyes of the blind shall see out of obscurity and out of darkness" (v29); "they that erred in spirit shall come to understanding, and they that murmured shall learn doctrine" (v35).

**Thoughts:**
- *The book prophesies its own coming forth.* Chapter 26's "voice from the dust" becomes concrete here: a *sealed book*, *three witnesses*, a *learned man* who cannot read it, an *unlearned man* who reads by the power of God. This is the Book of Mormon describing the manner of its own 19th-century emergence — and the learned-man scene (vv15-18) was fulfilled to the letter in 1828, when Martin Harris took Joseph Smith's transcript of characters to Professor Charles Anthon, who (per Joseph's account, JS-H 1:64-65) first vouched for it, then on learning the source was a sealed book, said he could not read a sealed book. A prophecy written ~550 BC, fulfilled in a specific New York episode.
- *The marvelous work runs through the unlearned on purpose (vv19-20, 26).* The learned reject the book ("Bring hither the book, and I will read them" — for "gain," v16), so God hands it to "him that is not learned" who protests "I am not learned." And the Lord's answer is the theology of the whole chapter: "**I am able to do mine own work**" (v20), and "the wisdom of their wise and learned shall perish" (v26, Isa 29:14). God deliberately chooses the weak instrument so that the result cannot be credited to human wisdom — the marvelous work is marvelous partly because of *who* it comes through. (This is the axe of chapter 20 inverted: there the instrument boasted; here God picks an instrument that *cannot* boast — "I am not learned" — precisely so He gets the glory.)
- *The made thing denying its maker (v27).* "Shall the work say of him that made it, he made me not? Or shall the thing framed say of him that framed it, he had no understanding?" (Isa 29:16, the potter and clay). The deepest version of the axe-boast: not just the instrument taking credit, but the *creation denying the Creator* — the pot claiming the potter does not exist, or has no understanding. A standing rebuke to every age that says of God "Who seeth us?" (v27) — including the learned confidence that explains the Maker away.
- *Lip-religion (v25).* "This people draw near unto me with their mouth, and with their lips do honor me, but have removed their hearts far from me, and their fear towards me is taught by the precepts of men." This is the verse the Lord quoted to Joseph Smith in the First Vision (JS-H 1:19), and Jesus quoted it to the Pharisees (Matt 15:8). Honor on the lips, heart far off, religion learned as "precepts of men" — the diagnosis that the marvelous work is sent to heal.
- *Faith-gated miracles (v23).* "I work not among the children of men save it be according to their faith." The sealed book, the witnesses, the whole marvelous work operate on the same condition as every miracle: faith first. God's power is not withheld arbitrarily; it meets faith and is bounded by its absence.
