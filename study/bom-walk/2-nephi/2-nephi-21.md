# 2 Nephi 21 — Isaiah 11: The Peaceable Kingdom Is a Knowing Kingdom

*(Isaiah block — Isaiah 11. Full protocol: exegesis + variants + likening. A Restoration keystone — D&C 113; Moroni quoted it to Joseph Smith, JS-H 1:40. Near-identical to KJV. Compare Isaiah 11.)*

**Binding idea:** Why does the wolf finally lie down with the lamb — and what does it mean that the cause Isaiah gives is *knowledge*, not restraint?

**Read:** "There shall come forth a rod out of the stem of Jesse, and a branch shall grow out of his roots" (v1). "The Spirit of the Lord shall rest upon him, the spirit of wisdom and understanding, the spirit of counsel and might, the spirit of knowledge and of the fear of the Lord" (v2); "**he shall not judge after the sight of his eyes, neither reprove after the hearing of his ears. But with righteousness shall he judge the poor**" (vv3-4); "righteousness shall be the girdle of his loins" (v5). Then the peaceable kingdom: "**The wolf also shall dwell with the lamb, and the leopard shall lie down with the kid**... and a little child shall lead them" (v6); "the lion shall eat straw like the ox" (v7); "the sucking child shall play on the hole of the asp" (v8). "**They shall not hurt nor destroy in all my holy mountain, for the earth shall be full of the knowledge of the Lord, as the waters cover the sea**" (v9). "A root of Jesse, which shall stand for an ensign of the people; to it shall the Gentiles seek" (v10). "**The Lord shall set his hand again the second time to recover the remnant of his people**... from the four corners of the earth" (vv11-12). "Ephraim shall not envy Judah, and Judah shall not vex Ephraim" (v13); a highway for the remnant "like as it was to Israel in the day that he came up out of the land of Egypt" (v16).

**Exegesis:**
- *Harm ends because knowing fills everything (v9).* The hinge of the chapter is the word "for." The wolf lies with the lamb, the lion eats straw, the child plays at the viper's hole — and then: "**for** the earth shall be full of the knowledge of the Lord, as the waters cover the sea." The peace is not enforced by restraint, a leash, or a cage; it is the natural consequence of a saturating knowledge. Predation ceases because the whole earth is full of God as the sea is full of water. The Millennium is, at bottom, an epistemological state: when the knowledge of the Lord covers everything, there is nothing left to hurt with. This is the project's own founding conviction — "the glory of God is intelligence" (D&C 93:36), "whatever principle of intelligence we attain unto in this life, it will rise with us" (D&C 130:18) — read forward to its end: a world so full of knowing that violence has no room.
- *The Judge who does not judge by appearance (vv3-4).* "He shall not judge after the sight of his eyes, neither reprove after the hearing of his ears." The Messiah's justice is not surface-reading or hearsay; it is righteousness applied "for the meek of the earth." He weighs by what is true, not by what looks or sounds true. (The footnote: "by appearances and by hearsay" — cf John 7:24, "judge not according to the appearance.") A standard for any judging: the eyes and ears are not enough.
- *The stem, the rod, the root (vv1, 10) — the D&C 113 keystone.* Joseph Smith asked what these are, and the answer (D&C 113:1-6) reads: the **Stem of Jesse** is Christ; the **rod** is "a servant in the hands of Christ... on whom there is laid much power"; the **root** is "a descendant of Jesse, as well as of Joseph... on whom is laid much of the priesthood and the keys of the kingdom, for an ensign." The chapter is doubly Christ-and-latter-day: the Branch is the Redeemer, and the rod/root are His latter-day servants raising the ensign.
- *The second gathering (v11).* "The Lord shall set his hand again **the second time**" — the first recovery was the return from Babylon; the second is the latter-day gathering. This is the Restoration's self-understanding, and the chapter Moroni quoted to Joseph Smith as "about to be fulfilled" (JS-H 1:40). The reconciliation of Ephraim and Judah (v13) is the two-sticks of Ezekiel 37 — the very union the Book of Mormon enacts (the stick of Joseph joining the stick of Judah, cf 2 Ne 3).

**The Book of Mormon variants (verified by direct comparison this session):**
- This is one of the **most nearly identical** chapters in the entire block — and that itself is the finding. The peaceable-kingdom and second-gathering verses are reproduced essentially verbatim; the only differences are trivial: v6 reads "and fatling together" for KJV "and the fatling together"; v13 moves "also" ("The envy of Ephraim also shall depart" for KJV "The envy also of Ephraim shall depart"). **The pattern across the block is now clear: the variants cluster in the judgment chapters (12:9, 13:6, 15:8) and the great Messianic/millennial chapters (9, 11) come through almost untouched.** The Restoration text does not rework the glory; it reworks (or restores) the warnings.

**Nephite framing — likening (four questions):**
- *Why did Nephi include Isaiah 11?* The branch of Jesse is the Redeemer he saw (11:2 — Nephi's "all things typify Christ"), and the second gathering is the work of the very record he is writing. The peaceable kingdom is the destination the whole block has been moving toward — after the woes and the rod and the axe, the wolf and the lamb.
- *Was it for us, last-dispensation readers?* As directly as any chapter in scripture. Moroni quoted it to Joseph Smith and said it "was about to be fulfilled" (JS-H 1:40); D&C 113 is its revealed interpretation; the ensign and the "second time" gathering *are* the Restoration. This chapter is the Restoration reading its own charter in Isaiah.
- *How does it apply to us?* The cure for a world that hurts and destroys is, by v9, *knowledge* — the earth full of the knowledge of the Lord. That reframes study itself: gaining the knowledge of God is not private enrichment; it is participation in the thing that ends predation. And v3 — do not judge by the sight of the eyes or the hearing of the ears — is a discipline for every judgment we make: weigh by righteousness, not by appearance.
- *How do we read it better through Nephi's framing?* For a broken-off remnant, the second gathering (v11) and the healing of Ephraim's envy (v13) are the promise that the scattered house is reassembled — and the Book of Mormon is the instrument of it. Nephi is not just copying a millennial poem; he is copying the prophecy his own record fulfills.
