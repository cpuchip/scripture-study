# Mosiah 3 — The Natural Man Yields and Becomes a Child

*(The angel's words to Benjamin — the coming of Christ, the Atonement, the natural man. A cornerstone Christology + transformation chapter. Mini-study.)*

**Binding idea:** An angel gives Benjamin the fullest pre-Christian portrait of the Savior — name, mother, blood from every pore. At its center is the one move that changes a man: the natural man *yields*. What does that yielding produce, and why is the model a child?

**Read:** An angel wakes Benjamin to declare "glad tidings of great joy" (v3). "The Lord Omnipotent who reigneth, who was, and is from all eternity to all eternity, shall come down from heaven... and shall dwell in a **tabernacle of clay**" (v5), working miracles. "He shall suffer... even more than man can suffer, except it be unto death; for behold, **blood cometh from every pore, so great shall be his anguish for the wickedness and the abominations of his people**" (v7). "He shall be called **Jesus Christ, the Son of God, the Father of heaven and earth, the Creator of all things from the beginning; and his mother shall be called Mary**" (v8); they will "scourge him, and shall crucify him" (v9); "he shall rise the third day" (v10). His blood "atoneth for the sins of those who have... died not knowing the will of God, or who have ignorantly sinned" (v11) — "but **wo, wo unto him who knoweth that he rebelleth against God!**" (v12). "The law of Moses availeth nothing except it were through the atonement of his blood" (v15); little children are saved, for "the blood of Christ atoneth for their sins" (v16). "**There shall be no other name given... whereby salvation can come... only in and through the name of Christ, the Lord Omnipotent**" (v17); men must "**become as little children**" (v18). Then the cornerstone: "**For the natural man is an enemy to God, and has been from the fall of Adam, and will be, forever and ever, unless he yields to the enticings of the Holy Spirit, and putteth off the natural man and becometh a saint through the atonement of Christ the Lord, and becometh as a child, submissive, meek, humble, patient, full of love, willing to submit to all things which the Lord seeth fit to inflict upon him, even as a child doth submit to his father**" (v19). The wicked "drink damnation," shrink from the presence of the Lord, "drunk out of the cup of the wrath of God" (vv25-26), to torment "as a lake of fire and brimstone" (v27).

**Thoughts:**
- *The natural man *yields* — the hinge of the whole verse (v19).* Benjamin's most-quoted line is built on one verb: "**unless he yields**." The natural man does not improve himself into a saint; he *yields* "to the enticings of the Holy Spirit," and then the change is described in passive-receptive terms — "putteth off the natural man" and "becometh a saint **through the atonement of Christ**." The transformation is not achievement but surrender: the enemy of God becomes the saint of God by ceasing to resist the Spirit and letting the Atonement do the becoming. This is the same yielding that runs through the walk — agency that consents (2 Ne 2:16), the heart given away (the give-away-all-my-sins study), reconcile-then-grace (2 Ne 10:24). The work is to stop fighting the Spirit and let go.
- *And becomes a child (vv18-19).* The result of yielding is not a hardened spiritual veteran but "**a child** — submissive, meek, humble, patient, full of love, willing to submit to all things which the Lord seeth fit to inflict upon him." The saint is a child, and the list is the inverse of the natural man's posture: where the natural man asserts, the child submits; where the natural man resists affliction, the child accepts it "even as a child doth submit to his father." The goal of the gospel is not to make you formidable; it is to make you teachable — to return you to the trust a child has in a good father. (And it is grounded: little children are themselves saved by the Atonement, v16 — the model is also the saved.)
- *Blood from every pore (v7) — Gethsemane, ~124 BC.* "Blood cometh from every pore, so great shall be his anguish." This is the earliest explicit statement in the Book of Mormon of the suffering that the New Testament reports in Gethsemane (Luke 22:44) and that D&C 19:18 confirms in Christ's own voice ("which suffering caused myself... to bleed at every pore"). The angel locates the Atonement's deepest agony not only on the cross but in the bleeding-at-every-pore anguish "for the wickedness and the abominations of his people" — sin felt in the body.
- *The fullest pre-Christian naming (v8).* "Jesus Christ, the Son of God, the Father of heaven and earth, the Creator of all things from the beginning; and his mother shall be called Mary." Name, titles, and *mother* — given by an angel a century before Bethlehem. And the Father/Son duality ("the Son of God, the Father of heaven and earth") is the condescension Christology that Abinadi will expand (Mosiah 15): the same Christ is both Son (in the flesh) and Father (as Creator). Nephite revelation is strikingly specific about the One it awaits.
- *Justice calibrated to knowledge (vv11-12).* The Atonement covers "those who have died not knowing the will of God, or who have ignorantly sinned" — but "wo... unto him who knoweth that he rebelleth against God." The same principle as Benjamin's own warning (2:36-39): culpability rises with knowledge. The ignorant are covered; the knowing rebel, who sins against light, is not — not because God is harsher but because the rebellion is more total. (This is mercy's wide reach and justice's precision in one breath.)
