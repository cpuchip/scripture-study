# Mosiah 8 — A Seer Is Greater Than a Prophet: God Provides the Means to Know

*(Ammon teaches Limhi's people; the 24 Jaredite gold plates surface; the canonical seer doctrine. The textual origin of the Book of Ether. Mini-study.)*

**Binding idea:** Limhi has a record no one can read and a desperate question — why was a whole people destroyed? Ammon's answer introduces the Book of Mormon's definition of a *seer*. What is a seer, why is the gift "greater than a prophet," and what does the chapter say about why knowledge that God offers goes unclaimed?

**Read:** Ammon "rehearse[s] unto them all that had happened... from the time that Zeniff went up" and "also rehearsed... the last words which king Benjamin had taught" (vv2-3). Limhi brings out records — and tells how he sent "forty and three of my people" to find Zarahemla; lost, "yet they were diligent," they returned having found "a land which was covered with bones of men, and of beasts... and ruins of buildings of every kind, having discovered a land which had been peopled with a people who were as numerous as the hosts of Israel" (v8). For testimony they brought "**twenty-four plates which are filled with engravings, and they are of pure gold**" (v9), with breastplates and rust-cankered swords (vv10-11). "There is no one in the land that is able to interpret the language... Canst thou translate?" (v11) — "I am desirous to know **the cause of their destruction**" (v12). Ammon: "I can assuredly tell thee... of a man that can translate... he has wherewith that he can look... it is a gift from God. And the things are called **interpreters**, and no man can look in them except he be commanded, lest he should look for that he ought not and he should perish. And whosoever is commanded to look in them, the same is called **seer**" (v13) — the king of Zarahemla (Mosiah) "is the man" (v14). Limhi: "**a seer is greater than a prophet**" (v15). Ammon: "**a seer is a revelator and a prophet also; and a gift which is greater can no man have, except he should possess the power of God**" (v16). "A seer can know of things which are past, and also of things which are to come... **hidden things shall come to light, and things which are not known shall be made known by them, and also things shall be made known by them which otherwise could not be known**" (v17). "Thus **God has provided a means** that man, through faith, might work mighty miracles" (v18). Limhi rejoices: "a great mystery is contained within these plates, and these interpreters were doubtless **prepared for the purpose of unfolding all such mysteries**" (v19). "O how marvelous are the works of the Lord... yea, and **how blind and impenetrable are the understandings of the children of men; for they will not seek wisdom, neither do they desire that she should rule over them!**" (v20) — "they are as a **wild flock which fleeth from the shepherd**" (v21).

**Thoughts:**
- *The seer doctrine — the gift just short of God's own power (vv15-18).* This is the Book of Mormon's defining passage on seership, the one the Church's "prophet, seer, and revelator" formula rests on. Limhi's instinct — "a seer is greater than a prophet" — is confirmed and *expanded*: a seer is not a separate office but a fuller one, "a revelator and a prophet **also**" (v16). The prophet speaks for God; the revelator unveils what God reveals; the seer does both and *sees* — past and future, "hidden things... made known by them which otherwise could not be known" (v17). And Ammon sets the ceiling precisely: "a gift which is greater can no man have, **except he should possess the power of God, which no man can**." Seership is the highest gift available to mortals — one rung below omniscience itself. The point is not the seer's grandeur but the *reach* of what God will make knowable through him: things that "otherwise could not be known" become known. Revelation is not vague impression; it can deliver hard facts about vanished peoples and unread tongues.
- *God provides the means (vv13, 18, 19).* The recurring word is *means*. The interpreters are "wherewith that he can look"; "God has provided **a means**" (v18); the interpreters were "**prepared** for the purpose of unfolding... mysteries" (v19). This is a doctrine about *instruments* of revelation — God does not leave the unknowable simply unknown; he prepares physical means (the interpreters, the Urim and Thummim, footnote → Ex 28:30) and calls men (seers) to use them. Revelation has appointed channels and tools; it is provided, not merely hoped for. (And the guardrail: "no man can look in them except he be commanded... lest he should look for that he ought not and he should perish," v13. The instruments of revelation are a stewardship, not a curiosity — bounded by calling, dangerous when seized.)
- *The 24 plates — the Book of Ether is born here (vv8-12).* Limhi's diligent-but-lost search party stumbled on the ruins of "a people who were as numerous as the hosts of Israel" — the Jaredites — and carried home twenty-four gold plates. These are the plates Mosiah will translate (28:11-17) and Moroni will abridge into the Book of Ether. This chapter is the textual headwater of Ether. And note the *motive*: Limhi wants them translated "to know **the cause of their destruction**" (v12). The Jaredite record is sought as a *warning* — to learn why a civilization perished. That is exactly why Moroni later includes it: a destroyed people's record handed to a people about to be destroyed, so they might read their own end in time and turn. (A record kept so the living can avoid the fate of the dead — the whole Book of Mormon's purpose in miniature.)
- *The wisdom that goes unclaimed (vv20-21).* Limhi's doxology turns to lament: God's works are marvelous and his patience long — "yea, and how **blind and impenetrable** are the understandings of the children of men; for they will not seek wisdom, neither do they desire that she should rule over them." Wisdom is personified as a *she* (the editors note the Hebrew/Greek feminine — Prov 9:1's Lady Wisdom), a queen men refuse to be ruled by. The tragedy the chapter names is not that knowledge is unavailable — God provides seers, interpreters, means — it is that men *will not seek* what is offered. They are "a wild flock which fleeth from the shepherd" (v21), scattering of their own will into the jaws of the beasts. This is the root beneath ch 7's bondage-as-covenant-in-reverse: the destruction comes not because God hid wisdom but because men fled the shepherd who offered it.

**Notable / flag:** No spin-off. Doctrinally this is **the Book of Mormon's defining seer passage** (vv15-18) — a seer is "a revelator and a prophet also," the highest gift short of "the power of God," able to make known "things... which otherwise could not be known"; and the governing idea is that **God provides the *means*** (interpreters, seers) to know the unknowable — revelation has instruments, guarded by commandment (v13). Textually, **the 24 gold plates that surface here are the Jaredite record → the Book of Ether** (Mosiah will translate them, ch 28; Moroni abridges), sought specifically to learn "the cause of their destruction" (v12) — a warning record for a people about to repeat the fate. And Limhi's lament names the root beneath ch 7's bondage: **the destruction comes not because wisdom is hidden but because men "will not seek" it** — a wild flock fleeing the shepherd (vv20-21).
