# 1 Nephi 6 — Why Nephi Writes: To Persuade Men to Come unto Christ

**Binding idea:** Why is Nephi keeping this particular record at all — what is his editorial rule?

**Read:** A short, programmatic chapter. Nephi declines to give his genealogy here — it is on his father's record — saying "it sufficeth me to say that we are descendants of Joseph." He will not give "a full account of all the things of my father," because "I desire the room that I may write of the things of God." His stated purpose: "the fulness of mine intent is that I may persuade men to come unto the God of Abraham, and the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob, and be saved." He will write what is "pleasing unto God," not "pleasing unto the world," and commands his seed not to fill the plates "with things which are not of worth unto the children of men."

**Thoughts:**
- *This is the small plates' mission statement.* Six verses, and they tell you how to read everything else: Nephi is not writing a history, he is writing a persuasion. "That I may persuade men to come unto [Christ]... and be saved" (v4) is the same purpose John names for his gospel — "these are written, that ye might believe" (John 20:31, the footnote). The book will end on the same word: Moroni's "come unto Christ" (Moro 10:32). Nephi states on page twelve the invitation the whole record exists to extend.
- *Curation is a doctrine here, not just a constraint.* The plates are scarce metal; Nephi must choose. So he makes *worth* the criterion — "the room... to write of the things of God," only what is "of worth unto the children of men." The physical limit becomes a spiritual filter. (Mormon and Moroni inherit the rule; cf. W of M 1:4, and the recurring "I cannot write a hundredth part.") A scripture that selects *for* God is a different kind of document than a chronicle.
- *"Pleasing unto God," not "unto the world" (v5).* The criterion is Paul's exactly — "do I seek to please men? ... I should not be the servant of Christ" (Gal 1:10). Nephi names the audience problem every writer of scripture faces, and chooses his reader: God, and "those who are not of the world."
- *The two-records structure surfaces.* v1 distinguishes "these plates which I am writing" from "the record which has been kept by my father" — the first clear marker of the multiple-plates architecture (the small plates were a separate, later command — 1 Ne 9; 19:1-6; Words of Mormon) on which the book's whole transmission depends.

**Connections:**
- *Purpose = persuade to Christ:* v4 → John 20:31, Luke 1:4 (gospel-purpose statements); 2 Ne 9:41; the "come unto Christ" thread (Omni 1:26; Moro 10:32).
- *Please God, not the world:* v5 → Gal 1:10; 1 Thes 2:4; Heb 13:21; W of M 1:4.
- *Selection / "the room":* v3 → Jacob 7:27; the "hundredth part" refrain (Jacob 3:13; 3 Ne 26:6).
- *Descendants of Joseph:* v2 → 5:16 (the ch-5 lineage).
