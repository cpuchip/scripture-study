# 1 Nephi 14 — Two Churches Only, and the Vision Sealed to John

**Binding idea:** How does Nephi's vision end — and what is the "great and abominable church" actually?

**Read:** The angel sets the Gentile future as conditional: *if* they hearken to the Lamb and "harden not their hearts," they "shall be numbered among the seed of thy father... a blessed people upon the promised land forever"; if they harden, "wo." The Lord will "work a great and a marvelous work... either on the one hand or on the other" — peace and life eternal, or hardness and captivity. The "great pit" the abominable church digged "shall be filled by those who digged it." Then the defining word: "there are save two churches only; the one is the church of the Lamb of God, and the other is the church of the devil; wherefore, whoso belongeth not to the church of the Lamb of God belongeth to that great church... she is the whore of all the earth." The whore sits "upon many waters," with dominion "among all nations"; the church of the Lamb is "few" and its "dominions... were small" — yet "the power of the Lamb of God... descended upon the saints... who were scattered upon all the face of the earth; and they were armed with righteousness and with the power of God in great glory." The wrath of God is poured on the great church (wars and rumors of wars), and "then... the work of the Father shall commence" to fulfill the covenants to Israel. Finally Nephi sees "one of the twelve apostles of the Lamb" in white, who "shall see and write the remainder of these things... concerning the end of the world" — and Nephi is told, "the things which thou shalt see hereafter thou shalt not write; for the Lord God hath ordained the apostle of the Lamb... that he should write them." The apostle's name "was John." Nephi closes: "I have written but a small part of the things which I saw... the things which I have written are true."

**Thoughts:**
- *Two churches only — and it is not a denomination (v10).* The most-misread verse here. "Whoso belongeth not to the church of the Lamb of God belongeth to that great church." The great and abominable church is not a sect but a *category* — defined by desire and the praise of the world (13:6-9), not a creed. The binary is allegiance to the Lamb or not. Read against ch 13, it aims the warning at *everyone*, including the covenant Gentiles, and refuses to let any reader locate "the whore" safely in someone else's pew.
- *Few, scattered, and armed with righteousness (v12-14).* The Lamb's church is small — "few" (Matt 7:14), dominions "small" — against a whore with "dominion over all the earth." But the saints are "armed with righteousness and with the power of God in great glory." The weapon is righteousness, not numbers and not force. For a church that will always be a minority, this is the chapter's promise: outnumbered is the normal condition, and the defense is holiness, not size.
- *The marvelous work cuts both ways (v7).* Isaiah's "marvelous work and a wonder" (Isa 29:14, the Restoration) is "everlasting, either on the one hand or on the other" — it convinces unto peace and eternal life *or* delivers to hardness and captivity. The same work that saves also condemns, by response. Revelation is never neutral; the marvelous work forces a verdict.
- *John writes what Nephi may not (v18-28).* Nephi sees the end of the world — and is *forbidden* to write it, because "the Lord God hath ordained the apostle of the Lamb" — John — "that he should write them." Two prophets, six centuries apart, shown the same apocalypse, but only one is ordained to record it; the rest is "sealed up to come forth in their purity, in the own due time." The canon is curated across prophets by divine assignment, and Nephi's silence is obedience, not ignorance. (This is why Revelation and 1 Nephi's vision rhyme — they are the same vision, divided.)
- *The pit-digger falls in (v3).* The abominable church's pit "shall be filled by those who digged it" — the old justice-motif (Ps 57:6; Prov 26:27). The trap built for the saints becomes the builder's grave. The Gadianton secret works will end the same way.
- *Gentiles numbered among Israel by hearkening (v1-2).* Covenant membership by faith, not blood — "numbered among the seed of thy father" if they hearken (Gal 3:7, 29). The olive-tree grafting (ch 10) stated as a promise to the Gentiles: hearken, and you are Israel.
- *Nephi's closing disclaimer (v28-30).* "I have written but a small part of the things which I saw." He saw vastly more than he recorded, wrote only what he was permitted, and vouches: "the things which I have written are true." The curation-by-worth of ch 6 and the obedience-without-the-whole of ch 9, applied to the vision itself.
