## Gap & Critical Analysis

**Structural completeness:** All ten chapters (17–26) read and sourced. The four mechanisms of the binding question are all grounded in specific verses — no claim requires unsupported inference.

**Tensions surfaced:**
1. The Amalekites and Amulonites (apostate Nephites who'd joined the Lamanites) were NOT converted — not even one Amulonite (23:14). This complicates a simple "the gospel cures any tradition" thesis. The mos10 study had already noted from Alma 60:32 that apostates "redoubled" the hatred. The study must acknowledge: the cure worked on people who inherited the tradition involuntarily; it did not work on people who chose apostasy with full knowledge. That's Alma 24:30's explicit observation about post-enlightenment hardness being worse.

2. The "never did fall away" claim (23:6) is remarkable but needs care — subsequent chapters show the Ammonites facing ongoing pressure (Alma 27), some Lamanite attacks continuing, and the younger generation later having to make their own covenant (Alma 53:10–22, the 2,000 stripling warriors). The firmness of ch 23 is real; it's not that no Ammonite ever sinned, but that the community as a body never returned to the tradition.

3. The over-king conversion (ch 22) is presented somewhat abruptly — one teaching session, he falls as if dead, rises, ministers to his household. The text is intentional but a reader may notice: Lamoni had a longer, more gradual arc. The study should acknowledge: Aaron's success with the father is enabled by what Ammon had already done — the over-king had been astonished at Ammon's mercy at the sword's point (ch 20:26-27) before Aaron ever arrived. The soil was prepared.

4. The Philippians 4:13 parallel (Alma 26:12) is real — the footnote in the source text confirms it. But "in his strength I can do all things" is Ammon speaking of missionary labor, not Paul's generalized contentment. The resonance is genuine; the contexts differ. Handle with precision.

5. Isaiah 2:4 / burying swords parallel: the text does NOT quote or allude to Isaiah 2 explicitly. The topical resonance (nations beating swords to plowshares) is real but it is the reader's connection, not the text's. State it as such.

**Ring check:** The binding question — what broke the tradition? — is answered in four interlocking moves that the text itself provides: (1) servant posture, (2) patience and love, (3) gospel reframing the origin question, (4) refusal to perpetuate the cycle. The study circles back to this at the end. The ring holds.

**Posture check:** The mos10 study diagnosed the disease honestly, including the partial exculpation of the Lamanites. This study extends that honesty — it shows what worked AND what didn't (the apostates), and it doesn't over-spiritualize the mechanics. The text is doing the work; the study is tracing it.

**Voice concerns to watch:** Em-dash budget. No "let that land." No "stops me cold." The Alma 24 section is at risk of going lyrical; the text is lyrical enough — let it carry.
