# Moroni 5 — the sacrament prayer on the wine: the blood shed, and "that they do always remember him"

**Binding idea:** "The manner of administering the wine—Behold, they took the cup, and said: O God, the Eternal Father, we ask thee, in the name of thy Son, Jesus Christ, to bless and sanctify this wine to the souls of all those who drink of it, that they may do it in remembrance of the blood of thy Son, which was shed for them; that they may witness unto thee, O God, the Eternal Father, that they do always remember him, that they may have his Spirit to be with them. Amen" (5:1-2).

**Read:** Moroni 5 (full, verbatim via gospel_get). 2 verses. The sacrament prayer on the wine.

## Thoughts

The prayer on the wine is the companion to the prayer on the bread, and the differences between them are quiet but real. The bread is taken "in remembrance of the *body* of thy Son" (4:3); the wine "in remembrance of the *blood* of thy Son, *which was shed for them*" (5:2). Between the two prayers, the whole atonement is held: the body given and the blood shed. The bread points to the body broken; the wine adds the explicit note of *cost* — "which was shed for them." The blood was not merely remembered but *shed*, and shed *for them* — the second emblem makes personal what the first declared: this was poured out for you.

And the wine prayer is notably *shorter* than the bread prayer, in a way that is theologically deliberate. The bread prayer named the full threefold covenant: the partakers witness that they are *willing to take upon them the name* of the Son, *and always remember him*, *and keep his commandments* (4:3). The wine prayer drops the "take his name" and "keep his commandments" clauses and centers on a single thing: "that they do *always remember him*" (5:2). And the shift in tense is worth noticing — the bread says the partaker is "willing to... always remember him" (a covenant being made), while the wine says "that they *do* always remember him" (the covenant affirmed). The bread is the *making* of the covenant; the wine is its *sealing*. The first cup commits; the second confirms. The two emblems together are not redundant but sequential — the body remembered and the covenant made over the bread, the blood remembered and the covenant sealed over the wine. And both end in the same promise, the heart of the whole ordinance: "that they may have his Spirit to be with them." Whether over the body or the blood, the purpose is constant — to remember Christ, that His Spirit may abide. Moroni preserves both prayers exactly, the body and the blood, so that the church reconstituted in a later day would have the full sacrament Christ instituted — the weekly remembrance of the whole atonement, body and blood, and the weekly renewal of the covenant that secures the Spirit.
