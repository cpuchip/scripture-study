# Episode: The Warning We Missed

**Series:** Enjoy the Words of Eternal Life
**Episode:** 002
**Target length:** ~5 minutes
**Source:** [enjoy-the-words-of-eternal-life-reflection.md](../enjoy-the-words-of-eternal-life-reflection.md)

---

## Hook (30 sec)

"There's a verse in the Doctrine and Covenants where God reveals that His word is truth, truth is light, and light is the Spirit of Jesus Christ. It's one of the most beautiful chains of identity in all of scripture. And then, eleven verses later, He says the entire church is under condemnation. Same section. Same breath."

---

## Setup (1 min)

- Last time we talked about what it means to *enjoy* the words of eternal life — to have them, possess them, use them with satisfaction
- That was the promise, from Moses 6:59
- But there's a companion verse — a *command* — in D&C 84:43
- And it comes with both the most extraordinary definition of what God's words actually are... and one of the sharpest warnings He's ever given His own people

---

## The Discovery (3 min)

### The chain

**Read aloud — [D&C 84:43–45](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/84.md):**
> "I now give unto you a commandment to beware concerning yourselves, to give diligent heed to the words of eternal life. For you shall live by every word that proceedeth forth from the mouth of God. For the word of the Lord is truth, and whatsoever is truth is light, and whatsoever is light is Spirit, even the Spirit of Jesus Christ."

- *(Pause. Let them hear it.)*
- This isn't metaphor. It's identity. Word = truth = light = Spirit.
- When you read scripture — when you really sit with it — you're not just learning *about* God. You're receiving truth, which is light, which is the Spirit of Christ itself entering you.
- The words are a delivery mechanism for the Spirit. That's what Jesus meant when He said "the words that I speak unto you, they are spirit, and they are life."
- And verse 47 says everyone who hearkens to that Spirit "cometh unto God, even the Father." There's a gravitational pull in the words of eternal life. They draw you toward the Father Himself.

### The warning

- So that's the invitation. Now here's the warning — same section, eleven verses later:

**Read aloud — [D&C 84:54–57](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/84.md):** *(read this one slowly)*
> "And your minds in times past have been darkened because of unbelief, and because you have treated lightly the things you have received — which vanity and unbelief have brought the whole church under condemnation. And this condemnation resteth upon the children of Zion, even all. And they shall remain under this condemnation until they repent and remember the new covenant, even the Book of Mormon and the former commandments which I have given them, not only to say, but to do according to that which I have written."

- Notice who's under condemnation. Not the world. Not the skeptics. The *children of Zion*. The covenant people.
- And what's the sin? Not rejection. Not rebellion. They **treated lightly** what they received.
- Think about that phrase. "Treated lightly" — that's the *exact opposite* of "enjoy." Webster's "enjoy" means to have, possess, use with satisfaction. To treat lightly is to *have* something of infinite worth and just... set it aside.
- The condemnation isn't for people who never had the words. It's for people who *had them* and didn't value them.

### The remedy

- And it's specific: "remember the new covenant, even the Book of Mormon... **not only to say, but to do**."
- It's not enough to *say* you believe the Book of Mormon. You have to *live* it. The words of eternal life aren't meant to stay on the page. They're meant to change how you treat your spouse. Your kids. Your neighbors.

---

## Landing (45 sec)

"So there's the question D&C 84 puts in front of you. The words of eternal life are truth, which is light, which is the Spirit of Jesus Christ. They're designed to pull you toward the Father. And they've been given to you.

Now — are you *enjoying* them? Having them, possessing them, using them, delighting in them? Or are you treating them lightly?

Because the remedy isn't complicated. It's just honest. Remember the covenant. Open the book. And then — don't just say it. *Do* it."
