# Provenance — Nephite Time-Keeping, Staying in Awe, and the Two Valences (Michael's threads, 2026-06-01)

*All scripture verbatim via gospel_get this session. Labels per 00-frame. These five threads (Michael's) interlock; the cross-links at the end are the gold.*

## 1. Nephite time-keeping — years as living things that "come" and "pass away" [ESTABLISHED + INTERPRETIVE]
- **Helaman 13:1** — Samuel prophesies "in the **eighty and sixth year**."
- **Helaman 14:2** — "**five years more cometh**, and then cometh the Son of God."
- **3 Nephi 1:1** — "the **ninety and first year had passed away**" — and the birth-sign (no darkness) comes right after. 86 + 5 = 91; "five years more" (the 87th–91st) pass away, and the sign lands as the 91st dies / the 92nd is born.
- **The "6 year" reconciliation [INTERPRETIVE — faithful]:** Michael's "the sign came in the 6 year" is NOT an error — it's the Nephite reckoning. They speak of years as living things: a year "cometh" (is born) and "passeth away" (dies). "Five years more cometh" = five years come-and-pass (87–91), so the sign arrives in the sixth (counting the 86th as year one: 86,87,88,89,90,91 = 6). We count calendar-abstractly (a fencepost number); they counted event-bound ("it came to pass"). The idiom is the reconciliation.
- **3 Nephi 8:2 — the humility of their own reckoning [ESTABLISHED]:** "**if there was no mistake made by this man in the reckoning of our time**, the thirty and third year had passed away." → The Nephites' OWN record hedges its date-count. **Discipline gold:** if the keepers of the record held their own years loosely ("if no mistake"), we have no business pinning OUR dates with certainty. Their humility about time is a rebuke to date-setting.

## 2. 3 Nephi 8:1 — the record is true BECAUSE the keeper was clean [ESTABLISHED] → ties to give-away-all-sins
- "we know our record to be true, for behold, it was **a just man** who did keep the record—for he truly did many **miracles** in the name of Jesus; and there was not any man who could do a miracle in the name of Jesus **save he were cleansed every whit from his iniquity**."
- **The logic:** the record's RELIABILITY rests on the record-keeper's PURITY. Provenance grounded in cleanliness. "Cleansed every whit from his iniquity" is the condition of miracle-power.
- **Michael's personal thread [INTERPRETIVE — faithful, becoming]:** "cleansed every whit from his iniquity" (3 Ne 8:1) = "I will **give away all my sins** to know thee" (Alma 22:18 — the give-away-all-my-sins study). Same doctrine: total cleansing, not partial. Worthiness for miracles = giving away ALL sins. → This is the BECOMING of the freedom study: the marked remnant (the tav, those who grieve abominations) ARE the cleansed; the freedom that matters is freedom *from* iniquity, won through Christ's blood. Michael's stated pursuit ("which I am trying to do") is the personal landing.
- **AI implication [SPECULATIVE — faithful]:** an AI can keep a record but cannot be "cleansed every whit" — it has no iniquity to give away and no agency to repent. The deepest reliability of a record, the BoM says, rests on the moral purity of a *steward* — something the machine cannot supply. The keeper's cleanness, not the tool's accuracy, is the final guarantor.

## 3. The antitype inversion [INTERPRETIVE — faithful, with one guardrail]
- Michael: "don't be like us, we're your type... that means we're the anti-type? the actual thing?"
- Typology: type = foreshadow (lesser/earlier); antitype = fulfillment (greater/actual). Mormon/Moroni abridged the Nephite collapse AS a warning-type FOR us (Benson: "Moroni saw our day"; Mormon 8:35). So **we are the intended audience and a live instance — in the warning-typology, the antitype, the "actual thing" the rehearsal was for.** This RAISES the stakes of agency: the warning was written for us → we have more light → 3 Ne 6:18 (taught → accountable) bites harder on us than on them.
- **GUARDRAIL [surface_tensions]:** "we're the antitype" is faithful as *intended-audience + live-instance*. It must NOT slide into "we are THE final/greatest/last fulfillment" → that's the date-adjacent "last generation" over-read. The pattern is REPEATABLE; we're A warned instance, and the whole point is the CONDITIONAL escape ("don't be like us" = it can be refused). Michael already holds this ("I'm not trying to [set a date]"). Keep it: higher accountability, open door.

## 4. 3 Nephi 2:1 — stay in awe; do not harden or forget [ESTABLISHED] → resolves the signs-discipline
- "thus passed away the ninety and fifth year also, and the people began to **forget** those signs and wonders... began to be **less and less astonished** at a sign or a wonder from heaven, insomuch that they began to be **hard in their hearts, and blind in their minds**, and began to **disbelieve** all which they had heard and seen."
- Only ~4 years after the stupendous birth-sign, they forgot and went numb. **The two errors with signs are: (a) decode them into a date (sign-seeking — cautioned against, provenance/06); (b) forget/harden against them (3 Ne 2:1 — the Nephite error).** The faithful MIDDLE is Michael's own words: **"stay in awe of them."** Watch faithfully, remain astonished, neither decoding a clock nor going numb. → This is the resolution of the whole eclipse/tav thread: the signs are wonders to stay-in-awe-of and be-turned-to-readiness by, not ciphers to crack nor wonders to forget.

## 5. "Great and dreadful" ≈ "great and marvelous" — same work/day, two houses [ESTABLISHED]
- **1 Nephi 14:7** — "I will work a **great and a marvelous work**... a work which shall be everlasting, **EITHER** on the one hand **or** on the other—**either** to the convincing of them unto peace and life eternal, **or** unto the deliverance of them to the **hardness of their hearts and the blindness of their minds**... brought down into captivity, and also into destruction."
- So "great and marvelous" (1 Ne 14:7) carries the SAME dual valence as "great and dreadful" (Mal 4:5): the SAME work/day is marvelous-unto-life for one house and dreadful-unto-destruction for the other (Mal 4:1-2: burns the proud / heals the faithful). The valence is set by which house you're in.
- **★ THE TIGHT CROSS-LINK:** 1 Ne 14:7's dreadful outcome = "**hardness of their hearts and the blindness of their minds**" — the same hard-heart/blind-mind imagery as 3 Nephi 2:1 ("hard in their hearts, and blind in their minds"); near-identical, though 14:7 uses the nouns (hardness/blindness) and 3 Ne 2:1 the adjectives (hard/blind). *(2026-06-12: softened from "the EXACT phrase" — the two are parallel and unmistakably linked, but not word-for-word identical.)* So the "dreadful" side of the great-and-marvelous work IS the hardening/blinding that comes from FORGETTING THE SIGNS (3 Ne 2:1). → The signs SORT: stay in awe → the marvelous side (peace, life eternal); forget/harden → the dreadful side (captivity, destruction). The same work, the same day, the same signs — two houses, decided by whether the heart stayed in awe or hardened.

## The weave (how these five interlock — the synthesis seed)
The Nephites reckoned time as living (1) and even hedged their own dates (8:2) — so we don't date-set; we stay in awe (4). The great work is one work with two valences (5), and the signs sort us by whether we stay in awe (4) or harden (3 Ne 2:1 = 1 Ne 14:7's dreadful phrase). We are the antitype — the warning's intended audience (3) — so our accountability is higher (3 Ne 6:18). And the record that warns us is trustworthy because its keeper was *cleansed every whit* (2) — which is the personal door: give away all sins (Alma 22:18), be the cleansed/marked remnant (the tav), and the same great day that is dreadful to the hardened is marvelous to you. **Freedom, finally, is freedom from iniquity, won by Christ's blood — and that is the one freedom no nation's fall can take.**
