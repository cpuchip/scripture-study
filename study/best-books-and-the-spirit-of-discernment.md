# The Best Books, and the Spirit That Knows Them Apart

*A study on when scripture itself licenses going outside its own walls, and what discernment standard the same scriptures give for doing so.*

---

## Binding Question

The Restoration explicitly commands its people to "seek out of the best books." When does that license operate, and what is the discernment standard the same scriptures give for it?

This study sits in a real practical tension. The workspace this study lives in was deliberately built around the LDS canon and Gospel Library — to keep the philosophies of men from getting mingled with scripture in the very tools we use to study scripture. That guardrail was wise. It is also true that the same canon, in several places, *commands* seeking light and truth more widely than the canon itself contains. Both are doctrine. Neither dissolves the other.

So the question is not whether to obey D&C 88:118. The question is what *the same revelation* means by it, and what gate it puts at the door.

---

## I. The License — Wider Than Most LDS Reading Patterns Assume

The "best books" verse is the one most often quoted, but it is the *culmination* of a much broader command, not a standalone proof-text. Read in context:

> "Teach ye diligently and my grace shall attend you, that you may be instructed more perfectly in theory, in principle, in doctrine, in the law of the gospel, in all things that pertain unto the kingdom of God, that are expedient for you to understand; Of things both in heaven and in the earth, and under the earth; things which have been, things which are, things which must shortly come to pass; things which are at home, things which are abroad; the wars and the perplexities of the nations, and the judgments which are on the land; and a knowledge also of countries and of kingdoms—" — [D&C 88:78-79](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)

Forty verses later, in the same revelation, the command surfaces in its more famous form:

> "And as all have not faith, seek ye diligently and teach one another words of wisdom; yea, seek ye out of the best books words of wisdom; seek learning, even by study and also by faith." — [D&C 88:118](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)

The same phrasing reappears in the Kirtland temple dedicatory prayer — Joseph speaks the Lord's words *back to the Lord*, over the temple itself:

> "And as all have not faith, seek ye diligently and teach one another words of wisdom; yea, seek ye out of the best books words of wisdom, seek learning even by study and also by faith." — [D&C 109:7](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/109.md)

> "And do thou grant, Holy Father, that all those who shall worship in this house may be taught words of wisdom out of the best books, and that they may seek learning even by study, and also by faith, as thou hast said." — [D&C 109:14](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/109.md)

The command is given *to the dedication of a temple*. This is not peripheral instruction. This is a covenant standard set into the consecration of sacred space.

It also is not solitary. Other revelations pile the same instruction in different shapes:

> "And set in order the churches, and study and learn, and become acquainted with all good books, and with languages, tongues, and people." — [D&C 90:15](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/90.md)

> "And, verily I say unto you, that it is my will that you should hasten to translate my scriptures, and to obtain a knowledge of history, and of countries, and of kingdoms, of laws of God and man, and all this for the salvation of Zion. Amen." — [D&C 93:53](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93.md)

The Articles of Faith make the same posture binding on every member:

> "If there is anything virtuous, lovely, or of good report or praiseworthy, we seek after these things." — [Article of Faith 13](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/a-of-f/1.md)

And finally, the eternal stakes of the command:

> "Whatever principle of intelligence we attain unto in this life, it will rise with us in the resurrection. And if a person gains more knowledge and intelligence in this life through his diligence and obedience than another, he will have so much the advantage in the world to come." — [D&C 130:18-19](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/130.md)

The pattern is dense and structural. *Six independent canonical witnesses* (D&C 88, 90, 93, 109, 130, AoF 13) command seeking light and intelligence beyond the immediate text of scripture itself. The burden of proof is not on the seeker who reads outside the canon. The burden is on the canon-only reader, who has to explain why six revelations got it wrong.

---

## II. The Standard — "By Study AND Also By Faith"

The same revelations that issue the license install the gate. Most readings catch the first half — *seek ye out of the best books* — and skim past the back half. *Seek learning, even by study **and also by faith***. The second clause is doing structural work.

What does "by faith" mean as a method of learning? Scripture answers in several places, and the answers converge.

**The Spirit is the discerner of what to receive.** Nephi gives the cleanest version:

> "Cursed is he that putteth his trust in man, or maketh flesh his arm, or shall hearken unto the precepts of men, save their precepts shall be given by the power of the Holy Ghost." — [2 Nephi 28:31](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/28.md)

The verse is usually quoted with the warning amputated from its exception clause. Read whole, it does not say avoid the precepts of men. It says *do not hearken to them — except when they are given by the power of the Holy Ghost*. The exception clause is the architecture. The Spirit is the gate. A precept of a man, attended by the Holy Ghost, is acceptable. The same precept without the Spirit is cursed. Source-location is not the determining variable. Spirit-presence is.

**The same Spirit is identifiable by what it leads to.** The Lord defines his own Spirit by its fruit:

> "Put your trust in that Spirit which leadeth to do good — yea, to do justly, to walk humbly, to judge righteously; and this is my Spirit." — [D&C 11:12](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/11.md)

Discernment is not opaque. It has a shape. *Doing good. Doing justly. Walking humbly. Judging righteously.* These are the marks. A book or a teacher or a video that draws the soul toward those four motions is yielding fruit consistent with the Spirit. One that draws away from them is not, no matter how clever or how scriptural-sounding.

**Moroni gives the same standard in a different image:**

> "It is given unto you to judge, that ye may know good from evil; and the way to judge is as plain, that ye may know with a perfect knowledge, as the daylight is from the dark night." — [Moroni 7:15](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/moro/7.md)

The discernment is not exotic equipment. It is daylight-vs-dark-night. If the discernment is not happening, the problem is not the absence of equipment. It is the eye.

**Which leads to the deepest part of the standard. Discernment is a function of sanctification.** The same revelation that gives the best-books command first sets the condition:

> "If your eye be single to my glory, your whole bodies shall be filled with light, and there shall be no darkness in you; and that body which is filled with light comprehendeth all things. Therefore, sanctify yourselves that your minds become single to God." — [D&C 88:67-68](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)

This is the precondition for the license fifty verses later. *First* the eye becomes single. *Then* the body fills with light. *Then* that body comprehends all things. *Then* the seeking of best books becomes safe, because the comprehension is already in place.

**And finally, scripture gives a definition of truth that lets the standard be applied:**

> "And truth is knowledge of things as they are, and as they were, and as they are to come; And whatsoever is more or less than this is the spirit of that wicked one who was a liar from the beginning." — [D&C 93:24-25](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93.md)

A precept that describes things as they actually are passes the test. A precept that adds to that or subtracts from that fails — and the failure is identified, by name, as the spirit of the adversary. This is not a warning about content alone. It is a warning about *fit with reality*. Truth is correspondence with the actual.

## III. The Real Mingling Problem

The phrase *philosophies of men mingled with scripture* is familiar in temple-language tradition, but the exact wording is not in canonical scripture. The closest canonical equivalent is 2 Ne 28:31, which has already been examined. Read carefully, that verse does not say the danger is *outside-source contamination of canon*. It says the danger is *any precept, from any source, taught without the power of the Holy Ghost*. The location of the source — inside or outside the canon — is not the variable. The Spirit's presence is.

This is verifiable from inside the canon itself. Abinadi diagnosed the priests of Noah:

> "And ye have not applied your hearts to understanding; therefore, ye have not been wise." — [Mosiah 12:27](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/12.md)

The priests *had memorized the law*. They could quote Isaiah on demand. They had inside-the-canon credentials. Abinadi's accusation was that they had taken canonical material and taught it *without the power of the Holy Ghost* — and the resulting precepts, though sourced from scripture, were precepts of men. The mingling problem can occur entirely inside a canon-only reading list. Memorization is not discernment. Quotation is not light.

Brigham Young, in *Teachings of Presidents of the Church: Brigham Young* ch. 27, qualifies the seek-best-books command from the other side:

> "We are in a great school, and we should be diligent to learn... and read good books, although I cannot say that I would recommend the reading of all books, for it is not all books..."

The qualifier is honest. The license is not unbounded. Wide reading without discernment is not the gospel; it is liberal-arts curiosity dressed up. Discernment without wide reading risks becoming the Noahite priest pattern — knowing the right verses while missing the Spirit. The two halves balance.

The risk Michael named in his deliberate context-engineering choice was real: if we let untested non-canon sources sit alongside canon in the *tools we use for study*, the soft assumption can develop that they carry equivalent weight. The covenant in this workspace says read before quoting and let the Spirit be the discerner. That covenant works for canon. It also works for non-canon, *if* the discerner is in place. The integrity of the work is not protected by source-cordon alone. It is protected by the eye being single.

## IV. Cause Precedes Effect

This study began in a chat about a video that argued LDS Church members rank well on family stability metrics, *therefore* the church is doctrinally true. Michael named the inversion: scripture has the chain running the other direction. Spirit produces fruits; fruits produce Zion. Outcomes are the *effect*, not the *cause*, and not the *evidence by which doctrine is established*.

The chain, in canonical order:

- *Spirit*, received via covenant and the gifts ([D&C 46](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/46.md))
- *Fruits of the Spirit* — "love, joy, peace, longsuffering, gentleness, goodness, faith, meekness, temperance" ([Galatians 5:22-23](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/gal/5.md))
- *Faith → hope → charity*, the Mormon and Moroni triplet ([Moroni 7:40-48](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/moro/7.md))
- *Zion* — "and they had all things common among them; therefore there were not rich and poor, bond and free" ([4 Nephi 1:3](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/4-ne/1.md))

Reversing the chain — *Zion-shaped outcomes prove the doctrine* — is the same theological move Job's friends made and the same one Mormon edits the Book of Mormon to warn against:

> "We can see that the Lord in his great infinite goodness doth bless and prosper those who put their trust in him. Yea, and we may see at the very time when he doth prosper his people... yea, then is the time that they do harden their hearts." — [Helaman 12:1-2](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/hel/12.md)

This matters for the best-books question because the same inversion shows up in how Latter-day Saints sometimes choose what outside reading to receive. *Did this book make me feel uplifted? Did it move my metrics?* These are not the standard. The standard is whether the precepts in it are given by the power of the Holy Ghost (2 Ne 28:31), whether they lead to do good and walk humbly (D&C 11:12), whether they describe things as they actually are (D&C 93:24). Cause first, then effect. The book that flatters the worldview without yielding spiritual fruit fails the test even if it produced good feelings. The book that disrupts the worldview while drawing the soul toward Christ may pass it, even if it was uncomfortable to read.

The discernment standard is not "did this make me happy." It is "did this draw me closer to the One who is the source of light."

## V. The Whispered Foundation

There is a layer beneath all of this. Michael shared in the chat that prompted the study:

> "I have had effects before causes on numerous occasions with promptings from the Holy Ghost where I was placed by him through whisperings that helped me avoid injury... I have had visions of Jesus and loved ones beyond the veil when I needed it most, in my darkest of hours."

This is testimony. It is also the structural answer to "when does the license operate?" The license operates *for the soul that has the foundation*. The Spirit-discerner described in 2 Ne 28:31, in D&C 11:12, in Moroni 7:15, is not a method that someone can learn from a book. It is a relationship that develops through covenant, through obedience, through repeated whispered guidance, through the kind of dark-hour vision that puts steel in the spine. *That* is the substrate that makes wide reading safe.

A reader without the substrate — picking up Augustine or MacIntyre or Hart for the first time, with no Spirit-discerner in place — is in real danger of the 2 Ne 28:31 condition: hearkening to precepts of men, without the power of the Holy Ghost, and being cursed in the hearkening. A reader *with* the substrate can read the same Augustine, and the Spirit will whisper *take this; leave that*, the way the Spirit whispers in the dark-hour moments to take this turn and not that one. Same Spirit. Same discernment. Different domain.

This is also the answer to whether the workspace's deliberate canon-only foundation was wise. It was. And the wisdom of it was not "non-canon sources are bad." The wisdom was that the foundation has to be laid before the wider reading is safe. Canon-only first; canon-anchored seeking later. The license in D&C 88:118 was given to a *people who already had the temple, who had Joseph, who had ongoing revelation, who had the Spirit as their daily companion*. The order matters. Foundation, then expansion.

## VI. So When?

The honest answer is not a scheduling question. The Restoration does not say "after three years of canon-only study, branch out." It says: study and also by faith. The Spirit is the gate. If the Spirit is whispering *now is the time*, the time is now. If the Spirit is whispering *not yet*, the time is not yet. The discernment is daylight-vs-dark-night plain, given to the soul whose eye is single.

What can be said without overstepping:

- The foundation in this workspace is in place. Canonical depth, prophetic teaching, ongoing study, the discipline of read-before-quoting, the covenant of reading what is written, the Spirit-anchored relationship.
- The license in D&C 88:118 and its parallels is not optional. *Whatever principle of intelligence we attain unto in this life, it will rise with us in the resurrection.* What we choose not to learn here, we do not get there as a free gift.
- The standard is "by study AND also by faith." Wide reading without discernment risks Brigham Young's warning. Narrow reading without seeking risks the warning embedded in D&C 88:118 itself — *as all have not faith*. The verse is not addressed to the people who have already arrived. It is addressed to people still on the way, including us.

The choice of *when* and *what* and *how much* is exactly the kind of choice the Spirit is given to make. Not the agent's choice. Not the LDS subculture's choice. The choice of a soul whose eye is single, asking the Father in the Son's name, listening for the answer.

---

## VII. Becoming

A few honest questions, not prescriptions:

- Is the foundation actually in place, or has the canon-only discipline started to substitute *for* the Spirit-discerner rather than train it? The first is wisdom. The second is the Noahite priest pattern in slow motion.
- Where in this week's study has the Spirit whispered *take this; leave that*? If the answer is "I haven't been listening for that," the discernment muscle is not being exercised, regardless of how canonical the reading list is.
- If a non-canon source were to be brought in, what is the *binding question* it would serve? Bringing Augustine in to add a name to the footnotes is not the license. Bringing Augustine in because a specific question — *what does the Restoration mean by* X *that the wider Christian tradition has wrestled with for 1,500 years* — would actually be helped by that engagement, is closer to the license. The question precedes the source. Always.
- Where is the Spirit currently giving "more"? [2 Nephi 28:30](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/28.md) — *unto him that receiveth I will give more.* The license to seek widely is paired with a promise that the seeking will be answered. If the seeking has stopped, the giving has not been refused; it has been declined.

The most honest version of this study's conclusion is this: the workspace was wisely founded on canon. The canon itself, multiple times and in temple-consecrated form, commands seeking widely. The standard for that seeking is the Spirit, identifiable by its fruits, and exercised by a soul whose eye is single. The when is the Spirit's call. The work in front of us is to make sure the eye is single, so the call can be heard.
