## Source 1 — "Player Character Deaths" (Mercer) — extraction (verbatim-grounded)

The through-line: a PC death is crafted on **two tracks at once — the fiction AND
the people.** Both are persona-defining.

**FICTION track:**
- **Stakes need real danger.** "it's the danger you must maintain in your game if
  it's to hold any real weight and stakes for the players" (t37); death is "a real
  possibility" told to players up front (t53–58). → *sacrifice-and-loss*: loss is
  only meaningful if genuinely possible; establish it early.
- **Don't gloss the beat — make it cinematic.** "when the moment does happen, don't
  gloss over it. Explain the death as cinematically as you can, letting the epic
  final moments ring out" (t74–83). → *emotional-resonance*: dwell in the climax,
  don't rush past it.
- **A crystallized last line.** "let the player get a few last words out as a
  battle cry or inspiration… a very clear crystal moment for that character to
  really be remembered" (t102). → *character-voice* + *sacrifice-and-loss*: the
  death earns a defining final line.
- **Persistent memory = causal callbacks.** bracelets of remembrance, "name their
  new keep after the dead," "use their name as a war cry in future struggles"
  (t195–205). → *therefore-but-not-and-then*: the loss CAUSES later beats; it
  leaves marks the ongoing story refers back to.
- **Reincorporate the new PC causally — 7 hooks.** tie to the overarching quest /
  the antagonist / a backstory NPC / an essential artifact / avenging the fallen /
  a promoted minor NPC (t224–290). Every hook is a *causal* tie, never "and then a
  new guy shows up." → *therefore-but-not-and-then* in its purest teaching form.

**PEOPLE track (the gamemaster-persona core):**
- **In-fiction grief ritual.** "let the group mourn in game, in character… hold a
  role playing wake," "celebration of their life over hanging on to the sadness"
  (t113–120). → *emotional-resonance*: process loss inside the story; turn grief to
  celebration.
- **Give the bereaved player authorship.** "let the player… write an epilogue…
  down those unspoken words" (t138). → agency returned to the one who lost.
- **Tend the people out-of-game.** the after-session meal/drinks, "remind yourself
  that it's just a game and you're all friends still having a good time even when
  the sad moments happen" (t157–164). → **GM persona trait: care for the table, not
  just the campaign.**
- **Candor, not detached omnipotence.** "Trust me. It sucks." (t225). → **GM
  persona trait: warmth + honesty**; the DM is a fellow human, not an oracle.

## Gamemaster persona traits (emerging)
1. **Dual attention** — every big beat is crafted as story AND tended as people.
2. **Care for the table** — the DM safeguards the players' experience, not just the
   plot (after-session ritual; "it's just a game, we're friends").
3. **Candor + warmth** — admits difficulty ("it sucks"); not a detached omniscient.
4. **Hands authorship back** — the bereaved player writes the epilogue; agency is
   shared, not hoarded by the DM.
(These pair with the presiding/judges-not-executors ethos: the DM presides over the
table's shared story, governing by invitation, not compulsion.)
