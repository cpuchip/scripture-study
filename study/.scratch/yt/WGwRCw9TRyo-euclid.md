## The honest tension (surface_tensions — covenant)
Euclid = pure rationalism: "derive, with complete certainty, from pure thought
alone, results not at all obvious" (t516–530). Scripture study is **Euclidean in
METHOD, NOT in EPISTEMOLOGY.** Our verification is the Spirit + fruit (D&C 9,
Moroni 10, Alma 32's seed/fruit), not deductive necessity. Russell wanted
"everything proven from nothing," was disappointed you must accept axioms — the
gospel's axioms (faith) aren't self-evident postulates accepted by intellect;
they're received and TESTED BY LIVING THEM. This bounds the parallel and makes it
honest: we borrow Euclid's rigor of *form*, not his claim of certainty.
