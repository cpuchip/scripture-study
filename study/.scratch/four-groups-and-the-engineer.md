The new study cites these by reference and does not re-quote scriptures whose verbatim text was already verified in those scratch files.

### New apostolic source added

**Elder David A. Bednar, "In the Space of Not Many Years," October 2024 General Conference.** Local file: [study/talks/202410-35bednar.md](../talks/202410-35bednar.md). Canonical source: [general-conference/2024/10/35bednar.md](../../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2024/10/35bednar.md).

The Bednar 2024 talk's load-bearing contribution to this study:
1. The prosperity → pride → forgetting → boasting → Spirit-withdraws mechanism from Helaman, with specific verse citations.
2. The reading of 1 Nephi 8:28 (Group 2's fall into forbidden paths) as a worked example of pride. Bednar's exact framing in the local study notes: "After tasting the fruit of the tree of life, some 'were ashamed, because of those that were scoffing at them; and they fell away into forbidden paths and were lost.' They had tasted the love of God and still fell. That's the power of pride."
3. The "in the space of not many years" phrase, repeated three times across Helaman (4:26; 6:32; 7:6), as the speed of collapse.
4. The Lamanite virtues of "easiness and willingness to believe" ([Helaman 6:36](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/hel/6.md)) and "walking circumspectly" ([Helaman 15:5](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/hel/15.md)).
5. The self-diagnostic on pride: if you believe you are too strong to fall, the belief itself is the disease.
6. The institutional-versus-individual apostasy distinction: the Church will stand; the individuals in the Church can still fall.

All Bednar 2024 readings in this new study are *paraphrased* from his framing as captured in the local study notes. The new study does NOT put direct quotes from Bednar in quotation marks. If/when this study is published on cpuchip.net, a final pass should verify Bednar's exact wording against the canonical talk text before any direct quotation is added.

## II. Engineer-application synthesis — what is new here

The new contributions of this study, not in the prior work:

1. **The Group 2 failure mechanism expanded.** The prior study (iron-rod-anchor) treats Group 2's failure as caving to the building's external mockery. This new study expands the failure to three structurally identical forms:
   - External shame (the building's mockery — the prior reading)
   - Self-shame (the soul's own assessment of falling short — Michael's contribution)
   - Comparison (looking at peers who appear to have arrived faster/easier — Michael's contribution)
   All three are the eye drifting from the rod to a comparator. Bednar's framing of Group 2's failure as pride underwrites the expansion: pride is the choice to measure with something other than the rod.

2. **The Helaman prosperity-pride-collapse mechanism applied to engineers.** Mapping:
   - Prosperity = the work shipping cleanly under the discipline
   - Pride = "I know how this works now, I have transcended the spec"
   - Forgetting = drops the spec / covenant / watching for the quick fix
   - Boasting = ships vibe-code
   - Spirit withdraws = the discernment and craft-resonance goes; the work degrades; the engineer doesn't notice
   This mapping is the new study's primary applied contribution.

3. **The Lamanite virtue as engineering discipline.** Easiness and willingness to believe + walking circumspectly = the daily, small, humble engagement with the principles that prevents pride. Specifically: the junior engineer new to the discipline can outpace the senior who hardened into pride.

4. **The Bednar self-diagnostic for engineers.** The senior engineer who thinks "I am in the discipline" is the engineer most at risk of dropping it. The corrective is the inverted posture: assume you could fall.

5. **The four groups as postures, not identities.** Reiterated from the prior study but made explicit for the engineer reader: the dream is diagnostic, not deterministic. Most days you are in Group 3, holding continually. Some days you catch yourself in Group 2 with the eye drifted. The work is to grip again.
