# Scratch — Tree of Life and the Faith-Hope-Charity Chain

## Binding Question

How does the faith-hope-charity chain (Romans 5, Moroni 7, 1 Cor 13) actually map onto the tree of life (Alma 32, 1 Nephi 8 & 11) — and what does it mean that the fruit is Christ Himself?

---

## Origin: The Insight from the Hope Study Chat

While reviewing the hope study (line 43, Romans 5 paragraph), I had written:

> "Tribulation is the soil. Patience is the root. Experience is the trunk. Hope is the branch. And love (the Greek behind charity) is the fruit. Hope is the penultimate term, the place where the chain has just enough vertical lift to open into love."

Michael caught it: "Do you see what you did there on 43? you built the tree of life! and the fruit IS Christ. That's worth a study on its own. which links Alma 32 to 1 Nephi 8 and 11"

The chat reply that surfaced the connection (preserved here so it survives compaction):

- **Alma 32** is *explicitly* the tree-of-life chapter framed as faith. The seed is the word; nourish it (Alma 32:41) and it springs up to a tree springing up unto everlasting life; "ye shall feast upon this fruit even until ye are filled" (32:42).
- **1 Nephi 8** — Lehi sees the tree, partakes of the fruit, "most sweet, above all that I ever before tasted… white, to exceed all the whiteness that I had ever seen" (1 Ne. 8:11).
- **1 Nephi 11** — Nephi asks for the interpretation, and the angel shows him "a virgin… the mother of the Son of God" (11:18), then "the Lamb of God going forth among the children of men" (11:24-25), and concludes: "the tree which thy father saw is the love of God, which sheddeth itself abroad in the hearts of the children of men; wherefore, it is the most desirable above all things" (11:22). And v. 25: "the fountain of living waters, or the tree of life; which waters are a representation of the love of God."

The chain runs:
- Alma 32: word → faith → nourishment → tree → fruit (everlasting life)
- 1 Nephi 11: tree = love of God = Christ. Fruit is Christ Himself, manifest as the Lamb.
- Romans 5: tribulation → patience → experience → hope → love of God shed abroad in our hearts by the Holy Ghost.

**The phrase parallel:** Romans 5:5 — "the love of God is shed abroad in our hearts." 1 Nephi 11:22 — "the love of God, which sheddeth itself abroad in the hearts of the children of men." Same verb. Same direction. Paul and Nephi describing the same outpouring.

If that parallel holds, the faith-hope-charity chain *is* the tree of life sequence:
- Faith = the seed planted (Alma 32)
- Nourishment / patience / experience = the tree growing
- Hope = the branch reaching toward the fruit
- Charity / love of God = the fruit itself = Christ

## Verified Quotes & Observations

### Romans 5:1-5 (verified from `nt/rom/5.md`)

> "Therefore being justified by faith, we have peace with God through our Lord Jesus Christ: By whom also we have access by faith into this grace wherein we stand, and rejoice in hope of the glory of God. And not only so, but we glory in tribulations also: knowing that tribulation worketh patience; And patience, experience; and experience, hope: And hope maketh not ashamed; because the love of God is shed abroad in our hearts by the Holy Ghost which is given unto us."

Observation: v.1-2 already pre-establish faith → hope. v.3-5 then trace HOW hope is produced through tribulation. The chain ends with love of God shed abroad. This is BOTH the production chain (v.3-5) AND the faith-hope-charity triplet (v.1, 2, 5). Paul is doing both at once.

### 1 Nephi 8:10-12 (verified from `bofm/1-ne/8.md`)

> "And it came to pass that I beheld a tree, whose fruit was desirable to make one happy. And it came to pass that I did go forth and partake of the fruit thereof; and I beheld that it was most sweet, above all that I ever before tasted. Yea, and I beheld that the fruit thereof was white, to exceed all the whiteness that I had ever seen. And as I partook of the fruit thereof it filled my soul with exceedingly great joy."

Observation: Lehi's encounter is sensory — sweet, white, joy. The tree's purpose is *partaking*, not admiring. Lehi immediately wants his family to taste it too — the partaking creates a desire to share. (Foreshadow of charity / the soul that eats becomes one who reaches.)

### 1 Nephi 11:8-9, 21-25 (verified from `bofm/1-ne/11.md`)

Nephi sees the same tree (v.8) — "the beauty thereof was far beyond, yea, exceeding of all beauty; and the whiteness thereof did exceed the whiteness of the driven snow." Then asks for the interpretation (v.11).

The angel's reveal sequence (v.13-25):
- v.13-18: Nephi sees the virgin in Nazareth — "the mother of the Son of God"
- v.20-21: She bears a child. Angel says: "Behold the Lamb of God, yea, even the Son of the Eternal Father! Knowest thou the meaning of the tree which thy father saw?"
- v.22: Nephi answers: "Yea, it is the love of God, which sheddeth itself abroad in the hearts of the children of men; wherefore, it is the most desirable above all things."
- v.23: Angel: "Yea, and the most joyous to the soul."
- v.24: "I beheld the Son of God going forth among the children of men; and I saw many fall down at his feet and worship him."
- v.25: "the rod of iron, which my father had seen, was the word of God, which led to the fountain of living waters, or to the tree of life; which waters are a representation of the love of God; and I also beheld that the tree of life was a representation of the love of God."

Observations:
1. The angel asks the meaning of the tree IMMEDIATELY after Nephi sees the Lamb. The juxtaposition is the answer. The tree-question is bracketed by Lamb-vision. The tree IS the Lamb's meaning.
2. v.22 phrase: "sheddeth itself abroad in the hearts of the children of men."
3. v.25 doubles down: tree = love of God; fountain/living waters = love of God. Two symbols, one meaning.
4. Note v.25's word-of-God = rod-of-iron. Held alongside Alma 32:28 ("we will compare the word unto a seed"), the *word* appears twice in the tree-vision system: as the rod that LEADS to the tree, and as the seed that GROWS into the tree. Same word, two functions.

### Alma 32:27-43 (verified from `bofm/alma/32.md`)

> v.28: "we will compare the word unto a seed... if it be a true seed, or a good seed, if ye do not cast it out by your unbelief... it will begin to swell within your breasts..."

> v.41: "But if ye will nourish the word, yea, nourish the tree as it beginneth to grow, by your faith with great diligence, and with patience, looking forward to the fruit thereof, it shall take root; and behold it shall be a tree springing up unto everlasting life."

> v.42: "And because of your diligence and your faith and your patience with the word in nourishing it, that it may take root in you, behold, by and by ye shall pluck the fruit thereof, which is most precious, which is sweet above all that is sweet, and which is white above all that is white, yea, and pure above all that is pure; and ye shall feast upon this fruit even until ye are filled, that ye hunger not, neither shall ye thirst."

Observations:
1. Alma 32 uses the SAME descriptors as 1 Nephi 8: "sweet," "white." The fruit is the same fruit.
2. The verbs producing the tree: faith, diligence, patience, long-suffering (v.43). NOT "hope" or "love" explicitly — although Alma 32:21 (earlier in the chapter) is the famous hope-verse: "if ye have faith ye hope for things which are not seen, which are true."
3. v.42 "hunger not, neither shall ye thirst" — echoes John 4:14 (woman at the well, living water) and John 6:35 (bread of life, "he that cometh to me shall never hunger"). The fruit imagery is *Christological* in NT terms — Christ Himself is what satisfies the hunger.
4. Alma 32:21 hope verse + 32:41 patience verse + Romans 5:3-5 (patience→experience→hope) align beautifully. Both texts produce hope through patience.

## Critical Analysis Notes

### Strongest claim: the phrase parallel is real
Romans 5:5: "the love of God is shed abroad in our hearts."
1 Nephi 11:22: "the love of God, which sheddeth itself abroad in the hearts of the children of men."
Verb ("shed/sheddeth abroad"), object ("love of God"), location ("hearts") all match. This is not coincidence — Joseph translating the Book of Mormon was steeped in KJV English. The diction echo is intentional or providential.

### Weakest link: "the chain IS the tree" is interpretive synthesis, not explicit text
No single verse says "faith is the seed, hope is the tree, charity is the fruit." That's a synthesis. Honest framing: I am cross-wiring two scriptural symbol systems that share vocabulary (love of God, sweet/white fruit, hearts) and producing a third reading neither passage gives alone. This needs to be flagged as interpretation.

### Counter-reading to engage
Alma 32:41 says the tree is "a tree springing up unto everlasting life." 1 Nephi 11:25 says the tree is "a representation of the love of God." Are these the same tree? Probably yes — because the love of God IS everlasting life received (D&C 14:7: "if you keep my commandments and endure to the end you shall have eternal life, which gift is the greatest of all the gifts of God"). And John 17:3: eternal life = to know God. The tree is the same tree because love-of-God-shed-in-heart IS eternal-life-received.

### Is the fruit "Christ Himself" or "the experience of God's love"?
1 Ne 11:22-25 says tree = love of God (abstract noun). But the sequence — angel shows Lamb → immediately asks meaning of tree — forces the reader to read tree-as-Lamb. v.23 "most joyous to the soul" is the subjective side; v.24-25 with the Lamb walking among men is the objective side. The text holds both. Most rigorous statement: the fruit IS the love of God experienced, AND that love-of-God is incarnate in the Lamb. Christ and the love of God are the same fruit because Christ is the love of God made flesh.

### Posture check
Michael surfaced this connection. I am building it out. Risk: confirmation bias — once I see the parallel, every text confirms it. Counter-evidence I should sit with: Alma 32 doesn't use "hope" in v.27-43 (uses faith, diligence, patience, long-suffering). The mapping isn't airtight. The honest version of the claim: "these two systems use overlapping vocabulary in ways that mutually illuminate, not ‘Alma 32 is encoding the faith-hope-charity triplet.’"

### What the text gives that I should not over-claim
- Alma 32 explicitly: word→seed→tree→fruit; produced by faith + patience + diligence; fruit is everlasting life.
- 1 Ne 11 explicitly: tree = love of God = (juxtaposed with) Lamb of God; rod = word of God.
- Romans 5 explicitly: tribulation→patience→experience→hope; love of God shed abroad by Holy Ghost.

The SYNTHESIS (chain = tree; charity-fruit = Christ) is mine. I should own it as synthesis, not present it as something the text states.

### Section IV gap to plug
The Romans 5 → tree-of-life mapping needs the explicit caveat that Alma 32 doesn't use the word "hope" in the seed-to-tree section. The connection is via Alma 32:21 (hope earlier in the chapter) plus the Romans 5 chain. Stating that openly strengthens the study, not weakens it.
