# Scratch — The Prosperity Covenant
## Binding Question

What is the prosperity covenant, how does it run through the whole Book of Mormon, and what does it actually promise (and not promise)?

---

## Outline

I. The Fountainhead (1 Ne 2:20–21)
II. The Chain: How the Covenant Runs (2 Ne 1:20; 2 Ne 4:4; Mosiah 2:22; Alma 9:13; Alma 50:20)
III. The Jaredite Parallel (Ether 2:7–12)
IV. What "Prosper" Means — and the trap (Hel 12:1-3; Hel 3:33-36)
V. The Two Halves: temporal/communal vs. spiritual/personal
VI. Conditional-Land Theology (1 Ne 17:35) — tenure by righteousness, not lineage
VII. The Hard Edge: national covenant vs. individual and modern application

## Verified Quotes and Observations

### 1 Ne 2:20–21 (the fountainhead — spoken to Nephi personally)
Verified from gospel_get "1 Nephi 2":
- v.20: "And inasmuch as ye shall keep my commandments, ye shall prosper, and shall be led to a land of promise; yea, even a land which I have prepared for you; yea, a land which is choice above all other lands."
- v.21: "And inasmuch as thy brethren shall rebel against thee, they shall be cut off from the presence of the Lord."

OBSERVATION: This first utterance is notably *asymmetric*. The positive side (v.20) says "prosper and be led to a land." The negative side (v.21) says "cut off from the presence of the Lord." Two different things — prosperity is temporal/geographic, cutting off is spiritual/relational. The mature formulation in 2 Ne 1:20 will fuse them into a single conditional statement, but the fountainhead keeps them in separate verses addressed to different people (ye = Nephi; thy brethren = Laman/Lemuel).

OBSERVATION: "Choose above all other lands" — the land has a moral/theological status, not merely a geographic one. Land itself is a covenantal character.

fn-20a cross-refs: Josh 1:7; 1 Ne 4:14; Mosiah 1:7 — the prosper-in-the-land formula has OT roots.
fn-21a: "cut off" cross-refs include 2 Ne 5:20–24, Alma 9:14 — these will become important.

### 2 Ne 1:9, 20, 31 (Lehi's deathbed speech — the full theology)
Verified from gospel_get "2 Nephi 1":
- v.9: "Wherefore, I, Lehi, have obtained a promise, that inasmuch as those whom the Lord God shall bring out of the land of Jerusalem shall keep his commandments, they shall prosper upon the face of this land; and they shall be kept from all other nations, that they may possess this land unto themselves."
- v.20: "And he hath said that: Inasmuch as ye shall keep my commandments ye shall prosper in the land; but inasmuch as ye will not keep my commandments ye shall be cut off from my presence."
- v.31: "Wherefore, because thou hast been faithful thy seed shall be blessed with his seed, that they dwell in prosperity long upon the face of this land; and nothing, save it shall be iniquity among them, shall harm or disturb their prosperity upon the face of this land forever."

OBSERVATION: v.9 is the broader covenant — addressed not just to Lehi's family but to "those whom the Lord God shall bring out of the land of Jerusalem." It's already proto-universal for this promised land. The security promise is communal ("kept from all other nations").

OBSERVATION: v.20 is the crystallized formula — this is the most quoted form. Note the parallelism: "ye shall prosper IN the land" // "ye shall be cut off FROM my presence." Two different prepositions: *in* (spatial, material) vs. *from* (relational, divine). This is the key to the two halves.

OBSERVATION: v.31 to Zoram — "nothing, save it shall be iniquity among them, shall harm or disturb their prosperity." The only thing that can break the covenant is iniquity. This is a striking inversion: the threat is internal, not external.

fn-20a "Inasmuch" cross-refs: Jarom 1:9; Omni 1:6; Mosiah 1:7; Alma 9:13; 36:30; 37:13; 3 Ne 5:22. The formula recurs throughout the entire book.
fn-7c: "cursed shall be the land for their sakes" — the land itself is cursed, not just the people.
fn-9b "inasmuch" → 2 Ne 4:4; Alma 9:13 — explicit cross-reference chain.

### 2 Ne 4:4 (Lehi to Laman's children — a third generation)
Verified from gospel_get "2 Nephi 4:4":
- v.4: "For the Lord God hath said that: Inasmuch as ye shall keep my commandments ye shall prosper in the land; and inasmuch as ye will not keep my commandments ye shall be cut off from my presence."

OBSERVATION: This is Lehi quoting back the original covenant to Laman's children — the formula has stabilized into the exact bicolon form. Notably it is addressed to the grandchildren of the murmurers. The covenant isn't inherited genetically; it must be personally received by each generation. Lehi is making sure even Laman's seed hears it directly.

OBSERVATION: Wording difference from 2 Ne 1:20 is minimal — both halves parallel exactly. The covenant is now portable language, not just a personal promise to Nephi.

### Mosiah 2:22, 31, 36–38, 41 (Benjamin's covenant renewal)
Verified from gospel_get "Mosiah 2":
- v.22: "And behold, all that he requires of you is to keep his commandments; and he has promised you that if ye would keep his commandments ye should prosper in the land; and he never doth vary from that which he hath said; therefore, if ye do keep his commandments he doth bless you and prosper you."
- v.31: "As ye have kept my commandments, and also the commandments of my father, and have prospered, and have been kept from falling into the hands of your enemies, even so if ye shall keep the commandments of my son, or the commandments of God which shall be delivered unto you by him, ye shall prosper in the land, and your enemies shall have no power over you."
- v.36–38: "if ye should transgress and go contrary to that which has been spoken, that ye do withdraw yourselves from the Spirit of the Lord … that man … cometh out in open rebellion against God … the demands of divine justice do awaken his immortal soul to a lively sense of his own guilt, which doth cause him to shrink from the presence of the Lord, and doth fill his breast with guilt, and pain, and anguish, which is like an unquenchable fire."
- v.41: "they are blessed in all things, both temporal and spiritual; and if they hold out faithful to the end they are received into heaven, that thereby they may dwell with God in a state of never-ending happiness."

OBSERVATION: Benjamin amplifies the "cut off" side dramatically. In v.38, "cut off from presence" becomes phenomenological — the sinner's own soul *shrinks from* the Lord's presence. The cutting off is partly self-inflicted, not just divine rejection.

OBSERVATION: v.41 is the fullest expansion of what prosperity means — "blessed in all things, both temporal and spiritual." Benjamin holds both halves simultaneously. The covenant isn't purely material; it is also the condition for dwelling with God "in a state of never-ending happiness." That phrase is terminal — it carries through to resurrection and eternal life.

OBSERVATION: v.22 says "he never doth vary from that which he hath said." This reliability cuts both ways — the promise is as dependable as the warning.

fn-22c "promised" cross-refs: 1 Ne 4:14; Omni 1:6; Ether 2:7–12 — explicitly ties this promise to the Jaredites.
fn-41a "happy" cross-refs: Alma 50:23 — connects Benjamin's covenant to the only truly-happy-people passage in the whole book.

### Alma 9:13–14 (Alma cites it back to Lehi — 82 B.C.)
Verified from gospel_get "Alma 9":
- v.13: "Behold, do ye not remember the words which he spake unto Lehi, saying that: Inasmuch as ye shall keep my commandments, ye shall prosper in the land? And again it is said that: Inasmuch as ye will not keep my commandments ye shall be cut off from the presence of the Lord."
- v.14: "Now I would that ye should remember, that inasmuch as the Lamanites have not kept the commandments of God, they have been cut off from the presence of the Lord. Now we see that the word of the Lord has been verified in this thing, and the Lamanites have been cut off from his presence, from the beginning of their transgressions in the land."
- v.15: "Nevertheless I say unto you, that it shall be more tolerable for them in the day of judgment than for you, if ye remain in your sins, yea, and even more tolerable for them in this life than for you, except ye repent."

OBSERVATION: Alma quotes it as already-verified history. The Lamanites are living proof — they have been cut off "from the beginning of their transgressions." This is not future prophecy being preached; it is present historical fact being named.

OBSERVATION: The critical move in v.15 — the covenant *inverts* in terms of judgment. The Lamanites violated it in ignorance (through inherited false tradition, v.16). The Nephites of Ammonihah are violating it with "so much light and so much knowledge given unto them" (v.23). Greater privilege under the covenant means greater accountability, not greater protection. The covenant is NOT a permanent entitlement for "our people."

fn-13a "Inasmuch" → 2 Ne 1:20; Mosiah 1:7; Alma 37:13 — this cross-ref chain is the nervous system of the book.

### Alma 50:19–23 (Mormon's editorial retrospective — 67 B.C.)
Verified from gospel_get "Alma 50":
- v.19: "And thus we see how merciful and just are all the dealings of the Lord, to the fulfilling of all his words unto the children of men; yea, we can behold that his words are verified, even at this time, which he spake unto Lehi, saying:"
- v.20: "Blessed art thou and thy children; and they shall be blessed, inasmuch as they shall keep my commandments they shall prosper in the land. But remember, inasmuch as they will not keep my commandments they shall be cut off from the presence of the Lord."
- v.21: "And we see that these promises have been verified to the people of Nephi; for it has been their quarrelings and their contentions, yea, their murderings, and their plunderings, their idolatry, their whoredoms, and their abominations, which were among themselves, which brought upon them their wars and their destructions."
- v.22: "And those who were faithful in keeping the commandments of the Lord were delivered at all times, whilst thousands of their wicked brethren have been consigned to bondage, or to perish by the sword, or to dwindle in unbelief, and mingle with the Lamanites."
- v.23: "But behold there never was a happier time among the people of Nephi, since the days of Nephi, than in the days of Moroni."

OBSERVATION: Mormon is doing what he does constantly — citing the formula as an interpretive lens on his own war narrative. The covenant isn't just a promise; it is the HERMENEUTIC the entire record uses to explain history.

OBSERVATION: v.21 is remarkable — the source of their destruction is listed as "quarrelings … contentions … murderings … plunderings … idolatry … whoredoms … abominations … AMONG THEMSELVES." Not enemy military superiority. The external Lamanite threat materializes because of internal covenant-breaking. This is the self-implicating move.

OBSERVATION: v.22–23 holds the tension — "those who were faithful were delivered at all times" AND "there never was a happier time." The covenant works at the individual level AND the collective level simultaneously. But their coordination is not mechanical — faithful individuals can live in an unfaithful society and still receive personal deliverance.

fn-23a "happier" cross-refs: 2 Ne 5:27; Mosiah 2:41; 4 Ne 1:16. The "happiness" chain clusters around covenant-keeping moments.

### Ether 2:7–12 (the Jaredites — the same land, same terms, same warning)
Verified from gospel_get "Ether 2":
- v.7: "And the Lord would not suffer that they should stop beyond the sea in the wilderness, but he would that they should come forth even unto the land of promise, which was choice above all other lands, which the Lord God had preserved for a righteous people."
- v.8: "And he had sworn in his wrath unto the brother of Jared, that whoso should possess this land of promise, from that time henceforth and forever, should serve him, the true and only God, or they should be swept off when the fulness of his wrath should come upon them."
- v.9: "And now, we can behold the decrees of God concerning this land, that it is a land of promise; and whatsoever nation shall possess it shall serve God, or they shall be swept off when the fulness of his wrath shall come upon them. And the fulness of his wrath cometh upon them when they are ripened in iniquity."
- v.10: "For behold, this is a land which is choice above all other lands; wherefore he that doth possess it shall serve God or shall be swept off; for it is the everlasting decree of God. And it is not until the fulness of iniquity among the children of the land, that they are swept off."
- v.11: "And this cometh unto you, O ye Gentiles, that ye may know the decrees of God—that ye may repent, and not continue in your iniquities until the fulness come, that ye may not bring down the fulness of the wrath of God upon you as the inhabitants of the land have hitherto done."
- v.12: "Behold, this is a choice land, and whatsoever nation shall possess it shall be free from bondage, and from captivity, and from all other nations under heaven, if they will but serve the God of the land, who is Jesus Christ, who hath been manifested by the things which we have written."
- v.15: "my Spirit will not always strive with man; wherefore, if ye will sin until ye are fully ripe ye shall be cut off from the presence of the Lord."

OBSERVATION: This is the covenant's deepest statement. Three things distinguish Ether 2 from the Nephite formulations:
1. The language shifts from "prosper/cut off" to "serve / swept off." Not prosperity — freedom from bondage. Not cut off from presence — physically destroyed as a people. This is the darker half of the covenant's range.
2. The covenant is given **before** the Jaredites are even on the land. The covenant precedes possession. It is constitutional — the terms of occupancy.
3. "Whatsoever nation" — v.9, v.10, v.12. The covenant is not ethnic. It is not tied to Jared's seed. It is tied to the land and applies to every people that possesses it. The Canaanites (Nephite equivalent in the land's prior history), the Jaredites, the Nephites, and (v.11) "ye Gentiles" — i.e., us — are all under the same decree. Moroni is applying this directly to 19th-century America.
4. "The God of the land, who is Jesus Christ" — v.12. The covenant is explicitly Christological. This is not generic monotheism; it is covenant allegiance to the specific person of Christ.

fn-9c "ripened" → Gen 15:16. The Canaanites are the original type — God told Abraham they couldn't have the land yet because "the iniquity of the Amorites is not yet full." Ripening takes time. The Lord gives full opportunity.

### 1 Ne 17:33–38 (conditional-land theology — tenure by righteousness)
Verified from gospel_get "1 Nephi 17":
- v.33: "And now, do ye suppose that the children of this land, who were in the land of promise, who were driven out by our fathers, do ye suppose that they were righteous? Behold, I say unto you, Nay."
- v.34: "Do ye suppose that our fathers would have been more choice than they if they had been righteous? I say unto you, Nay."
- v.35: "Behold, the Lord esteemeth all flesh in one; he that is righteous is favored of God. But behold, this people had rejected every word of God, and they were ripe in iniquity; and the fulness of the wrath of God was upon them; and the Lord did curse the land against them, and bless it unto our fathers; yea, he did curse it against them unto their destruction, and he did bless it unto our fathers unto their obtaining power over it."
- v.37: "And he raiseth up a righteous nation, and destroyeth the nations of the wicked."
- v.38: "And he leadeth away the righteous into precious lands, and the wicked he destroyeth, and curseth the land unto them for their sakes."

OBSERVATION: v.34 is the most direct anti-triumphalist statement in the entire book. Nephi explicitly tells his own brothers: *if the Canaanites had been righteous, WE would have been the ones swept off.* Privilege of birth confers nothing. The covenant is utterly non-ethnic. This collapses the Israelite/Nephite/"chosen people" triumphalism completely.

OBSERVATION: v.35 — "he that is righteous is favored of God." Full stop. Not "he who is of our lineage." The favored state tracks moral condition, not genealogy.

fn-35a: cross-refs to Acts 10:15 (Peter's vision — no man is common or unclean), Rom 2:11 (no respect of persons with God), 2 Ne 26:33. The NT and BoM coordinate on impartiality of God's covenant.

### Helaman 12:1–3 (the pride-cycle stated as law — ca. 6 B.C.)
Verified from gospel_get "Helaman 12":
- v.1: "And thus we can behold how false, and also the unsteadiness of the hearts of the children of men; yea, we can see that the Lord in his great infinite goodness doth bless and prosper those who put their trust in him."
- v.2: "Yea, and we may see at the very time when he doth prosper his people, yea, in the increase of their fields, their flocks and their herds, and in gold, and in silver, and in all manner of precious things of every kind and art; sparing their lives, and delivering them out of the hands of their enemies; softening the hearts of their enemies that they should not declare wars against them; yea, and in fine, doing all things for the welfare and happiness of his people; yea, then is the time that they do harden their hearts, and do forget the Lord their God, and do trample under their feet the Holy One—yea, and this because of their ease, and their exceedingly great prosperity."
- v.3: "And thus we see that except the Lord doth chasten his people with many afflictions, yea, except he doth visit them with death and with terror, and with famine and with all manner of pestilence, they will not remember him."

OBSERVATION: Mormon names the internal logic that makes the covenant's reward dangerous: prosperity → ease → forgetting → hardening → trampling. The very thing the covenant promises becomes the engine of its undoing. This is not a bug in the covenant — it is the anthropological problem the covenant exists within.

OBSERVATION: "at the very time" — this is not "after many years." It is synchronous. The prosperity is still ongoing when the forgetting begins. The trap is not that prosperity eventually leads to pride; it is that prosperity AS prosperity, in the very moment of receiving it, is the trigger.

OBSERVATION: "this because of their ease, and their exceedingly great prosperity" — Mormon names the cause explicitly. The covenant's blessing is the cause of the covenant's breaking.

### Helaman 3:33–36 (prosperity → pride in the church — ca. 46–51 B.C.)
Verified from gospel_get "Helaman 3":
- v.33: "And in the fifty and first year of the reign of the judges there was peace also, save it were the pride which began to enter into the church—not into the church of God, but into the hearts of the people who professed to belong to the church of God—"
- v.34: "And they were lifted up in pride, even to the persecution of many of their brethren."
- v.35: "Nevertheless they did fast and pray oft, and did wax stronger and stronger in their humility, and firmer and firmer in the faith of Christ, unto the filling their souls with joy and consolation, yea, even to the purifying and the sanctification of their hearts, which sanctification cometh because of their yielding their hearts unto God."
- v.36: "And it came to pass that the fifty and second year ended in peace also, save it were the exceedingly great pride which had gotten into the hearts of the people; and it was because of their exceedingly great riches and their prosperity in the land; and it did grow upon them from day to day."

OBSERVATION: v.36 traces the pride back to "their exceedingly great riches and their prosperity in the land." Same pattern as Hel 12 — prosperity → pride. But here the counter-witness is named: v.35's community of the humble, who "wax stronger and stronger in their humility" BECAUSE of adversity (they were being persecuted), not despite it. The humble thrive under pressure; the proud thrive under ease and then fail. The covenant's reward sorts people differently depending on which spirit they bring to it.

OBSERVATION: v.33 is precise — "pride which began to enter into the church — not into the church of God, but into the hearts of the people who professed to belong." Mormon's disclaimer here is extraordinary: the covenant community does not escape the pride trap by virtue of membership. The institution's formal boundaries don't determine who is inside the covenant.

### Alma 36:1 (Alma to Helaman — additional chain node)
Verified from gospel_get "Alma 36:1":
- v.1: "My son, give ear to my words; for I swear unto you, that inasmuch as ye shall keep the commandments of God ye shall prosper in the land."

OBSERVATION: This is Alma's most personal transmission of the formula — to his son, with an oath ("I swear unto you"). He is not quoting policy; he is staking his personal witness. The formula has moved from divine oracle (1 Ne 2) to patriarchal covenant (2 Ne 1) to pedagogical formula (Mosiah 2) to historical interpretation (Alma 9, 50) to parental oath (Alma 36). By this stage it is the Book of Mormon's most internalized teaching.

### Webster 1828 Word Study
Verified via mcp__webster__define:

**PROSPER** (1828):
1. "To favor; to render successful."
2. (implied in the intransitive sense) to thrive, succeed, make gain, grow, increase.

OBSERVATION: The 1828 sense has an active-voice first meaning — to prosper is "to render successful." This is important: the covenant promise is not passively receiving good fortune; it is being *made successful* by divine favor. The subject is the Lord rendering, not the person receiving. The modern sense shifts slightly to the intransitive ("to be prosperous"), but the 1828 sense keeps God as the active agent.

**PRESENCE** (1828):
1. "The state of being present, or of being within sight or call, or at hand"
2. "Neighborhood to the person of one of superior or exalted rank"
3. "The whole of the personal qualities of an individual; person; personality; especially, the person of a sovereign"

OBSERVATION: "Cut off from my presence" — in 1828, "presence" of a sovereign is not merely "being nearby." It is access to the royal person, with all that entails: protection, favor, jurisdiction, guidance, the whole of the relationship. To be cut off from the Lord's *presence* is to be removed from that relationship entirely — from his notice, his care, his guidance, his protection. This is not just geographic distance; it is relational severance.
