# Two Triplets, One Architecture

**Binding Question:** How do the triplets Faith-Hope-Charity and The Way-The Truth-The Life interrelate? Are they the same concepts viewed from different angles, or genuinely different? Is one human-centered (us-centric) and the other Christ-centered? Are they the same point seen from different vantage points?

---

## I. The Two Triplets — Mapping the Terrain

They arrive in scripture as two separate declarations, spoken by two different voices, on two different continents, separated by centuries and oceans.

The first comes from the Book of Mormon, echoing through Moroni's final sermon:

> "Wherefore, there must be **faith**; and if there must be faith there must also be **hope**; and if there must be hope there must also be **charity**." — [Moroni 10:20](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/moro/10.md)

And then, in Ether 12, Moroni traces the same three virtues through his father's teachings and ends with a sentence that reframes the whole chain:

> "Faith, hope and charity **bringeth unto me** — the **fountain of all righteousness**." — [Ether 12:28](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/ether/12.md)

That is Christ speaking. The triad doesn't just make us better people. It brings us *to Him*.

Paul, writing from a Roman prison, closes his great discourse on love with nearly identical phrasing:

> "And now abideth **faith, hope, charity**, these three; but the greatest of these is charity." — [1 Corinthians 13:13](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/1-cor/13.md)

Three witnesses — Paul, Mormon, Moroni — each giving the same triplet in the same order, across oceans and centuries. The corpus has already established that this triplet is doctrinally significant, not rhetorical decoration. The [hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs](#) study shows that gospel doublets and triplets are structural, not pedagogical — the truth itself comes bound. The [enoch-charity](#) study demonstrates that these three are the architecture of Zion itself.

The second triplet arrives from the Upper Room:

> "Jesus saith unto him, I am **the way**, **the truth**, and **the life**: no man cometh unto the Father, but by me." — [John 14:6](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/john/14.md)

This is not a list of attributes. This is a triple declaration of *who He is*. The [way-truth-life](#) study established that this declaration connects directly to the tabernacle structure — the veil, the high priest, the holy of holies — and that Hebrews 10:19-20 explicitly links Christ's flesh to "a new and living way, through the veil."

Both triplets have clear scriptural anchors. Both appear multiple times across independent witnesses. Both have a progressive structure — each term builds on the previous one. But no study in the corpus has yet asked: *how do they relate to each other?*

That is the question this study answers.

---

## II. The Vantage Point Question — Whose Eyes?

The binding question asks whether one triplet is human-centered and the other Christ-centered, or whether they're the same reality viewed from different angles.

The answer is more complicated than either option alone.

Faith-Hope-Charity *is* the soul's progression — it describes what the traveler must exercise, feel, and become. Faith is what we exercise. Hope is what we feel. Charity is what we become. The [enoch-charity](#) study shows this progression mapped onto Enoch's life: faith to obey despite fear, hope to sustain the building of Zion through 365 years of difficulty, charity as the love that filled his bowels and made him weep with God.

But the destination of that progression is not self-improvement. Ether 12:28 is explicit: the triad "bringeth unto me." Christ is speaking. The us-centric progression is Christ-centric in its destination.

Way-Truth-Life, on the other hand, *is* a declaration of Christ's nature. He IS the Way. He IS the Truth. He IS the Life. The [way-truth-life](#) study shows how each term maps to His identity: the Way as the door and the path ([John 10:9](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/john/10.md)), the Truth as the embodiment of all knowledge ([D&C 93:24-26](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93.md)), the Life as the resurrection and the source of eternal life ([John 11:25](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/john/11.md)).

But its purpose is "no man cometh unto the Father, but by me." The Christ-centric declaration is us-centric in its application.

So neither triplet is purely one or the other. They're the same architecture described from opposite ends of the journey. One describes the path from the traveler's perspective. The other describes the same path from the destination's perspective.

Like describing a road as "the path from here to Zion" versus "the road Zion sends back."

The [discernment-and-the-comprehending-eye](#) study establishes something crucial for this section: comprehension is ontological, not merely intellectual. D&C 88:67-68 teaches:

> "If your eye be single to my glory, your whole bodies shall be filled with light, and there shall be no darkness in you; and that body which is filled with light comprehendeth all things."

The eye is the gate. What the eye is tuned to determines what the body fills with. What the body fills with determines what the body comprehends. Faith-Hope-Charity describes the progressive purification of the eye. Way-Truth-Life describes what the eye is moving toward. They're not different concepts. They're the same process, named from different stations along the way.

## III. The Structural Parallel — Progressive Sequence

The pattern in both triplets suggests something deeper than coincidence.

Both triplets share an identical progressive structure: **initiation → sustenance → culmination.**

### Faith-Hope-Charity

The [hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs](#) study traces this progression through Moroni 7 and Romans 5:

**Faith** is the trust that initiates. The [faith-01](#) study, drawing on the Lectures on Faith, establishes that faith is "the principle of action in all intelligent beings" and "a principle of power." It is not passive belief. It is the moving cause.

**Hope** is the orientation that sustains. Mormon teaches that "if a man have faith he must needs have hope" ([Moroni 7:42](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/moro/7.md)), and Paul traces hope's production: "tribulation worketh patience; and patience, experience; and experience, hope" ([Romans 5:3-4](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rom/5.md)). Hope sits in the middle structurally, not arbitrarily. It is the thing that keeps the journey going between start and finish.

**Charity** is the love that completes. Moroni 7:47 defines it: "charity is the pure love of Christ, and it endureth forever." The [charity](#) study shows this is not something we generate — it is "bestowed as a gift from God when we pray for it with all our heart" ([Moroni 7:48](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/moro/7.md)). And the [tree-of-life-and-the-chain](#) study shows that charity is the fruit of the tree of life — what you become after you have eaten.

### Way-Truth-Life

The [way-truth-life](#) study makes the same progression explicit:

**The Way** (beginning) implies a journey. You must start somewhere. It points to obedience — following a path. It requires faith — trusting the direction before seeing the destination. The temple endowment enacts this: we learn the plan, make covenants, enter the path.

**The Truth** (middle) implies understanding. It points to knowledge — light and revelation along the way. D&C 93:24 defines truth as "knowledge of things as they are, and as they were, and as they are to come." Christ embodies this truth. He "received a fulness of truth" ([D&C 93:26](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93.md)). In the endowment, we receive instruction, knowledge, and promises.

**The Life** (culmination) implies arrival. It points to eternal life — dwelling with God. John 17:3 is explicit: "This is life eternal, that they might know thee." The [know-god](#) study establishes that eternal life IS knowing God — not knowing about Him, but knowing Him. His character, His heart, His attributes. In the endowment, we pass through the veil into the celestial room.

### The Grace-for-Grace Framework

Both progressions mirror the pattern in D&C 93:12-13:

> "And he received not of the fulness at first, but continued from **grace to grace**, until he received a fulness."

The [way-truth-life](#) study connects this directly to the triplet: we enter the Way (repentance, baptism, covenant), grow in Truth (receive the Holy Ghost, temple ordinances), and finally receive Life (endure to the end, exaltation). The same grace-for-grace progression that describes Christ's growth in fulness describes our growth toward Him.

The middle term in each triplet — hope / truth — is the sustaining element. Hope keeps the soul oriented toward what is not yet visible. Truth illuminates the path as we walk it. Without the middle term, the journey collapses. Faith without hope has no direction. The Way without Truth is a path we cannot see.

The structural parallel between the two triplets is too precise to dismiss as incidental. The pattern suggests they describe the same architecture.

## IV. The Convergence Point — "Bringeth Unto Me"

The two triplets do not converge in abstract theology. They converge in the person of Christ.

Ether 12:28, the closing of Moroni's teaching on faith, hope, and charity, is spoken by Christ Himself:

> "Faith, hope and charity **bringeth unto me** — the **fountain of all righteousness**."

John 14:6, the declaration of Way-Truth-Life, is also spoken by Christ Himself:

> "I am the way, the truth, and the life: **no man cometh unto the Father, but by me**."

Both triplets end at the same place: communion with Christ.

The [enoch-charity](#) study shows that the triad's destination is not moral improvement — it is Christ. The [charity](#) study shows that the pure love of Christ is what we become when we pray for it with all our heart. And the [know-god](#) study establishes that eternal life — which is "the Life" in the second triplet — is knowing God. John 17:3 and Moses 1:39 form a matched pair: eternal life IS knowing God, and God's work and glory IS bringing us to that eternal life.

The two triplets are not parallel paths. They're the same path described from opposite ends:

- Faith-Hope-Charity describes **what the soul must become** (faith → hope → charity)
- Way-Truth-Life describes **what the soul must move toward** (way → truth → life)

The destination is identical: Christ.

The [tree-of-life-and-the-chain](#) study adds a crucial layer here. The fruit of the tree of life is the love of God, and the love of God is Christ Himself. The angel in 1 Nephi 11 shows Nephi the Lamb of God and then asks, "Knowest thou the meaning of the tree?" The juxtaposition is the answer. To partake of the fruit is to receive Christ.

So the faith-hope-charity chain ends at charity, which is the pure love of Christ. The way-truth-life declaration ends at life, which is knowing God. And knowing God produces charity. And charity is the pure love of Christ. And Christ is the way, the truth, and the life.

The circle closes. The convergence is not theoretical. It is personal. It is Christ.

## V. The Temple as the Physical Enactment

The temple endowment physically enacts both triplets simultaneously.

The [way-truth-life](#) study establishes the temple connection in detail. The endowment moves participants through a progressive journey:

1. **Creation** — the paradisiacal state
2. **The Fall** — separation from God's presence
3. **Law and Covenants** — the Savior taught, covenants made
4. **Through the Veil** — entering the celestial room, God's presence

This progression is the Way (the path from fallen state to redemption), the Truth (the instruction, knowledge, and promises received along the way), and the Life (entering God's presence in the celestial room).

Simultaneously, the participant's transformation follows the faith-hope-charity chain:

- **Faith** to enter — to make covenants, to trust the path
- **Hope** sustained by the anchor within the veil — Hebrews 6:19 teaches that hope "entereth into that within the veil"
- **Charity** as the love that completes the journey — the love of God shed abroad in the heart

The [hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs](#) study puts it beautifully: "The temple is hope's anchor made visible. We walk to where the anchor is, stand on the bottom for a few minutes, and walk back out."

The temple doesn't enact two different triplets. It enacts one architecture that has two names. The way-truth-life progression moves through sacred space. The faith-hope-charity transformation moves through the soul. They happen at the same time, in the same place, toward the same end.

The [discernment-and-the-comprehending-eye](#) study adds the final piece: the eye-tuning mechanism. D&C 88:67-68 teaches that if the eye is single to God's glory, the body fills with light, and "that body which is filled with light comprehendeth all things." The temple is where the eye is tuned — where we learn to see as God sees, where we are filled with the light that enables comprehension, where we pass through the veil that hope has been anchored to all along.

The temple provides physical witness that the two triplets describe the same architecture.

## VI. Synthesis — One Architecture, Two Names

The two triplets are **the same point seen from different vantage points**, but not because they're "the same concepts viewed from different angles" (which would make them interchangeable synonyms). They're **complementary descriptions of a single architecture**.

| | Faith-Hope-Charity | Way-Truth-Life |
|---|---|---|
| **Scriptural anchor** | Moroni 7, Ether 12, 1 Cor 13 | John 14:6 |
| **Starting point** | The soul (what we must become) | Christ (what He is) |
| **Direction** | Us → Christ ("bringeth unto me") | Christ → Father ("no man cometh unto the Father, but by me") |
| **Middle term** | Hope (orientation that sustains) | Truth (knowledge that illuminates) |
| **Culmination** | Charity (pure love of Christ) | Life (eternal life = knowing God) |
| **Destination** | Christ (Ether 12:28) | The Father through Christ (John 14:6) |
| **Vantage point** | The traveler looking forward | The destination looking back |

**They are not the same concepts.** Faith is not the Way. Hope is not the Truth. Charity is not the Life. Faith is what we exercise; the Way is what Christ IS. Hope is what we feel; the Truth is what Christ embodies. Charity is what we become; the Life is what Christ gives.

**But they ARE the same architecture.** The faith-hope-charity chain describes the *transformation of the traveler*. The way-truth-life declaration describes the *nature of the destination*. One answers "what must I become?" The other answers "who is it that I'm going to?"

And in the gospel, those are the same question.

Because eternal life IS knowing God ([John 17:3](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/john/17.md)), and knowing God produces charity ([Moroni 7:47-48](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/moro/7.md)), and charity is the pure love of Christ, and Christ is the way, the truth, and the life.

The temple enacts this simultaneously. The scriptures testify of it from both directions. The corpus already contains the pieces — this meta-study assembles them.

## Becoming

**Questions to sit with:**

- Where am I in this architecture? Am I at the beginning, exercising faith to enter the Way? Am I in the middle, sustained by hope and growing in truth? Am I nearing the culmination, where charity is what I'm becoming and life is what I'm approaching?

- When I pray for charity — for the pure love of Christ — am I aware that I'm praying to become like the Way, the Truth, and the Life? That the person I'm becoming is the person I'm moving toward?

- The temple enacts both triplets simultaneously. When I enter the temple, do I feel the architecture closing around me — the way-truth-life progression through the veil, and the faith-hope-charity transformation of my soul? Or do I go through the motions without sensing the convergence?

**This week:**

- Before entering the temple, pray: "Lord, let me feel both triplets at work. Let me feel the Way opening before me, the Truth illuminating my path, and the Life drawing me forward. And let me feel faith moving me, hope sustaining me, and charity completing me."

- When you read scripture this week, notice which triplet the passage is speaking from. Is it describing what you must become (faith-hope-charity) or what Christ IS (way-truth-life)? The text may surprise you.
