# Scratch — Softening What I Cannot Soften

## Binding Question

How does a person receive a softened heart when scripture says they cannot soften their own — and what is the human's actual part in that exchange?

## Verified Quotes

**1 Nephi 2:16** — "I, Nephi, being exceedingly young, nevertheless being large in stature, and also having great desires to know of the mysteries of God, wherefore, I did cry unto the Lord; and behold he did visit me, and did soften my heart that I did believe all the words which had been spoken by my father."

**1 Nephi 2:18–19** — "being grieved because of the hardness of their hearts I cried unto the Lord for them. And it came to pass that the Lord spake unto me, saying: Blessed art thou, Nephi, because of thy faith, for thou hast sought me diligently, with lowliness of heart." [Nephi cried for his brothers with the same verb that worked for himself; they were not softened. The cry that works is the cry of someone who has sought diligently with lowliness of heart.]

**Alma 22:15** — Lamoni's father: "What shall I do that I may be born of God, having this wicked spirit rooted out of my breast, and receive his Spirit, that I may be filled with joy, that I may not be cast off at the last day? Behold, said he, I will give up all that I possess, yea, I will forsake my kingdom, that I may receive this great joy." [The transactional offer. Aaron does not accept it.]

**Alma 22:16** — Aaron's redirection: "If thou desirest this thing, if thou wilt bow down before God, yea, if thou wilt repent of all thy sins, and will bow down before God, and call on his name in faith, believing that ye shall receive, then shalt thou receive the hope which thou desirest." [Aaron names what the king CAN bring: desire, bowing, repentance, calling, believing. Not the kingdom.]

**Alma 22:17–18** — "the king did bow down before the Lord, upon his knees; yea, even he did prostrate himself upon the earth, and cried mightily, saying: O God, Aaron hath told me that there is a God; and if there is a God, and if thou art God, wilt thou make thyself known unto me, and I will give away all my sins to know thee, and that I may be raised from the dead, and be saved at the last day. And now when the king had said these words, he was struck as if he were dead." [The trade: I will give all my sins, you make yourself known. Notice the conditional order — the revelation is asked for first; the giving is offered in exchange.]

**Ether 3:4–6** — "touch these stones, O Lord, with thy finger, and prepare them that they may shine forth in darkness... And it came to pass that when the brother of Jared had said these words, behold, the Lord stretched forth his hand and touched the stones one by one with his finger. And the veil was taken from off the eyes of the brother of Jared."

**Ether 3:13** — "Because thou knowest these things ye are redeemed from the fall; therefore ye are brought back into my presence."

**Alma 12:10–11** — "he that will harden his heart, the same receiveth the lesser portion of the word; and he that will not harden his heart, to him is given the greater portion of the word, until it is given unto him to know the mysteries of God until he know them in full. And they that will harden their hearts, to them is given the lesser portion of the word until they know nothing concerning his mysteries." [CRITICAL REFRAME. The agency-verb is "harden," not "soften." We cannot soften ourselves; we can stop hardening ourselves. The Lord then does the giving.]

**Helaman 3:35** — "they did fast and pray oft, and did wax stronger and stronger in their humility, and firmer and firmer in the faith of Christ, unto the filling their souls with joy and consolation, yea, even to the purifying and the sanctification of their hearts, which sanctification cometh because of their yielding their hearts unto God."

**Mosiah 5:2** — "the Spirit of the Lord Omnipotent... has wrought a mighty change in us, or in our hearts, that we have no more disposition to do evil, but to do good continually." [The result. The verb "wrought" is the Spirit's, not the people's.]

**Moroni 10:32** — "come unto Christ, and be perfected in him, and deny yourselves of all ungodliness; and if ye shall deny yourselves of all ungodliness, and love God with all your might, mind and strength, then is his grace sufficient for you, that by his grace ye may be perfect in Christ."

**D&C 93:36–37, 39** — "The glory of God is intelligence, or, in other words, light and truth. Light and truth forsake that evil one... And that wicked one cometh and taketh away light and truth, through disobedience, from the children of men." [The physics of softening. Light and truth FORSAKE the evil one — they're substrate that can be added to or taken away. Disobedience is the mechanism of subtraction.]

**Ezekiel 36:25–27** — "Then will I sprinkle clean water upon you, and ye shall be clean: from all your filthiness, and from all your idols, will I cleanse you. A new heart also will I give you, and a new spirit will I put within you: and I will take away the stony heart out of your flesh, and I will give you an heart of flesh. And I will put my spirit within you, and cause you to walk in my statutes." [Every active verb here is the Lord's: sprinkle, cleanse, give, put, take away, give, put, cause. The human is in the dative throughout — the recipient of action, not the actor.]

**Job 23:16** — "For God maketh my heart soft, and the Almighty troubleth me." [Said in lament, not in triumph. The softening is the same act as the troubling.]

## The Pattern That Emerges

The verbs cluster into two columns:

| Human side | Lord's side |
|---|---|
| desire, cry, seek diligently | visit |
| lowliness of heart | soften |
| bow, prostrate, call | reveal himself |
| repent, give away (sins), forsake | wrought a mighty change |
| yield | sprinkle, cleanse |
| deny (self of ungodliness) | give a new heart, put a new spirit |
| not harden | take away the stony heart |
| bring (the molten stones) | touch, take away the veil |
| love God with all might | sanctify, perfect, redeem |

The human verbs are all bring/give/yield/stop. None of them are "soften" or "change" or "make new." Those belong to the Lord. The human's whole part is at the boundary — bringing the broken thing, stopping the active break, yielding what we cannot fix. What crosses the boundary is the Lord's work.

And Job 23:16 names what it feels like from inside: the softening hurts. It is the same act as the troubling. We don't get softened *and then* feel peace; we feel troubled *because* we are being softened. Which is also exactly what happened to Lamoni's father — he was struck as if he were dead.

## Threads to Pull (Phase 3)

- The "two cries" of 1 Nephi 2 — Nephi's cry for himself worked; his cry for his brothers did not. The difference is in v.19 ("sought me diligently, with lowliness of heart"). Cry alone isn't enough; the cry has to come out of a sought-and-lowly heart.
- The "transactional offer" Aaron refuses (give up kingdom) vs. the trade Aaron accepts (give away sins). The king first offers what he OWNS; Aaron redirects to what he IS. The kingdom isn't the price. The pride is.
- The Lamoni-father conditional order in v.18 — *make yourself known to me AND I will give away all my sins to know thee*. He doesn't promise to repent first and then ask for the revelation. He asks for the revelation in the same breath as the promise. The trade is genuinely simultaneous — both sides happen at the moment of contact.
- Alma 12:10's NOT-harden as the locus of agency. This is the bridge between Todd's "you can't soften your own heart" and the practical question "then what am I supposed to DO Monday morning?" Answer: stop the things that are actively hardening.
- The morning-hour question: what specifically is hardening? In Michael's life: Marshfield-scroll, news, podcasts, politics, anything that lets the morning hour pass without bowing.

## Gap & Critical Analysis (Phase 3a)

**Tested claims:**

1. **Claim: scripture says we cannot soften our own hearts.** Tested against 1 Nephi 2:16 (true — the verbs are clear; Nephi did not soften his heart, the Lord did) and Job 23:16 (true — "God maketh my heart soft"). Also Ezekiel 36 — every active verb is the Lord's. The claim holds.

2. **Claim: the human's agency is in the not-hardening, per Alma 12:10.** Tested — the verse is explicit. "He that will not harden his heart" is the agency-bearing clause; "to him is given" follows. This holds.

3. **Claim: the cry is necessary but not sufficient.** Tested against 1 Nephi 2:18-19 — Nephi cried for his brothers with the same verb, and they weren't softened. The Lord's response (v.19) names what made Nephi's cry effective: he had "sought me diligently, with lowliness of heart." Holds. This is an important nuance — cheap crying out doesn't work. The cry has to be the surface of a sought, lowly heart.

4. **Claim: the trade in Alma 22:18 is simultaneous, not sequential.** Tested by reading the verse closely. "Wilt thou make thyself known unto me, and I will give away all my sins to know thee." The structure is conditional-and-promise in one breath. Not "I'll repent first then you reveal yourself." Both sides cross the boundary at the moment of contact. Holds — and this is genuinely important because it contradicts a common bargaining frame where we feel we must "earn" the revelation by some prior cleanness.
