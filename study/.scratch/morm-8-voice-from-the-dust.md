# Scratch — Mormon 8: The Book Speaks of Its Own Coming Forth

## Binding question

What is Mormon 8 actually *about*, verse by verse — once you can track Moroni's
shifting referents (he/it/they/ye keep changing)? And how does his prophecy of
the record's own coming-forth interlock with (a) Isaiah's "voice from the dust"
(Isa 29 / 2 Ne 26–27), (b) Nephi's vision handing the latter-day record off to
John's Revelation (1 Ne 14:18–27), and (c) the actual 1828–29 translation
history recorded in D&C 3, 5, and 10?

Michael's sub-questions:
- Break down each verse as a commentary; the antecedents are hard to follow.
- Study the footnotes; use gospel-engine for semantic links.
- Does v34 point to Revelation by John?
- How does it relate to Nephi's tree-of-life vision leading into Revelation?
- The headers/footnotes say this chapter is about the Book of Mormon itself
  coming out — connect it.
- Line it up with D&C and church history while Joseph was translating.

## Referent map — "what is each referring to" (the core ask)

Mormon 8 is hard because the antecedent of *it / they / he / ye* moves five
times. Tracked:

- **vv. 1–9 — Moroni's present.** "they/them" = the destroyed Nephites + the
  Lamanites hunting the survivors. Moroni is the last man (v3 "I even remain
  alone").
- **vv. 10–11 — the Three Nephites.** "the disciples of Jesus who did tarry"
  (3 Ne 28); "my father and I have seen them."
- **v. 12 — the record itself.** "this record" = the Book of Mormon (the plates
  Moroni holds). "you" = the future reader. Pivot from past to future.
- **vv. 13–22 — the record's coming forth.** "it / this thing" = the record.
  "whoso shall bring it to light" / "him that the Lord should suffer to bring
  these things forth" = the translator (Joseph Smith; fn 8:14b → JS—H 1:53).
  "he that condemneth" / "he that saith Show unto me, or ye shall be smitten"
  = the future skeptic & sign-seeker (fn 8:18a → JS—H 1:60–61). "he that shall
  breathe out wrath... say We will destroy the work" = those who attack the
  record (prophetically = the D&C 10 conspirators).
- **vv. 23–25 — the dead crying from the dust.** "those saints who have gone
  before me" = the buried Nephites. "him that the Lord should suffer to bring
  these things forth" = the translator again.
- **vv. 26–32 — "it shall come in a day when…"** anaphora. "it" = the
  coming-forth of the record. Each clause = a condition of OUR day, the day the
  book emerges.
- **vv. 33–41 — direct address.** "O ye wicked… ye" = the latter-day reader and
  the latter-day churches. Moroni turns from describing the day to indicting its
  people. v35: "Jesus Christ hath shown you unto me, and I know your doing" —
  Moroni saw US.

## The cross-reference web (footnote-verified, not inferred)

1. **Morm 8:22 ⇄ D&C 3:3.** Reciprocal cross-reference. Moroni's "eternal
   purposes shall roll on" = the Lord's 1828 "purposes cannot be frustrated."
2. **Morm 8:2–3 ⇄ D&C 3:18.** Reciprocal. The destruction Moroni records is the
   destruction the Lord names to Joseph.
3. **Morm 8:24–26 ⇄ D&C 10:46.** The old prophets' prayers "in behalf of him
   that should bring these things forth" = the prayers the Lord says He granted.
4. **Morm 8:33 → 1 Ne 14:18–27 + Ether 4:16 → Rev 1:1.** "the revelations of
   God" routes to Nephi-sees-John and Moroni-names-John's-Revelation.
5. **Morm 8:23 → Isa 29:4 → 2 Ne 26:16 / 27.** The voice from the dust.
6. **Morm 8:28 → 1 Ne 14:9–10.** The defiled churches = the two-churches vision.
7. **Morm 8:33 "stiffnecked" ⇄ D&C 5:8 "stiffnecked."** Same indictment word.
8. **Morm 8:14 → JS—H 1:53; 8:18 → JS—H 1:60–61.** The translator and the
   sign-demanders, mapped to Joseph's own history.

## Church-history timeline (from D&C section headers, verified)

- **July 1828** — D&C 3. The 116 pages lost through Martin Harris. The first
  great attack on the work. Joseph loses the gift for a season.
- **≈ summer 1828 → April 1829** — D&C 10. The Lord reveals the conspiracy to
  alter the lost pages and the small-plates workaround. (Header.)
- **March 1829** — D&C 5. The three-witnesses promise; "stiffnecked
  generation"; "stop for a season."
- **April–June 1829** — Oliver Cowdery scribes; the bulk of the current text
  (Mosiah→Moroni, then 1 Ne→W of M) translated. Mormon 8 was translated in this
  window. (General history; the section headers fix the surrounding revelations;
  I have NOT independently dated the day Morm 8 was rendered — state as "in this
  same spring-1829 window," not a specific date.)
- **June 1829** — the Three Witnesses (D&C 17, referenced by 2 Ne 27:12a and
  Ether 5). Translation completed; copyright filed June 11, 1829.
- **March 1830** — Book of Mormon published.

The point: while Joseph was translating Moroni's words about people who would
"say: We will destroy the work of the Lord" (8:21), he had *personally just
lived* an attempt to do exactly that (the 116 pages), and held in hand the
revelations (D&C 3, 10) recording God's countermove. The prophecy and its first
fulfillment overlapped on the same desk.

## Gap & Critical Analysis

**Strongest, best-supported claims:**
- The chapter is structurally about the record's own coming-forth (vv. 12–41),
  not mainly about the Nephite destruction (vv. 1–9 are the setup). Headers +
  TG "Book of Mormon" / "Scriptures to Come Forth" tags confirm.
- The voice-from-the-dust reading (Isa 29 → 2 Ne 26–27 → Morm 8:23–26) is
  airtight: footnote-chained and semantic-search-confirmed.
- The D&C 3 ⇄ Morm 8:22 and D&C 10:46 ⇄ Morm 8:24–26 links are reciprocal
  cross-references in the apparatus, not my inference.

**Where I must NOT overclaim (honesty checks):**
- **v34 and Revelation.** The phrase "must shortly come" (8:34) verbally echoes
  Rev 1:1, but the chapter's *footnoted* Revelation link sits on v33 ("the
  revelations of God" → 1 Ne 14:18–27 + Ether 4:16), not on v34. "The
  revelations of God" is plural/general and need not mean ONLY John's book;
  the strongest anchor that Moroni means John is INTERNAL — his own Ether 4:16.
  So: answer = "yes, it points there, with Ether 4:16 as the decisive evidence,"
  NOT "v34 quotes Revelation." Flag the nuance; don't flatten it.
- **Translation date of Morm 8.** I have the surrounding revelations dated by
  their headers, and the general April–June 1829 window for the current text. I
  have NOT verified the specific day/week Mormon 8 itself was dictated. State the
  window, not a false precision.
- **"the learned" = Charles Anthon.** 2 Ne 27 / Isa 29:11–12 are the sealed-book
  / learned-man prophecy; the Anthon identification comes via JS—H 1:64–65,
  which I referenced through footnotes but did not read in full this session.
  Attribute it as the traditional identification routed through JS—H, not as
  something I re-verified from JS—H today.

**Missing voices considered:**
- Modern prophets / conference: I did NOT pull a BYU-citation or conference talk
  on Morm 8:35–37. Could strengthen the "Moroni saw us" application. Optional
  follow-up; the scripture chain already answers the binding question.
- Webster 1828 on "transfigured" (8:33) and "pollutions" (8:31/36) — could add
  word-depth. "Transfigured the holy word of God" (fn 33c → 1 Ne 13:26, plain
  & precious things taken away) is the load-bearing one. Optional.

**Ring check:** Does the study answer the binding question? Yes — referent map +
verse commentary + the four connection threads (dust / John / two-churches /
translation history), each footnote-grounded.

**Posture check (discovering vs confirming):** The Ether 4:16 link genuinely
sharpened the v34 answer mid-study (I'd have asserted "yes, Rev 1:1" too fast;
the text made me route it through v33 + Moroni's own naming of John). The D&C 3
