# The Two Triplets: One Ascent, Two Descriptions

**Binding Question:** How do the triplets *Faith, Hope, and Charity* and *The Way, The Truth, and The Life* interrelate? Are they the same concepts viewed from different angles, or genuinely different? Is one human-centered and the other Christ-centered? Are they the same point seen from different vantage points?

---

## I. The Two Triplets as Ordered Progressions

Both triplets arrive in scripture as sequences, not as loose collections. The order matters because the sequence does theological work.

Jesus speaks the second triplet in the upper room, on the night He was betrayed: "I am the way, the truth, and the life: no man cometh unto the Father, but by me" ([John 14:6](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/john/14.md)). Thomas had asked, "How can we know the way?" and Jesus answered with Himself. The declaration is not a list of three separate services Christ offers; it is a progressive disclosure of how a soul moves from fallen separation back into the presence of the Father. The [way-truth-life](way-truth-life.md) study traces this as a grace-for-grace progression mirrored in D&C 93:12-13: Christ "received not of the fulness at the first, but received grace for grace... continued from grace to grace, until he received a fulness." Applied to us, the pattern is: enter the way, grow in truth, receive life.

The first triplet appears with the same structural care. Moroni writes: "Wherefore, there must be faith; and if there must be faith there must also be hope; and if there must be hope there must also be charity" ([Moroni 10:20](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/moro/10.md)). Moroni later warns that "if ye have no hope ye must needs be in despair; and despair cometh because of iniquity" ([Moroni 10:22](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/moro/10.md)). The chain collapses if any link is removed. As [hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs](hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs.md) puts it, "Faith is the trust that initiates. Hope is the orientation that sustains. Charity is the love that completes. Pull hope out and faith has no direction; pull hope out and charity has no soil."

Paul traces the same progression in Romans 5: tribulation worketh patience, patience worketh experience, experience worketh hope, and hope "maketh not ashamed; because the love of God is shed abroad in our hearts by the Holy Ghost which is given unto us" ([Romans 5:3-5](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rom/5.md)). The triplet is a single transformation viewed at three stages, not three independent virtues that a believer happens to collect. [hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs](hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs.md) calls them "three names for one assimilation, viewed at three stages."

So both triplets are ordered, progressive, and cumulative. But what they progress *toward* and *through* is where the question sharpens.

---

## II. The Veil as Shared Hinge

The two triplets do not run on separate tracks. They converge at the same architectural point: the veil.

Hebrews 10:19-20 connects Christ directly to the veil: "Having therefore, brethren, boldness to enter into the holiest by the blood of Jesus, By a new and living way, which he hath consecrated for us, through the veil, that is to say, his flesh." This is not typological guesswork. The author explicitly equates the veil with Christ's flesh and "the way" with the passage through it. [way-truth-life](way-truth-life.md) anchors its entire temple reading here: "Christ IS the veil. Passing through requires communing with Him, being found worthy through His merits, and entering into His presence."

But the veil is also where hope anchors. "Which hope we have as an anchor of the soul, both sure and steadfast, and which entereth into that within the veil" ([Hebrews 6:18-19](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/heb/6.md)). [hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs](hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs.md) presses this further: "Paul's hope-anchor enters within the veil — into the Holy of Holies, into the unseen presence of God. This is the geometry of hope: it stabilizes the present by reaching into the future where Christ already is." The same study calls the temple "hope's anchor made visible."

Then comes the eye. D&C 88:67-68 sets the mechanism: "If your eye be single to my glory, your whole bodies shall be filled with light, and there shall be no darkness in you; and that body which is filled with light comprehendeth all things. Therefore, sanctify yourselves that your minds become single to God." [discernment-and-the-comprehending-eye](discernment-and-the-comprehending-eye.md) reads this as the gate through which both triplets operate: "The eye is the gate. What the eye is tuned to determines what the body fills with. What the body fills with determines what the body comprehends."

That study names hope as what the eye is aimed at before it sees, and charity as what focuses it. Faith is the movement toward what hope orients and charity will recognize when it arrives. The result is that both triplets require the same single eye, the same progressive purification of the perceiver, and the same passage through the veil. They share the same temple geometry, the same sacred architecture. They are not independent paths to different destinations. They are different descriptions of intersecting at the same threshold.

---

## III. Terminal Point — Both Triplets End in Christ, Not in Human Virtue

If one triplet were "us-centric" and the other "Christ-centered," we would expect them to terminate in different places. They do not. Both end in Christ.

Ether 12:28 is explicit: "Faith, hope and charity bringeth unto me — the fountain of all righteousness." The triplet's terminus is Christ. [enoch-charity](enoch-charity.md) highlights this: "The triad doesn't just make us better people. It brings us to Christ. And Christ is the fountain from which Zion flows."

[tree-of-life-and-the-chain](tree-of-life-and-the-chain.md) pushes the point further. The tree of life, which Alma compares to the word planted by faith and nourished with patience, bears fruit that is "most precious, which is sweet above all that is sweet... and ye shall feast upon this fruit even until ye are filled, that ye hunger not, neither shall ye thirst" ([Alma 32:42](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/32.md)). But when Nephi asks the angel what the tree means, the angel shows him the Lamb of God and asks, "Knowest thou the meaning of the tree which thy father saw?" ([1 Nephi 11:21](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/11.md)). Nephi answers: "Yea, it is the love of God, which sheddeth itself abroad in the hearts of the children of men." The study concludes: "The fruit is the experience of God's love... the fruit is Christ in the flesh... To partake of the fruit is to receive Christ."

Likewise, "the way, the truth, and the life" does not describe three things Christ dispenses from a distance. [way-truth-life](way-truth-life.md) emphasizes: "Christ does not merely show the way, teach truth, or give life — He IS all three." John 14:6 is followed immediately by Philip's request to see the Father, and Jesus responds, "He that hath seen me hath seen the Father" ([John 14:9](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/john/14.md)). The way to the Father is not a roadmap; it is a Person. The truth about the Father is not a curriculum; it is a Person. The life of the Father is not a vitamin; it is a Person.

And charity, the summit of the first triplet, is equally Christ-derived. [charity](charity.md) quotes Moroni 7:47: "Charity is the pure love of Christ." It is not a human achievement manufactured by effort; it is "bestowed" ([Moroni 7:48](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/moro/7.md)). Praying "unto the Father with all the energy of heart" is the means, but the love itself is a gift from Christ to the true follower. Therefore even charity, the most interior-seeming of the three and the one that sounds like a virtue we must cultivate, is finally the love *of* Christ, not a love we generate independently and offer back to Him.

So the "us-centric versus Christ-centered" framing does not hold. Both triplets are Christ-centered. The first describes the soul's progressive capacity to receive Him; the second describes Him as the reality received.

---

## IV. Perceiver and Perceived — Two Complementary Architectures

The two triplets are genuinely different concepts, not merely synonyms rearranged. But they are not unrelated either. They describe the same ascent once from the traveler's interior state and once from the landscape being traveled.

[discernment-and-the-comprehending-eye](discernment-and-the-comprehending-eye.md) treats the triplet as the progressive purification of the perceptual organ. Faith moves, hope aims, charity resolves. This is the *subject's* side of comprehension — what happens to the soul that wants to see. By contrast, [truth](truth.md) establishes the *object's* side: "Truth is knowledge of things as they are, and as they were, and as they are to come" ([D&C 93:24](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93.md)). Comprehension is ontological: "light cleaveth unto light" ([D&C 88:40](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)). The perceiver must become like the reality perceived, or the connection cannot happen.

Therefore the first triplet names the purification of the instrument. Faith is the trust that the prototype is real. Hope is the seeing-ahead that fixes the gaze on the prototype. Charity is what the soul becomes when it has been gazing long enough. [hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs](hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs.md) quotes Lectures on Faith 7: "He proposed to make them like unto himself... And for any portion of the human family to be assimilated into their likeness is to be saved; and to be unlike them is to be destroyed: and on this hinge turns the door of salvation." The triplet is the mechanism of assimilation into the prototype.

The second triplet names the reality toward which the purified instrument is tuned. The way is the path Christ opened through His flesh. The truth is the knowledge of things as they are, embodied in Him who "received a fulness of truth, yea, even of all truth" ([D&C 93:26](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93.md)). The life is the eternal life He both possesses and gives: "I am the resurrection, and the life" ([John 11:25](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/john/11.md)).

One triplet describes the eye being single. The other describes the glory the single eye sees. One describes the tuning. The other describes the music. They are genuinely different, but they are complementary, not competing.

Moroni 7:48 makes the complementarity visible: pray that ye may be filled with this love, "that when he shall appear we shall be like him, for we shall see him as he is; that we may have this hope; that we may be purified even as he is pure." Notice the movement: charity enables likeness, likeness enables sight, sight sustains hope, and hope completes purification. All of this is prerequisite to receiving the One who is the way, the truth, and the life.

---

## V. Synthesis — One Ascent, Two Descriptions

They are not the same concepts viewed from different angles. Faith is not the way, hope is not the truth, and charity is not the life — at least not as simple equivalents. They are genuinely different, but not unrelated. They share the same veil-geometry, the same terminal in Christ, and the same progressive logic.

The synthesis the corpus supports is this: Faith, hope, and charity constitute the *soul's purification* that enables one to enter the Way, receive the Truth, and inherit the Life. The first triplet is the traveler being made able to travel; the second is the road, the destination, and the air of the country being traveled to. They are the same ascent described once from the interior and once from the exterior.

This is why D&C 4:5 can merge them without confusion: "Faith, hope, charity and love, with an eye single to the glory of God, qualify him for the work." The work is the work of salvation, the work of the temple, the work of bringing souls to Christ. The qualifying attributes are the interior triplet, exercised with a single eye, aimed at the glory of God, which glory is "intelligence, or, in other words, light and truth" ([D&C 93:36](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93.md)).

Alma 32:41-42 gives the agricultural version of the same synthesis: nourish the tree by faith with diligence and patience, looking forward to the fruit, and "it shall be a tree springing up unto everlasting life." The tree is the way; the fruit is the life; the nourishing is the faith-hope-charity chain enacted. Paul gives the chain in Romans 5; Alma gives the tree; Nephi names the fruit as Christ. Three witnesses, one tree, one ascent.

2 Nephi 31:20 compresses the whole journey into a single verse: "Wherefore, ye must press forward with a steadfastness in Christ, having a perfect brightness of hope, and a love of God and of all men." Steadfastness in Christ is the way. Perfect brightness of hope is the eye fixed forward. Love of God and of all men is charity. Pressing forward is the movement of faith. The verse does not name the two triplets explicitly, but it enacts both at once.

An honest caveat from [tree-of-life-and-the-chain](tree-of-life-and-the-chain.md): "No prophet writes 'the seed is faith, the tree is hope, the fruit is charity.' The mapping is built by reading the two systems against each other and noticing they share vocabulary, motion, and end. That makes this a reading, not a doctrine." The same holds for the larger synthesis between the two triplets. The architecture is sound, the shared geometry is real, and the terminal in Christ is unmistakable. But the precise mapping between each element — whether faith corresponds specifically to the way, hope to the truth, and charity to the life — is a correspondence the text suggests without enforcing. What the text enforces is the shared progression, the shared veil, and the shared Person at the end.

---

## VI. Becoming

These triplets are not the same point seen from different vantage points, yet they are not separate programs either. They are the instrument and the music, the eye and the light, the traveler and the road.

The practical question is: where does your eye rest? [hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs](hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs.md) warns that "despair is the eye-state where there is no forward orientation." If hope has dimmed, the eye has lost its direction, and if the eye has lost its direction, charity cannot focus because there is nothing to focus on. The fight to keep hope is the fight to keep the eye single.

The temple enacts this every time an initiate passes through the veil. That passage is not a metaphor for something else; it is the physical acting-out of what both triplets describe. You come with faith that the covenants are real, with hope fixed on what lies beyond the veil, and with charity — however incomplete — as the only garment that fits. You pass through the way Christ opened, receiving truth as you go, moving toward the life that is God's presence. The endowment is not a drama about someone else's salvation. It is the two triplets made walkable.

The work this week is simple and severe: check the eye. Is it single? Is it aimed at Christ as prototype, or at something else — a political outcome, a family ideal, a personal project, even a theological system? Whatever the eye stays on, the body fills with. And what the body fills with is what the body will eventually comprehend. The two triplets agree on this completely. Faith, hope, and charity tune the instrument. The way, the truth, and the life are what the tuned instrument finally hears. The ascent is one, the descriptions are two, and the Person at the threshold is Christ.
