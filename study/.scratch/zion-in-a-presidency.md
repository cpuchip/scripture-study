## Verified Quotes

### Moses 7:16-21 — `gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/7.md`

> "And from that time forth there were wars and bloodshed among them; but the Lord came and dwelt with his people, and they dwelt in righteousness." (v.16)

> "The fear of the Lord was upon all nations, so great was the glory of the Lord, which was upon his people." (v.17)

> "And the Lord called his people Zion, because they were of one heart and one mind, and dwelt in righteousness; and there was no poor among them." (v.18)

> "And Enoch continued his preaching in righteousness unto the people of God. And it came to pass in his days, that he built a city that was called the City of Holiness, even Zion." (v.19)

> "Zion, in process of time, was taken up into heaven." (v.21)

**Observations:**
- Four conditions: one heart, one mind, dwelt in righteousness, no poor. "Called Zion *because*" — diagnostic criteria, not aspiration.
- v.16-17 precede v.18: the Lord came and dwelt, *then* they were called Zion. Zion is given when He comes; not manufactured first.
- v.19 — Enoch *continued* preaching even after the people were called Zion. Tending is permanent.
- v.21 — "in process of time." Slow build.

### D&C 38:25-27 — `gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/38.md`

> "Let every man esteem his brother as himself." (v.25)

> "What man among you having twelve sons, and is no respecter of them, and they serve him obediently, and he saith unto the one: Be thou clothed in robes and sit thou here; and to the other: Be thou clothed in rags and sit thou there—and looketh upon his sons and saith I am just?" (v.26)

> "Behold, this I have given unto you as a parable, and it is even as I am. I say unto you, be one; and if ye are not one ye are not mine." (v.27)

**Observations:**
- v.27 is binary: not one, not His.
- v.26 parable — unequal treatment fails the test. Pre-echoes 4 Nephi's "divided into classes."

### D&C 82:14, 17, 19, 20 — `gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/82.md`

> "For Zion must increase in beauty, and in holiness; her borders must be enlarged; her stakes must be strengthened; yea, verily I say unto you, Zion must arise and put on her beautiful garments." (v.14)

> "And you are to be equal, or in other words, you are to have equal claims on the properties, for the benefit of managing the concerns of your stewardships, every man according to his wants and his needs, inasmuch as his wants are just—" (v.17)

> "Every man seeking the interest of his neighbor, and doing all things with an eye single to the glory of God." (v.19)

> "This order I have appointed to be an everlasting order unto you, and unto your successors, inasmuch as you sin not." (v.20)

**Observations:**
- v.14: Zion is process. Must increase. Not static.
- v.17: equal = sufficient for stewardship, "according to his wants and his needs." Not identical possessions.
- v.19: operational rule = "seeking the interest of his neighbor." Operational definition of one heart.
- v.20: "everlasting order, inasmuch as you sin not." Conditional permanence. (Makes 4 Nephi's collapse non-mysterious.)
- Footnote 17b links "claims" to TG Consecration.

### D&C 105:1-6 — `gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/105.md`

> "Behold, I say unto you, were it not for the transgressions of my people, speaking concerning the church and not individuals, they might have been redeemed even now." (v.2)

> "But behold, they have not learned to be obedient to the things which I required at their hands, but are full of all manner of evil, and do not impart of their substance, as becometh saints, to the poor and afflicted among them;" (v.3)

> "And are not united according to the union required by the law of the celestial kingdom;" (v.4)

> "And Zion cannot be built up unless it is by the principles of the law of the celestial kingdom; otherwise I cannot receive her unto myself." (v.5)

> "And my people must needs be chastened until they learn obedience, if it must needs be, by the things which they suffer." (v.6)

**Observations:**
- v.5 is the structural diagnosis: Zion only exists when celestial law is being lived. Not an org outcome you can plan toward; it's the natural shape of celestial law in operation.
- v.4: "the union required by the law of the celestial kingdom" — there's a kind of unity that doesn't qualify.
- v.3: failure was concrete — disobedience plus failure to impart substance.

### Mosiah 18:7-31 — `gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/18.md`

> "There were a goodly number gathered together at the place of Mormon, to hear the words of Alma." (v.7)

> "As ye are desirous to come into the fold of God, and to be called his people, and are willing to bear one another's burdens, that they may be light;" (v.8)

> "And now when the people had heard these words, they clapped their hands for joy, and exclaimed: This is the desire of our hearts." (v.11)

> "And he commanded them that there should be no contention one with another, but that they should look forward with one eye, having one faith and one baptism, having their hearts knit together in unity and in love one towards another." (v.21)

> "And there was one day in every week that was set apart that they should gather themselves together to teach the people, and to worship the Lord their God, and also, as often as it was in their power, to assemble themselves together." (v.25)

> "And again Alma commanded that the people of the church should impart of their substance, every one according to that which he had; if he have more abundantly he should impart more abundantly; and of him that had but little, but little should be required; and to him that had not should be given." (v.27)

> "And they did walk uprightly before God, imparting to one another both temporally and spiritually according to their needs and their wants." (v.29)

**Observations:**
- This is a Zion society being built in real time, ~450 souls, in hiding, in a forest, by a fountain. Not a holy city. Not after a consecration revelation. A people willing to bear burdens and a leader willing to teach.
- v.21 has every Zion vocabulary marker: no contention, one eye, one faith, hearts knit, unity. This is Moses 7:18 in different words. "Hearts knit together" = operational test of "one heart and one mind."
- v.25 (gathering), v.27 (imparting), v.29 (walking uprightly) — the mechanics of Zion are mundane: meet weekly, share what you have, walk uprightly.
- The waters of Mormon are the original Latter-day-style council. Alma was de facto president. He taught, ordained, commanded covenant-living. The 200+ became counselors-and-members in one gesture.

### Acts 4:32-35 — `gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/acts/4.md`

> "And the multitude of them that believed were of one heart and of one soul: neither said any of them that ought of the things which he possessed was his own; but they had all things common." (v.32)

> "Neither was there any among them that lacked: for as many as were possessors of lands or houses sold them, and brought the prices of the things that were sold," (v.34)

> "And distribution was made unto every man according as he had need." (v.35)

**Observations:**
- Acts 4:32: "of one heart and of one soul" — Greek-culture rendering of Moses 7:18's pattern. Same diagnostic, different culture.
- Acts 2:42 — *steadfastly* in four practices: doctrine, fellowship, breaking of bread, prayers. The four practices our wards still do. The question is steadfastness.
- Acts 2:46 — "with one accord in the temple" daily AND "from house to house." Both scales operating at once.
- Acts 4:34 — "Neither was there any among them that lacked." Moses 7:18's third condition tested.

### 4 Nephi 1:2-3, 15-18, 24-27, 43 — `gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/4-ne/1.md`

> "The people were all converted unto the Lord, upon all the face of the land, both Nephites and Lamanites, and there were no contentions and disputations among them, and every man did deal justly one with another." (v.2)

> "And they had all things common among them; therefore there were not rich and poor, bond and free, but they were all made free, and partakers of the heavenly gift." (v.3)

> "And it came to pass that there was no contention in the land, because of the love of God which did dwell in the hearts of the people." (v.15)

> "There were no robbers, nor murderers, neither were there Lamanites, nor any manner of -ites; but they were in one, the children of Christ, and heirs to the kingdom of God." (v.17)

> "Now, in this two hundred and first year there began to be among them those who were lifted up in pride, such as the wearing of costly apparel, and all manner of fine pearls, and of the fine things of the world." (v.24)

> "And from that time forth they did have their goods and their substance no more common among them." (v.25)

> "And they began to be divided into classes; and they began to build up churches unto themselves to get gain, and began to deny the true church of Christ." (v.26)

> "And also the people who were called the people of Nephi began to be proud in their hearts, because of their exceeding riches, and become vain like unto their brethren, the Lamanites." (v.43)

**Observations:**
- v.15: "*because of the love of God which did dwell in the hearts of the people*." The cause is love-dwelling. The structures are downstream symptoms.
- v.17: "no manner of -ites." All identity-categories that divided collapsed. "They were in one, the children of Christ."
- v.24-26 sequence: pride → goods no longer common → divided into classes → counterfeit churches for gain. The first failure is internal (pride). Everything cascades from a heart-condition.

## Conference Talks

### Cook, "Hearts Knit in Righteousness and Unity," October 2020 — `gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2020/10/15cook.md`

> "Our desire is that our hearts and minds will be knit in righteousness and unity and that we will be one with Them."

> "*Unity* is also a broad, comprehensive term but most certainly exemplifies the first and second great commandments to love God and love our fellowmen. It denotes a Zion people whose hearts and minds are 'knit together in unity.'"

> "The historical record we read in 4 Nephi describes a people where there were no envyings, strifes, tumults, lyings, murders, or any manner of lasciviousness. … Unfortunately, 4 Nephi then describes a dramatic change that began in the 'two hundred and first year,' when iniquity and division destroyed righteousness and unity."

> "At this 200-year hinge point in our Church history, let us commit ourselves as members of the Lord's Church to live righteously and be united as never before."

**Observations:** Cook anchors modern Zion language in 4 Nephi specifically. Pairs righteousness AND unity — not unity alone. Notes the 200-year hinge parallel between 4 Nephi and the Restoration.

### Uchtdorf, "Fit and Belonging," April 2025 — `gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2025/04/27uchtdorf.md`

> "The Lord invites us not just to *join* His kingdom but also to be anxiously engaged in *building* it. God envisions a people who are 'of one heart and one mind.' And to be of *one* heart, we must seek *pure* hearts, and that requires a *mighty change of heart*."

> "But that doesn't mean changing my heart to align with yours. Nor does it mean changing your heart to align with mine. It means that we all change our hearts to align with the Savior."

> "The unity we seek is not to have everyone stand in the same place; it is to have everyone face in the same direction—toward Jesus Christ."

**Observations:** Essential reframe — "one heart" ≠ uniformity. Common direction, not common position. Solves the misreading that Zion erases individuality. Chain: pure hearts → mighty change → one heart. No shortcut.

### Ballard, "Counseling with Our Councils," April 1994 — already verified in art-of-presidency
> When the bishop opened the floor to the council instead of presenting decisions, "the effect was like opening the floodgates of heaven."

**Observations:** "Floodgates" is Zion-language. When the council functions as designed, something heavenly *flows*. This is one of the grounds the Lord comes and dwells on.

## Critical Analysis (Phase 3a)

**Ring check:** The binding question has two halves — what does Zion in a presidency look like, and how does it propagate outward? Sections I-III address (a). Sections IV-VI address (b). Section VII lands it personally.

**Strongest claims to pressure-test:**

1. *"The presidency is the cell from which Zion propagates."* — The text supports cells: Mosiah 18 (Alma + 200), Acts (upper room + multitudes), Enoch (one prophet + a city). The cell-pattern is scripturally clear. Applying it specifically to *modern presidencies* is an inference, well-supported but inferential. Surface this honestly in the study.

2. *"Classes should be councils."* — Michael's insight, compelling. It's an inference from TSW + Acts 2:46 (house-to-house) + Mosiah 18:25 (gather to teach) + Ballard's floodgates. Scripture doesn't dictate classroom format. Present as thoughtful application, not doctrinal mandate.

3. *"The first failure in 4 Nephi is internal (pride), then cascades."* — Verified directly in v.24-26. Strong claim.

**Posture check:**
The study reads Zion as something we *build*, which is Restoration-favored framing. But Moses 7:16-18 is structurally clear: the Lord *came and dwelt*, then they were called Zion. Zion is given, not earned. The president's work isn't to manufacture Zion but to remove obstacles to His coming and dwelling. Worth surfacing in Section VI.

**Counterargument I'm avoiding:** "What if my counselors don't want to counsel? What if my teachers don't want to be in council mode?" Mosiah 18's people *clapped for joy*. Sometimes that joy isn't there. The president's first work may be cultivating the desire, not implementing the structure.
