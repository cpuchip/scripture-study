## Binding Question (draft)

What does it mean to "liken" scripture, and where is the line between likening and rewriting? Nephi reads Isaiah more aggressively than any other Book of Mormon prophet — splicing whole chapters, framing them with editorial commentary, and in 2 Nephi 26-27 producing material that is presented as what Isaiah saw but does not appear in extant Isaiah 29. Does Nephi's example license that move for us, or is it prophetic-only?

Subordinate questions:
- What is "likening" (Nephi's own term, [1 Ne 19:23](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/19.md), [2 Ne 11:8](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/11.md))?
- How does Nephi's reading differ from Abinadi's reading of Isaiah 52? (Abinadi expands typology within verse boundaries; Nephi splices and reframes.)
- What does Nephi's expansion of Isaiah 29 in 2 Ne 26-27 (sealed book, three witnesses, marred servant) reveal about how the Spirit authorizes a reader?
- What guardrails does Nephi himself name? ("They are plain unto all those that are filled with the spirit of prophecy" — 2 Ne 25:4. So the guardrail IS the spirit of prophecy. What is that?)

## Working Hypothesis (very tentative)

Likening has at least three modes, in increasing aggressiveness:
1. **Typological expansion** (Abinadi) — text says X about Israel; under the Spirit X also figures Christ. No words added to the text.
2. **Editorial splicing** (Nephi 1 Ne 20-21, 2 Ne 12-24) — chapters dropped in with surrounding frame; sometimes with variant readings (which may reflect plates-of-brass version vs Masoretic, not Nephite editing).
3. **Prophetic extension** (Nephi 2 Ne 26-27) — material presented as what Isaiah saw, expanding past what Isaiah's words alone deliver. This requires *the spirit of prophecy* — Nephi sees what Isaiah saw, and reports both.

If that schema holds, the modern reader gets mode 1 by study and the Spirit, and is barred from modes 2 and 3 (those require apostolic/prophetic authority over canon). The instructive thing for us is that we can NOTICE the moves Nephi makes without licensing ourselves to repeat them.
