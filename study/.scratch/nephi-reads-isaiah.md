## Binding Question (draft)

What does it mean to "liken" scripture, and where is the line between likening and rewriting? Nephi reads Isaiah more aggressively than any other Book of Mormon prophet — splicing whole chapters, framing them with editorial commentary, and in 2 Nephi 26-27 producing material that is presented as what Isaiah saw but does not appear in extant Isaiah 29. Does Nephi's example license that move for us, or is it prophetic-only?

Subordinate questions:
- What is "likening" (Nephi's own term, [1 Ne 19:23](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/19.md), [2 Ne 11:8](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/11.md))?
- How does Nephi's reading differ from Abinadi's reading of Isaiah 52? (Abinadi expands typology within verse boundaries; Nephi splices and reframes.)
- What does Nephi's expansion of Isaiah 29 in 2 Ne 26-27 (sealed book, three witnesses, marred servant) reveal about how the Spirit authorizes a reader?
- What guardrails does Nephi himself name? ("They are plain unto all those that are filled with the spirit of prophecy" — 2 Ne 25:4. So the guardrail IS the spirit of prophecy. What is that?)

## Working Hypothesis (very tentative)

Likening has at least three modes, in increasing aggressiveness:
1. **Typological expansion** (Abinadi) — text says X about Israel; under the Spirit X also figures Christ. No words added to the text.
2. **Editorial splicing** (Nephi 1 Ne 20-21, 2 Ne 12-24) — chapters dropped in with surrounding frame; sometimes with variant readings (which may reflect plates-of-brass version vs Masoretic, not Nephite editing).
3. **Prophetic extension** (Nephi 2 Ne 26-27) — material presented as what Isaiah saw, expanding past what Isaiah's words alone deliver. This requires *the spirit of prophecy* — Nephi sees what Isaiah saw, and reports both.

If that schema holds, the modern reader gets mode 1 by study and the Spirit, and is barred from modes 2 and 3 (those require apostolic/prophetic authority over canon). The instructive thing for us is that we can NOTICE the moves Nephi makes without licensing ourselves to repeat them.

## Comparison: Isaiah 29 vs. 2 Nephi 27

Isaiah 29 has 24 verses. 2 Nephi 27 has 35.

Material in 2 Ne 27 with NO parent in Isaiah 29:
- vv. 6-10 (the book delivered, the sealed/unsealed split, the words read on housetops)
- vv. 12-14 (THREE WITNESSES shall behold the book)
- vv. 15-22 (the dramatized learned-man exchange — what Isaiah 29:11-12 sketches in 2 brief verses, Nephi expands into a full prophetic narrative including the seal-and-hide commandment)

Authorizing principle: 2 Ne 11:2-3. Isaiah saw the Redeemer; Jacob saw the Redeemer; Nephi saw the Redeemer. "By the words of three, God hath said, I will establish my word." This connects directly to the witness law worked out in [hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs.md](../hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs.md) Section V.

Guardrail: 2 Ne 25:4. Plainness is in the READER, not in the text. "The spirit of prophecy" is the condition that makes Isaiah readable.

## Gap & Critical Analysis (added 2026-04-25, retroactively)

Michael flagged that I declared the study done after announcing I was "skipping the gap and critical-analysis phases." Running them now, honestly. What follows changes the v1 study.

### Gaps from the original outline I dropped without addressing

1. **Christ's own Isaiah hermeneutic in 3 Nephi 20-23.** Listed in source inventory; never read; never used. This is the biggest gap. 3 Ne 23:1-13 is a perfect parallel to what Nephi does — Christ approves Isaiah ("great are the words of Isaiah"), then *commands* the addition of Samuel the Lamanite's resurrection prophecy to the record because it had been omitted (vv. 9-13). That is canonical extension by direct command. It both (a) gives the three-mode schema a Christ-witness (mode 3 is what Christ himself authorizes) and (b) shows the same verb — "after he had expounded all the scriptures" (v. 6). The study is incomplete without this.
   **Decision:** add a paragraph in Section V (Prophetic Extension) connecting Nephi's move to Christ's parallel move in 3 Ne 23. Don't expand into a new section — the study's binding question is about Nephi, not Christ. But Christ's parallel act is a missing voice that strengthens the claim.

2. **Plates of brass = Isaiah as family text.** Outline section dropped. This actually matters tonally — Nephi is not reading a stranger's text, he is reading the family book. But it is not load-bearing for the binding question (likening vs. rewriting). **Decision:** leave dropped. Note the gap and move on.

3. **2 Ne 28-30.** Source inventory flagged: "Nephi continues prophesying *as if extending Isaiah's voice*." Not read. Could be evidence for or against the schema (does Nephi continue in extension mode after ch 27, or does he shift to first-person prophecy in his own name?). **Decision:** flag as honest gap. Future study could check.

4. **JST-style variants in 1 Ne 20-21 vs Isaiah 48-49.** Outline flagged "sometimes with variant readings (which may reflect plates-of-brass version vs Masoretic, not Nephite editing)." Never addressed. This is the single most important question for the boundary the study draws: **if Nephi's variant readings come from a different source text (brass plates) rather than from him, then mode 3 ("prophetic extension") may not be a separate category at all — it may be mode 2 with a different parent.** I evaded this. **Decision:** acknowledge the alternative reading explicitly in Section V or VI. The study still stands (2 Ne 27 has material that no manuscript tradition preserves), but the strongest counter-reading needs naming.

### Overreaches in the v1 study

1. **Section V — "Three witnesses. Named here, more than two thousand years before the Three Witnesses to the Book of Mormon would sign their testimony."** This is true at the level of fulfillment. But the sentence structure implies Nephi was *predicting* the Three Witnesses. The text says "three witnesses shall behold it" — it is fulfilled in the Three Witnesses, but Nephi may have been seeing the witness law applied to the latter-day book without specifically seeing the 1829 signing. Calibrate: keep the connection, soften the implied prediction.

2. **Section VI — "Nephi's hermeneutic, laid out in his own seams, has three operations of increasing weight."** The schema is mine, not Nephi's. Nephi uses one verb — *liken* — plus *expound* in the seams. He never classifies three modes. The categorization is the study's synthesis. **Fix:** label it as the study's reading of Nephi's seams, not as Nephi's own taxonomy.

3. **Section IV — "The Bible Dictionary captures the cargo of 'spirit of prophecy' tightly."** I never read the BD entry. The Rev 19:10 quote ("the testimony of Jesus is the spirit of prophecy") IS verified, but the BD attribution is not. **Fix:** quote Rev 19:10 directly without claiming the BD frame.

4. **Section VI — "It is reserved" / "What they cannot do is extend Isaiah."** Substantive doctrinal claim about authority. Defensible by the witness-law logic the study itself develops, but I cite no modern prophetic source saying "don't extend canonical scripture." **Decision:** keep the claim — it follows from the study's own logic — but frame as "the schema this study is drawing" rather than "what scripture forbids."
