# Scratch — The Two Deaths and the Third Witness

Working ledger for `study/two-deaths-and-the-third-witness.md`. Verified-quote provenance + structural notes. All quotes read-before-quoting this session (2026-06-28).

## Origin

Michael's friend at church: epiphany that the sacrament is done in two parts — the body, and the shed blood. Michael felt the Spirit as a third part he "didn't know how to place." Then: Christ's act has three movements — the garden, the cross, the resurrection from the tomb — "which is what the sacrament calls us to remember." Michael's refinement when I raised the over-statement caveat (both garden and cross shed blood): *"maybe they are different kinds? spirit death blood, physical death blood."* That refinement is the doctrinal hinge — two deaths.

Companion to `study/hope-and-the-grammar-of-pairs.md` (the witness law: pairs/triads carry doctrinal weight; "the form of the doctrine is part of the doctrine").

## Binding question

Why does the sacrament come as a pair — body and blood? What does each emblem carry that the other does not? Where does the Spirit belong, given that it is neither bread nor water? And do the two blood-sheddings (garden, cross) answer two different deaths — the blood the spiritual death, the body the temporal death?

## Verified quotes (read this session)

- Moroni 5:2 (wine): "...in remembrance of the blood of thy Son, which was shed for them... that they do always remember him, that they may have his Spirit to be with them. Amen."
- D&C 20:77 / 20:79 — canonical form, same ("which was shed for them").
- 3 Ne 18:7 (bread): "in remembrance of my body... And if ye do always remember me ye shall have my Spirit to be with you."
- 3 Ne 18:11 (wine): "in remembrance of my blood, which I have shed for you... And if ye do always remember me ye shall have my Spirit to be with you."
- Luke 22:19: "This is my body which is given for you: this do in remembrance of me." 22:20: "This cup is the new testament in my blood, which is shed for you."
- Matt 26:27-28: "Drink ye all of it; For this is my blood of the new testament, which is shed for many for the remission of sins."

**Two deaths (the spine)**
- 2 Ne 9:10: "that monster, death and hell, which I call the death of the body, and also the death of the spirit." 9:11: temporal "which death is the grave." 9:12: "the spiritual death... which spiritual death is hell... the grave must deliver up its captive bodies, and the bodies and the spirits of men will be restored one to the other; and it is by the power of the resurrection."
- Hel 14:16: "this death bringeth to pass the resurrection, and redeemeth all mankind from the first death—that spiritual death; for all mankind, by the fall of Adam being cut off from the presence of the Lord, are considered as dead, both as to things temporal and to things spiritual." 14:17: "the resurrection of Christ redeemeth mankind, yea, even all mankind, and bringeth them back into the presence of the Lord." 14:18: "a spiritual death, yea, a second death."
- Alma 42:7: "cut off both temporally and spiritually from the presence of the Lord." 42:9: "the fall had brought upon all mankind a spiritual death as well as a temporal... it was expedient that mankind should be reclaimed from this spiritual death."

**Body → temporal death → resurrection**
- Alma 11:42: "the death of Christ shall loose the bands of this temporal death, that all shall be raised." 11:43: "The spirit and the body shall be reunited again in its perfect form." 11:45: "their spirits uniting with their bodies, never to be divided."
- Luke 24:39: "a spirit hath not flesh and bones, as ye see me have." (Risen body = flesh and *bones*; the blood was shed, not resumed.)
- Alma 7:12: "he will take upon him death, that he may loose the bands of death."

**Blood → spiritual death → remission**
- Lev 17:11: "the life of the flesh is in the blood: and I have given it to you upon the altar to make an atonement for your souls: for it is the blood that maketh an atonement for the soul." 17:10: "I will even set my face against that soul that eateth blood" (Law forbade eating blood → reversal in the sacrament).
- 1 Jn 1:7: "the blood of Jesus Christ his Son cleanseth us from all sin."
- Eph 1:7: "redemption through his blood, the forgiveness of sins."
- 3 Ne 27:19: "washed their garments in my blood, because of their faith, and the repentance of all their sins." 27:20: "sanctified by the reception of the Holy Ghost, that ye may stand spotless."
- Alma 7:13: "the Son of God suffereth according to the flesh that he might take upon him the sins of his people, that he might blot out their transgressions."

**Two kinds of blood (Michael's refinement)**
- Garden / spiritual-death blood: Mosiah 3:7: "blood cometh from every pore, so great shall be his anguish for the wickedness and the abominations of his people." D&C 19:18: "to bleed at every pore, and to suffer both body and spirit." 19:20: "at the time I withdrew my Spirit."
- Cross / temporal-death blood: John 19:30: "It is finished: and he bowed his head, and gave up the ghost." 19:34: "one of the soldiers with a spear pierced his side, and forthwith came there out blood and water."
- HONEST CAVEAT (critical-analysis gate): Matt 27:46: "My God, my God, why hast thou forsaken me?" — the spiritual forsaking culminates *on the cross*. So the deaths are clean categories; the garden/cross are where each PREDOMINATES, not airtight bins. Don't make the geography do more than it can.

**The Spirit (the third) — three convergent placements**
1. Prayer = promised fruit (apodosis): 3 Ne 18:7,11 "if ye do always remember me ye shall have my Spirit."
2. Canonical triad: Moses 6:59: "born into the world by water, and blood, and the spirit... born again... of water, and of the Spirit, and... cleansed by blood, even the blood of mine Only Begotten." 6:60: "by the water ye keep the commandment; by the Spirit ye are justified; and by the blood ye are sanctified."
3. Witness law: 1 Jn 5:6: "This is he that came by water and blood... And it is the Spirit that beareth witness." 5:8: "there are three that bear witness in earth, the Spirit, and the water, and the blood: and these three agree in one." [TEXT NOTE: v.7 = Johannine Comma, a late interpolation absent from earliest Greek MSS; KJV-canonical so usable, but build on v.6 + v.8 (the earthly witnesses), which are textually secure. Don't lean on v.7.]
- Spirit seals BOTH victories: Rom 8:10-11: "the body is dead because of sin; but the Spirit is life because of righteousness... he that raised up Christ from the dead shall also quicken your mortal bodies by his Spirit that dwelleth in you." Eph 1:13-14: "sealed with that holy Spirit of promise, Which is the earnest of our inheritance."

**Faith-hope-charity enactment**
- Moroni 7:41: "ye shall have hope through the atonement of Christ and the power of his resurrection, to be raised unto life eternal." (hope's content = the resurrection — ties to hope study)
- Rom 5:5: "the love of God is shed abroad in our hearts by the Holy Ghost which is given unto us." (charity's source = the Spirit)

**Corroborating talk (verified, read)**
- Elder James J. Hamula, "The Sacrament and the Atonement," General Conference Oct 2014.
  - Bread→body→resurrection: "our bodies will be released from the bonds of death, rise triumphantly from the grave, and be restored to our eternal spirits."
  - Water→blood→remission: "we remember the blood Jesus spilled and the spiritual suffering He endured... His blood and suffering atoned for our sins and that He will remit our sins." Groups BOTH Gethsemane drops AND Calvary spilling under the water/blood emblem → supports Michael's two-bloods.
  - Sequence: "The sequence of bread first and water second is not inconsequential."
  - Conditional/unconditional: "While every one of us will return to the presence of God, not every one of us will remain with Him."
  - Spirit: "Receiving anew the Spirit is a consummate blessing because the Spirit is the agent who cleanses and purifies us from sin and transgression."

## Discernment / Ben-test check

The two-deaths spine is canonical (Jacob, Samuel, Alma). The body→resurrection / blood→remission mapping is General-Conference doctrine (Hamula), not my invention. The water/blood/Spirit triad is canonical (Moses 6, 1 Jn 5). MY synthesis (mark as interpretive): the Spirit as the third *witness* that seals both victories; the whole read through the grammar-of-pairs + faith-hope-charity lens; the Leviticus 17 reversal. Keep interpretive moves marked; let the canonical spine carry the weight.
