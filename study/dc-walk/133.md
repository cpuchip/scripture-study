# D&C 133 — the Appendix: prepare ye, go out from Babylon

**Setting:** **Hiram, Ohio, November 3, 1831** — one of the *earliest* revelations in the book, given the day after §1 (the Preface), and placed *last* as the **Appendix**. The structure is deliberate: the book opens with the Lord's voice "unto all men" (§1) and closes with the gathering and the Second Coming (§133) — the Preface and the Appendix frame the whole as a last-days book. The elders had asked about preaching the gospel and the gathering; the answer is a sweeping Isaiah-woven prophecy of the Coming.

**Binding idea:** **"Prepare ye, prepare ye, O my people; sanctify yourselves; gather ye together… Go ye out from Babylon. Be ye clean that bear the vessels of the Lord" (133:4-5).** The gathering is *preparation* for the Lord who "shall suddenly come to his temple" (133:2) — flee spiritual Babylon, be clean, and watch, "for ye know neither the day nor the hour" (133:11).

**Read:**
- The charge: "The Lord who shall suddenly come to his temple… For he shall make bare his holy arm in the eyes of all the nations" (133:2-3). "Prepare ye, prepare ye, O my people; sanctify yourselves; gather ye together… Go ye out from Babylon. Be ye clean that bear the vessels of the Lord" (133:4-5). "Watch, therefore, for ye know neither the day nor the hour" (133:11). "let not your flight be in haste, but let all things be prepared before you; and he that goeth, let him not look back lest sudden destruction shall come upon him" (133:15).
- The two faces of the Coming: "I have trodden the wine-press alone, and have brought judgment upon all people; and none were with me… for this was the day of vengeance which was in my heart" (133:50-51); but: "In all their afflictions he was afflicted. And the angel of his presence saved them; and in his love, and in his pity, he redeemed them, and bore them, and carried them all the days of old" (133:53).
- The weak things: "To prepare the weak for those things which are coming on the earth… in the day when the weak shall confound the wise… And by the weak things of the earth the Lord shall thresh the nations by the power of his Spirit" (133:58-59).
- The close: "unto him that repenteth and sanctifieth himself before the Lord shall be given eternal life" (133:62).

**Thoughts:**

- *★ The two faces of the Coming — the winepress and the pity (133:50-53).* The most striking turn in the section sets two images of the same Lord side by side. The day of vengeance: "I have trodden the wine-press alone… none were with me… the day of vengeance which was in my heart" (133:50-51, Isa 63:3). And immediately: "**In all their afflictions he was afflicted**… in his love, and in his pity, he redeemed them, and bore them, and carried them all the days of old" (133:53, Isa 63:9). (The same Christ who treads the winepress *alone* in judgment is the one who was "afflicted" in *all* His people's afflictions — the §122:8 "the Son of Man hath descended below them all" given its Second-Coming form. The judgment and the tenderness are not two Gods but one: He who "bore them, and carried them" is He who comes "red in his apparel." For the walk's matter-spectrum/suffering-God thread: the Lord who *suffered-with* [§19 Gethsemane, §122 descended-below-all] is the same who *judges* — the pity and the vengeance flow from one heart, and the vengeance is *for* the afflicted He carried. The comfort and the warning are the same fact: He feels everything.)

- *★ Watch, but flee not in haste (133:11,15).* Two balanced commands govern the gathering's *posture*. "Watch, therefore, for ye know neither the day nor the hour" (133:11, the §49:7 withheld-day / §101 keep-the-watch) — *and* "let not your flight be in haste, but let all things be prepared before you" (133:15). (The §10:4 pace-to-strength applied to the last days: readiness is *vigilant* but not *panicked* — watch constantly, but flee in *order*, "all things prepared," not in a scramble. The §101 watchman who sees the enemy afar *and* the §125 gather-to-be-prepared joined: the prepared watch is calm, not frantic. For the project's Adjacent-Surfaces/foresight discipline: watchfulness is a *standing posture*, not an emergency reaction; the one who prepares in peace flees in order when the day comes.)

- *★ The weak things thresh the nations (133:58-59).* "To prepare the weak… in the day when the weak shall confound the wise… by the weak things of the earth the Lord shall thresh the nations by the power of his Spirit." (The §1:19/§35:13/§124:1 weak-things-of-the-earth thread reaching its eschatological scale: God does His greatest work *through* the weak, not the mighty [1 Cor 1:27] — the gospel restored "through the weak things" precisely so the glory is His. For the project working through limited instruments: the weakness is not a disqualification but the *chosen channel* — "by the weak things… the Lord shall thresh the nations.")

**Notable / flag:** No spin-off. **Walk's contribution:** **§133 is the Appendix — the earliest revelation in the book, placed last to close it on the gathering and the Coming.** The structural move is the teaching: §1 (the Preface, the Lord's voice "unto all men") and §133 (the Appendix, "prepare ye… go ye out from Babylon") *frame* the whole as a last-days book. The charge is to gather and prepare — "be ye clean that bear the vessels of the Lord" (133:5) — and to *watch*, "for ye know neither the day nor the hour" (133:11), yet flee "not in haste" but with "all things prepared" (133:15): vigilant, not panicked. The section's most striking turn sets the two faces of the same Lord side by side — the day of vengeance, "I have trodden the wine-press alone… none were with me" (133:50), and the pity, "in all their afflictions he was afflicted… he redeemed them, and bore them, and carried them" (133:53): the §122:8 "the Son of Man hath descended below them all" in Second-Coming form, the pity and the vengeance flowing from one heart, the judgment *for* the afflicted He carried. And the work is done "by the weak things of the earth" (133:59) — the chosen channel, so the glory is His. For Michael: the book's closing frame — gather, be clean, watch in calm readiness; the Lord who judges is the same who "was afflicted" in every affliction of His people; and He threshes the nations through the weak.
