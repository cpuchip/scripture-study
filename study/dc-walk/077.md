# D&C 77 — the sea of glass, and the spirit in the likeness of the temporal

**Setting:** **Hiram, Ohio, about March 1832** — alongside the Bible translation. "In connection with the translation of the Scriptures, I received the following explanation of the Revelation of St. John." A question-and-answer revelation: fifteen Q&A pairs decoding the symbols of the Apocalypse — the sea of glass, the four beasts, the sealed book, the 144,000, the little book, the two witnesses. Not a vision (like §76) but a *key* to John's vision.

**Binding idea:** Twice the revelation, decoding John's symbols, opens a window on the *matter-spectrum*: "the sea of glass… is the earth, in its sanctified, immortal, and eternal state" (77:1), and "that which is spiritual being in the likeness of that which is temporal; and that which is temporal in the likeness of that which is spiritual" (77:2). The symbols of heaven turn out to teach the *correspondence* of spirit and matter, and the *destiny* of the earth.

**Read:** "Q. What is the sea of glass spoken of by John…? A. It is the earth, in its sanctified, immortal, and eternal state" (77:1). "They are figurative expressions… that which is spiritual being in the likeness of that which is temporal; and that which is temporal in the likeness of that which is spiritual; the spirit of man in the likeness of his person, as also the spirit of the beast, and every other creature which God has created" (77:2). "They are limited to four individual beasts… to represent the glory of the classes of beings in their destined order or sphere of creation, in the enjoyment of their eternal felicity" (77:3). "Their eyes are a representation of light and knowledge… and their wings are a representation of power, to move, to act" (77:4). "The book which John saw… contains the revealed will, mysteries, and the works of God… concerning this earth during the seven thousand years of its continuance" (77:6). "Those who are sealed are high priests, ordained unto the holy order of God… to bring as many as will come to the church of the Firstborn" (77:11). "As God made the world in six days, and on the seventh day he finished his work, and sanctified it… even so, in the beginning of the seventh thousand years will the Lord God sanctify the earth, and complete the salvation of man" (77:12). "It was a mission, and an ordinance, for him to gather the tribes of Israel; behold, this is Elias, who, as it is written, must come and restore all things" (77:14).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "The sea of glass… is the earth, in its sanctified, immortal, and eternal state" (77:1).* John saw "a sea of glass like unto crystal" before God's throne (Rev 4:6) — and it is *the earth*, glorified. (The earth-transfigured thread the walk has tracked [§63:20-21 the earth transfigured; §76:77 the sea of glass before the throne] gets its plainest statement: the earth's *destiny* is to become a "sea of glass," celestialized — and §130:9 will complete it [the sanctified earth "will be a Urim and Thummim… a globe like a sea of glass and fire"]. The planet is not a disposable stage but a *being* on the path to glory, headed for its own sanctified, immortal, eternal state. The matter-spectrum's cosmology again: the earth is resurrected and glorified as the body is — "this earth… must die… [and] be quickened again, and shall abide the power by which it is quickened, and the righteous shall inherit it" [§88:25-26, weeks later]. For a faith that sometimes treats the physical world as merely temporary scaffolding: the earth itself is bound for celestial glory, a sea of glass before the throne of God.)
- *★ "That which is spiritual being in the likeness of that which is temporal" (77:2) — the matter-spectrum's correspondence.* Decoding the four beasts, the Lord drops a foundational principle: "that which is spiritual being in the likeness of that which is temporal; and that which is temporal in the likeness of that which is spiritual; the spirit of man in the likeness of his person, as also the spirit of the beast" (77:2). (The §29:31-35 "all things… are spiritual" thread deepened with a *structural* claim: spirit and matter *mirror each other*. The spirit is "in the likeness of" the body — the same form, the same shape; and the body is "in the likeness of" the spirit. This is not Platonic dualism [spirit good, matter bad, the body a prison]; it's *correspondence* — the temporal is the visible likeness of the spiritual, and the spiritual the pattern of the temporal. The spirit of man has a *person*, a form; so does the spirit of the beast. And animals have spirits and "eternal felicity" [77:3, §29:24-25] — the whole creation, not just humanity, has a spiritual reality destined for joy. The matter-spectrum's anthropology and cosmology fuse: spirit is refined matter [§131:7, later], in the likeness of its embodiment; the line between spiritual and temporal is one of *refinement*, not *kind*. For the project's matter-spectrum thread, this is a keystone: the temporal world is the *visible likeness* of a spiritual reality it mirrors, not its opposite.)
- *The structure of John's vision decoded — and the gathering Elias (77:6-15).* The remaining Q&A give the apocalyptic frame: the sealed book = the earth's "seven thousand years of its continuance" [77:6-7], each seal a millennium; the seventh trumpet/seventh thousand years = when "the Lord God [will] sanctify the earth, and complete the salvation of man" [77:12]; the 144,000 = "high priests, ordained unto the holy order of God… to bring as many as will come to the church of the Firstborn" [77:11, §76:54]; and *Elias* recurs twice [77:9,14] as the one who comes "to gather together the tribes of Israel and restore all things." (The gathering thread [§29/§45/§133] given its apocalyptic place: the sealing of the servants in their foreheads [77:9, Rev 7] precedes the destructions; the 144,000 are *missionaries* — ordained high priests gathering the church of the Firstborn before the end. And "restore all things" [77:14, Matt 17:11] ties the whole Restoration back to the Elias-keys of §27:6-7. The eyes = "light and knowledge," wings = "power, to move, to act" [77:4] — even the cherubim's anatomy teaches that glorified beings are *full of knowledge* and *power to act*, the §93 light-and-truth and the §76 dominion previewed.)

**Notable / flag:** No spin-off. **Walk's contribution:** **§77, decoding John's Revelation, drops two matter-spectrum keystones.** First, **"the sea of glass… is the earth, in its sanctified, immortal, and eternal state" (77:1)** — the earth's *destiny* is to become a sea of glass before God's throne, celestialized (the §63:20-21/§76:77 earth-transfigured thread's plainest statement → §130:9; the planet bound for glory like the resurrected body, not disposable scaffolding). Second, and deeper, **"that which is spiritual being in the likeness of that which is temporal; and that which is temporal in the likeness of that which is spiritual… the spirit of man in the likeness of his person, as also the spirit of the beast" (77:2)** — spirit and matter *mirror* each other (the §29:31-35 "all things spiritual" given a structural claim): not Platonic dualism with the body as a prison, but *correspondence* — the spirit has a person, a form, and the temporal world is the visible *likeness* of the spiritual reality it mirrors (spirit = refined matter, §131:7; even beasts have spirits and "eternal felicity," §29:24). Plus the apocalyptic frame decoded: the earth's seven-thousand-year course, the seventh-thousand-year sanctification, the 144,000 as ordained gatherers to the church of the Firstborn (§76:54), and the gathering Elias who comes to "restore all things" (§27:6-7). For Michael: the section that says the earth itself is headed for celestial glory — a sea of glass before the throne — and that spirit and matter are not opposites but *likenesses* of each other, the temporal world the visible image of a spiritual reality it mirrors.
