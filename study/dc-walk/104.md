# D&C 104 — the earth is full, and there is enough and to spare

**Setting:** **At or near Kirtland, Ohio, April 23, 1834** — a council of the United Firm (later "United Order"), facing "pressing temporal needs." An April 10 meeting had voted to *dissolve* the firm; this revelation directs that it be *reorganized* instead — its properties divided among the members as individual *stewardships*. The breach (covetousness, "feigned words") had nearly destroyed it; the Lord rebuilds it as stewardship-with-accountability.

**Binding idea:** "It is my purpose to provide for my saints, for all things are mine. But it must needs be done in mine own way… For the earth is full, and there is **enough and to spare**; yea, I prepared all things, and have given unto the children of men to be agents unto themselves" (104:15-17). There is no scarcity in God's economy — "enough and to spare." The problem is never supply; it is *distribution*, and the agency of stewards.

**Read:** "Some of my servants have not kept the commandment, but have broken the covenant through covetousness, and with feigned words; I have cursed them with a very sore and grievous curse" (104:4). "Organize yourselves and appoint every man his stewardship; that every man may give an account unto me of the stewardship which is appointed unto him. For it is expedient that I, the Lord, should make every man accountable, as a steward over earthly blessings" (104:11-13). "It is my purpose to provide for my saints, for all things are mine. But it must needs be done in mine own way… that the poor shall be exalted, in that the rich are made low" (104:15-16). "For the earth is full, and there is enough and to spare; yea, I prepared all things, and have given unto the children of men to be agents unto themselves" (104:17). **Thoughts:**
- *★ "Enough and to spare" — abundance, not scarcity (104:17-18).* The economic heart of the section, and a doctrine that reorders how one sees *want*. "The earth is full, and there is **enough and to spare**" (104:17) — God made abundance, *sufficient for all*. So poverty is *not* a supply problem; it is a *distribution* problem caused by *hoarding*: "if any man shall take of the abundance… and impart not his portion… unto the poor and the needy, he shall, with the wicked, lift up his eyes in hell" (104:18). (The §49:20 ["the world lieth in sin… one man possesses above another"] and §56 [the reciprocal-wo] reaching their root principle: there is no scarcity in God's creation — the earth is *full*, "enough and to spare" — so the *only* reason anyone goes without is that someone *kept more than his portion*. This dismantles the scarcity-mindset that justifies hoarding: "I must grasp because there isn't enough" is *false* — there is enough; the grasping *creates* the want. And the judgment on the hoarder is severe — "lift up his eyes in hell" [Luke 16, the rich man and Lazarus]. For the project's consecration/stewardship thread: the foundational economic fact is *abundance*, and the sin is *not imparting your portion* — the steward holds enough-and-to-spare *in trust for the poor*, and to hoard it is to be the rich man in torment. "Agents unto themselves" [104:17] — the abundance is given to *free agents* to distribute, and the distribution is the test of the heart.)
- *★ "All these properties are mine, then ye are stewards" (104:11-13,55-57).* The stewardship doctrine stated as a *logical hinge*. "Appoint every man his stewardship; that every man may give an account… make every man accountable" (104:11-13). And the sharp form: "all these properties are mine, or else your faith is vain… **and if the properties are mine, then ye are stewards; otherwise ye are no stewards**" (104:55-56). (The §70:3/§72:3 stewardship-accounting given its *premise*: stewardship *depends on the prior fact that the property is God's*. If "all things are mine" [104:15], then you *hold* but do not *own* — you are a steward, accountable. But if you *deny* God's ownership ["otherwise ye are no stewards"], then there is no stewardship at all, only possession. The whole stewardship framework [the project's own] rests on this hinge: *received, not owned*. "No man among you shall call it his own" [104:62] — the moment a steward says "mine," he has stepped outside the order. For the project: the stewardship pattern *is* the recognition that what you hold is God's; deny that, and "ye are no stewards" — there is no faithful holding without the prior surrender of ownership.)
- *Provide "in mine own way," and the curse on the covenant-breaker (104:4-16).* Two framing threads: God provides "in mine own way… that the poor shall be exalted, in that the rich are made low" [104:16 — the welfare order *inverts* the world's hierarchy; the §59:16/§42 consecration "way"]; and the *breaker* of the order, who "broke the covenant through covetousness," is "cursed… delivered over to the buffetings of Satan" [104:4,9, §82:21]. (The §82:10 bilateral covenant's *negative* side, enacted: the United Order *was* the covenant, and *covetousness broke it* — "the covenants being broken through transgression, by covetousness and feigned words" [104:52], so the order was *dissolved and divided* [104:47-53]. The very thing the order existed to defeat [covetousness] is what destroyed it from within [§101:6 — Zion fell from within]. The lesson the project's covenant pattern must hold: the gravest threat to a covenant-of-stewardship is *covetousness within* — the steward who says "mine." God provides *His* way [the poor exalted], but the way *fails* when the stewards covet.)
