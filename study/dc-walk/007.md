# D&C 7 — a man's desire becomes his destiny

**Setting:** Harmony, Pennsylvania, **April 1829.** Joseph and Oliver, translating, hit John 21:20-23 — Jesus' cryptic word about the beloved disciple: "if I will that he tarry till I come, what is that to thee?" The early Church had debated for eighteen centuries whether John died or lingered. They asked through the Urim and Thummim, and the answer was **a translation of a record John himself wrote on parchment and hid up.** Eight verses; the heading's claim is striking — this section is not *about* John's parchment, it *is* John's parchment, translated.

**Binding idea:** Two apostles, two desires, two destinies. Peter desired to "speedily come" to Christ's kingdom; John desired to "bring souls" and tarry to do it. Both were good — and the Lord granted each man exactly the eternity his desire was shaped like (7:8).

**Read:** "John, my beloved, what desirest thou?" (7:1). John: "give unto me power over death, that I may live and bring souls unto thee" (7:2). The Lord: "because thou desirest this thou shalt tarry until I come in my glory, and shalt prophesy before nations, kindreds, tongues and people" (7:3). To Peter: "this was a good desire; but my beloved has desired that he might do more, or a greater work yet among men" (7:5) — "I will make him as flaming fire and a ministering angel; he shall minister for those who shall be heirs of salvation" (7:6). "Unto you three I will give this power and the keys of this ministry until I come" (7:7). "Ye shall both have according to your desires, for ye both joy in that which ye have desired" (7:8).

**Thoughts:**
- *Desire shapes destiny — the §4-6 thread reaches its sharpest form.* §4: desire = the call. §6: "because of thy desires." Here: a man's *eternal mode of existence* is granted to fit what he most wanted. John wanted to keep working, so he was made a translated being, "flaming fire," still laboring two thousand years on. The Lord doesn't override desire; He *honors* it — "ye both joy in that which ye have desired" (7:8). Heaven is not one-size; it is desire, fulfilled. (Compare Alma 29:4 from the BoM walk — "God granteth unto men according to their desires, whether… unto death or unto life.")
- *Peter's desire was "good," not lesser-because-rebuked.* The Lord is careful: "this was a good desire" (7:5). He's not scolding Peter for wanting heaven sooner; He's distinguishing two goods. John's was *greater* only in that it chose more labor over rest. The text refuses to flatten the comparison into Peter-failed/John-won. Two faithful men, two honorable wants.
- *The keys link forward.* "Unto you three I will give this power and the keys of this ministry until I come" (7:7) — Peter, James, and John, who in 1829 (weeks or months from this revelation) would restore the Melchizedek Priesthood, and at Kirtland confer keys. The footnote ties 7:7 to D&C 6:28 and Matt 16:19. The parchment Joseph translated names the very three who would lay hands on his head. (The priesthood-line thread from the PoGP walk — Moses 6:7 / Abr 1:3 — is now running live in the Restoration's own founding.)
