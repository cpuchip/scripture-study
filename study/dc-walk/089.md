# D&C 89 — a principle with promise: the Word of Wisdom

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, February 27, 1833.** The early brethren used tobacco in their meetings of the School of the Prophets (just founded, §88) — and the mess "concerned his wife, Emma Smith," who cleaned the floor after. Joseph inquired of the Lord, and this revelation, "known as the Word of Wisdom," resulted. It is a law of health — but framed, remarkably, *not as a command* but as *wisdom with a promise*.

**Binding idea:** "A Word of Wisdom… to be sent greeting; **not by commandment or constraint, but by revelation and the word of wisdom**… Given for a principle with promise, adapted to the capacity of the weak and the weakest of all saints" (89:2-3). A law of the body offered as *counsel and promise*, accommodated to the weak — persuasion, not compulsion.

**Read:** "Not by commandment or constraint, but by revelation and the word of wisdom, showing forth the order and will of God in the temporal salvation of all saints in the last days— Given for a principle with promise, adapted to the capacity of the weak and the weakest of all saints" (89:2-3). "In consequence of evils and designs which do and will exist in the hearts of conspiring men in the last days, I have warned you, and forewarn you, by giving unto you this word of wisdom by revelation" (89:4). "Strong drinks are not for the belly, but for the washing of your bodies" (89:7). "Tobacco is not for the body, neither for the belly, and is not good for man… And again, hot drinks are not for the body or belly" (89:8-9). "All wholesome herbs God hath ordained for the constitution, nature, and use of man— every herb in the season thereof, and every fruit in the season thereof; all these to be used with prudence and thanksgiving" (89:10-11). "Flesh also of beasts and of the fowls of the air, I, the Lord, have ordained for the use of man with thanksgiving; nevertheless they are to be used sparingly" (89:12). "All grain is ordained for the use of man and of beasts, to be the staff of life" (89:14). "All saints who remember to keep and do these sayings, walking in obedience to the commandments, shall receive health in their navel and marrow to their bones; and shall find wisdom and great treasures of knowledge, even hidden treasures; and shall run and not be weary, and shall walk and not faint. And I, the Lord, give unto them a promise, that the destroying angel shall pass by them, as the children of Israel, and not slay them" (89:18-21).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "Not by commandment or constraint, but… a principle with promise" (89:2-3).* The framing is as striking as the content. A law of the body — and the Lord gives it *not* as a command but as *wisdom*, "not by commandment or constraint," "adapted to the capacity of the weak and the weakest of all saints." (The presiding covenant's persuasion-not-compulsion [§121:41] applied to *personal habit*: the Lord teaches the *principle* and attaches a *promise*, then leaves the keeping to the steward's agency — the §58:26 "anxiously engaged… of their own free will" at the scale of the dinner table. He *accommodates the weak* [§1:24 period-language / §78:18 "I will lead you along"] — the standard is set where "the weakest of all saints" can begin. [The Church later made it a *commandment* and a temple-recommend standard — but *as given* it is wisdom-with-promise, the gentlest mode of a law.] The doctrine for the project's own counsel: the deepest compliance is won by *teaching the principle and showing the promise*, not by constraint — and the standard meets the weak where they are. A principle freely embraced outlasts a rule merely imposed.)
- *★ The body is sacred — the temporal-spiritual link (89:8,18-19).* The Word of Wisdom treats the *body* as worth guarding: tobacco "is not good for man" [89:8], flesh "sparingly" [89:12], grain "the staff of life" [89:14] — and the reward is *both* physical and spiritual: "health in their navel and marrow to their bones" *and* "wisdom and great treasures of knowledge, even hidden treasures" [89:18-19]. (The matter-spectrum's life-affirming side [§59:16-20 the goodness of the earth; §49 against asceticism] extended to *diet*: the physical is *good* and to be received "with prudence and thanksgiving" [89:11, gratitude again, §59:7]. But more — the promise links *bodily* obedience to *mental/spiritual* reward: keep the law of the body, and you "find… great treasures of knowledge." The §88:67 "eye single → filled with light → comprehend all things" given a bodily dimension: a body kept clean is a clearer vessel for the light; the temporal and spiritual are not separate [§29:34 "all things… are spiritual"]. For the project: the body affects the mind — clarity of understanding is not purely intellectual but rests on a *clean, ordered vessel*; "treasures of knowledge" are promised to the *whole* person, body included.)
- *★ The promise — run and not be weary, the destroying angel passes by (89:20-21).* The principle's promise closes with two images: "shall run and not be weary, and shall walk and not faint" [89:20, Isa 40:31], and "the destroying angel shall pass by them, as the children of Israel, and not slay them" [89:21, Ex 12:23, the Passover]. (Isaiah's "run and not be weary" — the *endurance* promise: the body kept by the Word of Wisdom is *sustained* in the long labor [the §10:4 pace-to-strength given a physical floor; for Michael's intermittent-burst work, "run and not be weary" is the promise of *stamina*]. And the Passover image — "the destroying angel shall pass by them": the obedient are *protected* as Israel was on the night of the firstborn, the blood on the door [Christ's blood] sparing them. The promise is not merely health but *preservation* — the §61:6 "the faithful shall not perish" at the scale of the destroying angel. The law of the body is, finally, about *being kept* — sustained for the work and spared in the judgment.)

**Notable / flag:** No spin-off. **Walk's contribution:** **§89, the Word of Wisdom, is a law of the body given as wisdom, not command — "not by commandment or constraint, but by revelation and the word of wisdom… a principle with promise, adapted to the capacity of the weak" (89:2-3).** The presiding covenant's persuasion-not-compulsion (§121:41) applied to *personal habit*: the Lord teaches the principle, attaches a promise, and leaves the keeping to the steward's agency (§58:26), with the standard set where "the weakest of all saints" can begin — a principle freely embraced outlasts a rule merely imposed. The body is treated as *sacred* (the temporal-spiritual link): bodily obedience earns *both* "health in their navel and marrow to their bones" *and* "wisdom and great treasures of knowledge, even hidden treasures" (89:18-19) — the matter-spectrum's life-affirming side (§59:16-20) extended to diet, the §88:67 light-filled body given a bodily dimension (a clean vessel is a clearer channel; the body affects the mind). And the promise closes with endurance and preservation: "shall run and not be weary, and shall walk and not faint" (Isa 40:31 — stamina for the long labor, a word for Michael's intermittent-burst work) and "the destroying angel shall pass by them" (the Passover — the faithful *kept*). For Michael: the section that gives a law of the body in the gentlest mode — wisdom with a promise, meeting the weak where they are — and ties the clean body to a clear mind and a sustained, preserved life.
