# D&C 103 — after much tribulation cometh the blessing

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, February 24, 1834** — the same day Parley P. Pratt and Lyman Wight arrived from Missouri "to counsel with the Prophet as to the relief and restoration of the Saints to their lands in Jackson County." The revelation answers *what to do* about the lost Zion: it commands the gathering of **Zion's Camp** — an organized company to march to Missouri — and frames the whole effort with the law that blessing *follows* tribulation.

**Binding idea:** "After much tribulation, as I have said unto you in a former commandment, cometh the blessing… your redemption… restoration to the land of Zion" (103:12-13). And the means: "the redemption of Zion must needs come by power" (103:15) — the §101 parable enacted, Joseph the servant told to "gather… the strength of my house."

**Read:** "I have suffered them thus far, that [the enemies] might fill up the measure of their iniquities… and that those who call themselves after my name might be chastened… because they did not hearken altogether unto the precepts and commandments" (103:3-4). "After much tribulation… cometh the blessing… your redemption, and the redemption of your brethren… to be established, no more to be thrown down" (103:12-13). "Nevertheless, if they pollute their inheritances they shall be thrown down" (103:14). "The redemption of Zion must needs come by power" (103:15). "I will raise up unto my people a man, who shall lead them like as Moses led the children of Israel… ye must needs be led out of bondage by power, and with a stretched-out arm" (103:16-17). "I say not unto you as I said unto your fathers: Mine angel shall go up before you, but not my presence. But I say unto you: **Mine angels shall go up before you, and also my presence**" (103:19-20). "Let my servant Joseph Smith, Jun., say unto the strength of my house… Gather yourselves together unto the land of Zion" (103:22). "Let no man be afraid to lay down his life for my sake; for whoso layeth down his life for my sake shall find it again. And whoso is not willing to lay down his life for my sake is not my disciple" (103:27-28). "Ask and ye shall receive; but men do not always do my will" (103:31). "All victory and glory is brought to pass unto you through your diligence, faithfulness, and prayers of faith" (103:36).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "After much tribulation cometh the blessing" (103:12).* The law that frames the whole redemption: the blessing is *real and certain* — but it comes *after* the tribulation, not instead of it. "After much tribulation… cometh the blessing… your redemption… to be established, no more to be thrown down" (103:12-13). (The §58:3-4 order ["after much tribulation come the blessings… the doubters who say 'this is not the work of the Lord' misread the *sequence*"] reaffirmed at the lowest point: the Saints had lost everything, and the Lord does *not* deny the tribulation or promise its absence — He promises the blessing *on the far side of it*. The §98:3 "all things… work together for your good" given its *temporal shape*: tribulation *first*, blessing *after*. For the discouraged: the order is not blessing-or-tribulation but tribulation-*then*-blessing; the present suffering is not the contradiction of the promise but the *road to* it. And the caveat holds [103:14]: "if they pollute their inheritances they shall be thrown down" — the blessing after tribulation is still *conditional* on faithfulness [§97:25].)
- *★ "Mine angels shall go up before you, and also my presence" (103:19-20).* A pointed contrast with Moses's generation. When Israel sinned with the golden calf, the Lord said He would send "an angel" before them "but I will not go up in the midst of thee" [Ex 33:3] — His *presence* withdrawn. Here the Lord *reverses* it: "I say *not* unto you as I said unto your fathers… Mine angels shall go up before you, **and also my presence**" (103:19-20). (The Restoration generation is offered what the wilderness generation *forfeited*: not merely an angel but God's *presence*. This is the §84:23-24 [Moses sought to bring Israel into God's presence; they "could not endure" it, so the higher priesthood was taken] *answered* — what was lost at Sinai is *restored* in the latter day. The promise is staggering for a beaten, scattered people: you get *more* than your fathers, His presence itself goes with you. Conditioned, as always, on hearkening [103:5-8] — but offered. For the project: the latter-day covenant offers the presence the earlier dispensation forfeited; the gift is *greater*, not lesser, for those who keep it.)
- *★ Zion's Camp organized — the §101 parable enacted (103:15-40).* "The redemption of Zion must needs come by power" [103:15]; Joseph is named "the man to whom I likened the servant" in the §101 nobleman parable [103:21], told to "say unto the strength of my house… Gather yourselves together unto the land of Zion" [103:22] — to raise a company of 500 [or 300, or at least 100, 103:30-34] to march to Missouri. (The §101:55-56 "gather the strength of mine house… redeem my vineyard" *enacted*: Zion's Camp. And the realism — "ask and ye shall receive; *but men do not always do my will*" [103:31, §82:10's footnote] — the number depends on the *Saints' response*; God commands 500, but if the people won't, then 300, then 100. The redemption "by power" led "like as Moses" [103:16] is hedged by the people's *agency*: the Lord calls, but cannot compel the response. [Historically, Zion's Camp marched, did *not* retake Jackson County, and was largely seen as a "failure" — yet the Lord later said it was a *proving* [§105], and the future leadership of the Church was forged in it. The march that "failed" its stated goal *succeeded* at God's deeper one — proving and training the men.] "All victory and glory is brought to pass unto you through your diligence, faithfulness, and prayers of faith" [103:36] — the victory is *conditioned on the camp's faithfulness*, not guaranteed by the cause.)
- *The willingness-to-die test (103:27-28).* "Let no man be afraid to lay down his life for my sake… whoso layeth down his life for my sake shall find it again. And whoso is not willing to lay down his life for my sake is not my disciple" [103:27-28, Luke 9:24]. (The §98:13 martyr's promise as a *discipleship test*: not that all *will* die, but that *willingness* to is the mark of a disciple. The men marching to Zion's Camp are asked, in advance, for the willingness — "find it again" [the life surrendered restored]. The §18:10 worth-of-souls and the §101:35 "all they who suffer persecution… shall partake of all this glory": the disciple holds his life loosely, willing to lose it, and *thereby* finds it.)

**Notable / flag:** No spin-off. **Walk's contribution:** **§103 commands Zion's Camp and frames it with the law "after much tribulation cometh the blessing" (103:12).** To Saints who had lost everything, the Lord does not deny the tribulation or promise its absence — He promises the blessing *on the far side of it* (the §58:3-4 order reaffirmed at the lowest point; the present suffering is the *road to* the promise, not its contradiction, though still conditional — "if they pollute their inheritances they shall be thrown down," 103:14). And a staggering offer: where the wilderness generation was told "an angel… but *not* my presence" (Ex 33:3, after the golden calf), this generation is told "mine angels shall go up before you, **and also my presence**" (103:19-20) — what Sinai forfeited (§84:23-24) *restored*, the latter-day gift *greater*, not lesser. The §101 nobleman parable is *enacted*: "the redemption of Zion must needs come by power" (103:15), Joseph the servant told to "gather the strength of my house" — Zion's Camp, 500 men (or 300, or at least 100) to march to Missouri. But the realism: "ask and ye shall receive; *but men do not always do my will*" (103:31) — the number, and the victory, depend on the *Saints' response* ("all victory… through your diligence, faithfulness, and prayers of faith," 103:36); God calls but cannot compel. (The march would "fail" its stated goal yet *prove* the men — §105.) And the willingness-to-die discipleship test: "whoso is not willing to lay down his life for my sake is not my disciple" (103:28). For Michael: the section that says the blessing is sure but comes *after* the tribulation, that the latter-day Saints are offered God's *presence* where their fathers got only an angel, and that even a redemption "by power" waits on the people's free response — God calls the strength of His house, but the camp's faithfulness, not the cause, secures the victory.
