# D&C 26 — by common consent

**Setting:** **Harmony, Pennsylvania, July 1830** (with §24, §25) — to Joseph, Oliver, and John Whitmer, still under the early persecution. Two verses: a near-term instruction (study, preach, work the land until the next conference) and one enduring principle — the law of common consent.

**Binding idea:** "All things shall be done by common consent in the church, by much prayer and faith" (26:2). The Church is not run by command from the top; its acts are ratified by the *consent of its members*, sought through prayer and faith. Authority is conferred from God (§13), but it is *exercised* with the agreement of the governed.

**Read:** "Let your time be devoted to the studying of the scriptures, and to preaching, and to confirming the church at Colesville… and to performing your labors on the land… until after you shall go to the west to hold the next conference; and then it shall be made known what you shall do" (26:1). "And all things shall be done by common consent in the church, by much prayer and faith, for all things you shall receive by faith. Amen" (26:2).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ Common consent — authority by agreement, not command (26:2).* This is the constitutional principle §20:65 stated in passing, now given as *law*: "all things shall be done by common consent in the church." The Restoration's government is neither a democracy (the people don't *originate* the revelations) nor an autocracy (the leaders don't *impose* them) — it is *consent*. The Lord reveals through the prophet (§21); the Church *ratifies* by common consent. Both are required: revelation *and* agreement. (This is the presiding covenant structurally encoded again — "no power or influence… only by persuasion" [D&C 121:41]. Even God's own revelations are put to the Church's consent. Authority that must be *consented to* cannot be *compelled*; common consent is the institutional form of governing without compulsory means.)
- *"By much prayer and faith… all things you shall receive by faith" (26:2).* The consent isn't a show of hands as a political mechanism; it's *spiritual*. "Common consent" is sought "by much prayer and faith" — the members ratify by *seeking confirmation*, the §9 study-it-out-then-confirm done corporately. The body agrees because the body has *prayed* and received the same witness. (So common consent is the §9 verification epistemology *scaled to a church*: the prophet brings the revelation [studies it out, receives], and the body confirms it by the Spirit [prays, receives the burning]. The Church is governed by distributed confirmation, not by vote-counting.)
- *The near-term and the enduring, side by side (26:1-2).* The section pairs a *dated* instruction ("until… the next conference; and then it shall be made known what you shall do") with a *permanent* law (common consent). The Lord gives just enough for now — study, preach, work, wait for the conference — and withholds the rest ("then it shall be made known"). The §25:4 discipline of the withheld, applied to *guidance*: you get the next step and the standing principle, not the whole map. (Line upon line, §1:25-28 — even the instructions come incrementally.)

It's neither democracy nor autocracy but *consent* — revelation through the prophet, ratification by the body — and it's the presiding covenant structurally encoded (even God's revelations are put to the Church's consent; authority that must be consented to cannot be compelled). And the consent is *spiritual*: the §9 study-then-confirm epistemology scaled to a church — distributed confirmation by prayer, not vote-counting. For Michael: two verses, but one of them is the whole reason the Restoration's hierarchy isn't a tyranny — even God governs His Church by consent, and the consent is the members praying their way to the same witness.
