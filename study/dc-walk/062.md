# D&C 62 — the advocate who knows weakness, and testimony recorded in heaven

**Setting:** **On the bank of the Missouri River at Chariton, Missouri, August 13, 1831** — the day after §61. Joseph's group, returning from Independence to Kirtland, *met several elders going the other way, toward Zion.* "After joyful salutations," this revelation came. Nine verses to elders crossing paths on the road — a word about the *succoring Advocate*, testimony *recorded in heaven*, and (again) the means of travel left to their own judgment.

**Binding idea:** "Jesus Christ, your advocate, who knoweth the weakness of man and how to succor them who are tempted" (62:1) — the Advocate (§45:3) who is also the *succorer*, who knows weakness from the inside and helps the tempted. And to elders who'd been preaching: "the testimony which ye have borne is recorded in heaven for the angels to look upon; and they rejoice over you" (62:3). What you witness on earth is written in heaven, and the angels rejoice.

**Read:** "Jesus Christ, your advocate, who knoweth the weakness of man and how to succor them who are tempted" (62:1). "Mine eyes are upon those who have not as yet gone up unto the land of Zion; wherefore your mission is not yet full. Nevertheless, ye are blessed, for the testimony which ye have borne is recorded in heaven for the angels to look upon; and they rejoice over you, and your sins are forgiven you" (62:2-3). "Assemble yourselves upon the land of Zion; and hold a meeting and rejoice together, and offer a sacrament unto the Most High" (62:4). "Then you may return to bear record… altogether, or two by two, as seemeth you good, **it mattereth not unto me**; only be faithful" (62:5). "I, the Lord, promise the faithful and cannot lie" (62:6). "If any among you desire to ride upon horses, or upon mules, or in chariots, he shall receive this blessing, if he receive it from the hand of the Lord, with a thankful heart in all things" (62:7). "These things remain with you to do according to judgment and the directions of the Spirit" (62:8). "The kingdom is yours. And behold, and lo, I am with the faithful always" (62:9).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "The advocate… who knoweth the weakness of man and how to succor them who are tempted" (62:1).* The Advocate of §45:3 (pleading our cause by His blood) is here named also as the *succorer* — the One who "knoweth the weakness of man." This is the Hebrews 2:18 / Alma 7:11-13 doctrine: Christ helps the tempted *because He has been tempted*; He knows weakness *from the inside*, having taken on mortal infirmity. (The Advocate doesn't plead for us as a distant attorney; He *succors* us as one who *knows* — the §19 first-person Atonement [He suffered "body and spirit"] making Him not only our redeemer but our *sympathizer*. "How to succor them who are tempted" — He learned, in the flesh, *how* to help, by being helped-less Himself. The pleading [§45] and the succoring [§62] are the same Christ: He argues our case before the Father *and* holds us up in the temptation, because He has stood where we stand.)
- *★ "Your testimony… is recorded in heaven for the angels to look upon; and they rejoice over you" (62:3).* The witness these elders bore on dusty Missouri roads is *written down in heaven* — recorded in the book of life "for the angels to look upon," and the angels *rejoice*. (The §47 record-keeping thread reaches its other end: not only does the *Church* keep its history [§47], but *heaven* keeps the record of every testimony borne — your faithful witness is *logged above*, and watched, and *celebrated* by angels [Luke 15:7,10, the joy in heaven]. Nothing faithful is unwitnessed; the smallest testimony on a back road is "recorded in heaven." For anyone laboring unseen: you are *seen*, and your record is kept where it counts, and the angels rejoice over it. The §50:22 "rejoice together" extended to heaven — the angels join the rejoicing.)
- *★ The agency refrain, again — "it mattereth not unto me… according to judgment and the directions of the Spirit" (62:5,8).* For the *third* travel section running (§60:5, §61:22, §62:5), the Lord declines to dictate the means: return "altogether, or two by two, as seemeth you good, **it mattereth not unto me**; only be faithful" (62:5); "these things remain with you to do **according to judgment and the directions of the Spirit**" (62:8). (The §58:26 agency doctrine as a settled *pattern* of the Lord's dealings: on the *how*, He leaves it to "judgment and the directions of the Spirit" — the steward's own discernment, Spirit-guided. "Only be faithful" — the *principle* is commanded [faithfulness], the *method* is the steward's. And note 62:7: even the *blessing* of riding in comfort is available "if he receive it… **with a thankful heart in all things**" [§59:7,21] — the agency to choose the means comes with the duty of *gratitude* for whatever's chosen. Agency + thanksgiving together.)
- *"I am with the faithful always" (62:9).* The close, like §61:36: *presence*. "The kingdom is yours… I am with the faithful always" (Matt 28:20). (The recurring sign-off of these Missouri sections — after the danger [§61], the crossing-paths, the agency-on-the-means — the constant is *with-ness*: "I am with the faithful always." The condition is *faithfulness*; the promise is *presence*. The whole §24/§30/§31/§34/§61/§62 antidote thread: the answer to every road's uncertainty is "I am with you.")
