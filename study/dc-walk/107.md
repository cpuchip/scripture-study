# D&C 107 — on priesthood: the keys, the councils, and let every man learn his duty

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, late winter or spring 1835** — "a revelation on the priesthood," compiled around the organization of the **Quorum of the Twelve** (Feb-March 1835), delivered to those about to depart May 4 on the Twelve's first quorum mission. (Verses 60-100 incorporate an earlier revelation of November 11, 1831.) Where §84 gave the *oath and covenant* of the priesthood, §107 gives its *structure*: the two priesthoods, the presiding quorums, the order from Adam, the courts, and the law that governs them all — "let every man learn his duty."

**Binding idea:** "The power and authority of the higher, or Melchizedek Priesthood, is to hold the keys of all the spiritual blessings of the church— to have the privilege of receiving the mysteries… to have the heavens opened unto them, to commune with the general assembly and church of the Firstborn, and to enjoy the communion and presence of God the Father, and Jesus" (107:18-19). The greater priesthood is, at root, the *keys to God's presence* — and everything else (quorums, councils, courts) is the ordered structure that carries that.

**Read:** "There are, in the church, two priesthoods, namely, the Melchizedek and Aaronic" (107:1); "before his day it was called the Holy Priesthood, after the Order of the Son of God… to avoid the too frequent repetition of his name, they… called that priesthood after Melchizedek" (107:3-4). "All other authorities or offices in the church are appendages to this priesthood" (107:5). "The power and authority of the higher, or Melchizedek Priesthood, is to hold the keys of all the spiritual blessings of the church— to have the privilege of receiving the mysteries of the kingdom of heaven, to have the heavens opened unto them… and to enjoy the communion and presence of God the Father, and Jesus the mediator of the new covenant" (107:18-19). "The power and authority of the lesser, or Aaronic Priesthood, is to hold the keys of the ministering of angels, and to administer in outward ordinances" (107:20). "Three Presiding High Priests… form a quorum of the Presidency of the Church" (107:22); "the twelve traveling councilors are called to be the Twelve Apostles, or special witnesses" (107:23), "equal in authority and power to the three presidents" (107:24); "the Seventy… equal in authority to that of the Twelve" (107:25-26). "Every decision made by either of these quorums must be by the unanimous voice of the same" (107:27). "The decisions of these quorums… are to be made in all righteousness, in holiness, and lowliness of heart, meekness and long-suffering, and in faith, and virtue, and knowledge, temperance, patience, godliness, brotherly kindness and charity" (107:30). "This order was instituted in the days of Adam, and came down by lineage" (107:41). "Three years previous to the death of Adam, he called [his righteous posterity]… into the valley of Adam-ondi-Ahman… and the Lord appeared unto them, and they rose up and blessed Adam, and called him Michael, the prince, the archangel" (107:53-54). "There is not any person belonging to the church who is exempt from this council of the church… inasmuch as a President of the High Priesthood shall transgress, he shall be had in remembrance before the common council… that all things may be done in order" (107:81-84). "The President of the office of the High Priesthood is to preside over the whole church, and to be like unto Moses— yea, to be a seer, a revelator, a translator, and a prophet" (107:91-92). "Now let every man learn his duty, and to act in the office in which he is appointed, in all diligence. He that is slothful shall not be counted worthy to stand, and he that learns not his duty and shows himself not approved shall not be counted worthy to stand" (107:99-100).

**Thoughts:**
- *★★ The greater priesthood = the keys to God's presence (107:18-20).* The deepest definition of the Melchizedek Priesthood is not *administrative* but *relational*: "to hold the keys of all the spiritual blessings… to have the heavens opened unto them, to commune with the general assembly and church of the Firstborn, and **to enjoy the communion and presence of God the Father, and Jesus**" (107:18-19). (The §84:19-22 [the power of godliness in the ordinances → "no man can see the face of God… and live" without it] given its *positive* statement: the greater priesthood's whole point is *access to God* — the heavens opened, communion with the church of the Firstborn [§76:54], the *presence* of the Father and the Son. Everything structural in §107 [quorums, courts, offices] exists to carry *this*. The lesser priesthood holds "the keys of the ministering of angels" [107:20] — even the *lesser* opens heaven, to angels. The whole priesthood is, at root, the *ordered channel by which heaven is opened to earth*. I flag the priesthood-as-keys-to-God's-presence as the temple-reserved core, Michael's. For the project: authority/structure exists not for its own sake but to *carry the access to God* — the org chart serves the communion, never the reverse.)
- *★★ The councils decide by unanimity, grounded in the virtues (107:27-32).* The most project-relevant passage: the presiding quorums [First Presidency, Twelve, Seventy] are *equal in authority* [107:24,26], and "every decision made by either of these quorums must be by the **unanimous voice** of the same" (107:27). And *how* they decide: "the decisions of these quorums… are to be made in **all righteousness, in holiness, and lowliness of heart, meekness and long-suffering, and in faith, and virtue, and knowledge, temperance, patience, godliness, brotherly kindness and charity**" (107:30, 2 Pet 1:5-7). (Two stunning design choices. First, *unanimity*: the highest councils do not decide by majority but by *complete agreement* [§26:2 common consent at its apex] — a single dissent blocks the decision's full validity [107:27,29]. This forces the council to *reason together until one* [§88:122 the council order; Abr 4:26 "took counsel"], not to outvote a minority. Second, and deeper, the decisions are to be made *in the virtues* — not merely *procedurally correct* but *charactered*: holiness, meekness, long-suffering, charity. A decision reached *without* these virtues, "made in unrighteousness," can be *appealed* [107:32]. The council's *manner* [the virtues] is as binding as its *form* [unanimity]. For the project's whole council/stewardship discipline: the model decision is *unanimous* [reasoned to oneness, not voted] and *virtue-grounded* [made in meekness and charity, not just logic] — the *how* of a decision is part of its validity. This is the deepest scriptural charter for the workspace's council pattern.)
- *★ Accountability reaches the top — "none shall be exempt" (107:78-84).* The court structure ends in a striking equality-before-the-law: the highest cases go to the First Presidency with twelve high-priest counselors [107:78-79], "this is the highest council… a final decision" [107:80]. And then: "**there is not any person belonging to the church who is exempt** from this council… inasmuch as a **President of the High Priesthood** shall transgress, he shall be had in remembrance before the common council… that all things may be done in order" (107:81-84). (Even *the prophet* is accountable — if the President of the High Priesthood transgresses, he is *tried* by a common council of the church. "None shall be exempted from the justice and the laws of God" [107:84]. The presiding covenant's anti-tyranny [§121:41-43] built into the constitution: the one at the very top is *not above* the law he administers; accountability runs *all the way up*. The §102 correctable-decision and §90:36 even-Zion-chastened reaching the apex — there is no office so high it escapes accountability. For the project's presiding covenant: the presider is *himself* accountable, to the top of the chain; no steward, however high, is exempt. The structure that would be tyranny vests accountability *over* its own head.)
- *★ The priesthood from Adam, and Adam-ondi-Ahman (107:40-57).* The §84 lineage retold with detail: "this order was instituted in the days of Adam, and came down by lineage" [107:41] — each patriarch ordained by Adam's hand [Seth, Enos, Cainan… to Noah], with their ages. And the great scene: "three years previous to the death of Adam, he called [his righteous posterity]… into the valley of **Adam-ondi-Ahman**… and the Lord appeared unto them, and they rose up and blessed Adam, and called him **Michael, the prince, the archangel**" [107:53-54]; "I have set thee to be at the head… thou art a prince over them forever" [107:55]. (The §84:6-17 cross-walk lineage given its *patriarchal* form and its *gathering*: Adam, "bowed down with age, being full of the Holy Ghost, predicted whatsoever should befall his posterity unto the latest generation" [107:56]. Adam = Michael [§27:11, §78:16 — presiding *under* the Holy One], gathering his righteous seed three years before death for a last blessing, and the Lord *appears*. Adam-ondi-Ahman [§116 — the future gathering place] is named here as the *first* such gathering, the pattern. The lineage-and-gathering [bin-4-adjacent, Michael's]; the priesthood is a *family* line presided by Adam under Christ.)
- *★★ "Let every man learn his duty… in all diligence" (107:99-100).* The section — all its grand structure — lands on a *personal* charge: "**now let every man learn his duty, and to act in the office in which he is appointed, in all diligence. He that is slothful shall not be counted worthy to stand, and he that learns not his duty and shows himself not approved shall not be counted worthy to stand**" (107:99-100). (After ten dozen verses of quorums, councils, courts, and lineage, the whole thing reduces to *each person learning and doing his own duty diligently*. The §84:109 "stand in your own office" + the §58:29 anti-slothfulness made the *capstone* of the priesthood: the entire ordered structure *works* only if "every man learns his duty… and acts in all diligence." Two failures bar a man from "standing": *slothfulness* [not acting] and *not learning his duty* [not knowing]. The structure is not self-executing; it runs on *diligent, informed stewards*. For the project's stewardship pattern: the most elaborate org is only as good as each steward *learning his duty and acting in it diligently* — "let every man learn his duty" is the operating instruction under all the architecture. The slothful and the willfully ignorant "shall not be counted worthy to stand." This is the §58:26 agency doctrine's *imperative* form: you are an agent — so *learn your duty and do it*.)
