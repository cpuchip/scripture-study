# D&C 6 — the witness Oliver already had

**Setting:** Harmony, Pennsylvania, **April 1829** — days after **Oliver Cowdery** arrived (April 5) and began as scribe (April 7). Oliver, a schoolteacher boarding with Joseph's parents, had heard of the plates, prayed about them, and received a private manifestation; he came to Harmony already half-convinced and sat down to write. Joseph inquired through the Urim and Thummim and received this. It is the Lord introducing Himself to a new disciple — and proving He already knows him.

**Binding idea:** A witness isn't given to Oliver so much as *named back to him* — the Lord reminds Oliver of a private prayer no one else knew about ("the night that you cried unto me in your heart," 6:22) and asks, "What greater witness can you have than from God?" (6:23). The deepest evidence isn't a new sign; it's the recognition that God was already answering you.

**Read:** The marvelous work is coming (6:1); the Lord's word is "quick and powerful, sharper than a two-edged sword" (6:2); the field is white, "whoso desireth to reap, let him thrust in his sickle with his might… that he may treasure up for his soul everlasting salvation" (6:3); "whosoever will thrust in his sickle and reap, the same is called of God" (6:4). "Seek not for riches but for wisdom, and behold, the mysteries of God shall be unfolded unto you, and then shall you be made rich" (6:7); "even as you desire of me so it shall be unto you" (6:8). Oliver has a gift, "sacred… cometh from above" (6:10); if he inquires he will know mysteries (6:11). Then the witness: "thou hast inquired of me and I did enlighten thy mind… that thou mayest know that thou hast been enlightened by the Spirit of truth" (6:15) — "there is none else save God that knowest thy thoughts and the intents of thy heart" (6:16). "Stand by my servant Joseph… in whatsoever difficult circumstances" (6:18); "treasure up these words in thy heart… and I will encircle thee in the arms of my love" (6:20). Then the clincher: "if you desire a further witness, cast your mind upon the night that you cried unto me in your heart, that you might know concerning the truth of these things. Did I not speak peace to your mind concerning the matter? What greater witness can you have than from God?" (6:22-23). "If I have told you things which no man knoweth have you not received a witness?" (6:24). Ends in Christ's own voice — "Behold, I am Jesus Christ, the Son of God… I am the light which shineth in darkness" (6:21) — "Look unto me in every thought; doubt not, fear not" (6:36); "Behold the wounds which pierced my side, and also the prints of the nails in my hands and feet" (6:37).

**Thoughts:**
- *The witness is retrospective recognition, not a new sign (6:22-24).* This is the sharpest move in the section, and it threads straight off §5. Oliver wants to *know*; the Lord doesn't hand him a fresh manifestation — He points backward: *remember the night you prayed in secret, and I gave you peace? No one knew about that but you and me. That was already the witness.* The evidence God offers is the proof that He was listening before Oliver asked anyone. "If I have told you things which no man knoweth, have you not received a witness?" (6:24). This is the period-language/Spirit-distillation thread from another angle: the witness isn't proof forced from outside; it's the *recognition* that the peace you already felt was God. (And it's tender — the Lord stakes His credibility on a private memory, betting Oliver will recognize it.)
- *"Did I not speak peace to your mind?" (6:23) — peace as the form of revelation.* Not fire, not a vision: *peace to the mind*. This pairs with §8's "I will tell you in your mind and in your heart" (next note) and §9's "burn in your bosom." The D&C is building, across these four sections, a whole theology of *how* revelation feels — and the first datapoint is the quietest one: peace where there had been a question.
- *"Even as you desire of me so it shall be unto you" (6:8) — desire again, now load-bearing.* The desire-as-entry-point thread from §4 (Joseph Sr.) returns and intensifies: Oliver's gift, his call, the witness itself, all turn on *desiring*. "Thou art Oliver, and I have spoken unto thee because of thy desires" (6:20). Across §§4-7 the Lord keeps making *desire* the hinge — He works with what a person actually wants. (And §7 will make a man's eternal destiny itself the shape of his desire.)
- *"Seek not for riches but for wisdom… and then shall you be made rich" (6:7).* The order is the doctrine — wisdom first, and riches follow as a *consequence* not a *target*. "He that hath eternal life is rich." The seek-the-kingdom-first logic of Matthew 6 (the footnote points to JST Matt 6:38). It's also a quiet warning to Oliver, who will later leave the Church partly over temporal matters.
- *"I will encircle thee in the arms of my love" (6:20).* The single warmest image in the early sections. The Lord, having just proven He reads Oliver's secret thoughts (6:16 — omniscience that could feel like surveillance), turns that same intimacy into *embrace*. He knows everything about you, and the knowing is love, not exposure. (This is the answer to the fear that being fully known is dangerous — the same fear the temple-reserved figures in the PoGP facsimiles guard against on the other side: here the fully-knowing God's posture toward the disciple is *arms of love*.)

**Notable / flag:** No spin-off. **Walk's contribution:** **the witness as *retrospective recognition* (6:22-24)** — the cleanest statement yet of the walk's recurring epistemology. Oliver wanted proof; the Lord pointed to a private prayer and the peace that answered it: *that was already the witness; you just hadn't named it.* It completes the §5 teaching (proof can't compel faith) with its positive form — faith's evidence is recognizing that the peace you already felt was God. Plus **"I will encircle thee in the arms of my love" (6:20)** — the warmest early image, and the answer to the fear that being fully known (6:16) is exposure: to God it's embrace. For Michael: the section where the Lord proves Himself not by a new sign but by remembering, with the disciple, a night only the two of them knew about.
