# D&C 84 — the oath and covenant, and truth which is light which is Spirit

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, September 22-23, 1832.** Through September the elders had been returning from their eastern missions, reporting their labors "in this season of joy." With Joseph and six elders, "as they united their hearts and lifted their voices on high," this came — which Joseph "designated… a revelation on priesthood." It is one of the densest sections in the canon: the New Jerusalem and temple, the priesthood lineage back to Adam, the two priesthoods, the *oath and covenant*, the light of Christ, the condemnation of the whole church, the law of the missionary, and the body of Christ.

**Binding idea:** "All those who receive the priesthood, receive this **oath and covenant** of my Father, which he cannot break… For he that receiveth my servants receiveth me… and he that receiveth my Father receiveth my Father's kingdom; therefore **all that my Father hath shall be given unto him**" (84:38-40). God *swears* an oath — His part, "which he cannot break" — and the man *covenants* faithfulness; the reward is everything the Father has.

**Read:** "The line of priesthood… the sons of Moses, according to the Holy Priesthood which he received under the hand of his father-in-law, Jethro… [back through] Abraham… Melchizedek… Noah… Enoch… Abel… by the hand of his father Adam, who was the first man" (84:6-16). "Which priesthood continueth in the church of God in all generations, and is without beginning of days or end of years" (84:17). "This greater priesthood… holdeth the key of the mysteries of the kingdom, even the key of the knowledge of God. Therefore, in the ordinances thereof, the power of godliness is manifest. And without the ordinances thereof, and the authority of the priesthood, the power of godliness is not manifest unto men in the flesh; for without this no man can see the face of God, even the Father, and live" (84:19-22). "The lesser priesthood… holdeth the key of the ministering of angels and the preparatory gospel" (84:26). "Whoso is faithful unto the obtaining these two priesthoods… and the magnifying their calling, are sanctified by the Spirit unto the renewing of their bodies" (84:33). "He that receiveth my servants receiveth me; and he that receiveth me receiveth my Father; and he that receiveth my Father receiveth my Father's kingdom; therefore all that my Father hath shall be given unto him" (84:36-38). "This is according to the oath and covenant which belongeth to the priesthood" (84:39). "Whoso breaketh this covenant after he hath received it, and altogether turneth therefrom, shall not have forgiveness of sins in this world nor in the world to come" (84:41). "You shall live by every word that proceedeth forth from the mouth of God" (84:44). "For the word of the Lord is truth, and whatsoever is truth is light, and whatsoever is light is Spirit, even the Spirit of Jesus Christ. And the Spirit giveth light to every man that cometh into the world" (84:45-46). "Your minds in times past have been darkened because of unbelief, and because you have treated lightly the things you have received— which vanity and unbelief have brought the whole church under condemnation" (84:54-55). "From henceforth I shall call you friends" (84:77). "Take ye no thought for the morrow… consider the lilies of the field" (84:81-82). "Whoso receiveth you receiveth me… I will go before your face. I will be on your right hand and on your left, and my Spirit shall be in your hearts, and mine angels round about you, to bear you up" (84:88). "Let every man stand in his own office, and labor in his own calling; and let not the head say unto the feet it hath no need of the feet" (84:109). "The body hath need of every member, that all may be edified together, that the system may be kept perfect" (84:110).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ The priesthood lineage back to Adam — the cross-walk lands (84:6-17).* The revelation traces the Holy Priesthood as an unbroken *line of hands*: Moses received it from Jethro, Jethro from Caleb, back through Esaias [who "lived in the days of Abraham"], to Abraham who "received the priesthood from Melchizedek, who received it through the lineage of his fathers, even till Noah… Enoch… Abel… by the hand of his father Adam, who was the first man" (84:14-16). "Which priesthood… is without beginning of days or end of years" (84:17). (This is the **priesthood-lineage cross-walk landing** the walk has tracked across all three canon-walks: the PoGP planted the line [Moses 6:7 "a genealogy… the record of the fathers"; Abr 1:3 "the right of the firstborn… from the fathers"], and here it is *named link by link* from Adam to Moses, and re-spliced into the Restoration through §13 [the Aaronic by John] and §27/§7 [the Melchizedek by Peter-James-John]. The doctrine: authority is not self-generated or institutionally invented — it is *conferred hand to hand* in an unbroken succession reaching to the first man, and *through* him to God [Esaias "received it under the hand of God," 84:12]. The whole apostasy-and-restoration logic [§13] rests on this: a line can be *broken* on earth and must be *literally re-conferred* from heaven — which is exactly what the Restoration claims. The line "without beginning of days or end of years" is the priesthood's eternity: it predates and outlasts every dispensation. The cross-walk thread spanning BoM/PoGP/D&C reaches its genealogical statement here.)
- *★ The ordinances and the power of godliness — "no man can see the face of God… and live" without it (84:19-22).* The greater priesthood "holdeth the key of the mysteries of the kingdom, even the key of the **knowledge of God**" — and the channel is the *ordinances*: "in the ordinances thereof, the power of godliness is manifest. And without the ordinances… and the authority of the priesthood, the power of godliness is not manifest unto men in the flesh; for without this **no man can see the face of God, even the Father, and live**" (84:20-22). (The §67:11 doctrine [no natural man can abide God's presence except quickened by the Spirit] given its *means*: the priesthood *ordinances* are how "the power of godliness" — the transforming, sanctifying power that fits a man to see God — is "manifest… in the flesh." This is the temple thread [the ordinances → power of godliness → the capacity to behold God]; Moses "sought diligently to sanctify his people that they might behold the face of God" [84:23], but they "hardened their hearts and could not endure his presence" [84:24], so the higher priesthood was taken with Moses [84:25] and only the lesser — "the ministering of angels and the preparatory gospel" [84:26] — remained until John the Baptist. The ordinances are not empty ritual but the *conduit of godliness's power*, the means by which the §76:58 exaltation and the §67 capacity-to-bear-God's-presence are actually grown in a man. I flag the "power of godliness in the ordinances → see the face of God" thread as temple-reserved, Michael's.)
- *★★ The oath and covenant — God's oath, the man's covenant, "all that my Father hath" (84:33-44).* The section's heart, and the priesthood version of §82:10's bilateral covenant. The man's part: "faithful unto the obtaining these two priesthoods… and the *magnifying their calling*" (84:33) — not merely *holding* the priesthood but *magnifying* it. God's part: an *oath* — "this oath and covenant of my Father, **which he cannot break, neither can it be moved**" (84:40). And the reward ascends a chain: receive the priesthood → receive Christ's servants → receive Christ → receive the Father → "receiveth my Father's kingdom; therefore **all that my Father hath shall be given unto him**" (84:37-38). (This is the §82:10 covenant ["I am bound when ye do what I say"] in its priesthood form: God *swears* — binds Himself by an oath He "cannot break" — and the man *covenants* to magnify his calling; the two together are "the oath and covenant which belongeth to the priesthood." The reward is the §76:55-59 exaltation stated at its most staggering: "all that my Father hath" — the heir receives *everything God has* [Rom 8:17 joint-heirs]. And the warning matches §76's perdition: "whoso breaketh this covenant… and *altogether turneth therefrom*" [84:41] — the full, knowing apostasy, not ordinary failure — "shall not have forgiveness." The oath-and-covenant is the most exalted bilateral covenant in the canon: God stakes His own unbreakable oath, the man stakes his faithfulness, and the prize is the Father's all. For the project's covenant pattern [§82:10]: this is the same structure at the highest level — God self-bound by oath, the steward bound to *magnify* [not just hold] the trust, the reward proportioned to everything.)
- *★★ "Truth is light is Spirit" — the light-and-truth chain (84:45-47).* The matter-spectrum / light-and-truth thread's spine, before §93 [truth.md]. "The word of the Lord is **truth**, and whatsoever is truth is **light**, and whatsoever is light is **Spirit**, even the **Spirit of Jesus Christ**. And the Spirit giveth light to *every* man that cometh into the world" (84:45-46). (A chain of *identity*: truth = light = Spirit = the Spirit of Christ — four names for *one reality*. This is the §50:23-24 ["that which is of God is light… brighter… until the perfect day"] given its metaphysics: light is not a metaphor for truth; truth *is* light *is* Spirit, a single substance that is Christ's own. The matter-spectrum's deepest claim — that spirit and truth and light are *one continuum*, not separate categories — stated as an equation. And it is *universal*: "the Spirit giveth light to *every* man that cometh into the world" [the Light of Christ, John 1:9] — every soul born has this light, and "every one that hearkeneth to the voice of the Spirit cometh unto God" [84:47]. The discernment thread [§50/§52 — light edifies, is of God] and the matter-spectrum [§29/§77 — all is spiritual] *fuse* here into one equation: truth-light-Spirit-Christ, given to all, the means by which "you may know the righteous from the wicked" [84:53 — those who come to the light vs. those in "the bondage of sin"]. This is the §93 truth.md thread's foundational verse; I note it as the spine for §88 and §93 ahead.)
- *★ "You have treated lightly the things you have received" — the whole church under condemnation (84:54-57).* A startling rebuke turned on the *Saints*: "your minds… have been darkened because of unbelief, and because you have **treated lightly the things you have received**— which vanity and unbelief have brought the **whole church under condemnation**" (84:54-55), to remain "until they repent and remember the new covenant, even the Book of Mormon and the former commandments… **not only to say, but to do**" (84:57). (The condemnation is not for *rejecting* the revelations but for *treating them lightly* — receiving the Book of Mormon and the commandments and then *not doing* them [James 1:22, the hearer who is not a doer]. This is the teaching covenant and the discernment thread turned inward: the danger is not the world's unbelief but the *Saints' casualness* with what they've been given. "Not only to say, but to do" — the whole condemnation hinges on the gap between *having* the word and *living* it. For the project — which works *with* the sources [the corpus, the covenant, the principles]: "treating lightly the things you have received" is the precise failure to guard against; the source verified-and-quoted but not *lived* is the same casualness. The remedy is not more revelation but *doing* the revelation already given.)
- *Friends not servants, without purse or scrip, and the body of Christ (84:77-110).* The missionary law and the close: "from henceforth I shall call you **friends**" [84:77, John 15:15 — the servants-to-friends elevation]; sent "without purse or scrip," "take ye no thought for the morrow… consider the lilies" [84:78-82, Matt 6]; "treasure up… continually the words of life, and it shall be given you in the very hour" [84:85 — don't pre-script, the Spirit supplies it; §100:6]; and the with-ness at its fullest: "I will go before your face. I will be on your right hand and on your left, and my Spirit shall be in your hearts, and mine angels round about you, to bear you up" [84:88]. Then the *body*: "let every man stand in his own office, and labor in his own calling; and let not the head say unto the feet it hath no need of the feet… the body hath need of every member, that all may be edified together, that the system may be kept perfect" [84:109-110, 1 Cor 12]. (The §46 gifts-as-interdependence given its body-image: each member *needed*, each in *his own office*, "the system kept perfect" by the whole — and the strong taking the weak "that he may become strong also" [84:106, the §81:5 succor / the mentoring pattern]. For the project's council and fan-out: "stand in his own office… the body hath need of every member" is the doctrine of complementary stewardships — no member dispensable, the system perfected by all working their own callings, the strong lifting the weak into strength.)
