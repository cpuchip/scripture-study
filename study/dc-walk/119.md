# D&C 119 — the law of tithing: a standing law forever

**Setting:** **Far West, Missouri, July 8, 1838** — in answer to Joseph's supplication, "O Lord! Show unto thy servants how much thou requirest of the properties of thy people for a tithing." Earlier uses of *tithing* (§64:23; §85:3; §97:11) had meant *all* free-will offerings; the law of consecration (§42/§51) had proven too high for the Saints to keep, and the United Order had broken (§104). Into the financial wreckage — Kirtland Temple debts, the Missouri losses, a national panic — comes the *standing law* that would endure: one-tenth.

**Binding idea:** "Those who have thus been tithed shall pay one-tenth of all their interest annually; and this shall be a **standing law unto them forever**, for my holy priesthood" (119:4). A *durable* law — surplus first, then a tenth annually, forever — and the means by which "the land of Zion" is *sanctified*.

**Read:** "I require all their surplus property to be put into the hands of the bishop of my church in Zion, for the building of mine house, and for the laying of the foundation of Zion and for the priesthood, and for the debts of the Presidency of my Church. And this shall be the beginning of the tithing of my people" (119:1-3). "And after that, those who have thus been tithed shall pay one-tenth of all their interest annually; and this shall be a standing law unto them forever, for my holy priesthood, saith the Lord" (119:4). "All those who gather unto the land of Zion shall be tithed of their surplus properties, and shall observe this law, or they shall not be found worthy to abide among you" (119:5). "If my people observe not this law, to keep it holy, and by this law sanctify the land of Zion… it shall not be a land of Zion unto you" (119:6). "This shall be an ensample unto all the stakes of Zion" (119:7).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ Tithing as the durable form of consecration (119:1-4).* The law of tithing is the *enduring, scaled-down* form of the consecration the Saints could not keep. The full law of consecration [§42/§51 — consecrate *all*, receive a stewardship] had failed in practice [§104, the United Order broken by covetousness, §101 Zion lost]; tithing is the law that *would last* — "a standing law unto them forever." (The sequence is telling: first "all their surplus property" [119:1, a one-time consecration of the surplus, settling the debts and founding Zion], *then* "one-tenth of all their interest annually" [119:4, the permanent law]. Tithing is *not* a retreat from consecration but its *sustainable rhythm*: where consecration asks *everything* at once [a height the Saints couldn't hold], tithing asks *a tenth, regularly, forever* [a discipline they could]. The §78:18 "I will lead you along" / §10:4 pace-to-strength applied to the law of giving: God *paced down* to a law the people could actually keep — a tenth — making the principle *durable* rather than ideal-but-broken. The higher law remains the aspiration [the celestial law, §105:5]; tithing is the *standing* floor that endures. For the project's consecration thread: a principle that *lasts* at a sustainable level beats one that's ideal but unkept — "a standing law forever" is worth more than a higher law abandoned.)
- *★ Tithing sanctifies Zion — without it, no Zion (119:5-6).* The stakes are high: "all those who gather unto the land of Zion shall be tithed… or they shall not be found worthy to abide among you" (119:5), and "if my people observe not this law… **it shall not be a land of Zion unto you**" (119:6). (Tithing is not a *fundraising* mechanism but a *sanctifying* one: it "sanctif[ies] the land of Zion." The §105:5 "Zion cannot be built up unless… by the law of the celestial kingdom" given its *practical entry*: a people who will not keep even *this* — a tenth — cannot build Zion. The §101:6 Zion-fell-from-within [covetousness] answered with a *standing discipline*: tithing is the recurring *practice* of putting God before property [the §117:8 "what is property unto me?"], and a people who won't practice it "shall not" have Zion. For the project's stewardship: the *recurring small discipline* [a tenth, regularly] is what sanctifies — not a grand one-time gesture but a *standing law kept*; the community that won't keep the durable discipline forfeits the consecrated end.)
