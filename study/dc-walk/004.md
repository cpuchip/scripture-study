# D&C 4 — desire is the call

**Setting:** Harmony, Pennsylvania, February 1829 — given through Joseph to his **father, Joseph Smith Sr.**, who wanted to know his part in the coming work. Seven verses; memorized by generations of missionaries since.

**Binding idea:** You are not called to God's work by a formal summons but by *desire*: "if ye have desires to serve God ye are called to the work" (4:3). And what qualifies you is *character*, not credentials (4:5-6).

**Read:** "A marvelous work is about to come forth among the children of men" (4:1, Isa 29:14). "Therefore, O ye that embark in the service of God, see that ye serve him with all your heart, might, mind and strength" (4:2). "If ye have desires to serve God ye are called to the work; for behold the field is white already to harvest" (4:3-4). "And faith, hope, charity and love, with an eye single to the glory of God, qualify him for the work" (4:5) — "remember faith, virtue, knowledge, temperance, patience, brotherly kindness, godliness, charity, humility, diligence" (4:6). "Ask, and ye shall receive; knock, and it shall be opened unto you" (4:7).

**Thoughts:**
- *"Desires to serve" = "called" (4:3) — the call is open by wanting.* No ecclesiastical summons, no special vision required: the *desire* itself is the call. This makes the work radically accessible — anyone who wants to serve is, by that wanting, already commissioned. And it threads back across the walks: **Abraham's covenant began with desire** ("I sought… desiring," Abr 1:2), and the Articles of Faith closed on seeking ("we seek after these things," A of F 13). Desire is the recurring entry point — the gospel meets the heart that *wants* before it issues a formal call. (A father asks his place in the work; the answer is, *do you desire it? then you're called.*)
- *Character qualifies, not credentials (4:5-6).* The list is the 2 Peter 1:5-7 attribute-ladder, expanded — faith, virtue, knowledge, temperance, patience, brotherly kindness, godliness, charity, humility, diligence. None of them are skills or offices; all are *attributes of soul*. What fits a person for God's work is who they are becoming, not what they know how to do. And the hinge attribute is motive: "an eye single to the glory of God" (4:5) — undivided purpose. (Forward-link: D&C 88:67, "if your eye be single to my glory, your whole bodies shall be filled with light" — the single eye → light, the matter-spectrum's measure of capacity. Pure motive is what lets the light in.)
- *Whole-souled service (4:2) is the great commandment applied.* "Heart, might, mind and strength" is Deut 6:5 / Mark 12:30 (love God with all…) turned toward *service*. The standard isn't competent service; it's *whole* service. And it "saves… his soul" (4:4) — the one who serves with his might is the first beneficiary.
- *"Ask… knock" (4:7)* — the section ends on the asking-promise (Matt 7:7), the same experimental epistemology the walk keeps meeting (James 1:5; D&C 9:8 ahead; Moroni 10:4). The work begins with desire and is sustained by asking.
