# D&C 113 — the Stem, the rod, and the root of Jesse

**Setting:** **At or near Far West, Missouri, March 1838** — "answers to certain questions on the writings of Isaiah," some asked by Elias Higbee. The Saints, driven from Kirtland and gathering in Missouri, were reading Isaiah 11 and 52; Joseph gives the Lord's interpretation of the Stem, the rod, and the root of Jesse, and of Zion "putting on her strength."

**Binding idea:** "Who is the Stem of Jesse…? Verily thus saith the Lord: It is Christ" (113:1-2). Isaiah's messianic figures are decoded: the Stem is Christ; the rod and the root are *latter-day servants* on whom "is laid much power," holding "the priesthood, and the keys of the kingdom… for the gathering of my people in the last days" (113:4,6).

**Read:** "Who is the Stem of Jesse…? Verily thus saith the Lord: It is Christ" (113:1-2). "What is the rod… that should come of the Stem of Jesse? It is a servant in the hands of Christ, who is partly a descendant of Jesse as well as of Ephraim, or of the house of Joseph, on whom there is laid much power" (113:3-4). "What is the root of Jesse…? It is a descendant of Jesse, as well as of Joseph, unto whom rightly belongs the priesthood, and the keys of the kingdom, for an ensign, and for the gathering of my people in the last days" (113:5-6). "To put on her strength is to put on the authority of the priesthood, which she, Zion, has a right to by lineage; also to return to that power which she had lost" (113:8). "The scattered remnants are exhorted to return to the Lord from whence they have fallen; which if they do, the promise of the Lord is that he will speak to them, or give them revelation" (113:10).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ The Stem is Christ; the rod and root are latter-day servants (113:1-6).* The Lord reads Isaiah 11 as *Restoration* prophecy. "The Stem of Jesse… is Christ" (113:2) — the trunk from which all grows. "The rod… is a servant in the hands of Christ… partly a descendant of Jesse as well as of Ephraim… on whom there is laid much power" (113:4), and "the root of Jesse… a descendant of Jesse, as well as of Joseph, unto whom rightly belongs the priesthood, and the keys of the kingdom, for an ensign, and for the gathering" (113:6). (Isaiah's messianic imagery [Isa 11:1,10] given a *dual* reading: the Stem is *Christ Himself*, but the *rod* and *root* are *servants in His hands* — latter-day instruments [of the lineage of both Jesse and Joseph] bearing "much power" and "the keys… for the gathering." The §110:11 gathering keys [Moses] and the §86:8 "lawful heirs" thread surfacing in Isaiah's prophecy: the gathering is accomplished by a *servant* who holds the keys, an *ensign* to the nations. The walk does not pin the servant's identity [the text leaves it to the lineage-and-keys description]; what it teaches is that *Christ is the Stem* and the latter-day work is done through *servants in His hands*. The Restoration was *prophesied in Isaiah*, decoded here.)
- *★ Zion's strength = the priesthood, "which she has a right to by lineage" (113:7-10).* The second Q&A: "put on thy strength, O Zion" [Isa 52:1] means "to put on the authority of the priesthood, which she, Zion, **has a right to by lineage**; also to return to that power which she had lost" (113:8). (Zion's "strength" is the *priesthood* — and her "right to it by lineage" is the §86:8 "lawful heirs" / Abrahamic-lineage thread [bin-4-adjacent, Michael's]. "Return to that power which she had lost" — the §1/§13 apostasy-and-restoration: the priesthood *lost* in the apostasy, *restored* in the latter day, and Zion *puts it back on*. And the scattered remnants are "exhorted to return to the Lord… which if they do… he will speak to them, or give them revelation" [113:10] — the return is rewarded with *renewed revelation* [the §1:30/§121:26 promise]. The "bands of her neck" loosed [113:10] are "the curses of God upon her… in their scattered condition" — the gathering is a *return* from the curse to the covenant. I note the priesthood-by-lineage as part of the reserved bin-4 thread, Michael's.)
