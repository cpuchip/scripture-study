# D&C 98 — renounce war and proclaim peace

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, August 6, 1833** — in consequence of the Missouri persecution. On July 20, a mob had destroyed Church property, tarred and feathered two members, and demanded the Saints leave Jackson County; three days later the leaders signed an agreement to go. The news could not yet have reached Kirtland (900 miles away) by any natural means — "the seriousness of the situation could have been known to him at this date only by revelation." Into the persecution the Lord gives the *law of how His people meet affliction*: patience, the constitution, renounced war, and forgiveness.

**Binding idea:** "Renounce war and proclaim peace" (98:16) — and the graduated law beneath it: bear the smiting patiently, forgive the trespasser again and again, lift "a standard of peace" before ever returning blow for blow; force is justified *only* by the Lord's command, after peace has been offered and refused. The persecuted are not to retaliate but to *forgive and proclaim peace*.

**Read:** "Fear not, let your hearts be comforted; yea, rejoice evermore, and in everything give thanks; waiting patiently on the Lord" (98:1-2). "All things wherewith you have been afflicted shall work together for your good, and to my name's glory" (98:3). "That law of the land which is constitutional, supporting that principle of freedom in maintaining rights and privileges, belongs to all mankind, and is justifiable before me… I, the Lord, justify you… in befriending that law which is the constitutional law of the land; and as pertaining to law of man, whatsoever is more or less than this, cometh of evil" (98:5-7). "When the wicked rule the people mourn. Wherefore, honest men and wise men should be sought for diligently, and good men and wise men ye should observe to uphold" (98:9-10). "He will give unto the faithful line upon line, precept upon precept; and I will try you and prove you herewith" (98:12). "Whoso layeth down his life in my cause… shall find it again, even life eternal" (98:13). "Renounce war and proclaim peace, and seek diligently to turn the hearts of the children to their fathers" (98:16). "If men will smite you… once, and ye bear it patiently and revile not against them, neither seek revenge, ye shall be rewarded" (98:23). "As oft as thine enemy repenteth of the trespass… thou shalt forgive him, until seventy times seven" (98:40). "If he trespass against thee and repent not the first time, nevertheless thou shalt forgive him" (98:41); "the second time, and repent not, nevertheless thou shalt forgive him" (98:42); "the third time, and repent not, thou shalt also forgive him" (98:43). "They should not go out unto battle against any nation… save I, the Lord, commanded them. And if any nation… should proclaim war against them, they should first lift a standard of peace" (98:33-34).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "All things… shall work together for your good" (98:3).* The first word to a persecuted people: not vengeance but *trust*. "Rejoice evermore, and in everything give thanks; waiting patiently on the Lord… all things wherewith you have been afflicted shall work together for your good" (98:1-3, Rom 8:28). (The §59:7 gratitude-in-all-things and the §58:3 "after much tribulation come the blessings" given to people *in the fire*: the affliction itself is promised to "work together for your good" — not removed, but *turned to good*. This reaches forward to §122:7 [Liberty Jail — "all these things shall give thee experience, and shall be for thy good"]: the suffering is not meaningless or merely endured but *redemptive*, woven into good by a God who has "sworn and decreed" it [98:2]. For the persecuted — and for anyone in affliction: the first command is *trust and thanks*, because the affliction is on its way to *good*, by covenant.)
- *★ The constitutional law — befriend it, uphold good leaders (98:5-10).* A foundational civic doctrine: "that law of the land which is constitutional, supporting that principle of freedom in maintaining rights and privileges… is justifiable before me" (98:5), and "whatsoever is more or less than this, cometh of evil" (98:7). (The law that *secures freedom* is divinely justified — and the Saints are to *befriend* it [98:6], even as it was *failing* to protect them in Missouri. The §134 / §101:80 inspired-constitution thread: the principle of freedom is God's, and a just law is the floor — "more or less than this cometh of evil" [the §93:24-25 "more or less" formula applied to *law*: the constitutional floor admits no tyranny-above and no anarchy-below]. And the civic *duty*: "when the wicked rule the people mourn… honest men and wise men should be sought for diligently, and good men and wise men ye should observe to uphold" [98:9-10] — the faithful are to *seek out and uphold* honest, wise, good leaders. For the project's presiding-and-governance thread: God's people are commanded to *befriend lawful order* and to *uphold the good and wise* — civic engagement is a commandment, and the just law that secures freedom is sacred.)
- *★★ The law of war and forgiveness — "renounce war and proclaim peace" (98:16,23-48).* The heart of the section, and the presiding covenant's force-doctrine at the scale of *conflict*. The graduated law: when smitten, "bear it patiently… revile not… neither seek revenge" — once, "ye shall be rewarded"; a second time, "an hundred-fold"; a third, "doubled… four-fold" [98:23-26]. Forgive the *repentant* enemy "until seventy times seven" [98:40, Matt 18:22]; and forgive even the *unrepentant* the first, second, and third time [98:41-43]. War is justified *only* by the Lord's command, and *only after* "a standard of peace" has been lifted three times and refused [98:33-36]. (This is §63:29 [Zion by purchase, not blood] and §121:41 [persuasion, not compulsion] given their *fullest law*: the people of God *renounce war*, bear affliction patiently, forgive past counting, and lift the standard of peace *again and again* before force is ever contemplated — and even then, *only by God's explicit command*, never of their own initiative. The presiding covenant's "force where persuasion is available = breach" at the scale of nations: the standard of peace must be offered *three times*; vengeance belongs to God [98:45, "I will avenge"]; the steward's part is patience, forgiveness, and peace. There is a *narrow* warrant for self-defense ["if he has sought thy life… thine enemy is in thine hands and thou art justified," 98:31] — but it is bounded, last-resort, and the *whole weight* of the law is toward *renouncing* war and *proclaiming* peace. For the project's presiding covenant [emergency force must be accounted; persuasion before compulsion]: §98 is the scriptural template — exhaust peace, forgive abundantly, and use force only under explicit warrant, never freelance.)
- *Line upon line, martyrdom, and Kirtland reproved (98:12-22).* Three threads woven in: "he will give unto the faithful **line upon line, precept upon precept**… and I will try you and prove you" [98:12, Isa 28:10 — the pacing of revelation, §78:18, paired with the *proving* of affliction]; "whoso layeth down his life in my cause… shall find it again, even life eternal" [98:13, Luke 9:24 — the martyr's promise, for a people under threat of death]; and the Lord is "not well pleased with many… in the church at Kirtland; for they do not forsake their sins… the pride of their hearts, and their covetousness" [98:19-20]. (The honesty again [§90/§93/§95]: in the same revelation comforting the *persecuted* Missouri Saints, the Lord *reproves* the *Kirtland* Saints — affliction from without does not excuse sin within; *both* communities are called to repent. The persecution is not a license to ignore one's own faults [the §56 reciprocal-wo logic]. And "line upon line… I will try you" couples the *gentle* unfolding of truth with the *severe* proving of trial — both are how the faithful grow.)
