# D&C 120 — disposed of by a council

**Setting:** **Far West, Missouri, July 8, 1838** — the same day as the tithing law (§119), "making known the disposition of the properties tithed." One verse: *who decides* how the tithes are used.

**Binding idea:** "It shall be disposed of by a council, composed of the First Presidency of my Church, and of the bishop and his council, and by my high council; and **by mine own voice unto them**" (120:1). The sacred funds are spent not by one man but by a *council* — and by the *Lord's own voice* within it.

**Read:** "Verily, thus saith the Lord, the time is now come, that it shall be disposed of by a council, composed of the First Presidency of my Church, and of the bishop and his council, and by my high council; and by mine own voice unto them, saith the Lord. Even so. Amen" (120:1).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ The sacred funds disposed by council — and by the Lord's voice (120:1).* The single verse names a *check* on the most temptable thing in any institution: *the money*. The tithes are not at any one person's disposal — not even the prophet's — but disposed "by a **council**, composed of the First Presidency… the bishop and his council… [and] my high council" — *three* bodies, deciding together — "and by **mine own voice** unto them." (Two safeguards on the treasury [the §104:62-77 sealed-treasury / common-consent given its *governance*]. First, *plural authority*: no individual controls the sacred funds; a *council of councils* disposes them — diffusing the temptation that destroyed the United Order [§104:52, covetousness]. The §107:27 unanimity / §26:2 common-consent applied to *money*: the most corruptible resource is governed by the *least concentrated* authority. Second, "**by mine own voice unto them**" — the council does not merely *vote*; it seeks the *Lord's voice*. The disposition of sacred funds is a *spiritual* act [§102:23 the council inquiring of the Lord], not a budget meeting. For the project's stewardship and council pattern: the resource most likely to corrupt [the money, the power] should be governed by the *broadest* council and *under the Lord's voice* — never one hand, never mere procedure. The institution that vests its treasury in a *council seeking the Lord's voice* guards against the covetousness that fells from within [§101:6]. This is the modern Council on the Disposition of the Tithes — still the pattern.)
