# D&C 29 — all things are spiritual

**Setting:** **Fayette, New York, September 1830**, before six elders, days before the conference of September 26. A sweeping revelation: it runs from the gathering of the elect through the Second Coming, the Millennium, the resurrection and final judgment, and then *backward* to the Creation, the pre-mortal rebellion, the Fall, and the redemption of little children. One of the D&C's great doctrinal panoramas — the whole arc from the war in heaven to the new heaven and new earth.

**Binding idea:** "All things unto me are spiritual, and not at any time have I given unto you a law which was temporal" (29:34). From God's vantage the spiritual/temporal divide collapses — He created "all things both spiritual and temporal… by the power of my Spirit" (29:31), and His commandments are spiritual through and through. And the engine of the whole drama is *agency*: the devil rebelled over it, mortality requires opposition to exercise it, and "if they never should have bitter they could not know the sweet" (29:39).

**Read:** Christ "will gather his people even as a hen gathereth her chickens under her wings, even as many as will hearken" (29:2); "ye are called to bring to pass the gathering of mine elect; for mine elect hear my voice and harden not their hearts" (29:7). The Second Coming: "I will reveal myself from heaven with power and great glory… and dwell in righteousness with men on earth a thousand years" (29:11); the signs (29:14-21); the resurrection — "Michael, mine archangel, shall sound his trump, and then shall all the dead awake… yea, even all" (29:26); "the righteous shall be gathered on my right hand unto eternal life" (29:27). Then the turn inward: "all things shall become new… and not one hair, neither mote, shall be lost, for it is the workmanship of mine hand" (29:24-25). "By the power of my Spirit created I them; yea, all things both spiritual and temporal—first spiritual, secondly temporal, which is the beginning of my work; and again, first temporal, and secondly spiritual, which is the last of my work" (29:31-32). "All things unto me are spiritual, and not at any time have I given unto you a law which was temporal" (29:34); "I gave unto him that he should be an agent unto himself… my commandments are spiritual" (29:35). "The devil was before Adam, for he rebelled against me, saying, Give me thine honor, which is my power; and also a third part of the hosts of heaven turned he away from me because of their agency" (29:36-37). "It must needs be that the devil should tempt the children of men, or they could not be agents unto themselves; for if they never should have bitter they could not know the sweet" (29:39). Adam transgressed, "became spiritually dead, which is the first death, even that same death which is the last death" (29:41); the days of probation appointed (29:43). "Little children are redeemed from the foundation of the world through mine Only Begotten; wherefore, they cannot sin, for power is not given unto Satan to tempt little children, until they begin to become accountable" (29:46-47).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "All things unto me are spiritual" (29:34-35) — the matter-spectrum's foundation laid early.* This is the D&C's first full statement of what `truth.md` and the matter-spectrum studies build on: the spiritual/temporal divide is *ours, not God's*. "Not at any time have I given unto you a law which was temporal… my commandments are spiritual; they are not natural nor temporal, neither carnal nor sensual" (29:35). From God's side there is no separate "physical" realm to govern by separate "physical" rules — everything He made, "both spiritual and temporal," He made "by the power of my Spirit" (29:31), and every law He gives is spiritual. (This is D&C 93/131's matter-is-spirit-refined seen from the law side: if all matter is spirit at a finer scale [131:7-8], then all "temporal" commands are spiritual commands about spirit-stuff. §29 plants the seed twelve sections before 93 waters it. The Word of Wisdom, tithing, the law of consecration — all the "temporal" laws — are *spiritual* from God's vantage, which is why obeying them with the body changes the spirit.)
- *★ The order of creation: spiritual first, then temporal — and redemption runs the other way (29:31-32).* "First spiritual, secondly temporal, which is the beginning of my work; and again, first temporal, and secondly spiritual, which is the last of my work." A precise structure: **creation** moves spiritual→temporal (everything was created spiritually before it was created physically, Moses 3:5); **redemption** moves temporal→spiritual (the work *ends* by making the temporal spiritual again — resurrection, the new earth, all things made new). The whole plan is a *chiasm*: spirit out into matter, matter back into spirit, with mortality at the turn. (This is the matter-spectrum's deep structure as *narrative* — not just "matter is refined spirit" but "the story of everything is spirit descending into matter and returning, glorified." The §27 "gather together in one all things" is the temporal→spiritual return completing.)
- *★ Agency requires opposition — 2 Nephi 2 in the Lord's own voice (29:39).* "It must needs be that the devil should tempt the children of men, or they could not be agents unto themselves; for if they never should have bitter they could not know the sweet." Lehi's great teaching (2 Ne 2:11, "opposition in all things") is here stated by *Christ Himself*: the tempter is *necessary* to agency. Without a real pull toward the bitter, the choice of the sweet would be empty — you cannot be "an agent unto thyself" in a world with only one option. So the devil's presence, while evil, serves the plan's *structure*: he supplies the opposition without which agency is impossible. (This is sobering and clarifying — God didn't fail to prevent the tempter; the tempter's role is *built in*. And it bounds the problem of evil: evil is not God's intent but the cost of the only kind of world where free agents can become anything. The §19 "exquisite" suffering and §29's necessary bitter are the same economy — the cost of a world that can produce gods.)
- *★ The pre-mortal rebellion was over agency (29:36-37).* "The devil was before Adam, for he rebelled… saying, Give me thine honor, which is my power; and also a third part of the hosts of heaven turned he away from me because of their agency." The war in heaven (Moses 4:1-4; Rev 12:4; Abr 3) is named here as a conflict about *agency and honor*: the devil sought God's honor/power, and the third part followed "because of their agency" — i.e., they exercised the very agency the plan was meant to preserve, against the plan. The deep irony: the rebellion *against* the agency-plan was itself an *act of agency*. Agency is so fundamental that even rejecting it is done by it. (And the devil's role in mortality [29:39] is the continuation of his pre-mortal stance: he who rebelled over agency now supplies the opposition that agency needs — the adversary conscripted, against his will, into the plan he opposed.)
- *★ The first death and the last death are both spiritual (29:41).* Adam "became spiritually dead, which is the first death, even that same death which is the last death, which is spiritual, which shall be pronounced upon the wicked when I shall say: Depart, ye cursed." A precise and sobering identity: the **first death** (the Fall = separation from God's presence, 29:41) and the **second/last death** (final judgment = "Depart from me," 29:28) are the *same kind* of death — *spiritual*, being cast out of God's presence. The Fall is a *foretaste* of hell; hell is the Fall made permanent for those who won't be redeemed. (And mortality is the space between: born into the first spiritual death [separated], with a probation to be redeemed before the last one is pronounced. The whole mortal drama is the window to escape having the first death become the last.)
- *★ Little children cannot sin; Satan has no power to tempt them until accountability (29:46-47).* "Little children are redeemed from the foundation of the world… they cannot sin, for power is not given unto Satan to tempt little children, until they begin to become accountable." The Restoration's complete reversal of original-sin/infant-damnation doctrine (the terror behind §22's "dead works" baptisms — infants baptized to save them from hell). Children are *redeemed already*, not by their innocence but "through mine Only Begotten" — the Atonement covers them before they can choose. And Satan is *barred* from them until accountability (eight, D&C 68:25). (This is the §19 mercy applied to children: the dread of infant damnation, like the dread of infinite hell, is dissolved — not by softening doctrine but by revealing the Atonement reaches the child "from the foundation of the world." Moroni 8, the BoM walk's fierce chapter against infant baptism, is the same doctrine.)
- *"Not one hair, neither mote, shall be lost" (29:25).* In the renewal of all things, *nothing of the creation is lost* — "for it is the workmanship of mine hand." Every atom ("mote") is conserved in the resurrection of the earth itself. (The §10 wise-purpose "nothing lost" at cosmic scale, and a matter-spectrum note: matter is not destroyed but *renewed/glorified* — "all things shall become new," not annihilated. The temporal is not discarded in the temporal→spiritual return; it is *transfigured*.)
