# D&C 108 — strengthen your brethren in all your doings

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, December 26, 1835** — to **Lyman Sherman**, one of the seven presidents of the Seventy, who "had come to the Prophet with a request for a revelation to make known his duty." He came seeking his *duty*; the answer first gives him *rest*.

**Binding idea:** "Let your soul be at rest concerning your spiritual standing, and resist no more my voice… strengthen your brethren in all your conversation, in all your prayers, in all your exhortations, and in all your doings" (108:2,7). The assurance of standing first — then the duty: *strengthen the brethren*, in everything.

**Read:** "Your sins are forgiven you, because you have obeyed my voice in coming up hither this morning to receive counsel of him whom I have appointed" (108:1). "Therefore, let your soul be at rest concerning your spiritual standing, and resist no more my voice" (108:2). "Be more careful henceforth in observing your vows" (108:3). "Wait patiently until the solemn assembly… then you shall be remembered with the first of mine elders" (108:4). "Strengthen your brethren in all your conversation, in all your prayers, in all your exhortations, and in all your doings" (108:7). "I am with you to bless you and deliver you forever" (108:8).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "Let your soul be at rest concerning your spiritual standing" (108:1-2).* Lyman came *anxious* — about his duty, and beneath it, about his *standing*. The Lord's first word is *rest*: "your sins are forgiven you… let your soul be at rest concerning your spiritual standing, and resist no more my voice." (The assurance the §6:23 peace-to-the-mind gives a worried disciple: before any *task*, the *rest* — "be at rest concerning your spiritual standing." And the cause of the forgiveness is gentle: Lyman "obeyed my voice in coming up hither… to receive counsel" [108:1] — the very act of *coming humbly to ask* was the obedience that brought the peace. "Resist no more my voice" — the rest comes when the resisting stops. For anyone anxious about where they stand: the Lord's word is often *rest* before *duty* — you cannot serve well from a soul gnawing at its own standing; settle the standing, then strengthen others.)
- *★ "Strengthen your brethren in all your doings" (108:7).* The duty Lyman sought, given in its widest form: "strengthen your brethren in all your conversation, in all your prayers, in all your exhortations, and in **all your doings**." (Luke 22:32 — Christ to Peter, "when thou art converted, strengthen thy brethren." The duty is not a *task* among tasks but a *posture across everything*: every conversation, every prayer, every exhortation, every *doing* is to *strengthen the brethren*. The §81:5 "succor the weak" / §84:106 the strong takes the weak generalized — the steward's whole life is to be *strengthening*, not just in his official acts but "in all [his] doings." For the project's stewardship: the duty isn't a discrete deliverable; it's a *manner of being* — strengthen, in everything you do. And note the order: *first* the rest [108:2], *then* the strengthening [108:7] — the one whose own soul is at rest is the one who can strengthen others.)
