# D&C 12 — the qualifications of a helper

**Setting:** Harmony, Pennsylvania, **May 1829** — to **Joseph Knight Sr.**, a Colesville farmer who had repeatedly given Joseph and Oliver material help (food, provisions, money) so the translation could continue. Knight believed; he wanted to know his part. Nine verses; the first six are the standard harvest preface (= §11). The distinct content is 12:7-9 — what it takes to *assist*.

**Binding idea:** "No one can assist in this work except he shall be humble and full of love, having faith, hope, and charity, being temperate in all things, whatsoever shall be entrusted to his care" (12:8). The qualification to *help* is not skill or means — it is *character*. The man who literally fed the Restoration is told that what makes his help count is who he is, not what he gave.

**Read:** Harvest preface (12:1-6). "I speak unto you, and also to all those who have desires to bring forth and establish this work" (12:7); the qualification (12:8); "Behold, I am the light and the life of the world, that speak these words, therefore give heed with your might, and then you are called" (12:9).

**Thoughts:**
- *Character qualifies the helper, exactly as it qualified the laborer (12:8 ↔ 4:5-6).* §4 told Joseph Sr. that "faith, hope, charity and love, with an eye single to the glory of God, qualify him for the work." §12 says the *same attributes* qualify the one who merely *assists* — humble, full of love, faith/hope/charity, temperate. There is no lower bar for the supporter. Joseph Knight's wagonloads of provisions were real and necessary, but the Lord locates the worth of his help in his *soul*, not his storehouse. The man behind the scenes is held to the front-line standard. (And it dignifies the unseen labor — the one who funds and feeds the work is "called" just as surely as the one who translates, 12:9.)
- *"Whatsoever shall be entrusted to his care" (12:8) — the steward's clause.* The qualifications culminate in *trustworthiness with what's entrusted.* This is the stewardship pattern in seed: to assist is to be *given something to keep*, and the keeping requires the character. (It rhymes with the project's own `exercise_stewardship` — faithfulness "in that which is least," Luke 16:10. Joseph Knight was faithful in the least — provisions — and that fidelity is what the Lord names.)
- *The shortest path from desire to call (12:7,9).* "All those who have desires to bring forth… this work" (12:7) → "give heed with your might, and then you are called" (12:9). The desire-is-the-call thread (§4, §6, §7) compressed to two verses: desire + heed-with-might = called. No office, no ordination required to be a true helper — just the wanting and the whole-hearted attention.
