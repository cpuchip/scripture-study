# D&C 131 — ★★ there is no such thing as immaterial matter

**Setting:** **Ramus, Illinois, May 16–17, 1843** — more "instructions," given over two days, on the same teaching visit as §130. Eight verses, three distinct doctrines: the degrees within celestial glory (and that the highest requires the new and everlasting covenant of marriage), the "more sure word of prophecy" (being sealed up to eternal life), and the verse that states the matter-spectrum's whole metaphysics outright — *all spirit is matter*.

**Binding idea:** **"There is no such thing as immaterial matter. All spirit is matter, but it is more fine or pure, and can only be discerned by purer eyes" (131:7).** The matter-spectrum the walk has tracked from §29:34 reaches its plainest statement: spirit and matter are not two categories but *one continuum* — spirit is *refined* matter. And alongside it, the knowledge-is-salvific claim that grounds the whole study discipline: "It is impossible for a man to be saved in ignorance" (131:6).

**Read:** "In the celestial glory there are three heavens or degrees; And in order to obtain the highest, a man must enter into this order of the priesthood [meaning the new and everlasting covenant of marriage]; And if he does not, he cannot obtain it. He may enter into the other, but that is the end of his kingdom; he cannot have an increase" (131:1-4). "The more sure word of prophecy means a man's knowing that he is sealed up unto eternal life, by revelation and the spirit of prophecy, through the power of the Holy Priesthood" (131:5). "It is impossible for a man to be saved in ignorance" (131:6). "There is no such thing as immaterial matter. All spirit is matter, but it is more fine or pure, and can only be discerned by purer eyes; We cannot see it; but when our bodies are purified we shall see that it is all matter" (131:7-8).

**Thoughts:**

- *★★ Spirit is matter — the matter-spectrum's metaphysical floor (131:7-8).* This is the verse the whole matter-spectrum thread has been building toward, stated as flatly as the canon ever states a metaphysics: "**There is no such thing as immaterial matter. All spirit is matter**, but it is more fine or pure." (The thread's arc: §29:34 "all things unto me are spiritual" [the divide is ours] → §77:2 spirit-and-temporal *mirror* each other → §93:33 "spirit and element, inseparably connected" → §130:22 the *embodied God* → §131:7 the *flat statement* — spirit is not the *opposite* of matter but its *refinement*, "more fine or pure," invisible only because our eyes are too coarse ["can only be discerned by purer eyes… when our bodies are purified we shall see that it is all matter," 131:8]. This dissolves the Platonic/classical spirit-vs-matter dualism entirely: there is *one* substance, graded from coarse to fine, and "spiritual" names the fine end, not a different stuff. For the walk's matter-spectrum thread, this is the *keystone of the metaphysics* — it is why §129's handshake test works [the devil's spirit-hand "you will not feel anything" because spirit-matter is too fine for unpurified flesh], why the resurrection is a *refinement* not an escape from matter [§88:25-26], why the earth can be *transfigured* rather than abandoned [§77:1/§130:9], why God is *embodied* [§130:22]. The universe is matter all the way down *and* all the way up — the most anti-dualist verse in scripture. For the project, working at the seam of mind and matter: there is no ghost-in-the-machine dualism in this cosmology; "intelligence" [§93:36/§130:18] is not immaterial spirit floating free but *refined matter* that rises with us — what you become is *substantial*, not abstract.)

- *★ Impossible to be saved in ignorance — knowledge is salvific (131:6).* "It is impossible for a man to be saved in ignorance." (The §130:18-19 "intelligence rises with us" given its *sharp* corollary: if exaltation *is* the growth of intelligence/light/truth [§93:36], then ignorance is not neutral — it is *incompatible with salvation*, because salvation just *is* the knowing [Mal 2:7 "the priest's lips should keep knowledge"; §107:99 learn-your-duty; §90:15 study-and-learn]. Not that facts save, but that one cannot be *exalted* while remaining ignorant of God and His order — the becoming requires *knowing*. For the project's whole premise — deep study as a spiritual discipline, not an intellectual hobby: 131:6 is the warrant. The study *matters salvifically*; "impossible to be saved in ignorance" means the patient learning is not optional enrichment but the very shape of the salvation [pairs with the §130:18-19 epigraph — what rises with you is the intelligence you attained, so attaining it is the work]. The §58:29 willfully-ignorant-not-counted-worthy at its most absolute.)

- *Celestial marriage + the more sure word (131:1-5) — temple-reserved.* Two reserved doctrines frame the section. The degrees *within* the celestial glory (131:1-4 — the highest requires "the new and everlasting covenant of marriage," else "he cannot have an increase") and the "more sure word of prophecy" (131:5 — being "sealed up unto eternal life… through the power of the Holy Priesthood," 2 Pet 1:19, the calling-and-election thread). (Both are sealing/temple doctrine — reserved-bin-4, Michael's; §132 expands the marriage doctrine. I flag the frames; the walk's load-bearing teaching here is 131:6-8 — knowledge-is-salvific and spirit-is-matter. The one structural note worth keeping: even the celestial kingdom is *graded* [the §76 degrees-of-glory recursing *inward* — glory within glory], so the becoming never hits a flat ceiling; "increase" is the celestial economy.)
