# D&C 44 — assemble, organize, care for the poor

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, late February 1831.** Six verses, brief and practical: the Lord commands the elders to be gathered "from the east and from the west, and from the north and from the south" for a conference (held that June). In compliance, the Church appointed the June 1831 conference — at which the high priesthood was first conferred. A short administrative revelation with three quiet but load-bearing instructions: *assemble*, *organize under the law of the land*, *care for the poor*.

**Binding idea:** Three things hold the young Church together: the Spirit poured out when the elders *assemble* (44:2), the legal *organization* that protects them from their enemies (44:4-5), and the *care of the poor* without which "all things" cannot be done "according to my law" (44:6). Gathering, lawful structure, and the poor — the practical scaffolding of the §42 law.

**Read:** "It is expedient… that the elders of my church should be called together, from the east and from the west… by letter or some other way" (44:1). "Inasmuch as they are faithful, and exercise faith in me, I will pour out my Spirit upon them in the day that they assemble" (44:2). They go forth and preach (44:3); "many shall be converted, insomuch that ye shall obtain power to organize yourselves according to the laws of man; that your enemies may not have power over you… that every bond may be broken wherewith the enemy seeketh to destroy my people" (44:4-5). "Ye must visit the poor and the needy and administer to their relief, that they may be kept until all things may be done according to my law which ye have received" (44:6).

**Thoughts:**
- *The Spirit is poured out *in the assembling* (44:2).* "I will pour out my Spirit upon them **in the day that they assemble**." There's a blessing specific to *gathering together* — the Spirit comes upon the elders *as they convene*. (The council/assembly pattern again: §43:8 "instruct and edify each other," §41:2 "assemble… to agree" — the Restoration keeps locating revelation and the Spirit in the *gathered* body, not only the solitary seeker. Where two or three gather [§6:32], He is in the midst; the assembling is itself a means of grace.)
- *★ Organize "according to the laws of man" — for protection (44:4-5).* A striking, practical instruction: the converts' growth will give the Church "power to organize yourselves **according to the laws of man**; that your enemies may not have power over you." The Church is to *legally incorporate* — to use the civil law as a *shield* against persecution. (The §38:21-22 theocracy [God as king] does *not* mean lawlessness or withdrawal from civil society; the Saints are to *use* the laws of the land for protection — render-unto-Caesar [the §44:4 footnote → 1 Pet 2:13], the §134 honoring-of-government thread. Theocratic in ultimate allegiance, lawful in civil practice. The garrison uses lawful walls: civil incorporation is one of the "walls around the field" the presiding covenant calls lawful protection.)
- *The poor are the condition of the law's fulfillment (44:6).* "Ye must visit the poor and the needy and administer to their relief, **that** they may be kept **until all things may be done according to my law**." Care for the poor isn't an item on the list; it's the *condition* without which the §42 law cannot be completed. The poor must be *kept* (sustained) for the law of consecration to work — you can't build a "be one" Zion (§38:27) while the needy go unrelieved. (The §42:38 "inasmuch as ye do it unto the least of these" as a *structural prerequisite*: the law's machinery [consecration, the storehouse] exists *for* the poor, so neglecting them isn't a failure within the law — it's the collapse of the law's whole purpose. James 1:27, the footnote: pure religion is to "visit the fatherless and widows.")
