# D&C 109 — the dedicatory prayer: a house of prayer, a house of order, a house of God

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, March 27, 1836** — the **dedication of the Kirtland Temple**, the first temple of this dispensation, built "through great tribulation" out of the Saints' poverty. Remarkably, "the prayer had been given to the Prophet and other Church leaders **by revelation** as they worked late the previous night to prepare it." It is thus a *revealed prayer* — and it became the **pattern** for temple dedications ever since (the "Hosanna Shout" of 109:79 is still used).

**Binding idea:** "Establish a house, even a house of prayer, a house of fasting, a house of faith, a house of learning, a house of glory, a house of order, a house of God" (109:8) — and the plea beneath it: "that thy glory may rest down upon thy people, and upon this thy house… that thy holy presence may be continually in this house" (109:12). The prayer asks for *one thing above all*: God's presence to dwell with His people in His house.

**Thoughts:**
- *★ A revealed prayer — the pattern (heading, 109:6-8).* The most distinctive thing about §109 is *what it is*: a *prayer*, but *given by revelation* — "given to the Prophet… by revelation as they worked late the previous night." (A prayer is normally *our* words *to* God; here God *gives* the words *for* the prayer. This is the §65 "thy kingdom come" model prayer at the scale of a *temple dedication*: God teaches His people *what to ask* for His house. And it became the *pattern* — every temple since dedicated with this prayer as the template, the Hosanna Shout [109:79] enduring. For the project: a prayer can be *received*, not only *composed* — the deepest petitions are sometimes given to us, and a revealed prayer is a gift of knowing *what God Himself would have us ask*. The §90:5 "beware how you hold the oracles" extended: even our prayers can be oracular, received and then offered.)
- *★ The seven-fold house — and the house of learning (109:8,14).* The house is named seven ways: "a house of prayer, a house of fasting, a house of faith, a house of learning, a house of glory, a house of order, a house of God" (109:8 = §88:119). And the *learning* is specified: "seek ye out of the best books words of wisdom, seek learning even by study and also by faith" (109:7,14 = §88:118). (The temple is, among its seven names, a *house of learning* — and the learning is the project's own epistemology [study *and* faith, §88:118/§9:8]. Worship and learning are *one* in God's house: "all those who shall worship in this house may be taught words of wisdom out of the best books… by study, and also by faith" [109:14]. The house of *order* and the house of *learning* and the house of *God* are the *same* house — order, study, and worship integrated. For the project's "house" [the workspace, the council]: the model is a place of *prayer and order and learning together*, where one seeks "out of the best books" *and* "by faith.")
- *★ The presence sought, conditioned on holiness (109:12-13,20-21).* The central petition is *presence*: "that thy holy presence may be continually in this house" (109:12), "that all people who shall enter… may feel thy power, and feel constrained to acknowledge… it is thy house, a place of thy holiness" (109:13). And the *condition*: "no unclean thing shall be permitted to come into thy house to pollute it" (109:20). (The §94:8-9 / §97:15-17 holiness-conditions-the-presence woven into the dedicatory prayer itself: the temple is *for* God's presence, and the presence requires the house be *kept undefiled*. And the mercy for failure: "when thy people transgress… they may speedily repent and return unto thee, and find favor" [109:21] — the house is holy, *and* a place of return for the repentant. The presence is the *point* [§107:18-19 the priesthood's keys to God's presence], and the holiness is its *condition*.)
- *★ Armed with power, no weapon prospering (109:22-25).* The endowment's purpose, prayed: "thy servants may go forth from this house **armed with thy power**… thine angels have charge over them" (109:22), and "no weapon formed against them shall prosper; that he who diggeth a pit for them shall fall into the same himself" (109:25, Isa 54:17/§71:9). (The §95:8/§105:11 endowment-of-power given its *prayed* form: the servants are to go *out* from the house *armed* — the temple is not a refuge to hide in but a *source of power for the work*. And the Isa 54:17 promise [§71:9 — no weapon shall prosper] folded into the dedication: the endowed go out protected, "angels have charge over them," the pit-digger falling into his own pit. The endowment is *for sending*, not for sheltering — power received in the house, exercised in the world.)
- *The kingdom filling the earth, and the Hosanna Shout (109:72-80).* The prayer climbs to the cosmic and the joyful: "the kingdom, which thou hast set up **without hands**, may become a great mountain and fill the whole earth" [109:72, Dan 2 / §65:2 — the rolling stone again]; "thy church… shine forth fair as the moon, clear as the sun, and terrible as an army with banners" [109:73, Song 6:10]; and the close, the **Hosanna Shout**: "mingle our voices with those bright, shining seraphs around thy throne… singing Hosanna to God and the Lamb! … and thy saints shout aloud for joy. Amen, and Amen" [109:79-80]. (The dedicatory prayer ends not in petition but in *praise and joy* — the §65 "thine is the honor, power and glory" doxology at the temple, the saints joining the seraphs' song. The §59:15 cheerful worship and the §25:12 "song of the righteous is a prayer" reaching their highest pitch: the house dedicated, the prayer answered in *shouting for joy*. For Michael: even the most solemn dedication ends in a *shout of joy* — the proper end of the sacred is gladness.)

**Notable / flag:** ★ **reserved thread (Michael's):** the temple / the endowment / God's presence in the house (the whole prayer is temple-centered). **Walk's contribution:** **§109 is the dedicatory prayer of the first temple — and it was itself *given by revelation*, becoming the pattern for every temple dedication since.** A prayer that God *gave the words for*: the §65 model prayer at the scale of a temple, teaching the Saints *what to ask* for His house (prayers can be *received*, not only composed). The house is named seven ways — "a house of prayer, a house of fasting, a house of faith, a house of learning, a house of glory, a house of order, a house of God" (109:8) — and the *learning* is the project's own epistemology, "seek learning even by study and also by faith" (109:14): worship, order, and study are *one* in God's house. The central petition is *presence* — "that thy holy presence may be continually in this house" (109:12) — conditioned, as always, on holiness ("no unclean thing… to pollute it," 109:20; the presence the point, holiness its condition), with mercy built in for the repentant (109:21). The endowment is prayed as power *for sending*: "thy servants may go forth from this house armed with thy power… angels have charge over them" (109:22), and "no weapon formed against them shall prosper" (Isa 54:17) — the temple is a *source* of power for the work, not a refuge to hide in. And the prayer climbs to the kingdom "set up without hands… a great mountain" filling the earth (Dan 2/§65:2), closing not in petition but in the **Hosanna Shout** — "singing Hosanna to God and the Lamb!… and thy saints shout aloud for joy" (109:79-80). For Michael: the section that shows a prayer *received* rather than composed — the template for dedicating God's house — where worship and learning and order are one, where the presence is sought on the condition of holiness, where the endowed go *out* armed with power, and where even the most solemn dedication ends in a shout of joy.
