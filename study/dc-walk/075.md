# D&C 75 — he shall in nowise lose his crown

**Setting:** **Amherst, Ohio, January 25, 1832** — at a conference where **Joseph Smith was sustained and ordained President of the High Priesthood**. Certain elders "desired to learn more about their immediate duties." Two revelations of the same day: verses 1-22 send out missionary pairs with a promise (and revoke-and-renew McLellin's commission); verses 23-36 govern how the Church *supports the families* of those sent to preach.

**Binding idea:** Two halves held together: to those sent — "if ye are faithful ye shall be laden with many sheaves, and crowned with… eternal life" (75:5); and to those who must stay — "every man who is obliged to provide for his own family, let him provide, and he shall **in nowise lose his crown**" (75:28). The gospel does not pit the mission against the family; it crowns the faithful sender *and* the faithful provider.

**Read:** "Hearken, O ye who have given your names to go forth to proclaim my gospel, and to prune my vineyard" (75:2). "Go forth and not tarry, neither be idle but labor with your might" (75:3). "Lifting up your voices as with the sound of a trump, proclaiming the truth according to the revelations and commandments which I have given you" (75:4). "If ye are faithful ye shall be laden with many sheaves, and crowned with honor, and glory, and immortality, and eternal life" (75:5). "I revoke the commission which I gave unto [William E. McLellin]… and I give unto him a new commission… in the which I, the Lord, chasten him for the murmurings of his heart; and he sinned; nevertheless, I forgive him" (75:6-8). "Calling on the name of the Lord for the Comforter, which shall teach them all things that are expedient for them— praying always that they faint not; and inasmuch as they do this, I will be with them even unto the end" (75:10-11). "In whatsoever house ye enter, and they receive you, leave your blessing upon that house" (75:19). "In the day of judgment you shall be judges of that house" (75:21). "It is the duty of the church to assist in supporting the families of those… who are called and must needs be sent unto the world to proclaim the gospel" (75:24). "Every man who is obliged to provide for his own family, let him provide, and he shall in nowise lose his crown; and let him labor in the church" (75:28). "Let every man be diligent in all things. And the idler shall not have place in the church, except he repent and mend his ways" (75:29).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "He shall in nowise lose his crown" — the provider honored equally (75:24-28).* The pastoral heart of the section, and a balance the gospel insists on. The Church has a *duty* to "assist in supporting the families of those… who are called and must needs be sent" (75:24) — the missionary's family is *cared for by the body* so he can go. And then the counterweight: "every man who is obliged to provide for his own family, let him provide, and he shall **in nowise lose his crown**; and let him labor in the church" (75:28). (Two truths the gospel refuses to let compete. First: going out to preach is a real sacrifice, and the *community* shoulders the missionary's family [the §70:12/§72:24 consecration applied — the body supports those who labor in the word]. Second, and tender: the man who *cannot* go — who is "obliged to provide for his own family" [1 Tim 5:8 — "if any provide not for his own… he hath denied the faith"] — does **not** rank lower; he "shall in nowise lose his crown." The same eternal reward [the "crown" of 75:5] is open to the provider who stays and the preacher who goes. This dissolves a false hierarchy that haunts religious life — the notion that the dramatic sacrifice [leave everything, go] is *holier* than the daily faithfulness [stay, provide, labor in the church locally]. It is *not*. "Let him provide… and let him labor in the church" — the provider is doubly charged [provide AND labor], and crowned the same. For Michael, who works in "intermittent bursts" around a family and a job: the section that says the man who must provide loses *nothing* of the crown — the kingdom honors the faithful provider exactly as it honors the sent missionary; the quiet duty is not the lesser calling.)
- *★ McLellin revoked, chastened, forgiven, resent (75:6-8) — the §66 warning unfolds.* Three months after §66 named McLellin "clean, but not all" and warned him, his §66 commission to "the eastern countries" is *revoked*: "I chasten him for the murmurings of his heart; and he sinned; nevertheless, I forgive him" (75:7-8). McLellin had failed to fulfill the mission (he'd turned back). The Lord revokes, chastens, *forgives*, and *resends* — "Go ye into the south countries" (75:8). (The §66 warning-given-early playing out in real time: McLellin murmured and turned back, and here is the consequence [revoked + chastened] *with* the mercy [forgiven + recommissioned]. The reprove-then-love pattern [§3/§121:43] at work — the chastening is real, but it ends in a *renewed* call, not a discarding. And the §61:20 "mine anger is turned away" rhythm: "he sinned; nevertheless, I forgive him" in a single breath. The Lord doesn't give up on the murmurer; He chastens and *sends again*. Though McLellin would, in the end, not endure [bin-4-adj], at *this* point the door is still wide open — the Lord forgives the failed mission and hands him a new one. The tragedy of McLellin is not that grace ran out but that he eventually stopped receiving it.)
- *The missionary promise and the dust-shaking judgment (75:3-22).* The sent elders get the full charge: "lift up your voices as with the sound of a trump" (75:4), "calling on the name of the Lord for the Comforter, which shall teach them all things… praying always that they faint not" (75:10-11, Luke 18:1), going "from house to house" (75:18). Where received, "leave your blessing upon that house" (75:19); where rejected, "shake off the dust of your feet" — and "in the day of judgment you shall be judges of that house" (75:20-21, Matt 10:14-15). (The dignity and weight of the witness: the missionary's testimony, received or rejected, *establishes accountability* — "more tolerable for the heathen in the day of judgment than for that house" [75:22], because the heathen never heard and that house *did*. The §75:21 "you shall be judges of that house" is the §64:37-40 church-as-judge thread personalized: the faithful witness becomes, at the judgment, a *witness* to the hearer's response. And the antidote to the hard road is the same constant — "I will be with them even unto the end" [75:11,13,14], the with-ness sounded *three times* over the pairs. Plus the idler excluded again [75:29 — "the idler shall not have place in the church, except he repent," the §56/§68:31 thread]: the kingdom has no room for idleness, whether in the field or at home.)

**Notable / flag:** No spin-off. **Walk's contribution:** **§75 refuses to rank the sent missionary above the faithful provider — "he shall in nowise lose his crown" (75:24-28).** The Church has a *duty* to support the families of those "called and must needs be sent" (the body carries the missionary's household, §70:12/§72:24) — *and* the man "obliged to provide for his own family, let him provide, and he shall **in nowise lose his crown**; and let him labor in the church" (1 Tim 5:8). The same eternal crown (75:5) is open to the provider who stays and the preacher who goes — dissolving the false hierarchy that makes the dramatic sacrifice holier than the daily faithfulness (a word for Michael's own intermittent-burst work around a family and a job: the quiet duty is *not* the lesser calling). Plus **McLellin revoked, chastened, forgiven, and resent (75:6-8)** — the §66 warning unfolding in real time: he murmured and turned back, so his commission is revoked and he's chastened — but "he sinned; nevertheless, I forgive him" and is handed a *new* mission (the reprove-then-love §3/§121:43; the §61:20 "nevertheless" rhythm; grace still wide open — his tragedy was not that grace ran out but that he eventually stopped receiving it). And the witness that *establishes accountability* (75:20-22 — received or rejected, the testimony makes the hearer answerable, "more tolerable for the heathen… than for that house"; the §64:37-40 church-as-judge personalized), with "I will be with them even unto the end" sounded three times over the missionary pairs. For Michael: the section that crowns the stay-at-home provider exactly as it crowns the sent missionary — the kingdom does not measure faithfulness by how dramatic the sacrifice looks — and shows the Lord chastening a failed servant only to forgive him and send him out again.
