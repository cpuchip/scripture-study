# D&C 49 — marriage is ordained of God

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, May 7, 1831** — to Sidney Rigdon, Parley P. Pratt, and **Leman Copley**, sent to preach to the **Shakers** (the United Society of Believers in Christ's Second Appearing), to which Copley had belonged. The Shakers held that Christ's Second Coming had *already* occurred — in the form of a woman, Ann Lee; they rejected water baptism, forbade marriage (total celibacy), and some forbade eating meat. The three brethren read this revelation to the Shaker community; it was rejected. It is a point-by-point correction that incidentally lays down significant doctrine on marriage and the use of the earth.

**Binding idea:** Against a religion of *renunciation* — no marriage, no meat, the world already ended — the Lord affirms *creation*: "marriage is ordained of God unto man" (49:15), the animals are "ordained for the use of man" (49:19), and the Second Coming's "hour and the day no man knoweth… until he comes" (49:7). The gospel does not flee the created world; it *fulfills its purpose*.

**Read:** Leman is to "reason with them, not according to that which he has received of them, but according to that which shall be taught him by you my servants" (49:4). "The hour and the day no man knoweth, neither the angels in heaven, nor shall they know until he comes" (49:7). "All are under sin, except those which I have reserved unto myself, holy men that ye know not of" (49:8). "Whoso forbiddeth to marry is not ordained of God, for marriage is ordained of God unto man. Wherefore, it is lawful that he should have one wife, and they twain shall be one flesh, and all this that the earth might answer the end of its creation; and that it might be filled with the measure of man, according to his creation before the world was made" (49:15-17). "Whoso forbiddeth to abstain from meats… is not ordained of God; for, behold, the beasts of the field and the fowls of the air… is ordained for the use of man for food and for raiment" (49:18-19); "but it is not given that one man should possess that which is above another, wherefore the world lieth in sin" (49:20); "wo be unto man that sheddeth blood or that wasteth flesh and hath no need" (49:21). "The Son of Man cometh not in the form of a woman, neither of a man traveling on the earth" (49:22); "be not deceived" (49:23). "Jacob shall flourish in the wilderness, and the Lamanites shall blossom as the rose" (49:24). "I will go before you and be your rearward… and you shall not be confounded" (49:27).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "Marriage is ordained of God" (49:15-17) — against renunciation, for creation.* The Shakers made *celibacy* a religion; the Lord answers that "whoso forbiddeth to marry is **not ordained of God**" (1 Tim 4:3). Marriage isn't a concession to weakness or a lower path — it is *ordained*, with a *cosmic purpose*: "that the earth might answer the end of its creation; and that it might be filled with the measure of man, according to his creation **before the world was made**" (49:16-17). Two stunning claims: (1) the earth has an *end of its creation* that marriage-and-family *fulfills* (procreation is the earth completing its purpose, not a fall from purity), and (2) there is a foreordained "measure of man" — a number of God's children appointed *before the world was made* — that the earth is to be "filled with." (This is the matter-spectrum's anthropology: the premortal spirits [§29, §38:2] are a fixed company waiting for bodies, and *marriage is how the earth answers the end for which it was created* — to give them flesh. Renunciation of marriage isn't holiness; it's a refusal of the earth's purpose. The §38:27 "be one" and the §42 family-economy rest on this: Zion is *families*, not celibates. And it dignifies the body and procreation against every gnostic instinct that the flesh is the problem — the §29 "all things are spiritual to God" means marriage of *bodies* is spiritual work.)
- *★ The earth for use, not waste; and equality (49:18-21).* The animals are "ordained for the use of man for food and for raiment… that he might have in abundance" (49:19) — against the Shaker vegetarianism. *But* the affirmation comes fenced with two limits: "**wo be unto man that… wasteth flesh and hath no need**" (49:21) — use is ordained, *waste* is condemned (the stewardship of creation, not exploitation); and "**it is not given that one man should possess that which is above another, wherefore the world lieth in sin**" (49:20) — *inequality of possession* is named as the world's sin. (So the Lord rejects asceticism [the earth is good, use it] *and* greed [don't waste, don't hoard above your brother] in the same breath. The abundance of creation is for *all*, used *without waste* — the §42 consecration ethic [no robes/rags, §38:27] applied to the earth itself: the world's sin is precisely that "one man possesses above another." Creation is good and *to be shared*, neither despised nor hoarded.)
- *★ Date unknown — "the hour and the day no man knoweth… until he comes" (49:7).* The Shakers believed the Second Coming *had happened*; the Lord flatly states no one knows the hour — "neither the angels in heaven" (Matt 24:36). This is the standing rebuke to *all* date-setting and "it already occurred" claims (and to the §43/§45 anxious watchers): the day is *reserved*, even from angels. (The discipline of the withheld [§25:4] applied to the supreme date: you watch and prepare without knowing *when*, and any claim to know — or that it's already happened — is by definition false. "Be not deceived," 49:23.)
- *"Reason… according to that which shall be taught by you, not according to what he received of them" (49:4) — the convert teaches the new, not the syncretic mix.* Leman Copley still half-held his Shaker beliefs; he's told to preach *the gospel as received*, not his personal blend of old-and-new. (The §32:4 "pretend to no other revelation" / declare-the-written-word applied to a convert from another tradition: you don't carry your prior framework into the teaching; you teach what you've *received*, cleanly. Copley, notably, soon fell away — the half-held old belief proving the seam. bin-4-adjacent biography.)

**Notable / flag:** No spin-off. **Walk's contribution:** **§49 answers a religion of renunciation with the goodness of creation.** **Marriage is ordained of God (49:15-17)** — not a concession but the means by which "the earth answers the end of its creation," filling it with the foreordained "measure of man… before the world was made" (the matter-spectrum's anthropology: premortal spirits awaiting bodies; Zion is families, not celibates; against every gnostic instinct that the flesh is the problem). **The earth is for use, not waste** (49:19,21) — abundance ordained, but "wo… that wasteth flesh and hath no need," and "the world lieth in sin" precisely because "one man possesses above another" (49:20 — the consecration ethic on creation: good, shared, neither despised nor hoarded). And **the date is unknown even to angels** (49:7) — the standing rebuke to date-setting and "it already happened." For Michael: the section that refuses both asceticism and greed — the created world is *good*, marriage *fulfills* the earth's purpose, the abundance is for all to use without waste, and the day of the Lord is reserved even from the angels.
