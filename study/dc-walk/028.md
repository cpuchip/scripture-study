# D&C 28 — the order of revelation

**Setting:** **Fayette, New York, September 1830** — just before a Church conference. **Hiram Page**, one of the Eight Witnesses, had a seerstone and was producing "revelations" through it "concerning the upbuilding of Zion and the order of the Church." Several members believed them — "even Oliver Cowdery was wrongly influenced." Joseph inquired, and this revelation, addressed to *Oliver*, settled the order of revelation in the Church. (Note the irony: it had to be given to Oliver, who had himself been taken in.)

**Binding idea:** Revelation for the *whole Church* comes through *one appointed channel* — "no one shall be appointed to receive commandments and revelations in this church excepting my servant Joseph Smith, Jun., for he receiveth them even as Moses" (28:2). Hiram's stone-revelations weren't false because they were obviously evil; they were false because they were *out of order* — he was not appointed to receive for the Church. The test of a revelation is not only *does it feel true* but *is it within your stewardship*.

**Read:** To Oliver: "thou shalt be heard by the church in all things whatsoever thou shalt teach them by the Comforter" (28:1). "But… no one shall be appointed to receive commandments and revelations in this church excepting my servant Joseph Smith, Jun., for he receiveth them even as Moses" (28:2). "Thou shalt be obedient unto the things which I shall give unto him, even as Aaron… to declare faithfully" (28:3). Oliver may teach by the Comforter (28:4), "but thou shalt not write by way of commandment, but by wisdom; and thou shalt not command him who is at thy head, and at the head of the church" (28:5-6); "I have given him the keys of the mysteries, and the revelations which are sealed, until I shall appoint unto them another in his stead" (28:7). Oliver is sent to "the Lamanites" (28:8); where the city Zion will be "is not revealed… but it shall be on the borders by the Lamanites" (28:9). Then how to correct Hiram: "take thy brother, Hiram Page, between him and thee alone, and tell him that those things which he hath written from that stone are not of me and that Satan deceiveth him" (28:11); "these things have not been appointed unto him" (28:12); "for all things must be done in order, and by common consent in the church, by the prayer of faith" (28:13).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ The order of revelation answers "is it God or me?" with a structural test (28:2).* The walk's recurring epistemology problem — revelation is interior (§8:2), felt like your own mind, so how do you know it's God and not self (or Satan)? — gets a *new kind of answer* here. Hiram's revelations may well have *felt* spiritual; the test that exposes them isn't the felt-quality but the *structural* one: **was he appointed to receive this?** Revelation for the *whole Church* belongs to the *one* appointed channel; everyone may receive revelation *for their own stewardship*, but no one receives *for the body* except the one set to do it. So the discernment question gains a second prong: not only "does it burn / bring peace?" (§9:8) but "*is this within my stewardship, in order?*" A prompting to run someone else's stewardship — however luminous it feels — is out of order, and out-of-order is the signature of counterfeit (28:11, "Satan deceiveth him"). (This is enormous for the project's discernment thread: the felt signal [period-language / Spirit-distillation] is necessary but not sufficient; *order* is the cross-check. A true distillation respects stewardship boundaries; a counterfeit overruns them.)
- *★ Hiram's stone vs. Oliver's rod — same instrument, opposite verdict (28:11 ↔ §8:6-8).* A sharp comparison the walk earns. In §8 the Lord *claimed* Oliver's folk instrument (the "gift of Aaron," the divining rod), redirecting it toward Himself. Here He *rejects* Hiram's folk instrument (the seerstone), naming it Satan's deception (the footnote even files it under "sorcery/superstition"). Same *kind* of object — a folk seer-instrument — opposite verdict. Why? Not the instrument: the *order*. Oliver's rod operated *within* his stewardship under proper authority; Hiram's stone produced revelations *for the whole Church*, which was never his to receive. The discriminator is never the object or even the felt experience — it is *stewardship and order*. (The same gift, in order, is of God; out of order, it is deception. This is the period-language principle's necessary guardrail: the Holy Ghost distills light on the prepared mind, yes — *within the bounds the Lord has set*. Light claimed outside your stewardship is the counterfeit's entry point.)
- *★ Correct him "between him and thee alone" — private persuasion, not public shaming (28:11).* The remedy for Hiram's error is Matthew 18:15: go to him *privately*, "between him and thee alone." Not denounced at the conference, not humiliated — reasoned with one-on-one. And Oliver's own role is bounded in the same breath: "thou shalt not write by way of commandment, but by wisdom; and thou shalt not command him who is at thy head" (28:5-6). The whole correction runs on *persuasion and order*, not force or public censure. (This is the presiding covenant exactly — reprove with sharpness *when moved by the Holy Ghost, then increase of love* [121:43], by persuasion not compulsion [121:41]. A false-revelation crisis, the kind that could split a young church, is handled by a private conversation and a vote, not by an authority crushing a dissenter. Hiram, so corrected, submitted — and stayed.)
- *"All things must be done in order, and by common consent… by the prayer of faith" (28:13).* The §26 common-consent law is the *resolution mechanism* here: the conference will settle it by common consent. So order isn't only the prophet's singular channel (28:2) — it's *also* the body's common consent (28:13). The two together are the Restoration's safeguard against both anarchy (everyone with a stone) and tyranny (a leader unchecked): one appointed to receive, *and* the body to confirm. (Hiram's claims fail *both* tests — not the appointed channel, and not by common consent — which is exactly why they're "out of order.")
- *"Until I shall appoint another in his stead" (28:7) — orderly succession built in.* Even as the Lord establishes Joseph as the sole channel, He names that the keys pass — "until I shall appoint unto them another in his stead." The singular channel is an *office*, not a cult of personality; it will be filled by succession. (The order outlasts the man — the institutional safeguard against the channel dying with Joseph.)
