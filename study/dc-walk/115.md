# D&C 115 — The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints

**Setting:** **Far West, Missouri, April 26, 1838.** The Saints, driven from Kirtland, were gathering at Far West. The revelation does something the Church had lacked a fixed form of: it *names the Church* — "The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints" — and commands a temple at Far West.

**Binding idea:** "For thus shall my church be called in the last days, even **The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints**" (115:4). The name the Lord gives His own church centers on *Jesus Christ* — it is *His* church — and on *the Saints of the latter days*: whose it is, and who they are.

**Read:** "Unto all the elders and people of my Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, scattered abroad in all the world; for thus shall my church be called in the last days, even The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints" (115:3-4). "Arise and shine forth, that thy light may be a standard for the nations" (115:5). "That the gathering together upon the land of Zion, and upon her stakes, may be for a defense, and for a refuge from the storm, and from wrath when it shall be poured out without mixture upon the whole earth" (115:6). "Let the city, Far West, be a holy and consecrated land… called most holy, for the ground upon which thou standest is holy" (115:7). "Let not my servant Joseph… get in debt any more for the building of a house unto my name; but let a house be built… according to the pattern which I will show… and if my people build it not according to the pattern… I will not accept it" (115:13-15). "Unto him have I given the keys of this kingdom and ministry" (115:19).

**Thoughts:**
- *★★ The name — Christ's church, the latter-day Saints (115:3-4).* The Lord *names* His church, and the name is a *doctrine*: "**The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints**." (Three claims in one name. First, it is "**of Jesus Christ**" — *His* church, not a man's [not "Mormonite," not after Joseph]; the §3 Ne 27:8 principle ["it shall be called in my name… for it is my church, if it so be that they are built upon my gospel"]. Second, "**Latter-day**" — situated in *this* dispensation, the last [the §27:13 fulness of times]. Third, "**Saints**" — the people are *saints* [the New Testament word for the covenant members, not a title of unusual holiness but of *set-apartness*, §20]. The name answers *whose* the church is [Christ's], *when* it is [the latter days], and *who* its people are [saints]. For the project, which thinks carefully about *names* [the Callie/Party lesson — "never name a persona a common noun in its own domain"]: the Lord's own naming centers the church on the *Person* it belongs to — the name is a confession of *ownership*, "my church." A name done right declares whose the thing is.)
- *★ The gathering as defense and refuge (115:5-7).* "Arise and shine forth, that thy light may be a standard for the nations" [115:5, Isa 60:1] — and the gathering "may be for a **defense**, and for a **refuge** from the storm, and from wrath when it shall be poured out" [115:6]. (The §45:66/§101:21 gathering-as-refuge given its *stakes* form: the gathering places — Zion *and her stakes* — are "a defense, and a refuge from the storm." The church is *both* a "standard for the nations" [outward, a light lifted up, Isa 11:12] *and* a "refuge from the storm" [inward, a shelter]: it shines *out* and shelters *in*. And Far West is "holy ground" [115:7, Ex 3:5] — the gathering place sanctified. For the project: the community is both a *standard* [seen, a light to others] and a *refuge* [a shelter for its own] — the two are not opposed; the same gathering that lights the nations shelters the saints.)
- *The temple by revealed pattern — or not accepted (115:13-16).* The temple at Far West commanded, with the §111 debt-lesson applied: "let not my servant Joseph… **get in debt any more** for the building" [115:13]; and the §94/§95 pattern-thread sharpened: "build it according to the pattern which I will show… and **if my people build it not according to the pattern… I will not accept it**" [115:14-15]. (God's house must be built *to His shown pattern* — and a house built *otherwise* He "will not accept." The revealed pattern is not a suggestion but the *condition of acceptance*; order received, not invented [§94:2]. For the project: when the form is *given*, building it to a different form forfeits the acceptance — the pattern is binding, not advisory.)
