# D&C 106 — that day shall not overtake you as a thief

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, November 25, 1834** — to **Warren A. Cowdery** (Oliver's older brother), called as "a presiding high priest" over the Church "in the land of Freedom" (Freedom, New York) and the regions round about.

**Binding idea:** "The coming of the Lord draweth nigh, and it overtaketh the world as a thief in the night— Therefore, gird up your loins, that you may be the children of light, and that day shall not overtake you as a thief" (106:4-5). The Second Coming surprises *the world* like a thief — but *not* the prepared; the children of light are not caught off guard.

**Read:** "Warren A. Cowdery should be appointed and ordained a presiding high priest… and should preach my everlasting gospel, and lift up his voice and warn the people" (106:1-2). "Devote his whole time to this high and holy calling… seeking diligently the kingdom of heaven and its righteousness, and all things necessary shall be added thereunto; for the laborer is worthy of his hire" (106:3). "The coming of the Lord draweth nigh, and it overtaketh the world as a thief in the night— Therefore, gird up your loins, that you may be the children of light, and that day shall not overtake you as a thief" (106:4-5). "There was joy in heaven when my servant Warren bowed to my scepter, and separated himself from the crafts of men" (106:6). "Notwithstanding the vanity of his heart, I will lift him up inasmuch as he will humble himself before me" (106:7). "If he continue to be a faithful witness and a light unto the church I have prepared a crown for him" (106:8).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ The children of light are not overtaken (106:4-5).* The Second Coming "overtaketh the world as a thief in the night" — but the warning turns immediately to the *exception*: "gird up your loins, that you may be the children of light, and **that day shall not overtake you as a thief**" (1 Thes 5:2-5). (Paul's "ye, brethren, are not in darkness, that that day should overtake you as a thief" given to a single steward: the *thief* image is for *the world*, caught unprepared; the *children of light* are *expecting* the day and so are not surprised by it. The §87:8 "stand in holy places" and the §45:57 wise-virgins-undeceived: the disciple is not *exempt* from the day's coming but is *ready* for it — readiness, not exemption, is the difference. For anyone anxious about the end: the question is not *when* but *whether you are a child of light* — the prepared are never "surprised" by what they've been watching for.)
- *★ Grace despite vanity (106:6-7).* A tender note: "there was joy in heaven when my servant Warren bowed to my scepter, and separated himself from the crafts of men… **notwithstanding the vanity of his heart, I will lift him up inasmuch as he will humble himself**" (106:6-7). (The Lord names Warren's flaw — "the vanity of his heart" — *and* promises to lift him anyway, *on the condition of humility*. The §66:3 "clean, but not all" / the canon's honesty about its servants [§3/§20:5/§93:47]: God calls the flawed, names the flaw, and offers grace *contingent on humbling*. "Joy in heaven" over the man who "separated himself from the crafts of men" [Luke 15, the rejoicing over the returning one] — the call itself is celebrated in heaven, even with the vanity still to be overcome. For anyone called despite their faults: God sees the vanity, calls you anyway, and will lift you — if you humble yourself. Warren would, tragically, later leave the Church [bin-4-adj] — but the *offer* here is grace-on-condition-of-humility, freely extended.)
