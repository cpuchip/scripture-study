# D&C 57 — the center place

**Setting:** **In Zion, Jackson County, Missouri, July 20, 1831.** The elders had journeyed from Ohio to Missouri's western border, as commanded (§52). Joseph, contemplating the Lamanites, had wondered: "When will Zion be built up in her glory, and where will Thy temple stand, unto which all nations shall come in the last days?" This revelation is the answer — and the end of a long withholding. After §48:5 ("the place is not yet to be revealed"), §52, and §54-56's enemy-ground hints, **Zion's location is finally named: Independence, the center place.**

**Binding idea:** "This is the land of promise, and the place for the city of Zion… the place which is now called Independence is the **center place**; and a spot for the temple is lying westward, upon a lot which is not far from the courthouse" (57:2-3). The withheld destination (§25:4/§48:5) is disclosed at last — a real town, a real lot — and the practical work begins: buy land, open an honest store, set up the press.

**Read:** Missouri "is the land which I have appointed and consecrated for the gathering of the saints… this is the land of promise, and the place for the city of Zion" (57:1-2). "If you will receive wisdom here is wisdom. Behold, the place which is now called Independence is the center place; and a spot for the temple is lying westward, upon a lot which is not far from the courthouse" (57:3). "It is wisdom that the land should be purchased by the saints… that they may obtain it for an everlasting inheritance" (57:4-5). Sidney Gilbert is the agent, "to receive moneys… to buy land" (57:6); Edward Partridge to "divide unto the saints their inheritance" (57:7); Gilbert is to "establish a store, that he may sell goods **without fraud**… to buy lands for the good of the saints" (57:8); William W. Phelps "be established as a printer unto the church" (57:11); Oliver Cowdery to "copy, and to correct, and select, that all things may be right before me, as it shall be proved by the Spirit" (57:13).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ Zion located — the withholding ends at a courthouse lot (57:2-3).* For months the Saints gathered *toward* a place they couldn't see (§48:5, "the place is not yet to be revealed"); they were told to "act as for years" without knowing where (§51:16). Now the Lord names it with startling *specificity*: not "somewhere in the West" but "the place which is now called **Independence** is the center place," and the temple lot "is lying westward, upon a lot which is not far from the courthouse." The grandest theological promise in the Restoration — *Zion, the New Jerusalem, to which all nations shall come* — is pinned to an ordinary frontier town and a surveyable lot by a county courthouse. (This is the §29:32 spiritual→temporal logic at its most concrete: the heavenly city descends onto a *real address*. And it rewards the discipline of the withheld [§25:4]: those who gathered faithfully *toward* an unnamed place are now *shown* it. The withheld is disclosed to those who acted on it in faith. Note too the realism — the "center place" sits in the land that §52:42 called "now the land of your enemies"; the promised city's lot is real ground that will have to be *won* and, tragically, *lost* [the 1833 expulsion] before it is built. Zion is named, not yet given.)
- *"Sell goods without fraud" (57:8) — honest commerce as kingdom-building.* The Church's store, run by Sidney Gilbert, is commissioned to "sell goods *without fraud*… to buy lands for the good of the saints." The kingdom's *commerce* is to be *honest* — the merchant of Zion sells fairly, and the profit serves the poor's inheritance. (The §49:21 use-not-waste and the §56 reciprocal-wo ethic applied to *trade*: business in Zion is not exempt from righteousness; the store is held to "without fraud," and its purpose is *consecration* [land for the saints], not private gain. Even the marketplace is sanctified — honest dealing is the economic form of "be one." The §53:2 "forsake the world" agent now runs a *worldly business* righteously: in the world's trade, not of its fraud.)
- *The press and the record, again (57:11-13).* Zion's founding immediately establishes the *printer* (Phelps) and the *editor* (Oliver, to "copy, correct, and select… as proved by the Spirit"). Before the city is built, the Lord sets up its *publishing* — the gospel and the revelations to go forth in print, Spirit-checked. (The §47 record + §55 children's-books theme: a covenant people is a *publishing* people; Zion's first institutions include a press whose corrections are "proved by the Spirit." The Word multiplied in type is part of the city's foundation, alongside the store and the temple lot.)
