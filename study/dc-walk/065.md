# D&C 65 — the stone cut out of the mountain, and a prayer for the kingdom

**Setting:** **Hiram, Ohio, October 30, 1831** — where Joseph had just moved (from §64's Kirtland) to resume the Bible translation at the John Johnson home. The heading calls it a "Revelation on prayer." Six verses: the keys committed to earth, the gospel rolling forth as Daniel's stone, and a model prayer — "thy kingdom come" given its fullest Restoration voice.

**Binding idea:** "The keys of the kingdom of God are committed unto man on the earth, and from thence shall the gospel roll forth unto the ends of the earth, **as the stone which is cut out of the mountain without hands shall roll forth, until it has filled the whole earth**" (65:2). Daniel's stone (Dan 2:44-45) is the Restoration: the kingdom set up by no human hand that grows until it fills the earth. And the close is a prayer — "may the kingdom of God go forth, that the kingdom of heaven may come" (65:6).

**Read:** "A voice as of one sent down from on high… whose voice is unto men—Prepare ye the way of the Lord, make his paths straight" (65:1). "The keys of the kingdom of God are committed unto man on the earth, and from thence shall the gospel roll forth unto the ends of the earth, as the stone which is cut out of the mountain without hands shall roll forth, until it has filled the whole earth" (65:2). "Prepare ye the way of the Lord, prepare ye the supper of the Lamb, make ready for the Bridegroom" (65:3). "Pray unto the Lord, call upon his holy name, make known his wonderful works among the people" (65:4). "Call upon the Lord, that his kingdom may go forth upon the earth, that the inhabitants thereof may receive it, and be prepared for the days to come, in the which the Son of Man shall come down in heaven, clothed in the brightness of his glory, to meet the kingdom of God which is set up on the earth" (65:5). "Wherefore, may the kingdom of God go forth, that the kingdom of heaven may come, that thou, O God, mayest be glorified in heaven so on earth, that thine enemies may be subdued; for thine is the honor, power and glory, forever and ever. Amen" (65:6).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ The stone cut out of the mountain without hands (65:2 / Dan 2:44-45).* The defining image: Nebuchadnezzar's dream-stone, "cut out of the mountain without hands," that struck the image of the kingdoms of the world and "became a great mountain, and filled the whole earth" (Dan 2:35,45). Daniel's interpretation: "in the days of these kings shall the God of heaven set up a kingdom, which shall never be destroyed… it shall break in pieces and consume all these kingdoms, and it shall stand for ever" (Dan 2:44). Here the Lord *claims* the prophecy for the Restoration: the gospel rolling forth from the keys-committed-to-earth *is* that stone. (Two things in the image. First, "*without hands*" — the kingdom is *not* a human construction; no man cut the stone, and no man's strength rolls it. The Restoration's growth is *God's* doing, which is why §64:33's "out of small things proceedeth that which is great" is its companion — a stone that starts small and fills the earth by a power not its own. Second, it *rolls* — the kingdom is not static but *advancing*, gathering momentum, "unto the ends of the earth." For a Church of a few hundred in 1831, to be told they're Daniel's world-filling stone is the §38/§45 gathering-of-the-nations promise in a single rolling image: small now, the whole earth eventually, and the rolling is not theirs to stop or start — it's *cut without hands*.)
- *★ "Thy kingdom come" given content — a model prayer (65:5-6).* The section is "a revelation on prayer," and its last two verses *are* a prayer — recognizably the Lord's Prayer's central petition expanded: "may the kingdom of God go forth, that the kingdom of heaven may come, that thou, O God, mayest be glorified in heaven so on earth… for thine is the honor, power and glory, forever and ever. Amen" (65:6, cf. Matt 6:10,13). (The Lord teaches the Saints *what to pray for* — not their own comfort but *the kingdom's advance*. And the structure reveals a doctrine: there are *two* kingdoms — the kingdom of God *set up on the earth* [the Church, the stone] and the kingdom of *heaven* that *comes down* at the Second Coming, "the Son of Man… clothed in the brightness of his glory, to meet the kingdom of God which is set up on the earth" [65:5]. The earthly kingdom is built *upward* to *meet* the heavenly kingdom descending — the two join at the coming. So "thy kingdom come" is a prayer with *work* in it: we pray the kingdom of God forth *so that* the kingdom of heaven may come; the petition enlists the one who prays. The §64:29 "agents on the Lord's errand" given its prayer — you pray the errand you also labor.)
- *"Prepare ye the way… make ready for the Bridegroom" (65:1,3).* The voice that cried in Isaiah and through John the Baptist — "Prepare ye the way of the Lord, make his paths straight" (Isa 40:3; Matt 3:3) — is now the *whole Church's* cry. And it gathers the wedding-feast imagery: "prepare ye the supper of the Lamb, make ready for the Bridegroom" (65:3). (The forerunner's voice is no longer one man in the wilderness but the rolling stone itself; and the preparation is for a *wedding* — the §33:17/§45:56 ten-virgins / Bridegroom thread. The kingdom rolls forth *to make a people ready to meet the Bridegroom* — Daniel's stone and the wise virgins are the same preparation, the same coming.)
