# D&C 37 — gather to the Ohio

**Setting:** **Near Fayette, New York, December 1830** — to Joseph and Sidney. Four verses, and the heading names their weight: "Herein is given the **first commandment concerning a gathering** in this dispensation." Within weeks the New York Saints would uproot and move to Kirtland, Ohio — the Church's first physical gathering, and the start of the Ohio period (temple, priesthood revelations, the School of the Prophets). A short revelation that turns the whole young Church westward.

**Binding idea:** "It is expedient in me that they should assemble together at the Ohio" (37:3). The doctrine of *gathering* — scattered Saints drawn into one place — begins here. And it begins with a pause on the translation work ("translate no more until ye shall go to the Ohio, and this because of the enemy and for your sakes," 37:1) and ends with *agency*: "let every man choose for himself until I come" (37:4).

**Read:** "It is not expedient in me that ye should translate any more until ye shall go to the Ohio, and this because of the enemy and for your sakes" (37:1). "Ye shall not go until ye have preached my gospel in those parts, and have strengthened up the church… more especially in Colesville; for, behold, they pray unto me in much faith" (37:2). "A commandment I give unto the church, that it is expedient in me that they should assemble together at the Ohio, against the time that my servant Oliver Cowdery shall return" (37:3). "Here is wisdom, and let every man choose for himself until I come" (37:4).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ The gathering begins — and it's a *physical* gathering (37:3).* The Restoration's gathering doctrine, sounded abstractly before (§29:7-8 "gathered in unto one place," §33:6 "gather mine elect from the four quarters"), here becomes a *concrete command to relocate*: assemble at the Ohio. The §29 promise gets boots and wagons. (Gathering in the Restoration is not only spiritual [hearts gathered to Christ] but *geographic* [bodies gathered to a place] — the spiritual→temporal logic of §29:32 enacted: the spiritual gathering of the elect [§29:7] becomes a temporal assembling [§37:3]. Zion is a *place*, not only a state of heart — the consecration/Zion thread that §38 opens and the Missouri sections [§57] will locate.)
- *"Because of the enemy and for your sakes" (37:1) — the gathering as refuge.* The first reason given is *protection*: stop translating, leave New York, because of "the enemy" (the intensifying persecution of §24) and "for your sakes." The gathering is partly a *flight to safety and strength* — scattered, isolated Saints are vulnerable; gathered, they can build and defend (the §29:8 "prepare… against the day when tribulation… are sent forth"). (Gathering as garrison: drawing the laborers in from the exposed field to a fortified center — the presiding/protection logic at the scale of a whole people. Walls around the field are lawful protection [the presiding covenant]; the gathering *is* the wall.)
- *"Let every man choose for himself" (37:4) — the gathering is by agency, not compulsion.* The command to gather closes on *choice*: "let every man choose for himself until I come." Even a divine command to relocate is left to each person's agency — no one is *forced* to Ohio. (This is the §26 common-consent / D&C 121:41 persuasion-not-compulsion principle applied to the gathering itself: the Lord *commands* the gathering and *leaves the choosing free*. The §29:39 agency that requires opposition is honored even in obedience — you gather because you choose to, not because you're driven. The Restoration's central physical act is voluntary.)
- *Care for Colesville before the move (37:2).* Before gathering, they must "strengthen up the church… more especially in Colesville; for… they pray unto me in much faith." The gathering doesn't abandon the scattered branches — they're to be strengthened first. (Pastoral order: the leaders don't simply decamp; they secure the flock they're leaving. The §20:53 watchcare honored in the transition — gather the center, but don't neglect the praying-in-much-faith branch on the way out.)

**Notable / flag:** No spin-off. **Walk's contribution:** **§37 turns the abstract gathering doctrine physical** — "assemble together at the Ohio" (37:3): the §29 promise to gather the elect gets boots and wagons, and the dispensation's first relocation begins (the move to Kirtland). Gathering in the Restoration is geographic, not only spiritual — Zion is a *place* (the §38/§57 thread opening). And the gathering is **a refuge** ("because of the enemy and for your sakes," the garrison logic) and **by agency** ("let every man choose for himself," 37:4 — even a command to relocate is left free; the §26/121:41 persuasion-not-compulsion at the scale of a whole people). For Michael: four verses that turn the young Church westward — the gathering becomes real ground under real feet, a refuge drawn in from an exposed field, and even so, left to each person to *choose*.
