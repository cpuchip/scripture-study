# D&C 2 — Elijah, the promises to the fathers, and why the earth isn't wasted

**Setting:** Moroni's words to Joseph Smith, the night of September 21, 1823, in the Smith log home at Palmyra — the same first visit walked at JS—H 1:27-49. Placed as section 2 (positionally, like section 1) because it is the *seed* of the Restoration's temple theology: the first doctrinal content the angel delivered. Three verses; enormous weight.

**Binding idea:** Moroni quotes Malachi 4:5-6 to Joseph — but *changed* — and the changes name the whole sealing/temple/genealogy project: Elijah comes to **reveal the priesthood** (the sealing power), and to plant "the **promises made to the fathers**" in the hearts of the children, "for if it were not so, the whole earth would be utterly wasted at his coming."

**Read:** "Behold, I will reveal unto you the Priesthood, by the hand of Elijah the prophet, before the coming of the great and dreadful day of the Lord" (2:1). "And he shall plant in the hearts of the children the promises made to the fathers, and the hearts of the children shall turn to their fathers" (2:2). "If it were not so, the whole earth would be utterly wasted at his coming" (2:3).

**Thoughts:**
- *The two changes from KJV Malachi are the doctrine.* Malachi 4:5 says "I will send you Elijah"; D&C 2:1 says "I will **reveal unto you the Priesthood, by the hand of Elijah**." So Elijah's mission is identified as the restoration of the **priesthood** — specifically the *sealing power* (the keys to bind on earth and in heaven, Matt 16:19), which Elijah literally returns in D&C 110:13-16 (Kirtland temple, April 3, 1836). And Malachi 4:6 says "he shall turn the heart of the fathers to the children"; D&C 2:2 says "he shall **plant in the hearts of the children the promises made to the fathers.**" The "promises made to the fathers" are the **Abrahamic covenant** — the seed/land/priesthood/gospel-to-all-nations promises the PoGP walk traced (Abr 2:9-11). So Moroni's first lesson reframes Malachi: the turning of hearts isn't sentiment; it's the *covenant of the fathers* taking root in the children, binding the generations into one chain.
- *"Utterly wasted" tells you the stakes (2:3).* Why would the whole earth be "utterly wasted" without Elijah's sealing? Because the *purpose* of the earth (Abr 3:24-25; Moses 1:39) is to gather an eternal family — and a family that cannot be *sealed* across death is no eternal family at all, just a sequence of disconnected lives. Without the sealing power, the living and the dead stay severed, the covenant chain back to Adam and forward to the last generation never closes, and the entire creation fails of its purpose. The sealing isn't a temple add-on; it is the thing that *saves the creation from being pointless.* That's an astonishing weight to hang on three verses: no sealing power, no eternal families, and the earth itself is wasted.
- *This is where the whole temple project begins.* Genealogy, baptism for the dead (D&C 128), eternal marriage (D&C 132), the welding link of "a whole and complete and perfect union… of dispensations" (D&C 128:18) — all of it flows from these three verses Moroni recited to a 17-year-old. The Restoration's most distinctive practice (proxy ordinances for the dead) has its taproot in Moroni's *first* doctrinal word.

**Critical analysis / tensions named:**
- *The JST-style change to a canonical OT text.* Moroni delivers Malachi *altered* — and the alteration is doctrinally load-bearing (priesthood + promises-to-the-fathers). The faithful reading: the angel restores Malachi's fuller meaning (the same accommodation-and-restoration principle as D&C 1:24 / the JST). The critical reading: it's a revision of scripture to fit later doctrine. Naming it: the change is deliberate and is the point — Moroni isn't quoting Malachi for memory's sake; he's *interpreting* it as he delivers it.
- *Positional placement (1823 content as section 2).* Like section 1, this is early content placed for foundational importance, not chronology. Hold that pattern.
