# D&C 79 — the Comforter will teach him the way

**Setting:** **Hiram, Ohio, March 12, 1832.** A four-verse revelation calling **Jared Carter** to "go again into the eastern countries… proclaiming glad tidings of great joy."

**Binding idea:** "I will send upon him the Comforter, which shall teach him the truth and the way whither he shall go" (79:2). The missionary is not sent with a map but with the *Comforter* — who teaches both *the truth* (what to say) and *the way* (where to go).

**Read:** "It is my will that my servant Jared Carter should go again into the eastern countries, from place to place… proclaiming glad tidings of great joy, even the everlasting gospel" (79:1). "I will send upon him the Comforter, which shall teach him the truth and the way whither he shall go" (79:2). "Inasmuch as he is faithful, I will crown him again with sheaves" (79:3). "Let your heart be glad, my servant Jared Carter, and fear not, saith your Lord, even Jesus Christ" (79:4).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "The Comforter… shall teach him the truth and the way whither he shall go" (79:2).* The Comforter is given a *double* office here: He teaches "the truth" — the *content* of the message — *and* "the way whither he shall go" — the *direction* of the mission. (The §75:27 promise ["be made known from on high, even by the Comforter, whither they shall go"] personalized: the missionary's *itinerary* is the Spirit's to give, not just his doctrine. The Comforter is both *teacher* of truth and *guide* of steps — the §8:2 mind-and-heart revelation governing not only what one believes but where one walks. Jared Carter is sent without a plotted route precisely because the route is *the Spirit's to reveal as he goes* — the agency-by-the-Spirit pattern [§58:26 / §62:8 — "according to judgment and the directions of the Spirit"]. Sent with a Comforter, not a map.)
- *"Let your heart be glad… and fear not" (79:4).* The four-verse call ends, as these do, with the antidote: gladness and the absence of fear. (The fear-not thread [§67:3 fear blocks the blessing; §68:6 be of good cheer; §75:6 McLellin chastened *for* murmuring] sounded gently over a single faithful man: be *glad*, don't *fear*. The crown is conditional — "inasmuch as he is faithful, I will crown him again with sheaves" [79:3, §31:5/§75:5] — but the posture is *joy*, not anxiety. Sent with the Comforter, crowned for faithfulness, told to be glad: the whole shape of a faithful sending in four verses.)
