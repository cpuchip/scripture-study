# D&C 127 — deep water is what I am wont to swim in; recorded on earth, recorded in heaven

**Setting:** **Nauvoo, Illinois, September 1, 1842** — an epistle from Joseph to the Saints, written while *in hiding*. His enemies in Missouri and Illinois were again pursuing him (the Boggs assassination attempt had been blamed on him); he had "left the place for a short season, for my own safety and the safety of this people" (127:1). From hiding, he writes two things: a testimony that he *glories* in tribulation, and the first instruction on *keeping records* of baptisms for the dead.

**Binding idea:** Two: **"Deep water is what I am wont to swim in… I feel, like Paul, to glory in tribulation" (127:2)** — the §122 "experience" doctrine now spoken in Joseph's *own* voice, from the receiving end. And **"in all your recordings it may be recorded in heaven; whatsoever you bind on earth, may be bound in heaven" (127:7)** — the earthly record *is* the heavenly record; the §123 record-as-duty deepened into the binding of heaven and earth (the seed of §128).

**Read:**
- From hiding: "my enemies… were again in the pursuit of me; and inasmuch as they pursue me without a cause… I have thought it expedient and wisdom in me to leave the place for a short season, for my own safety and the safety of this people" (127:1).
- Glorying in tribulation: "And as for the perils which I am called to pass through, they seem but a small thing to me, as the envy and wrath of man have been my common lot all the days of my life… But nevertheless, deep water is what I am wont to swim in. It all has become a second nature to me; and I feel, like Paul, to glory in tribulation; for to this day has the God of my fathers delivered me out of them all, and will deliver me from henceforth… I shall triumph over all my enemies, for the Lord God hath spoken it" (127:2). "Let all the saints rejoice, therefore, and be exceedingly glad; for Israel's God is their God, and he will mete out a just recompense of reward upon the heads of all their oppressors" (127:3).
- Redoubled diligence: "Let the work of my temple, and all the works which I have appointed unto you, be continued on and not cease; and let your diligence, and your perseverance, and patience, and your works be redoubled, and you shall in nowise lose your reward… For all this there is a reward in heaven" (127:4).
- The record binds heaven and earth: "When any of you are baptized for your dead, let there be a recorder, and let him be eye-witness of your baptisms; let him hear with his ears, that he may testify of a truth" (127:6). "That in all your recordings it may be recorded in heaven; whatsoever you bind on earth, may be bound in heaven; whatsoever you loose on earth, may be loosed in heaven" (127:7). "And again, let all the records be had in order, that they may be put in the archives of my holy temple, to be held in remembrance from generation to generation" (127:9).

**Thoughts:**

- *★ "Deep water is what I am wont to swim in" — §122 in Joseph's own voice (127:2).* The man to whom §122 said "all these things shall give thee experience" now answers it from inside the affliction. (Three years after Liberty Jail, hunted again, he does not merely *endure* the perils — he says they "seem but a small thing to me," that tribulation "has become a **second nature**," and that he feels "like Paul, to **glory** in tribulation" [Phil's "rejoice in the Lord," 2 Cor 11-12, Rom 5:3]. This is the §122 curriculum *graduated*: affliction first *given* as a teacher [§122:7], now *metabolized* into a man's character — "second nature." The fear-not thread [§3:7/§122:9] reaches its furthest point: not the absence of fear but its *transmutation into glory* — "deep water is what I am wont to swim in." For the project's theology of trial: the affliction that §122 promised would "give experience" is here shown *having done so* — the prophet hunted into hiding writes not a complaint but a doxology. The evidence that the curriculum works is a man who *swims* in the deep water by second nature.)

- *★ Recorded on earth, recorded in heaven — the §123 record becomes the binding (127:6-9).* The instruction on baptism-for-the-dead records is, underneath, a doctrine of *what a faithful record is*. "Let there be a recorder, and let him be **eye-witness**… let him **hear with his ears**, that he may **testify of a truth**" (127:6) — the record must be made by a true witness — "**that in all your recordings it may be recorded in heaven; whatsoever you bind on earth, may be bound in heaven**" (127:7). (The §123 "let no man count them as small things" given its mechanism: the earthly record is not a *copy* of a heavenly one — under the priesthood keys [Matt 16:19], the act of *recording on earth* is what *binds in heaven*. The record is *constitutive*, not merely commemorative. And it must be *true* — made by an eye-witness who heard with his ears — because a false record would bind a falsehood. For the project's verification covenant: this is read-before-quoting at its deepest root — the record's *truth* is load-bearing because the record *binds*; the recorder must be an "eye-witness" who can "testify of a truth," not a confabulator [§93:24-25 the liar's "more or less"]. The journals, the ledger, the verified quote — "had in order… in the archives of my holy temple, to be held in remembrance from generation to generation" [127:9] — are 127:7 in the project's idiom: the faithful, true record kept so that what is bound on earth holds in heaven. Forward to §128, which expands this whole doctrine.)

- *Redoubled diligence under pursuit (127:4).* Even hunted, the charge is *more*, not less: "let the work of my temple… be continued on and not cease; and let your diligence… be **redoubled**" (127:4). (The §123:17 cheerfully-do-all under the §124:49 hindrance: the enemy's pursuit is not a reason to slacken but to *redouble* — "you shall in nowise lose your reward." The work continues *through* the persecution, the diligence rising to meet the opposition.)
