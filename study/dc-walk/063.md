# D&C 63 — signs come by faith, not faith by signs

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, August 30, 1831** — three days after Joseph, Sidney, and Oliver returned from Missouri. "In these infant days of the Church, there was a great anxiety to obtain the word of the Lord upon every subject"; Joseph inquired about the gathering, the purchase of land, and "other matters." The result is a wide-ranging revelation: a rebuke of *sign-seeking*, a warning to the adulterous, the promise of the *transfigured earth*, the command to obtain Zion *by purchase not blood*, and a redefinition of *taking the Lord's name in vain*.

**Binding idea:** "He that seeketh signs shall see signs, but not unto salvation… **faith cometh not by signs, but signs follow those that believe**. Yea, signs come by faith, not by the will of men… but by the will of God" (63:7-10). The order is fixed and irreversible: faith *first*, signs *after*. The sign-seeker has it backwards — and gets signs only "in wrath unto their condemnation" (63:11).

**Read:** "He that seeketh signs shall see signs, but not unto salvation" (63:7); "faith cometh not by signs, but signs follow those that believe. Yea, signs come by faith, not by the will of men, nor as they please, but by the will of God" (63:9-10); "without faith no man pleaseth God; and with whom God is angry he is not well pleased; wherefore, unto such he showeth no signs, only in wrath unto their condemnation" (63:11); "I, the Lord, am not pleased with those among you who have sought after signs and wonders for faith, and not for the good of men unto my glory" (63:12). On adultery: "he that looketh on a woman to lust after her, or if any shall commit adultery in their hearts, they shall not have the Spirit, but shall deny the faith and shall fear" (63:16). "He that endureth in faith and doeth my will… shall receive an inheritance upon the earth when the day of transfiguration shall come; when the earth shall be transfigured, even according to the pattern which was shown unto mine apostles upon the mount; of which account the fulness ye have not yet received" (63:20-21). "Unto him that keepeth my commandments I will give the mysteries of my kingdom, and the same shall be in him a well of living water, springing up unto everlasting life" (63:23). "The land of Zion—I, the Lord, hold it in mine own hands; nevertheless, I, the Lord, render unto Cæsar the things which are Cæsar's" (63:25-26); "the land of Zion shall not be obtained but by purchase or by blood… and if by purchase, behold you are blessed; and if by blood, as you are forbidden to shed blood… ye shall be scourged from city to city" (63:29-31). Newel Whitney: "these things are in his own hands, let him do according to wisdom" (63:44). Sidney Rigdon "exalted himself in his heart, and received not counsel, but grieved the Spirit; wherefore his writing is not acceptable… and he shall make another" (63:55-56). "Let all men beware how they take my name in their lips—for… many there be who… use the name of the Lord, and use it in vain, having not authority" (63:61-62); "that which cometh from above is sacred, and must be spoken with care, and by constraint of the Spirit" (63:64). "These things remain to overcome through patience, that such may receive a more exceeding and eternal weight of glory" (63:66).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ Signs come by faith, not faith by signs (63:7-12) — the order is irreversible.* This is the sharpest statement in the canon of the walk's recurring epistemology: faith is the *cause*, signs the *effect*, never the reverse. "Faith cometh not by signs, but signs follow those that believe" (63:9). The sign-*seeker* has the order backwards — he wants a sign *to produce* faith, and that's impossible: "signs come by faith… by the will of God" (63:10), so a sign demanded *before* faith is no sign of salvation at all. Worse, the sign-seeker who does get a sign gets it "in wrath unto their condemnation" (63:11) — like the Three Witnesses' sight that leaves the hardened *accountable* (§5:18). And the *motive* test is exact: signs are given "for the good of men unto my glory," not "for faith" (63:12) — a sign sought *to manufacture belief* is sought wrongly. (This is the §5 [proof can't compel faith] → §17 [faith precedes sight] → §63 [signs come BY faith] progression at its sharpest: the entire Restoration epistemology in one law. You cannot reason or be signed *into* faith; faith is the prior condition, and the signs *follow* it, by God's will, for others' good. The period-language/Spirit-distillation thread's foundation: the Spirit distills light on the *believing* mind; the demand "show me first" forecloses the very faith that would make the showing meaningful. For an age — and a project — tempted to *verify into belief*, the order stands: faith first, then the sign follows.)
- *★ The earth transfigured — the Mount of Transfiguration as the pattern (63:20-21).* A stunning matter-spectrum doctrine: the faithful "shall receive an inheritance upon the earth **when the day of transfiguration shall come; when the earth shall be transfigured**, even according to the **pattern which was shown unto mine apostles upon the mount**" (Matt 17, the Transfiguration). The *earth itself* will be *transfigured* — glorified, raised to a higher state — and the Mount of Transfiguration (where Christ shone, Moses and Elijah appeared) was a *preview of the earth's destiny*. (The matter-spectrum's cosmology: not only the *body* is resurrected and the *individual* glorified [§29, §35:2], but the *planet* is transfigured — matter raised to its glorified state, "all things become new" [63:49]. The earth is on the same trajectory as the soul: §50:24's "light groweth brighter… until the perfect day" applied to the *globe*. And "of which account the fulness ye have not yet received" [63:21] — the Transfiguration's full meaning is *withheld* [§25:4], a reserved temple-adjacent thread: what happened on that mount is a pattern of the world's end not yet fully disclosed. I flag the withheld Transfiguration account as a reserved thread, Michael's.)
- *★ Zion by purchase, not blood (63:25-31) — the presiding/persuasion principle at the scale of a homeland.* "The land of Zion shall not be obtained but by purchase or by blood… and **if by purchase, behold you are blessed**; and if by blood, as you are forbidden to shed blood… ye shall be scourged" (63:29-31). Zion is to be acquired *lawfully and peacefully* — *bought*, with legal title ("render unto Cæsar," 63:26), so the existing settlers "may not be stirred up unto anger" (63:27). The Lord *owns* the land (63:25) and could give it by force — but commands its acquisition by *purchase*, not violence. (This is the presiding covenant's "persuasion, not compulsion" [121:41] at the scale of *founding a homeland*: even the promised land is to be gotten *without shedding blood*, by lawful purchase, lest the kingdom become a thing seized by force. The §44 lawful-walls and §58:21 obey-the-laws-of-the-land carried to the very acquisition of Zion. Tragically, the Saints were driven out of Jackson County in 1833 anyway — but the *command* was peaceful, lawful purchase; the violence came *from* their enemies, not from them. Zion built by force would not be Zion.)
- *★ Taking the name in vain = using His name/authority without having it (63:61-64).* A redefinition of the third commandment. "Taking the name in vain" is usually read as profanity; here it's *using the Lord's name without authority*: "many there be who… use the name of the Lord, and use it **in vain, having not authority**" (63:62). To invoke God's name — to claim to speak or act *for* Him — without the authority to do so is to take His name *in vain* (emptily, falsely). "That which cometh from above is sacred, and must be spoken with care, and by constraint of the Spirit" (63:64). (This deepens the §18 name-theology and the §28/§43 order: the name of Christ is *power*, and using it without authority is not just careless but *condemning*. The connection to the false-revelator thread [§28, §43] is exact — the one who claims revelation without the appointed authority is *taking the name in vain*. And it's a word to *everyone*: "let all men beware how they take my name in their lips" — invoking God lightly, claiming His sanction without His Spirit's constraint, empties the name. Speak from above only "by constraint of the Spirit," or you take the name in vain.)
- *The mysteries to the keeper; the agency again; Sidney rebuked.* Three more threads: (1) "unto him that keepeth my commandments I will give the **mysteries** of my kingdom… a well of living water" (63:23) — the mysteries are given to the *obedient*, not the curious (§6:11, §42:61; John 4:14); knowledge follows obedience. (2) "These things are in his own hands, let him do according to wisdom" (63:44, Newel Whitney) — the *agency* refrain yet again (§58:26/§60:5/§61:22). (3) Sidney Rigdon, the prophet's counselor, "exalted himself in his heart, and received not counsel, but grieved the Spirit; wherefore his writing is not acceptable… and he shall make another" (63:55-56) — even the *prophet's right hand* is rebuked for pride, his work *rejected*, told to *redo it* (the canon's honesty, §3/§20:5; pride grieves the Spirit, §121:37).
