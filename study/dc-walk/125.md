# D&C 125 — the Saints in Iowa: build up cities

**Setting:** **Nauvoo, Illinois, March 1841** — a four-verse word answering "What is the will of the Lord concerning the saints in the Territory of Iowa?" (125:1). Across the Mississippi from Nauvoo, Saints were gathering on the Iowa side; the Lord directs them to build cities and take up inheritances in the appointed stakes.

**Binding idea:** "If they will do my will and keep my commandments concerning them, let them gather themselves together unto the places which I shall appoint… and build up cities unto my name, that they may be prepared for that which is in store for a time to come" (125:2). Gather, build, and be *prepared* — the same gathering-as-readiness the walk has tracked since §29/§37.

**Read:** "What is the will of the Lord concerning the saints in the Territory of Iowa?" (125:1). "Verily, thus saith the Lord… if those who call themselves by my name and are essaying to be my saints, if they will do my will and keep my commandments concerning them, let them gather themselves together unto the places which I shall appoint unto them by my servant Joseph, and build up cities unto my name, that they may be prepared for that which is in store for a time to come" (125:2). "Let them build up a city unto my name upon the land opposite the city of Nauvoo, and let the name of Zarahemla be named upon it" (125:3). "And let all those who come from the east, and the west, and the north, and the south, that have desires to dwell therein, take up their inheritance in the same… and in all the stakes which I have appointed" (125:4).

**Thoughts:**
- *Gather to be prepared — readiness, not refuge only (125:2).* The gathering is "that they may be prepared for that which is in store for a time to come." (The §29:8/§37:3 gathering-as-readiness reaffirmed at the Nauvoo scale: the Saints assemble not merely to escape but to *prepare* — the city built now against a future "in store." The §101 keep-the-watch posture in civic form: build in the time of peace for what is coming.)
- *Zarahemla — a Book of Mormon name on a living city (125:3).* The Lord directs the Iowa city be named *Zarahemla* (the Nephite capital, Omni 1:14). (A small but striking move: the sacred *past* named onto the gathering *present* — the §115 principle [a name declares whose the thing is] extended to *continuity*; the latter-day gathering consciously bound to the ancient covenant people whose record the Saints carry. The naming makes the new city an heir, not a stranger.)
