# D&C 93 — the glory of God is intelligence, light and truth

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, May 6, 1833.** No special crisis is named — the heading is bare. But the revelation that came is one of the most metaphysically dense in the canon: Christ's own progression "from grace to grace," the eternal nature of intelligence, the definition of truth, the body as a temple, and the verse the project's own *truth.md* is built on — "the glory of God is intelligence… light and truth."

**Binding idea:** "The glory of God is intelligence, or, in other words, light and truth" (93:36). God's *glory* — what makes God God — is *intelligence*, which is *light and truth*; and the whole revelation is the unfolding of how a soul grows in that light and truth, "from grace to grace," toward a fulness.

**Read:** "Every soul who forsaketh his sins and cometh unto me, and calleth on my name, and obeyeth my voice, and keepeth my commandments, shall see my face and know that I am" (93:1). "I am the true light that lighteth every man that cometh into the world" (93:2). "John… saw and bore record… I, John, saw that he received not of the fulness at the first, but received grace for grace; and he received not of the fulness at first, but continued from grace to grace, until he received a fulness; and thus he was called the Son of God, because he received not of the fulness at the first" (93:6,12-14). "If you keep my commandments you shall receive of his fulness, and be glorified in me as I am in the Father; therefore… you shall receive grace for grace" (93:20). "Ye were also in the beginning with the Father; that which is Spirit, even the Spirit of truth; and truth is knowledge of things as they are, and as they were, and as they are to come" (93:23-24). "He that keepeth his commandments receiveth truth and light, until he is glorified in truth and knoweth all things" (93:28). "Man was also in the beginning with God. Intelligence, or the light of truth, was not created or made, neither indeed can be" (93:29). "All truth is independent in that sphere in which God has placed it, to act for itself, as all intelligence also; otherwise there is no existence. Behold, here is the agency of man" (93:30-31). "For man is spirit. The elements are eternal, and spirit and element, inseparably connected, receive a fulness of joy; and when separated, man cannot receive a fulness of joy" (93:33-34). "The elements are the tabernacle of God; yea, man is the tabernacle of God, even temples; and whatsoever temple is defiled, God shall destroy that temple" (93:35). "The glory of God is intelligence, or, in other words, light and truth. Light and truth forsake that evil one" (93:36-37). "But I have commanded you to bring up your children in light and truth" (93:40). "I will call you friends, for you are my friends, and ye shall have an inheritance with me— I called you servants for the world's sake" (93:45-46). "Hasten to translate my scriptures, and to obtain a knowledge of history, and of countries, and of kingdoms, of laws of God and man, and all this for the salvation of Zion" (93:53).

**Thoughts:**
- *★★ "The glory of God is intelligence… light and truth" (93:36).* The verse the project's *truth.md* is built on, and the keystone of the whole light-and-truth thread. "The glory of God is intelligence, or, in other words, light and truth." (What makes God *God* — His *glory* — is not power-as-domination or majesty-as-distance but **intelligence**, immediately glossed as **light and truth**. The §84:45 chain [truth = light = Spirit = Christ] and the §88:6-13 cosmic Light of Christ [the light that fills space and governs all things] arrive here at their *theological* summit: God's very glory *is* this light-and-truth, this intelligence. So to grow in light and truth is to grow in *God's own glory* — the §50:24 "light groweth brighter… until the perfect day," the §76:58 "they are gods," and the §88 "comprehendeth all things" all rest on *this*: glory and intelligence and light and truth are *one thing*, and it is what God is made of. "Light and truth forsake that evil one" [93:37] — and the evil one is precisely the *absence* of light and truth, the un-glory. For the project: this is the deepest grounding of why *truth* matters — truth is not merely useful or accurate; truth is *light*, is *intelligence*, is *the glory of God*. To pursue truth is to pursue God's own substance.)
- *★★ "Intelligence… was not created or made, neither indeed can be" (93:29-31).* The doctrine that *grounds agency* in the nature of being. "Man was also in the beginning with God. Intelligence, or the light of truth, was not created or made, neither indeed can be" (93:29) — and "all truth is independent in that sphere in which God has placed it, **to act for itself**, as all intelligence also; otherwise there is no existence. Behold, **here is the agency of man**" (93:30-31). (A staggering claim: the core of you — your *intelligence*, the "light of truth" that you are — is *uncreated*, co-eternal with God, not made and *not makeable*. God did not *manufacture* your essential self; He *organized* and *exalts* an intelligence that always was. And because intelligence is *self-acting* by its very nature — "to act for itself… otherwise there is no existence" — *agency is built into the structure of being*: you are free not by divine permission alone but because a self-acting intelligence *is what you fundamentally are*. The §29:36-39 / §58:26-28 agency doctrine reaches its *metaphysical root*: agency is not a gift bolted onto a creature but the *nature* of an eternal, self-acting intelligence. This dissolves the problem of a God who creates beings ex nihilo and then judges them: God did *not* create the raw self; He invites an eternal intelligence to grow in light and truth, freely. For the project's whole stewardship/agency framework [the §58:26 source]: §93:29-31 is the *floor under the floor* — agency is real because the agent is, at core, an uncreated self-acting intelligence.)
- *★★ Grace for grace — even Christ grew to a fulness (93:12-20).* John's record: Christ "**received not of the fulness at the first, but received grace for grace**… continued from grace to grace, until he received a fulness; and thus he was called the Son of God, because he received not of the fulness at the first" (93:12-14). And the application: "if you keep my commandments you shall receive of his fulness, and be glorified in me as I am in the Father… **you shall receive grace for grace**" (93:20). (The most consequential clause for the doctrine of *becoming*: even *Christ* did not start with a fulness — He *grew*, "grace for grace," "from grace to grace," until He received a fulness of the Father's glory. The Son of God *became* the Son of God by progression [Luke 2:52 "increased in wisdom and stature"]. And *that* is the pattern for *us*: "you shall receive grace for grace," "be glorified in me as I am in the Father." The deification thread [§76:58, §84:38 "all that my Father hath"] given its *mechanism*: not a sudden leap but *grace for grace* — grace received and then *given onward*, light upon light, the §50:24 growth-law as the very road Christ Himself walked. "Grace for grace" — each grace received becomes a grace extended, and the giving draws down more. For Michael: the staggering comfort that even the Savior grew into His fulness; you are not failing because you don't have it all now — you are *on the road He walked*, grace for grace, and the keeping of commandments is how the next grace comes.)
- *★ "Truth is knowledge of things as they are, and as they were, and as they are to come" (93:24).* The canonical *definition of truth* — and a project anchor. "Truth is knowledge of things as they are, and as they were, and as they are to come; and whatsoever is more or less than this is the spirit of that wicked one who was a liar from the beginning" (93:24-25). (Truth is *correspondence to reality* across all time — things *as they actually are*, were, and will be. And the lie is anything "*more or less* than this" — exaggeration *and* diminishment are both falsehood [the §1:24/§67 honest-source thread, the §85 still-small-voice, the verification discipline]: to add to the truth or subtract from it is the spirit of "the liar from the beginning." For the project's verification covenant [read-before-quoting, no confabulation, "more or less" is fabrication]: 93:24-25 is the *definition* under it — truth is things as they are, and "close-enough" that is *more or less than this* is, precisely, the liar's spirit. The Webster-walk's "close wording is fabrication" and the cite-count discipline are 93:24-25 enforced. Truth admits no inflation and no shrinkage.)
- *★ Spirit and element, the body as temple — fulness of joy (93:33-35).* "For man is spirit. The elements are eternal, and **spirit and element, inseparably connected, receive a fulness of joy; and when separated, man cannot receive a fulness of joy**" (93:33-34). "The elements are the tabernacle of God; yea, man is the tabernacle of God, even temples; and whatsoever temple is defiled, God shall destroy that temple" (93:35). (The matter-spectrum's *anthropology of joy*: the body is not a prison the spirit escapes [Platonism] — *spirit and element together* are the condition of "a fulness of joy," and *separation* [death] *diminishes* joy until the resurrection reunites them. The §88:15 "the spirit and the body are the soul of man" given its *value*: the elements are *eternal* [the §77:2 spirit-in-the-likeness; matter not annihilated, §29:25], and the embodied soul is the only vessel of *full* joy. And "man is the tabernacle of God, even temples" [1 Cor 3:16-17] — the body is a *temple*, sacred, and "whatsoever temple is defiled, God shall destroy" [the §89 Word of Wisdom's ground — the body guarded because it's a temple]. The matter-spectrum's life-affirmation at its peak: the physical body is *essential to joy* and *holy as a temple* — never to be despised or escaped, but kept and, finally, glorified.)
- *★ Bring up your children in light and truth — and the leaders rebuked (93:38-50).* The application turns personal and severe. "Every spirit of man was innocent in the beginning… men became again, in their infant state, innocent before God" [93:38, the §29:46/§74:7 children-redeemed], but "that wicked one cometh and taketh away light and truth… through disobedience… and because of the tradition of their fathers" [93:39]. "**I have commanded you to bring up your children in light and truth**" [93:40, the §68:25 parental teaching duty in light-and-truth terms]. And then the Lord *rebukes the leaders by name*: Frederick Williams "you have not taught your children light and truth… set in order your own house" [93:41-43]; Sidney [93:44]; **Joseph himself** — "you have not kept the commandments, and must needs stand rebuked before the Lord… your family must needs repent" [93:47-48]; Newel Whitney [93:50]. (The canon's honesty [include_failures, §3/§20:5] at its most pointed: in the *same revelation* that gives the loftiest metaphysics [intelligence, grace for grace, the glory of God], the Lord *rebukes the prophet and the entire First Presidency for failing to teach their own children*. The grand doctrine and the homely failure sit in one section: "the glory of God is intelligence" [93:36] *and* "Joseph, you have not kept the commandments" [93:47]. The teaching of *light and truth* begins *at home* — and the men who received this towering revelation are told they've neglected it with their own children. For Michael: the loftiest truth obligates the nearest duty — to "bring up your children in light and truth" — and even the prophet is rebuked for the gap. No height of doctrine excuses the failure at home.)
