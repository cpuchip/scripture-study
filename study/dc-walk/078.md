# D&C 78 — equal in earthly things, that ye may be equal in heavenly

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, March 1, 1832** — Church leaders assembled "to discuss Church business." The revelation organized Joseph, Sidney, and Newel K. Whitney to create a **"firm"** (the United Firm / United Order) to oversee the Church's mercantile and publishing efforts, generating funds for Zion and the poor. (Organized April 1832, disbanded 1834. Later, under Joseph's direction, the wording was changed — "the affairs of the storehouse for the poor" for "literary and mercantile establishments," and "order" for "firm" — to obscure the names during a time of persecution.)

**Binding idea:** "That you may be equal in the bonds of heavenly things, yea, and earthly things also… **For if ye are not equal in earthly things ye cannot be equal in obtaining heavenly things**" (78:5-6). Temporal equality is not a side concern of the gospel — it is the *prerequisite* for spiritual equality. You cannot rise together in heaven if you are divided on earth.

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "If ye are not equal in earthly things ye cannot be equal in obtaining heavenly things" (78:5-7).* The sharpest statement in the canon of the temporal-spiritual *link*. Equality is not merely *commanded* [§38:27 be one, §51 equal-by-need] — here it is made a *condition of celestial inheritance*: "if you will that I give unto you a place in the celestial world, you must prepare yourselves" (78:7), and the preparation includes *earthly* equality, "for the obtaining of heavenly things" (78:5). (This is §70:14 ["be equal… or the manifestations of the Spirit shall be withheld"] raised to its full doctrinal height: economic inequality doesn't just throttle the *Spirit* now — it *bars the celestial world* later. The reason is not arbitrary: the celestial order *is* an order of equality ["he makes them equal in power, and in might, and in dominion," §76:95] — so a people who will not *be* equal on earth are, by their own grasping, unfit for a heaven whose nature is equality. You cannot practice division here and inherit oneness there; the celestial world is not a *reward* bolted onto an unequal life but the *flowering* of a life learned in equality. The consecration thread [§42→§51→§70→§78] reaches its theological ground: equality is celestial *practice*, the earthly rehearsal of the heaven we hope to inherit. For any community — the project included — that aspires to the higher law: you cannot reach the equal heaven on an unequal road.)
- *★ Michael/Adam presides under the Holy One — the presiding chain (78:15-16).* A glimpse of the order of heaven: God "hath appointed Michael your prince… and given unto him the keys of salvation **under the counsel and direction of the Holy One**" (78:16). (Michael — Adam, the §27:11 Ancient of Days — holds the keys of salvation, *presides* over the human family as "your prince" — but the clause is load-bearing: "*under the counsel and direction of the Holy One*" [Christ]. The presiding chain the walk has tracked through the presiding covenant [D&C 121]: even Adam, the greatest of the patriarchs, the prince of the race, presides *under* Christ — his dominion is *delegated*, not autonomous, exercised under direction. The §1:38 voice-of-servants and the whole stewardship architecture: the one who presides is himself *presided over*; authority is held in a chain that runs up to the Holy One "who is without beginning of days or end of life." Adam-ondi-Ahman — the place where Adam will gather his posterity [§107:53, §116] — is the seat of a *delegated* prince. For the project's presiding covenant: the highest steward under God still acts under counsel and direction; no delegated authority is ever sovereign. Michael presides, and is presided over.)
- *★ "Ye cannot bear all things now… I will lead you along" (78:17-18).* The tenderest turn in the section. After the high doctrine [equality, the buffetings of Satan, Michael's keys], the Lord softens: "ye are little children, and ye have not as yet understood how great blessings the Father hath… prepared for you; and ye cannot bear all things now; nevertheless, be of good cheer, for I will lead you along" (78:17-18). (The line-upon-line patience [§50:40 "little children… ye cannot bear all things now"; §76:114 the withheld fulness; John 16:12 "I have yet many things to say… but ye cannot bear them now"]: God does *not* dump the whole truth on the immature; He *leads them along*, paced to their capacity [the §67:11 capacity-to-bear thread, gentled]. "I will lead you along" — not drive, not abandon, but *lead*, at the pace the child can walk. And the reassurance is lavish: "the kingdom is yours… the riches of eternity are yours" [78:18] — the children who can't yet bear all things are nonetheless *heirs of everything*. For anyone overwhelmed by how much they don't yet understand: you are not expected to bear it all now; He leads you along, and the kingdom is *already* yours. The §10:4 pace-to-strength applied to *revelation itself* — God paces the unfolding to the receiver.)
- *Gratitude, and the faithful steward inherits all (78:19,22).* Two closing notes: "he who receiveth all things with thankfulness shall be made glorious" [78:19, the §59:7/§62:7 gratitude thread — thanksgiving is the road to glory], and "he that is a faithful and wise steward shall inherit all things" [78:22, §72:4/Matt 24:45 — the faithful steward, the §76:59 "all things are theirs"]. (The two virtues that crown the section: *gratitude* [receive with thankfulness → made glorious] and *stewardship* [faithful and wise → inherit all]. They're the same posture from two angles — the grateful steward who holds everything as a gift to be managed faithfully *becomes glorious* and *inherits all*. The consecration order [§78] runs on grateful, faithful stewards — exactly the project's pattern.)

**Notable / flag:** No spin-off. **Walk's contribution:** **§78 makes temporal equality the prerequisite for the celestial world — "if ye are not equal in earthly things ye cannot be equal in obtaining heavenly things" (78:5-7).** This is the §70:14 principle raised to its full height: economic inequality doesn't merely throttle the Spirit *now* — it *bars the celestial world* later, because the celestial order *is* an order of equality ("he makes them equal in power, and in might, and in dominion," §76:95), so a people who will not be equal on earth are unfit for a heaven whose nature is oneness. The consecration thread (§42→§51→§70→§78) reaches its theological ground: equality is *celestial practice*, the earthly rehearsal of the heaven we hope to inherit — you cannot reach the equal heaven on an unequal road. Plus the **presiding chain — Michael presides under the Holy One (78:16)** — even Adam, the prince of the human family, holds the keys of salvation "under the counsel and direction of the Holy One" (Christ): the highest delegated authority is never sovereign; the one who presides is himself presided over (the presiding covenant, D&C 121). And the **tender line-upon-line** — "ye cannot bear all things now; nevertheless, be of good cheer, for I will lead you along" (78:17-18): God paces revelation to capacity, never dumping the whole truth on the immature, while the children who can't yet bear it are heirs of everything anyway ("the kingdom is yours… the riches of eternity are yours"). The section crowns its order with gratitude ("receiveth all things with thankfulness shall be made glorious," §59:7) and stewardship ("a faithful and wise steward shall inherit all things," §72:4). For Michael: the section that says you can't inherit an equal heaven while clinging to an unequal earth — that even Adam presides *under* Christ — and that you're not expected to bear it all now; He leads you along, and the kingdom is already yours.
