# D&C 94 — my glory shall be there, if no unclean thing come in

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, August 2, 1833** — outlining the layout of Kirtland, "a stake of Zion," and appointing Hyrum Smith, Reynolds Cahoon, and Jared Carter as a Church building committee. The revelation assigns lots: a house for the Presidency, a printing house, and inheritances — all "according to the pattern."

**Binding idea:** "Ye shall not suffer any unclean thing to come in unto it; and my glory shall be there, and my presence shall be there. But if there shall come into it any unclean thing, my glory shall not be there; and my presence shall not come into it" (94:8-9). The house draws God's presence *only if kept holy*; an unclean thing drives the glory out.

**Read:** "Commence a work of laying out and preparing a beginning and foundation of the city of the stake of Zion, here in the land of Kirtland, beginning at my house" (94:1). "It must be done according to the pattern which I have given unto you" (94:2). "Let the first lot on the south be consecrated unto me for the building of a house for the presidency, for the work of the presidency, in obtaining revelations" (94:3). "It shall be dedicated unto the Lord from the foundation thereof, according to the order of the priesthood" (94:6). "Ye shall not suffer any unclean thing to come in unto it; and my glory shall be there, and my presence shall be there" (94:8). "But if there shall come into it any unclean thing, my glory shall not be there; and my presence shall not come into it" (94:9). The printing house "shall be wholly dedicated unto the Lord… to be holy, undefiled, according to the pattern" (94:12).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ The presence conditioned on holiness (94:8-9).* The house of the Presidency — built for "the work of the presidency, in obtaining revelations" (94:3) — is promised God's *glory and presence* on one condition: "ye shall not suffer any unclean thing to come in." (The §93:35 "whatsoever temple is defiled, God shall destroy" applied to the *building*: a holy place is holy *by exclusion of the unclean*, and God's presence is *conditional* on that holiness — "if there shall come into it any unclean thing, my glory shall not be there." The principle isn't ritual fussiness; it's that *God's presence and uncleanness cannot share a space*. The temple-presence thread [§88:8 the glory fills the house; §97:15-16; §110 the Lord appears] rests on this: the glory comes *to the clean place* and *withdraws from the defiled*. For the project's own "house" — the workspace, the record, the work: a holy thing draws the Spirit *by being kept clean*; let an unclean thing in, and the presence withdraws. Holiness is a condition, not a decoration.)
- *Built "according to the pattern" — by revelation, not preference (94:2,6).* Twice the houses are to be built "according to the pattern which I have given" / "which shall be given unto you hereafter" (94:2,5,6). (The §52:14 / §88:119 pattern thread: the house of God is not designed by human taste but *revealed* — "according to the order of the priesthood" [94:6]. The same in §95:14 [the temple's pattern "shown unto three of you"]. God's house is built *to His shown pattern*, not the builders' improvisation — order received, not invented.)
