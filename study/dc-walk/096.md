# D&C 96 — that my word should go forth

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, June 4, 1833** — a conference of high priests deadlocked over who should take charge of "the French farm" the Church owned near Kirtland. Unable to agree, "all agreed to inquire of the Lord." The answer assigns the stewardship, divides the land for inheritances, and brings John Johnson into the united order.

**Binding idea:** "This is the most expedient in me, that my word should go forth unto the children of men, for the purpose of subduing the hearts of the children of men for your good" (96:5). The disposal of a farm is settled by reference to *the one purpose*: that God's *word* go forth — and the land, the order, the labor all serve that.

**Read:** "It is expedient in me that this stake that I have set for the strength of Zion should be made strong" (96:1). "Let my servant Newel K. Whitney take charge of the place… upon which I design to build mine holy house" (96:2). "Let it be divided into lots, according to wisdom, for the benefit of those who seek inheritances, as it shall be determined in council among you" (96:3). "This is the most expedient in me, that my word should go forth unto the children of men, for the purpose of subduing the hearts of the children of men for your good" (96:5). John Johnson "is a descendant of Joseph and a partaker of the blessings of the promise made unto his fathers" (96:7); "it is expedient in me that he should become a member of the order, that he may assist in bringing forth my word" (96:8).

(The §28 order-of-revelation applied to a *committee deadlock*: when human counsel stalls, the resolution comes by inquiring of the Lord and *re-anchoring on the purpose*. "Determined in council among you" [96:3] — the inquiry doesn't replace the council; it *orders* it, giving the council its anchor. For the project's own deadlocks: the resolution is to *re-anchor on the purpose* — what serves the mission? — and the council, so anchored, can decide.
