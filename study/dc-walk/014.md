# D&C 14 — the Creator calls a witness

**Setting:** **Fayette, New York, June 1829.** The translation had moved from Harmony to the farmhouse of **Peter Whitmer Sr.**, where Joseph finished the Book of Mormon. Three Whitmer sons — David, John, and Peter Jr. — believing and eager, each asked the Lord (through the Urim and Thummim) what their duty was. §14 answers **David Whitmer**; §§15-16 answer John and Peter Jr. David would become one of the **Three Witnesses** (the §5 promise; the formal call comes in §17). Eleven verses: the harvest preface again, then a personal commission and a startling self-identification by Christ.

**Binding idea:** The Lord who calls David to "stand as a witness" (14:8) identifies Himself, in the same breath, as the One "who created the heavens and the earth, a light which cannot be hid in darkness" (14:9). The witness is being recruited by the *Creator* — and a witness to the Creator's work cannot, finally, be hidden any more than the Creator's light can.

**Read:** Harvest preface (14:1-6). "If you keep my commandments and endure to the end you shall have eternal life, which gift is the greatest of all the gifts of God" (14:7). "If you shall ask the Father in my name, in faith believing, you shall receive the Holy Ghost, which giveth utterance, that you may stand as a witness of the things of which you shall both hear and see, and also that you may declare repentance unto this generation" (14:8). "Behold, I am Jesus Christ, the Son of the living God, who created the heavens and the earth, a light which cannot be hid in darkness; wherefore, I must bring forth the fulness of my gospel from the Gentiles unto the house of Israel" (14:9-10). "Thou art David, and thou art called to assist; which thing if ye do, and are faithful, ye shall be blessed both spiritually and temporally, and great shall be your reward" (14:11).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "Jesus Christ… who created the heavens and the earth" (14:9) — the is-Christ-God thread, now in the Restoration's voice.* Across the BoM and PoGP walks, one datapoint kept accumulating: the Old Testament Creator-God (Jehovah) *is* Jesus Christ. §14:9 states it flatly in a casual aside to a young farmer — the One asking David to be a witness is the One who *made the heavens and the earth.* The footnote to 14:9 even points to **Abr 4:12** (the Gods organizing the earth), tying this self-identification straight to the PoGP creation account. The Restoration doesn't argue the doctrine; it has Christ *assume* it. (This is the matter-spectrum's Christology landing in the D&C: the Creator and the Redeemer are one Person, which is why His light "cannot be hid in darkness" — He is the source-light of the creation He's redeeming. Cf. the repeated "light which shineth in darkness," 6:21/10:58, now intensified to "cannot be hid.")
- *The witness "cannot be hid" because the light "cannot be hid" (14:8-9).* There's a deliberate resonance: David is called to *stand as a witness* (visible, public, undeniable), and Christ identifies as a *light which cannot be hid in darkness.* The witness participates in the un-hideable-ness of the One witnessed. A true witness of the Restoration becomes, like its Author, something the darkness cannot finally cover. (David Whitmer's life bears the irony out: he left the Church in 1838 and never returned — yet to his death in 1888 he *never denied* his witness of the plates and the angel; he even had it engraved on his tombstone. The light he witnessed could not be hid, even by his own estrangement. I note this as documented biography, not to resolve his story — bin-4-adjacent, Michael's to weigh.)
- *"Eternal life… the greatest of all the gifts of God" (14:7).* The same phrase as §6:13 ("there is no gift greater than the gift of salvation"). The D&C keeps ranking the gifts and putting *eternal life* at the top — above translation, above seeing angels, above the witness itself. David is offered the supreme gift (eternal life, by enduring) in the same passage that offers him the rare one (to be a witness). The rare gift serves the supreme one; the witness is a means, eternal life the end. (Echoes §5's unique-vs-democratic witness: the *sight* of the plates is rare, but the *eternal life* is the greater gift, available to all who endure.)
- *"From the Gentiles unto the house of Israel" (14:10) — the reversal.* The fulness comes *first* to the Gentiles, who carry it *to* Israel — the inversion the Book of Mormon repeatedly prophesies (1 Ne 13-15, the footnote chain). The Restoration's delivery route runs backwards from the ancient pattern: the gospel goes out through the Gentile nations and gathers scattered Israel last. David's "assist" is a link in that reversed chain.
