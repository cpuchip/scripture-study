# D&C 80 — ye cannot go amiss

**Setting:** **Hiram, Ohio, March 7, 1832.** A five-verse revelation to **Stephen Burnett**, calling him (with Eden Smith as companion) to "go ye into the world and preach the gospel to every creature that cometh under the sound of your voice."

**Binding idea:** "Go ye and preach my gospel, whether to the north or to the south, to the east or to the west, **it mattereth not, for ye cannot go amiss**" (80:3). The *direction* is left entirely to the missionary — because with a true message faithfully declared, there is no wrong way to go.

**Read:** "Go ye, go ye into the world and preach the gospel to every creature that cometh under the sound of your voice" (80:1). "Inasmuch as you desire a companion, I will give unto you my servant Eden Smith" (80:2). "Go ye and preach my gospel, whether to the north or to the south, to the east or to the west, it mattereth not, for ye cannot go amiss" (80:3). "Declare the things which ye have heard, and verily believe, and know to be true" (80:4). "This is the will of him who hath called you, your Redeemer, even Jesus Christ" (80:5).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "It mattereth not… for ye cannot go amiss" (80:3).* The agency-on-the-means refrain at its most freeing. Where to go? "North or south… east or west, it mattereth not, for **ye cannot go amiss**." (The §58:26 / §60:5 / §61:22 / §62:5 agency thread — "it mattereth not unto me" — given its warmest form: not only is the choice the steward's, but *any* choice is *right*. "Ye cannot go amiss" — with a true message and a faithful heart, there is no wrong direction; every road leads to "every creature that cometh under the sound of your voice" [80:1]. This is liberty grounded in *trust*: the Lord doesn't dictate the route because the route doesn't matter — the *message* and the *faithfulness* do. For a steward paralyzed by choosing the "right" path: sometimes the answer is "it mattereth not… ye cannot go amiss" — go, and the going itself, faithfully done, is right. The freedom is real because the message is true everywhere.)
- *★ "Declare the things which ye have heard, and verily believe, and know to be true" (80:4).* The one condition under the total freedom: declare what you *know to be true*. (The obtain-before-declare thread [§11:21 / §42:14 / §68:3-4]: the freedom of direction comes *with* the discipline of content — preach only "the things which ye have heard, and verily believe, and know to be true." The route is open; the message is *fixed* — your own verified witness, not speculation. The §80:3 "ye cannot go amiss" holds *because* §80:4 holds: you can't go wrong geographically as long as you don't go wrong *doctrinally* — declaring what you actually know. Freedom in the means, fidelity in the message: the two clauses are one instruction. The agency the Lord grants on the *how* is bounded by the truth required in the *what*.)

**Notable / flag:** No spin-off. **Walk's contribution:** **§80 gives the agency-on-the-means refrain its warmest form — "it mattereth not… for ye cannot go amiss" (80:3).** Where to preach? North, south, east, west — it doesn't matter, *because you cannot go wrong*. The §58:26/§60:5 "it mattereth not unto me" thread reaches its most freeing point: not only is the choice the steward's, but *any* choice is right — liberty grounded in trust, because with a true message faithfully declared there is no wrong direction (a word for the steward paralyzed by choosing the "right" path: go, and the faithful going is itself right). And the one condition under the total freedom: **"declare the things which ye have heard, and verily believe, and know to be true" (80:4)** — the obtain-before-declare discipline (§11:21/§68:3-4). Freedom in the means, fidelity in the message: the two clauses are one instruction — you cannot go amiss *geographically* precisely because you do not go amiss *doctrinally*, declaring only what you actually know. For Michael: the five-verse call that says when the message is true, every road is the right road — go where you will, and tell what you know.
