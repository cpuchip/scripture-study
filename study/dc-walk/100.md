# D&C 100 — it shall be given you in the very hour

**Setting:** **Perrysburg, New York, October 12, 1833.** Joseph and Sidney, away from home preaching in the East, "having been absent from their families for several days, felt some concern about them." Missouri was in crisis (the Saints being driven from Jackson County), and their own families were nine hundred miles behind them. The revelation begins by answering the worry — and then promises the Spirit's words "in the very hour."

**Binding idea:** "Your families are well; they are in mine hands… for in me there is all power" (100:1). And to the preachers: "it shall be given you in the very hour, yea, in the very moment, what ye shall say" (100:6). God holds the families they left, and gives the words they need — both in His hands.

**Read:** "Your families are well; they are in mine hands, and I will do with them as seemeth me good; for in me there is all power" (100:1). "I have much people in this place… an effectual door shall be opened" (100:3). "Lift up your voices unto this people; speak the thoughts that I shall put into your hearts, and you shall not be confounded before men" (100:5). "For it shall be given you in the very hour, yea, in the very moment, what ye shall say" (100:6). "Declare whatsoever thing ye declare in my name, in solemnity of heart, in the spirit of meekness" (100:7). "Inasmuch as ye do this the Holy Ghost shall be shed forth in bearing record unto all things whatsoever ye shall say" (100:8). "Sidney, [shalt] be a spokesman… and [Joseph] shall be a revelator unto thee" (100:9-11). "Continue your journey and let your hearts rejoice; for behold… I am with you even unto the end" (100:12). "Zion shall be redeemed, although she is chastened for a little season" (100:13). "All things shall work together for good to them that walk uprightly… For I will raise up unto myself a pure people" (100:15-16).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "Your families are well… in mine hands" (100:1).* The first word answers the *worry*, not the mission. Two men anxious about families they left to serve — and the Lord says "your families are well; they are in mine hands… for in me there is all power" (100:1). (The §75:24/§99:6 family-care thread at the *emotional* level: the servant who leaves family to labor is *not* abandoning them — they are "in mine hands." The same God who calls a man away *holds* what he left. For anyone serving at a cost to the near ones: "they are in mine hands" — the leaving is not abandonment when the One who called you keeps what you couldn't. And "I will do with them as seemeth me good" — the surrender includes trusting God's care to be *better* than your anxious presence would be.)
- *★ "It shall be given you in the very hour… what ye shall say" (100:5-8).* The promise of *unrehearsed* utterance: "speak the thoughts that I shall put into your hearts… it shall be given you in the very hour, yea, in the very moment, what ye shall say" (100:5-6, Matt 10:19; §84:85). (The §68:3-4 [Spirit-moved speech is scripture] given its *real-time* form: the faithful servant does not pre-script — the words come "in the very moment," and "the Holy Ghost shall be shed forth in bearing record" [100:8]. But note the *condition* [100:7]: "declare… in solemnity of heart, in the spirit of meekness" — the in-the-hour gift comes to the *meek and solemn*, not the glib. The §85 "treasure up… the words of life" [§84:85] is the *preparation*; the "in the very hour" is the *delivery* — you fill the reservoir continually, and the Spirit draws from it in the moment. For the project working in real time: the words come *as you need them* when the heart is meek and the reservoir is full — not pre-scripted, but Spirit-given in the hour.)
- *Zion redeemed after chastening; a pure people raised up (100:13-16).* The close turns to Zion: "Zion shall be redeemed, although she is chastened for a little season" (100:13), and "all things shall work together for good to them that walk uprightly… For I will raise up unto myself a pure people, that will serve me in righteousness" (100:15-16). (The §97:21 "Zion is the pure in heart" reaffirmed: even as the *place* Zion is being lost [chastened], the Lord promises to "raise up… a pure people" — Zion redeemed not by reclaiming the geography first but by *purifying the people*. The chastening is "for a little season" [§90:36/§122:4 — bounded, toward cleansing], and the redemption is sure. The §98:3 "all things… work together for your good" repeated [100:15, Rom 8:28]: the loss of Jackson County is folded into the raising-up of a pure people. For the discouraged: Zion *shall* be redeemed; the chastening is a season, not the end; and the redemption comes through the *purifying* — the pure people are the redeemed Zion.)
