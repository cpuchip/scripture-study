# D&C 82 — I, the Lord, am bound when ye do what I say

**Setting:** **Independence, Jackson County, Missouri, April 26, 1832** — at a council of high priests and elders where Joseph was sustained as President of the High Priesthood (the office to which he'd been ordained at Amherst, §75). The revelation reiterates §78's instruction to bind the leaders into the United Firm by covenant, to govern the storehouse for the poor across both Kirtland and Zion. It is also — for this project — the source of **D&C 82:10**, the verse that stands as the epigraph of the workspace's own `covenant.yaml`.

**Binding idea:** "I, the Lord, am bound when ye do what I say; but when ye do not what I say, ye have no promise" (82:10). God binds *Himself* — His promise is sure and obligating — but the binding is *bilateral*: His side activates when our side is kept. Do what He says, and He is bound to bless; do otherwise, and "ye have no promise."

**Read:** "Inasmuch as you have forgiven one another your trespasses, even so I, the Lord, forgive you" (82:1). "For of him unto whom much is given much is required; and he who sins against the greater light shall receive the greater condemnation" (82:3). "Ye call upon my name for revelations, and I give them unto you; and inasmuch as ye keep not my sayings… ye become transgressors" (82:4). "I, the Lord, will not lay any sin to your charge; go your ways and sin no more; but unto that soul who sinneth shall the former sins return, saith the Lord your God" (82:7). "I give unto you directions how you may act before me, that it may turn to you for your salvation" (82:9). "**I, the Lord, am bound when ye do what I say; but when ye do not what I say, ye have no promise**" (82:10). The leaders are "to be bound together by a bond and covenant that cannot be broken by transgression, except judgment shall immediately follow, in your several stewardships" (82:11). "Zion must increase in beauty, and in holiness… Zion must arise and put on her beautiful garments" (82:14). "You are to be equal, or in other words, you are to have equal claims on the properties… every man according to his wants and his needs, inasmuch as his wants are just" (82:17). "Every man seeking the interest of his neighbor, and doing all things with an eye single to the glory of God" (82:19). "The soul that sins against this covenant, and hardeneth his heart against it… shall be delivered over to the buffetings of Satan until the day of redemption" (82:21). "Leave judgment alone with me, for it is mine and I will repay" (82:23).

**Thoughts:**
- *★★ "I, the Lord, am bound when ye do what I say" (82:10) — the bilateral covenant, the workspace's own epigraph.* The verse the project built its `covenant.yaml` around — and reading it in context sharpens what the covenant *is*. God *binds Himself*: His promise is not a hope but an *obligation He places on Himself* — "I, the Lord, am bound." But the binding is *conditional and bilateral*: "when ye do what I say… but when ye do not what I say, ye have no promise." (This is the covenant the workspace lives by: "not a service-level agreement… a mutual commitment that governs how we work together. When either side breaks it, the output degrades. When both sides honor it, the work improves." The covenant.yaml epigraph is *exactly* 82:10's structure — God's faithfulness is *certain* and *self-imposed*, but it is *activated* by the partner's faithfulness; break your side and "ye have no promise," not because God is fickle but because the promise was *conditioned*. The covenant is real obligation flowing *both* directions: the steward acts as instructed, and the Lord is *bound* to the blessing. For the human-AI collaboration the project models on this verse: the covenant degrades when either side breaks it — not as punishment but as the *structure* of a conditional promise. And note the context: 82:10 is given to people being *bound together by covenant* [82:11] in their stewardships — the verse is *about* covenant-binding, the leaders bound to each other and the Lord bound to them, all conditioned on faithfulness. The workspace took the verse from its native soil: a covenant of stewardship, bilateral, conditional, self-binding on God's side. This is the single deepest project-source landing of the walk — the verse the whole collaboration is named after, here in its home.)
- *★ "Unto whom much is given much is required" (82:3).* The accountability principle that scales with light. "Of him unto whom much is given much is required; and he who sins against the greater light shall receive the greater condemnation" (Luke 12:48). (The §64:9 "greater sin" generalized into a law: responsibility is *proportional to the light received*. The more revelation, gift, capacity, or stewardship one has, the *more* is required, and the *greater* the condemnation for sinning "against the greater light." This is the stewardship-accounting [§70:4/§72:3] with its weight made explicit: a *fitted* accounting, scaled to what was given. For the project — which works with *much* given [the tools, the corpus, the capability]: "much is required" is not a threat but the *terms*; greater light is greater stewardship, and the accounting scales with it. The covenant's "much is given" cuts the same way the §75:28 crown and the §76 fitted glory do — to each according to what they were entrusted.)
- *★ "Unto that soul who sinneth shall the former sins return" (82:7).* A sobering nuance on forgiveness. "I, the Lord, will not lay any sin to your charge; go your ways and sin no more; **but unto that soul who sinneth shall the former sins return**." (The forgiveness of §64 is real — "I will not lay any sin to your charge" [John 8:11, the woman taken in adultery] — but it is *not* unconditional-forever: if one returns to sin, "the former sins return" [Matt 18:32-34, the unmerciful servant whose forgiven debt is reinstated when he proves unmerciful; Ezek 18:24]. Forgiveness, like the covenant of 82:10, is *bilateral and conditional* — pardon is granted and then *kept* by "sin no more"; abandoning the repentance reinstates the debt. This is not a God eager to re-charge old sins but the *logic of repentance* [§58:43 — confess AND forsake → "I remember them no more"; forsaking is the condition]. The mercy is genuine; the keeping of it is the partner's part. For the covenant-partnership the project models: forgiveness received is held by faithfulness continued, not banked and spent.)
- *★ The consecration ethic distilled — "seeking the interest of his neighbor… an eye single to the glory of God" (82:17-19).* The United Firm's economic heart, in one verse: "every man seeking the interest of his neighbor, and doing all things with an eye single to the glory of God" (82:19). (The consecration thread [§42→§51→§70→§78] given its two-fold *motive*: outward — "seeking the interest of his neighbor" [1 Cor 10:24, not one's own]; and upward — "an eye single to the glory of God" [§4:5, §88:67]. Equality here is "equal claims on the properties… according to his wants and his needs, **inasmuch as his wants are just**" [82:17] — the §51:3 equal-by-need with a guard: wants must be *just*, not unlimited [the §56 reciprocal-wo, the covetous heart checked]. And the talent-multiplication: "every man may improve upon his talent… gain other talents… to be cast into the Lord's storehouse, to become the common property of the whole church" [82:18] — consecration is not stagnant sharing but *productive* stewardship, each improving his talent [Matt 25] and the *increase* flowing to the common good. The economy of Zion: just wants, neighbor-interest, God's-glory, productive talents, common storehouse. The project's stewardship pattern at the scale of a community.)
