# D&C 35 — the scriptures as they are in mine own bosom

**Setting:** **Fayette, New York, December 7, 1830.** **Sidney Rigdon** — a prominent Campbellite/Reformed Baptist preacher from Ohio who had converted whole congregations to a "restorationist" primitive Christianity — came east with Edward Partridge "to inquire of the Lord" after Parley P. Pratt (his former associate) brought him the Book of Mormon. Joseph was "engaged almost daily in making a translation of the Bible" (the JST, begun June 1830); his scribes Oliver and John had been sent on missions. This revelation calls Sidney to be the new scribe — and reframes his entire prior ministry as forerunner work he didn't know he was doing.

**Binding idea:** "Thou wast sent forth, even as John, to prepare the way before me… and thou knewest it not" (35:4). Sidney's years of restorationist preaching were *preparation he didn't understand* — and now his commission is to "write for him; and the scriptures shall be given, even as they are in mine own bosom" (35:20): to help restore the Bible toward its *original divine form*, the word as God Himself holds it.

**Read:** Christ, "Alpha and Omega… whose course is one eternal round" (35:1), died that believers "may become the sons of God, even one in me as I am one in the Father… that we may be one" (35:2). "Sidney, I have looked upon thee and thy works… and prepared thee for a greater work… thou wast sent forth, even as John, to prepare the way before me, and before Elijah which should come, and thou knewest it not" (35:3-4). "Thou didst baptize by water unto repentance, but they received not the Holy Ghost; but now… thou shalt baptize by water, and they shall receive the Holy Ghost by the laying on of the hands, even as the apostles of old" (35:5-6). "I call upon the weak things of the world, those who are unlearned and despised, to thresh the nations by the power of my Spirit; and their arm shall be my arm" (35:13-14). "I have sent forth the fulness of my gospel by the hand of my servant Joseph; and in weakness have I blessed him; and I have given unto him the keys of the mystery of those things which have been sealed… if he abide in me, and if not, another will I plant in his stead. Wherefore, watch over him that his faith fail not" (35:17-19). "A commandment I give unto thee—that thou shalt write for him; and the scriptures shall be given, even as they are in mine own bosom, to the salvation of mine own elect" (35:20). "Inasmuch as ye do not write… thou shalt preach my gospel and call on the holy prophets to prove his words" (35:23). "Fear not, little flock, the kingdom is yours until I come" (35:27).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "The scriptures… as they are in mine own bosom" (35:20) — the JST's charter, and a profound claim about scripture.* The Bible re-translation isn't a scholarly retroversion to better manuscripts; it is the *restoration of the text toward the form God holds in His own bosom* — the un-accommodated original behind the transmitted, sometimes-corrupted page. This is the deep companion to §1:24 (revelation given "after the manner of their language," accommodated to the receiver): if scripture descends into human language and suffers human transmission, then the JST is the *reverse motion* — recovering it toward "mine own bosom," the divine source-form. (For the period-language principle: §1:24 names the *descent* of the word into language; §35:20 names a *recovery* toward the word as God holds it. Reading scripture in its period-language [the principle] and restoring scripture toward God's bosom [the JST] are two directions on the same axis — both seek the word *as God means it*, beneath the accommodation. The JST is the Lord doing, by revelation, what the careful reader does by the Spirit: getting back to what He actually meant.)
- *★ "I have looked upon thee… thou knewest it not" (35:3-4) — retrospective recognition at the scale of a life.* The §6 move (the Lord naming back a private prayer) at biographical scale: Sidney's *entire restorationist ministry* — his preaching of primitive Christianity, his baptizing "unto repentance" — was *forerunner work he didn't know he was doing*, "to prepare the way before me, and before Elijah which should come." The Lord reframes a man's whole past as preparation for a calling he couldn't have foreseen. (This is the tender pattern again: God was working in your life toward this before you understood it. Sidney's years that might have looked like a *different* religious project were, unbeknownst to him, the runway. The Spirit distills the meaning of a life *backward* — you see what it was *for* only when the greater work arrives.)
- *★ The weak things of the world thresh the nations (35:13-14).* "I call upon the **weak** things of the world, those who are unlearned and despised, to thresh the nations by the power of my Spirit; and their arm shall be my arm." The Restoration's method is the inversion of worldly power: God works through the *weak*, the *unlearned*, the *despised* (1 Cor 1:27; D&C 1:19,23) — and *makes up the deficit Himself* ("their arm shall be my arm… I will be their shield"). The strength isn't in the instrument; it is *supplied to* the instrument. (This is the gospel's deep pattern of weak-vessel + divine-supply: an unlearned farmer translates scripture, weak missionaries thresh nations, "in weakness have I blessed [Joseph]," 35:17. The instrument's weakness is not a problem to overcome but the *condition* God prefers — so the glory is unmistakably His, not the vessel's. The harness, not the instrument, carries the work.)
- *★ "Watch over him that his faith fail not" (35:19) — Sidney to watch over the prophet.* Even Joseph, who holds the keys (35:18), needs *watching over* — and Sidney is charged with it: "watch over him that his faith fail not." The watching runs *both ways* in the order: Joseph presides, yet a fellow servant is appointed to guard the presider's own faith. (The presiding/watching thread [Abr 4:18] applied to the prophet himself — no one, not even the channel, is unwatched; the order includes guardianship *of* the leader, not only *by* him. And the succession clause is right beside it, 35:18: "if he abide in me, and if not, another will I plant in his stead" — the keys are conditional even for Joseph, which is *why* he must be watched over.)
- *Oneness — "that we may be one" (35:2).* The section opens on John 17's unity: believers become "one in me as I am one in the Father… that we may be one." The becoming-sons doctrine (§11:30/§34:3) deepened into *oneness with God* — not merely adopted but made *one* with Christ as Christ is one with the Father. (The matter-spectrum's destination: the refinement of a soul ends not in proximity to God but in *unity* with Him; §29's "all things spiritual" resolving into §35's "that we may be one." §38, next, will demand this oneness *horizontally* — "be one, or ye are not mine.")
