# D&C 71 — no weapon formed against you shall prosper

**Setting:** **Hiram, Ohio, December 1, 1831.** Joseph and Sidney had been steadily translating the Bible — until this revelation *temporarily laid it aside*. The reason: **Ezra Booth**, a former Methodist preacher who had joined and then apostatized, was publishing a series of hostile letters in the *Ohio Star* (Ravenna), stirring "unfriendly feelings" against the Church. Joseph and Sidney are sent out to *preach* — to meet the attack openly and "allay" it by proclaiming the gospel.

**Binding idea:** "There is no weapon that is formed against you shall prosper; and if any man lift his voice against you he shall be confounded in mine own due time" (71:9-10). The answer to public attack is not to hide or to retaliate but to *open your mouths*, meet the critics in the open, and trust the vindication to God's timing — no weapon formed against the work will finally prosper.

**Read:** "The time has verily come that it is necessary and expedient in me that you should open your mouths in proclaiming my gospel, the things of the kingdom, expounding the mysteries thereof out of the scriptures, according to that portion of Spirit and power which shall be given unto you" (71:1). "This is a mission for a season, which I give unto you" (71:3). "Labor ye in my vineyard. Call upon the inhabitants of the earth, and bear record, and prepare the way for the commandments and revelations which are to come" (71:4). "Unto him that receiveth it shall be given more abundantly, even power" (71:6). "Confound your enemies; call upon them to meet you both in public and in private; and inasmuch as ye are faithful their shame shall be made manifest" (71:7). "Let them bring forth their strong reasons against the Lord" (71:8). "Verily, thus saith the Lord unto you—there is no weapon that is formed against you shall prosper" (71:9). "If any man lift his voice against you he shall be confounded in mine own due time" (71:10). "Keep my commandments; they are true and faithful" (71:11).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ Meet the attack in the open — "confound your enemies… in public and in private" (71:7-8).* The response to Booth's published slander is striking for what it *isn't*: not silence, not counter-slander, not a retreat into the translation. It's *open engagement* — "call upon them to meet you both in public and in private," and "let them bring forth their strong reasons against the Lord" (71:7-8). (The Lord invites the *debate*. The critics are told to bring their "strong reasons" [Isa 41:21 — God's own challenge to the idols, "produce your cause… bring forth your strong reasons"] into the open, and the promise is that "inasmuch as ye are faithful their shame shall be made manifest." This is *not* sign-seeking [§63 — the demand for proof] nor contention for its own sake [§10:63, the spirit of contention]; it's *reasoned public defense*, the same confidence as §67's "make one like unto it" — let the critique come fully into the light, because what is true withstands the test and what is false is *exposed* by it. The faithful don't fear the open question; they invite it. The condition is "inasmuch as ye are faithful" — the vindication is tied to *their* faithfulness, not to their cleverness in argument.)
- *★ "No weapon… shall prosper… confounded in mine own due time" (71:9-10).* The promise, lifted straight from Isaiah 54:17 ("no weapon that is formed against thee shall prosper… this is the heritage of the servants of the Lord"). But note the *timing* clause: "confounded **in mine own due time**" (71:10). (The vindication is *certain* but not *immediate*. The weapon will not *finally* prosper — but it may wound in the meantime, and the confounding comes on God's schedule, not the attacked party's. This is the §61:20 "in mine own due time" patience and the §24:8 "I am with thee in tribulation" applied to *slander*: the faithful are not promised that the attack won't *hurt* or won't *land for a season*, only that it won't *prosper* in the end and the attacker will be confounded *when God chooses*. Booth's letters did real damage — they helped stir the mob spirit that, three months later, tarred and feathered Joseph at this very Hiram house. The weapon was *formed* and even *struck*; it did not *prosper*. For anyone under attack for the work: the promise is not exemption from the weapon but the assurance it won't finally win — and the discipline to leave the timing of vindication to God.)
- *Preaching as preparation — "prepare the way for… the revelations which are to come" (71:4).* A quiet forward-pointer: this season of preaching is also to "prepare the way for the commandments and revelations which are to come." (Weeks after this, at this same Hiram house, Joseph and Sidney would receive §76 — the Vision of the three glories. The mission to answer Booth was *also* a preparation: the laying-aside of the translation, the public proclaiming, the keeping of the commandments [71:11], readied them for one of the grandest revelations in the canon. The interruption was not a detour from the work but part of it — "prepare the way." Often the thing that looks like it pulls you off the main task is the preparation for the next, greater thing.)

**Notable / flag:** No spin-off. **Walk's contribution:** **§71's answer to public attack is "open your mouths" and "no weapon… shall prosper" (71:7-10).** When Ezra Booth's apostate letters stirred hostility, the Lord's response was *not* silence or counter-attack but *open engagement*: "confound your enemies; call upon them to meet you both in public and in private… let them bring forth their strong reasons against the Lord" (Isa 41:21) — reasoned public defense, the same confidence as §67's "make one like unto it" (the true withstands the test, the false is exposed by it; *not* sign-seeking §63, *not* contention §10:63). The interruption *was* the preparation.
