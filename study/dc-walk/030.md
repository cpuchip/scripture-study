# D&C 30 — fearing man, and not fearing man

**Setting:** **Fayette, New York, September 1830**, right after the three-day conference (the one §28 was given before) — to **David Whitmer, Peter Whitmer Jr., and John Whitmer**, before the elders dispersed. Originally three separate revelations, combined by Joseph for the 1835 edition. It is the *personal aftermath of §28*: David Whitmer had been one of those "wrongly influenced" by Hiram Page's seerstone, and here he is chastened for it by name.

**Binding idea:** The section is framed by *fear of man*. David is rebuked because "you have feared man and have not relied on me for strength" (30:1); John is sent out "not fearing what man can do, for I am with you" (30:11). Between the two stands the §28 lesson made personal: David was "persuaded by those whom I have not commanded" (30:2) — he followed an out-of-order voice, and the consequence is to be "left to inquire for yourself."

**Read:** To David: "you have feared man and have not relied on me for strength as you ought. But your mind has been on the things of the earth more than on the things of me… and you have not given heed unto my Spirit, and to those who were set over you, but have been persuaded by those whom I have not commanded" (30:1-2). "Wherefore, you are left to inquire for yourself at my hand, and ponder upon the things which you have received" (30:3); "your home shall be at your father's house, until I give unto you further commandments" (30:4). To Peter Jr.: "take your journey with your brother Oliver… fear not, but give heed unto the words and advice of your brother" (30:5); "none have I appointed to be his counselor over him… except… Joseph Smith, Jun." (30:7). To John: "proclaim my gospel, as with the voice of a trump" (30:9); "ever open your mouth in my cause, not fearing what man can do, for I am with you" (30:11).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ David's fault is the §28 failure, named personally (30:2).* "You have not given heed unto my Spirit, and to those who were set over you, but have been **persuaded by those whom I have not commanded**." This is precisely the Hiram Page episode (§28): David let himself be swayed by a voice *not appointed* to give revelation. So §30 is the order-of-revelation doctrine of §28 applied to a real person who got it wrong. The diagnosis pairs two failures: he didn't heed the *appointed* channel ("those who were set over you," §28:2's order) *and* he heeded an *unappointed* one (Hiram, "those whom I have not commanded"). Both halves of §28's safeguard, breached. (And note: David's error wasn't stupidity or malice — it was *fear of man* (30:1) and a *mind on earthly things* (30:2). The out-of-order voice got in through a heart that was anxious and distracted. The discernment cross-check [§28] fails first in the *affections* — fear and worldliness make a person persuadable by counterfeits. The guard on the period-language principle isn't only intellectual [is this in order?] but devotional [is my heart fixed on God or fearful of men?].)
- *"You are left to inquire for yourself" (30:3) — the consequence is mild and apt.* David isn't excommunicated or cursed; the consequence *fits the fault*. He failed to rely on God and got swayed by others, so he's "left to inquire for yourself at my hand, and ponder" — the privilege of being *directed* is paused, and he must do the §9 study-it-out work *himself* for a season. The remedy for "you leaned on the wrong voices" is "now lean directly on Me, alone, and ponder." (Reproof calibrated to the person again — §9's gentle "my son," §23's "beware of pride"; here, a consequence designed to *cure* the specific weakness, not merely punish it. And "until I give unto you further commandments" [30:4] — the door isn't shut; the directing resumes when he's ready.)
- *★ The fear-of-man bookends (30:1 ↔ 30:11) — the section's frame.* David "feared man" (30:1); John is charged to go "not fearing what man can do, for I am with you" (30:11). The same temptation, the two responses, set side by side. Fear of man is the recurring sin of the early sections — it was *Joseph's* sin over the 116 pages (§3:7, "thou… hast feared man more than God"), and now David's. The antidote is identical each time: *"I am with you"* (30:11, Matt 28:20 — the §24:8 comfort). Fear of man is cured not by courage summoned but by *Presence relied on* — the same "rely on me for strength" David failed at (30:1). (The whole section turns on one axis: whom do you fear, and on whom do you rely? David feared men and relied on men; John is told to fear no man because God is *with* him. The cure for fear-of-man is the felt with-ness of God.)
- *Peter Jr. sent into another's afflictions (30:5-7).* Peter is to go with Oliver to the Lamanites and "be afflicted in all his afflictions… for his and your deliverance" (30:6). The junior companion shares the senior's trials as a *bond*, not a burden — and the line of authority is kept clear ("none have I appointed to be his counselor over him… except Joseph," 30:7 — the §28 order again, applied to the mission). Even on the road, the order holds: Peter heeds Oliver, Oliver answers only to Joseph. (Order isn't just for revelation; it's for the missionary companionship — a clear, accountable chain, which is the presiding structure scaled down to two men in a wagon.)

**Notable / flag:** No spin-off. **Walk's contribution:** **§30 applies §28's order-cross-check to a real person who got it wrong** — David Whitmer, swayed by Hiram Page's stone, is named: he "feared man" and was "persuaded by those whom I have not commanded." And the diagnosis goes deeper than §28's structural test: the out-of-order voice got in through *fear and worldliness* — the **devotional** root beneath the intellectual guard. The period-language principle's safeguard isn't only "is this in order?" but "is my heart fixed on God, or anxious about men?"; a fearful, distracted heart is persuadable by counterfeits. Plus **the fear-of-man bookends (30:1 ↔ 30:11)** — David feared man, John is told to fear no man "for I am with you," the same sin as Joseph's (§3:7), cured the same way: not by summoning courage but by *relying on Presence*. For Michael: the quiet personal sequel to §28 — the man who followed the wrong voice did it because he was afraid and his mind was on earthly things; the cure is to be left alone with God for a season, and to learn that the answer to fearing men is *"I am with you."*
