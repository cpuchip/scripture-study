# Official Declaration 1 — the Manifesto (1890)

**Setting:** **Salt Lake City, accepted by the Church October 6, 1890** (the Manifesto itself dated September 24, 1890). President Wilford Woodruff's official declaration discontinuing the practice of plural marriage, after decades of federal anti-polygamy laws (upheld by the Supreme Court) that threatened the temples, the ordinances, and the Church's leadership and property. Printed with three later addresses explaining the revelation behind it. This is the closure of the §132 *practice* — and on this walk I record it, with its central public doctrine engaged, and **flag the plural-marriage history itself as reserved — Michael's and the Spirit's, not mine to adjudicate.**

**Binding idea:** **"I now publicly declare that my advice to the Latter-day Saints is to refrain from contracting any marriage forbidden by the law of the land."** And from the addresses: **"The Lord will never permit me or any other man who stands as President of this Church to lead you astray… If I were to attempt that, the Lord would remove me out of my place."** The practice ends by revelation, to preserve the temples and the work for the living *and the dead*.

**Read:**
- The Manifesto: "I hereby declare my intention to submit to those laws, and to use my influence with the members of the Church over which I preside to have them do likewise." "And I now publicly declare that my advice to the Latter-day Saints is to refrain from contracting any marriage forbidden by the law of the land."
- The prophetic safeguard: "The Lord will never permit me or any other man who stands as President of this Church to lead you astray. It is not in the programme. It is not in the mind of God. If I were to attempt that, the Lord would remove me out of my place, and so He will any other man who attempts to lead the children of men astray from the oracles of God and from their duty."
- The revelation behind it: "The Lord showed me by vision and revelation exactly what would take place if we did not stop this practice." "...leave our Prophets and Apostles and fathers free men, and the temples in the hands of the people, so that the dead may be redeemed… and shall the work go on or stop?"
- The sealing-order (Lorenzo Snow's motion): Woodruff is "the only man on the earth at the present time who holds the keys of the sealing ordinances."

**Thoughts:**

- *★ The prophet will not be permitted to lead the Church astray (the safeguard).* The most-quoted line of the addresses is a doctrine about *the office itself*: "The Lord will never permit… the President of this Church to lead you astray… If I were to attempt that, the Lord would remove me out of my place" (addresses). (The §21:5 "his word ye shall receive… in all patience and faith" given its *guardrail*: the living prophet is a *learning* steward [§20:5's relapse, §93:47's rebuke — even prophets are corrected], yet the *office* is protected — the Lord will not let His authorized servant lead the whole Church into apostasy, because He would *remove* him first [the §107:81-84 "none exempt," the §43:3-4 standing-law of order]. For the project's delegation/trust frame: the principal protects the *system* even where individual delegates err — the safeguard is not the man's infallibility but the *Lord's active oversight* of the office. Not "the prophet can't be wrong" but "the Lord won't let the office destroy the work.")

- *★ The practice yielded to preserve the temples — and the redemption of the dead (§138).* Woodruff's reasoning, laid before the Saints as a *question* they would "all answer alike" by the Spirit, is a weighing: continue the practice and lose "all the Temples, and the stopping of all the ordinances therein, both for the living and the dead" — *or* cease the practice and "leave the Temples in the hands of the people, so that **the dead may be redeemed**" (addresses). (A striking connection to the walk's *last section*: the §132 plural-marriage *practice* was ended so the temples could stay open and the §138 *redemption of the dead* could go forward — "a large number has already been delivered from the prison house in the spirit world by this people, and shall the work go on or stop?" The greater work [the redemption of the dead, the §110/§128/§138 taproot] was preserved by relinquishing the practice that endangered it. For the walk: the *eternal covenant* [§132:7, sealing by the Holy Spirit of promise] endures; the time-bound *practice* yielded when it threatened the very temples the covenant runs through. The §10:4 "do not run faster than you have strength" at the scale of a people — God paced the Church through a crisis by ending what the nation would otherwise have used to destroy the whole work.)

- *The plural-marriage history — reserved, recorded, not adjudicated.* The Manifesto closes the practice §132:1 inquired about. (The whole arc — the institution of the practice [§132], the decades of conflict, the federal pressure, the Manifesto [OD 1], and the difficult history around and after it — is the most historically-fraught thread in the Restoration. Per the walk's discipline, as at §132: I record that OD 1 *ended the practice by revelation* and engage its public doctrine [the prophetic safeguard, the temples preserved], and I leave the weighing of the practice itself, its history, and its hard questions to Michael and the Spirit. The section itself distinguishes the *covenant* [eternal] from the *practice* [commanded, then ended] — I keep that distinction and adjudicate nothing.)
