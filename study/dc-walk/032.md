# D&C 32 — the Lamanite mission party completed

**Setting:** **Manchester, New York, early October 1830** — to **Parley P. Pratt** and **Ziba Peterson**, added to the mission already given Oliver Cowdery and Peter Whitmer Jr. (§28, §30). The elders were stirred by the Book of Mormon's promises to the Lamanites and asked whether missionaries should be sent west; this is the answer. The four-man "Lamanite mission" — the Restoration's first major missionary journey, ~1,500 miles to the Missouri frontier — is now complete.

**Binding idea:** Parley is called to "declare my gospel and learn of me, and be meek and lowly of heart" (32:1); the company is promised "I myself will go with them and be in their midst; and I am their advocate with the Father, and nothing shall prevail against them" (32:3). And the one instruction: "give heed to that which is written, and pretend to no other revelation" (32:4) — the §28 order, sent down the road with the missionaries.

**Read:** "Parley P. Pratt… shall declare my gospel and learn of me, and be meek and lowly of heart" (32:1); he goes "with my servants, Oliver Cowdery and Peter Whitmer, Jun., into the wilderness among the Lamanites" (32:2). "Ziba Peterson also shall go with them; and I myself will go with them and be in their midst; and I am their advocate with the Father, and nothing shall prevail against them" (32:3). "They shall give heed to that which is written, and pretend to no other revelation; and they shall pray always that I may unfold the same to their understanding" (32:4). "Give heed unto these words and trifle not, and I will bless them" (32:5).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "Pretend to no other revelation" (32:4) — the §28 order on the road.* Days after Hiram Page's seerstone crisis (§28), the missionaries are given the same boundary in positive form: *give heed to what is written, and claim no revelation beyond it.* The danger §28 named (out-of-order revelation) is pre-empted for the mission — these elders are to preach the *written* word (the Book of Mormon, the scriptures), not freelance new doctrine. And the way they grow in it is the §9 method: "pray always that I may **unfold** the same to their understanding" (32:4). Not new revelation, but the *opening up* of the given word — study-and-ask applied to scripture. (This is the order-of-revelation made portable: a missionary's authority is to *declare what's written*, and his revelation is the Spirit *unfolding* it, not adding to it. The same structural guard, on the move.)
- *"I myself will go with them… and be in their midst" (32:3) — the with-ness, intensified.* The "I am with you" thread (§24/§30/§31) reaches its strongest early form: not just *with* them but *in their midst*, as their *advocate with the Father*, so that "nothing shall prevail against them." For four men about to walk 1,500 miles into the wilderness, the promise isn't safety from hardship but *Presence and advocacy* — Christ goes along, pleading their case before the Father. (The Advocate doctrine, §29:5, applied to a specific company: the Lord doesn't send missionaries out *to* God; He goes *with* them *as* their advocate.)
- *"Meek and lowly of heart" (32:1) — the missionary posture.* Parley, who would become a forceful, brilliant preacher and writer, is told first to "learn of me, and be meek and lowly of heart" (Matt 11:29 — Christ's own self-description). The gift of declaration is grounded in the posture of meekness — the §31:9 "govern in meekness" extended to the missionary. (Power to declare, rooted in lowliness of heart — the opposite of priestcraft, which §33 will name as the apostasy's engine.)
