# D&C 73 — the rhythm of preaching and translating

**Setting:** **Hiram, Ohio, January 10, 1832.** Since early December (§71), Joseph and Sidney had been preaching to answer Ezra Booth's attacks — and "by this means much was accomplished in diminishing the unfavorable feelings." The mission worked. Now a short revelation directs the *next* rhythm: keep preaching until the coming conference, then *resume the translation of the Bible* and carry it "until it be finished."

**Binding idea:** "It is expedient to translate again; and, inasmuch as it is practicable, to preach in the regions round about until conference; and after that it is expedient to continue the work of translation until it be finished" (73:3-4). Two labors held in rhythm — preach for a season, then return to the translation and *finish it*.

**Read:** "It is expedient in me that they should continue preaching the gospel, and in exhortation to the churches in the regions round about, until conference" (73:1). "Then… it shall be made known unto them, by the voice of the conference, their several missions" (73:2). "It is expedient to translate again" (73:3). "Inasmuch as it is practicable, to preach in the regions round about until conference; and after that it is expedient to continue the work of translation until it be finished" (73:4). "Let this be a pattern unto the elders until further knowledge" (73:5). "Now I give no more unto you at this time. Gird up your loins and be sober" (73:6).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ Two labors in rhythm — preach, then finish the translation (73:3-4).* The §71 mission had a *season* — it answered Booth, "diminished the unfavorable feelings," and now it yields back to the translation. The Lord doesn't choose *between* the two labors; He *sequences* them: preach "until conference," then "continue the work of translation until it be finished." (The walk noted at §71:4 that the preaching was "to prepare the way for… the revelations which are to come" — and here the return to the translation is named, the translation out of which §76 [the Vision] will come in just five weeks. The rhythm is purposeful: the interruption [the Booth mission] did its work and is set down; the main labor [the translation] is taken back up "until it be finished." Two things for the project: *finish what's begun* — "until it be finished" is the standing word over the translation [the ammon principle, carry it to completion]; and *labors alternate without either being abandoned* — the urgent defense [§71] and the patient construction [the translation] each get their season, neither dropped. "Let this be a pattern unto the elders" [73:5] — the rhythm itself is the lesson.)
- *"Gird up your loins and be sober" (73:6).* The whole word of a short revelation: get ready, and be sober. (1 Pet 1:13 — "gird up the loins of your mind, be sober." The close of a transitional section is *readiness*: no new doctrine "at this time," just the charge to be prepared and clear-headed for what comes. Sometimes the Lord's word for a season is not a new vision but *get ready for the one coming* — and §76 was five weeks out. The sobriety is the posture of one who knows a great thing is near and prepares for it.)

**Notable / flag:** No spin-off. **Walk's contribution:** **§73 sets the rhythm of two labors — preach for a season, then "continue the work of translation until it be finished" (73:3-4).** The §71 mission to answer Booth had done its work ("much was accomplished in diminishing the unfavorable feelings"), and now it yields *back* to the main labor: the Bible translation, to be carried "until it be finished." The Lord doesn't choose between the urgent defense and the patient construction — He *sequences* them, each in its season, neither abandoned ("until it be finished" = finish-what's-begun, the ammon principle). And the preaching that "prepare[d] the way for… the revelations which are to come" (§71:4) pays off: the resumed translation produces §76, the Vision, five weeks later. The close is readiness — "gird up your loins and be sober" (1 Pet 1:13), the posture of one who knows a great thing is near.
