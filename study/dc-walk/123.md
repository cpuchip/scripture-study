# D&C 123 — gather up the record; let no man count them small things

**Setting:** **Liberty Jail, ~March 22, 1839** — the close of the same epistle as §§121-122. From the prophet who can do nothing, a practical charge to the Saints who can: *gather and publish the record* of what was done to them — the facts, the damages, the names of the oppressors — and lay it before the government and the world. Not for revenge (the vengeance was reserved to God, §121:23-25), but as the *last lawful effort* before God acts, and as a *duty owed* — to God, to angels, to their families, and to "all the rising generation."

**Binding idea:** "It is an imperative duty that we owe… to all the rising generation, and to all the pure in heart" (123:11) — to "waste and wear out our lives in bringing to light all the hidden things of darkness" (123:13). And the hinge: **"Let no man count them as small things; for there is much which lieth in futurity, pertaining to the saints, which depends upon these things" (123:15).** A small helm steers a large ship in a storm (123:16) — the small, faithful, recorded thing carries weight far beyond its size.

**Read:**
- The charge: "we would suggest for your consideration the propriety of all the saints gathering up a knowledge of all the facts, and sufferings and abuses put upon them… And also of all the property and amount of damages… And also the names of all persons that have had a hand in their oppressions… And perhaps a committee can be appointed to find out these things, and to take statements and affidavits" (123:1-4).
- The purpose: "That we may not only publish to all the world, but present them to the heads of government… as the last effort which is enjoined on us by our Heavenly Father, before we can fully and completely claim that promise which shall call him forth from his hiding place; and also that the whole nation may be left without excuse before he can send forth the power of his mighty arm" (123:6).
- The duty named: "It is an imperative duty that we owe to God, to angels, with whom we shall be brought to stand, and also to ourselves, to our wives and children…" (123:7). "And also it is an imperative duty that we owe to all the rising generation, and to all the pure in heart—" (123:11).
- The reason it reaches the future: "For there are many yet on the earth among all sects, parties, and denominations, who are blinded by the subtle craftiness of men… and who are only kept from the truth because they know not where to find it—" (123:12). "Therefore, that we should waste and wear out our lives in bringing to light all the hidden things of darkness, wherein we know them… These should then be attended to with great earnestness" (123:13-14).
- The hinge + the helm: "Let no man count them as small things; for there is much which lieth in futurity, pertaining to the saints, which depends upon these things. You know, brethren, that a very large ship is benefited very much by a very small helm in the time of a storm, by being kept workways with the wind and the waves" (123:15-16).
- The close: "Therefore, dearly beloved brethren, let us cheerfully do all things that lie in our power; and then may we stand still, with the utmost assurance, to see the salvation of God, and for his arm to be revealed" (123:17).

**Thoughts:**

- *★ The record as an imperative duty — owed to the future (123:7,11,15).* The charge is not bookkeeping for its own sake; it is a *duty owed* — "to God, to angels… to ourselves, to our wives and children" (123:7), and especially "to all the rising generation, and to all the pure in heart" (123:11). (The reason it reaches the *future* is in 123:12: there are honest souls "kept from the truth **because they know not where to find it**" — the record, faithfully kept, is *findable truth* for those who come after. "Let no man count them as small things; for there is much which lieth in **futurity**… which depends upon these things" [123:15]. For the project's whole memory architecture — the journals, the active board, the recurring-thread ledger, *this walk itself*: §123 is its scriptural charge. The discipline of "gathering up a knowledge of all the facts," of taking the careful record so the truth is *findable* by the rising generation, is "an imperative duty," not optional housekeeping — and the things that look small now are the things "much in futurity depends upon." The covenant's memory discipline [the read-before-quoting record, the journal kept so we arrive as more than a stranger] is 123:15 enacted: do not count the small faithful record a small thing.)

- *★ The small helm — small faithful things steer the storm (123:16).* The image is precise: "a very large ship is benefited very much by a **very small helm** in the time of a storm." (The §64:33 "out of small things proceedeth that which is great" given a *governing* image — not just that small things *grow* into great, but that small things *steer* the great; the helm is tiny against the hull, yet it sets the whole ship's course against "the wind and the waves." For the project: the small disciplines — the verified quote, the logged decision, the lane kept current, the one faithful section of a walk — are the *helm*; they look negligible beside the storm, but they hold the course. The storm does not need to be calmed to be survived; the ship needs only to be "kept workways with the wind" by the small helm faithfully held.)

- *Cheerfully do all, then stand still (123:17).* The close fuses *full effort* with *full trust*: "let us **cheerfully do all things** that lie in our power; **and then** may we **stand still**, with the utmost assurance, to see the salvation of God." (The §98/§101:81-92 pattern — exhaust the lawful means, *then* God acts — given its posture: not grim resignation but *cheerful* doing, and not anxious doing but doing-then-resting. The "therefore/but" of faith: *therefore* [having done all] we may stand still. The agency-then-grace seam [§58:26 do-many-things-of-your-own-free-will → then the Lord's arm revealed]; the steward acts to the limit of his power and leaves the outcome — the part beyond his power — to God, cheerfully and with assurance, not white-knuckled.)
