# D&C 86 — let the wheat and the tares grow together, and ye are lawful heirs

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, December 6, 1832** — "received while the Prophet was reviewing and editing the manuscript of the translation of the Bible." Reaching the parable of the wheat and tares (Matthew 13), the Lord gives its *interpretation* — and then turns to the elders' own *priesthood lineage* as "lawful heirs."

**Binding idea:** "Let the wheat and the tares grow together until the harvest is fully ripe; then ye shall first gather out the wheat from among the tares" (86:7). The apostasy sowed tares among the wheat — and God's restraint in not uprooting them early is *mercy to the tender wheat*. The harvest will sort them; for now, they grow together.

**Read:** "The field was the world, and the apostles were the sowers of the seed; and after they have fallen asleep the great persecutor of the church, the apostate… even Babylon… soweth the tares; wherefore, the tares choke the wheat and drive the church into the wilderness" (86:2-3). "In the last days, even now while the Lord is beginning to bring forth the word, and the blade is springing up and is yet tender" (86:4). "The angels are crying unto the Lord day and night, who are ready and waiting to be sent forth to reap down the fields; but the Lord saith unto them, pluck not up the tares while the blade is yet tender (for verily your faith is weak), lest you destroy the wheat also" (86:5-6). "Let the wheat and the tares grow together until the harvest is fully ripe" (86:7). "Thus saith the Lord unto you, with whom the priesthood hath continued through the lineage of your fathers— for ye are lawful heirs, according to the flesh, and have been hid from the world with Christ in God" (86:8-9). "Your life and the priesthood have remained, and must needs remain through you and your lineage until the restoration of all things" (86:10). "Blessed are ye if ye continue in my goodness, a light unto the Gentiles, and through this priesthood, a savior unto my people Israel" (86:11).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ The wheat and tares — the apostasy and restoration in one parable (86:1-7).* The Lord reads His own parable [Matt 13] as *church history*: the apostles "sowed the seed" [the original Church]; "after they have fallen asleep" the apostasy ["Babylon… the whore"] sowed tares that "choke the wheat and drive the church into the wilderness" [the Great Apostasy, Rev 12:6]; and "now while the Lord is beginning to bring forth the word… the blade is springing up and is yet tender" [the Restoration, 1832]. (The whole sweep — original Church → apostasy → restoration — in the parable's frame. The wheat and tares are *not* the righteous and wicked individuals at the last day so much as the *true church and the apostate counterfeit* growing together through history. The walk's apostasy-restoration thread [§1 the falling away; §13 the re-conferral; §27 the gathering of keys] given Jesus's own image: the seed was sown, the tares choked it into the wilderness, and the tender blade is springing up again. The Restoration is *young* — "the blade… is yet tender" — which is exactly why the next verse matters.)
- *★ "Pluck not up the tares while the blade is yet tender" — restraint as mercy (86:5-7).* The angels are "ready and waiting to be sent forth to reap down the fields" — eager for judgment — but the Lord *restrains* them: "pluck not up the tares while the blade is yet tender (for verily your faith is weak), lest you destroy the wheat also" (86:6). (A profound word on the *delay* of judgment: God holds back the harvest *to protect the tender wheat*. If the tares were ripped out now, the young, weak-rooted wheat would be torn up with them [the roots intertwined, Matt 13:29]. The patience of God with a wicked world is not indifference or weakness — it is *mercy to the still-growing righteous*, who are not yet strong enough to survive the separation. "Your faith is weak" — the Lord names the wheat's own tenderness as the reason for restraint. For anyone impatient for God to *judge* the world's evil now: the delay is *for the wheat's sake* — for the new convert, the half-grown faith, the tender blade that a premature harvest would destroy. Judgment waits "until the harvest is fully ripe" [86:7] — until the wheat is strong enough to be gathered safely. The mercy is in the *timing*. The angels' eagerness is restrained by the Father's patience with the weak.)
- *★ "Ye are lawful heirs, according to the flesh" — the Abrahamic lineage (86:8-11).* The §84 priesthood-lineage personalized: "the priesthood hath continued through the lineage of your fathers— for ye are lawful heirs, according to the flesh, and have been hid from the world with Christ in God" (86:8-9). "Your life and the priesthood have remained… through you and your lineage until the restoration of all things" (86:10). (The §84 line from Adam given its *living end*: these elders are *lawful heirs* — the blood of Abraham [Abr 2:9-11], the right to the priesthood "according to the flesh," "hid… with Christ in God" [Col 3:3] through the long apostasy and now surfacing in the Restoration. This is the Abrahamic covenant's *lineage* clause [the bin-4-adjacent lineage material the walk has tracked through the PoGP — Abr 1:21-27, Abr 2:10] in its *blessing* aspect: the heirs are preserved "until the restoration of all things." And the *purpose* is the Abrahamic blessing — "a light unto the Gentiles, and through this priesthood, a **savior** unto my people Israel" [86:11, Obad 1:21 "saviors… on mount Zion"]: the lineage is not for privilege but for *service* — blessed to *bless*, heirs in order to be *saviors* to others [the redemption-of-the-dead / saviors-on-Mount-Zion seed, temple-adjacent]. The lineage I note as part of the reserved bin-4 thread, Michael's — but its *cashing-out* here is unambiguous: heirship exists to make one a *light* and a *savior* to others, never a possessor.)

**Notable / flag:** ★ **reserved/bin-4 thread (Michael's):** 86:8-9 — "lawful heirs, according to the flesh" / the priesthood through "the lineage of your fathers" (the Abrahamic-lineage material). **Walk's contribution:** **§86 reads the wheat and tares as church history and turns the elders' lineage to service.** Jesus's parable (Matt 13) becomes the sweep of the apostasy and restoration: the apostles sowed the seed; "after they have fallen asleep" Babylon sowed tares that "choke the wheat and drive the church into the wilderness" (the Great Apostasy); and "now… the blade is springing up and is yet tender" (the young Restoration). And the heart of it — **restraint as mercy (86:5-7)**: the angels are eager to "reap down the fields," but the Lord holds them back — "pluck not up the tares while the blade is yet tender… lest you destroy the wheat also." God's patience with a wicked world is not indifference; it is *mercy to the still-growing righteous*, whose roots are too tangled with the tares and whose faith is still too weak to survive a premature harvest — the mercy is in the *timing*, judgment delayed "until the harvest is fully ripe." Then the elders' **lawful heirship** (86:8-11): the §84 lineage personalized — "ye are lawful heirs, according to the flesh… hid… with Christ in God" through the apostasy (the Abrahamic line, bin-4, Michael's) — with its *purpose* unambiguous: "a light unto the Gentiles, and through this priesthood, a **savior** unto my people Israel." Heirship exists for *service*, not privilege — blessed to bless, heirs in order to be saviors to others. For Michael: the section that says God lets the weeds grow not from weakness but to spare the tender wheat, and that being a lawful heir means becoming a light and a savior to others — never a possessor.
