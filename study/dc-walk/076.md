# D&C 76 — the Vision: that he lives, and the kingdoms of glory

**Setting:** **Hiram, Ohio, February 16, 1832** — Joseph and Sidney, having resumed the Bible translation (§73), reached **John 5:29** ("they… shall come forth; they that have done good, unto the resurrection of life; and they that have done evil, unto the resurrection of damnation"). The single word *damnation* against *life* seemed too small a frame: "if God rewarded every one according to the deeds done in the body the term 'Heaven'… must include more kingdoms than one." As they meditated, "the Lord touched the eyes of our understandings" — and the heavens opened. What followed is the most expansive vision in the canon: the living Christ, the fall of Lucifer, the sons of perdition, and the three kingdoms of glory. The Saints called it simply **"the Vision."**

**Binding idea:** "And now, after the many testimonies which have been given of him, this is the testimony, last of all, which we give of him: That **he lives**! For we saw him, even on the right hand of God" (76:22-23). Everything else in the Vision — the kingdoms, the gradations, the perdition — radiates out from a single eyewitness fact: *Christ lives*, and the universe of salvation is ordered around Him.

**Read:** "The Lord is God, and beside him there is no Savior" (76:1). "To them will I reveal all mysteries… by my Spirit will I enlighten them… even those things which eye has not seen, nor ear heard, nor yet entered into the heart of man" (76:7,10). "By the power of the Spirit our eyes were opened and our understandings were enlightened, so as to see and understand the things of God" (76:12). "We beheld the glory of the Son, on the right hand of the Father, and received of his fulness" (76:20). "This is the testimony, last of all, which we give of him: That he lives! For we saw him, even on the right hand of God… That by him, and through him, and of him, the worlds are and were created, and the inhabitants thereof are begotten sons and daughters unto God" (76:22-24). "An angel of God who was in authority in the presence of God, who rebelled against the Only Begotten Son… was thrust down… and was called Perdition, for the heavens wept over him—he was Lucifer, a son of the morning" (76:25-26). On the sons of perdition: "having denied the Holy Spirit after having received it… the only ones on whom the second death shall have any power" (76:35,37); "he saves all except them" (76:44). On the celestial: "they who received the testimony of Jesus, and believed on his name and were baptized… and who overcome by faith, and are sealed by the Holy Spirit of promise" (76:51,53); "they are they who are the church of the Firstborn" (76:54); "they are gods, even the sons of God" (76:58); "these are they whose bodies are celestial, whose glory is that of the sun" (76:70). On the terrestrial: "these are they who died without law… honorable men of the earth, who were blinded by the craftiness of men… these are they who are not valiant in the testimony of Jesus… they obtain not the crown… even as the moon differs from the sun" (76:72,75,78-79). On the telestial: "the glory of the stars differs from that of the glory of the moon" (76:81); "these are they who received not the gospel of Christ, neither the testimony of Jesus" (76:82); "they were as innumerable as the stars… or as the sand upon the seashore" (76:109); "they shall be servants of the Most High; but where God and Christ dwell they cannot come, worlds without end" (76:112). "Every man shall receive according to his own works, his own dominion, in the mansions which are prepared" (76:111). "These… are only to be seen and understood by the power of the Holy Spirit, which God bestows on those who love him, and purify themselves before him" (76:116).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "That he lives! For we saw him" (76:22-24) — the testimony at the center.* Before the kingdoms, before the gradations, the Vision plants its anchor: a living, *seen* Christ. "This is the testimony, last of all… That he lives! For we saw him, even on the right hand of God" (76:22-23). (The whole vast architecture of glory hangs from an *eyewitness fact* — not a theology of the afterlife reasoned out, but two men who "saw him… and with whom we conversed in the heavenly vision" [76:14]. The walk's faith-precedes-sight thread [§5→§17→§63] reaches its reward here: these are men who, having believed, were *shown* — sight as faith's fruit. And the testimony is "last of all… *which we give of him*" — Joseph and Sidney adding their witness to "the many testimonies which have been given of him" across the canon, the living chain of those who *saw*. The is-Christ-the-Creator thread [§14:9 "a light which cannot be hid"] reaches its climax in 76:24: "by him, and through him, and of him, the worlds are and were created, and the inhabitants thereof are *begotten sons and daughters unto God*." Christ the Creator of worlds [Moses 1] — and we are God's *offspring*, begotten through the Atonement. The Vision's foundation is not a map of heaven; it's a *Person*, alive, who made the worlds and calls their inhabitants His children.)
- *★ The graded salvation — "heaven must include more kingdoms than one" (76:50-112).* The doctrinal revolution. Against the binary of Christendom — saved or damned, heaven or hell — the Vision reveals that God "rewards every man according to his works" with a *fitted* glory, so salvation is *graded*, three kingdoms ascending like sun, moon, and stars (1 Cor 15:40-42, Paul's own three glories made literal):
  - **Celestial** (76:50-70) — those who "received the testimony of Jesus… were baptized… overcome by faith, and are sealed by the Holy Spirit of promise" (76:51-53): the church of the Firstborn, who dwell in God's presence, "whose glory is that of the sun."
  - **Terrestrial** (76:71-80) — "the moon differs from the sun": those who died without law, the honorable "blinded by the craftiness of men," and those "not valiant in the testimony of Jesus" (76:79); they "receive of the presence of the Son, but not of the fulness of the Father" (76:77).
  - **Telestial** (76:81-112) — "the stars differ": those who "received not the gospel," even "liars, and sorcerers, and adulterers" (76:103), "thrust down to hell" until the last resurrection (76:84-85) — *and yet* heirs of a salvation whose glory "surpasses all understanding" (76:89), "innumerable as the stars… or as the sand upon the seashore" (76:109).
  (This is the §19:6-12 door walked through. §19 redefined "eternal/endless punishment" as *God's* punishment, not infinite duration — dissolving the Calvinist terror of a uniform hell. §76 builds the positive structure on that cleared ground: not one heaven and one hell, but a *spectrum of glory*, each soul rendered the kingdom it is *fitted* for. The justice is exact — "every man shall receive according to his own works, his own dominion, in the mansions which are prepared" [76:111, the stewardship-accounting of §70:4/§72:3 cashed out at the judgment]. The mercy is staggering — *almost everyone is saved to some glory*; even the telestial, the murderers and liars, after suffering "the wrath of God… until the last resurrection," inherit a glory beyond mortal understanding. The God of the Vision is *more just and more merciful at once* than the binary allowed: He fits the reward to the life with perfect precision, and the floor of that fitting is a glory that "surpasses all understanding." Joseph said of this section it was "a transcript from the records of the eternal world.")
- *★ "They are gods, even the sons of God" (76:58) — exaltation, the matter-spectrum's summit.* The celestial inheritors are not merely *admitted* to glory — they are *exalted*: "they are gods, even the sons of God… all things are theirs… and they shall overcome all things" (76:58-60); "priests and kings, who have received of his fulness" (76:56); God "makes them equal in power, and in might, and in dominion" (76:95). (The matter-spectrum's anthropological *summit*: the thread that began with "all things… are spiritual" [§29:34] and "light groweth brighter… until the perfect day" [§50:24] and the earth transfigured [§63:20] and the seer quickened to bear God's presence [§67:11] arrives at its destination — *man's destiny is to become as God*, a son of God in the fullest sense, joint-heir with Christ [Rom 8:17], receiving "of his fulness." This is deification — not absorption into God but *becoming like Him*, "equal in power, and in might, and in dominion," because the Father's whole project is to raise His begotten children [76:24] to His own glory. The §76:58 "they are gods" is the King Follett doctrine in seed, and the temple's whole telos. And note what *qualifies* for it [76:51-53]: not greatness but the testimony of Jesus received, baptism, overcoming by faith, and the *seal of the Holy Spirit of promise* — the ratifying seal [the §2/§132 sealing thread, temple-adjacent], which I flag as a reserved temple connection, Michael's. Exaltation is offered to the *faithful ordinary*, sealed.)
- *★ The sons of perdition — the only truly lost (76:31-49).* The one category outside the universal mercy: those who "know my power, and have been made partakers thereof, and suffered themselves… to deny the truth and defy my power… having denied the Holy Spirit after having received it" (76:31,35). They are "the only ones on whom the second death shall have any power" (76:37); "he saves all except them" (76:44). (The terrible precision: perdition is *not* for the sinner, the ignorant, the weak, the unbeliever — *all* those inherit some glory. It is reserved for those who *fully knew* — "made partakers" of God's power, having *received* the Holy Spirit — and then turned in open warfare against the truth they knew, "crucified him unto themselves and put him to an open shame" [76:35]. It takes a *full light* to become a son of perdition; you cannot fall that far without first having risen very high [the §76:25-26 pattern — Lucifer himself "was in authority in the presence of God" before he fell, "a son of the morning"]. The mercy of the doctrine: the ordinary sinner is *nowhere near* perdition; the door to it requires a knowledge almost no one has. And even of perdition's end, "the end thereof, neither the place thereof… no man knows" [76:45] — the withheld again. For anyone tormented by the fear of being lost: the Vision says you have to *fight the known God in open war* to be lost — and almost no one does. The floor under the rest is glory.)
- *★ The gospel reaches past the grave (76:73-74, 86-88).* In the terrestrial are "the spirits of men kept in prison, whom the Son visited, and preached the gospel unto them… who received not the testimony of Jesus in the flesh, but afterwards received it" (76:73-74). And the kingdoms *minister* to one another: the telestial "receive of the Holy Spirit through the ministration of the terrestrial; and the terrestrial through the ministration of the celestial" (76:86-87). (The seed of §138 [the vision of the redemption of the dead] and §128 [baptism for the dead]: salvation is not sealed at death; the Son preached to the spirits in prison [1 Pet 3:19; 4:6], and those who "afterwards received it" are saved. The gospel's reach is *post-mortal* — the §2 Elijah/sealing thread's purpose ["the promises made to the fathers," turning hearts] begins to show its scope: God's redeeming work crosses the veil. And the kingdoms are not sealed-off tiers but a *ministering* order — celestial to terrestrial to telestial — light passed downward, the §50:24 light-economy structured into the eternities. Even the lowest glory is *ministered to* by the higher; no one is abandoned to darkness.)
- *The withheld fulness — "not lawful for man to utter" (76:114-118).* The Vision, for all it reveals, *withholds*: "great and marvelous are the works… which he showed unto us, which surpass all understanding… which he commanded us we should not write… and are not lawful for man to utter" (76:114-115). They are "only to be seen and understood by the power of the Holy Spirit, which God bestows on those who love him, and purify themselves before him" (76:116). (The reserved-knowledge thread [§25:4, §63:21] at the grandest scale: even *this* transcendent vision has a fulness held back, available only to the purified, "that… they may be able to bear his presence in the world of glory" [76:118 = §67:11, the capacity-to-bear-the-presence grown, not given]. The deepest things are not withheld by God's stinginess but by our *capacity* — they can only be *borne* by those quickened to bear them. I flag the withheld fulness of the Vision as a reserved thread, Michael's.)

**Notable / flag:** ★ **reserved threads (Michael's):** (1) 76:53 — the seal of the Holy Spirit of promise (the §2/§132 sealing/temple); (2) 76:114-118 — the withheld fulness of the Vision, "not lawful for man to utter." **Walk's contribution:** **§76 is the Vision — the canon's most expansive revelation, and the keystone of the degrees-of-glory thread.** Everything in it radiates from a single eyewitness fact: "this is the testimony, last of all… **That he lives! For we saw him**, even on the right hand of God" (76:22-23) — the living Christ, Creator of worlds, whose inhabitants are "begotten sons and daughters unto God" (76:24, the is-Christ-Creator thread's climax). From that center the Vision reveals a **graded salvation** that revolutionized the binary heaven/hell of Christendom: God rewards every soul with a *fitted* glory — celestial, terrestrial, telestial, "as the stars differ" (1 Cor 15:40-42 made literal) — so "heaven must include more kingdoms than one." It walks through the door §19:6-12 opened (eternal punishment = God's, not infinite duration), and the result is a God *more just and more merciful at once*: each receives exactly his "own dominion, in the mansions which are prepared" (76:111, the §70:4/§72:3 accounting cashed out), and *almost everyone is saved* — even the telestial glory "surpasses all understanding" (76:89). The **matter-spectrum reaches its summit**: the celestial "are gods, even the sons of God" (76:58), exalted to be "equal in power, and in might, and in dominion" — man's destiny is to become as God (§29:34 → §50:24 → §63:20 → §67:11 → §76:58). The **sons of perdition** are the *only* lost — and only those who *fully knew* and warred against the truth ("denied the Holy Spirit after having received it"), so the ordinary sinner is nowhere near it. And the **gospel reaches past the grave** (the Son preached to the spirits in prison; the kingdoms minister downward — the seed of §128/§138). For Michael: the section that says you were *seen* into existence by a living Christ who made the worlds and calls you His child — that almost no one is finally lost, the floor under nearly everyone being a glory beyond understanding — and that the faithful, sealed by the Spirit of promise, are bound for a glory like the sun, "gods, even the sons of God." The binary collapses; in its place, a universe of mercy graded with perfect justice.
