# D&C 38 — be one, or ye are not mine

**Setting:** **Fayette, New York, January 2, 1831** — a conference of the Church, weeks after the gathering command (§37). The Saints are about to uproot for Ohio, and persecution is rising. This revelation gives the *reasons* for the gathering and the *terms* of the Zion they're to build — and lands one of the most demanding sentences in the D&C: "be one; and if ye are not one ye are not mine" (38:27). It also drops the first word of the **endowment** (38:32).

**Binding idea:** Zion is *oneness* — and oneness is not sentiment but *economic and relational equality*: "let every man esteem his brother as himself" (38:24-25), illustrated by a father who would be *unjust* to clothe one son in robes and another in rags (38:26). "Be one; and if ye are not one ye are not mine" (38:27). The gathering to Ohio exists to make this people *one*, to give them the *law* (§42) and to *endow them with power from on high* (38:32).

**Read:** Christ, "the same which looked upon the wide expanse of eternity… before the world was made; the same which knoweth all things, for all things are present before mine eyes; I am the same which spake, and the world was made" (38:1-3). "I am the same which have taken the Zion of Enoch into mine own bosom" (38:4). "Mine eyes are upon you. I am in your midst and ye cannot see me; but the day soon cometh that ye shall see me… for the veil of darkness shall soon be rent" (38:7-8). "All flesh is corrupted before me" (38:11); "I show unto you a mystery, a thing which is had in secret chambers, to bring to pass even your destruction… and ye knew it not" (38:13). "I will be merciful unto your weakness" (38:14); "be ye strong from henceforth; fear not, for the kingdom is yours" (38:15). "The poor have complained before me, and the rich have I made, and all flesh is mine, and I am no respecter of persons" (38:16). "In time ye shall have no king nor ruler, for I will be your king and watch over you… ye shall have no laws but my laws when I come, for I am your lawgiver" (38:21-22). "Let every man esteem his brother as himself, and practice virtue and holiness before me" (38:24-25). The parable: a man with twelve sons who clothes one "in robes" and one "in rags"—"and looketh upon his sons and saith I am just?" (38:26). "This I have given unto you as a parable… I say unto you, **be one; and if ye are not one ye are not mine**" (38:27). "Let your preaching be the warning voice, every man to his neighbor, in mildness and in meekness" (38:41).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "Be one; and if ye are not one ye are not mine" (38:27) — and it means economic equality.* This is the hinge of the whole revelation and the foundation of Zion. Critically, the Lord does *not* leave "be one" abstract — He defines it with the parable of the twelve sons: a father who robes one son and rags another, treating his children unequally, *cannot say "I am just"* (38:26). So oneness is *not* mere agreement or affection; it is the refusal of the robes-and-rags distinction — *economic* equality, "esteem his brother as himself" (38:24-25), the poor lifted (38:16,35). And the stakes are absolute: "if ye are not one ye are **not mine**." Belonging to Christ is *conditioned on* being one with each other — there is no private discipleship that tolerates a divided, unequal people. (This completes the oneness thread: §35:2 was oneness *with God* — "one in me as I am one in the Father." §38:27 is oneness *with each other*, and the parable makes plain they are the *same* oneness: you cannot be one with the Father while robing one brother and ragging another. The vertical and horizontal unity are one fabric — which is exactly the consecration doctrine §42 and §51 will build, and the Enoch-Zion [38:4, "no poor among them," Moses 7:18] this people is meant to become.)
- *★ "All things are present before mine eyes" (38:2) — the eternal present.* "The same which knoweth all things, for all things are present before mine eyes; I am the same which spake, and the world was made" (38:2-3). God's omniscience is framed not as foreknowledge-of-the-future but as an *eternal present*: past and future are *present* to Him. (The matter-spectrum Christology again — the Creator "which spake, and the world was made" [§14:9, §29], here with His relation to *time*: He stands in a now that contains all nows. This grounds both His foreknowledge and His foreordination without erasing agency — He *sees* all things present, He doesn't *cause* them. And it's pastoral: "mine eyes are upon you" [38:7] — the all-seeing present-tense gaze is *watchful care*, the §6:16 omniscience-as-love.)
- *★ "Endowed with power from on high" (38:32) — the endowment's first word.* The gathering to Ohio has three named purposes: "I will give unto you my law" (→ §42, the law of consecration), "and there you shall be **endowed with power from on high**." This is the first mention in the Restoration of the *endowment* (Luke 24:49 — the disciples told to tarry in Jerusalem "until ye be endued with power from on high"). The temple-endowment thread begins here — the gathering is *for* an endowment of power, fulfilled at Kirtland (§110) and unfolded at Nauvoo. (I flag this as the opening of the reserved temple/endowment thread — Michael's + the Spirit's; here I mark only that 38:32 is where the word first sounds, tied to the gathering's purpose. The §27 Elijah/keys and §13 sons-of-Levi threads converge toward this.)
- *★ "The enemy in the secret chambers seeketh your lives" (38:13,28-29) — the threat is at home.* "A thing which is had in secret chambers, to bring to pass even your destruction… ye knew it not" (38:13); "the enemy in the secret chambers seeketh your lives… ye know not the hearts of men in your own land" (38:28-29). The secret-combinations thread (Gadianton, Ether 8 — the BoM walk's grim warning about conspiracies sworn in secret) is turned on the *present*: the danger isn't only distant wars (38:29) but conspiracy *close by*, hearts in "your own land" you can't read. (And it's part of *why* gather: scattered Saints are exposed to the secret enemy; gathered and made one, they're a fortified people. The §37 garrison-refuge logic, now with a named threat.)
- *★ "I will be your king… and watch over you… I am your lawgiver" (38:21-22).* The Zion-government is theocracy: no human king, "for I will be your king and watch over you… ye shall have no laws but my laws when I come." God Himself as king, watcher, and lawgiver. (The presiding/watching thread [Abr 4:18] at the *divine* scale — the Lord watches over His own people directly, the top of the chain of watches. And note the freedom in it, 38:22: "you shall be a free people" — God's kingship *liberates* rather than subjugates; His law is the condition of freedom, not its abolition. The theocracy is the opposite of tyranny — the §26 common-consent people whose only King governs without compulsion.)
- *"Beware of pride, lest ye become as the Nephites of old" (38:39).* Even as the Lord promises "the riches of eternity," He flags the precise danger: *pride* — the Nephite cycle (riches → pride → fall, 4 Ne / Helaman, the BoM walk). The promised abundance is a *test*; the warning is built into the promise. (The consecration economy of "be one" is the *answer* to the Nephite pride-cycle: a people with no robes-and-rags distinction cannot stratify into the pride that destroyed the Nephites. Equality is the structural guard against the wealth-pride that fells covenant peoples.)
