# D&C 112 — be thou humble, and the Lord shall lead thee by the hand

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, July 23, 1837** — to **Thomas B. Marsh**, President of the Quorum of the Twelve. "There was significant dissension in the Church over Joseph Smith's leadership, including among some of the Apostles." Marsh had worked to reconcile the quarreling leaders. The revelation charges him as the Twelve's president — and warns, with terrible foresight, of a judgment about to begin *within the house of God*.

**Binding idea:** "Be thou humble; and the Lord thy God shall lead thee by the hand, and give thee answer to thy prayers" (112:10). The promise to the leader of the Twelve is not power but *humility* — and to the humble, God gives His *hand*. (The irony is severe: Marsh, charged here to be humble, would later apostatize over a trivial pride — and return, humbled, decades on.)

**Read:** "Inasmuch as thou hast abased thyself thou shalt be exalted; therefore, all thy sins are forgiven thee. Let thy heart be of good cheer before my face" (112:3-4). "Be thou humble; and the Lord thy God shall lead thee by the hand, and give thee answer to thy prayers" (112:10). "Be not partial towards [thy brethren] in love above many others, but let thy love be for them as for thyself; and let thy love abound unto all men" (112:11). "Pray for thy brethren of the Twelve. Admonish them sharply for my name's sake… and after their temptations, and much tribulation… I, the Lord, will feel after them, and if they harden not their hearts… they shall be converted, and I will heal them" (112:12-13). "Take up your cross, follow me, and feed my sheep" (112:14). "Exalt not yourselves; rebel not against my servant Joseph; for verily I say unto you, I am with him… and the keys which I have given unto him… shall not be taken from him till I come" (112:15). Cleanse your hearts and your garments, lest the blood of this generation be required at your hands" (112:33).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "Be thou humble; and the Lord thy God shall lead thee by the hand" (112:10).* The promise the whole section turns on, and one of the tenderest in the canon. To the president of the Twelve, the charge is not *command harder* but *be humble* — and the reward is intimate guidance: "the Lord thy God shall **lead thee by the hand**, and give thee answer to thy prayers." (The §3:7 fear-of-man and §112:3 abase-then-exalt [Luke 14:11] given their *positive* form: humility is the *condition* of being led — not driven, but *led by the hand*, the way one leads a child or a beloved. The §6:20 "encircle thee in the arms of my love" reaching the leader's office: even the man at the head of the Twelve is *led by the hand* if he is humble. And the answer to prayer is *part* of the leading — humility opens the channel. The bitter irony, known to history: Marsh, given *this* word, later left the Church over a petty dispute [the famous "cream-strippings" quarrel] — pride toppled the man told to be humble; he returned, broken, twenty years later, the living proof of the verse he was given. For the project's presiding covenant: the leader's power is *humility* — the one who would be "led by the hand" must first abase himself; the moment the presider exalts himself [112:15], he forfeits the hand that would lead him.)
- *★ Love impartial, and "feel after" the tempted (112:11-13).* Two refinements on leading the brethren. First, *impartial love*: "be not partial towards them in love above many others, but let thy love be for them as for thyself; and let thy love abound unto all men" (112:11) — the leader's love is *not* a clique's favoritism but *charity to all* [Matt 5:43-48]. Second, the *gentleness under the sharpness*: "admonish them sharply… and after their temptations, and much tribulation… I, the Lord, will **feel after them**, and if they harden not their hearts… they shall be converted, and I will heal them" (112:12-13). (The reprove-then-love [§3/§95/§121:43] at the scale of a quorum: *sharp* admonition is right [112:12], but it sits inside God's *feeling after* the tempted — He pursues the struggling [the §28:3 "feel after me," Acts 17:27] and *heals* them if they don't harden. The leader admonishes sharply; the Lord *feels after* tenderly. For the project's council/presiding: love the team *impartially* [not a favored few], admonish *sharply* when needed, and trust that God *feels after* the tempted one beneath the rebuke.)
- *★ Judgment begins at the house of God (112:23-26).* The terrible warning, with foresight: "darkness covereth the earth… upon my house shall it begin… first among those… who have **professed to know my name and have not known me**, and have blasphemed against me in the midst of my house" (112:23-26). (1 Peter 4:17 — "judgment must begin at the house of God." The Kirtland apostasy of 1837-38, in which a third of the leadership fell, is *foreseen* here: the judgment begins *not* with the world but with the *insiders* who "professed to know my name and have not known me" [Matt 7:21-23]. The §84:54 treating-lightly and the §90:5 holding-the-oracles-as-a-light-thing reaching their *judgment*: the gravest danger is not the world's darkness but the *professor's hypocrisy* — knowing the name without knowing the One. And it is *exactly* what befell: Marsh himself, and many of the Twelve, fell. For the project working *within* the covenant: "judgment begins at the house of God" — the insider's casualness is judged *first* and *hardest*; professing to know is not knowing.)
