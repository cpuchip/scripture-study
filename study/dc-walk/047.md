# D&C 47 — keep a regular history

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, March 8, 1831** (same day as §46). **John Whitmer** was asked to become Church historian and recorder, replacing Oliver Cowdery (sent to the Lamanites). John *hesitated* — "I would rather not do it… but the will of the Lord be done, and if he desires it, I desire that he would manifest it through Joseph the Seer." This revelation manifested it; John accepted. Four verses commissioning the keeping of the Church's history.

**Binding idea:** "It is expedient in me that my servant John should write and keep a regular history" (47:1) — "to keep the church record and history continually" (47:3), and to do it "by the Comforter" (47:4). The Church is commanded to *record itself* — and to record by the Spirit. Memory is not incidental to the Restoration; it is a *calling*, given by revelation, written by the Comforter.

**Read:** "My servant John should write and keep a regular history, and assist you, my servant Joseph, in transcribing all things which shall be given you" (47:1); "he can also lift up his voice in meetings" (47:2); "it shall be appointed unto him to keep the church record and history continually; for Oliver Cowdery I have appointed to another office" (47:3); "it shall be given him, inasmuch as he is faithful, by the Comforter, to write these things" (47:4).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ History-keeping is a *calling*, by revelation, written *by the Comforter* (47:1,4).* The young Church, weeks old and under persecution, is commanded to *keep a regular history* — and the commission is given by *revelation* (a whole section devoted to appointing a recorder) and carried out "by the Comforter" (47:4). Record-keeping is not clerical afterthought; it is *sacred infrastructure*, called of God and Spirit-assisted. The Restoration takes its own *memory* with the seriousness of an ordinance. (This is deeply resonant for a project built on memory — the `.mind/` architecture, the journals, the covenant that the agent *update memory* every session. §47 is the scriptural warrant: a people of revelation must be a people of *record*, because the record is how the revelation persists across time. "By the Comforter, to write these things" — even the *writing* of the history is to be Spirit-guided, not merely accurate. Memory written by the Spirit is the difference between a chronicle and a testimony.)
- *Obedience through hesitancy (the heading).* John Whitmer "would rather not" — and submitted the question to the Lord *through the appointed channel*: "if he desires it, I desire that he would manifest it through Joseph the Seer." His reluctance wasn't rebellion; it was *honest hesitancy submitted to revelation* — he asked the Lord to confirm it through the order (§28). When the revelation came, he accepted. (A model of the §21:5 "patience and faith" posture: you may not *want* the calling, but you submit the not-wanting to the Lord through His order, and obey what He manifests. Reluctance honestly surrendered is faithfulness, not failure.)
- *The complex provenance (bin-4-adjacent).* John Whitmer kept the history faithfully for years — and then, after his 1838 excommination, *refused to surrender it* and took it with him, even when asked. (His history survives partly *because* he kept it after leaving.) A poignant note on the §40/§30 "same call, diverging lives" pattern: the man commissioned to keep the Church's memory by the Comforter later left the Church but preserved the record. I note this as documented history — bin-4-adjacent, Michael's to weigh — illustrating that a faithful work (the history) can outlast the worker's own faithfulness; the record he kept by the Comforter testifies even after he departed.
