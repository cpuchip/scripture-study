# D&C 13 — the priesthood returns to the earth

**Setting:** Near Harmony, Pennsylvania, on the bank of the Susquehanna River, **May 15, 1829.** Joseph and Oliver, translating the Book of Mormon, came upon 3 Nephi's teaching on baptism and went into the woods to pray about it. An angel descended in a cloud of light, laid his hands on them, and ordained them. He announced himself as **John the Baptist**, acting under the direction of **Peter, James, and John** (the keys-holders from §7). The text is a single verse — the angel's own words — extracted from Joseph Smith's history (JS—H 1:68-75). It is the first restoration of priesthood authority to the earth in this dispensation.

**Binding idea:** Authority is not assumed, inherited by reading, or self-generated — it is *conferred*, by hands, from someone who holds it. "Upon you my fellow servants… I confer the Priesthood of Aaron" (13:1). The Restoration's claim turns on this: the power to act in God's name had to be *given back*, by a messenger sent for that purpose.

**Read (the whole section):** "Upon you my fellow servants, in the name of Messiah I confer the Priesthood of Aaron, which holds the keys of the ministering of angels, and of the gospel of repentance, and of baptism by immersion for the remission of sins; and this shall never be taken again from the earth, until the sons of Levi do offer again an offering unto the Lord in righteousness."

**Thoughts:**
- *"I confer" — authority comes by conferral, not by claim (13:1).* The single most consequential word in the verse is the verb. John the Baptist doesn't *recognize* an authority Joseph already had; he *confers* one Joseph did not. This is the hinge of the whole Restoration's logic: the long apostasy was, centrally, a *loss of authority* — the forms of religion continued, but the power to bind and baptize had to come back by a literal sending. (It closes a thread from the PoGP walk: the priesthood line "without beginning of days or end of years," handed down "from the fathers" — Moses 6:7, Abr 1:3 — broke in the meridian apostasy and is here *re-spliced* by the very man who baptized Christ. And it answers JS—H: Joseph, told in 1820 that the churches "were all wrong," receives in 1829 the positive remedy — not a corrected creed but restored *authority*.)
- *The three keys of the Aaronic Priesthood (13:1).* The verse defines the office by its keys: (1) **the ministering of angels** — the Aaronic order opens the heavens to messengers; (2) **the gospel of repentance**; (3) **baptism by immersion for the remission of sins.** The "preparatory priesthood" (D&C 84:26-27) — it governs the *entry* ordinances and the *turning* (repentance, baptism), the threshold of the covenant path. Notably the *ministering of angels* belongs here: the lesser priesthood is the one that brings heaven *near* in the form of sent messengers — fitting, since an angel is conferring it.
- *"Shall never be taken again from the earth" — the dispensation of the fulness (13:1).* Every prior dispensation ended in apostasy; the authority came and went. This one carries a promise the others didn't: it will *not* be lost again. The Restoration is framed as terminal — the last gathering-up, not another cycle. (This is the structural claim behind "the dispensation of the fulness of times," D&C 27, 128 — the priesthood restored *to stay*.)
- *"Until the sons of Levi… offer again an offering in righteousness" — the one temporal limit (13:1, Malachi 3:3).* The single qualification on "never taken again" reaches back to **Malachi 3:3** — "he shall purify the sons of Levi… that they may offer unto the Lord an offering in righteousness." This is a *temple* clause. It ties §13 to §2 (Moroni's Malachi, Elijah and the sealing power): the Aaronic Priesthood's full restoration anticipates a future temple-order completion ("the sons of Levi" offering) that belongs to the same sealing/temple project Elijah's keys inaugurate. The lesser priesthood is restored pointing *toward* its temple fulfillment. (I flag the "sons of Levi" offering as a genuinely forward-looking, not-yet-fully-understood clause — the kind of reserved-knowledge thread the PoGP facsimiles guarded; its full meaning is among the things "yet to be revealed," and is properly Michael's + the Spirit's to weigh, not mine to resolve.)
- *"My fellow servants" — the angel's humility (13:1).* John the Baptist, the forerunner who baptized the Son of God, calls these two young men "my fellow servants." The greater messenger addresses the lesser as equals in the work. (The §1:38 principle from the other end — the hierarchy of heaven runs on *fellowship in service*, not rank pulled.)
