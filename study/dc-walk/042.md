# D&C 42 — the Law

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, February 9 and 23, 1831** — given in two parts (vv. 1-72, then 73-93) before twelve elders, in fulfillment of the promise that "my law" would be given when the Saints gathered to Ohio (§38:32). Joseph called it "the law of the Church." It is the constitution of Zion's *life* — how the Church teaches, how its members live morally, how they hold property (the law of consecration), how the sick are cared for, and how offenses are handled. One of the foundational revelations of the Restoration.

**Binding idea:** The "law" the gathering was *for* (§38:32) turns out to be the practical architecture of a Zion people: **consecrate your property and become a steward** (42:30-34), **teach only by the Spirit** (42:14), **keep the moral law** (42:18-29), **heal by faith within God's appointment** (42:43-52), and **handle offenses privately and proportionately** (42:88-92). The "be one" of §38:27 gets its *statutes*.

**Read (the load-bearing pieces):** Elders go "two by two" (42:6); none preaches unless "ordained by some one who has authority… known to the church" (42:11). On *teaching*: "teach the principles of my gospel, which are in the Bible and the Book of Mormon… as they shall be directed by the Spirit. And the Spirit shall be given unto you by the prayer of faith; **and if ye receive not the Spirit ye shall not teach**" (42:12-14). The moral law: "Thou shalt not kill… Thou shalt not steal… Thou shalt not lie… Thou shalt love thy wife with all thy heart, and shalt cleave unto her and none else… Thou shalt not commit adultery… Thou shalt not speak evil of thy neighbor" (42:18-27). The **law of consecration**: "thou wilt remember the poor, and consecrate of thy properties for their support… with a covenant and a deed which cannot be broken" (42:30); "every man shall be made accountable unto me, a steward over his own property" (42:32); the residue "kept in my storehouse, to administer to the poor and the needy" (42:34); "inasmuch as ye do it unto the least of these, ye do it unto me" (42:38). "Thou shalt not be proud in thy heart; let all thy garments be plain… Thou shalt not be idle" (42:40-42). On the *sick*: "whosoever among you are sick, and have not faith to be healed, but believe, shall be nourished with all tenderness, with herbs and mild food" (42:43); the elders lay on hands, "and if they die they shall die unto me, and if they live they shall live unto me" (42:44); "those that die in me shall not taste of death, for it shall be sweet unto them" (42:46); "he that hath faith in me to be healed, **and is not appointed unto death**, shall be healed" (42:48). "If thou shalt ask, thou shalt receive revelation upon revelation, knowledge upon knowledge, that thou mayest know the mysteries and **peaceable things**—that which bringeth joy" (42:61). On *offenses*: "if thy brother or sister offend thee, thou shalt take him or her between him or her and thee alone; and if he or she confess thou shalt be reconciled" (42:88); "if any one offend openly… rebuked openly… If any shall offend in secret… rebuked in secret" (42:91-92).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ The law of consecration (42:30-34) — the Zion economy §38:27 demanded.* "Be one; and if ye are not one ye are not mine" (§38:27) was the *principle*; here is its *mechanism*. The member **consecrates** his property "with a covenant and a deed which cannot be broken" (42:30), then **receives back a stewardship** "sufficient for himself and family" (42:32) — he is "made accountable unto me, a steward over his own property." The *residue* goes to the bishop's **storehouse** "to administer to the poor and the needy… that every man who has need may be amply supplied" (42:33-34). So consecration is *not* communism (no abolition of stewardship — each remains an accountable steward) and *not* capitalism (no accumulation of residue — the surplus serves the poor). It is **stewardship under covenant**: you own nothing absolutely (it's all consecrated), you steward what you need, and what exceeds your need belongs to the needy. And the warrant is the §38:27 / Matt 25 identification: "inasmuch as ye do it unto the least of these, ye do it unto me" (42:38) — the poor *are* Christ; consecration is loving Him by lifting them. (This is the same stewardship pattern the whole project rests on — *received, not owned; accountable; the surplus serves* — at the scale of a people's economy. The covenant.yaml stewardship is D&C 42's consecration in miniature: you steward the code within the intent, accountable, the residue [the soundness] serving the whole.)
- *★ "If ye receive not the Spirit ye shall not teach" (42:14) — the teaching covenant made law.* This is the project's teaching extension stated as a *commandment*. Teaching is conditioned on *having the Spirit*: "the Spirit shall be given unto you by the prayer of faith; and if ye receive not the Spirit ye shall not teach." Not "teach better with the Spirit" but "**don't teach** without it." Teaching the gospel from an unfilled vessel is *forbidden*, not merely suboptimal. (This is §11:21 "obtain before declare" and the teaching covenant's discovery-not-performance, hardened into law. For the Ben Test: the discipline of not teaching past what you've actually received by the Spirit isn't a best-practice — it's the *law of the Church*. And it pairs with the §36:2/§42:61 "peaceable things" — the Spirit you must have before teaching is the one that teaches *peace*, so teaching that breeds contention or self-display is teaching without the Spirit by its fruit.)
- *★ Healing by faith — within God's appointment (42:43-52).* The healing law is strikingly *balanced*. The sick are first "nourished with all tenderness, with herbs and mild food" (42:43 — natural means, not despised); elders lay on hands (42:44); but the outcome is held in *two* hands: "if they die they shall die unto me, and if they live they shall live unto me" (42:44), and "he that hath faith… **and is not appointed unto death**, shall be healed" (42:48). Healing is faith *and* God's appointment — not a mechanism faith can force. Some are "appointed unto death" (42:48, the footnote → Alma 12:27, D&C 121:25), and for them faith yields not healing but a death that "shall be sweet" (42:46). (This is crucial against a faith-healing triumphalism: faith does not *guarantee* healing, because God's will [appointment] is the other variable. The §9 study-it-out epistemology applied to healing — you exercise faith, but the result is confirmed or withheld by God's will, not compelled by your believing. And the tenderness frame [42:43, "herbs and mild food… not by the hand of an enemy"] keeps it humane: faith *and* medicine *and* love, the sick cared for whether they live or die.)
- *★ The Matt-18 offense law (42:88-92) — private and proportionate.* The Church's disciplinary law is Matthew 18: "if thy brother or sister offend thee, thou shalt take him or her between him or her and thee alone; and if he or she confess thou shalt be reconciled" (42:88). And it scales by the *scope* of the offense: a *secret* offense is "rebuked in secret" (42:92), an *open* one "rebuked openly" (42:91) — the discipline *matched* to the wrong, never broader. This is the §28:11 private-correction principle (Hiram Page, "between him and thee alone") made the Church's *standing law*, and the presiding covenant's persuasion-and-proportion encoded: reprove no more publicly than the offense was public, aim always at *reconciliation* (42:88) and *confession* (42:92), not exposure. (Reproof with sharpness "when moved upon by the Holy Ghost" then increase of love [121:43] — here as a graduated, reconciliation-seeking process, the opposite of public shaming. The §92 detail is tender: a secret offender gets a secret rebuke "that the church may not speak reproachfully of him" — the discipline protects the offender's name even while correcting him.)
- *The moral law, restated with Restoration edges (42:18-29).* The Decalogue is renewed — no kill, steal, lie, adultery, "speak evil of thy neighbor" — with sharpenings: lust "shall deny the faith, and shall not have the Spirit" (42:23, Matt 5:28 internalized); adultery forgiven *once* if forsaken "and doeth it no more," not twice (42:25-26); "love thy wife with all thy heart, and… cleave unto her and none else" (42:22 — fidelity as positive command, not just prohibition). (The law of a Zion people is the moral law *plus* the economic law [consecration] *plus* the teaching law [Spirit] *plus* the care law [healing/poor] — a whole architecture of holiness, not a list of don'ts. "Be one" requires *all* of it.)
- *"Revelation upon revelation… the peaceable things" (42:61).* The promise to the asker: "revelation upon revelation, knowledge upon knowledge, that thou mayest know the mysteries and peaceable things—that which bringeth joy, that which bringeth life eternal." Line-upon-line (§1:25-28) as an *ascending* gift — each revelation founds the next, toward "life eternal." And again "peaceable things" (§36:2, §39:6) — the consistent texture of true revelation is *peace and joy*, not agitation. (The discernment thread's quiet refrain, now in the Law itself: what God reveals brings *joy*; the counterfeit's signature is its absence.)
