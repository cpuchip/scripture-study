# D&C 102 — the high council, with justice built in

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, February 17, 1834** — not a "thus saith the Lord" revelation but the **minutes** of the organization of the first **high council** of the Church (recorded by Oliver Cowdery and Orson Hyde, revised by Joseph the next day, accepted unanimously as "a form and constitution of the high council"). It is a *procedural* document — and what it reveals is a council designed, from the ground up, to deliver *justice* and *correctability*.

**Binding idea:** "The accused, in all cases, has a right to one-half of the council, to prevent insult or injustice" (102:15). The council is structured so that *fairness is built into its very form*: half its members are appointed to *defend* the accused, both sides speak, and the president's decision can be *corrected* by the council. Justice is engineered into the procedure, not left to goodwill.

**Read:** "The high council was appointed by revelation for the purpose of settling important difficulties which might arise in the church, which could not be settled by the church or the bishop's council" (102:2). "The twelve councilors shall consider whether it is a difficult one or not; if it is not, two only… shall speak… if difficult, four… if more difficult, six" (102:13-14). "The accused, in all cases, has a right to one-half of the council, to prevent insult or injustice" (102:15). "Every man is to speak according to equity and justice" (102:16). "Those councilors who draw even numbers… are the individuals who are to stand up in behalf of the accused, and prevent insult and injustice" (102:17). "The accuser and the accused shall have a privilege of speaking for themselves" (102:18). "The president shall give a decision… and call upon the twelve councilors to sanction the same by their vote" (102:19). "But should the remaining councilors… discover an error in the decision of the president, they can manifest it, and the case shall have a re-hearing" (102:20). "And if, after a careful re-hearing, any additional light is shown upon the case, the decision shall be altered accordingly" (102:21). "In case of difficulty respecting doctrine or principle, if there is not a sufficiency written… the president may inquire and obtain the mind of the Lord by revelation" (102:23).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ Justice engineered into the form (102:15-18).* The council's most striking feature: *half of it is the defense*. "The accused… has a right to one-half of the council, to prevent insult or injustice" (102:15), and the even-numbered councilors "are to stand up in behalf of the accused" (102:17). Both accuser and accused "speak for themselves" (102:18), and "every man is to speak according to equity and justice" (102:16). (This is *structural* fairness — not "be fair" as exhortation but fairness *built into the seating chart*: a guaranteed advocacy for the accused, regardless of guilt, so that no one is steamrolled. The §50/§88:122 council pattern [each edified, equal privilege] given a *judicial* form with *adversarial balance* engineered in. For the project's council discipline: the deepest version of "surface the tensions" / "build the case against the thesis" — *appoint someone to defend the position you're inclined to condemn*. Half the council exists to prevent injustice, by design. Goodwill is not trusted to produce fairness; the *structure* is built to produce it. The devil's-advocate / red-team is not optional garnish but *half the council*.)
- *★ The decision is correctable — and revelation is the floor (102:20-23).* The president decides — *and* the council can overturn him: "should the remaining councilors… discover an error in the decision of the president, they can manifest it, and the case shall have a re-hearing… if any additional light is shown… the decision shall be altered accordingly" (102:20-21). And when the written word runs out: "the president may inquire and obtain the mind of the Lord by revelation" (102:23). (Two safeguards on the presiding officer. First, his decision is *reviewable* — even the president's judgment is subject to correction "if any additional light is shown," and the case is *altered* accordingly [the §9:8 study-then-confirm at the scale of a court; new light reopens the verdict]. Second, when human procedure and the written word are *insufficient*, the recourse is *revelation* — "the mind of the Lord." The presiding covenant's anti-tyranny [§121:41] built into the court: the president presides, but is *not* infallible and is *not* the final word — new light corrects him, and the Lord's mind is the floor. For the project: the decision-maker's call is *correctable by new light*, and the deepest recourse when the record is silent is to *inquire of the Lord* — not to bluff a ruling. A presiding decision held open to correction is a feature, not a weakness.)
- *Graduated effort to the difficulty (102:13-14).* A small but wise design: "if it is not [difficult], two only… shall speak… if difficult, four… if more difficult, six; but in no case shall more than six" (102:13-14). (The §76-style depth-scaling [Michael's "depth-scaled-to-section"]: effort is *matched to the difficulty* — a simple case gets two voices, a hard one gets six, but never more than six [bounded even at the top]. The council does not over-deliberate the easy case nor under-deliberate the hard one; the procedure *scales*. For the project: match the deliberation to the weight of the question — neither over-process the trivial nor rush the grave.)
