# D&C 50 — that which is of God is light

**Setting:** **Kirtland, Ohio, May 9, 1831.** "So-called spiritual phenomena were not uncommon among the members" — some claiming visions, falling into trances, manifesting strange "spirits." The elders "did not understand the manifestations of different spirits abroad," and Joseph inquired. This revelation is the Lord's answer, and it is the **capstone of the walk's discernment thread**: how to tell the true Spirit from the false, stated as a *test* and a *protocol*, and grounded in one of the most luminous verses in the canon.

**Binding idea:** "That which doth not edify is not of God, and is darkness. **That which is of God is light; and he that receiveth light, and continueth in God, receiveth more light; and that light groweth brighter and brighter until the perfect day**" (50:23-24). The test of the true Spirit is *edification* — does it build up, give light? And the trajectory of the true Spirit is *growth* — light upon light, toward the perfect day. Truth is not only recognized by its light; it is *generative* of more light.

**Read:** "There are many spirits which are false spirits, which have gone forth in the earth, deceiving the world… Satan hath sought to deceive you" (50:2-3). "Come… by the Spirit… and let us reason together… even as a man reasoneth one with another face to face" (50:10-11); "even so will I, the Lord, reason with you that you may understand" (50:12). "Unto what were ye ordained? To preach my gospel by the Spirit, even the Comforter which was sent forth to teach the truth" (50:13-14). The test: "he that is ordained of me and sent forth to preach the word of truth by the Comforter… doth he preach it by the Spirit of truth or some other way? And if it be by some other way it is not of God" (50:17-18); "he that receiveth the word of truth, doth he receive it by the Spirit of truth or some other way? If it be some other way it is not of God" (50:19-20). "He that preacheth and he that receiveth, understand one another, and both are edified and rejoice together" (50:22). "That which doth not edify is not of God, and is darkness. That which is of God is light; and he that receiveth light, and continueth in God, receiveth more light; and that light groweth brighter and brighter until the perfect day" (50:23-24) — "that you may know the truth, that you may chase darkness from among you" (50:25). The protocol: "if you behold a spirit manifested that you cannot understand, and you receive not that spirit, ye shall ask of the Father in the name of Jesus; and if he give not unto you that spirit, then you may know that it is not of God" (50:31) — proclaim against it "not with railing accusation… neither with boasting nor rejoicing, lest you be seized therewith" (50:32-33). "If ye are purified and cleansed from all sin, ye shall ask whatsoever you will in the name of Jesus and it shall be done" (50:29). "Ye are little children and ye cannot bear all things now; ye must grow in grace and in the knowledge of the truth. Fear not, little children, for you are mine, and I have overcome the world" (50:40-41); "I am the good shepherd, and the stone of Israel" (50:44).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "That which is of God is light… brighter and brighter until the perfect day" (50:24) — the matter-spectrum's growth law, and the discernment test.* This is the section's summit and one of the great verses of the Restoration. It does *two* things at once. As a **discernment test**: "that which doth not edify is not of God, and is darkness; that which is of God is light" (50:23-24) — you tell the true Spirit by whether it *gives light and builds up* (edifies). As a **growth law**: light, received and continued-in, *begets more light*, "brighter and brighter until the perfect day." Truth is not static recognition but a *trajectory* — the one who receives light and "continueth in God" is on an ascending path toward the perfect day (the becoming-perfect, the matter-spectrum's destination, §35:2 oneness, §29 spiritual). (For the walk's two great threads, this is where they *fuse*: the discernment thread [§28→§43→§45:57→§46→§50] and the matter-spectrum/light thread [§14:9, §29, the recurring "light which shineth in darkness"] meet in 50:24 — *the test of truth and the law of growth are the same light*. True revelation is recognized by its light and proven by the *more light* it generates. The period-language/Spirit-distillation principle reaches its capstone: the Spirit distills *light* on the prepared mind, and the mark of true distillation is that it *edifies* and *grows* — light upon light. A "revelation" that produces confusion, agitation, or darkness [the trance-manifestations of the setting] is, by this test, not of God. The fruit is light.)
- *★ The mutual-edification test (50:17-22).* The Lord poses it as a *question* the elders must answer themselves: when a true preacher (in the Spirit of truth) meets a true hearer (receiving by the Spirit of truth), "they understand one another, and both are **edified and rejoice together**" (50:22). The sign of genuine spiritual communication is *mutual understanding and shared edification* — both built up, both rejoicing. (This is profound and practical: the test of a true spiritual experience isn't its *intensity* [the trances and strange manifestations could be intense] but its *fruit in the body* — does it produce *mutual* understanding and edification, or private, incommunicable, unbuilding phenomena? The §46:30 "asketh in the Spirit" and the §42:14 "if ye receive not the Spirit ye shall not teach" complete here: when *both* sides are in the Spirit of truth, the communication *lands* and *builds*. A spiritual gift that edifies the body is true; one that puffs up the individual or confuses the body is suspect. The Spirit's signature is *shared light*.)
- *★ The practical protocol — ask, and if not given, it is not of God (50:31-32).* The Lord gives an actual *method* for a manifestation you can't place: (1) *don't receive it* — withhold judgment, don't embrace it; (2) *ask the Father in the name of Jesus*; (3) *if He gives it not unto you, you may know it is not of God.* The discernment isn't left to feeling or argument — it's a *prayer test*: bring the unknown spirit to God and ask Him to give it to you; His *withholding* is the verdict. (This completes the §9 study-it-out [propose, then ask] applied to *spirits*: you don't accept or reject on impression; you bring it to God and let *His* response decide. And the humility clause [50:33] is striking — even when rejecting a false spirit, "not with railing accusation… neither with boasting nor rejoicing, lest you be seized therewith." Discernment is not a license for spiritual triumphalism; the one who casts out a false spirit with pride is vulnerable to being "seized" himself. The §28:11 / §42:88 private-and-gentle correction extended to the spirit-world: discern, reject, but *humbly* — the proud discerner is the next target.)
- *★ "I will reason with you… face to face" (50:11-12) — the period-language principle at the source.* "Let us reason even as a man reasoneth one with another face to face… even so will I, the Lord, reason with you that you may understand" (the footnote ties 50:12 to **D&C 1:24** — "after the manner of their language"). God *condescends to reason at the human level* — face-to-face, as a man reasons with a man — *so that we can understand*. (This is the period-language principle [§1:24, Michael's articulation] stated by the Lord as His own *method*: He doesn't speak over our heads; He reasons *as a man reasons*, accommodating Himself to our frame "that you may understand." The whole revelation that follows — the careful question-and-answer logic of 50:13-22 — *is* God reasoning face-to-face. The condescension of revelation [§1:24] and the JST's recovery toward "mine own bosom" [§35:20] meet here: God speaks down to our level [50:12] precisely so the light can grow up toward the perfect day [50:24].)
- *"Ye are little children… fear not… I have overcome the world" (50:40-41) — the tender close.* After the rigorous discernment teaching, the Lord softens to *reassurance*: "ye are little children and ye cannot bear all things now; ye must grow in grace" (50:40, John 16:12) — the elders *aren't expected* to discern everything at once; they're *growing*, and growth in grace is the means. "Fear not, little children… you are mine… I have overcome the world… none of them that my Father hath given me shall be lost" (50:41-42). (The discernment teaching ends not in anxiety but in *security*: you won't get it all right yet, and that's allowed — you're little children growing in light [50:24], held by a Shepherd [50:44] who has already overcome. The §50:24 "brighter and brighter" is the *children's* growth — the discernment isn't a test you pass or fail once; it's a light you grow into, safe in the meantime.) And "the Father and I are one… ye are in me and I in you" (50:43) — the §35:2 oneness, the destination of the growing light.
