# D&C 11 — obtain the word before you declare it

**Setting:** Harmony, Pennsylvania, **May 1829** — to **Hyrum Smith**, Joseph's older brother, who came eager to join the work and preach. Joseph's history places this *after* the Aaronic Priesthood restoration (May 15). Hyrum wants to *do* something; the answer is, not yet — first *become* ready. (One of four near-identical "great and marvelous work" openings — §§11,12,14 all begin with the §6/§4 harvest preface; the distinct material is in the back half.)

**Binding idea:** "Seek not to declare my word, but first seek to obtain my word, and then shall your tongue be loosed" (11:21). The order is the whole lesson — *get* the word before you *give* it. Eagerness to teach runs ahead of the readiness to teach; the Lord slows Hyrum down to fill him first.

**Read:** The harvest preface (11:1-9, = §6). Then to Hyrum: "thou hast a gift, or thou shalt have a gift if thou wilt desire of me in faith… believing in the power of Jesus Christ… which speaketh unto thee" (11:10-11). "Put your trust in that Spirit which leadeth to do good — yea, to do justly, to walk humbly, to judge righteously; and this is my Spirit" (11:12) — Micah 6:8 made a *test of spirits*. "I will impart unto you of my Spirit, which shall enlighten your mind, which shall fill your soul with joy" (11:13). Then the restraint: "you need not suppose that you are called to preach until you are called" (11:15); "wait a little longer, until you shall have my word, my rock, my church, and my gospel, that you may know of a surety my doctrine" (11:16). **"Seek not to declare my word, but first seek to obtain my word, and then shall your tongue be loosed; then, if you desire, you shall have my Spirit and my word, yea, the power of God unto the convincing of men"** (11:21). "Study my word which hath gone forth among the children of men [the Bible], and also study my word which… is now translating [the Book of Mormon]" (11:22). "Deny not the spirit of revelation, nor the spirit of prophecy" (11:25). To all: "as many as receive me, to them will I give power to become the sons of God" (11:30).

**Thoughts:**
- *★ "Obtain the word before you declare it" (11:21) — the teaching covenant's scriptural root.* This is the exact discipline behind this project's teaching extension and the Ben Test: don't teach past what you've actually obtained. Hyrum's instinct — *I believe, let me go preach* — is good-hearted and premature. The Lord doesn't crush it; He *sequences* it: obtain → then your tongue is loosed. Preaching from an unfilled vessel is the performer-drift the teaching covenant guards against ("Am I still learning while I teach?"). And the obtaining is concrete: "study my word… and also study my word which… is now translating" (11:22) — read the Bible *and* the Book of Mormon, both. The readiness to teach is built by study, not by zeal. (This rhymes hard with §9: there, revelation crowns *study*, not the bare ask; here, teaching crowns *obtaining*, not the bare desire. The D&C keeps insisting the inward work comes first.)
- *Micah's three turned into a discernment test (11:12).* "That Spirit which leadeth to do good — to do justly, to walk humbly, to judge righteously… this is my Spirit." Micah 6:8 ("do justly, love mercy, walk humbly") is repurposed as a *spectrometer for spirits*: you know it's God's Spirit by where it leads — toward justice, humility, right judgment. A practical answer to §8's discernment seam (is it God or me?): test the *direction*. A prompting that leads to doing good, justly, humbly is His; the fruit identifies the source (Moroni 7 / Matt 7:16, the BoM walk). The interior signal (8:2) gets an external check (11:12) — what does it move you toward?
- *"You need not suppose you are called to preach until you are called" (11:15).* A quiet rebuke of self-commissioning. Desire opens the door (§4) but does not by itself ordain you to a *public* office — there's a difference between being called *to the work* (anyone who desires) and being called *to preach* (which awaits an actual call). Hyrum will get his call; he's told to wait for it, not assume it. (The honor_scope principle from the other side — don't reach for an office not yet given; cf. §5:4's "pretend to no other gift.")
- *The repeated preface is itself data.* Four sections (11, 12, 14, and earlier echoes) open with the same harvest text. The Lord is not being lazy; He's establishing a *liturgy of the call* — every new laborer hears the same words: the work is marvelous, the field is white, desire qualifies you, ask and receive. The sameness is the point: the call is *one call*, issued identically to Hyrum, Joseph Knight, David Whitmer. No one gets a bespoke gospel; everyone gets the same white field.
