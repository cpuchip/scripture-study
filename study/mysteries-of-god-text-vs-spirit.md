# What Are the Mysteries of God?

**Binding question:** Nephi opens the Book of Mormon with a claim to "a great knowledge of the goodness and the mysteries of God" (1 Nephi 1:1). Are these mysteries discoverable from the text alone? Or are they obtained only through the Spirit and personal revelation?

---

The Book of Mormon opens with a young man making an audacious claim. Before the wars and the ministries and the final destructions, before the brass plates and the Liahona and the visitation of Christ, Nephi says he has been taught in all the learning of his father and has acquired a great knowledge of the goodness and the mysteries of God. That phrase sits at the threshold of the entire book. It raises a question the rest of the text keeps answering: What are these mysteries? And can you get them from reading alone, or only through the Spirit?

The substrate corpus does not give a simple yes-or-no to either half of that question. What it gives is a path — a way that text and Spirit and covenant and a sanctified eye work together. The path does not run through literacy alone. It also does not bypass the text. The mysteries are received only when the whole pattern is intact.

## I. The Mysteries Are the Knowledge of God, Not Hidden Trivia

The word "mystery" in modern English usually means a puzzle waiting to be solved, something concealed that curiosity can uncover. The corpus suggests something different. The mysteries are not esoteric facts about God. They are the knowledge of God himself.

D&C 84 makes this explicit:

> "And this greater priesthood administereth the gospel and holdeth the key of the mysteries of the kingdom, even the key of the knowledge of God. Therefore, in the ordinances thereof, the power of godliness is manifest. And without the ordinances thereof, and the authority of the priesthood, the power of godliness is not manifest unto men in the flesh; For without this no man can see the face of God, even the Father, and live."
> — D&C 84:19-22

The key of the mysteries and the key of the knowledge of God are the same key. The [order-of-god](order-of-god.md) study traces this connection carefully. The purpose of the holy order is not to administer trivia. It is to bring people into the presence and knowledge of the Father. The [truth-enjoy](truth-enjoy.md) study adds the material witness: "The words that I speak unto you, they are spirit, and they are life." The word of the Lord is truth, truth is light, light is Spirit. What is being transmitted through the ordinances is not information. It is substance.

The [know-god](know-god.md) study frames this in the language of John 17:3 — eternal life is knowing God. Not knowing *about* Him. Knowing *Him*. When the Lord promises in D&C 76 to "reveal all mysteries, yea, all the hidden mysteries of my kingdom from days of old," He makes the promise to those who "serve me in righteousness and in truth unto the end." The mysteries are relational. They are given to those entering a relationship, not to those solving a riddle.

But there is a tension to hold. Alma, teaching Corianton, admits there are many mysteries "which are kept, that no one knoweth them save God himself" ([Alma 40:3](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/40.md)). The [plan-of-salvation-notes-03-spirit-world](plan-of-salvation/notes-03-spirit-world.md) study notes this honestly. Not everything is open. Yet Alma does not stop there. He inquired diligently of God, and he received. The pattern is not that all mysteries are automatically available. It is that God keeps some things, and God also reveals some things, and the difference depends on something more than curiosity.

## II. The Pattern Is Incremental Because Even the Son Received Not the Fulness at the First

If the mysteries were hidden facts, you might expect them to be unlocked all at once by a password or a ritual. The corpus describes a different pattern. The Lord gives line upon line.

The [ten-virgins-parable](ten-virgins-parable.md) study cites Elder Bednar's teaching that conversion is acquired "one drop at a time — 'line upon line [and] precept upon precept,' patiently and persistently. No shortcut is available; no last-minute flurry of preparation is possible." The pattern is not bureaucratic delay. It is how light actually enters a soul.

The [know-god](know-god.md) study adds a crucial witness: even the Son "received not of the fulness at the first, but received grace for grace; And he received not of the fulness at first, but continued from grace to grace, until he received a fulness." If Christ himself progressed by degrees, the demand for instant comprehension is not spiritual ambition. It is impatience.

The [order-of-god](order-of-god.md) study connects this to Alma 13's progression — called, ordained, sanctified, purified, entering into rest:

> "Now they, after being sanctified by the Holy Ghost, having their garments made white, being pure and spotless before God, could not look upon sin save it were with abhorrence; and there were many, exceedingly great many, who were made pure and entered into the rest of the Lord their God."
> — Alma 13:12

Grace for grace is the shape of the whole path. Alma 13:3 adds the doctrine of foreknowledge and choice: the priests were "called and prepared from the foundation of the world according to the foreknowledge of God, on account of their exceeding faith and good works; in the first place being left to choose good or evil; therefore they having chosen good, and exercising exceedingly great faith, are called with a holy calling." Equal starting position. The difference is choice. The order is open to all who do not harden their hearts.

This is why D&C 50:24 says that "light groweth brighter and brighter until the perfect day." The [discernment-and-the-comprehending-eye](discernment-and-the-comprehending-eye.md) study notes the same law: "he that receiveth light, and continueth in God, receiveth more light." The condition for receiving more is continuing in what you have already received. The text offers the lines. But the lines only accumulate for the soul that keeps walking.

## III. The Holy Ghost Unfolds What the Text Names

The text names the mysteries. It traces their lineage from Nephi through Alma to Joseph Smith. It commands seeking. But the text itself says it cannot complete the work alone.

The [order-of-god](order-of-god.md) study anchors the entire "one eternal round" passage in the phrase "by the power of the Holy Ghost":

> "...the mysteries of God shall be unfolded unto them, by the power of the Holy Ghost, as well in these times as in times of old, and as well in times of old as in times to come; wherefore, the course of the Lord is one eternal round."
> — 1 Nephi 10:19

That prepositional phrase is doing the heavy lifting. The course of the Lord is one eternal round, and the unfolding happens by the Holy Ghost.

The [know-god](know-god.md) study quotes Moroni's promise: "By the power of the Holy Ghost ye may know the truth of all things." Not by diligent reading. Not by scholarly research. By the power of the Holy Ghost. The [gifts](gifts.md) study catalogs the gifts of the Spirit and notes that "all these gifts come from God, for the benefit of the children of God" and "come by the Spirit of Christ." The gift of knowledge, the word of wisdom, the discerning of spirits — these are how the Holy Ghost unfolds mysteries through specific people for the benefit of others.

The [gifts](gifts.md) study also gives the pattern of asking: "He that asketh in the Spirit asketh according to the will of God; wherefore it is done even as he asketh." Asking in the Spirit means your desires are already tuned to what God wants to give. The ask is not a blank check. It is a tuning. And there are guardrails: "Seek ye earnestly the best gifts, always remembering for what they are given." Gifts are not trophies. They are tools distributed across the body so that everyone benefits.

Therefore, the text is necessary but not sufficient. Without the text, the Spirit has no vocabulary to speak to you in. Without the Spirit, the text is just ink. The [best-books-and-the-spirit-of-discernment](best-books-and-the-spirit-of-discernment.md) study captures this exactly. It notes that the priests of Noah had memorized Isaiah but taught without the power of the Holy Ghost. They had the text. They lacked the unfolding. The result was precepts of men, not mysteries of God. The [discernment-and-the-comprehending-eye](discernment-and-the-comprehending-eye.md) study adds Jacob's warning about "looking beyond the mark" (Jacob 4:14). They knew the words of Isaiah and missed Christ speaking through Abinadi. Displacement of the eye's focus from the actual to the elaborated can happen entirely within canonical reading. Memorization is not discernment. Quotation is not light.

## IV. Priesthood Keys and Ordinances Are the Authorized Channel

The corpus insists that mysteries are not free-floating ideas. They are tied to keys, covenants, and ordinances. You do not stumble upon them by accident.

The [order-of-god](order-of-god.md) study gives extensive treatment to Alma 13 and D&C 84:6-22. The holy order is the pattern "how intelligence progresses from agency through sanctification to fulness." The [priesthood-oath-and-covenant](priesthood-oath-and-covenant.md) study unpacks D&C 84:33-41 — the oath and covenant that binds God to reveal and binds the receiver to serve:

> "For whoso is faithful unto the obtaining these two priesthoods... and the magnifying their calling, are sanctified by the Spirit unto the renewing of their bodies. They become the sons of Moses and of Aaron and the seed of Abraham, and the church and kingdom, and the elect of God. And also all they who receive this priesthood receive me, saith the Lord... And he that receiveth my Father receiveth my Father's kingdom; therefore all that my Father hath shall be given unto him. And this is according to the oath and covenant which belongeth to the priesthood."
> — D&C 84:33-39

"All that my Father hath shall be given unto him." This is not a casual offer. It is an oath-bound promise attached to covenant faithfulness. The [priesthood-oath-and-covenant](priesthood-oath-and-covenant.md) study emphasizes the distinction: an oath is a unilateral promise solemnly binding. God adds His oath to His covenant promises, meaning He cannot fail to keep His word if we keep ours.

The [truth-enjoy](truth-enjoy.md) study frames D&C 84:19 as the gateway to "the key of the knowledge of God." The ordinances are where the power of godliness is manifest. Without them, the power is hidden.

But priesthood office by itself does not guarantee reception. The [know-god](know-god.md) study quotes D&C 121:36 — the powers of heaven "cannot be controlled nor handled only upon the principles of righteousness." Those principles are spelled out in the next verses: "by persuasion, by long-suffering, by gentleness and meekness, and by love unfeigned." The [art-of-presidency](art-of-presidency.md) study shows that authority without character becomes a one-cylinder operation: structural but empty. The channel is real. The channel also requires a worthy vessel.

## V. A Single Eye Is What Turns Text and Spirit into Comprehension

It is not "read your scriptures" and it is not "wait for a feeling." It is a sanctified eye that can read the text in the presence of the Spirit and comprehend what is being offered.

The [discernment-and-the-comprehending-eye](discernment-and-the-comprehending-eye.md) study is the definitive treatment here. It traces D&C 88:67-68:

> "And if your eye be single to my glory, your whole bodies shall be filled with light, and there shall be no darkness in you; and that body which is filled with light comprehendeth all things. Therefore, sanctify yourselves that your minds become single to God."

Read the chain in order. Eye singleness. Body fills with light. No darkness. Comprehends all things. Therefore, sanctify. The comprehension is the result, and the condition is sanctification. Like cleaves to like. Light receives light. The perceiver has to become the kind of person who can receive what is being given.

The same study traces hope as the eye's forward direction: "Wherefore, whoso believeth in God might with surety hope for a better world... which hope cometh of faith, maketh an anchor to the souls of men." Without hope, the eye has no forward orientation. It sees only what is, with no horizon. Charity focuses the lens. Moroni 7:48 promises that when we are filled with charity, "we shall see him as he is." Like cleaves to like. Moroni 7:45 adds that charity "rejoiceth not in iniquity but rejoiceth in the truth." The lens that sees persons as God sees them is the same lens that sees facts as they really are. There is not one eye for love and another eye for truth. There is one eye, purified by charity, that does both.

The [best-books-and-the-spirit-of-discernment](best-books-and-the-spirit-of-discernment.md) study adds the study-and-faith standard. D&C 88:118 commands "seek learning, even by study and also by faith." The same revelation that licenses seeking widely installs the gate: study and faith. Not study alone. Not faith without study. Both. D&C 88:78-79 expands the license extravagantly — things in heaven, in earth, under earth, things which have been, things which are, things which must shortly come to pass — but the precondition fifty verses earlier is the single eye.

Moroni 7:15 gives the discernment standard: "as the daylight is from the dark night." The [best-books-and-the-spirit-of-discernment](best-books-and-the-spirit-of-discernment.md) study notes that if the discernment is not happening, the problem is not the absence of equipment. It is the eye.

Therefore, the answer to the binding question is neither text alone nor Spirit alone. It is the whole pattern: the text establishes that mysteries exist and names them; the Spirit unfolds them; the priesthood channel authorizes and binds; the incremental pattern gives time for growth; and the single eye makes the receiver capable of receiving. Remove any piece, and the path stalls.

## A Gap to Name

The classic "hardened hearts" passage — Alma 12:9-11 — and Jacob's "unsearchable depths" (Jacob 4:8) are absent from the substrate corpus. These are canonical voices that would deepen this study. What the corpus does contain are the nearby witnesses: Alma 13's treatment of the holy order, Alma 40:3's admission that some mysteries are kept by God alone, and 1 Nephi 10:19's declaration that the course of the Lord is one eternal round. The argument stands on these witnesses, but the gap is real and worth flagging.

## Becoming

- Read one verse slowly this week, not to finish a chapter but to let the Spirit unfold it. Ask before you read, "What would you have me see?" Then read, and wait.
- Name one place where you are reading scripture for information rather than for transformation. Repentance here is not guilt. It is re-tuning the eye.
- If you hold priesthood keys or a calling, magnify it this week as a channel for light, not as administration. The oath and covenant promises that all the Father has will be given. The condition is faithfulness unto the end.
- Pray for one gift of the Spirit you lack. The command is explicit: "Seek ye earnestly the best gifts." Ask in the Spirit, which means aligning your desire with God's will before you speak.
- Where hope has dimmed, re-aim the eye. Hope is the forward direction that makes faith possible. Without it, the eye sees only what is, not what could yet be.
