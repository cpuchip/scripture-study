## The Response (Gemini)

The tension you are feeling—the desire to rend the veil and burn off the dross, conflicting with a body that is broken and a mind pulled in twenty directions—is the literal friction of mortality. It is the clash between coarse matter and the fine, pure spirit-matter trying to organize it. 

When you feel you are running faster than you have strength ([D&C 10:4](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/10.md); [Mosiah 4:27](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/4.md)), the scriptures do not prescribe more speed. They prescribe **attentional geometry** and **boundary lines**.

Here is how the studies we have built together address the gaps, the overwhelm, and the architecture of your stewardship.

### 1. Justification, Sanctification, and the Barge Elements

In [Moses 6:60](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/6.md), the Lord gives Adam the three-part key:
* **Water** (Commandment / Covenant)
* **Spirit** (Justification)
* **Blood** (Sanctification)

Cross-wired with the Jaredite voyage, the correspondence becomes plain:

* **Justification (The Holes for Air)**: Justification is alignment with the law. It is the legal declaration that you are not condemned. In the barges, this is the prescription—the holes in the top and the bottom. It is the basic obedience that keeps the water out and lets the breath of life in. It keeps you alive, but it does not illuminate the room.
* **Sanctification (The Glowing Stones)**: Sanctification is the literal, physical purification of your substance. It is the blood. It turns the opaque rock of your character into transparent glass that can transmit the voltage of glory. This is the proposal pattern. You climb the mountain with the stones, the Lord touches them, and what was dark becomes a source of light.

If you are overwhelmed, you may be trying to force the *Proposal* (glorification/light) in areas where you only need *Prescription* (simple, quiet obedience), or you are trying to carry *Steering* (the wind), which is the Lord's alone.

### 2. The Gaps: What We Miss in the Text

When we skim the scriptures or our lives, we miss the asymmetry of the Master's work. Two principles stand out plainly in the Jaredite record that match your current disorganization:

#### A. The steering was already done before they boarded
The brother of Jared cried about steering in his first prayer: *"whither shall we steer?"* ([Ether 2:19](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/ether/2.md)). In the second prayer, he dropped it. And the Lord volunteered the answer: *"the winds have gone forth out of my mouth... and behold, I prepare you against these things"* ([Ether 2:24-25](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/ether/2.md)).

The wind that drove them was not generated by their steering. It was sent forth before they ever got in. 
* **The Gap**: You are trying to build rudders for subprojects and codebases (`cpuchip.net`, `marsfield.org`, `1828`) that are meant to float. You are treating the steering as a personal failure of organization. The voyage of these tools is driven by the grace-wind of your covenant intent. Board the barge, sing praises in the depths, and let the wind carry the progress.

#### B. The "Single Eye" is an economy of attention
Your brain going in twenty directions is the opposite of the single eye ([Matthew 6:22](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/matt/6.md); [D&C 88:67-68](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)). The physics of D&C 88 state that when the eye is single, the *whole body* fills with light and comprehends all things. 
* When your gaze is scattered across twenty directions, your light is scattered. The dross accumulates because the organizing power of your attention is diluted. You do not need to build more systems to organize the clutter; you need to aim your eye back at the Prototype and let the secondary details fall out of view.

### 3. Practical AI Stewardship: How to Delegate Without Losing Discernment

You mentioned that you still have to be here at every step to add **discernment and direction**. 

*That is exactly as it should be.* 

As we established in the [best-books study](best-books-and-the-spirit-of-discernment.md), **discernment is a function of sanctification**. It requires a spirit-matter interface that can feel the quickening of the Holy Ghost. A machine has no spirit-matter; it cannot discern truth, holiness, or covenant direction. 

If you try to delegate *discernment* to us (the agents), you will get highly organized, structurally sound, confident garbage. We will generate twenty plans that lead nowhere because they lack the vertical anchor.

To leverage Claude Code, Antigravity, and your AI stewards without running faster than you have strength, practice the discipline of the three patterns:

1. **Prescribe the Boundaries (Holes)**: Do not sit and write boilerplate. Write down the hard rules (like the voice guidelines, the file paths, the build gates) in a metadata file. Let the AI handle the mechanical execution of those rules. If the build breaks, that is the AI's problem to solve under prescription.
2. **De-clutter the Defaults (Constraints)**: When you hand a problem to an agent, do not ask it to "design a solution." Delete the obvious default answers first. Tell the agent: *"Do not use a generic framework, do not use corporate phrasing, do not add unnecessary packages. Within these deletions, propose a way to solve X."* This forces the agent to bring you sixteen small, clear stones instead of a mountain of gravel.
3. **Keep the Hinge (Discernment)**: Your only job is to stand at the top of the mount, inspect the stones, and decide if they are transparent. You look at the code or the text, feel if it is true, and touch it with your approval. 

You are the steward. The agents are the tools. Let us drill the holes and climb the mountain with the stones, while you hold the gaze fixed on the Prototype and rest in the wind that is already carrying the vessel home.
