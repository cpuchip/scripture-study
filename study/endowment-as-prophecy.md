# Is the Endowment a Prophecy? The Seven Seals, the Six Days, and Where We Are

*Prompted by a Facebook post (Hyrum W. Hawks) proposing that the temple Endowment is a prophecy, that Genesis 1–3 is a coded timeline of the seven thousand years, and that the seals and trumpets of Revelation map onto that timeline closely enough to "figure out where we are in it" — currently, he suggests, the second trumpet (an Iran war, oil as the burning mountain). The question Michael brought: gather the scriptures, build the narrative, and discern — is there something to it?*

**Binding question:** Does scripture support reading the Creation account and the Endowment as a prophecy of the seven thousand years, mapped seal-by-seal and trumpet-by-trumpet onto Revelation — and onto current events — so that a careful reader can locate the present moment on the clock?

Short answer, stated up front so the rest can earn it: **the deep structure he's reaching for is real and revealed — and it's bigger and cleaner than his framing.** Where he moves from the pattern to a calendar (Palmyra in 2000, the Garden from 2000 to 2020, this year's war as the second trumpet), he crosses from doctrine into private reading, and he says so himself ("that's my opinion," "I was wrong about 90%"). The honest version keeps the type and drops the timetable.

---

## I. The framework he's proposing

Laid out plainly, the post makes one structural claim and a series of date-pins hung on it.

The structural claim: the seven days of Creation are a prophecy of seven thousand years, and the book of Revelation runs the same clock — sixth seal is the sixth day, the seventh seal's silence is the pause in Eden, the trumpets are the curses pronounced after the Fall. The temple Endowment, which walks through Creation → Garden → Fall → mortal world → return, is therefore itself a prophecy of that same arc, and "where we are in it" can be read off the ceremony.

The date-pins hung on it: Revelation 7 is the Palmyra Temple dedication (2000 AD); the silence of Revelation 8:1 corresponds to Eden's quiet and the years 2000–2020; the curses of Genesis 3:16–19 are the trumpets of Revelation 8; and we are presently enduring the second trumpet — read as the present Iran war, "oil as the mountain burning with fire."

The two layers need to be judged separately, because one is revealed and one is not.

---

## II. The revealed backbone — and it is strong

Start with the thing most people don't know is doctrine: the seven-thousand-year scheme is not folk speculation. It is Joseph Smith's own revealed interpretation of Revelation, given in [D&C 77](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/77.md).

**A day is a thousand years.** The arithmetic of the scheme rests on a measure scripture states twice. Peter: "one day is with the Lord as a thousand years, and a thousand years as one day" ([2 Peter 3:8](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/2-pet/3.md)). Abraham, taught by the Urim and Thummim, says the same of Kolob's reckoning — "one revolution was a day unto the Lord … it being one thousand years according to the time appointed unto that whereon thou standest" ([Abraham 3:4](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/abr/3.md)).

**The sealed book is the seven millennia.** John saw a scroll sealed with seven seals. Asked what it is, the Lord answered: "it contains the revealed will, mysteries, and the works of God; the hidden things of his economy concerning this earth during the seven thousand years of its continuance, or its temporal existence" ([D&C 77:6](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/77.md)). And the seals: "the first seal contains the things of the first thousand years, and the second also of the second thousand years, and so on until the seventh" ([D&C 77:7](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/77.md)).

This is not a fringe reading. The Church's current *Scripture Helps: New Testament* states it as the plain sense of the chapter: "The book, or scroll, with seven seals represents 7,000 years of human history. Each seal represents 1,000 years" ([Revelation 6–14](../gospel-library/eng/manual/scripture-helps-new-testament/51-revelation-6-14.md)).

**The seals run forward from Adam.** This is the detail that decides the whole question, so it deserves the manual's own chart, which assigns each seal to a *past* millennium:

| Seal | Revelation | The millennium it covers (per the manual) |
|------|-----------|--------------------------------------------|
| First | [6:1–2](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rev/6.md) | Enoch and his people; the founding of Zion |
| Second | [6:3–4](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rev/6.md) | The wickedness and violence of the days of Noah |
| Third | [6:5–6](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rev/6.md) | The days of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob; famine |
| Fourth | [6:7–8](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rev/6.md) | The warring empires — Assyria, Egypt, Babylon, Persia, Greece, Rome |
| Fifth | [6:9–11](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rev/6.md) | The early Christian martyrs under the altar |
| Sixth | [6:12–17](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rev/6.md); [7](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rev/7.md) | The time just before the Second Coming — the Restoration and the signs |
| Seventh | [8–11](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rev/8.md) | The trumpets; the dawn of the Millennium |

The clock runs from creation toward consummation. The Fall belongs to the front of the scroll — the first and second seals, the world of Adam, Enoch, and Noah — not to the trumpets at the end.

**Creation week *is* a type of the seven millennia — and the revelation says so directly.** Here is the legitimate heart of what Hawks is feeling after. Asked about the trumpets of Revelation 8, the Lord answered by laying the Creation week over the whole of history:

> as God made the world in six days, and on the seventh day he finished his work, and sanctified it, and also formed man out of the dust of the earth, even so, in the beginning of the seventh thousand years will the Lord God sanctify the earth, and complete the salvation of man, and judge all things, and shall redeem all things … and the sounding of the trumpets of the seven angels are the preparing and finishing of his work, in the beginning of the seventh thousand years — the preparing of the way before the time of his coming. ([D&C 77:12](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/77.md))

So the type Hawks reaches for is genuinely in the text. Six days of God's labor stand for six thousand years of the world's labor. The seventh day — when God "rested" and "blessed the seventh day, and sanctified it" ([Genesis 2:2–3](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/gen/2.md)) — stands for the seventh thousand years, the millennial Sabbath when the Lord sanctifies the earth. That is the Creation-as-prophecy structure, revealed and exact.

Notice what the type does *not* do: it works at the scale of whole millennia, not at the granularity of individual curses keyed to individual trumpets. The match is six-work-days to six-labor-millennia, seventh-rest-day to seventh-rest-millennium. Macro, not a cipher.

**The trumpets are a review of all seven millennia, not a countdown of last-day disasters.** This is the second revelation people skip, and it reframes the trumpets entirely. In [D&C 88:108–110](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md), the Lord describes the seven angels sounding:

> And then shall the first angel again sound his trump in the ears of all living, and reveal the secret acts of men, and the mighty works of God in the first thousand years. And then shall the second angel sound his trump, and reveal the secret acts of men, and the thoughts and intents of their hearts, and the mighty works of God in the second thousand years — And so on, until the seventh angel shall sound his trump … that there shall be time no longer; and Satan shall be bound.

Each trump *unveils* a millennium — "the mighty works of God in the first thousand years," then the second, and so on. The seven trumps are a divine retrospective at the threshold of the Millennium: the whole record of the earth opened and read aloud. They are not seven escalating catastrophes to be matched against the evening news.

**The temple connection to Revelation 7 is real — but it is about covenant sealing, not a building's dedication date.** Hawks ties Revelation 7 to the temple, and on that instinct he is on solid ground. [D&C 77:10](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/77.md) places Revelation 7 "in the sixth thousand years, or the opening of the sixth seal" — the Restoration. The sealing of God's servants in their foreheads ([Revelation 7:3](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rev/7.md)), Joseph Smith explained, "signifies sealing the blessing upon their heads, meaning the everlasting covenant, thereby making their calling and election sure," and the work of the 144,000 high priests "could have symbolic reference to the work performed under priesthood keys in latter-day temples" ([Revelation 6–14](../gospel-library/eng/manual/scripture-helps-new-testament/51-revelation-6-14.md)). The temple is woven into Revelation 7 — through the *sealing ordinances and the everlasting covenant*, not through the calendar date of any one dedication.

## III. Sorting the claims into three grades

The post's power and its problem both come from blending these grades into one seamless story. Separated, they read very differently.

**Revealed doctrine (stand on it):**
- The earth's temporal existence is seven thousand years; the seven seals are the seven millennia ([D&C 77:6–7](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/77.md)).
- The six days of Creation are a type of the six millennia of labor; the seventh day's rest and sanctifying are a type of the seventh-thousand-year Millennium ([D&C 77:12](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/77.md); [Genesis 2:2–3](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/gen/2.md)).
- Revelation 7 — the sealing, the 144,000, the gathering — belongs to the Restoration in the sixth seal, and connects to temple covenant work ([D&C 77:9–11](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/77.md)).
- The seventh seal and its trumpets prepare the way for the Second Coming; we live near the end of the sixth seal, looking toward the seventh ([D&C 77:12–13](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/77.md)).

**Faithful inference (plausible, edifying, not binding):**
- The Endowment recapitulates Creation → Garden → Fall → mortal probation → return to God's presence. Because it rehearses the whole plan from beginning to end, it *is* prophetic in the way every type is prophetic: it shows the end from the beginning and points each person home. The temple as "a prophecy" holds up at the level of *pattern*.

**Speculation — his private reading (he flags it as such):**
- Revelation 7 = the Palmyra Temple dedicated in 2000 AD.
- Revelation 8:1's half-hour silence = the quiet of Eden, and the calendar window 2000–2020.
- The curses of Genesis 3:16–19 = the trumpets of Revelation 8.
- The present second trumpet = an Iran war, oil as the burning mountain.
- That a reader can, on this basis, "figure out where we are in it."

---

## IV. Two cautions, named plainly

**1. He folds the front of the scroll onto the back.** The seals run forward from Adam (Section II). The Creation and the Fall sit at the *opening* of the book — the first and second thousand years, the age of Enoch and Noah. Hawks reads Genesis 3 (the Fall and its curses) onto Revelation 8 (the trumpets of the seventh seal), which places the beginning of the story at its end. The revealed type runs the other direction and at a coarser scale: six work-days = six labor-millennia, seventh day = millennial rest. When [D&C 88:108–110](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md) shows the trumpets *unveiling* each past millennium in turn, it confirms the trumps are a backward-looking review of the whole record, not a forward-running sequence of curses to be matched against this decade's wars. The curse-to-trumpet-to-headline cipher is built against the grain of the very scheme that gives the framework its authority.

**2. The Lord keeps the calendar closed — deliberately, and on the record.** Joseph Smith wanted exactly what Hawks wants: to know the time. He "was once praying very earnestly to know the time of the coming of the Son of Man," and the answer he received withheld the date and gave him a riddle instead: "Joseph, my son, if thou livest until thou art eighty-five years old, thou shalt see the face of the Son of Man; therefore let this suffice, and trouble me no more on this matter." His own conclusion: "I was left thus, without being able to decide whether this coming referred to the beginning of the millennium or to some previous appearing, or whether I should die and thus see his face" ([D&C 130:14–17](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/130.md)). The prophet who restored the seven-seal interpretation was himself denied the schedule.

That refusal is consistent across scripture: "of that day and hour knoweth no man, no, not the angels of heaven, but my Father only" ([Matthew 24:36](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/matt/24.md)). Even the Church's manual, citing scholars on the seals, warns that "the images should not be taken too literally" and show only "a general outline of how history will flow" ([Revelation 6–14](../gospel-library/eng/manual/scripture-helps-new-testament/51-revelation-6-14.md)). Pinning the second trumpet to a particular present war is the exact move the Lord has repeatedly declined to authorize — and it is a moving target. Every generation since John has found its own war in the trumpets, and every generation so far has been wrong about the timing while right that the day is coming.

A side note on his Adam-ondi-Ahman pointer, which is sound on its own footing: Spring Hill "is named by the Lord Adam-ondi-Ahman, because … it is the place where Adam shall come to visit his people, or the Ancient of Days shall sit, as spoken of by Daniel the prophet" ([D&C 116:1](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/116.md)). That a great priesthood council precedes the Coming is doctrine. Its date is not.

## V. So — is there something to it?

Yes. Just not the part he's most excited about.

The "something" is the type, and it is genuinely thrilling once you see it from the revealed side rather than the speculative side. The earth keeps a seven-day week written across seven thousand years. Six days of labor, then a Sabbath of rest and sanctifying. The Endowment walks every person through that same arc in miniature — created, placed in a garden, fallen into a lone and dreary world, taught the gospel, brought back by covenant into the presence of God. The temple is a prophecy in the truest sense a type can be: it shows you the end from the beginning, and the end is *return*. Hawks is feeling a real shape in the scriptures. [D&C 77:12](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/77.md) puts it in the Lord's own words.

What the type will not do is print a date. It tells you the *kind* of moment you live in — late in the sixth day, the labor nearly done, the seventh-day rest approaching — without telling you the hour the bell rings. Reading the silence of Revelation 8:1 as the years 2000–2020, or this year's war as the second trumpet, adds precision the revelation withholds and the manual cautions against. He knows this; he wrote "that's my opinion" and "I was wrong about 90%." Believe him on both counts. Take the 10% he's right about, which is the structure, and hold the 90% loosely.

---

## VI. Becoming

Here is the turn that redeems the whole exercise: the temple-as-prophecy, read correctly, was never meant to tell you *when*. It was meant to tell you *to be ready whenever*. The Lord's answer to the man who asked for the date was "let this suffice, and trouble me no more on this matter" ([D&C 130:15](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/130.md)). The sufficiency he was pointed toward was not a decoded calendar. It was a covenant kept.

And that is exactly where Hawks lands at the end of his post — "keep the covenants … or we'll be part of the troubled masses." On that, he is right, and it is the one conclusion the framework actually warrants. If the Endowment is a prophecy, the prophecy is not a countdown. It is an invitation, repeated every time you go: this is the plan, this is the way home, walk it. The faithful response to "we may be near the end of the sixth day" is not to refine the timeline. It is to live the seventh day's rest now — to be found, whenever the trumpet sounds, already keeping the covenant the seal in the forehead signifies.

The date is the Father's to keep. The covenant is ours to keep. Keep the one you were given.
