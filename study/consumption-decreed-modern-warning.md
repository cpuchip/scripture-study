# The Consumption Decreed: What Modern Prophets Say

*A follow-up study tracing the "consumption decreed" through the voices of latter-day prophets and apostles*

> **Related studies:** [The Consumption Decreed](consumption-decreed.md) · [End Times](end-times.md) · [The Serpent and the Dragon](serpent-and-dragon.md) · [Enoch](enoch.md)

---

## Why This Study?

In the [first study](consumption-decreed.md), we traced the phrase "the consumption decreed" from [D&C 87:6](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/87.md) back through its Hebrew roots (*kālāh* — a determined completion), through Webster 1828, through Isaiah 10, Isaiah 28, Daniel 9, and into the Book of Mormon. We learned that the consumption is not random destruction — it is wickedness reaching its logical, inevitable conclusion.

Now we ask: **What have the prophets of the Restoration said about this?** Have they just prophesied doom, or have they shown us the way through?

What emerges is a strikingly consistent message across 50 years, from Benson to Nelson: *The consumption is real. It is underway. The nations will not survive their own wickedness. But there is a refuge — and it is not a bunker. It is a covenant.*

---

## Part 1: "As Decreed, Until a Full End"

The prophets who engaged most directly with D&C 87:6 didn't soften it. They quoted it plainly and let it land.

### President Ezra Taft Benson — "A Message to the World" (October 1975)

In what reads almost like a modern appendix to D&C 1, President Benson delivered a formal apostolic warning to the nations. He didn't hedge:

> "The nations of the earth continue in their sinful and unrighteous ways. Much of the unbounded knowledge with which men have been blessed has been used to destroy mankind instead of to bless the children of men as the Lord intended. Two great world wars, with fruitless efforts at lasting peace, are solemn evidence that peace has been taken from the earth because of the wickedness of the people. Nations cannot endure in sin. They will be broken up but the kingdom of God will endure forever."
> — [President Ezra Taft Benson, "A Message to the World," October 1975 General Conference](../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/1975/10/a-message-to-the-world.md)

Then he delivered a line that echoes the exact language of D&C 87:6:

> "If this voice is not heeded, the angels of destruction will increasingly go forth, and the chastening hand of Almighty God will be felt upon the nations, *as decreed*, until a full end thereof will be the result."
> — [President Ezra Taft Benson, "A Message to the World," October 1975 General Conference](../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/1975/10/a-message-to-the-world.md)

Note the precision: *as decreed*. Not as a possibility, but as a certainty already pronounced. The decree has been issued. The only variable is whether nations will repent.

### Elder Neal A. Maxwell — "Be of Good Cheer" (October 1982)

Elder Maxwell took the same prophecy and placed it within its historical trajectory — then pointed toward the only durable response:

> "We do live in a time when peace has been taken from the earth. War has been the almost continuing experience of modern man. There have been 141 wars, large and small, just since the end of World War II in 1945. As the American Civil War was about to begin, the Lord declared there would be a succession of wars poured out upon all nations, resulting in the 'death and misery of many souls.'"
> — [Elder Neal A. Maxwell, "Be of Good Cheer," October 1982 General Conference](../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/1982/10/be-of-good-cheer.md)

And then, the direct citation:

> "Moreover, that continuum of conflict will culminate in 'a full end of all nations.' Meanwhile, let mortals, if they choose, put overreliance upon mortal arms. As for us, we shall 'put on the whole armour of God'!"
> — [Elder Neal A. Maxwell, "Be of Good Cheer," October 1982 General Conference](../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/1982/10/be-of-good-cheer.md)

That contrast is pure Maxwell. The nations are trusting in arms; the Saints are trusting in armor — but a different kind. And then he pivots to the heart of the matter: the last days will be "a day of gloominess" (quoting Joel and Zephaniah), but "the more iniquity, the more despair. And unless there is widespread repentance, despair will both deepen and spread—except among those who have gospel gladness."

### Elder Bruce R. McConkie — "The Seven Christs" (October 1982)

In the same conference, Elder McConkie spoke with characteristic directness about the Second Coming:

> "In that dread day the world that now is shall end; wickedness will cease; every corruptible thing will be consumed. And the glory of the Lord will shine daily upon all men from the rising of the sun until it sinks in the western sky."
> — [Elder Bruce R. McConkie, "The Seven Christs," October 1982 General Conference](../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/1982/10/the-seven-christs.md)

"Every corruptible thing will be consumed." That's the consumption decreed stated in final, absolute terms.

### Elder Bruce R. McConkie — "The Lord God of the Restoration" (October 1980)

Two years earlier, McConkie laid out the landscape:

> "Peace has been taken from the earth. This is a day of wars and rumors of wars. Plagues and pestilence and desolation shall soon sweep the earth."
> — [Elder Bruce R. McConkie, "The Lord God of the Restoration," October 1980 General Conference](../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/1980/10/the-lord-god-of-the-restoration.md)

And this, which captures both the diagnosis and the only cure:

> "Wars and plagues and desolation shall continue to sweep the earth as with a flood. Crime and evil will increase; iniquity will abound; the love of men toward each other shall wax cold. We need not look for a day when men of themselves shall usher in an age of righteousness."
> — [Elder Bruce R. McConkie, "The Lord God of the Restoration," October 1980 General Conference](../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/1980/10/the-lord-god-of-the-restoration.md)

"Men of themselves" — that is the key. Left to their own devices, the nations consume themselves. Righteousness doesn't emerge as civilization's natural trajectory. It comes by revelation, by covenant, by divine intervention.

## Part 2: The Silver Lining — "The Lord Shall Have Power Over His Saints"

Every true prophet who speaks of destruction also speaks of deliverance. The pattern is Jeremiah's: "I will make a full end of all the nations whither I have driven thee: but I will not make a full end of thee" ([Jeremiah 30:11](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/jer/30.md)).

### President Marion G. Romney — "A Silver Lining" (April 1977)

President Romney quoted the Lord's warning from [D&C 5:19](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/5.md):

> "A desolating scourge shall go forth among the inhabitants of the earth, and shall continue to be poured out from time to time, if they repent not, until the earth is empty, and the inhabitants thereof are consumed away and utterly destroyed by the brightness of my coming."
> — [President Marion G. Romney, "A Silver Lining," April 1977 General Conference](../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/1977/04/a-silver-lining.md)

Terrifying in isolation. But then he quoted [D&C 45:31–35](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/45.md) — the passage where the Lord tells His disciples that in the midst of all these things, "my disciples shall stand in holy places, and shall not be moved."

And then the silver lining itself — [D&C 1:35–36](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/1.md):

> "The devil shall have power over his own dominion. And also the Lord shall have power over his saints, and shall reign in their midst."
> — quoted by [President Marion G. Romney, "A Silver Lining," April 1977 General Conference](../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/1977/04/a-silver-lining.md)

Two dominions. Two powers. The one consuming; the other sustaining. Romney saw the contrast clearly: just as disobedience brought the flood, obedience sanctified Enoch's Zion. Same generation. Two peoples. Two outcomes.

### President Gordon B. Hinckley — "If Ye Are Prepared Ye Shall Not Fear" (October 2005)

After cataloguing the catastrophes of 2005 — Hurricane Katrina, the Southeast Asian tsunami — President Hinckley turned to the scriptural pattern with remarkable calm. He quoted [D&C 88:89–91](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md) about earthquakes, thunderings, and waves heaving beyond their bounds. And then, the clear command:

> "The primary preparation is also set forth in the Doctrine and Covenants, wherein it says, 'Wherefore, stand ye in holy places, and be not moved, until the day of the Lord come.'"
> — [President Gordon B. Hinckley, "If Ye Are Prepared Ye Shall Not Fear," October 2005 General Conference](../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2005/10/if-ye-are-prepared-ye-shall-not-fear.md)

He then drew the principle: "We can so live that we can call upon the Lord for His protection and guidance. This is a first priority. We cannot expect His help if we are unwilling to keep His commandments."

The logic is simple and unrelenting. The consumption operates by natural law — wickedness devours. The protection operates by covenant law — obedience shelters.

## Part 3: The Mechanism — How Nations Consume Themselves

The first study traced the Hebrew *kālāh* and showed that the consumption is self-inflicted — iniquity reaching its determined end. The modern prophets teach the same thing, each naming particular dimensions of the collapse.

### Elder Dallin H. Oaks — "Preparation for the Second Coming" (April 2004)

Elder Oaks provided the most vivid modern description of the consumption's character:

> "Evil that used to be localized and covered like a boil is now legalized and paraded like a banner. The most fundamental roots and bulwarks of civilization are questioned or attacked. Nations disavow their religious heritage. Marriage and family responsibilities are discarded as impediments to personal indulgence."
> — [Elder Dallin H. Oaks, "Preparation for the Second Coming," April 2004 General Conference](../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2004/04/preparation-for-the-second-coming.md)

And then, naming the specific inversions:

> "The good, the true, and the beautiful are being replaced by the no-good, the 'whatever,' and the valueless fodder of personal whim."
> — [Elder Dallin H. Oaks, "Preparation for the Second Coming," April 2004 General Conference](../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2004/04/preparation-for-the-second-coming.md)

This *is* the consumption. Not fire from the sky — but a civilization eating its own foundations. Nations don't need an external destroyer when they are legalizing their own dissolution.

Oaks then asked the only question that matters — and offered D&C 87:8 as the answer:

> "Are we following the Lord's command, 'Stand ye in holy places, and be not moved, until the day of the Lord come; for behold, it cometh quickly'? What are those 'holy places'? Surely they include the temple and its covenants faithfully kept. Surely they include a home where children are treasured and parents are respected. Surely the holy places include our posts of duty assigned by priesthood authority, including missions and callings faithfully fulfilled in branches, wards, and stakes."
> — [Elder Dallin H. Oaks, "Preparation for the Second Coming," April 2004 General Conference](../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2004/04/preparation-for-the-second-coming.md)

Holy places are not geographic coordinates. They are covenantal relationships.

### Elder D. Todd Christofferson — "Come to Zion" (October 2008)

Christofferson named the Babylon side of the equation — the way consumption operates through materialism and pride:

> "Materialism is just one more manifestation of the idolatry and pride that characterize Babylon."
> — [Elder D. Todd Christofferson, "Come to Zion," October 2008 General Conference](../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2008/10/come-to-zion.md)

And then the standard of Zion that is its opposite — [Moses 7:18](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/7.md) repeated four times in his talk: "one heart and one mind, and dwelt in righteousness; and there was no poor among them."

His conclusion was that Zion does not arrive when the geography is right. Zion comes "because virtuous covenant people are drawn together and build it." The consumption consumes because wickedness erodes from within. Zion stands because righteousness builds from within. Same mechanism, opposite direction.

### Bishop Keith B. McMullin — "Come to Zion! Come to Zion!" (October 2002)

McMullin quoted President Hinckley with a phrase that cuts to the heart of consumption:

> "If we are to build that Zion of which the prophets have spoken and of which the Lord has given mighty promise, we must set aside our consuming selfishness."
> — President Gordon B. Hinckley, quoted by [Bishop Keith B. McMullin, "Come to Zion! Come to Zion!" October 2002 General Conference](../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2002/10/come-to-zion-come-to-zion.md)

*Consuming selfishness.* There it is — the consumption named as a spiritual disease, not just a geopolitical event. The nations consume themselves because their citizens are consumed by selfishness. The solution is not better politics but a people who consecrate rather than consume.

## Part 4: The Living Prophet's Voice — "These Are the Latter Days"

President Russell M. Nelson has not used the phrase "consumption decreed" by name. But he has described the reality it points to — and the response it demands — with unmistakable clarity.

### "The Temple and Your Spiritual Foundation" (October 2021)

> "My dear brothers and sisters, these *are* the latter days. If you and I are to withstand the forthcoming perils and pressures, it is imperative that we each have a *firm* spiritual foundation built upon the rock of our Redeemer, Jesus Christ."
> — [President Russell M. Nelson, "The Temple and Your Spiritual Foundation," October 2021 General Conference](../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2021/10/47nelson.md)

"Forthcoming perils and pressures" — this is not retrospective. This is a prophet speaking to what is *coming*. And his answer is the temple:

> "Whenever any kind of upheaval occurs in your life, the safest place to be spiritually is living inside your temple covenants!"
> — [President Russell M. Nelson, "The Temple and Your Spiritual Foundation," October 2021 General Conference](../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2021/10/47nelson.md)

Note how this echoes D&C 87:8 without quoting it directly. "Stand ye in holy places" → "living inside your temple covenants." The holy place is not where you stand geographically; it is where you stand covenantally.

### "Embrace the Future with Faith" (October 2020)

President Nelson drew on Captain Moroni's three principles of preparation and applied them to our day:

1. **Create places of security** — "When your home becomes a personal sanctuary of faith — where the Spirit resides — your home becomes the first line of defense."
2. **Prepare your mind to be faithful** — "Life without God is a life filled with fear. Life with God is a life filled with peace."
3. **Never stop preparing** — physical and spiritual, ongoing, relentless.

> "The stakes of Zion are 'a refuge from the storm' because they are led by those who hold priesthood keys and exercise priesthood authority."
> — [President Russell M. Nelson, "Embrace the Future with Faith," October 2020 General Conference](../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2020/10/37nelson.md)

This is the modern prophet's articulation of the same dual reality Marion G. Romney identified in 1977: two dominions, two powers, two outcomes. The consumption marches on in the world. But "the Lord shall have power over his saints."

## Synthesis: The Prophetic Pattern

Across five decades and ten voices, a remarkably consistent pattern emerges:

### 1. The Warning Is Absolute

The prophets do not soften D&C 87:6. Benson says "as decreed, until a full end." Maxwell says the continuum of conflict "will culminate in a full end of all nations." McConkie says "every corruptible thing will be consumed." Romney quotes the Lord: "consumed away and utterly destroyed." This is not fear-mongering — it is honesty. The consumption is decreed.

### 2. The Mechanism Is Internal

The consumption is not primarily an external attack on civilization. It is civilization eating itself. Oaks describes evil being "legalized and paraded." Christofferson names materialism and idolatry. McMullin/Hinckley call it "consuming selfishness." McConkie says "men of themselves" will never produce righteousness. The common thread: *wickedness is self-consuming*. Nations don't need an enemy — they become their own.

### 3. The Answer Is Covenantal, Not Political

Not one of these prophets prescribed political reform as the solution. Every one of them pointed to the same three things:

- **Covenant** — temple covenants, baptismal covenants, the oath and covenant of the priesthood
- **Zion** — one heart, one mind, dwelling in righteousness, no poor among us
- **Holy places** — temples, homes of faith, stakes of Zion, posts of duty

Hinckley: "Stand ye in holy places." Oaks: "holy places include the temple... a home... posts of duty." Nelson: "living inside your temple covenants." The prophets agree: you cannot stop the consumption of Babylon. But you can choose not to live there.

### 4. The Silver Lining Is Bright

Romney's "silver lining" is the most hopeful phrase in the entire discussion: [D&C 1:35–36](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/1.md) — the devil has power over his dominion, *and also* the Lord has power over his saints. These are parallel, simultaneous realities. The consumption operates in one sphere; the covenant operates in another. And the covenant sphere is where joy lives.

Maxwell said it best: the Saints can have "gospel gladness" — a cheer that comes not from favorable circumstances but from unshakeable truths. "Be of good cheer" is not naïveté. It is faith that sees farther than the news cycle.

---

## Becoming

The first study asked what the consumption decreed means. This study asks what the prophets have told us to *do* about it. Their answer is unified:

**Build Zion.** Not as a political project, but as a covenantal one. One heart and one mind. Dwell in righteousness. Have no poor among you. Stand in holy places and be not moved.

The consumption consumes because people consume — resources, each other, their own futures. Zion stands because people consecrate — time, talent, substance, heart. The battle between consumption and consecration is not fought on a battlefield. It is fought in the kitchen, at the temple, on Sunday morning, in how we treat our neighbor, in whether we tithe, in what we watch and what we worship.

### What This Demands of Me

1. **Temple as fortress.** President Nelson called it "a place of security unlike any other." This isn't metaphor — it is strategy. Regular attendance. Deep engagement with covenants. Teaching my children what the temple means before they go.

2. **Home as holy ground.** Oaks said holy places include "a home where children are treasured and parents are respected." My home is either a Zion outpost or a place where Babylon seeps in through screens and schedules. There is no neutral.

3. **Consecration over consumption.** The Hinckley phrase McMullin quoted — "set aside our consuming selfishness" — is a daily discipline. Not just tithing, but the posture of heart that asks "what can I give?" instead of "what can I get?"

4. **Choose gospel gladness.** Maxwell's "be of good cheer" is not personality — it is *testimony*. The reason I can be cheerful in a world that is consuming itself is not ignorance. It is because I have seen, up close, the power of the covenant. The Spirit that whispered "move your family" is the same Spirit that sustains Zion. I trust that voice.

5. **Remember the dual dominion.** When the news is dark — and it will get darker — remember Romney's silver lining: "The Lord shall have power over his saints, and shall reign in their midst." Both things are true simultaneously. The consumption is real. The covenant is more real.

> "Wherefore, stand ye in holy places, and be not moved, until the day of the Lord come; for behold, it cometh quickly."
> — [D&C 87:8](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/87.md)
