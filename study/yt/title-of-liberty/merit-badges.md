# Title of Liberty — Merit Badge Catalog

Badges are organized by pillar. Each badge has **required** and **elective** designation — some badges must be earned for rank advancement, others are chosen by the participant.

Badges scale by degree (age band). The catalog below shows the general requirements; specific degree-scaled versions are noted where they differ significantly.

---

## How Badges Work

### Earning a Badge

1. **Declare intent** — Tell the family council which badge you're working on
2. **Complete requirements** — Do the work, with evidence (journal entry, photo, demonstration, or witness)
3. **Present to council** — Show your work at family council. Family affirms completion
4. **Record** — Badge is logged in the Becoming app and marked on the family chart

### Badge Types

- **🔴 Required** — Must be earned for rank advancement. Specified per rank in [program-structure.md](program-structure.md)
- **🔵 Elective** — Choose from the catalog. Each rank requires a minimum number across multiple pillars
- **⭐ Honors** — Advanced version of an elective badge. Earns double credit toward elective count. Requires deeper mastery

### Degree Scaling

Requirements scale by degree. General pattern:
- **1st Degree (4–6):** Activity-based, family-supported, verbal/visual
- **2nd Degree (7–9):** Independent work begins, short writing, supervised
- **3rd Degree (10–12):** Full requirements, teaching others, producing work
- **4th Degree (13+):** Leadership, mastery, teaching, mentoring, real-world application

## ⚔️ Faith & Covenant Badges

### 🔴 Scripture Mastery
*Required for every rank.*

Memorize and be able to recite assigned scriptures. Uses spaced repetition (tracked in the Becoming app's memorize feature). Cumulative — prior scriptures must be maintained.

| Degree | Rank 1 | Rank 2 | Rank 3 | Rank 4 | Rank 5 |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| 1st | 1 | 2 total | 3 total | 4 total | 5 total |
| 2nd | 2 | 4 total | 7 total | 10 total | 15 total |
| 3rd | 3 | 5 total | 10 total | 13 total | 18 total |
| 4th | 5 | 7 total | 14 total | 17 total | 25 total |

### 🔴 Book of Mormon Reading
*Required for every rank.*

Progressive reading of the Book of Mormon. Each rank assigns specific chapters; Rank 5 requires the complete book. See [program-structure.md](program-structure.md) for details by rank and degree.

### 🔵 Fasting
*Elective. Available from Rank 2.*

Fast with purpose and record the experience.

- **2nd Degree:** Participate in a family fast. Write or draw about the experience
- **3rd Degree:** Fast independently for a specific purpose. Journal about what you learned and any answers received
- **4th Degree:** Complete 3 purposeful fasts over the rank period. Journal each. Identify patterns in how fasting affects your spiritual sensitivity
- **⭐ Honors:** Fast weekly for 1 month for a specific intention. Write a reflection on the relationship between physical sacrifice and spiritual receptivity

### 🔵 Personal Revelation
*Elective. Available from Rank 2.*

Learn to hear and recognize the voice of the Lord in your life.

- **1st Degree:** Share a time you felt happy or peaceful when doing something good
- **2nd Degree:** Keep a "spiritual impressions" page for 2 weeks. Record moments you felt the Spirit
- **3rd Degree:** Study D&C 8:2–3, D&C 9:7–9. Keep a revelation journal for 30 days. Identify at least 3 times you received personal guidance
- **4th Degree:** 60-day revelation journal. Study Elijah's experience (1 Kings 19:11–12) and Nephi's (1 Nephi 11). Write about how the Lord speaks to you specifically
- **⭐ Honors:** Read Elder Bednar's "Patterns of Light" series. Teach a family lesson on recognizing revelation

### 🔵 Temple Preparation
*Elective. Available from Rank 3.*

Learn about and prepare for temple worship.

- **3rd Degree:** Study what temples are and why they matter. Visit a temple (exterior) and journal about it. Memorize 1 scripture about temples
- **4th Degree:** Study the temple symbolism in the Book of Mormon (Mosiah 1–6, 2 Nephi 5:16). Attend baptisms for the dead (if age-appropriate). Write about what covenants mean to you
- **⭐ Honors:** Complete personal indexing/family history work that contributes to temple ordinances. Study the temple's connection to gathering Israel

### 🔵 Prophets Study
*Elective. Available from Rank 2.*

Study the lives and teachings of prophets.

- **2nd Degree:** Learn about 2 prophets from the Book of Mormon. Tell their stories to the family
- **3rd Degree:** Study 3 Book of Mormon prophets in depth. Compare their messages. Write about what they have in common
- **4th Degree:** Study 5 prophets (Book of Mormon + modern). Write a comparative essay. Present to family
- **⭐ Honors:** Study a living prophet's teachings over the last 5 years on a single topic. Identify patterns and compile key quotes

### 🔵 Sacrament Study
*Elective. Available from Rank 2.*

Deepen understanding of the sacrament ordinance.

- **2nd Degree:** Memorize the sacrament prayers (Moroni 4–5). Pay close attention during sacrament for 4 Sundays. Journal about it
- **3rd Degree:** Study the meaning of each phrase in the sacrament prayers. Write about how the sacrament connects to your covenants
- **4th Degree:** Study 3 Ne 18 (the Savior instituting the sacrament). Research the Hebrew/Greek meanings of "remembrance" and "witness." Teach a family lesson

### 🔵 Conference Study
*Elective. Available from Rank 3.*

Engage deeply with general conference.

- **3rd Degree:** Watch/read 3 conference talks. Journal 1 key insight from each. Identify 1 action item
- **4th Degree:** Watch/read 6 talks. Identify themes across speakers. Write a synthesis essay. Present to family
- **⭐ Honors:** Study an entire conference session. Map the flow of topics. Identify how the Spirit directed the order of speakers

## 🛡️ Liberty & Service Badges

### 🔴 Service Project
*Required for Ranks 2–5.*

Plan, execute, and reflect on service. Scale increases by rank and degree. See [program-structure.md](program-structure.md) for specific requirements.

### 🔵 Emergency Preparedness
*Elective. Available from Rank 2.*

Prepare for temporal emergencies.

- **2nd Degree:** Learn what a 72-hour kit is. Help build/check the family emergency supply
- **3rd Degree:** Know how to respond to 3 emergency scenarios (fire, earthquake, power outage). Help maintain family food storage. Learn basic first aid (cuts, burns, CPR awareness)
- **4th Degree:** Complete a first aid certification (or equivalent training). Build a personal 72-hour kit. Create a family emergency plan. Know how to shut off utilities
- **⭐ Honors:** Lead a family emergency drill. Teach first aid to younger siblings. Create a neighborhood-level emergency response plan

### 🔵 Family History
*Elective. Available from Rank 2.*

Connect with ancestors and understand covenant lineage.

- **2nd Degree:** Learn the names of 4 great-grandparents. Hear a family story from a parent or grandparent
- **3rd Degree:** Interview a family member about their life. Record the story (written or audio). Find 3 ancestors on FamilySearch
- **4th Degree:** Complete a 4-generation family tree. Write a family history sketch (2+ pages). Submit names for temple work
- **⭐ Honors:** Research a family line back 6+ generations. Write a biographical sketch of an ancestor. Present to family

### 🔵 Community Builder
*Elective. Available from Rank 3.*

Strengthen your community beyond your family.

- **3rd Degree:** Identify 3 needs in your neighborhood or ward. Execute 1 act of service for each
- **4th Degree:** Plan and lead a community service project involving people outside your family. Spend 10+ hours
- **⭐ Honors:** Organize a recurring service initiative (monthly cleanup, food drive, friendship outreach). Run it for 3+ months

### 🔵 Citizenship
*Elective. Available from Rank 3.*

Understand and appreciate the principles of liberty.

- **3rd Degree:** Study the story of Captain Moroni's Title of Liberty (Alma 46). Learn about 1 founding document (Constitution, Bill of Rights, or Declaration of Independence). Write about what freedom means
- **4th Degree:** Study both Alma 46 and a founding document in depth. Compare the principles. Research a historical threat to liberty and how it was defended. Present to family
- **⭐ Honors:** Study D&C 98 and D&C 101 on the Lord's view of constitutional law. Write an essay connecting Book of Mormon liberty principles to modern governance

### 🔵 Peacemaker
*Elective. Available from Rank 2.*

Learn to resolve conflict and build peace.

- **2nd Degree:** Practice resolving a disagreement with a sibling without arguing. Tell about it at family council
- **3rd Degree:** Study how Moroni and Pahoran resolved their conflict (Alma 59–61). Apply the principles in a real situation. Journal about it
- **4th Degree:** Study 3 Ne 12:9 ("Blessed are the peacemakers"). Mediate a real dispute. Write about what you learned about peace from the Book of Mormon

## 🏗️ Knowledge & Skill Badges

### 🔴 Practical Skill
*Required for Ranks 2–5.*

Learn and demonstrate real-world competence. See [program-structure.md](program-structure.md) for rank-specific requirements.

### 🔵 Cooking
*Elective. Available from Rank 1.*

Learn to nourish yourself and others.

- **1st Degree:** Help prepare a family meal (mixing, measuring, setting table)
- **2nd Degree:** Cook 1 complete simple meal independently (or with minimal supervision)
- **3rd Degree:** Plan and cook 3 different meals for the family. Include at least 1 meal from scratch
- **4th Degree:** Plan a week of meals (budget included). Cook 5 different recipes. Master 1 technique (bread-making, grilling, etc.)
- **⭐ Honors:** Cook a meal for someone outside the family. Research food preparation from Book of Mormon era. Create a family recipe book

### 🔵 Gardening
*Elective. Available from Rank 1.*

Grow something from seed to harvest.

- **1st Degree:** Plant a seed and watch it grow. Water it daily for 2 weeks
- **2nd Degree:** Grow a plant from seed to maturity. Care for it independently
- **3rd Degree:** Plan and plant a garden plot (3+ varieties). Tend it for a full growing season. Harvest and use the produce
- **4th Degree:** Design and manage a garden. Compost. Save seeds. Produce enough to share with a neighbor
- **⭐ Honors:** Research self-sufficiency gardening. Grow enough to preserve food (canning, drying). Teach a younger sibling to garden

### 🔵 Building & Making
*Elective. Available from Rank 2.*

Create something with your hands.

- **2nd Degree:** Build something from a kit or pattern with help (birdhouse, simple project)
- **3rd Degree:** Design and build something from raw materials (woodworking, metalwork, sewing, craft)
- **4th Degree:** Complete a significant build project that requires planning, measurement, and multiple skills
- **⭐ Honors:** Build something functional that the family uses. Document the process

### 🔵 Coding & Technology
*Elective. Available from Rank 2.*

Create with digital tools.

- **2nd Degree:** Complete a beginner coding course or build something in Scratch/similar. Show the result
- **3rd Degree:** Write a program that does something useful. Explain how it works to the family
- **4th Degree:** Build a functional application, website, or digital tool. Deploy it. Demonstrate it
- **⭐ Honors:** Build something that serves the family or community (a tool, tracker, website). Contribute to an open-source project

### 🔵 Music & Art
*Elective. Available from Rank 1.*

Create beauty.

- **1st Degree:** Learn 1 song (singing or instrument). Perform for the family
- **2nd Degree:** Learn 3 songs or create 3 art pieces. Perform or display for the family
- **3rd Degree:** Develop a sustained practice in music or art (3+ months). Perform or exhibit
- **4th Degree:** Demonstrate skill development over time. Perform publicly or create a portfolio. Teach someone else
- **⭐ Honors:** Use your art in service (play for a church meeting, create art for someone, write a hymn arrangement)

### 🔵 Reading
*Elective. Available from Rank 1.*

Read widely and share what you learn.

- **1st Degree:** Be read to: 3 books (beyond scripture). Tell about your favorite
- **2nd Degree:** Read 3 books independently. Share 1 thing learned from each
- **3rd Degree:** Read 5 books (at least 2 non-fiction). Write a book report on 1. Present to family
- **4th Degree:** Read 8 books (at least 3 non-fiction, at least 1 biography of a person of faith). Write a synthesis: what themes do you see across what you've read?
- **⭐ Honors:** Read Lectures on Faith or another significant Restoration text. Write a study document

### 🔵 Language Study
*Elective. Available from Rank 3.*

Study language to deepen scripture understanding.

- **3rd Degree:** Learn 10 words in Hebrew or Greek that appear in the scriptures. Study their meanings
- **4th Degree:** Study 25+ biblical Hebrew/Greek words. Analyze how knowing the original word changes the meaning of 3 scriptures
- **⭐ Honors:** Complete a basic course in biblical Hebrew or Greek. Read and analyze 1 scripture passage in the original language

### 🔵 Writing
*Elective. Available from Rank 2.*

Develop the ability to express truth clearly.

- **2nd Degree:** Write a poem, story, or personal essay (1 page)
- **3rd Degree:** Write 3 pieces (any combination of poetry, fiction, essay, or personal narrative). Share 1 with the family
- **4th Degree:** Maintain a journal throughout the rank period (weekly entries). Write 1 substantial piece (3+ pages)
- **⭐ Honors:** Write something intended for others to read (blog post, lesson, study guide). Publish or share it

## 💪 Strength & Discipline Badges

### 🔴 Physical Challenge
*Required for every rank.*

Meet age-appropriate fitness standards that demonstrate growth over time. See [program-structure.md](program-structure.md) for specific requirements by rank and degree.

### 🔵 Hiking
*Elective. Available from Rank 1.*

Walk long distances in nature.

- **1st Degree:** Complete a 1-mile family walk in nature
- **2nd Degree:** Complete a 3-mile hike. Identify 3 plants or animals along the way
- **3rd Degree:** Complete a 5-mile hike. Pack your own supplies. Navigate a portion of the trail
- **4th Degree:** Complete a 10-mile hike. Plan the route, pack supplies, lead the group for a portion
- **⭐ Honors:** Complete a multi-day backpacking trip (2+ nights). Plan logistics, including food and water

### 🔵 Swimming
*Elective. Available from Rank 1.*

Learn water safety and swimming.

- **1st Degree:** Be comfortable in water. Practice basic water safety
- **2nd Degree:** Swim 25 meters unassisted. Know basic water safety rules
- **3rd Degree:** Swim 100 meters. Tread water for 2 minutes. Know rescue breathing basics
- **4th Degree:** Swim 500 meters continuously. Pass a swim fitness test. Learn rescue techniques
- **⭐ Honors:** Earn a lifeguard certification or equivalent water safety certification

### 🔵 Running
*Elective. Available from Rank 1.*

Build cardiovascular endurance.

- **1st Degree:** Run/walk 400 meters
- **2nd Degree:** Run 1 mile. Track your time
- **3rd Degree:** Run 2 miles. Beat your personal record. Train for 30 days
- **4th Degree:** Run a 5K (timed). Train on a schedule for 60+ days
- **⭐ Honors:** Complete a 10K or comparable endurance race. Document your training progression

### 🔵 Strength Training
*Elective. Available from Rank 2.*

Build physical strength safely.

- **2nd Degree:** Learn 5 bodyweight exercises with proper form. Practice 3x/week for 2 weeks
- **3rd Degree:** Follow a strength program for 30 days. Document progress (reps, form improvement)
- **4th Degree:** Follow a structured program for 60 days. Demonstrate measurable improvement. Understand nutrition basics for strength
- **⭐ Honors:** Train consistently for 90 days. Meet specific strength benchmarks (age-appropriate). Teach proper form to someone else

### 🔵 Self-Discipline
*Elective. Available from Rank 2.*

Master the basics of daily discipline.

- **2nd Degree:** Wake up on time every day for 2 weeks without help. Go to bed on time every night
- **3rd Degree:** Maintain a daily schedule for 30 days (wake time, scripture, exercise, chores — all without reminders). No screen time before responsibilities are done
- **4th Degree:** 60-day daily discipline challenge. Add a "hard thing" you don't want to do but know you should. Journal about what discipline teaches you
- **⭐ Honors:** 90-day discipline streak. Identify and eliminate a personal weakness or bad habit during the period

### 🔵 Outdoor Skills
*Elective. Available from Rank 2.*

Learn to function outdoors.

- **2nd Degree:** Help set up a tent. Learn to tie 3 knots. Spend a night camping
- **3rd Degree:** Set up camp independently. Build a fire safely. Cook 1 meal outdoors. Navigate with a compass or map
- **4th Degree:** Plan and execute a camping trip (logistics, food, shelter). Demonstrate fire-starting, navigation, and outdoor cooking. Lead a campout for younger participants
- **⭐ Honors:** Complete a backcountry overnight trip with minimal gear. Demonstrate wilderness safety and leave-no-trace principles

### 🔵 Martial Arts / Self-Defense
*Elective. Available from Rank 3.*

Learn disciplined physical defense.

- **3rd Degree:** Complete 3 months of martial arts or self-defense training. Demonstrate 5 basic techniques
- **4th Degree:** Train for 6+ months. Advance in rank (belt, level). Demonstrate controlled sparring or technique application
- **⭐ Honors:** Earn a belt advancement. Teach basic self-defense to a family member

## Creating Custom Badges

Families can create their own badges following this template:

```
### Badge Name
*Elective. Pillar: [pillar]. Available from Rank [X].*

[Description of what this badge develops]

- **1st Degree:** [requirement]
- **2nd Degree:** [requirement]
- **3rd Degree:** [requirement]
- **4th Degree:** [requirement]
- **⭐ Honors:** [advanced requirement]
```

Custom badges must:
1. Map to one of the four pillars
2. Have requirements that scale across degrees
3. Be approved by family council
4. Require demonstrable effort (not just attendance)
