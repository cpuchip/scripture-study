# Prophetic Destiny of the Elders of Israel & the Birthright to Defend Liberty

**Video:** [Send Me — Hannah Stoddard (Joseph Smith Foundation)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pZljklzcdEI)
**Published:** March 3, 2026
**Duration:** ~2 hours 3 minutes
**Speakers:** Alexander (Send Me host) + Hannah Stoddard (Director, Joseph Smith Foundation)

---

## Thesis

The Book of Mormon is the primary political and spiritual textbook for the last days. The elders of Israel — particularly those of the birthright lineage of Joseph — have a covenant responsibility not just to share the gospel but to **defend liberty, build Zion, and prepare a place for the Lord to return to**. The Latter-day Saints are under condemnation ([D&C 84:54–55](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/84.md)) because they have treated lightly the Book of Mormon and the revelations, and the path forward starts with taking the Book of Mormon seriously as a **survival manual and kingdom-building blueprint** for our day.

---

## Key Claims & Evaluation

### In Line with Scripture and Prophetic Teaching

**1. The Book of Mormon as written for our day**
The video's central claim — that Mormon and Moroni saw our day and wrote specifically for us — is bedrock doctrine. Moroni himself states it plainly. The video correctly grounds this in the condemnation passage:

> "And your minds in times past have been darkened because of unbelief, and because you have treated lightly the things you have received — Which vanity and unbelief have brought the whole church under condemnation." — [D&C 84:54–55](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/84.md)

President Ezra Taft Benson confirmed this condemnation was still in force during his presidency. The video's appeal to take the Book of Mormon more seriously as a comprehensive guide (not just a spiritual devotional) aligns with prophetic emphasis.

**2. Captain Moroni's appeal to Josephite identity**
Hannah correctly identifies that Moroni's Title of Liberty speech appeals not to generic patriotism but to **covenant identity as remnants of Joseph**:

> "Behold, we are a remnant of the seed of Jacob; yea, we are a remnant of the seed of Joseph, whose coat was rent by his brethren into many pieces" — [Alma 46:23](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/46.md)

> "Yea, let us preserve our liberty as a remnant of Joseph" — [Alma 46:24](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/46.md)

This is a strong textual observation. Moroni's call to action is rooted in who the people **are** by covenant, not merely in political necessity. This is the Title of Liberty's power — it's identity before action.

**3. Abraham 1:2 — The pattern of seeking the blessings of the fathers**
The video closes powerfully with Abraham's account:

> "Finding there was greater happiness and peace and rest for me, I sought for the blessings of the fathers, and the right whereunto I should be ordained to administer the same" — [Abraham 1:2](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/abr/1.md)

This is exactly what the text says. Abraham's desire to obtain and then **administer** — to bless others — is the Josephite pattern: receive, then serve.

**4. The 2,000 Stripling Warriors — faith transmitted through family**
The video references the mothers' faith as the foundation of the warriors' courage:

> "They had been taught by their mothers, that if they did not doubt, God would deliver them." — [Alma 56:47](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/56.md)

> "We do not doubt our mothers knew it." — [Alma 56:48](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/56.md)

The principle is sound and is the heartbeat of why a family-based boot camp matters: **faith is transmitted through parents who actually believe it themselves**.

**5. D&C 101 — The Parable of the Vineyard**
Hannah's reading of the parable of the nobleman and the olive trees ([D&C 101:43–62](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/101.md)) as a warning about neglecting the Lord's instructions to fortify is textually grounded. The watchmen who said "What need hath my Lord of this tower?" and became slothful is a powerful parallel to modern complacency.

**6. Book of Mormon parallels to our day**
The "For Our Day" model — reading the Book of Mormon as a chronological parallel to the signs of the times — is an approach Elder Bednar has implicitly endorsed by repeatedly teaching that the Book of Mormon was written for *our* situation, not merely as ancient history. The video is careful (at Hannah's insistence) to avoid pinning specific dates, which is wise:

> "Prophecy is always about themes, ideas, periods... The Lord does that to make you work through it." — [Hannah, ~1:46:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pZljklzcdEI&t=6360)

This is more responsible than date-matching approaches.

### Out of Line / Needs Caution

**1. Joseph Smith crowned "king over Israel"**
The Council of Fifty history is real and documented. However, the video presents this without adequate nuance about the difference between *eschatological* symbolism and *temporal* political claims. The Council of Fifty minutes (published by the Church) show a more complex picture than "he was crowned king" implies. This is an area where documentary evidence exists but interpretation varies widely. The video doesn't fabricate, but it leans hard.

**2. Joseph Smith's bloodline as "successor to the throne of David"**
Hannah states Joseph Smith was "not just Ephraim, he was also Judah" and "a direct bloodline rightful successor to the throne of David." While patriarchal blessings do speak of mixed lineage, the specific claim about Davidic succession through blood is speculative historical reconstruction, not established doctrine. Brigham Young made statements about Joseph's lineage, but these are historical records, not canonized revelation. The video presents this at the same confidence level as scripture. That's a category error.

**3. Resurrected Joseph Smith directing last-days events in person**
The video references Brigham Young statements about Joseph and Hyrum personally (resurrected) laying out inheritances in Jackson County. While this is a real Brigham Young quote, it's one leader's speculation/prophecy, not canonized scripture. Hannah does appropriately warn against "crazy tangents" and emphasizes proper keys, which is good. But the overall framing gives these ideas more weight than they can bear.

**4. George Washington's "second anointing" and founding fathers as high priests**
The Wilford Woodruff temple experience is documented in journals. Hannah's characterization of the founding fathers receiving "second anointings" and being ordained "high priests in the Enoch/Melchizedek sense" goes beyond what the historical record says. Woodruff recorded performing ordinances for them — that's different from claiming they reached a specific spiritual status.

### Missed the Mark

**1. "We are losing" without engagement with current prophetic direction**
The video's assessment that the Saints are "losing" statistically is supported by data on retention, marriage, etc. But there's a significant gap: the current prophet, President Nelson, has given very specific counsel for gathering Israel (the youth battalion invitation), home-centered gospel study, hearing His voice, etc. The video essentially says "the prophets told us what to do and we didn't listen" — but the examples are almost exclusively from *past* prophets (Benson, Kimball, Brigham Young). The implicit framing that the current church leadership is somehow part of the problem (through "progressive forces") is concerning and risks undermining the very prophetic authority they claim to champion.

**2. Education critique without balance**
The narrative about Latter-day Saints abandoning church schools for public education is historically interesting but presented as a simple morality tale. The actual history is far more complex — the Church itself transitioned away from running comprehensive school systems for practical and financial reasons. This isn't just the members "rejecting the prophets."

---

### Missed Opportunities

**1. The temple as covenant context**
The video dances around the temple endowment's connection to these themes but never lands it scripturally. [D&C 109:22–26](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/109.md) (the Kirtland Temple dedicatory prayer) explicitly asks that the Saints be armed with power, that angels bear them up, and that they gather the righteous. This would have powerfully connected their thesis to a canonized source rather than relying on Brigham Young quotes.

**2. Alma 48:11–17 — The character of Moroni**
The video emphasizes Moroni as a war general but skips the text that describes his character:

> "Moroni was a strong and a mighty man; he was a man of a perfect understanding; yea, a man that did not delight in bloodshed; a man whose soul did joy in the liberty and the freedom of his country" — [Alma 48:11](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/48.md)

> "If all men had been, and were, and ever would be, like unto Moroni, behold, the very powers of hell would have been shaken forever" — [Alma 48:17](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/48.md)

The emphasis is on *character* — understanding, reluctance, faith, joy in freedom — not just military prowess. A boot camp for our day should center this.

**3. The Stripling Warriors' actual attributes**
The video names the stripling warriors but doesn't dig into their specific qualities from the text:

> "They were exceedingly valiant for courage, and also for strength and activity; but behold, this was not all — they were men who were true at all times in whatsoever thing they were entrusted. Yea, they were men of truth and soberness, for they had been taught to keep the commandments of God and to walk uprightly before him." — [Alma 53:20–21](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/53.md)

Valiant, strong, active, true, trustworthy, sober, obedient, upright. **That's the character profile.** This is the training program.

## Overall Assessment

**Rating: 7/10 — Spiritually useful with meaningful caveats**

The core thesis is sound and important: the Book of Mormon is written for our day, the elders of Israel have a covenant responsibility beyond church attendance, the Title of Liberty appeals to identity before action, and taking the Book of Mormon seriously is the first step. These are powerful truths grounded in scripture.

The video gets into trouble when it mixes canonized scripture with historical speculation at equal confidence levels. The Joseph Smith Foundation material on bloodlines, political kingdoms, and resurrected prophets directing temporal affairs ventures into territory where faith and speculation blur. Hannah is clearly knowledgeable and passionate, and she appropriately warns against "crazy tangents" — but some of her own content lives near that line.

**What to take:** The call to know who you are (a remnant of Joseph with covenant responsibilities), to take the Book of Mormon seriously as a comprehensive guide, and to build rather than just survive. The Book of Mormon as a "boot camp" for Zion-building is a genuinely powerful framework.

**What to hold lightly:** The specific claims about bloodlines, political thrones, resurrected Joseph Smith in Jackson County, and the founding fathers' temple ordinance status. These are interesting historical rabbit holes but should not be treated as doctrine.

**Michael's note on the "For Our Day" timeline model:** You're right that this is more Elder Bednar's approach (thematic patterns, principles for our day) than the Pickering/Philpot approach (date matching). Hannah explicitly rejects date-matching and emphasizes themes, which is exactly the right instinct.
