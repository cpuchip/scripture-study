# Title of Liberty

**A Multi-Year Discipleship Program Grounded in the Book of Mormon**

---

## Why This Exists

The Lord told us plainly:

> "And your minds in times past have been darkened because of unbelief, and because you have treated lightly the things you have received — Which vanity and unbelief have brought the whole church under condemnation. And this condemnation resteth upon the children of Zion, even all. And they shall remain under this condemnation until they repent and remember the new covenant, even the Book of Mormon and the former commandments which I have given them, not only to say, but to **do** according to that which I have written" — [D&C 84:54–57](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/84.md)

This far, no farther.

We are under condemnation for treating the Book of Mormon lightly. Not someone else — *us*. The children of Zion, even all. And the way out isn't just reading it. It's *doing* according to what's written. Becoming the kind of people who live what it teaches.

Title of Liberty is a structured, multi-year discipleship program for families — designed to take the Book of Mormon off the shelf and into the bones. It is rigorous but approachable. It asks real things of real people. It is built for children as young as 4 and adults who want to walk the same path alongside them.

We are not bashful about this. The Book of Mormon is a covenant. We intend to keep it.

---

## What It Is

A **family training program** modeled after the best youth development traditions (Scouting, Pathfinders, Royal Rangers) but rooted entirely in the Book of Mormon and the restored gospel. It features:

- **5 Ranks** — progressive character milestones named for Book of Mormon figures
- **4 Degrees per rank** — age-appropriate difficulty scaling so any child can enter at any age
- **Merit Badges** — required and elective achievements across four pillars of growth
- **Family Council** — weekly check-ins, rank advancement ceremonies, and shared accountability
- **Digital Integration** — tracked through the Becoming app (ibeco.me / webeco.me)

It is not a Sunday School curriculum. It is not a reading plan. It is a way of life.

## Who It's For

**Phase 1: Our family.** Five kids, ages 4–15, led by parents who want to build the character described in Alma 53:20–21:

> "They were exceedingly valiant for courage, and also for strength and activity; but behold, this was not all — they were men who were true at all times in whatsoever thing they were entrusted. Yea, they were men of truth and soberness, for they had been taught to keep the commandments of God and to walk uprightly before him." — [Alma 53:20–21](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/53.md)

**Phase 2: Other families.** The program is designed to be shareable — through webeco.me as a community feature, and eventually as a standalone resource for wards, stakes, and homeschool families.

**Phase 3: Anyone.** Adults, missionaries, individuals. Anyone who wants a structured path through the Book of Mormon that demands more than passive reading.

## The Four Pillars

Every rank, badge, and challenge maps to one of four pillars of growth. These emerge from scripture and align with the Church's Children and Youth framework (Luke 2:52) and the Becoming app's pillar system.

| Pillar | Emoji | Scripture Foundation | What It Develops |
|--------|-------|---------------------|------------------|
| **Faith & Covenant** | ⚔️ | [Alma 53:21](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/53.md) — "men of truth and soberness ... taught to keep the commandments" | Prayer, scripture mastery, ordinances, personal revelation, covenant keeping |
| **Liberty & Service** | 🛡️ | [Alma 46:12](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/46.md) — "in memory of our God, our religion, and freedom" | Service, leadership, community building, emergency preparedness, defending truth |
| **Knowledge & Skill** | 🏗️ | [Abraham 1:2](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/abr/1.md) — "desiring also to be one who possessed great knowledge" | Practical skills, deep study, creative work, career preparation, wisdom |
| **Strength & Discipline** | 💪 | [Alma 53:20](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/53.md) — "exceedingly valiant for courage, and also for strength and activity" | Physical fitness, endurance, self-discipline, health, doing hard things |

## The Ranks

Each rank represents a stage of becoming — named for a Book of Mormon figure who embodies that stage. Ranks are earned sequentially. You don't skip ranks, but you enter at the degree appropriate to your age.

| Rank | Name | Character | Core Virtue |
|------|------|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | **Apprentice of Lehi** | Willing to begin | Obedience to the call |
| 2 | **Scout of Nephi** | Doing hard things | Faith in action |
| 3 | **Warrior of Helaman** | Trustworthy in all things | Reliability & integrity |
| 4 | **Captain of Moroni** | Defending & leading | Courage & preparation |
| 5 | **Builder of Zion** | Creating refuge for others | The Josephite birthright |

### Degrees

Each rank has **4 degrees**, corresponding to age bands. Degrees scale the difficulty and depth of requirements while preserving the same core character goals.

| Degree | Age Band | Description |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| **1st Degree** | 4–6 | Family-supported. Activity-based. Verbal and visual. |
| **2nd Degree** | 7–9 | Independent reading begins. Journals. Real but scaled challenges. |
| **3rd Degree** | 10–12 | Full participation. Teaching, building, leading. The core boot camp age. |
| **4th Degree** | 13+ | Leadership emphasis. Mentoring. Projects that serve others. |

**Starting late:** If a 10-year-old enters the program, they begin at Rank 1, 3rd Degree. They work through all five ranks at their degree level. The ranks build on each other — you can't lead (Rank 4) until you've learned to follow (Rank 2) and be trusted (Rank 3). But the degree ensures the work matches their capability.

**Moving up in degree:** A child who begins at 2nd Degree doesn't re-do their current rank when they age into the 3rd Degree band. They continue forward at the new degree level for their *next* rank. Growth is always forward.

## Merit Badges

Inspired by the BSA merit badge system, Title of Liberty uses **required** and **elective** badges to ensure breadth while allowing personal interest.

### Required Badges (per rank)

Each rank requires completing a set of core badges that ensure foundational growth across all four pillars:

| Badge | Pillar | Required For | Description |
|-------|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Scripture Mastery | ⚔️ | Every rank | Memorize specific scriptures (count scales by rank/degree) |
| Book of Mormon Reading | ⚔️ | Every rank | Read assigned chapters (scope scales by rank) |
| Service Project | 🛡️ | Ranks 2–5 | Plan and execute real service (scale increases) |
| Physical Challenge | 💪 | Every rank | Meet age-appropriate fitness standard |
| Practical Skill | 🏗️ | Ranks 2–5 | Learn and demonstrate a real-world skill |
| Teaching | ⚔️ | Ranks 3–5 | Teach a lesson to the family or others |
| Leadership | 🛡️ | Ranks 4–5 | Lead a project or mentor a younger participant |
| Capstone Project | 🏗️ | Rank 5 only | Sustained project that blesses others |

### Elective Badges (choose from catalog)

Each rank also requires a minimum number of elective badges. These are organized by pillar and allow participants to pursue their interests while earning credit toward advancement.

**Elective requirements per rank:**

| Rank | Required Electives | Minimum Pillars Represented |
|------|-------------------|----------------------------|
| 1 — Apprentice | 2 | 2 of 4 |
| 2 — Scout | 4 | 3 of 4 |
| 3 — Warrior | 6 | All 4 |
| 4 — Captain | 8 | All 4, with at least 2 per pillar |
| 5 — Builder | 12 | All 4, with at least 3 per pillar |

The full badge catalog is in [merit-badges.md](merit-badges.md).

## Program Rhythm

### Daily
- Personal scripture study (tracked via Becoming app)
- Prayer
- Physical activity
- One small act of service or kindness

### Weekly
- **Family Council** (Sunday): Review progress, celebrate wins, plan challenges for the week
- **Family Scripture Study**: Read the assigned Book of Mormon chapters together
- **Badge Work**: Dedicated time for working on merit badge requirements

### Monthly
- **Badge Review**: Family council reviews badge work and awards completed badges
- **Challenge Night**: A special family activity tied to the current rank's theme (e.g., a hike, a service project, a cooking challenge)

### Quarterly
- **Rank Check-in**: Formal review of rank progress
- **Testimony Meeting**: Each family member shares what they've learned and how they've grown

### Annually
- **Rank Advancement Ceremony**: When a rank is completed, celebrate as a family event
- **Title of Liberty Renewal**: Each family member updates their personal declaration
- **Boot Camp Week**: An intensive week of challenges, activities, and study (summer)

## Becoming App Integration

Title of Liberty is designed to run on the Becoming app infrastructure (ibeco.me and webeco.me). The mapping:

| Program Concept | Becoming App Feature | Notes |
|----------------|---------------------|-------|
| Pillars (4) | Pillars feature | Map directly — Faith & Covenant, Liberty & Service, Knowledge & Skill, Strength & Discipline |
| Daily practices | Practices (habit/tracker type) | Scripture study, prayer, exercise tracked as daily practices |
| Merit badge requirements | Tasks | Each badge requirement is a task with checklist items |
| Scripture memorization | Practices (memorize type) | Spaced repetition cards for required scriptures |
| Rank progress | Milestones / Reflections | Rank advancement triggers a milestone reflection |
| Badge completion | Task completion | Grouped under pillar, tagged to rank |
| Family council notes | Notes | Attached to weekly review |

### ibeco.me vs webeco.me

- **ibeco.me** — Personal tracker. Each family member has their own account tracking their daily practices, memorization, and badge progress.
- **webeco.me** — Community layer. The "Title of Liberty" program template lives here. Family leaders (parents) can create a "troop" (family), assign ranks, view progress across all members, and manage badge advancement. Other families can join the program with their own troop.

This split means the **personal discipline** stays personal (ibeco.me) while the **program structure and community** lives on the web (webeco.me). A family leader dashboard on webeco.me shows all members' progress at a glance — which badges are in progress, who's close to rank advancement, what needs attention.

## What Makes This Different

| Existing Program | What It Does Well | What Title of Liberty Adds |
|-----------------|-------------------|---------------------------|
| **BSA / Scouting** | Rank progression, merit badges, leadership development, service projects | Gospel foundation. Book of Mormon as primary text. Covenant identity. Family-centered (not troop-centered). |
| **Children and Youth (LDS)** | Four growth areas (Luke 2:52), self-directed goals, flexibility | Structure. Rank progression. Required achievements. Accountability system. Specific curriculum vs. open-ended goals. |
| **Royal Rangers** | Bible merits, skill merits, leadership merits, quarterly advancement | Book of Mormon focus, not generic Bible. Family program vs. church program. Integrated digital tracking. |
| **Pathfinders (SDA)** | 6 age-based levels, 7 tracks per level, honors system | Restored gospel framework. Covenant theology. Josephite identity. |
| **Awana** | Scripture memorization, age-progression, rewards | Deeper engagement (not just memorization but application). Four-pillar balance. Multi-year character development. |

**The gap this fills:** No existing program combines BSA-style structured rank progression with Book of Mormon-centered character development, digital practice tracking, and a family-first (not church-program-first) approach. The Church's Children and Youth program is intentionally flexible; Title of Liberty is intentionally structured. They complement, not compete.

## Research & Inspiration

### Programs Studied

1. **Boy Scouts of America** — Ranks (Scout → Eagle), 130+ merit badges (13 required for Eagle, 8 elective), service projects, leadership positions. Michael is an Eagle Scout. The merit badge model — required core + elective choice — is the direct inspiration for our badge system.

2. **SDA Pathfinders (Investiture Achievement)** — 6 levels (Friend → Guide), grades 5–10, 7 tracks per level. Age-appropriate requirements that build year to year. Inspired our degree system.

3. **Royal Rangers (Assemblies of God)** — Bible merits, skill merits, leadership merits. Bible studies use Read → Reflect → Respond pattern. Quarterly advancement. Inspired our three-type badge categorization (faith, skill, leadership).

4. **Awana** — Age-graded curriculum from preschool through high school. Heavy scripture memorization. Badge/award progression. Showed that even very young children can engage meaningfully with a ranked program.

5. **LDS Children and Youth** — Four growth areas (Spiritual, Social, Physical, Intellectual from Luke 2:52). Self-directed goals. Discover → Plan → Act → Reflect cycle. Our four pillars map directly to theirs. The difference: Children and Youth is flexible by design; Title of Liberty adds the structure that some families want.

6. **Nashua Stake "Stripling Warrior Camp" (2020)** — A 3-day virtual/in-person camp for Aaronic Priesthood youth. Theme: "Worthy to Be Called Sons" (Alma 56:9–10). Showed this concept resonates with youth.

7. **Friend Magazine Stripling Warrior Challenge** — Simple physical + spiritual challenge for Primary-age kids. Validated that even young children connect with the stripling warrior identity.

### Key Insight from Research

Every program that endures shares three traits:
1. **Clear progression** — Participants know where they are and what's next
2. **Visible recognition** — Achievements are celebrated and displayed
3. **Real challenge** — The requirements demand genuine effort, not just attendance

Title of Liberty aims for all three.

## The Covenant Thread

This program isn't about achievement for achievement's sake. It's about **covenant identity**.

The Book of Mormon is the story of a covenant people — their faithfulness, their failures, and their eventual triumph through Christ. The stripling warriors weren't remarkable because they were strong fighters. They were remarkable because "they had been taught by their mothers, that if they did not doubt, God would deliver them" ([Alma 56:47](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/56.md)).

That faith came from parents who lived it. Mothers who taught their children to keep covenants. A community that prepared its young people for the specific challenges of their day.

We face our own version of those challenges. Our children are growing up in a world that treats lightly everything the Book of Mormon teaches. This program is our family's answer: we will not treat it lightly. We will read it, memorize it, live it, teach it, and build the kind of character it describes.

> "They were all young men, and they were exceedingly valiant for courage, and also for strength and activity; but behold, this was not all — they were men who were true at all times in whatsoever thing they were entrusted." — [Alma 53:20](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/53.md)

That's the standard. That's what we're building toward.
