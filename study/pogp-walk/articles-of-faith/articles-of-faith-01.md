# The Articles of Faith — the whole walk in thirteen sentences

**Binding idea:** The Pearl of Great Price closes by distilling itself. Thirteen statements (the 1842 Wentworth letter) gather everything the walk traced — the embodied Godhead, accountability not inherited guilt, the gospel's ordinances, the restored priesthood, the open canon, the gathering and the renewed earth — into a creed a child can recite. And the keystone, Article 9 ("**we believe that He will yet reveal many great and important things**"), is the very thing the Pearl of Great Price *is*: a book of great and important things revealed after the Bible closed. The collection proves its own ninth article. The walk ends, fittingly, on Article 13 — not a prohibition but a seeking: "if there is anything virtuous, lovely, or of good report or praiseworthy, we seek after these things."

**Read:** Thirteen articles, signed "Joseph Smith." (1) Belief in "God, the Eternal Father, and in His Son, Jesus Christ, and in the Holy Ghost." (2) "Men will be punished for their own sins, and not for Adam's transgression." (3) "Through the Atonement of Christ, all mankind may be saved, by obedience to the laws and ordinances of the Gospel." (4) The first principles and ordinances: faith, repentance, baptism by immersion for the remission of sins, laying on of hands for the gift of the Holy Ghost. (5) A man "must be called of God, by prophecy, and by the laying on of hands by those who are in authority." (6) "The same organization that existed in the Primitive Church." (7) "The gift of tongues, prophecy, revelation, visions, healing, interpretation of tongues." (8) "The Bible to be the word of God as far as it is translated correctly; we also believe the Book of Mormon to be the word of God." (9) "We believe all that God has revealed, all that He does now reveal, and we believe that He will yet reveal many great and important things pertaining to the Kingdom of God." (10) "The literal gathering of Israel… the restoration of the Ten Tribes; that Zion (the New Jerusalem) will be built upon the American continent; that Christ will reign personally upon the earth; and, that the earth will be renewed and receive its paradisiacal glory." (11) "The privilege of worshiping Almighty God according to the dictates of our own conscience, and allow all men the same privilege." (12) "Being subject to kings, presidents, rulers, and magistrates, in obeying, honoring, and sustaining the law." (13) "Being honest, true, chaste, benevolent, virtuous, and in doing good to all men… If there is anything virtuous, lovely, or of good report or praiseworthy, we seek after these things."

**Thoughts:**
- *The thirteen articles are the Pearl of Great Price's own summary — every one roots in a chapter the walk just read.* This is the gift of finishing the walk before reading the creed: the Articles stop being abstractions and become *the distillate of the previous fifteen chapters.*
  - **Art. 1 (the Godhead)** — Moses 1 (God face to face, the Only Begotten the Creator) and JS-H 1:17 (two embodied Personages, the Father pointing to the Son: "Hear Him"). Three distinct beings, not one substance.
  - **Art. 2 (own sins, not Adam's)** — this is **Moses 6:54** made a creed: "the sins of the parents cannot be answered upon the heads of the children, for they are whole." The Restoration's rejection of inherited guilt, stated in the walk's own Book of Moses and now distilled here. (Footnote → Ezek 18:20, "the soul that sinneth, it shall die.")
  - **Art. 3 (Atonement + obedience)** — the gospel preached to Adam (Moses 5-6); the footnote on "saved" points to **Moses 5:9** ("as thou hast fallen thou mayest be redeemed").
  - **Art. 4-5 (the ordinances + the calling)** — the unbroken gospel Adam → Enoch → Noah (Moses 5:8, 6:52, 8:24) and the priesthood "called of God… by the laying on of hands" — Abraham *sought* it (Abr 1:2-4), and JS-H 1:68-72 *restored* it by ordination.
  - **Art. 7 (the gifts)** — the answer to the cessationism JS-H diagnosed (the Methodist's "revelations have ceased with the apostles," 1:21). The same anti-cessationism the BoM walk found in Mormon 9 and Moroni 10.
  - **Art. 8 (Bible "as far as translated correctly" + BoM)** — the footnote on "correctly" sends to **1 Ne 13:26** (the plain and precious things taken away), which is **Moses 1:41** ("they shall… take many of them from the book"). The whole PoGP is the recovery of what Art. 8 says the Bible lost.
  - **Art. 10 (gathering + Zion + renewed earth)** — the footnote on "Zion" points to **Moses 7:62** (Enoch's New Jerusalem), and "paradisiacal glory" is glossed as "a condition like the Garden of Eden." So Article 10 closes the walk's own arc: the earth that fell in Moses 4 is "renewed" to the Eden of Moses 2-3. Garden lost → garden regained.
- *★ Article 9 is the keystone, and the Pearl of Great Price is its proof (9).* "We believe all that God has revealed, all that He does now reveal, and we believe that **He will yet reveal many great and important things**." This is the open canon — the single most distinctive Restoration claim, and the exact refutation of the cessationism that JS-H 1:21 named as the apostasy's signature ("all such things had ceased with the apostles"). And here is the quiet wonder: *the Pearl of Great Price is itself the demonstration of Article 9.* Moses (1830), Abraham (1835), JS-Matthew (1831), JS-History (1838) — every one is a "great and important thing" revealed *after* the Bible was closed and sealed by the creeds. The book embodies its own ninth article. You don't have to argue for continuing revelation in the abstract; you've just walked fifteen chapters of it. The Pearl is the receipt. (And Article 9 is why the canon stays open — D&C, the Proclamations, living apostles: the same principle that produced these chapters keeps producing.)
- *The articles move in a deliberate order — God to neighbor (1-13).* God and the Godhead (1) → human accountability and the Atonement (2-3) → the gospel's ordinances (4-5) → the church's structure and gifts (6-7) → scripture and revelation (8-9) → the last days and the renewed earth (10) → freedom, citizenship, and ethics (11-13). It descends from heaven to the street: it begins with the Father and ends with "doing good to all men." A complete worldview, top to bottom, in thirteen sentences — and the motion is *outward*, from God toward the neighbor, which is the gospel's own direction (the two great commandments).
- *★ Article 13 is the walk's benediction (13).* After the cosmos (Abr 3), the council (Abr 4, Moses 4), the weeping God (Moses 7), and the grove (JS-H), the creed ends not with a warning or a boundary but with a *disposition*: "If there is anything virtuous, lovely, or of good report or praiseworthy, **we seek after these things**" (Philippians 4:8). The last word of the Pearl of Great Price is a posture of seeking the good — open, hungry, drawn toward the lovely. It rhymes with where the whole walk *began*: Abraham's covenant opened with *desire* ("I sought… desiring," Abr 1:2), and the creed closes with *seeking* ("we seek after these things"). The book that started with a man bound on an altar and a prophet on his face in the dirt ends with the simplest and most hopeful instruction in scripture: seek what is lovely. That's the right place for the walk to stop.

**Critical analysis / tensions named:**
- *A creed about not having creeds (8, 9).* The First Vision diagnosed "all their creeds were an abomination" (JS-H 1:19), yet the Articles of Faith *are* a creed. The resolution is in Article 9: this creed is *open*, not closed — it explicitly leaves room for "many great and important things" yet to be revealed. The abomination JS-H named was the creed that *closes* revelation ("a form of godliness, but they deny the power"); the Articles are a creed that keeps the canon open. Worth naming: the Restoration isn't anti-creed, it's anti-*closed*-creed.
- *Article 8's "as far as it is translated correctly" is doing precise work.* It affirms the Bible *and* qualifies it — the same move as Moses 1:41 / 1 Ne 13. Not "the Bible is wrong" (the Restoration reveres it), but "the Bible has lost things in transmission, and they are being restored." The walk is the evidence: Moses 2-8 *is* the restored front matter of Genesis. The qualification is a claim of recovery, not rejection.
- *Provenance.* The Articles come from the 1842 Wentworth letter (Joseph's reply to a newspaper editor) and were canonized into the PoGP in 1880. They are a *summary* statement, not a revelation in the Moses/Abraham sense — Joseph distilling the faith for an outsider. The walk reads them as the canonized creed they became, and notes they are summary, not theophany.
