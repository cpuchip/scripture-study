# Moses 3 — the creation was spiritual first, and the first commandment guards agency

**Binding idea:** The same creation just narrated (Moses 2) is now disclosed to be *spiritual before it was natural* (3:5) — and when God gives the one prohibition, He embeds the agency right into it: "nevertheless, thou mayest choose for thyself, for it is given unto thee" (3:17). Restoration creation is spirit-first and freedom-first.

**Read:** Moses 3 covers the Eden material of Genesis 2 — the rest on the seventh day, the forming of man from dust, the garden, the two trees, the four rivers, the naming of the animals, and the making of woman. But two restorations transform it. First, v5: "I, the Lord God, created all things… **spiritually, before they were naturally** upon the face of the earth… for in heaven created I them; and there was not yet flesh upon the earth." The physical creation of Moses 2 was preceded by a spiritual creation. v7 makes the seam explicit at man: "I… formed man from the dust… and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life; and man became a living soul, the first flesh upon the earth… **nevertheless, all things were before created; but spiritually were they created and made**." Even the trees (v9) and beasts (v19) "were also living souls." Second, the Eden command (v16-17): "Of every tree of the garden thou mayest freely eat, but of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil, thou shalt not eat of it, **nevertheless, thou mayest choose for thyself, for it is given unto thee; but, remember that I forbid it.**" God then says "unto mine Only Begotten, that it was not good that the man should be alone" (v18), Adam names every creature (v19-20), and woman is made — "bone of my bones, and flesh of my flesh… they shall be one flesh" (v23-24).

**Thoughts:**
- *The spiritual creation is the chapter's center of gravity (v5).* Moses 2 read as a flat physical account; Moses 3 reveals it was the *second* creation. "I… created all things… spiritually, before they were naturally… in heaven created I them." This is the doctrine the rest of the Restoration leans on: the antemortal existence of all things, including "all the children of men" created before the earth (v5, footnote → "Man, Antemortal Existence of"; D&C 29:31-34 — "all things unto me are spiritual"). It's the matter-spectrum in the creation narrative: spirit is the prior, finer state of every later physical thing. Nothing in Eden is fresh-minted matter; it's spirit clothed in flesh.
- *"Living soul" is a breath-creature, and the Hebrew already extended it to all life.* "Man became a living soul" (v7) is Genesis 2:7's *nephesh chayyah* — and *nephesh* (H5315) is "properly, a breathing creature… vitality," the same word Genesis uses for fish, fowl, and beast (Gen 1:20-21,24). So when Moses 3 calls the *trees* (v9) and the *beasts* (v19) "living souls," it isn't loose poetry — it's restoring what the Hebrew word always carried: *soul* is shared breath-life, not a uniquely human possession. v9 goes further than the Hebrew, naming the plants spirit-created beings that "remaineth in the sphere in which I, God, created it." D&C 77:2 (the spirit of the beasts) is the same teaching.
- *The body is dust + breath, and the man is "adam" from "adamah."* "Formed man from the dust of the ground" (v7): *adam* (H120, man) drawn from *adamah* (H127, ground/soil) — the wordplay is in the names. Man is *earthling*, the breathed-on dust. The dignity isn't in the material (dust) but in the breath (the breath of life from God). This is the same lesson as Moses 1: man is dust unless God's breath/glory is upon him. Genesis hides the pun in translation; the doctrine survives it.
- *3:17 is the single most important Restoration edit in the chapter — maybe in the creation account.* Genesis 2:17 reads only "thou shalt not eat of it… thou shalt surely die." Moses inserts the agency: "**nevertheless, thou mayest choose for thyself, for it is given unto thee**; but, remember that I forbid it." In one clause the Fall stops being a trap and becomes a genuine choice. God forbids *and* frees in the same breath — He states the law, states the consequence, and explicitly hands Adam the freedom to choose against it. This is Lehi's theology (2 Ne 2:15-16 in the footnote — "it must needs be that there was an opposition," "the Lord God gave unto man that he should act for himself") written into the command itself. The "nevertheless" here is doing the same hinge-work it did in Moses 1:20 — pivoting from prohibition to liberty.
- *The Only Begotten is in the room for Eve's creation too (v18).* "I, the Lord God, said unto mine Only Begotten, that it was not good that the man should be alone." Genesis has no addressee; Moses keeps Christ present through the whole Eden sequence, consistent with 2:26. Creation — including the making of the family — is a Father-and-Son work throughout.
- *Adam names the animals, and that is the first exercise of dominion (v19-20).* "to see what he would call them… whatsoever Adam called every living creature, that should be the name thereof." Naming is the first act of the human mind in the record — language as the instrument of dominion (footnote → TG Language). The God who created "by the word" (Moses 2) gives the man a derivative word-power: to name. (Our `language-of-adam` study works this directly.)

**Critical analysis / tensions named:**
- *Two creations, not two contradictory accounts.* A skeptical reading sees Genesis 1 and 2 as rival creation stories (different order, plants-before/after man). Moses 3:5 dissolves the conflict by making them *sequential phases* — spiritual then natural — rather than competing physical timelines. That's an interpretive move worth naming as the Restoration's resolution, not a neutral fact: it reframes the "two accounts" problem theologically.
- *"Thou shalt surely die" vs. the necessity of the Fall.* The command warns of death; yet the plan *required* the Fall (2 Ne 2). The tension is real and the chapter doesn't resolve it — Moses 4 (the actual Fall) and the broader plan-of-salvation theology do. v17's agency clause is the hinge that lets the warning be true *and* the choice be genuinely Adam's. I'm flagging, not smoothing.
- *Eden's geography is relocated (v13 footnote).* The footnote notes the Eden/Adam-ondi-Ahman names were "later attached to other lands and rivers," pointing to D&C 117:8-9 (Missouri). This is a distinctly LDS claim layered onto the Genesis rivers; honest to mark it as Restoration geography, not text-internal.
