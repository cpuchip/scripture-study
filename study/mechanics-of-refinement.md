# The Mechanics of Refinement: D&C 93 and the Physics of the Atonement

*A study on the physicality of spiritual transformation — how light, truth, and glory operate under law, and how the Atonement physically refines the human spirit to abide celestial glory. Sibling study to [truth-atonement.md](truth-atonement.md) and [what-was-the-atonement-for.md](what-was-the-atonement-for.md).*

---

## Binding Question

If light and truth are refined matter, how does the Atonement physically refine a person to hold a higher degree of glory? What are the mechanics of this transformation under the laws of D&C 93?

---

## I. The Physicality of Spirit and Light

Latter-day Saint theology rejects immaterialism, asserting instead a physical continuity across all existence. As Joseph Smith instructed, "There is no such thing as immaterial matter. All spirit is matter, but it is more fine or pure, and can only be discerned by purer eyes" ([D&C 131:7](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/131.md)). Spirit-matter is therefore subject to laws of substance, organization, and conductivity. This ontology means that spiritual growth is not a legal fiction or a change in database status, but a literal structural reorganization of the spirit's fine matter. While housed in the physical body, the spirit develops real, tangible attributes like love, integrity, and compassion. As Elder Russell M. Nelson observed, "Your spirit, by being housed in your body, is able to develop and express these attributes in ways that are vital to your eternal progression" ([Elder Nelson, October 1998](../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/1998/10/we-are-children-of-god.md)). The soul is a composite of spirit and element, structured to receive and organize eternal light.

This structural substance is defined scripturally as intelligence, or the light of truth, which is eternal and uncreated ([D&C 93:29, 36](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93.md)). Under the laws of D&C 93, this light is "independent in that sphere in which God has placed it, to act for itself" ([D&C 93:30](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93.md)). Because intelligence has agency, it can choose to receive or reject the light of truth. As a person keeps the commandments, their spirit-matter receives more truth and light, "until he is glorified in truth and knoweth all things" ([D&C 93:28](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93.md)). Conversely, disobedience allows the wicked one to take away light and truth ([D&C 93:39](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93.md)). This loss is a literal evacuation of the organizing substance from the spirit, degrading its structural integrity and reducing its capacity to contain glory.

## II. The Law of Glory and Capacity

The resurrected soul is animated by a physical quickening power. As D&C 88 teaches, the resurrected body receives that glory "by which your bodies are quickened" ([D&C 88:28](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)). But this quickening is governed by absolute boundaries of law. A spirit cannot be animated by celestial glory unless it has developed the capacity to abide a celestial law. As the Lord states, "For he who is not able to abide the law of a celestial kingdom cannot abide a celestial glory" ([D&C 88:22](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)). Law is not a set of arbitrary conditions to be met for entry, but the structural grid that preserves and perfects the spirit. "That which is governed by law is also preserved by law and perfected and sanctified by the same" ([D&C 88:34](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)).

Consequently, the Final Judgment is not an administrative tally of good and bad deeds. It is a recognition of the physical and spiritual condition the soul has achieved. As Elder Dallin H. Oaks argued, the judgment is not a listing of deposits made in a heavenly account, but "an acknowledgment of the final effect of our acts and thoughts—what we have become" ([Elder Oaks, October 2000](../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2000/10/the-challenge-to-become.md)). If the soul's fine matter has broken the law and willed to abide in sin, it "cannot be sanctified by law, neither by mercy, justice, nor judgment" ([D&C 88:35](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)). In this state of lawlessness, the spirit-matter remains filthy, unable to conduct the voltage of celestial glory without being consumed. Exaltation requires that the spirit's fine matter be purified and reorganized to align with the law of the celestial kingdom.

## III. The Comprehension Principle (Experiential Descent)

To reorganize the matter of a fallen spirit, the Redeemer had to establish a physical conducting circuit. This circuit was forged through Christ's descent below all things, in that He "comprehended all things, that he might be in all and through all things, the light of truth" ([D&C 88:6](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)). This comprehension is not merely cognitive data; it is an experiential state carved into the flesh. Alma teaches that Christ went forth suffering pains, afflictions, and temptations of every kind, "that his bowels may be filled with mercy, according to the flesh, that he may know according to the flesh how to succor his people" ([Alma 7:11-12](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/7.md)). Although the Spirit inherently "knoweth all things," the Son had to suffer "according to the flesh" to acquire this visceral, physical comprehension ([Alma 7:13](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/7.md)).

This experiential descent is the mechanism by which Christ connects with the elements in their most chaotic states. When the elements combine to hedge up the way and the jaws of hell gape wide, they act upon the soul as a furnace of experience ([D&C 122:7](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/122.md)). Because "the Son of Man hath descended below them all," His comprehension encompasses the absolute boundaries of the matter spectrum ([D&C 122:8](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/122.md)). He has traversed the depths of spiritual and physical resistance. This descent makes His refining capacity infinite, establishing Him as the perfect conductor of purifying power. Through His flesh-based comprehension, He can reach into the fractured, dross-heavy structure of a human spirit and initiate a physical reorganization.

## IV. The Mechanics of Atoning Refinement

The Atonement operates physically as a refiner's fire, separating the pure spirit-matter from the corrupting dross of sin. As prophesied, the Lord "shall sit as a refiner and purifier of silver; and he shall purify... and purge them as gold and silver" ([3 Nephi 24:3](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/24.md)). This purging is not a legal decree of forgiveness, but an active, physical separation of foreign elements from the spirit. Elder David A. Bednar clarified this dual operation, explaining that the Atonement provides both a "cleansing and redeeming power that helps us to overcome sin" (clean hands) and a "sanctifying and strengthening power that helps us to become better" (a pure heart) ([Elder Bednar, October 2007](../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2007/10/clean-hands-and-a-pure-heart.md)). Remission of sins cleanses the spirit of its past stains, but sanctification reorganizes the nature of the spirit so that it loses all disposition to do evil.

This structural sanctification is administered by the Holy Ghost, who acts as the physical agent of the refiner's fire. The Holy Ghost is a personage of spirit, which allows Him to dwell within the human tabernacle and interact directly with our fine spirit-matter ([D&C 130:22](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/130.md)). Through the baptism of fire, the Holy Ghost burns out the spiritual dross, consisting of the desires and inclinations of the natural man, and restructures the spirit's matter to cleave unto light and wisdom ([D&C 88:40](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)). As President Henry B. Eyring taught, "greater holiness is made possible through the Atonement of Jesus Christ cleansing and perfecting us" ([Elder Eyring, October 2019](../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2019/10/51eyring.md)). The Atonement is therefore the mechanism that allows the Holy Ghost to perform this physical purification safely, reorganizing the spirit's capacity to contain and abide the law of celestial glory.

## V. Becoming

If refinement is a physical process governed by eternal law, then daily repentance and obedience represent the structural training required to expand the spirit's capacity. We do not prepare to meet God through sporadic bursts of intense religious activity; rather, we make steady, incremental choices that align our fine matter with the celestial law. As Elder John C. Pingree Jr. taught, "Once the Holy Ghost confirms a specific truth to us, our understanding deepens as we put that principle into practice. Over time, as we consistently live the principle, we gain a sure knowledge of that truth" ([Elder Pingree, October 2023](../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2023/10/52pingree.md)). This experiential practice is what consolidates light and truth in our substance.

Therefore, our daily focus must shift from outward performance to the purification of our desires. When we choose to repent, we choose to let the Savior transform our natures, purging both the taint and the tyranny of sin from our souls. This change is not a reward we earn, but a state of being we allow the refiner's fire to work within us. Each choice to follow Christ is a vote for light, structuring our spirits to receive grace for grace until we are glorified in truth.
