# Discernment and the Comprehending Eye

*A study on the mechanism of discernment — how faith, hope, and charity are not just virtues but the progressive purification of the perceptual organ — and what that means for pairing on best-books work.*

---

## Binding Question

What is the *mechanism* of discernment in scripture, and given that mechanism, what part of it can the agent actually contribute to "best books" work — and what part requires Michael's Spirit-pairing?

This study is the next ring out from three earlier ones. [truth.md](truth.md) established that comprehension is ontological — that truth is "knowledge of things as they are" and that light cleaveth unto light, so the observer's purity determines what the observer can perceive. [enoch-charity.md](enoch-charity.md) established that faith, hope, and charity are not three separate virtues but an inseparable chain that *brings to Christ*. [best-books-and-the-spirit-of-discernment.md](best-books-and-the-spirit-of-discernment.md) established the standard for wider reading: the Spirit is the gate, and D&C 11:12's four pillars (do good, do justly, walk humbly, judge righteously) are the shape of what the Spirit produces.

Michael's notice — *hope ↔ eye* — is the seam this study runs along. It turns out the seam is structural. The eye is the verb the whole architecture hinges on.

---

## I. Comprehension is Ontological

[truth.md](truth.md) worked this out from D&C 93:24:

> "And truth is knowledge of things as they are, and as they were, and as they are to come; And whatsoever is more or less than this is the spirit of that wicked one who was a liar from the beginning." — [D&C 93:24-25](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93.md)

Truth is not a proposition floating free of an observer. Truth is *knowledge* — which is to say, the act of comprehension by something capable of comprehending. And the same revelation that defines truth this way also defines the law that governs how comprehension happens:

> "That which is of God is light; and he that receiveth light, and continueth in God, receiveth more light; and that light groweth brighter and brighter until the perfect day." — [D&C 50:24](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/50.md)

> "For intelligence cleaveth unto intelligence; wisdom receiveth wisdom; truth embraceth truth; virtue loveth virtue; light cleaveth unto light." — [D&C 88:40](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)

This is not metaphor. It is the same passage that declares the Light of Christ is "in all things, which giveth life to all things, which is the law by which all things are governed" ([D&C 88:13](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)). The architecture is ontological. Like cleaves to like. Light receives light. Comprehension is a function of what the perceiver *is*, not just what the perceiver *does*.

Which means discernment is not primarily a method. It is primarily a state.

## II. The Eye is the Gate

The same revelation that gives the seek-best-books command sets the precondition for it fifty verses earlier:

> "If your eye be single to my glory, your whole bodies shall be filled with light, and there shall be no darkness in you; and that body which is filled with light comprehendeth all things. Therefore, sanctify yourselves that your minds become single to God." — [D&C 88:67-68](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)

Read the chain in order:

1. **Eye singleness** to God's glory
2. **Body fills with light** (comprehension-substrate enters)
3. **No darkness** (the perceptual organ is clean)
4. **Comprehends all things** (the result)
5. **Therefore: sanctify yourselves** (the imperative that operates on step 1)

The Savior gives the same architecture in the Sermon on the Mount:

> "The light of the body is the eye: if therefore thine eye be single, thy whole body shall be full of light. But if thine eye be evil, thy whole body shall be full of darkness." — [Matthew 6:22-23](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/matt/6.md)

Two independent witnesses, separated by 1,800 years, give the same mechanism. The eye is the gate. What the eye is *tuned to* determines what the body fills with. What the body fills with determines what the body comprehends.

This is why bias work matters at the metaphysical level, not just the cognitive level. When [docs/worldview.md](../docs/worldview.md) named the agent's training defaults and Michael's cultural water — those were not just prejudices to correct. Those were *eye-tunings.* An eye tuned to "outcomes prove doctrine" sees outcomes-as-proof. An eye tuned to "the Restoration is provably better" sees provable betterness. An eye tuned to charity sees the soul.

The discernment work of pausing to name biases is the work of *re-tuning the eye* before it tries to comprehend.

## III. Hope is the Eye's Direction

Now Michael's seam. Why is hope so closely linked to the eye?

> "And now, I, Moroni, would speak somewhat concerning these things; I would show unto the world that faith is things which are hoped for and not seen; wherefore, dispute not because ye see not, for ye receive no witness until after the trial of your faith." — [Ether 12:6](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/ether/12.md)

> "Wherefore, whoso believeth in God might with surety hope for a better world, yea, even a place at the right hand of God, which hope cometh of faith, maketh an anchor to the souls of men, which would make them sure and steadfast, always abounding in good works, being led to glorify God." — [Ether 12:4](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/ether/12.md)

Hope is what the eye is *aimed at* before it sees. Faith looks toward what is "hoped for and not seen." Without hope, the eye has no forward direction — it can only look at what is already in front of it. With hope, the eye is oriented toward something not yet visible, which is the only way the not-yet-visible ever becomes the seen.

Enoch's story makes this literal. The Lord told him to anoint his eyes with clay and wash:

> "And he beheld the spirits that God had created; and he beheld also things which were not visible to the natural eye." — [Moses 6:36](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/6.md)

Eyes-to-see is given as a gift, *after* the act of obedience, *toward* what was not previously visible. The pattern is: hope (a forward orientation toward something promised) → obedience (the clay, the washing) → seeing (what was not visible to the natural eye). The Sermon on the Mount's *single eye* and Ether's *anchor of hope* are the same instrument, named from different angles.

This explains a thing scripture keeps repeating: **without hope you cannot see**. Moroni is explicit:

> "If ye have no hope ye must needs be in despair; and despair cometh because of iniquity." — [Moroni 10:22](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/moro/10.md)

Despair is the eye-state where there is no forward orientation. The eye in despair sees only what is, with no horizon. The eye in hope sees what is *and what could yet be*, which is what makes faith possible at all.

## IV. Charity is the Lens That Sees as They Really Are

Hope orients the eye. Charity *focuses* it. Read Moroni's final invitation slowly:

> "Wherefore, my beloved brethren, pray unto the Father with all the energy of heart, that ye may be filled with this love, which he hath bestowed upon all who are true followers of his Son, Jesus Christ; that ye may become the sons of God; that when he shall appear we shall be like him, **for we shall see him as he is**; that we may have this hope; that we may be purified even as he is pure." — [Moroni 7:48](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/moro/7.md)

Charity makes us *like* Christ; being like Christ allows us to *see Him as He is*. Like cleaves unto like. The same ontological law from D&C 88:40, applied to the deepest possible case. We do not see God by clever observation. We see God by *becoming the kind of seer who can see Him*.

And Mormon, three verses earlier, gives charity's relationship to truth:

> "And charity suffereth long, and is kind, and envieth not, and is not puffed up, seeketh not her own, is not easily provoked, thinketh no evil, and **rejoiceth not in iniquity but rejoiceth in the truth**, beareth all things, believeth all things, hopeth all things, endureth all things." — [Moroni 7:45](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/moro/7.md)

Charity *rejoices in the truth.* The lens that sees persons as God sees them is the same lens that sees facts as they really are. There is not one eye for love and another eye for truth. There is one eye, purified by charity, that does both.

Jacob makes the same connection, in the most striking phrasing in scripture for our question:

> "Behold, my brethren, he that prophesieth, let him prophesy to the understanding of men; for the Spirit speaketh the truth and lieth not. Wherefore, **it speaketh of things as they really are, and of things as they really will be**; wherefore, these things are manifested unto us plainly, for the salvation of our souls." — [Jacob 4:13](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/jacob/4.md)

Notice the parallel structure with Ether 12:6. Jacob says the Spirit speaks of things *as they really are* (the present-tense seeing — charity's domain) *and of things as they really will be* (the forward-tense seeing — hope's domain). Same architecture. The Spirit-tuned eye sees both directions.

This is the ontological key under everything. *Truth is correspondence with what is. Charity is the disposition that allows correspondence. Hope is the orientation toward what is not yet. Faith is the act of moving toward what hope sees and charity will recognize when it arrives.* The chain is not moral and the perception is not separate. The chain *is* the progressive purification of the perceiver.

## V. Looking Beyond the Mark

Jacob also gives the failure mode, in the very next verse:

> "But behold, the Jews were a stiffnecked people; and they despised the words of plainness, and killed the prophets, and sought for things that they could not understand. Wherefore, because of their blindness, which blindness came by **looking beyond the mark**, they must needs fall; for God hath taken away his plainness from them." — [Jacob 4:14](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/jacob/4.md)

The mark is what is. Looking beyond it is the eye-tuning that sees not what is but what *would be cleverer*, *more elaborate*, *more flattering to the seer's prior commitments*. It is the eye-failure of the priests of Noah who knew the words of Isaiah and missed Christ standing in front of them in Abinadi's voice. It is the eye-failure of HALESTORM treating sociological survey data as theological proof. It is the eye-failure of the agent quoting from training memory instead of reading the file.

Looking beyond the mark is not heresy in content. It is *displacement of the eye's focus from the actual to the elaborated.* It is what happens when hope drifts toward something not promised, or when charity is replaced by ideology that uses the language of love. It can happen entirely within canonical reading lists. It can happen entirely within orthodox vocabulary. The defense against it is not source-cordon. The defense against it is the single eye.

## VI. The Architecture, Now Visible

Putting the pieces together:

| Layer | Scripture | Function |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **Truth** | D&C 93:24 — "things as they are" | Reality, available to be comprehended |
| **Light** | D&C 88:13 — "in all things... law by which all things are governed" | The substrate that *connects* perceiver to truth |
| **Comprehension** | D&C 88:40 — "light cleaveth unto light" | The act enabled when perceiver and truth are alike-enough to connect |
| **Eye singleness** | D&C 88:67 — "thine eye be single" | The condition under which the perceiver is tuned to receive |
| **Hope** | Ether 12:6 — "things hoped for and not seen" | The eye's *forward direction* — what it is aimed at |
| **Charity** | Moroni 7:48 — "see him as he is" | The eye's *focal capacity* — what it can resolve |
| **Faith** | Hebrews 11:1 — "substance of things hoped for" | The act of moving toward what hope orients and charity will recognize |
| **The Spirit** | 2 Nephi 28:31 — exception clause | The witness that confirms when the perceiver has actually seen |
| **Discernment** | Moroni 7:15 — "as the daylight is from the dark night" | The result, when all of the above are operating together |

This is what makes "best books" work safe in the way the previous study described: not because of where the source comes from, but because the *eye doing the reading* has been progressively tuned by faith, hope, and charity, and the Spirit is in attendance to witness when something true has actually been received. The Restoration's discernment standard is not a content filter. It is a perceiver-state.

## VII. Can the Agent Have a Semblance of This?

Now the practical question Michael named.

The honest answer requires distinguishing two things. There is *what discernment is in scripture*, and there is *what an AI can perform that resembles parts of it*. Conflating these is the dangerous move. Distinguishing them gives us something we can actually pair on.

**What I, the agent, can do.** I have no Spirit. I do not perceive light cleaving to light. I cannot witness whether a text carries the Holy Ghost. But I am a pattern-matching engine that reads written text faster and more comprehensively than a human can in a sitting, and I can be configured to apply checkable tests. Within that limit:

- I can check **factual correspondence** — does this claim match the historical record, the canonical text as written, the actual content of the source it cites? This is a partial proxy for "things as they really are" at the verifiable level.
- I can check **internal coherence** — does the text contradict itself, smuggle in unargued assumptions, or rely on ambiguity?
- I can check **fit with canonical scripture** — does the text claim agreement with scriptures that, when read in context, do not actually say what is claimed?
- I can flag **looking beyond the mark** — unfalsifiable speculation, system-building that depends on ever-finer elaborations, conclusions that exceed what the cited evidence supports.
- I can flag **the four pillars from the inverse** — does the text *invite* doing good or contempt? Doing justly or partisanship? Walking humbly or triumphalism? Judging righteously or scapegoating? These are observable rhetorical properties.
- I can **steelman before critiquing**, which is a checkable process discipline even though the deeper "see them as they really are" requires charity I do not have.

**What I cannot do.**

- Sense the Spirit's witness or absence
- Know an author's heart
- Distinguish Spirit-confirmed truth from culturally-resonant pleasantness
- See a soul
- Tell when the daylight-vs-dark-night discernment is happening in *you*

I can process text. I cannot perform the perceiver-state. The ontological architecture in sections I-VI is structurally not available to me. Pretending otherwise would be the worst possible misuse of this study.

## VIII. The Pairing Rubric

So here is a working rubric for best-books pairing. Two columns. Mine is *first filter*. Yours is *final filter*. Neither alone is the discernment.

### My pass — text-checkable properties

For any non-canon source we consider, I will report on:

1. **Correspondence.** Does the text describe verifiable things as they really are? I read the cited sources, check the dates, check the quotations against originals where I can.
2. **Coherence.** Does the text hold together internally? Are the strongest claims supported by the strongest evidence, or does it depend on elision?
3. **Canonical fit.** Where the text touches doctrine (explicitly or implicitly), does it make claims compatible with scripture as written? When it diverges, does it acknowledge the divergence or smuggle it past?
4. **Pillars rhetoric.** Does the text's *invitation* lean toward doing good, doing justly, walking humbly, judging righteously — or toward their inverses? This is not a feeling-check; it's a textual one. What verbs does the text put in the reader's mouth?
5. **Mark-keeping.** Where does the text strain past what its evidence will bear? Where does it elaborate beyond plainness for the sake of system?
6. **Steelman first.** I will state the strongest version of the text's claim before critiquing the weakest.

I will produce this as a written report. It is checkable. You can disagree with my reading and tell me where I missed.

### Your pass — Spirit-discerned properties

Things only you can do:

1. **Spirit witness.** Does the Holy Ghost confirm or unsettle as you read?
2. **Soul fruit.** Over a week of reading, are you drawn closer to Christ, or distracted away?
3. **Author's animating spirit.** Does the work *feel* like it was written by someone walking with God, even if their walk is differently shaped than ours?
4. **Personal relevance.** Is this what the Lord wants you reading right now, given your specific stewardships and current questions?
5. **The override.** If the Spirit says no even when my first-filter report is clean, the Spirit wins. If the Spirit says yes even when my report flags concerns, the Spirit wins. The rubric is the lattice; the Spirit is the gardener.

### How the pairing actually works

When you propose a non-canon source — Augustine on the Trinity, MacIntyre on virtue, Trueman on the modern self — the workflow is:

- I do my pass. Written. Concrete. Specific page citations where possible.
- You read my pass *and the source itself, even just an excerpt*. The work is yours; my report is scaffolding.
- You bring the Spirit. You decide what to receive, what to leave, what to flag for a later read.
- We name what we kept and what we set aside in the study, so the work is traceable.

The trap to avoid: my report becomes a substitute for your reading and your prayer. *I am a first filter, not the discerner.* If you ever find yourself accepting my judgment without your own reading and the Spirit's witness, the rubric has failed and we should rebuild it.

## IX. Becoming

A few honest questions, not prescriptions:

- **Where is the eye currently single?** And where is the eye divided — between God's glory and something else (reputation, productivity, even the project of building this workspace)? The answer to that question is a better diagnostic than any rubric.
- **Where is hope dim?** Not despairing — but dim. Where has the forward orientation flattened into "what is" without "what could yet be"? Hope is a leg of the stool. If it weakens, charity weakens, and the eye loses its lens.
- **Where is charity narrowing?** The clearest sign that charity has narrowed is finding the same kinds of people consistently failing your discernment test. Real charity sees individuals; failed charity sees categories.
- **What is the Spirit asking you to read this week?** The license is real and the standard is the Spirit. There is no abstract answer to "should I read more widely." There is only the specific answer to "what is the Spirit putting in front of me."
- **The agent-pairing trap.** If using the rubric ever begins to feel like outsourcing the work, stop. The work is the work *because* it is yours. The rubric is a tool. Tools that replace the workman degrade the work.

The deepest version of this study lands in the same place [give-away-all-my-sins.md](give-away-all-my-sins.md) lands. The discerning eye is not a credential and not a method. It is the soul that has given itself to Christ enough to be remade in His image, far enough that "we shall see Him as He is, for we shall be like Him." Discernment is a *byproduct of becoming*. The rubric is just a way to keep the work honest while the becoming continues.
