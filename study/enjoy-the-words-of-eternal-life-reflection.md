# Enjoy the Words of Eternal Life — Reflection

**Extending:** [enjoy-the-words-of-eternal-life.md](enjoy-the-words-of-eternal-life.md)
**Date:** February 17, 2026

---

## The Question

The [original study](enjoy-the-words-of-eternal-life.md) traced what it means to "enjoy the words of eternal life" through Moses 6:59, its footnotes, and Webster 1828. That study found three layers — the scriptures, the living words of Christ, and the ongoing communication of the Holy Ghost. It also found that "enjoy" means not just pleasure but *possession*: to have, hold, and use with satisfaction.

This reflection asks the next question: **What happens when a people — not just a person — begins to enjoy the words of eternal life? And what does that have to do with Zion?**

---

## The Command Behind the Promise

The original study centered on Moses 6:59 — the *promise*: "that ye might be sanctified from all sin, and enjoy the words of eternal life in this world." But there's a companion verse that frames the same concept as a *command*:

> And I now give unto you a commandment to beware concerning yourselves, to **give diligent heed to the words of eternal life**.
> — [D&C 84:43](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/84.md)

Moses 6:59 says you *may* enjoy them. D&C 84:43 says you *must* attend to them. The promise and the command are two sides of the same coin — the gift is real, but it requires diligence.

And then D&C 84 does something extraordinary. It defines what those words actually *are*:

> For you shall live by every word that proceedeth forth from the mouth of God. For the **word of the Lord is truth**, and whatsoever is **truth is light**, and whatsoever is **light is Spirit**, even the Spirit of Jesus Christ.
> — [D&C 84:44–45](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/84.md)

This is a chain of identity, not metaphor:
- **Word** = truth
- **Truth** = light
- **Light** = Spirit = the Spirit of Jesus Christ

When you give diligent heed to the words of eternal life, you are not merely reading. You are receiving truth, which is light, which is the Spirit of Christ itself entering you. The words are a delivery mechanism for the Spirit. That's what Jesus meant when He said "the words that I speak unto you, they are spirit, and they are life" ([John 6:63](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/john/6.md)).

And that Spirit "giveth light to every man that cometh into the world; and the Spirit enlighteneth every man through the world, that hearkeneth to the voice of the Spirit. And every one that hearkeneth to the voice of the Spirit cometh unto God, even the Father" ([D&C 84:46–47](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/84.md)).

There's a gravitational pull in the words of eternal life. They draw you toward God. Not just toward knowledge *about* God — toward God Himself.

---

## The Warning: What Happens When You Don't

D&C 84 doesn't stop at the promise. It delivers one of the most sobering warnings in scripture:

> And your minds in times past have been darkened because of unbelief, and because you have **treated lightly** the things you have received—
>
> Which vanity and unbelief have brought the **whole church under condemnation**.
>
> And this condemnation resteth upon the **children of Zion, even all**.
>
> And they shall remain under this condemnation **until they repent and remember the new covenant**, even the Book of Mormon and the former commandments which I have given them, **not only to say, but to do** according to that which I have written.
> — [D&C 84:54–57](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/84.md)

Note who is under condemnation: the *children of Zion*. Not the world. Not the unbelievers. The covenant people. Those who have received the words of eternal life and treated them lightly.

"Treated lightly" is the opposite of "enjoy." Webster's "enjoy" means to have, possess, use with satisfaction. To treat lightly means to have received something of infinite worth and set it aside casually. The condemnation isn't for rejecting the words — it's for *having them and not valuing them*.

The remedy is specific: "remember the new covenant, even the Book of Mormon... not only to say, but to **do**." Enjoying the words of eternal life isn't passive. It demands action. Saying isn't enough. You have to *live* them.

---

## "We Have the Words of Eternal Life"

Brigham Young takes this phrase and drives it straight into the question of Zion:

> We have the words of eternal life, we have the privilege of obtaining glory, immortality, and eternal lives, **now will you obtain these blessings?** Will you spend your lives to obtain a seat in the kingdom of God, or will you lie down and sleep, and go down to hell?
> — [Brigham Young, Chapter 43](../gospel-library/eng/manual/teachings-brigham-young/chapter-43.md)

And then, in the context of preparing for the Second Coming:

> Whether the world is going to be burned up within a year, or within a thousand years, does not matter a groat to you and me. We have the words of eternal life, we have the privilege of obtaining glory, immortality, and eternal lives, **now will you obtain these blessings?**
>
> We have the Kingdom of God to build up, **Zion to redeem**; we have to **sanctify ourselves** so that we may be prepared to be caught up with the Church of the Firstborn, and if we improve every day and hour, then if we die we shall be found justified.
> — [Brigham Young, Chapter 45](../gospel-library/eng/manual/teachings-brigham-young/chapter-45.md)

The connection Brigham makes is direct: **having the words of eternal life** creates the **obligation to build Zion**. You can't possess the words and remain idle. The words demand that you become something — that you sanctify yourself, build the kingdom, redeem Zion.

And his timeline is striking: "now will you obtain these blessings?" Not someday. Not after the Second Coming. *Now.* The words of eternal life are present-tense, and so is the work of Zion.

---

## The Millennial Now

Brigham Young said something else in that same chapter that stops you in your tracks:

> If the people will not serve the Devil another moment whilst they live, if this congregation is possessed of that spirit and resolution, **here in this house is the Millennium**. Let the inhabitants of this city be possessed of that spirit, let the people of the territory be possessed of that spirit, and **here is the Millennium**. Let the whole people be possessed of that spirit and here is the Millennium, and so will it spread over all the world.
> — [Brigham Young, Chapter 45](../gospel-library/eng/manual/teachings-brigham-young/chapter-45.md)

The Millennium is not just a future event. It is a *condition* — a condition that begins the moment a people resolves to stop serving the adversary and starts living by the Spirit. It starts in a single house, spreads to a city, then a territory, then the world. It's fractal. It begins wherever the words of eternal life are actually enjoyed — not just heard, but lived.

Elder Christofferson echoed this in 2019:

> Zion is the pure in heart, a people of one heart and one mind, dwelling in righteousness with no poor among them. The Prophet Joseph Smith stated, "We ought to have the building up of Zion as our greatest object." We build up Zion in our homes, wards, branches, and stakes through unity, godliness, and charity.
> — [Elder D. Todd Christofferson, "Preparing for the Lord's Return," April 2019](../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2019/04/44christofferson.md)

The building of Zion begins where you are. Not in Independence, Missouri. Not after some great sign. In your home, your ward, your stake. In the conversations you have with your children. In the way you study scripture. In whether you merely *say* the covenant or *do* it.

## The Pattern of Zion

Every description of Zion in scripture centers on a people who have internalized the words of eternal life — who enjoy them in the full Webster 1828 sense of possess, hold, and use.

### Enoch's Zion

> And the Lord called his people **Zion**, because they were of **one heart and one mind**, and dwelt in righteousness; and there was **no poor among them**.
> — [Moses 7:18](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/7.md)

What made them one? They walked with God (v. 69). The Lord "dwelt with his people, and they dwelt in righteousness" (v. 16). They had internalized so deeply that God Himself lived among them. And the result: "it came to pass that Zion was not, for God received it up into his own bosom" (v. 69).

But the story doesn't end there. The Lord promises Enoch that in the last days:

> Righteousness will I send down out of heaven; and truth will I send forth out of the earth, to bear testimony of mine Only Begotten... and righteousness and truth will I cause to sweep the earth as with a flood, to **gather out mine elect** from the four quarters of the earth, unto a place which I shall prepare, an Holy City, that my people may gird up their loins, and be looking forth for the time of my coming; for there shall be my tabernacle, and it shall be called **Zion, a New Jerusalem**.
> — [Moses 7:62](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/7.md)

And then the reunion:

> Then shalt thou and all thy city meet them there, and we will receive them into our bosom, and they shall see us; and **we will fall upon their necks, and they shall fall upon our necks, and we will kiss each other**.
> — [Moses 7:63](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/7.md)

That image — Enoch's ancient Zion falling on the necks of the latter-day Zion, weeping with joy. That's where this is going. The words of eternal life, enjoyed and lived, produce a people worthy of that embrace.

### The Nephite Zion

After Christ's personal ministry among the Nephites, they lived this reality for nearly 200 years:

> And there was no contention in the land, because of the **love of God which did dwell in the hearts of the people**.
>
> And there were no envyings, nor strifes, nor tumults, nor whoredoms, nor lyings, nor murders, nor any manner of lasciviousness; and surely there could not be a **happier people** among all the people who had been created by the hand of God.
>
> There were no robbers, nor murderers, neither were there Lamanites, nor any manner of -ites; but they were in **one**, the children of Christ, and heirs to the kingdom of God.
> — [4 Nephi 1:15–17](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/4-ne/1.md)

What sustained this? They continued "in fasting and prayer, and in meeting together oft both to pray and **to hear the word of the Lord**" (v. 12). They were a people who never stopped enjoying the words of eternal life. They gathered to hear them, prayed over them, fasted with them. And the result was the happiest people who had ever existed.

## Enjoy Now, Eternal Life Later

Joy D. Jones made Moses 6:59's two-part promise the hinge of her April 2021 talk on teaching children:

> May our simple yet essential conversations with our children help them to "**enjoy the words of eternal life**" now so that they may enjoy "**eternal life in the world to come**, even immortal glory."
> — [Joy D. Jones, "Essential Conversations," April 2021](../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2021/04/13jones.md)

Her whole talk is built on the idea that conversion doesn't happen accidentally. "Accidental conversion is not a principle of the gospel of Jesus Christ." Children need to be taught *intentionally* — through essential conversations, through role-playing, through the daily work of living gospel truths together.

Her metaphor of the drill instructor is striking: children should "sweat in the safe learning environment of the home" rather than "bleed on the battlefields of life." And the footnote she cites? [Moses 6:58](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/6.md) — "Teach these things freely unto your children." The same verse that immediately precedes our verse 59. God commands us to teach our children the Fall, the Atonement, and being born again. Then He promises that those who are born again will enjoy the words of eternal life.

The teaching *is* the path to the enjoying. You can't enjoy what you haven't received. And you can't build Zion with people who haven't been taught.

## Steps to Take Now

If the words of eternal life are meant to be enjoyed *in this world* (Moses 6:59), and if enjoying them is what produces Zion, then the personal question is: **How do I move from treating them lightly to truly enjoying them?**

### 1. Remember the Covenant — Not Just Say, But Do
D&C 84:57 specifies: "not only to say, but to do." Reading the Book of Mormon is not enough. The question is whether you are *living* what you read. Are the words changing how you treat your spouse, your children, your neighbors? Are they making you more patient, more honest, more generous?

### 2. Give Diligent Heed
D&C 84:43's word is "diligent." The Hebrew concept behind diligence is *sharatsah* — persistence, carefulness, unwavering attention. This isn't casual morning reading. This is Nephi's model: delight, ponder, write. It's the difference between glancing at scripture and *dwelling* in it.

### 3. Teach Freely
Moses 6:58 says "teach these things freely unto your children." Joy D. Jones reminds us: conversion doesn't happen accidentally. Have the essential conversations. Don't wait for your children to ask. Create the environment where the words of eternal life are a normal, joyful part of daily life.

### 4. Sanctify Yourself
Brigham Young's charge is direct: "We have to sanctify ourselves so that we may be prepared to be caught up with the Church of the Firstborn." Sanctification is the process by which the words of eternal life move from your page to your character. It's what D&C 84:33 describes: magnifying your calling, being "sanctified by the Spirit unto the renewing of their bodies."

### 5. Build Zion Where You Are
Christofferson: "We build up Zion in our homes, wards, branches, and stakes through unity, godliness, and charity." Brigham Young: "Here in this house is the Millennium." You don't wait for Zion to happen to you. You start making it in your family, in your ward, in your neighborhood. One heart, one mind, no poor among you.

### 6. Follow the Word → Truth → Light → Spirit Chain
D&C 84:44–47 maps the path: the word leads to truth, truth leads to light, light is the Spirit, the Spirit leads you to the Father. Every time you give diligent heed to a word of eternal life, you are stepping further along that chain. The culmination isn't knowledge — it's arriving in the presence of the Father.

---

## The Thread

Moses 6:59 promises that the sanctified will enjoy the words of eternal life *in this world*.

D&C 84:43 commands us to give diligent heed to those words, and then reveals that word = truth = light = Spirit.

D&C 84:54–57 warns that treating those words lightly brings condemnation on the children of Zion — and the remedy is to *remember and do*.

Brigham Young says: "We have the words of eternal life... Zion to redeem; we have to sanctify ourselves." And: "Here is the Millennium."

Enoch's people were of one heart and one mind because the Lord dwelt with them — they lived in the words of eternal life until the words lived in them.

The Nephites after Christ had no contention "because of the love of God which did dwell in the hearts of the people" — and they "continued... in meeting together oft to hear the word of the Lord."

Joy D. Jones brings it to the most intimate scale: teach your children so they enjoy the words now, and eternal life later.

The pattern is consistent across every dispensation: **a people who truly enjoy the words of eternal life — who have them, possess them, use them, delight in them — become Zion.** And Zion is not a place you go. It's a condition you create. It starts in a single heart, spreads to a family, fills a congregation, and eventually covers the earth.

Whether the world is going to be burned up within a year or within a thousand years doesn't matter a groat to you and me. What matters is whether we're enjoying the words of eternal life *right now* — and letting them make us into the kind of people who can fall on Enoch's neck and weep with recognition.
