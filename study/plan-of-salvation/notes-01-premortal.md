# Phase 1: The Bookends — Pre-Mortal Existence & The Council

> *"One's life, therefore, is brevity compared to eternity—like being dropped off by a parent for a day at school. But what a day!"*
> — [Elder Neal A. Maxwell, "Premortality, a Glorious Reality," October 1985](../../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/1985/10/premortality-a-glorious-reality.md)

---

## Driving Questions

1. What do we *actually know* about pre-mortal life from scripture — stripped of cultural assumptions?
2. What was the nature of the council? What was decided, and what was the real conflict?
3. How does Abraham 3's teaching on intelligences connect to our existing intelligence/truth studies?
4. What does "noble and great ones" mean in context — and what was foreordination?
5. What does "prove" mean — and does it point toward school, test, or something else?

---

## The Scriptures Speak

### The Eternity of Intelligence

The plan of salvation doesn't begin with a council. It begins with what already *was*.

> "If there be two spirits, and one shall be more intelligent than the other, yet these two spirits, notwithstanding one is more intelligent than the other, have no beginning; they existed before, they shall have no end, they shall exist after, for they are gnolaum, or eternal."
> — [Abraham 3:18](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/abr/3.md)

> "Man was also in the beginning with God. Intelligence, or the light of truth, was not created or made, neither indeed can be."
> — [D&C 93:29](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93.md)

This is the starting point that changes everything. We didn't spring into existence *ex nihilo*. Intelligence — whatever it fundamentally is — is co-eternal with God. Joseph Smith via Maxwell: *"The 'ex nihilo' view lessens man in my estimation."* The scriptures push back hard against nothingness-origins.

D&C 93 adds that agency itself is woven into this uncreated existence:

> "All truth is independent in that sphere in which God has placed it, to act for itself, as all intelligence also; otherwise there is no existence."
> — [D&C 93:30](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93.md)

Agency isn't a gift that was *granted* at some point — it's inherent in intelligence. Without it, there literally "is no existence." This connects directly to our [intelligence study](../intelligence.md), which traced D&C 93's spectrum: intelligence → truth → light → spirit → element, and our [truth study](../truth.md), which framed truth as "knowledge of things as they are, and as they were, and as they are to come" ([D&C 93:24](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93.md)).

**Webster 1828 — "intelligence"**: *"The act or state of knowing; the exercise of the understanding"* — and also *"an intelligent being or spirit."* Both definitions appear in Abraham 3 and D&C 93 — the word bridges *capacity* and *being*.

### Organized Before the World Was

Abraham 3 then moves from intelligence-as-eternal-principle to intelligence-as-organized-beings:

> "Now the Lord had shown unto me, Abraham, the intelligences that were organized before the world was; and among all these there were many of the noble and great ones."
> — [Abraham 3:22](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/abr/3.md)

"Organized" — not "created from nothing." God worked with what already existed. And among them, variation: some were "noble and great." This raises the question — noble *how*? Great *at what*?

**Webster 1828 — "noble"**: *"Possessing eminence, elevation, dignity; above whatever is low, mean, degrading; magnanimous."* This is character, not rank. These intelligences had distinguished themselves.

D&C 138 gives more texture:

> "Even before they were born, they, with many others, received their first lessons in the world of spirits and were prepared to come forth in the due time of the Lord to labor in his vineyard for the salvation of the souls of men."
> — [D&C 138:56](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/138.md)

There it is — *first lessons in the world of spirits.* Pre-mortal life wasn't just existence; it was already a learning environment. That Maxwell quote about school isn't just metaphor — there was curriculum *before* we arrived here.

President Smith's vision also connects the noble and great ones directly back to Abraham:

> "Among the great and mighty ones who were assembled in this vast congregation of the righteous were... Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob... And I also observed... many other noble and great ones who were chosen in the beginning to be rulers in the Church of God."
> — [D&C 138:53, 55](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/138.md)

### The Council

Abraham 3:24-28 is the council narrative, and it's remarkably compact — the whole plan laid out in five verses:

> "And there stood one among them that was like unto God, and he said unto those who were with him: We will go down, for there is space there, and we will take of these materials, and we will make an earth whereon these may dwell; And we will prove them herewith, to see if they will do all things whatsoever the Lord their God shall command them; And they who keep their first estate shall be added upon; and they who keep not their first estate shall not have glory in the same kingdom with those who keep their first estate; and they who keep their second estate shall have glory added upon their heads for ever and ever."
> — [Abraham 3:24-26](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/abr/3.md)

Several things stand out:

1. **"We will go down"** — plural. This is collaborative creation. God doesn't monologue a world into being; He works with others. This fits the Abraham 4 creation account: "the Gods organized and formed the heavens and the earth."

2. **"We will prove them herewith"** — this is the purpose statement.

3. **Two estates, three outcomes**: Keep the first → "added upon" (receive a body). Keep not the first → lose that privilege. Keep the second → "glory added upon their heads for ever and ever." Simple. Elegant. High stakes.

**Webster 1828 — "prove"**: *"To try or ascertain by an experiment, or by a test or standard"* — but also: *"To gain experience of the good or evil of; to know by trial; to experience; to suffer."* Both meanings are embedded in Abraham 3:25. This isn't just a pass/fail exam. It's experiential — we learn by living through it. The "school" vs "test" question may be a false dichotomy. It's both: a trial *designed to educate*.

**Webster 1828 — "estate"**: *"Settled condition or form of existence; state; condition or circumstances of life."* The "first estate" and "second estate" aren't places — they're *conditions of existence*. Keeping the first estate means being faithful in the premortal condition; keeping the second means being faithful in the mortal condition.

### "Whom Shall I Send?"

The plan was the Father's. Abraham 3:27 records His question: "Whom shall I send?" He wasn't soliciting competing plans — He was calling for a volunteer to carry out *His* plan as Savior. Two responded. Moses 4 provides the detail:

> "Behold, here am I, send me, I will be thy son, and I will redeem all mankind, that one soul shall not be lost, and surely I will do it; wherefore give me thine honor."
> — [Moses 4:1](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/4.md)

> "But, behold, my Beloved Son, which was my Beloved and Chosen from the beginning, said unto me—Father, thy will be done, and the glory be thine forever."
> — [Moses 4:2](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/4.md)

The contrast is devastating:
- Satan: "I will redeem... give me thine honor" — he *takes* glory
- Christ: "Thy will be done, and the glory be thine forever" — He *returns* glory

And the core issue wasn't ambition alone:

> "Wherefore, because that Satan rebelled against me, and sought to destroy the agency of man, which I, the Lord God, had given him..."
> — [Moses 4:3](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/4.md)

The plan required agency. Satan's declaration to "redeem all mankind, that one soul shall not be lost" sounds compassionate on the surface — but the mechanism was the destruction of agency. As Elder Bednar put it plainly: "It is important to recognize that Lucifer did not present his plan. He rebelled against the Father's plan" ([Understanding Agency and God's Plan](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RZ_bKUuuvbI&t=210)). And as D&C 93:30 already established, without agency "there is no existence." Satan's rebellion wasn't salvation — it was annihilation dressed as rescue.

### The War and the Casualties

Revelation 12 fills in the battle:

> "And there was war in heaven: Michael and his angels fought against the dragon; and the dragon fought and his angels, and prevailed not; neither was their place found any more in heaven."
> — [Revelation 12:7-8](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rev/12.md)

> "And they overcame him by the blood of the Lamb, and by the word of their testimony."
> — [Revelation 12:11](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rev/12.md)

Note the mechanism of victory: testimony and the Atonement. Not force. The war was won by the same means we overcome in mortality — by bearing witness and relying on Christ.

D&C 29 gives the casualty count and its cause:

> "And a third part of the hosts of heaven turned he away from me because of their agency."
> — [D&C 29:36](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/29.md)

"Because of their agency." They weren't dragged away. They chose. The same agency that makes the plan work also makes rebellion possible. That's the cost of real freedom.

Jude adds a haunting detail:

> "And the angels which kept not their first estate, but left their own habitation, he hath reserved in everlasting chains under darkness unto the judgment of the great day."
> — [Jude 1:6](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/jude/1.md)

"Kept not their first estate" — the same language as Abraham 3:26. Jude confirms the New Testament writers knew this doctrine.

### Foreordination — Not Predestination

Alma 13 is the most detailed scriptural treatment of foreordination, and it carefully preserves agency:

> "And this is the manner after which they were ordained—being called and prepared from the foundation of the world according to the foreknowledge of God, on account of their exceeding faith and good works; in the first place being left to choose good or evil; therefore they having chosen good, and exercising exceedingly great faith, are called with a holy calling."
> — [Alma 13:3](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/13.md)

Key phrase: *"in the first place being left to choose."* Foreordination was based on demonstrated choices — faith and good works — not arbitrary selection. And critically:

> "Thus they have been called to this holy calling on account of their faith, while others would reject the Spirit of God on account of the hardness of their hearts and blindness of their minds, while, if it had not been for this they might have had as great privilege as their brethren. Or in fine, in the first place they were on the same standing with their brethren."
> — [Alma 13:4-5](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/13.md)

*"In the first place they were on the same standing."* Everyone started equal. The differentiation came from choices, not from some inherent superiority. This is radical — it protects against both Calvinist predestination and any notion of spiritual aristocracy.

**Webster 1828 — "foreordain"**: *"To ordain or appoint beforehand; to preordain."* Clean and simple. Ordained *beforehand*, not *regardless of choice*.

Maxwell captures this tension perfectly:

> "Premortality is not a relaxing doctrine. For each of us, there are choices to be made, incessant and difficult chores to be done, ironies and adversities to be experienced, time to be well spent, talents and gifts to be well employed. Just because we were chosen 'there and then,' surely does not mean we can be indifferent 'here and now.'"
> — [Elder Maxwell, "Premortality, a Glorious Reality"](../../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/1985/10/premortality-a-glorious-reality.md)

### The Joy of Choosing

Two witnesses attest to the emotional reality of premortal life:

> "Where wast thou when I laid the foundations of the earth? ... When the morning stars sang together, and all the sons of God shouted for joy?"
> — [Job 38:4, 7](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/job/38.md)

> "Before I formed thee in the belly I knew thee; and before thou camest forth out of the womb I sanctified thee, and I ordained thee a prophet unto the nations."
> — [Jeremiah 1:5](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/jer/1.md)

We weren't passive observers at the council. We *shouted for joy*. We knew what was coming — the difficulty, the forgetting, the proving — and we shouted for joy anyway. That's not the response of souls being forced into an examination. That's the response of people who understood the opportunity.

Paul adds the New Testament witness:

> "According as he hath chosen us in him before the foundation of the world, that we should be holy and without blame before him in love."
> — [Ephesians 1:4](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/eph/1.md)

And from Hebrews 12, a phrase that quietly confirms the Father-spirit relationship:

> "Shall we not much rather be in subjection unto the Father of spirits, and live?"
> — [Hebrews 12:9](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/heb/12.md)

"Father of spirits." God is literally the Father of our spirits. Not metaphorically. Not poetically. Literally.

## Prophetic Witness

### Elder Neal A. Maxwell — "Premortality, a Glorious Reality" (October 1985)

This is the landmark talk on premortality. Maxwell traced how the doctrine was *restored* — not invented — through Joseph Smith:

- **Alma 13:3-5** (1829-30) — foreordination based on faith and good works, equal standing
- **Book of Moses** (1830) — Satan's rebellion, agency destroyed
- **D&C 93** (1833) — intelligence uncreated, co-eternal with God
- **Book of Abraham** (1842) — council, intelligences organized, noble and great ones
- **King Follett sermon** (1844) — "If men do not comprehend the character of God, they do not comprehend themselves"

Maxwell's key insight: Each revelation built on the previous one. The doctrine of premortality wasn't delivered all at once — it unfolded *line upon line* even for the Prophet himself.

His most powerful lines:

> "Agreeing to enter this second estate, therefore, was like agreeing in advance to anesthetic—the anesthetic of forgetfulness. Doctors do not de-anesthetize a patient, in the midst of what was previously authorized, to ask him, again, if it should be continued. We agreed to come here and to undergo certain experiences under certain conditions."

We consented. The veil isn't cruel — it's the condition we accepted. And the forgetting is *necessary* for the walking by faith that constitutes the test.

> "Thus, when we now say 'I know,' that realization is rediscovery; we are actually saying 'I know—again!'"

Every testimony is remembering.

### Elder Bangerter — "Foreordained to Serve" (April 2024)

[Elder Bangerter](../../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2024/04/32bangerter.md) focused on the practical side: foreordination means God has specific callings and work prepared for each person. The application is personal revelation to discover what one was foreordained to do — a present-tense relationship with a pre-mortal appointment.

## Synthesis: What We Know

### The Timeline

1. **Intelligence exists eternally** — uncreated, self-acting, co-eternal with God ([D&C 93:29-30](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93.md); [Abraham 3:18](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/abr/3.md))
2. **God organizes intelligences into spirits** — "the intelligences that were organized before the world was" ([Abraham 3:22](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/abr/3.md)); He is "the Father of spirits" ([Hebrews 12:9](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/heb/12.md))
3. **Spirits receive first lessons** — learning, growing, choosing, developing faith and character ([D&C 138:56](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/138.md); [Alma 13:3-5](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/13.md))
4. **A council is held** — the Father presents His plan and calls for a Savior; two volunteer ([Abraham 3:24-28](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/abr/3.md); [Moses 4:1-4](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/4.md))
5. **War in heaven** — fought with testimony and the blood of the Lamb ([Revelation 12:7-11](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rev/12.md)); a third part cast out by their own agency ([D&C 29:36](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/29.md))
6. **Those who kept their first estate are "added upon"** — they receive bodies and enter mortality ([Abraham 3:26](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/abr/3.md))

### The Core Conflict

The real conflict in heaven wasn't just about who gets credit. It was about the nature of salvation itself:

- **Christ's response**: Accept the Father's plan as given. Agency preserved. Risk of failure. Growth through experience. "Thy will be done, and the glory be thine forever."
- **Satan's rebellion**: Reject the Father's plan. Destroy agency. Guarantee no failure — but no growth either. "Give me thine honor." Elder Bednar [observed](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RZ_bKUuuvbI&t=265) the self-centeredness: "I, I, I, I, I know what to do. I want what I want. I think I can do this better. We're headed for serious spiritual danger."

If D&C 93:30 is right that without agency "there is no existence," then Satan's rebellion wasn't just bad strategy — it was ontological destruction. You can't save beings by removing the thing that makes them beings.

### The Prove/School/Test Question

The word "prove" in Abraham 3:25 carries both meanings — *test* and *experience*. This isn't school *or* test. It's a school *that tests through experience.* The proving ground is the curriculum. We learn *by being tested*. This is exactly what Hebrews 12:6-11 describes:

> "For whom the Lord loveth he chasteneth... he for our profit, that we might be partakers of his holiness."
> — [Hebrews 12:6, 10](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/heb/12.md)

Maxwell saw this too: "Additional tutoring and suffering appears to be the pattern for the Lord's most apt pupils."

The answer to the driving question isn't "school vs. test." It's that *the test IS the school.* Learning through experience, with real stakes and real consequences, is the educating mechanism.

### Connections to Existing Studies

- **Intelligence study**: D&C 93:29 appears in both — intelligence as uncreated, self-acting. The pre-mortal narrative explains *why* intelligence is fundamental: without it, the plan literally cannot work.
- **Truth study**: D&C 93:24 (truth as knowledge of things as they are/were/will be) + D&C 93:30 (intelligence acts for itself). Maxwell quotes Jacob 4:13 — understanding "things as they really were, are, and will become" requires the premortal framework.
- **Agency study**: Agency isn't a feature of the plan — it's the foundation on which the plan is built. Satan's attack on agency was an attack on existence itself.
- **Creation study**: Abraham 3's council language flows directly into Abraham 4's creation language. The creation is the council's plan being enacted.
- **Truth-Atonement study**: Christ in the council offering Himself — "Thy will be done, and the glory be thine forever" — is the first act of the Atonement. He volunteered before the world was. The whole matter spectrum (intelligence → truth → light → spirit → element) traces through premortality into mortality and back.

## Open Questions for Later Phases

- **Phase 2**: What does "keeping the second estate" look like in practice? Is mortality a continuation of pre-mortal learning, or a qualitatively different kind of experience? What does the veil change about the nature of agency?
- **Phase 3**: If spirits "received their first lessons in the world of spirits," what is the spirit world like after death? Does learning continue? (D&C 138 covers both pre-mortal and post-mortal spirit world.)
- **Phase 5**: Abraham 3:26 promises those who keep their second estate will have "glory added upon their heads for ever and ever." What does that glory look like? How does resurrection relate to the proving?
- **The Comfort Thread**: If we shouted for joy at the plan — including its difficulties — what does that mean for friends who are suffering now? Not "you signed up for this" (that's glib), but rather: the Father proposed this plan, and the Son *volunteered His own life* as the cost. Christ understood suffering before we did. He didn't stand apart from it.
