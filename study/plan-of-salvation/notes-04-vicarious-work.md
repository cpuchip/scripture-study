# Phase 4: Baptism for the Dead & Vicarious Work

*Plan of Salvation Study — Phase 4 of 8*
*Date: 2026-02-20*

---

## The Big Idea

Phase 3 showed us one half of the picture: in the spirit world, the dead are *taught* the gospel — faith, repentance, "vicarious baptism for the remission of sins" ([D&C 138:33](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/138.md)). Phase 4 reveals the other half: on *this* side of the veil, *we* perform the ordinances they're being taught about. Two halves of one system. The spirit world missionaries teach; the temple workers act. Neither is complete without the other.

And neither are *we* complete without *them*.

> "For their salvation is necessary and essential to our salvation, as Paul says concerning the fathers — that they without us cannot be made perfect — neither can we without our dead be made perfect." — [D&C 128:15](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/128.md)

## Scripture Reading

### 1 Corinthians 15:29 — Paul's Witness

> "Else what shall they do which are baptized for the dead, if the dead rise not at all? why are they then baptized for the dead?"

Paul doesn't *introduce* baptism for the dead. He *assumes* it. This is mid-argument about the reality of the resurrection, and he drops this practice as evidence — if there's no resurrection, then why is anyone doing proxy baptism? The logic only works if his audience is already familiar with the practice and considers it legitimate.

This single verse in the New Testament testifies that vicarious baptism was practiced in the early church. Paul didn't invent it, didn't defend it, didn't explain it — he just *used* it as a given. Whatever happened to the practice after the apostasy, the fact remains: it was there.

### D&C 137:7-10 — The Vision That Started Everything

Joseph Smith, January 1836, Kirtland Temple. He sees the celestial kingdom — the blazing throne of God, streets paved with gold, Father Adam, Abraham — and his brother Alvin.

> "And marveled how it was that he had obtained an inheritance in that kingdom, seeing that he had departed this life before the Lord had set his hand to gather Israel the second time, and had not been baptized for the remission of sins." — [D&C 137:6](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/137.md)

Alvin died in 1823, before the Church was organized, before baptism was even available. And yet Joseph *sees* him in celestial glory. The Lord's response is one of the most far-reaching doctrinal declarations in scripture:

> "All who have died without a knowledge of this gospel, who would have received it if they had been permitted to tarry, shall be heirs of the celestial kingdom of God; Also all that shall die henceforth without a knowledge of it, who would have received it with all their hearts, shall be heirs of that kingdom; For I, the Lord, will judge all men according to their works, according to the desire of their hearts." — [D&C 137:7-9](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/137.md)

Three principles at once:
1. **Retroactive** — those who *already* died without the gospel are covered
2. **Prospective** — those who *will* die without it are covered too
3. **Heart-based judgment** — God judges by desires, not just circumstances

And verse 10 — all children who die before accountability are saved in the celestial kingdom. No exceptions.

This is a direct testimony of God's *fairness*. The plan doesn't penalize people for the timing of their birth or the geography of their life.

### D&C 127 — Joseph Under Persecution, Still Teaching

Written September 1, 1842, while Joseph is in hiding from his enemies. The contrast is remarkable — pursued, hunted, and yet his mind is occupied by *baptism for the dead*. Even in verse 2, he glories in tribulation "like Paul."

The practical instruction: when baptisms for the dead are performed, there must be a recorder present — an eyewitness — so the record is valid both on earth and in heaven ([D&C 127:6-7](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/127.md)). The binding/loosing power: "whatsoever you bind on earth, may be bound in heaven."

Joseph even says he "desired, with exceedingly great desire" to address the Saints about this topic from the pulpit but couldn't because enemies were pursuing him ([D&C 127:10](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/127.md)). That urgency is real.

### D&C 128 — Joseph's Grand Epistle

This is Joseph Smith at his most inspired, September 7, 1842. The letter starts with careful procedural instructions about record-keeping (ward recorders, general recorder, certificates, witnesses) and then builds to one of the most exalted passages in all of scripture.

**The key doctrinal moves:**

**Records that bind heaven and earth (vv. 6-9).** Joseph connects Revelation 20:12 ("the dead were judged out of those things which were written in the books") to the earthly records of baptisms for the dead. The books on earth correspond to the book of life in heaven. What we record *here* is recorded *there*. This isn't bureaucracy — it's cosmology.

> "Whatsoever those men did in authority, in the name of the Lord, and did it truly and faithfully, and kept a proper and faithful record of the same, it became a law on earth and in heaven, and could not be annulled, according to the decrees of the great Jehovah." — [D&C 128:9](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/128.md)

**The summum bonum (v. 11).** "The great and grand secret of the whole matter" is obtaining the powers of the Holy Priesthood. With these keys, "there is no difficulty in obtaining a knowledge of facts in relation to the salvation of the children of men, both as well for the dead as for the living."

**Baptism as resurrection symbol (vv. 12-13).** The baptismal font is "a similitude of the grave." Immersion = burial. Coming forth from water = resurrection. Baptism for the dead makes this symbolism explicit — the living go down into the water representing the dead, and come forth representing their rising. The font is placed "underneath where the living are wont to assemble" — below ground level — because it represents the grave.

**The mutual necessity (v. 15).** "They without us cannot be made perfect — neither can we without our dead be made perfect." Not just that we help them. We *need* them. The chain must be unbroken.

**The welding link (v. 18).** Joseph's unique image:

> "It is sufficient to know, in this case, that the earth will be smitten with a curse unless there is a welding link of some kind or other between the fathers and the children."

And then the scope expands: "a whole and complete and perfect union, and welding together of dispensations, and keys, and powers, and glories should take place, and be revealed from the days of Adam even to the present time."

**The hymn of praise (vv. 19-23).** Joseph breaks into ecstatic praise — "A voice of gladness! A voice of mercy from heaven; and a voice of truth out of the earth; glad tidings for the dead!" He lists every angelic restoration: Moroni, Peter James and John, Michael, Gabriel, Raphael — "all declaring their dispensation, their rights, their keys, their honors."

Then the call: "Brethren, shall we not go on in so great a cause? Go forward and not backward. Courage, brethren; and on, on to the victory!" And:

> "Let the dead speak forth anthems of eternal praise to the King Immanuel, who hath ordained, before the world was, that which would enable us to redeem them out of their prison; for the prisoners shall go free." — [D&C 128:22](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/128.md)

### D&C 124:28-36 — The Temple Command

The Lord commands the Saints to build a temple *specifically* for ordinances for the dead:

> "For there is not a place found on earth that he may come to and restore again that which was lost unto you, or which he hath taken away, even the fulness of the priesthood." — [D&C 124:28](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/124.md)

> "For a baptismal font there is not upon the earth, that they, my saints, may be baptized for those who are dead — For this ordinance belongeth to my house." — [D&C 124:29-30](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/124.md)

The urgency here is striking. The Lord gives them "sufficient time" to build, and during construction their temporary baptisms are acceptable — but after, they *must* have a temple ([D&C 124:31-33](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/124.md)). The consequence: "if you do not these things at the end of the appointment ye shall be rejected as a church, with your dead."

And verse 33 drops a quiet bomb: this ordinance "was instituted from before the foundation of the world." *Before* the Fall, *before* mortality, *before* anyone had ever died — God planned the mechanism for their redemption. (Connection to Phase 1: the entire plan was laid out in the pre-mortal council, including this.)

### Malachi 4:5-6 and D&C 2 — The Elijah Promise

Malachi, the last voice of the Old Testament prophets:

> "Behold, I will send you Elijah the prophet before the coming of the great and dreadful day of the Lord: And he shall turn the heart of the fathers to the children, and the heart of the children to their fathers, lest I come and smite the earth with a curse." — [Malachi 4:5-6](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/mal/4.md)

When Moroni appeared to Joseph Smith in 1823, he quoted this Malachi passage but with significant changes: "He shall plant in the hearts of the children the promises made to the fathers, and the hearts of the children shall turn to their fathers. If it were not so, the whole earth would be utterly wasted at his coming" ([D&C 2:2-3](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/2.md)).

Not just "turn" but *plant promises*. Not just "a curse" but "utterly wasted." The stakes could not be higher.

### D&C 110:13-16 — The Fulfillment

April 3, 1836. Kirtland Temple. Easter Sunday. After Jesus Himself appeared and accepted the temple, three more messengers came: Moses (keys of gathering), Elias (dispensation of Abraham's gospel), and then:

> "Elijah the prophet, who was taken to heaven without tasting death, stood before us, and said: Behold, the time has fully come, which was spoken of by the mouth of Malachi." — [D&C 110:13-14](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/110.md)

> "To turn the hearts of the fathers to the children, and the children to the fathers, lest the whole earth be smitten with a curse — Therefore, the keys of this dispensation are committed into your hands." — [D&C 110:15-16](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/110.md)

Malachi prophesied. Moroni quoted it. Elijah fulfilled it. The keys are here.

### Hebrews 11:40 — Paul's Other Witness

After cataloguing the great acts of faith across all ages — Abel, Enoch, Noah, Abraham, Moses — Paul concludes:

> "God having provided some better thing for us, that they without us should not be made perfect." — [Hebrews 11:40](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/heb/11.md)

Joseph quotes this in D&C 128:15,18. Paul knew: the ancient faithful were waiting. Something needed to happen in a later dispensation before their salvation could be complete.

## Webster 1828 Definitions

### Vicarious
**"Acting or suffering for another. Performed or suffered in the place of another; substituted; as, a vicarious sacrifice."**

This is the precise doctrine: temple ordinances are *vicarious* — performed by one person standing in the place of another. Joseph's term in D&C 128:8 is "agents" — "whether they themselves have attended to the ordinances in their own propria persona, or by the means of their own agents." The 1828 definition of **agent** is simply "acting" — one who acts for another by authority.

The phrase "vicarious sacrifice" in Webster's examples points directly to Christ: His Atonement is the ultimate vicarious act, and our temple work is a participation in that vicarious pattern.

### Redeem
**"To ransom, liberate, or rescue from captivity or bondage, or from any obligation or liability to suffer or to be forfeited, by paying a price or ransom."**

Also: **"To rescue and deliver from the bondage of sin and the penalties of God's violated law."**

This matches the spirit world language perfectly. Phase 3 established two kinds of bondage — disembodiment (all) and unrepented sin (wicked). Redemption addresses both: the Atonement pays the ransom, and vicarious ordinances are the mechanism by which that ransom is applied to those who've died without the gospel.

D&C 128:22: "that which would enable us to *redeem* them out of their *prison*; for the *prisoners* shall go free." Every Webster 1828 sense of "redeem" is at work: purchase back, ransom from captivity, deliver from bondage, pay the penalty.

### Perfect
**"Brought to consummation or completeness; completed; not defective nor redundant; having all the properties or qualities requisite to its nature and kind."**

When Joseph says "they without us cannot be made *perfect*," he means they cannot be brought to *completion*. Their progression has a gap — a missing ordinance, a broken link. And ours has the same gap in reverse. We are not complete without connection to our ancestors. The chain of humanity must be whole.

### Weld
In Webster 1828, "weld" means only **"to wield."** The metallurgical meaning — joining metals by heat and pressure — was emerging in Joseph's era but hadn't yet entered the dictionary. When Joseph coins the phrase "welding link" in D&C 128:18, he's using language slightly ahead of its own time to express something the dictionary couldn't yet articulate: a *permanent, inseparable joining* of human generations across the veil.

This is not a rope that could untie. Not a chain that could break. A *weld* — once fused, the joint *becomes* the material. That's what sealing does: it makes the connection between generations as real and permanent as the metal itself.

### Turn
**"To give another direction, tendency, or inclination to; to direct otherwise."** Also: **"To change the form, quality, aspect, or effect of; to alter; to convert; to transform."**

Malachi's promise that Elijah will "turn the heart of the fathers to the children" means a fundamental *change of direction* — a reorientation. The fathers in the spirit world turn *toward* their descendants. The children on earth turn *toward* their ancestors. The turning is mutual. And it's not just emotional — it's covenantal. The sealing ordinance *enacts* the turning.

## Prophetic Witness

### Elder D. Todd Christofferson, "The Sealing Power" (October 2023)

The most comprehensive recent apostolic treatment of this doctrine. Key teachings:

**The sealing power is broader than we think.** "We tend to think of the sealing authority as applying only to certain temple ordinances, but that authority is necessary to make *any* ordinance valid and binding beyond death." Even your own baptism is validated by the sealing power through the keys held by the President of the Church.

**Universal availability.** "The validity that the sealing power gives to priesthood ordinances includes, of course, vicarious ordinances performed in the place designated by the Lord — His temple. Here we see the majesty and sacredness of the sealing power — it makes individual salvation and family exaltation *universally available* to the children of God wherever and whenever they may have lived on the earth."

This is the answer to the fairness question. The sealing power *makes the plan fair*. Without it, billions who lived and died without hearing the gospel would be permanently excluded. With it, "no other theology or philosophy or authority can match such an all-inclusive opportunity."

**Without roots or branches.** Elder Christofferson explains Malachi's curse: "Without the sealings that create eternal families and link generations here and hereafter, we would be left in eternity with neither roots nor branches — that is, neither ancestry nor posterity."

**The living need the dead.** "The progress of those who are already on the other side, or who may yet cross through the veil of death without the benefit of sealings, is incomplete until vicarious ordinances bind them to us, their descendants, and us to them in the divine order." — [The Sealing Power](../../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2023/10/15christofferson.md)

### President Russell M. Nelson, "The Temple and Your Spiritual Foundation" (October 2021)

President Nelson, quoting Joseph Smith, explains *why* gathering matters for temples: "The main object was to build unto the Lord a house whereby He could reveal unto His people the ordinances of His house and the glories of His kingdom, and teach the people the way of salvation."

Then, in quoting President Nelson's words at Sister Pat Holland's funeral: **"Salvation is an individual matter, but exaltation is a family matter. No one can be exalted alone."** This is the most succinct expression of why vicarious work exists — because the plan's ultimate aim is *families*, not just individuals.

And Brigham Young, quoted by Nelson, captures the urgency with characteristic force: "When I think upon this subject, I want the tongues of seven thunders to wake up the people." — [The Temple and Your Spiritual Foundation](../../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2021/10/47nelson.md)

## Synthesis: The Architecture of Fairness

### The System Has Two Halves

| Spirit World Side (Phase 3) | Earth Side (Phase 4) |
|---|---|
| Messengers preach the gospel ([D&C 138:30](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/138.md)) | Saints perform ordinances in temples ([D&C 124:29](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/124.md)) |
| Spirits are taught faith, repentance, "vicarious baptism" ([D&C 138:33](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/138.md)) | Proxy baptisms are physically performed ([D&C 128:12-13](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/128.md)) |
| Dead exercise agency to accept or reject ([D&C 138:31-32](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/138.md)) | Records are kept, bound on earth and in heaven ([D&C 128:8](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/128.md)) |
| Spirits "wait" for deliverance ([D&C 138:49-50](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/138.md)) | Living become "saviors on Mount Zion" through temple work |

Neither half works alone. The spirit world teaching without earthly ordinances would be education without mechanism. The earthly ordinances without spirit world agency would be ritual without consent. Together: a complete system.

### The Scope of the Atonement

This phase answers the fairness question definitively. Consider:

- **D&C 137:7-9**: Those who *would have* received the gospel are heirs of the celestial kingdom. God sees the heart.
- **D&C 137:10**: Children who die before accountability are saved. No exceptions.
- **D&C 128:5**: The ordinance was "prepared before the foundation of the world, for the salvation of the dead who should die without a knowledge of the gospel."
- **1 Corinthians 15:29**: The practice existed in Paul's day — this isn't an innovation.
- **Hebrews 11:40**: "They without us should not be made perfect" — the ancient faithful were waiting for *this* dispensation.

The Atonement of Jesus Christ is not limited by geography, chronology, or a person's access to missionaries. It reaches backward and forward through all of time, mediated by two mechanisms: spirit world teaching and earthly temple ordinances.

### The Welding Link

Joseph's "welding link" metaphor in [D&C 128:18](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/128.md) deserves special attention. He writes of "a whole and complete and perfect union, and welding together of dispensations, and keys, and powers, and glories ... from the days of Adam even to the present time."

This isn't just about individuals being baptized for individual ancestors. It's about *all dispensations* being linked — Adam's to Noah's to Abraham's to Moses's to Christ's to Joseph's. Every prophet who held keys returned them in this dispensation (D&C 128:20-21). Every dispensation contributes something to the whole. The "welding" is temporal, institutional, familial, and cosmological all at once.

And the consequence of *not* welding? "The whole earth would be utterly wasted at his coming" ([D&C 2:3](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/2.md)). If the purpose of earth is to create eternal families connected across all generations, and that connection never happens, then the earth failed its purpose. Utterly wasted.

### God's Character Revealed

What does vicarious work teach us about *God*?

1. **He planned for every contingency.** The ordinance was "instituted from before the foundation of the world" ([D&C 124:33](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/124.md)). Before anyone fell, He planned how to save those who'd fall without knowing how to get up.

2. **He judges by hearts, not circumstances.** "According to the desire of their hearts" ([D&C 137:9](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/137.md)). A person born in ancient China who never heard the name of Christ is not disadvantaged in God's economy.

3. **He binds the whole human family together.** The work isn't just salvific — it's *connective*. We become linked to people we never met, across centuries we never lived in. The temple makes the human family literal.

4. **He invites us to participate in His work.** We don't just *receive* salvation — we *administer* it. We become agents of redemption. This is the Christlike pattern: one acts in behalf of another who cannot act for themselves.

## Connection to Previous Phases

**Phase 1 (Pre-mortal Existence):** The plan included this work from the beginning. D&C 128:5 says the ordinance was "prepared before the foundation of the world." D&C 124:33 says it was "instituted from before the foundation of the world." The pre-mortal council didn't just plan for mortality — it planned for how to redeem every person who would pass through mortality without the gospel.

**Phase 2 (Mortality):** In Phase 2 we learned that mortality is a school, not just a test. Vicarious work reveals that we're not just students — we're also *teachers and workers* for others. Performing ordinances for the dead is part of our own education in becoming like Christ (who performed the ultimate vicarious act).

**Phase 3 (Spirit World):** The spirit world teaching from D&C 138 and the temple ordinances of D&C 127-128 are two halves of the same system. D&C 138:33 explicitly names "vicarious baptism" as something spirits are *taught about*. They're learning what it means; we're *doing* what it means. The two sides must work together.

## Comfort Thread — For the Sick and Suffering

For your friends who are ill, this phase carries a specific kind of comfort:

1. **No one is overlooked.** If your friends worry about what happens to loved ones who died without the gospel, D&C 137:7-9 answers directly: God knows what they would have chosen, and He judges accordingly. The temple work ensures the ordinances are available.

2. **Death doesn't end the work.** If your friends themselves are facing death, they can know that the same system exists for *them*. If there's anything they didn't get to in mortality — ordinances not yet received, family history not yet completed — the system is designed to continue.

3. **We stay connected.** The sealing power means that relationships forged in this life don't dissolve at death. As Elder Christofferson taught, the sealings create permanent links — "welding links" — that death cannot break.

4. **The plan was designed for this.** This isn't a Plan B or an afterthought. Vicarious work was "instituted from before the foundation of the world." God always knew some of His children would need this path. It's built into the architecture.

5. **You can do something.** For those who feel helpless watching someone suffer, temple work is something concrete and sacred you can do. You can *act* on behalf of those who can't act for themselves — the living for the dead, the healthy for the sick in spirit, the connected for the disconnected.

## Open Questions

1. **D&C 128:8 — "propria persona" vs. "agents."** Joseph uses this legal-sounding Latin phrase to distinguish between doing the ordinance yourself and having someone do it for you. Does this imply that when spirits accept the vicarious ordinance, it's as if they themselves performed it? The language suggests full equivalency — not a lesser version.

2. **The scope of "they without us cannot be made perfect."** Is this purely about ordinances, or is there something more? Does our family history *research* — learning their stories, connecting to their lives — play a role in their "perfection" beyond the ordinances?

3. **Malachi 4:1 — "neither root nor branch."** The verse just before Elijah's promise warns that the wicked will be left "neither root nor branch." Christofferson applies this: without sealings, we'd have "neither ancestry nor posterity." Is there a connection between wickedness and disconnection from family bonds?
