# The Plan of Salvation: A 30,000-Foot View

*A synthesis study tracing the soul's journey from pre-mortal intelligence to eternal glory*
*February 20, 2026*

> "This is my work and my glory — to bring to pass the immortality and eternal life of man."
> — [Moses 1:39](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/1.md)

---

## Why This Study Exists

This study began with two questions. One was intellectual: *What is the Plan of Salvation — not as a Primary diagram, but as a living architecture?* The other was personal: *What comfort does this plan actually offer someone who is sick, dying, or grieving?*

The answers turned out to be the same. The architecture *is* the comfort. When you see the plan as a whole — not as disconnected doctrines but as a single coherent story — what emerges is a picture of a God who planned for every contingency, who judges by the desire of the heart, who consecrates every affliction for gain, and who saves all the works of His hands.

What follows is the 30,000-foot view. Six phases of deep study compressed into a single narrative. Every scripture quoted here was read from its source file. Every definition traced to Webster 1828. Every insight earned line by line through the actual text.

---

## Part 1: The Architecture — A Soul's Journey

### Stage 1: Intelligence — The Eternal Foundation

Before there was a plan, there was *us*.

> "Intelligence, or the light of truth, was not created or made, neither indeed can be."
> — [D&C 93:29](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93.md)

Intelligence is co-eternal with God. It was never created, never began. And it possesses something extraordinary:

> "All truth is independent in that sphere in which God has placed it, to act for itself, as all intelligence also; otherwise there is no existence."
> — [D&C 93:30](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93.md)

Agency isn't a feature of the plan. It's the foundation on which the plan is built. Without self-acting intelligence, "there is no existence." The whole architecture rests on this: we are beings who can choose.

### Stage 2: Spirit Children — Organization and First Lessons

God organized intelligences into spirits — His spirit children:

> "Now the Lord had shown unto me, Abraham, the intelligences that were organized before the world was; and among all these there were many of the noble and great ones."
> — [Abraham 3:22](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/abr/3.md)

Paul confirms the relationship: "Shall we not much rather be in subjection unto the Father of spirits, and live?" ([Hebrews 12:9](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/heb/12.md)). God is literally the Father of our spirits.

And these spirits weren't idle. They received "first lessons in the world of spirits and were prepared to come forth" ([D&C 138:56](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/138.md)). There was a pre-mortal curriculum. We were already learning.

### Stage 3: The Council — We Chose This

A council was held. God presented the plan:

> "And there stood one among them that was like unto God, and he said unto those who were with him: We will go down, for there is space there, and we will take of these materials, and we will make an earth whereon these may dwell; And we will prove them herewith, to see if they will do all things whatsoever the Lord their God shall command them."
> — [Abraham 3:24-25](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/abr/3.md)

Webster 1828 defines **prove** as both "to try by experiment" AND "to gain experience of the good or evil of." The word carries both meanings simultaneously — test *and* school. This matters enormously: the plan wasn't designed as a pass/fail exam. It was designed as an education through experience, with real stakes.

The Father asked, "Whom shall I send?" ([Abraham 3:27](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/abr/3.md)). Two volunteered. Christ: "Father, thy will be done, and the glory be thine forever" ([Moses 4:2](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/4.md)) — accepting the Father's plan as given, returning the glory. Satan: "I will redeem all mankind, that one soul shall not be lost, and surely I will do it; wherefore give me thine honor" ([Moses 4:1](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/4.md)). As Elder Bednar [taught plainly](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RZ_bKUuuvbI&t=210): "It is important to recognize that Lucifer did not present his plan. He rebelled against the Father's plan." His rebellion would have destroyed agency — and since D&C 93:30 says without agency "there is no existence," his rescue would have been annihilation.

A war followed. It was won "by the blood of the Lamb, and by the word of their testimony" ([Revelation 12:11](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rev/12.md)) — the same means we use in mortality. And those who chose the Father's plan?

> "When the morning stars sang together, and all the sons of God shouted for joy."
> — [Job 38:7](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/job/38.md)

We knew what was coming — the difficulty, the forgetting, the proving — and we *shouted for joy*. That's not the response of souls being forced into an examination. That's the response of people who understood the opportunity.

### Stage 4: The Fall — The Designed Doorway

The creation prepared the habitat. The Fall opened the door.

Lehi's theology in 2 Nephi 2 is the most complete single-chapter treatment of why the Fall was *necessary*:

> "Adam fell that men might be; and men are, that they might have joy."
> — [2 Nephi 2:25](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/2.md)

Without the Fall, Adam and Eve would have remained in the Garden — "having no joy, for they knew no misery; doing no good, for they knew no sin" ([2 Nephi 2:23](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/2.md)). Innocence without capacity. The Fall wasn't a catastrophe reversed by the Atonement — it was the *first step* in a designed sequence: innocence → fall → redemption → growth → glory. Each step requires the previous one.

Adam and Eve understood this in retrospect:

> "Blessed be the name of God, for because of my transgression my eyes are opened, and in this life I shall have joy, and again in the flesh I shall see God."
> — [Moses 5:10](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/5.md)

Webster 1828 defines **transgression** as "the act of passing over or beyond any law." Adam and Eve passed beyond the boundary of the Garden — and in doing so, passed into the realm of growth, experience, and eventual joy.

### Stage 5: Mortality — The School That Tests Through Experience

Alma uses the word "probationary" to describe mortality — and Webster 1828 reveals what we've lost: **probation** means not just testing but "the opportunity of proving his character, AND becoming qualified for a happier state." Testing AND qualifying. Alma himself confirms this: "this probationary state became a state for them to prepare; it became a preparatory state" ([Alma 42:10](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/42.md)).

The answer to "Is mortality a test or a school?" is that *the test IS the school*. Learning through experience, with real stakes and real consequences, is the educating mechanism. Elder Bednar put it precisely: "Tests in the school of mortality are a vital element of our eternal progression" ([We Will Prove Them Herewith](../../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2020/10/12bednar.md), October 2020).

And President Oaks gave us the gravity metaphor: "Just as our physical muscles cannot be developed or maintained without straining against the law of gravity, so mortal growth requires us to strain against Satan's temptations and other mortal opposition" ([Divine Helps for Mortality](../../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2025/04/47oaks.md), April 2025). Opposition isn't the enemy of growth — it's the resistance that makes growth possible.

The school operates with seven features:

1. **Embodiment** — Spirit + element = fulness of joy ([D&C 93:33-34](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93.md)). The body is half of what makes fulness possible.
2. **Opposition** — Required for agency and moral education ([2 Nephi 2:11](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/2.md)).
3. **The veil** — Forgetting, so we walk by faith. Maxwell: "Agreeing to enter this second estate was like agreeing in advance to anesthetic."
4. **Consecrated affliction** — "He shall consecrate thine afflictions for thy gain" ([2 Nephi 2:2](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/2.md)). Suffering isn't pointless; it's pedagogical.
5. **The gospel curriculum from day one** — Adam received commandments, sacrifice, baptism, the Holy Ghost, and the full plan ([Moses 5-6](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/5.md)). The school was never without a teacher.
6. **Divine helps** — The Light of Christ, commandments, covenants, and the Holy Ghost ([President Oaks, April 2025](../../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2025/04/47oaks.md)).
7. **Transformation as the goal** — "The natural man is an enemy to God ... unless he yields to the enticings of the Holy Spirit, and putteth off the natural man and becometh a saint through the atonement of Christ the Lord" ([Mosiah 3:19](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/3.md)).

Moses 6 captures the pedagogy beautifully: children "taste the bitter, that they may know to prize the good. And it is given unto them to know good from evil; wherefore they are agents unto themselves" ([Moses 6:55-56](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/6.md)). The bitter isn't incidental — it's pedagogical.

### Stage 6: The Spirit World — Rest, Reunion, and Continued Work

Death is a homecoming: "The spirits of all men, as soon as they are departed from this mortal body ... are taken home to that God who gave them life" ([Alma 40:11](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/40.md)). Home. Not "sent away." *Home.*

The righteous enter paradise — "a state of happiness, which is called paradise, a state of rest, a state of peace, where they shall rest from all their troubles and from all care, and sorrow" ([Alma 40:12](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/40.md)). Webster 1828 defines **rest** as "to cease from that which harasses or disturbs; to be quiet or still." For those who are suffering — the pain *stops*.

But even paradise carries a kind of bondage. "The dead had looked upon the long absence of their spirits from their bodies as a bondage" ([D&C 138:50](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/138.md)). Even the righteous feel the incompleteness of disembodiment, confirming D&C 93:33 — without the body, fulness is impossible.

The spirit world is not idle. Christ organized the righteous to teach the unprepared: "From among the righteous, he organized his forces and appointed messengers, clothed with power and authority, and commissioned them to go forth and carry the light of the gospel to them that were in darkness" ([D&C 138:30](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/138.md)). And the work continues now: "the faithful elders of this dispensation, when they depart from mortal life, continue their labors" ([D&C 138:57](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/138.md)). The school never closes.

Two kinds of bondage. Two kinds of liberation:

| Bondage | Who Experiences It | Liberation |
|---------|-------------------|------------|
| Disembodiment — separation of spirit from body | All spirits, including the righteous | Resurrection |
| Unrepented sin — "spirits in prison" | The wicked, unrepentant, or ignorant | Gospel teaching + repentance + vicarious ordinances |

### Stage 7: Vicarious Work — The Architecture of Fairness

The spirit world has its missionaries. The earth has its temples. Two halves of one system:

| Spirit World Side | Earth Side |
|---|---|
| Messengers preach the gospel ([D&C 138:30](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/138.md)) | Saints perform ordinances in temples ([D&C 124:29](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/124.md)) |
| Spirits are taught faith, repentance, "vicarious baptism" ([D&C 138:33](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/138.md)) | Proxy baptisms are physically performed ([D&C 128:12-13](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/128.md)) |
| Dead exercise agency to accept or reject ([D&C 138:31-32](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/138.md)) | Records are kept, bound on earth and in heaven ([D&C 128:8](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/128.md)) |

Neither half works alone. And neither are *we* complete without *them*: "They without us cannot be made perfect — neither can we without our dead be made perfect" ([D&C 128:15](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/128.md)).

This system was "instituted from before the foundation of the world" ([D&C 124:33](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/124.md)). Before anyone fell, God planned how to save those who'd fall without knowing how to get up. Joseph called it a "welding link" ([D&C 128:18](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/128.md)) — Webster 1828 hadn't even caught up with the metallurgical sense yet. Joseph's language was ahead of his dictionary: a weld makes two pieces *become* one material.

And D&C 137 answers the fairness question: God judges "according to the desire of their hearts" ([D&C 137:9](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/137.md)). A person born in ancient China who never heard the name of Christ is not disadvantaged in God's economy.

### Stage 8: Resurrection — The Redemption of the Soul

Resurrection is not just reassembly. It is *redemption*:

> "The spirit and the body are the soul of man. And the resurrection from the dead is the redemption of the soul."
> — [D&C 88:15-16](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)

The body returns perfected: "Every limb and joint shall be restored to its body; yea, even a hair of the head shall not be lost; but all things shall be restored to their proper and perfect frame" ([Alma 40:23](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/40.md)). Whatever disease or limitation defines the body now will be gone.

Paul gives us the four contrasts of transformation: sown in corruption, raised in incorruption; sown in dishonour, raised in glory; sown in weakness, raised in power; sown a natural body, raised a spiritual body ([1 Corinthians 15:42-44](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/1-cor/15.md)). Webster 1828 defines **incorruption** as "absence of, or exemption from, corruption" — permanently beyond decay.

And resurrection is universal: "As in Adam all die, even so in Christ shall all be made alive" ([1 Corinthians 15:22](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/1-cor/15.md)). This is a gift, not a reward. Without it, we would "become subject to that angel who fell from before the presence of the Eternal God, and became the devil, to rise no more" ([2 Nephi 9:8-9](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/9.md)). The stakes of resurrection are far higher than most of us realize.

### Stage 9: Judgment — What We Have Become

Judgment is not a courtroom. It is an acknowledgment of what we are:

> "The Final Judgment is not just an evaluation of a sum total of good and evil acts—what we have *done*. It is an acknowledgment of the final effect of our acts and thoughts—what we have *become*."
> — [Elder Dallin H. Oaks, "The Challenge to Become," October 2000](../../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2000/10/the-challenge-to-become.md)

Alma teaches the restoration principle: "The meaning of the word restoration is to bring back again evil for evil, or carnal for carnal ... good for that which is good; righteous for that which is righteous; just for that which is just; merciful for that which is merciful" ([Alma 41:13](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/41.md)). You get back what you are. The universe echoes your character. "They are their own judges" ([Alma 41:7](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/41.md)).

And the one who stands at the gate is not a stranger:

> "The keeper of the gate is the Holy One of Israel; and he employeth no servant there."
> — [2 Nephi 9:41](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/9.md)

Christ judges personally. He employs no servant. The one who weighs our souls is the one who bore their weight of sin. The Judge is also the Advocate.

### Stage 10: Kingdoms of Glory — The Destination

D&C 76 reveals what no Christian theology since the apostles had seen: the afterlife is not a binary of heaven and hell. It is a graduated, merciful architecture of glory.

**The law determines the kingdom.** D&C 88 gives the framework:

> "He who is not able to abide the law of a celestial kingdom cannot abide a celestial glory."
> — [D&C 88:22](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)

Webster 1828 defines **abide** as "to stay; to continue in a place; to have one's abode; to dwell." Not merely tolerate — *make your home there*. You inherit the kingdom you can comfortably, securely live in.

The **celestial** glory is the sun: those who received the testimony of Jesus, were baptized, kept the commandments, and were sealed by the Holy Spirit of Promise ([D&C 76:51-53](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/76.md)). "They are gods, even the sons of God" ([D&C 76:58](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/76.md)).

The **terrestrial** is the moon: honorable people who either never had the full gospel or who had it but weren't *valiant* — "not valiant in the testimony of Jesus" ([D&C 76:79](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/76.md)). They receive the presence of the Son, but not the fulness of the Father.

The **telestial** is the stars — and even this least glory "surpasses all understanding" ([D&C 76:89](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/76.md)). Its inhabitants are still "heirs of salvation" ([D&C 76:88](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/76.md)). A cascading ministry connects all kingdoms: celestial ministers to terrestrial, terrestrial to telestial. No one is abandoned.

And then the governing verse that reveals God's heart:

> "For what doth it profit a man if a gift is bestowed upon him, and he receive not the gift? Behold, he rejoices not in that which is given unto him, neither rejoices in him who is the giver of the gift."
> — [D&C 88:33](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)

This is not punishment. It is *capacity*. God isn't withholding — He's giving everything you're willing to take.

The only exceptions are the sons of perdition — those who stood in the full light and perfectly rejected it. For all the rest: "He saves all the works of his hands" ([D&C 76:43](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/76.md)).

### Stage 11: Exaltation — Eternal Life

Within the celestial kingdom, there are "three heavens or degrees" ([D&C 131:1](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/131.md)). The highest requires "the new and everlasting covenant of marriage" ([D&C 131:2](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/131.md)). Without it, one "cannot have an increase" ([D&C 131:4](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/131.md)). Webster 1828 defines **increase** as "to multiply by the production of young" — eternal offspring, eternal family, the continuation of life itself.

Moses heard the Father's mission statement: "This is my work and my glory — to bring to pass the **immortality** and **eternal life** of man" ([Moses 1:39](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/1.md)). Two gifts, not the same thing:

| Gift | Who Receives It | What It Means |
|------|----------------|---------------|
| **Immortality** | All resurrected beings | Unending existence; exemption from death; the free gift |
| **Eternal Life** | Those who receive exaltation | To know God, to live as God lives, to have eternal family |

President Kimball: "Immortality is to live forever in an assigned kingdom. Eternal life is to gain exaltation in the highest heaven and live in the family unit" ([An Eternal Hope in Christ](../../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/1978/10/an-eternal-hope-in-christ.md), October 1978).

And what is exaltation? Webster 1828 defines it as not just elevation but "the refinement or subtilization of a body, or the increasing of its virtue or principal property." Exaltation is not just going higher — it is *refinement*. The perfecting of what something already is. D&C 132:24 calls it "eternal lives" — plural. Not just duration, but generativity. A life that produces life.

> "And this is eternal lives — to know the only wise and true God, and Jesus Christ, whom he hath sent."
> — [D&C 132:24](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/132.md)

Eternal life is not a location. It's a relationship — knowing God so completely that you participate in His nature, His work, and His love.

And standing "betwixt them and justice" at every transition point is the same Person: the one who "broken the bands of death, taken upon himself their iniquity and their transgressions, having redeemed them, and satisfied the demands of justice" ([Mosiah 15:9](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/15.md)).

## Part 3: Mortality Reframed — The School That Tests

The most persistent discovery across all six phases was this: **mortality is not primarily an examination. It is an education that teaches through testing.**

The evidence is overwhelming:

- **Webster 1828 "probation"** = "the opportunity of proving his character, AND becoming qualified for a happier state."
- **Webster 1828 "prove"** = "to try by experiment" + "to gain experience of the good or evil of."
- **Alma 42:10** = "this probationary state became a state for them to prepare; it became a preparatory state."
- **Bednar (2020)** = "Tests in the school of mortality" — the word *school* and *tests* coexisting in the same phrase.
- **Oaks (2025)** = Opposition is like gravity for muscles — the *mechanism* of growth.
- **Moses 6:55** = Children "taste the bitter, that they may know to prize the good." The bitter is pedagogical.
- **Maxwell** = "Additional tutoring and suffering appears to be the pattern for the Lord's most apt pupils."

When you prove metal, you don't just pass/fail — you *refine* it. When you prove a student, the test itself teaches. Mortality is a probation-school: a designed environment where character is both tested and formed through the same experiences.

And the school operates from day one. Adam received the full gospel curriculum — faith, repentance, sacrifice, baptism, the Holy Ghost — immediately upon entering mortality ([Moses 5-6](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/5.md)). He obeyed before understanding: "I know not, save the Lord commanded me" ([Moses 5:6](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/5.md)). Then the angel taught him *why*. This is the pedagogy of mortality: obedience first, comprehension second. Faith precedes understanding.

The curriculum has one objective: transformation. "The natural man is an enemy to God ... unless he yields to the enticings of the Holy Spirit, and putteth off the natural man and becometh a saint through the atonement of Christ the Lord" ([Mosiah 3:19](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/3.md)). The goal isn't to endure mortality but to be *changed* by it.

And nothing learned here is wasted: "Whatever principle of intelligence we attain unto in this life, it will rise with us in the resurrection" ([D&C 130:18](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/130.md)). The school isn't preparation for something else — the education IS the diploma. It rises with us.

## Part 4: The Comfort Section — "Where Are They Now?"

*For friends who are sick, dying, or grieving.*

This section gathers every comfort promise we found across six phases of study. These are not platitudes. They are the direct testimony of scripture and prophets, traced to source.

### What Happens at Death

**Death is a homecoming.** "Taken home to that God who gave them life" ([Alma 40:11](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/40.md)). The spirit *returns* to where it came from ([Ecclesiastes 12:7](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/eccl/12.md)). "Home" is God's word for it.

**The pain stops.** Paradise is "a state of happiness ... a state of rest, a state of peace, where they shall rest from all their troubles and from all care, and sorrow" ([Alma 40:12](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/40.md)). Whatever is causing suffering now — it ends.

**They are not alone.** "An innumerable company of the spirits of the just" are gathered there together ([D&C 138:12](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/138.md)), "filled with joy and gladness, and were rejoicing together" ([D&C 138:15](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/138.md)). Sociality persists ([D&C 130:2](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/130.md)). They converse, rejoice, sing praises.

**They are not far from us.** Joseph Smith taught that the righteous dead "are not far from us, and know and understand our thoughts, feelings, and motions" (quoted by [President Oaks](../../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2019/10/17oaks.md)).

**They continue meaningful work.** "The faithful elders of this dispensation, when they depart from mortal life, continue their labors in the preaching of the gospel" ([D&C 138:57](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/138.md)). Life doesn't end. It *expands*.

### What Happens to the Body

**Every body will be made whole.** "Every limb and joint shall be restored to its body; yea, even a hair of the head shall not be lost; but all things shall be restored to their proper and perfect frame" ([Alma 40:23](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/40.md)). Whatever disease or limitation defines the body now — gone.

**Resurrection is universal.** "As in Adam all die, even so in Christ shall all be made alive" ([1 Corinthians 15:22](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/1-cor/15.md)). This is a free gift, not a reward. Everyone receives it.

**The separation is temporary.** Spirit and body will be "united never again to be divided, that they might receive a fulness of joy" ([D&C 138:17](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/138.md)).

**Death itself will be destroyed.** "The last enemy that shall be destroyed is death" ([1 Corinthians 15:26](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/1-cor/15.md)). "O death, where is thy sting? O grave, where is thy victory?" ([1 Corinthians 15:55](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/1-cor/15.md)).

### What About Those Who Didn't Get Everything Done

**No one is penalized by circumstances.** "All who have died without a knowledge of this gospel, who would have received it if they had been permitted to tarry, shall be heirs of the celestial kingdom of God" ([D&C 137:7](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/137.md)). God knows what you would have done. He judges "according to the desire of their hearts" ([D&C 137:9](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/137.md)).

**All children are saved.** "All children who die before they arrive at the years of accountability are saved in the celestial kingdom of heaven" ([D&C 137:10](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/137.md)). No exceptions.

**The system continues after death.** If there's anything not completed in mortality — ordinances, family history, covenants — the system is designed to continue. Spirit world missionaries teach. Temple workers on earth perform. Agency is preserved in both directions.

**The plan was designed for this.** Vicarious work was "instituted from before the foundation of the world" ([D&C 124:33](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/124.md)). This isn't Plan B. God always knew some of His children would need this path. It's built into the architecture.

### What About Suffering

**Suffering is consecrated.** "He shall consecrate thine afflictions for thy gain" ([2 Nephi 2:2](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/2.md)). Webster 1828 defines **consecrate** as "to make sacred." God makes the suffering sacred — not pointless, even when it feels that way.

**Your desires matter as much as your actions.** "If their works were good in this life, and the desires of their hearts were good" ([Alma 41:3](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/41.md)). People who want to be good but are limited by mortality's constraints — by illness, by exhaustion, by broken bodies — are *seen*.

**The Judge knows your pain from the inside.** He "descended below them all" ([D&C 122:8](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/122.md)). The one who judges you is the one who carried your suffering. He cannot be deceived ([2 Nephi 9:41](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/9.md)) — but He also cannot be callous.

**The Father proposed this plan, and the Son volunteered His own life as the cost.** Christ understood suffering before we did. He didn't stand apart from it.

### The Overwhelming Generosity of the System

**Every person inherits a kingdom of glory** — except the rarest of exceptions. Even the least kingdom "surpasses all understanding" ([D&C 76:89](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/76.md)). "He saves all the works of his hands" ([D&C 76:43](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/76.md)).

**God is offering everything.** "For what doth it profit a man if a gift is bestowed upon him, and he receive not the gift?" ([D&C 88:33](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)). He never withholds. He gives everything you're willing to take.

**Your relationships continue.** "That same sociality which exists among us here will exist among us there, only it will be coupled with eternal glory" ([D&C 130:2](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/130.md)).

**Nothing you become is lost.** "Whatever principle of intelligence we attain unto in this life, it will rise with us in the resurrection" ([D&C 130:18](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/130.md)). Every act of patience, every moment of love, every hard-won virtue — it rises with you.

**"I am not dead. I am alive."** Sister Aburto's brother's message, carried to her by the Spirit across four decades ([The Grave Has No Victory](../../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2021/04/42aburto.md), April 2021). The people who die are not extinguished. They live.

## Part 5: What Existing Studies Reveal

This study didn't emerge from nothing. It built on a remarkable foundation of prior work. Here's how those studies map onto the Plan of Salvation:

### Intelligence & Truth — The Building Blocks

The [intelligence study](../intelligence.md) and [truth study](../truth.md) established the foundational physics of the plan: intelligence is uncreated, self-acting, and co-eternal with God ([D&C 93:29-30](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93.md)). Without this foundation, the plan cannot work. Agency isn't a program feature — it's a property of the material. And truth is "knowledge of things as they are, and as they were, and as they are to come" ([D&C 93:24](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93.md)) — the substrate on which intelligence acts.

The truth study's spectrum — from intelligence to truth to light to spirit to element — traces through the entire plan. Spirit beings descend into element (birth), learn to integrate both (mortality), and ascend with both permanently joined (resurrection). The Fall is a descent on this spectrum that makes the ascent possible.

### The Atonement — The Upward Mechanism

The [truth-atonement study](../truth-atonement.md) showed how Christ makes upward traversal on the spectrum possible. The Atonement doesn't just forgive — it *transforms*. This maps directly onto the plan: Mosiah 3:19's transformation from natural man to saint isn't just behavioral change. It's the refinement of the *material of the soul* — intelligence and element being sanctified together.

### Creation — The Council Enacted

The [creation study](../creation.md) showed that Abraham 3's council language flows directly into Abraham 4's creation language. The creation is the council's plan being implemented: "spiritual before temporal" ([D&C 29:32](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/29.md)), with purpose encoded into every stage.

### Agency — The Non-Negotiable

The [agency study](../agency.md) demonstrated that 2 Nephi 2 isn't just about the Fall — it's about the fundamental partition of existence: "things to act and things to be acted upon" ([2 Nephi 2:14](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/2.md)). Satan's attack on agency wasn't just bad strategy — if D&C 93:30 is right that without agency "there is no existence," then his plan was ontological destruction. The entire plan exists to preserve and exercise the very thing intelligence *is*.

### Way, Truth, Life — The Temple as Enacted Plan

The [way-truth-life study](../way-truth-life.md) made the temple connection explicit:

| Temple Space | Kingdom | Christ's Declaration | Experience |
|-------------|---------|---------------------|------------|
| Outer Court | Telestial | "I am the Way" | Begin the journey |
| Holy Place | Terrestrial | "I am the Truth" | Receive light, instruction, covenants |
| Holy of Holies | Celestial | "I am the Life" | Enter God's presence; eternal life |

The endowment physically enacts the plan of salvation. You begin in a fallen state. You receive covenants. You pass through the veil — which is Christ's flesh ([Hebrews 10:20](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/heb/10.md)). You enter the celestial room. The temple doesn't just *teach* the plan. It lets you *walk* it.

### Covenants — Relationship, Not Contract

The [covenants study](../covenants.md) established that covenants are relational, not transactional. "Ye know me not" is the real tragedy — the covenant is about *knowing* God, not just obeying rules. This maps directly to the kingdoms of glory: they represent degrees of knowing God. Telestial = indirect knowledge (through intermediaries). Terrestrial = partial knowledge (Son's presence but not Father's fulness). Celestial = fulness (dwelling in the presence of God forever).

### Know God — Eternal Life as Relationship

The [know-god study](../know-god.md) made the ultimate connection: "This is life eternal, that they might know thee the only true God, and Jesus Christ, whom thou hast sent" ([John 17:3](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/john/17.md)). Eternal life isn't a place. It's a relationship. The entire plan — every stage, every trial, every covenant — is oriented toward one end: knowing God. And being known by Him.

### Moses 6 — The Course Catalog

The [Moses 6 study](../moses-6-gospel-to-adam.md) showed that the gospel was taught to Adam from the beginning. The "school" had a full catalog from day one: faith, repentance, baptism, the gift of the Holy Ghost, the plan of salvation explained in its entirety ([Moses 6:59-62](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/6.md)). Mortality was never intended to be figured out alone.

## Part 6: Open Questions — What We Still Don't Fully Understand

### The Alma 34:34 Tension

"That same spirit which doth possess your bodies at the time that ye go out of this life, that same spirit will have power to possess your body in that eternal world" ([Alma 34:34](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/34.md)). Yet D&C 138:58 says "the dead who repent will be redeemed." If disposition carries forward but repentance is still possible, then Amulek seems to be warning about lost *opportunity* — don't waste the advantage of mortality — rather than impossibility. The *direction* carries forward, but the door doesn't permanently close. This tension is real and deserves continued reflection.

### The Mechanics of Spirit Matter

D&C 131:7-8 says spirit is "more fine or pure" matter. What does that mean for the spirit world's environment? How does spirit matter interact with physical matter? How does the truth study's spectrum map onto what spirits actually experience between death and resurrection?

### The Scope of "They Without Us"

D&C 128:15 says "neither can we without our dead be made perfect." Is this purely about ordinances, or does our family history research — learning their stories, connecting to their lives — play a deeper role? The welding link is cosmological, not just procedural.

### What Life Looks Like in Each Kingdom

D&C 76 describes who *qualifies* for each kingdom but says remarkably little about what *daily existence* looks like there. We know the telestial glory "surpasses all understanding" ([D&C 76:89](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/76.md)), but that phrase is itself a boundary: understanding ends there. What does it mean for the terrestrial to receive the Son's presence but not the Father's fulness? What does "worlds without end" ([D&C 76:112](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/76.md)) mean for those who are "servants of the Most High" but cannot return to God's direct presence?

### The Timeline of Judgment

D&C 88:95-102 reveals resurrection happens in ordered waves — trumpets announcing celestial, terrestrial, telestial, and sons of perdition in sequence. But the specifics of timing, especially in relation to the Millennium, remain layered with complexity. What does it mean for the telestial to "not live again until the thousand years are ended" ([D&C 88:101](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md))?

### What We Shouted For

"All the sons of God shouted for joy" ([Job 38:7](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/job/38.md)). What did we see? What did we understand about the plan that made us shout? We don't remember — the veil is real. But the fact that we who are now suffering once *shouted with joy* at the prospect of this journey suggests that the view from that vantage point reveals something we cannot currently see.

## The Story in One Breath

Intelligence exists eternally — uncreated, self-acting, co-eternal with God. God organizes intelligences into spirits, His literal children. He teaches us. He presents a plan. Christ volunteers. We shout for joy.

We enter mortality through the Fall — not a catastrophe but a designed doorway. We forget, so we can learn to walk by faith. The school of mortality teaches through opposition, through bitter that teaches us to prize the good, through consecrated affliction. The curriculum is the gospel, delivered from Adam forward. The goal is not to survive the test but to be *transformed* by it — natural man into saint. The body itself is half the gift: spirit and element together receive fulness of joy.

At death, the righteous enter rest. The pain stops. The spirit world is not idle — the faithful teach, the ignorant learn, the rebellious can still repent. On earth, temple workers perform the ordinances the dead are being taught about. Two halves of one system, welded together. Neither is complete without the other. The plan was designed this way from before the foundation of the world.

Then resurrection — every soul, every body, restored to proper and perfect frame. The spirit and body reunited never again to be divided. Sown in corruption, raised in incorruption. The redemption of the soul.

Then judgment — not a courtroom scene but a recognition of what you've become. You get back what you are. Like attracts like. The keeper of the gate is the Holy One of Israel, and He employeth no servant there. The one who judges is the one who suffered for you.

Then kingdoms — not a binary of heaven and hell but a graduated architecture of glory. You inherit the kingdom whose law you can abide, whose light you can bear, whose presence you can dwell in. Even the least glory surpasses all understanding. He saves all the works of His hands.

And for those who covenant — exaltation. Refinement, not just elevation. Eternal family. Continuation of seeds. To know God so completely that you participate in His nature, His work, His love. "That same sociality which exists among us here will exist among us there, only it will be coupled with eternal glory."

This is the Plan of Salvation. God's own work and glory — the immortality and eternal life of man. And it completely honors your agency.

## Becoming

Six phases. Hundreds of scriptures. Dozens of definitions. But the plan of salvation isn't a research paper — it's a *life*. And the question now is the one Alma asked: "Have ye experienced this mighty change in your hearts?" ([Alma 5:14](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/5.md)). Not "do you understand the plan?" but "is the plan changing you?"

### Questions to Sit With

**If mortality is a school, what am I currently learning?**
Not in the abstract — right now, today. What is the lesson that keeps showing up? What opposition am I facing that might be the gravity my muscles need? President Oaks said opposition is the *mechanism* of growth. What is the specific opposition in my life that's building something I can't yet see?

**Am I treating this life as a probation or a preparation?**
Webster 1828 says probation includes "becoming qualified for a happier state." Am I just trying to pass — checking boxes, avoiding the worst sins, hoping to squeak by? Or am I actually preparing — leaning into the curriculum, asking what God is trying to teach me through the hard things?

**What do I actually say to my friends who are sick?**
The comfort section has the doctrine. But doctrine doesn't comfort people — *people* comfort people. The Spirit carries truth into hearts, but it uses human voices to do it. When I sit with someone who is scared, do I reach for theology or for presence? The Savior wept at the tomb of Lazarus even though He knew He was about to raise him ([John 11:35](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/john/11.md)). Weeping *with* someone may be the most Christlike thing I can do before I ever open my mouth.

**How does knowing the architecture change how I pray?**
If I really believe I'm a son of God — not metaphorically, but literally, a spirit child who shouted for joy and chose this — does that change how I talk to Him? If I believe Christ stands at the gate personally, employing no servant, does that change my relationship with Him? If I believe "whatever principle of intelligence we attain unto in this life, it will rise with us" — does that change what I spend my time learning?

**Where am I in the restoration principle?**
Alma 41:13 — "merciful for that which is merciful; just for that which is just." What am I cultivating? What would be restored to me *today* if the restoration happened this afternoon? Not what I know — what I *am*. Elder Oaks said it: "The Final Judgment is not just an evaluation of what we have *done*. It is an acknowledgment of what we have *become*."

### This Week

- **Pray differently.** Each morning, acknowledge that mortality is a school — not in those words, but in that posture. "What are you teaching me today?" instead of "Help me get through today."

- **Write down what you'd say.** Pick one friend who is sick or hurting. Before you see them or call them, write down — on paper — what you would actually say if they asked, "What happens when I die?" Not a doctrinal essay. Three or four sentences. The Spirit will refine the words. But start with something real.

- **Practice the restoration principle on yourself.** At the end of each day, ask: "What was restored to me today? What did I send out — patience or irritation, curiosity or indifference, love or distraction — and what came back?" This is Alma's cosmic echo in miniature: "intelligence cleaveth unto intelligence; mercy hath compassion on mercy and claimeth her own" ([D&C 88:40](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)).

- **Do one temple session this month with the plan in mind.** Walk through the endowment and consciously notice the architecture: fallen state → covenants → veil → celestial room. You've traced this journey across six phases of study. Now *walk* it. Let the symbols land differently.

### The North Star

This entire study keeps circling back to one Person. Christ at the council. Christ in the creation. Christ in the garden teaching Adam. Christ on the cross promising paradise to the thief. Christ in the spirit world declaring liberty. Christ as the resurrection itself. Christ at the gate, employing no servant.

The plan of salvation is not a system. It is a *relationship* — with the Father who designed it, and with the Son who enacted it, suffered for it, and stands at every transition point within it. "I am the way, the truth, and the life: no man cometh unto the Father, but by me" ([John 14:6](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/john/14.md)).

Every study I do, every lesson I prepare, every word of comfort I offer — does it point to Him? The user's north star: *"I seek to always point to the Savior who made all things, and all things testify of Him."* This study testifies of Him. May my life do the same.

**Connected to:** [becoming/charity.md](../../becoming/charity.md) — Charity is "the pure love of Christ." The comfort thread across all six phases is an exercise in charity: learning to see sick and grieving friends as Christ sees them — not as problems to solve but as souls on a journey the Father designed, the Son consecrated with His own blood, and walks with them through. The practice of praying to "see others as Christ sees them" is the becoming companion to everything in the comfort section.
