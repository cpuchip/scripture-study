# Phase 6: Kingdoms of Glory & Eternal Life

*Plan of Salvation Study — Phase 6 of 8*
*Date: February 20, 2026*

---

## The Big Question

What are we actually heading toward? Phases 1–5 have traced the journey from pre-mortal councils through mortality, death, the spirit world, vicarious work, resurrection, and judgment. Now comes the destination. What does it look like when we arrive? And what does it mean that there are *multiple* destinations?

---

## 1. "In My Father's House Are Many Mansions"

Before revealing the three kingdoms, Jesus Himself prepared the ground:

> "In my Father's house are many **mansions**: if it were not so, I would have told you. I go to prepare a place for you." — [John 14:2](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/john/14.md)

**Webster 1828** defines *mansion* as simply "a dwelling place." Not a grand palace — a *dwelling*. Jesus is telling Thomas: there is a place prepared for you. Many dwelling-places, many resting-points, in the Father's house.

The very next breath is the declaration studied in the [way-truth-life](../way-truth-life.md) document: "I am the way, the truth, and the life: no man cometh unto the Father, but by me" ([John 14:6](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/john/14.md)). The mansions exist. Christ is the way into them.

**Webster 1828** also notes a secondary meaning: "the place in the heavens occupied each day by the moon in its monthly revolution." A heavenly station. Given the sun-moon-stars imagery of D&C 76, this feels more than coincidental.

## 2. The Vision — D&C 76: Three Kingdoms Distinguished

Joseph Smith and Sidney Rigdon saw what no Christian theologian since the apostles had seen: the afterlife is not a binary of heaven and hell. It is a graduated, merciful architecture of glory.

### Sons of Perdition (v. 31–49) — Outside the System

Before describing the kingdoms of glory, the Vision shows those who fall *outside* them:
- They "knew my power, and have been made partakers thereof" ([D&C 76:31](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/76.md))
- They "denied the Holy Spirit after having received it" (v. 35)
- They are "the only ones on whom the second death shall have any power" (v. 37)
- They are "the only ones who shall not be redeemed in the due time of the Lord" (v. 38)

This is not hell for the wicked. This is the *refusal* of one who stood in the full light and then turned away. It requires perfect knowledge perfectly rejected.

**For all the rest:** "All the rest shall be brought forth by the resurrection of the dead, through the triumph and the glory of the Lamb" ([D&C 76:39](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/76.md)). He "saves all the works of his hands, except those sons of perdition" (v. 43).

### Celestial Glory (v. 50–70) — The Sun

| Qualifier | Source |
|-----------|--------|
| Received testimony of Jesus | v. 51 |
| Baptized after manner of His burial | v. 51 |
| Kept commandments to be washed and cleansed | v. 52 |
| Received Holy Spirit by laying on of hands | v. 52 |
| Overcome by faith | v. 53 |
| Sealed by the Holy Spirit of Promise | v. 53 |

Their inheritance:
- "Church of the Firstborn" (v. 54) — not just the Church on earth, but the family of God
- "Priests and kings, who have received of his fulness, and of his glory" (v. 56)
- "They are gods, even the sons of God" (v. 58)
- "All things are theirs" (v. 59)
- "Dwell in the presence of God and his Christ forever and ever" (v. 62)
- "Just men made perfect through Jesus the mediator of the new covenant" (v. 69)
- Glory "of the sun, even the glory of God, the highest of all" (v. 70)

That phrase — "just men made *perfect*" — connects directly to Phase 5's restoration principle ([Alma 41:13](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/41.md)). They become what Christ's atonement enables them to become.

### Terrestrial Glory (v. 71–80) — The Moon

| Who They Are | Source |
|-------------|--------|
| Died without law | v. 72 |
| Spirits in prison who heard the gospel but received it afterward | v. 73–74 |
| Honorable men of the earth, blinded by craftiness | v. 75 |
| Not valiant in the testimony of Jesus | v. 79 |

Their inheritance:
- "Receive of his glory, but not of his fulness" (v. 76)
- "Receive of the presence of the Son, but not of the fulness of the Father" (v. 77)
- "Obtain not the crown over the kingdom" (v. 79)

This is a remarkable category. These are not wicked people. They are *good, honorable* people who either never had the full gospel or who had it but weren't *valiant* — who believed but held back. They receive the *presence of Christ* but not the fulness of the Father. Still glorious. Still far beyond anything they could have imagined.

The word **valiant** here is key. It's not about perfection — it's about wholehearted engagement. The terrestrial are those who kept one foot on the covenant path and one somewhere else.

### Telestial Glory (v. 81–112) — The Stars

| Who They Are | Source |
|-------------|--------|
| Received not gospel nor testimony of Jesus | v. 82 |
| Did not deny the Holy Spirit | v. 83 |
| Thrust down to hell | v. 84 |
| Not redeemed until last resurrection | v. 85 |
| Liars, sorcerers, adulterers, whoremongers | v. 103 |

Their inheritance:
- Receive Holy Spirit through ministration of terrestrial → which receives through celestial (v. 86–87)
- "Heirs of salvation" (v. 88)
- **"The glory of the telestial, which surpasses all understanding"** (v. 89)
- "Innumerable as the stars in the firmament of heaven, or as the sand upon the seashore" (v. 109)
- "They shall be servants of the Most High; but where God and Christ dwell they cannot come, worlds without end" (v. 112)

Two extraordinary facts here:

**First:** Even the telestial glory "surpasses all understanding." This isn't damnation as Christianity typically imagines it. It is a kingdom of glory, just not the fulness.

**Second:** They are still called "heirs of salvation" (v. 88). The word saves them — it just doesn't exalt them.

**A cascading ministry:** Celestial → terrestrial → telestial → angels ministering to the telestial (v. 86–88). The light flows downward from God through every layer. No one is abandoned.

## 3. The Law Framework — D&C 88:17–40

Phase 5 explored how "the glory by which your bodies are quickened" determines your kingdom ([D&C 88:28](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)). Phase 6 sees the other side of this: the *law* that determines the glory.

> "He who is not able to **abide** the law of a celestial kingdom cannot abide a celestial glory." — [D&C 88:22](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)

**Webster 1828** defines *abide*: "to stay; to continue in a place; to have one's abode; to dwell." Not merely tolerate — *make your home there*. Can you live in this law? Can you dwell in it comfortably? Then that is your kingdom.

President Oaks reinforced this: "The kingdom of glory we receive in the Final Judgment is determined by the laws we choose to follow in our mortal journey. Under that loving plan, there are multiple kingdoms—many mansions—so that all of God's children will inherit a kingdom of glory whose laws they can comfortably 'abide'" ([Kingdoms of Glory](../../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2023/10/17oaks.md), Oct 2023).

The framework descends like a staircase:

| Law Abided | Glory Received | Cannot Abide... |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|
| Celestial law | Celestial glory | — |
| Terrestrial law | Terrestrial glory | Celestial glory |
| Telestial law | Telestial glory | Terrestrial glory |
| No law | No kingdom of glory | Any glory |

And then one of the most beautiful verses in all of revelation:

> "For what doth it profit a man if a gift is bestowed upon him, and he receive not the gift? Behold, he rejoices not in that which is given unto him, neither rejoices in him who is the giver of the gift." — [D&C 88:33](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)

This is not punishment. It's *capacity*. You receive what you can receive. God isn't withholding — He's giving everything you're willing to take.

### The Earth Itself Demonstrates the Principle

> "The earth abideth the law of a celestial kingdom, for it filleth the measure of its creation, and transgresseth not the law." — [D&C 88:25](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)

The earth obeys perfectly. It will be sanctified, die, be quickened, and become a celestial sphere — "crowned with glory, even with the presence of God the Father" (v. 19). It will become "a great Urim and Thummim" ([D&C 130:9](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/130.md)). The very planet will be a crystal instrument of revelation for its celestialized inhabitants.

The earth keeps the law. It receives the glory. The principle is cosmic, not just personal.

### Intelligence Cleaveth unto Intelligence

> "Intelligence cleaveth unto intelligence; wisdom receiveth wisdom; truth embraceth truth; virtue loveth virtue; light cleaveth unto light." — [D&C 88:40](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)

Like attracts like. This is why the kingdoms are self-sorting. You go where you *belong* — not by arbitrary assignment but by the gravity of what you've become. This connects directly to Phase 5's insight about self-judgment: "They are their own judges" ([Alma 41:7](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/41.md)).

## 4. Three Levels Within the Celestial — D&C 131

Even within the highest glory, there is structure:

> "In the celestial glory there are three heavens or degrees." — [D&C 131:1](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/131.md)

> "And in order to obtain the highest, a man must enter into this order of the priesthood [meaning the new and everlasting covenant of marriage]." — [D&C 131:2](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/131.md)

> "And if he does not, he cannot obtain it. He may enter into the other, but that is the end of his kingdom; he cannot have an **increase**." — [D&C 131:3–4](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/131.md)

**Webster 1828** defines *increase*: "To multiply by the production of young; to be fertile, fruitful, or prolific." This isn't just "more blessings" in some vague sense. It means eternal offspring. Eternal family. The continuation of life itself.

Without the marriage covenant, one can enter celestial glory but cannot have *increase*. D&C 132 makes this explicit: those who do not enter the everlasting covenant "remain separately and singly, without exaltation, in their saved condition, to all eternity; and from henceforth are not gods, but are angels of God forever and ever" ([D&C 132:17](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/132.md)).

This is the sharpest line in the plan: saved, yes. Celestial, even. But not exalted. Exaltation is reserved for those who covenant with another soul and with God.

## 5. Immortality vs. Eternal Life — The Critical Distinction

Moses heard the Father's mission statement:

> "For behold, this is my work and my glory — to bring to pass the **immortality** and **eternal life** of man." — [Moses 1:39](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/1.md)

Two words. Two gifts. Not the same thing.

**Webster 1828:**
- *Immortality* = "The quality or state of being immortal; exemption from death and annihilation; unending existence." The dictionary entry even quotes Paul: "This mortal must put on immortality" (1 Cor. 15:53).
- *Salvation* = "The redemption of man from the bondage of sin and liability to eternal death, and the conferring on him of everlasting happiness."

President Spencer W. Kimball drew the line as clearly as anyone ever has:

> "If we are true and faithful, we shall rise, **not alone in immortality but unto eternal life**. Immortality is to live forever in an assigned kingdom. Eternal life is to gain exaltation in the highest heaven and live in the family unit." — [An Eternal Hope in Christ](../../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/1978/10/an-eternal-hope-in-christ.md), Oct 1978

| Gift | Who Receives It | What It Means |
|------|----------------|---------------|
| **Immortality** | All resurrected beings (every person) | Unending existence; exemption from death; the free gift through Christ's Atonement |
| **Salvation** | All who inherit a kingdom of glory | Redemption from sin and hell; deliverance from the second death |
| **Eternal Life** | Those who receive exaltation (highest celestial) | To know God, to live as God lives, to have eternal family, to receive all the Father has |

Immortality is universal. Eternal life is conditional — not on perfection, but on covenant and transformation.

## 6. What Exaltation Actually Is — D&C 132:19–24

This is the most detailed description of exaltation in all scripture:

> "If a man marry a wife by my word, which is my law, and by the new and everlasting covenant, and it is sealed unto them by the Holy Spirit of promise ... they shall pass by the angels, and the gods, which are set there, to their **exaltation** and glory in all things ... which glory shall be a fulness and a **continuation of the seeds** forever and ever." — [D&C 132:19](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/132.md)

> "Then shall they be **gods**, because they have no end; therefore shall they be from everlasting to everlasting, because they continue; then shall they be above all, because all things are subject unto them. Then shall they be gods, because they have **all power**, and the angels are subject unto them." — [D&C 132:20](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/132.md)

**Webster 1828** defines *exaltation*: "The act of raising high; the state of being exalted; elevation." But note the second definition: "The refinement or subtilization of a body, or the increasing of its virtue or principal property." Exaltation is not just altitude — it is *refinement*. The perfecting of what something already is.

And then this — the verse that ties the entire study together:

> "This is **eternal lives** — to know the only wise and true God, and Jesus Christ, whom he hath sent. I am he. Receive ye, therefore, my law." — [D&C 132:24](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/132.md)

Note the plural: *eternal lives*. Not just duration but *generativity*. The [know-god study](../know-god.md) explored this in depth: eternal life IS knowing God. Not knowing *about* Him. Knowing Him — His character, His heart, His attributes. D&C 132 adds the generative dimension: this knowing produces *lives*.

### The Intercessory Prayer Confirms It

In the same discourse where He promised "many mansions," Jesus prayed:

> "And this is life eternal, that they might **know** thee the only true God, and Jesus Christ, whom thou hast sent." — [John 17:3](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/john/17.md)

And then:

> "That they all may be **one**; as thou, Father, art in me, and I in thee, that they also may be one in us." — [John 17:21](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/john/17.md)

Exaltation is not absorption. It is *unity*. Father and Son are distinct persons, yet perfectly one. The exalted enter that same unity — distinct, individual, yet one in glory, purpose, and love.

## 7. What Surprised Joseph — D&C 137

Joseph received this vision in the Kirtland Temple, January 1836 — years before the revelations on baptism for the dead:

> "I saw ... my brother **Alvin**, that has long since slept; And **marveled** how it was that he had obtained an inheritance in that kingdom, seeing that he had departed this life before the Lord had set his hand to gather Israel the second time, and had not been baptized for the remission of sins." — [D&C 137:5–6](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/137.md)

Joseph's surprise is profound. He *knew* baptism was essential. How could Alvin be in the celestial kingdom without it? The Lord's answer:

> "All who have died without a knowledge of this gospel, who **would have received it** if they had been permitted to tarry, shall be heirs of the celestial kingdom of God; Also all that shall die henceforth without a knowledge of it, who would have received it with all their hearts, shall be heirs of that kingdom." — [D&C 137:7–8](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/137.md)

And the governing principle:

> "For I, the Lord, will judge all men according to their works, according to the **desire of their hearts**." — [D&C 137:9](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/137.md)

And one more:

> "All children who die before they arrive at the years of accountability are **saved in the celestial kingdom** of heaven." — [D&C 137:10](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/137.md)

This vision does something extraordinary to the entire plan: it makes it *fair*. Not fair as in "everyone gets the same thing" but fair as in "no one is lost because of circumstances beyond their control." God reads hearts. He knows what you would have done. And He honors it.

Phase 4 showed us the *mechanism* (vicarious ordinances). Phase 6 shows us the *principle*: God judges by desire, not just by access.

## 8. The Temple as Enacted Plan

The [way-truth-life study](../way-truth-life.md) mapped this connection in detail, but it's worth summarizing here because Phase 6 completes the picture:

| Temple Space | Kingdom | Christ's Declaration | Experience |
|-------------|---------|---------------------|------------|
| Outer Court | Telestial | "I am the **Way**" | Begin the journey; altar of sacrifice |
| Holy Place | Terrestrial | "I am the **Truth**" | Receive light, instruction, covenants |
| Holy of Holies | Celestial | "I am the **Life**" | Enter God's presence; eternal life |

The endowment physically enacts the plan of salvation. You begin in a fallen state. You receive covenants. You pass through the veil — which is Christ's flesh ([Hebrews 10:20](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/heb/10.md)). You enter the celestial room — symbolic of the Father's presence.

The temple doesn't just *teach* the plan. It lets you *walk* it.

## 9. What the Know-God Study Adds

The [know-god study](../know-god.md) established that eternal life = knowing God (John 17:3), and that:

- **The Lectures on Faith** require three things for saving faith: (1) that God exists, (2) a correct idea of His character and attributes, (3) assurance that our life course accords with His will
- **D&C 93:1** promises that "every soul" who forsakes sin, comes to Christ, and keeps His commandments "shall see my face and know that I am"
- **D&C 93:12–13, 20** shows even Christ grew "from grace to grace" — and we are invited to the same progression: "you shall receive grace for grace"
- **Lecture Fifth** says the saints "are to be partakers of the same fulness" — heirs of God and joint heirs with Christ through the same Spirit

The kingdoms of glory, then, are not arbitrary reward tiers. They describe *degrees of knowing God*:

| Kingdom | Degree of Knowing |
|---------|------------------|
| Telestial | Receive Holy Spirit through intermediaries (D&C 76:86–88) — indirect knowledge |
| Terrestrial | Receive presence of the Son, but not fulness of Father (D&C 76:77) — partial knowledge |
| Celestial | Dwell in presence of God and Christ forever; know as they are known (D&C 76:62, 94) — fulness |

Eternal life isn't a *location*. It's a *relationship*. Exaltation is the fulness of that relationship — to know God so completely that you participate in His nature, His work, and His love.

## 10. Sociality Continued

One more gem from Joseph:

> "That same **sociality** which exists among us here will exist among us there, only it will be coupled with **eternal glory**, which glory we do not now enjoy." — [D&C 130:2](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/130.md)

> "Whatever principle of **intelligence** we attain unto in this life, it will rise with us in the resurrection." — [D&C 130:18](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/130.md)

The relationships, the connections, the intelligence, the love — none of it is wasted. Everything you learn, everything you become, every relationship you build in righteousness — it goes with you. It *rises* with you.

This is why the plan is a school (Phase 2's central insight). The education isn't thrown away at graduation. It IS the diploma.

## 11. President Nelson's Summary

President Russell M. Nelson captured the whole framework in a single paragraph, quoted by President Oaks:

> "Mortal lifetime is barely a nanosecond compared with eternity. But what a crucial nanosecond it is! Consider carefully how it works: During this mortal life you get to choose which laws you are willing to obey—those of the celestial kingdom, or the terrestrial, or the telestial—and, therefore, in which kingdom of glory you will live forever. What a plan! It is a plan that completely honors *your* agency." — quoted in [Kingdoms of Glory](../../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2023/10/17oaks.md), Oct 2023

And President Oaks added: "Salvation is an individual matter, but exaltation is a family matter."

## 12. Comfort Thread — For Friends Who Are Sick

Phase 6 adds these promises to our growing collection:

1. **Every person inherits a kingdom of glory** — except the rarest of exceptions, "all the children of God ... will ultimately inherit one of three kingdoms of glory, even the least of which 'surpasses all understanding'" (President Oaks, [Kingdoms of Glory](../../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2023/10/17oaks.md))

2. **God judges by the desire of your heart** — "I, the Lord, will judge all men according to their works, according to the desire of their hearts" ([D&C 137:9](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/137.md)). He knows what you would have done.

3. **All children are saved** — "All children who die before they arrive at the years of accountability are saved in the celestial kingdom of heaven" ([D&C 137:10](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/137.md))

4. **The gift is offered, not withheld** — "For what doth it profit a man if a gift is bestowed upon him, and he receive not the gift?" ([D&C 88:33](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)). God is offering everything. He never withholds.

5. **Your relationships continue** — "That same sociality which exists among us here will exist among us there, only it will be coupled with eternal glory" ([D&C 130:2](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/130.md))

6. **Nothing you learn or become is lost** — "Whatever principle of intelligence we attain unto in this life, it will rise with us in the resurrection" ([D&C 130:18](../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/130.md))

7. **Every blessing and advantage your choices allow** — "We have a loving Heavenly Father who will see that we receive every blessing and every advantage that our own desires and choices allow" (President Oaks, [Kingdoms of Glory](../../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2023/10/17oaks.md))

8. **The celestial marriage covenant will be available to all the faithful** — "Exaltation can be attained only through faithfulness to the covenants of an eternal marriage ... which marriage will **ultimately be available to all the faithful**" (President Oaks, [Kingdoms of Glory](../../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/2023/10/17oaks.md))
