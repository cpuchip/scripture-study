**Headlines**

**OpenAI ships GPT-5.5 and GPT-5.5 Instant.** OpenAI released GPT-5.5 alongside a faster variant, GPT-5.5 Instant, which is now the default model in ChatGPT. The company claims the new models reduce hallucinations, improve personalization, and deliver stronger coding performance at lower cost than competing frontier models. [Introducing GPT-5.5](https://openai.com/index/introducing-gpt-5-5/) | [GPT-5.5 Instant](https://openai.com/index/gpt-5-5-instant/). *Attribution note: Primary announcement pages were blocked during verification; summary compiled from indexed secondary reporting. **Microsoft Research open-sources Webwright.** The team released Webwright, a terminal-native web agent framework that treats the browser as a disposable runtime rather than a persistent environment. Instead of predicting individual clicks, the agent writes Playwright code, tests it, and iterates in a local workspace. On the Odysseys benchmark, it scored 60.1%, up from a 33.5% baseline for GPT-5.4. [GitHub repo](https://github.com/microsoft/Webwright) | [report](https://aigeneratorreviews.com/microsoft-research-releases-webwright-a-terminal-native-web-agent-framework-that-scores-60-1-on-odysseys-up-from-base-gpt-5-4s-33-5/).

**Google begins rolling out Gemini Spark.** The 24/7 cloud-based personal agent announced at I/O is now in the hands of trusted testers, with a public beta planned for U.S. Google AI Ultra subscribers next week. Google describes it as the next evolution of the Gemini app, designed to deliver proactive help continuously. [Next evolution of the Gemini app](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/products/gemini-app/next-evolution-gemini-app/).
