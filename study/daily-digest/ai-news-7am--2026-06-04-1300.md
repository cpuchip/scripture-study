**Headlines**

**Microsoft ships MAI-Thinking-1, a new reasoning model.** [MAI-Thinking-1](https://microsoft.ai/news/introducing-mai-thinking-1/) is a 35B-active sparse mixture-of-experts that Microsoft says was trained without third-party distillation. The company claims 97.0% on AIME 2025 and 94.5% on AIME 2026, plus a blind internal preference win over Claude Sonnet 4.6. If independent labs corroborate those numbers, it signals that Microsoft's native model stack has reached parity with frontier closed labs.

**OpenAI expands Codex beyond engineering.** [New plugins, Annotations, and hosted "Sites"](https://openai.com/index/codex-for-every-role-tool-workflow/) extend Codex into documents, spreadsheets, and interactive workspace apps. The strategic framing positions it as a horizontal SaaS orchestration tool; the unaudited claim that one-fifth of weekly active users are now non-coding knowledge workers is part of that narrative.

**Google releases Gemma 4 12B, an encoder-free multimodal model.** [Gemma 4 12B](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/developers-tools/introducing-gemma-4-12b/) projects raw audio and image signals directly into the text token space, eliminating separate vision and audio pipelines. Google says it nears the performance of its own 26B model, which—if confirmed—would make high-quality local multimodal agents viable on consumer hardware.

- **GPT-Rosalind** gained medicinal-chemistry and genomics reasoning and expanded global trusted-access eligibility, though it remains gated. [Introducing new capabilities to GPT-Rosalind](https://openai.com/index/introducing-new-capabilities-to-gpt-rosalind/)

**Skeptical takes**

Virtually every benchmark today is first-party and unchecked. Microsoft's AIME scores, Google's "nears 26B" claim, and Meta's "infinite team" rhetoric all came from the same marketing departments shipping the products. Scout's "always-on" promise is further blunted by a rollout limited to a small Frontier cohort with heavy IT attestation. Holo3.1's claim of "little to no degradation" under quantization and its cited ~2× speedup were measured on NVIDIA DGX Spark; your laptop will vary.

**Carry-forward**

Watch for independent evaluations of MAI-Thinking-1 and Gemma 4 12B. If either survives third-party testing, it warrants a deep-research run. Also track whether Scout expands beyond Frontier customers or remains a configured demo.
