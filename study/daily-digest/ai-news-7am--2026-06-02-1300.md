## AI Daily Digest — June 2, 2026

### Headlines

**NVIDIA shipped Cosmos 3, an open foundation model for physical AI.** NVIDIA released Cosmos 3, calling it the first fully open "omnimodel" that natively handles text, images, video, sound, and action prediction for physical AI. The concrete drop matters more than the keynote: model weights (Nano 16B and Super 64B) are on Hugging Face, inference is available via NVIDIA NIM microservices, Diffusers integration is included, and post-training scripts are on GitHub under the OpenMDW 1.1 license. Whether the "big bang of physical AI is just around the corner" or not, the weights and tooling are live now. [NVIDIA Launches Cosmos 3](https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/news/nvidia-launches-cosmos-3-the-open-frontier-foundation-model-for-physical-ai)

**NVIDIA followed with Alpamayo 2 Super, a 32B open model for robotaxi reasoning.** A day later, NVIDIA added Alpamayo 2 Super, a 32-billion-parameter reasoning vision-language-action model aimed at Level 4 autonomous driving. It adds surround-view perception and outputs meta-actions for planning. Alongside it, NVIDIA open-sourced AlpaGym, a closed-loop RL training framework with GRPO support at launch, and OmniDreams, a generative world model for AV scenario generation. The claim that this is "the moment cars begin to safely reason, not just drive" is polished marketing; the substantive signal is the open RL stack and model weights for AV developers. [NVIDIA Alpamayo 2 Super](https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/news/nvidia-alpamayo-2-super-robotaxis)

**MiniMax claims 1M-token context with new M3 coding model.** Chinese startup MiniMax released M3, a frontier multimodal coding model built on a new MiniMax Sparse Attention (MSA) architecture. The company reports a 1-million-token context window with a 9× prefill speedup at that scale, plus native image, video, and desktop operation support. The API is live; open weights are promised within ten days. Self-reported benchmarks place it above GPT-5.5 and Gemini 3.1 Pro on SWE-Bench Pro, but those results used Claude Code scaffolding and have not been independently verified. [MiniMax M3](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/06/01/minimax-releases-minimax-m3-with-msa-architecture-supporting-1m-token-context-native-multimodality-and-agentic-coding/)

### Notable

- **JetBrains open-sourced Mellum2**, a 12B-parameter MoE model (2.5B active) under Apache 2.0, positioned as a fast "focal model" for routing, RAG, and sub-agents inside larger pipelines. Weights and the technical report (arXiv 2605.31268) are already on Hugging Face. [Mellum2 Launch](https://huggingface.co/blog/JetBrains/mellum2-launch)

- **Alibaba's Qwen3.7-Plus adds vision and tool use** on the Bailian platform, bundling deep reasoning, self-programming, verification, and autonomous iteration. Pricing and context-window specs have not yet been published. [Qwen3.7-Plus](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/06/02/alibabas-qwen-team-launches-qwen3-7-plus-adding-vision-deep-reasoning-tool-invocation-and-autonomous-iteration-on-the-bailian-platform/)

- **ZeroDrift raised $10M** for a deterministic-rules-first compliance layer that uses conventional programs to flag violations and only invokes an LLM to rewrite flagged outputs. The architectural idea—deterministic guardrails with generative rewrite as a second stage—reflects growing enterprise demand for governance infrastructure between raw model output and end users. [ZeroDrift](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/02/zerodrift-raises-10-million-to-protect-ai-models-from-themselves/)

### Skeptical Takes

- Jensen Huang's "big bang of physical AI" framing is standard GTC hyperbole. Cosmos 3 and Alpamayo 2 are real shipments, but the distance between open weights and deployed, safe physical AI remains significant.
- MiniMax's headline benchmark claims are self-reported with Claude Code scaffolding; independent evaluators typically see lower scores under neutral conditions.
- ZeroDrift's fundraising narrative ("oversubscribed by 3x") is seed-stage theater. The architectural concept is more interesting than the round.

### Carry-Forward

- **Watch for:** Independent replication of MiniMax M3's 1M-token prefill speedup and SWE-Bench Pro scores. If open weights ship on schedule, eval labs should publish neutral results within days.
- **Deep-research candidate:** **MiniMax M3's MSA architecture.** If the 1M-context speedup claim holds under independent test, it could materially change the cost structure of long-context coding agents. A focused technical read is warranted once the weights and paper are available.
