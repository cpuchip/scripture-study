## AI Daily Digest — June 9, 2026

**Headlines**

Anthropic released Claude Fable 5 and Claude Mythos 5, making its Mythos-class models available to the public for the first time. Fable 5 is the consumer-facing version, equipped with safety classifiers; Anthropic claims it is the most capable model it has ever released to general users. The unrestricted Mythos 5 remains available only to existing Project Glasswing cybersecurity partners. [Claude Fable 5 and Claude Mythos 5](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-fable-5-mythos-5)

Google shipped Gemini 3.5 Live Translate, a continuous speech-to-speech translation model supporting more than 70 languages. Unlike conventional systems that pause for the speaker to finish before translating, the model renders speech continuously while trying to preserve accuracy without falling behind the speaker. It is rolling out today to Google Translate mobile apps, the Gemini Live API, and a private preview in Google Meet. [Fluid, natural voice translation with Gemini 3.5 Live Translate](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/models-and-research/gemini-models/gemini-live-3-5-translate/)

**Notable**

- Cohere open-sourced North Mini Code, a 30B-parameter mixture-of-experts agentic coding model with 3B active parameters, released under Apache 2.0 on Hugging Face and via the Cohere API. It is Cohere's first open-source developer model and the first in its next-generation series. [North Mini Code: Agentic Coding Model for Developers](https://cohere.com/blog/north-mini-code)

- OpenAI is expanding GPT-5.5 Instant's personalization improvements—drawing on past chats, uploaded files, and connected Gmail—to ChatGPT Go and free-tier users, having initially rolled them out to paid tiers. Free-tier responses will use a smaller history of past chats. [GPT-5.5 Instant: smarter, clearer, and more personalized](https://openai.com/index/gpt-5-5-instant/)

- OpenAI launched the Economic Research Exchange, a structured program inviting external researchers to apply for privacy-protected collaborations using OpenAI data to study AI's economic effects on labor, productivity, and firms. Chosen researchers will carry out structured, project-based work with OpenAI's Economic Research team. [Introducing the OpenAI Economic Research Exchange](https://openai.com/index/economic-research-exchange/)
