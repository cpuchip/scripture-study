## AI Daily Digest — May 25, 2026

### Headlines

**OpenAI model disproves 80-year-old Erdős conjecture.** An OpenAI reasoning model produced a 125-page proof disproving the Erdős planar unit distance conjecture from 1946, a discrete geometry problem that had stood for eight decades. Fields medalist Tim Gowers and Princeton mathematician Will Sawin reportedly reviewed and endorsed the result, a rare case of AI-generated mathematics receiving external peer validation. OpenAI framed it as the first autonomous solution to a prominent open field problem, though details of any human scaffolding remain undisclosed. [OpenAI announcement](https://openai.com/index/model-disproves-discrete-geometry-conjecture/)

**[Still Trending] DeepMind’s AlphaProof Nexus closes nine open Erdős problems.** Google DeepMind’s system, which pairs a large language model with the Lean formal proof assistant, autonomously solved 9 of 353 open Erdős problems and proved 44 of 492 open OEIS conjectures. The underlying paper was published on arXiv May 21 and continues to circulate in research channels. Secondary coverage claimed the runs cost only a few hundred dollars per problem, but that figure has not been verified against the primary paper. [Glitchwire coverage](https://glitchwire.com/news/google-deepminds-ai-cracks-9-long-open-math-problems-for-hundreds-of-dollars-eac/)

**Alibaba’s Qwen3.7-Max completes 35-hour autonomous kernel optimization.** Alibaba shipped the Qwen3.7-Max API, a proprietary model tuned for agentic coding, and demonstrated it operating nonstop for roughly 35 hours to optimize a Triton kernel for an Alibaba custom AI accelerator. The model reportedly issued 1,158 tool calls, compiled and benchmarked code iteratively, and achieved a claimed 10× speedup over the reference implementation. The benchmark suite is homegrown and the run has not been independently reproduced. [The Decoder](https://the-decoder.com/alibabas-latest-ai-model-ran-autonomously-for-35-hours-to-optimize-code-for-its-own-custom-chip/)

### Notable

- **NVIDIA** released [Gated DeltaNet-2](https://github.com/NVlabs/GatedDeltaNet-2), an open-source linear attention architecture that splits memory editing into channel-wise erase and write gates. A 1.3B-parameter model trained on 100B FineWeb-Edu tokens outperformed Mamba-2, KDA, and Mamba-3 on the RULER long-context retrieval benchmark.
- **Fujitsu** announced a [self-evolving multi-agent enterprise system](https://global.fujitsu/en-global/pr/news/2026/05/25-01) that updates its own prompts and evaluation criteria from operational feedback. It reported a 28-point average accuracy gain on domain-specific LLM tasks, but the benchmarks are unspecified and self-reported.

### Skeptical takes

The Qwen and Fujitsu claims rest on vendor-built benchmarks and internal testing; treat them as directional R&D, not validated product claims. The DeepMind cost narrative appears only in secondary coverage, not the arXiv paper itself. OpenAI’s “autonomously solved” framing should be weighed against whatever human prompting, problem formulation, or verification loops supported the work.
