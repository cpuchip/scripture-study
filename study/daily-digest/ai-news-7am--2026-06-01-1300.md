**AI Daily Digest — June 1, 2026**

---

### Headlines

**NVIDIA open-sources a world model and a robotaxi reasoning model.** NVIDIA launched [Cosmos 3](https://investor.nvidia.com/news/press-release-details/2026/NVIDIA-Launches-Cosmos-3-the-Open-Frontier-Foundation-Model-for-Physical-AI/default.aspx), an open world foundation model that unifies vision reasoning, world generation, and action prediction in a single mixture-of-transformers system for robotics and autonomous-vehicle development. Alongside it, the company shipped [Alpamayo 2 Super](https://investor.nvidia.com/news/press-release-details/2026/NVIDIA-Launches-Alpamayo-2-Super-Open-Reasoning-Model-for-Robotaxis/default.aspx), a 32-billion-parameter open reasoning vision-language-action model built to reason, plan, and act across the full Level 4 robotaxi stack. Together, the releases double down on NVIDIA's bet that generative world models become core infrastructure for physical AI, not just a research curiosity.

**NVIDIA bundles a 550B-parameter MoE into an enterprise agent toolkit.** NVIDIA also announced its [Agent Toolkit](https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/news/enterprise-software-leaders-build-ai-agents-with-nvidia), built around the 550-billion-parameter Nemotron 3 Ultra MoE model, plus NemoClaw blueprints and the OpenShell secure runtime for enterprise-grade agents. NVIDIA claims up to 5× faster inference and 30% lower cost versus comparable open frontier models, though those figures come from the company's own benchmarks.

**Anthropic ships Opus 4.8 and raises $65 billion.** Anthropic released [Claude Opus 4.8](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-opus-4-8), an incremental upgrade with improvements to coding, agentic reliability, and honesty, alongside dynamic workflows for Claude Code and user-controllable effort settings—at the same price. Simultaneously, the company announced a [$65 billion Series H](https://www.anthropic.com/news/series-h) at a $965 billion post-money valuation, reporting a $47 billion run-rate revenue. The round was led by Altimeter Capital, Dragoneer, Greenoaks, and Sequoia Capital.

### Skeptical takes

- Nemotron 3 Ultra's performance and cost claims are [vendor benchmarks](https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/news/enterprise-software-leaders-build-ai-agents-with-nvidia); independent replication has not yet been shown.
- Anthropic's $965 billion valuation is a private-market figure, not a public market cap, and reflects late-stage financing optimism rather than liquid price discovery.
