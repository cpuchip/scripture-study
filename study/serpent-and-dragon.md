# The Serpent and the Dragon: Contested Symbol Across Every Age

*Date: 2026-02-19*

A study tracing the serpent through every age of the world — from the Garden to the dragon bound at the Millennium — and the startling reality that the serpent is a **contested symbol**. Both Christ and Satan claim it. The brazen serpent heals; the Garden serpent destroys. The rod of Moses becomes a serpent that swallows the serpents of the magicians. The scapegoat bears sins away; the goats on the left hand are cast out. How do we tell which serpent we're looking at? And how does the serpent *grow* into a dragon?

> **Related studies:** [Priestcraft: Satan's Counterfeit of Priesthood](priestcraft.md) · [Beguiled: The Serpent's Craft](priestcraft-beguile.md) · [End Times](end-times.md)

---

## Starting Questions

- Does the serpent show up in every dispensation — every thousand-year period the seals of Revelation represent?
- How does the serpent *grow* into the dragon of Revelation?
- Christ is also represented by a serpent (the brazen serpent, the staff of Moses). The goat represents both Christ (scapegoat) and the devil (goats on the left). How do we discern which is which?
- How do we fight the serpent in our age?

## The Contested Symbol

This is the first thing to establish, because without it, every serpent passage becomes confusing.

The serpent is **not** exclusively a symbol of Satan. Scripture uses the serpent to represent *both* the Savior and the adversary. This is not an accident — it's one of the deepest patterns in the symbolic language of the scriptures.

### Two Serpents, Two Directions

| | Satan's Serpent | Christ's Serpent |
|---|---|---|
| **First appearance** | "The serpent was more subtil" ([Genesis 3:1](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/gen/3.md)) | Moses' rod becomes a serpent ([Exodus 4:3](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/ex/4.md)) |
| **Direction** | Pulls DOWN — "leadeth them away carefully down to hell" ([2 Nephi 28:21](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/28.md)) | LIFTED UP — "set it upon a pole" ([Numbers 21:8](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/num/21.md)) |
| **Effect on agency** | Destroys it — "sought to destroy the agency of man" ([Moses 4:3](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/4.md)) | Requires it — "whosoever would look upon it might live" ([Alma 33:19](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/33.md)) |
| **Relationship to truth** | "The father of all lies" ([Moses 4:4](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/4.md)) | "He that doeth truth cometh to the light" ([John 3:21](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/john/3.md)) |
| **Who gets glory** | "Give me thine honor" ([Moses 4:1](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/4.md)) | "The glory be thine forever" ([Moses 4:2](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/4.md)) |
| **Result** | Captivity — "lead them captive at his will" ([Moses 4:4](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/4.md)) | Eternal life — "whosoever believeth in him should not perish" ([John 3:16](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/john/3.md)) |

### The Goat: Same Pattern

The goat shows the identical dual symbolism in the Day of Atonement ritual ([Leviticus 16](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/lev/16.md)):

- **The Lord's goat** — killed as a sin offering, its blood sprinkled on the mercy seat ([Leviticus 16:9, 15](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/lev/16.md)). This is Christ, whose blood makes atonement.
- **The scapegoat** — Aaron lays "both his hands upon the head of the live goat, and confess over him all the iniquities of the children of Israel... putting them upon the head of the goat" and sends it into the wilderness ([Leviticus 16:21–22](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/lev/16.md)). This is *also* Christ — the one who bears our sins away.

Both goats are Christ. Both point toward atonement. Yet in [Matthew 25:33](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/matt/25.md), Christ separates the sheep from the goats — the goats being the wicked who are cast out.

Same animal. Opposite meaning. The discernment comes from **where it points and what it does.**

### The Test: Which Serpent Is This?

When you encounter a serpent in scripture, ask:

1. **Is it lifted up or crawling?** The brazen serpent is on a *pole* — elevated, visible, pointing toward heaven. The Garden serpent crawls on its belly ([Genesis 3:14](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/gen/3.md)).
2. **Does it require your choice or remove it?** The brazen serpent requires you to *look*. The Garden serpent manipulates.
3. **Does it heal or harm?** "If a serpent had bitten any man, when he beheld the serpent of brass, he lived" ([Numbers 21:9](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/num/21.md)). The Garden serpent brought death.
4. **Who gets the credit?** Moses points to God. Satan says "give *me* thine honor."

## The Serpent Across the Ages

Here's what makes the user's question so compelling: the serpent doesn't just appear at the beginning and end. He shows up in *every* age — and he grows.

### Pre-Mortality: The Dragon in Heaven

Before the earth was even formed, John sees the serpent in heaven — but already as a **dragon**:

> "And there appeared another wonder in heaven; and behold a great red **dragon**, having seven heads and ten horns, and seven crowns upon his heads. And his tail drew the third part of the stars of heaven" — [Revelation 12:3–4](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rev/12.md)

> "And the great **dragon** was cast out, that **old serpent**, called the Devil, and Satan, which deceiveth the whole world" — [Revelation 12:9](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rev/12.md)

John's vision reveals something crucial: **the serpent was always a dragon.** Or rather, Revelation shows us his *true form*. In the Garden, he appeared as a serpent — subtle, small, a creature among creatures. But his nature was always that of the dragon.

The word "old" in "that old serpent" connects Revelation directly back to Genesis. This is the same being. He hasn't changed species — he's been *unmasked*.

### 1st Seal / The Garden (Adam's Dispensation): The Subtle Serpent

> "Now the serpent was more **subtil** than any beast of the field" — [Genesis 3:1](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/gen/3.md)

> "Satan put it into the heart of the serpent, (for he had drawn away many after him,) and he sought also to **beguile** Eve, for he knew not the mind of God, wherefore he sought to **destroy the world**" — [Moses 4:6](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/4.md)

In this age, the serpent operates through **subtlety** — craft, guile, insinuation. His method is individual, personal, conversational. One tempter, one target, one lie at a time. But even here, God announces the end of the story:

> "I will put enmity between thee and the woman, and between thy seed and her seed; and **he shall bruise thy head**, and thou shalt **bruise his heel**" — [Moses 4:21](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/4.md)

The seed of the woman will crush the serpent's head. The serpent can only bruise the heel. This is the first Messianic prophecy — *and it's given to the serpent himself*.

**Christ's serpent in this age:** Not yet manifested, but promised. The "seed of the woman" who will crush the serpent's head.

### 2nd–3rd Seals (Enoch → Noah): The Serpent Organizes

We don't see the word "serpent" explicitly in the Enoch or Noah narratives, but the *pattern* from the [beguile study](priestcraft-beguile.md) applies. Jacob connects beguiling to the next stage:

> "that being who **beguiled** our first parents, who **transformeth** himself nigh unto an angel of light, and stirreth up the children of men unto **secret combinations** of murder" — [2 Nephi 9:9](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/9.md)

The serpent's craft *institutionalizes*. From individual temptation (Eve) to organized evil (Cain's covenant with Satan, the secret combinations before the flood). The serpent goes from being a solitary deceiver to being the architect of systems.

Moroni traces this lineage explicitly:

> "it is built up by the devil, who is the father of all lies; even that same liar who **beguiled** our first parents" — [Ether 8:25](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/ether/8.md)

Same serpent. New method. He's *growing*.

### 4th Seal (Abraham → Moses): The Serpent and the Rod

This age gives us the most dense serpent symbolism in all of scripture — and the most dramatic contest between the two serpents.

**Moses' Rod Becomes a Serpent:**

> "And the Lord said unto him, What is that in thine hand? And he said, A rod. And he said, Cast it on the ground. And he cast it on the ground, and it became a **serpent**; and Moses fled from before it." — [Exodus 4:2–3](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/ex/4.md)

God's rod becomes a serpent. God commands Moses to pick it up — and it becomes a rod again. The serpent is *under God's authority*. This is Christ's serpent: powerful, but submitted to the hand of God.

**The Contest Before Pharaoh:**

> "Aaron cast down his rod before Pharaoh, and before his servants, and it became a serpent. Then Pharaoh also called the wise men and the sorcerers: now the magicians of Egypt, they also did in like manner with their enchantments. For they cast down every man his rod, and they became serpents: but **Aaron's rod swallowed up their rods**." — [Exodus 7:10–12](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/ex/7.md)

This is one of the most stunning serpent passages in scripture. The magicians can *imitate* the sign — their rods become serpents too. Pharaoh's serpents are the devil's serpents: counterfeit power, mimicry, sorcery. But Aaron's serpent — God's serpent — **swallows them all**.

The true serpent consumes the false ones. The real thing destroys the counterfeits.

**The Brazen Serpent — The Height of the Type:**

> "And the Lord said unto Moses, Make thee a fiery serpent, and set it upon a pole: and it shall come to pass, that every one that is bitten, when he looketh upon it, shall live." — [Numbers 21:8](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/num/21.md)

The people are bitten by fiery serpents — Satan's serpents, serpents of consequence and judgment. The cure is *another serpent* — God's serpent, **lifted up** on a pole.

Jesus himself claimed this type:

> "And as Moses lifted up the **serpent** in the wilderness, even so must the **Son of man** be **lifted up**: That whosoever believeth in him should not perish, but have eternal life." — [John 3:14–15](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/john/3.md)

Nephi saw the same connection:

> "gave unto Moses power that he should heal the nations after they had been bitten by the poisonous serpents, if they would cast their eyes unto the **serpent** which he did raise up before them… there is none other **name** given under heaven save it be this Jesus Christ, of which I have spoken, whereby man can be saved." — [2 Nephi 25:20](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/25.md)

And the Book of Mormon prophets made the type explicit:

> "as he lifted up the brazen serpent in the wilderness, even so shall he be lifted up who should come. And as many as should **look upon that serpent** should **live**, even so as many as should **look upon the Son of God** with faith, having a contrite spirit, might **live**, even unto that life which is eternal." — [Helaman 8:14–15](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/hel/8.md)

Alma adds the terrible human tendency:

> "a type was raised up in the wilderness, that whosoever would look upon it might live. And many did **look and live**. But few understood the meaning of those things, and this because of the hardness of their hearts. But there were many who were so hardened that they would **not look**, therefore they perished." — [Alma 33:19–20](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/33.md)

They *would not look*. Not because it was hard, but because it was *easy*. Alma's rhetorical sting: "if ye could be healed by merely casting about your eyes that ye might be healed, would ye not behold quickly?" ([Alma 33:21](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/33.md))

**The Type Becomes an Idol — A Warning:**

Centuries later, the brazen serpent Moses had made was still around. And the Israelites had turned it into an object of worship:

> "He removed the high places, and brake the images, and cut down the groves, and brake in pieces the **brasen serpent** that Moses had made: for unto those days the children of Israel did **burn incense to it**: and he called it **Nehushtan**." — [2 Kings 18:4](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/2-kgs/18.md)

Hezekiah had to *destroy* the brazen serpent because the people were worshipping the symbol instead of looking *through* the symbol to God. The type that once pointed people to Christ had become an idol. Even Christ's serpent can be perverted when you stop looking through it and start worshipping the thing itself.

This is a pattern worth watching. The instrument of salvation becomes an instrument of idolatry when people worship the symbol instead of what it points to.

### 5th Seal (The Prophets → Christ): The Serpent in Prophecy

**Isaiah sees the serpent, the dragon, and the leviathan converging:**

> "In that day the Lord with his sore and great and strong sword shall punish **leviathan** the piercing **serpent**, even **leviathan** that crooked **serpent**; and he shall slay the **dragon** that is in the sea." — [Isaiah 27:1](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/isa/27.md)

Three names — leviathan, serpent, dragon — all for the same entity. Isaiah sees his end: "in that day" the Lord will slay him. And Isaiah also prophesies a serpent-to-serpent succession:

> "out of the **serpent's** root shall come forth a **cockatrice**, and his fruit shall be a **fiery flying serpent**" — [Isaiah 14:29](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/isa/14.md)

A serpent that gives birth to a fiercer serpent, whose offspring is fiercer still — a fiery *flying* serpent. The progression: serpent → cockatrice → fiery flying serpent. The creature gains venom. It gains *wings*. This is the serpent becoming the dragon in prophetic poetry.

**The Psalmist promises victory over both:**

> "Thou shalt tread upon the **lion** and **adder**: the young lion and the **dragon** shalt thou trample under feet." — [Psalm 91:13](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/ps/91.md)

The one who dwells "in the secret place of the most High" will trample the dragon underfoot — echoing the Genesis promise that the woman's seed would bruise the serpent's head.

**Jacob's prophecy about Dan:**

> "**Dan** shall be a serpent by the way, an **adder** in the path, that biteth the horse heels, so that his rider shall fall backward." — [Genesis 49:17](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/gen/49.md)

Even among the tribes of Israel, serpent imagery attaches to a people — Dan as the serpent tribe. Not all serpent imagery is Satan. Context and direction always matter.

### Christ's Ministry: The Serpent Claimed and Redeemed

Jesus explicitly claims the serpent symbol and redeems it:

> "as Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, even so must **the Son of man** be lifted up" — [John 3:14](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/john/3.md)

And then gives his disciples a serpent instruction:

> "Be ye therefore **wise as serpents**, and **harmless** as doves" — [Matthew 10:16](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/matt/10.md)

"Wise as serpents" — take the serpent's best quality (wisdom, awareness, strategic thinking) but pair it with harmlessness. This echoes the Ammon pattern from the [beguile study](priestcraft-beguile.md) — "wise, yet harmless" ([Alma 18:22](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/18.md)). Take the wisdom, leave the venom.

Paul warns that the Garden serpent's method continues into the apostolic age:

> "as the **serpent** beguiled Eve through his subtilty, so your **minds** should be **corrupted** from the **simplicity** that is in Christ" — [2 Corinthians 11:3](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/2-cor/11.md)

The serpent's target in Paul's day is the *mind* — corrupting people from the simplicity of the gospel through false apostles who "transform themselves" into ministers of righteousness ([2 Corinthians 11:13–15](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/2-cor/11.md)).

### The Last Days / 6th–7th Seals: The Serpent Becomes the Dragon

In Revelation, the full unmasking happens. The serpent is finally seen in his true form:

> "And there appeared another wonder in heaven; and behold a **great red dragon**, having **seven heads** and **ten horns**, and **seven crowns** upon his heads." — [Revelation 12:3](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rev/12.md)

The serpent started in the Garden as one creature with one voice and one lie and one target. Now he is a dragon with **seven heads** — seven centers of power, seven kingdoms, seven institutions. He has **ten horns** — military, political, economic force. He wears **crowns** — he rules openly.

**The Growth Pattern:**

| Age | Form | Method | Scale |
|-----|------|--------|-------|
| Garden | Serpent | Subtlety, one-on-one | Individual |
| Enoch–Noah | Hidden power | Secret combinations | Tribal/regional |
| Moses | Competing serpents | Counterfeit miracles | National |
| Kings & Prophets | Leviathan / dragon | Corrupt kingdoms | Empire |
| Apostolic | False apostles | "Angel of light" disguise | Institutional |
| Last Days | Great red dragon | Open war, all methods | Global |

The progression: **subtle** → **organized** → **counterfeit** → **massive** → **disguised** → **overt**.

The serpent grows into the dragon not by changing its nature but by expanding its *reach*. The same craft that worked on one woman in a garden now works through institutions, nations, media, and systems. The guile doesn't change — the scale does.

### The Dragon Pursues the Woman

In Revelation 12, the dragon can't destroy the man child (Christ), so he turns on the woman (the Church):

> "And when the dragon saw that he was cast unto the earth, he persecuted the woman which brought forth the man child" — [Revelation 12:13](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rev/12.md)

> "And the **serpent** cast out of his mouth **water as a flood** after the woman, that he might cause her to be carried away of the flood" — [Revelation 12:15](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rev/12.md)

Notice the method: a *flood from his mouth*. Words. Lies. Propaganda. Information. The serpent's weapon has always been speech — "hath God said?" The dragon's version is a flood of it, aiming to overwhelm and sweep away with sheer volume.

But the earth helps the woman: "the earth opened her mouth, and swallowed up the flood" ([Revelation 12:16](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rev/12.md)). God provides refuge.

### The Beast that Speaks as a Dragon

> "And I beheld another beast coming up out of the earth; and he had two horns **like a lamb**, and he **spake as a dragon**." — [Revelation 13:11](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rev/13.md)

This is the final-form counterfeit: something that *looks like a lamb* (looks like Christ, looks gentle, looks religious) but **speaks as a dragon**. The dual-symbol problem at its most dangerous. The lamb-dragon is the priestcraft of the last days — the serpent wearing the fleece, the craft wearing the hood.

### The Dragon Bound and Loosed

> "And he laid hold on the **dragon**, that **old serpent**, which is the Devil, and Satan, and **bound** him a thousand years" — [Revelation 20:2](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rev/20.md)

Four names in one verse: dragon, old serpent, Devil, Satan. John wants us to see that this is the same being from the beginning — every serpent reference in every age traced back to this one entity. The "old" tells us we're looking at the Garden serpent, now grown to dragon size, finally chained.

And after the millennium:

> "when the thousand years are expired, Satan shall be **loosed** out of his prison, and shall go out to **deceive** the nations" — [Revelation 20:7–8](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rev/20.md)

One last deception. Then:

> "the devil that **deceived** them was cast into the **lake of fire**" — [Revelation 20:10](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rev/20.md)

The serpent's head is finally and permanently crushed. The promise from [Moses 4:21](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/4.md) fulfilled.

## How the Serpent Grows into the Dragon

The user's question was perfect: *how does the serpent grow?*

Looking across the ages, the growth follows a pattern:

1. **Subtlety** (Garden) — One voice, one question, one lie. Individual temptation.
2. **Organization** (Enoch–Noah) — Secret covenants, combinations, systems of mutual corruption.
3. **Counterfeit** (Moses) — Magicians' serpents mimicking God's serpent. "I can do that too."
4. **Institution** (Kings/Prophets) — Corrupt kingdoms ("Pharaoh king of Egypt, the great dragon" — [Ezekiel 29:3](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/ezek/29.md)). The serpent becomes the leviathan — a nation-scale beast.
5. **Disguise** (Apostolic) — "Satan himself is transformed into an angel of light" ([2 Corinthians 11:14](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/2-cor/11.md)). The serpent hides inside religious clothing.
6. **Totality** (Last Days) — Seven heads, ten horns, crowns. Every method at once. The dragon doesn't need to be subtle anymore because he has *power*. Though he still uses subtlety when it serves ([2 Nephi 28:21](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/28.md) — "leadeth them away carefully down to hell").

The serpent becomes the dragon the same way craft becomes institution: **one lie at a time, organized and scaled.**

Isaiah prophesied this escalation: "out of the **serpent's** root shall come forth a **cockatrice**, and his fruit shall be a **fiery flying serpent**" ([Isaiah 14:29](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/isa/14.md)). A serpent gives birth to something more venomous, which produces something that can *fly*. The serpent gains venom. It gains wings. It becomes the dragon.

## How We Fight

### They Overcame Him

Revelation 12:11 gives the answer in three clauses:

> "And they overcame him by **the blood of the Lamb**, and by **the word of their testimony**; and they **loved not their lives unto the death**."

Three weapons against the dragon:
1. **The blood of the Lamb** — the Atonement. The serpent's power is sin and death. Christ's blood breaks both.
2. **The word of their testimony** — bearing witness. The serpent works through lies; testimony works through truth.
3. **Loving not their lives unto the death** — total commitment. The serpent's methods (rage, lull, flatter) all target comfort and self-preservation. When you're willing to lose everything, the serpent has nothing to threaten you with.

### Look and Live

The brazen serpent teaches the simplest and most profound defense:

> "if ye could be healed by merely casting about your eyes that ye might be healed, would ye not behold quickly?" — [Alma 33:21](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/33.md)

Look to Christ. That's it. The cure for the serpent's bite is looking at God's serpent — the one lifted up. The tragedy is that "there were many who were so hardened that they would **not look**" ([Alma 33:20](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/33.md)). Not because looking was hard, but because they didn't believe it would work.

Elder Asay taught: "The type raised up in the wilderness, the serpent on the pole, symbolized Christ upon the cross. Jesus himself taught this truth." ([Elder Asay, "Look to God and Live," October 1978](../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/1978/10/look-to-god-and-live.md))

### The Armor

> "Put on the whole armour of God, that ye may be able to stand against the **wiles** of the devil" — [Ephesians 6:11](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/eph/6.md)

As the [beguile study](priestcraft-beguile.md) showed, "wiles" = "beguilements." The armor of God is armor against beguiling. You don't out-guile the serpent. You put on truth, righteousness, faith, salvation, the word of God. You become guileless.

### Wise as Serpents, Harmless as Doves

> "Be ye therefore **wise as serpents**, and **harmless** as doves" — [Matthew 10:16](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/matt/10.md)

Jesus doesn't tell us to fear the serpent. He tells us to *learn from it*. Take the wisdom — the awareness, the strategic thinking, the ability to read situations — but leave the venom. The serpent's knowledge without the serpent's intent. This is what Ammon did: "wise, yet harmless" ([Alma 18:22](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/bofm/alma/18.md)).

### God's Serpent Swallows the Counterfeits

> "Aaron's rod **swallowed up** their rods" — [Exodus 7:12](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/ex/7.md)

In the end, God's power doesn't just resist the counterfeits — it *consumes* them. Aaron's serpent ate the magicians' serpents. The true thing destroys the false. The seed of the woman crushes the serpent's head. The angel lays hold on the dragon and binds him.

We fight the serpent not by becoming more serpent-like, but by holding up the true serpent — Christ lifted up, Christ crucified, Christ risen — and pointing everyone to look at him.

## The Thread: From Genesis to Revelation

The whole story of scripture, told through the serpent:

1. **Pre-mortality** — The dragon rebels, is cast out ([Revelation 12:7–9](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rev/12.md))
2. **Garden** — Appears as a subtle serpent, beguiles Eve ([Genesis 3:1](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/gen/3.md); [Moses 4:5–6](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/4.md))
3. **The Promise** — The woman's seed will crush his head ([Moses 4:21](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/moses/4.md))
4. **The Flood** — Secret combinations bring destruction; God cleanses and starts again
5. **Moses** — God's serpent swallows the counterfeits ([Exodus 7:12](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/ex/7.md))
6. **The Brazen Serpent** — Christ prefigured: "look and live" ([Numbers 21:8–9](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/num/21.md))
7. **The Type Corrupted** — Israel worships the symbol instead of what it points to; Hezekiah destroys it ([2 Kings 18:4](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/2-kgs/18.md))
8. **The Prophets** — Isaiah sees serpent → cockatrice → fiery flying serpent ([Isaiah 14:29](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/isa/14.md)); leviathan and dragon to be slain ([Isaiah 27:1](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/ot/isa/27.md))
9. **Christ** — "As Moses lifted up the serpent... even so must the Son of man be lifted up" ([John 3:14](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/john/3.md))
10. **Apostolic Age** — Paul: "as the serpent beguiled Eve, so your minds should be corrupted" ([2 Corinthians 11:3](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/2-cor/11.md))
11. **Last Days** — The great red dragon, seven heads, ten horns, open war ([Revelation 12:3](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rev/12.md))
12. **The Millennium** — The dragon bound, "that old serpent" ([Revelation 20:2](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rev/20.md))
13. **The End** — Cast into the lake of fire, forever ([Revelation 20:10](../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/nt/rev/20.md))

The serpent is there at the beginning. The dragon is there at the end. And at every stage, the Lord provides a counter-serpent, a way to look and live, a rod that swallows the imitations.

The whole story is the contest between the two serpents. And the story ends with the true serpent — the Son of Man, lifted up — swallowing the dragon whole.
