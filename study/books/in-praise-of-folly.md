## Tensions & Objections

The strongest objection to Erasmus's *In Praise of Folly* (when properly digested) is that satire, however brilliant, offers no constructive alternative. Folly's invective against clerics, scholars, and princes exposes hypocrisy and vanity with devastating wit — but exposure is not reform. Erasmus's humanist faith in education and gentle persuasion may have been naive in the face of entrenched institutional power. A reader could reasonably ask: if folly is so universal and so deeply woven into human nature, what is left to do but laugh? The book risks being a mirror that shows us our absurdity but gives us no lever to change it.
