# Two Methods, One Truth: What Joseph Said About Light Before Maxwell Knew

> "We are grateful to know that there are two methods of gaining needed knowledge: (1) the evolving disclosures of man discovered by the scientific method and (2) the truths disclosed by the spiritual method, which begins with faith in God and relies on scriptures, inspired teaching, and personal revelation. There is no ultimate conflict between knowledge gained by these different methods because God, our omnipotent Eternal Father, knows all truth and beckons us to learn by both methods."
> — [President Dallin H. Oaks, "Coming Closer to Jesus Christ," BYU devotional, February 2026](../../../gospel-library/eng/broadcasts/miscellaneous-events/2026/02/12oaks.md)

President Oaks said the same thing in 2015 ([Liahona, March](../../../gospel-library/eng/liahona/2015/03/stand-as-witnesses-of-god.md)) and again from BYU in February 2026. The frame is settled. God knows all truth. Two methods reach toward Him. They will agree at the end because there is only one reality.

The covenant in Oaks's framing is reciprocal. Faith does not need to be afraid of the laboratory. The laboratory does not need to be condescended to by faith. Where the two methods touch the same fact, they will eventually confirm one another.

This study takes that covenant at face value and asks a specific question: **what did Joseph Smith tell us about light, between 1832 and 1843, that physics did not catch up to until decades later?** And: where does the fit fail, so we can stop the project before it becomes the kind of clever sermon that flatters the gospel by lashing it to whatever was on last week's *Nature* cover?

The doctrinal floor for this work was laid in [study/truth.md](../../truth.md) (the matter spectrum) and in [study/truth-modern-prophets.md](../../truth-modern-prophets.md) (Burton, Uchtdorf, Maxwell). The new step here is opening the actual physics texts — now finally in our library as quotable sources — and reading them next to the revelations.

## I. The Doctrine, Read Slowly

The text comes first. Whatever physics says, the revelation has its own integrity.

In December 1832, in a letter the Prophet called "the olive leaf … plucked from the Tree of Paradise," the following came:

> "He that ascended up on high, as also he descended below all things, in that he comprehended all things, that he might be in all and through all things, the light of truth;
>
> Which truth shineth. This is the light of Christ. As also he is in the sun, and the light of the sun, and the power thereof by which it was made.
>
> As also he is in the moon, and is the light of the moon, and the power thereof by which it was made;
>
> As also the light of the stars, and the power thereof by which they were made;
>
> And the earth also, and the power thereof, even the earth upon which you stand.
>
> And the light which shineth, which giveth you light, is through him who enlighteneth your eyes, which is the same light that quickeneth your understandings;
>
> Which light proceedeth forth from the presence of God to fill the immensity of space—
>
> The light which is in all things, which giveth life to all things, which is the law by which all things are governed, even the power of God who sitteth upon his throne, who is in the bosom of eternity, who is in the midst of all things."
>
> — [D&C 88:6-13](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)

Eight verses. Read them again, slowly, as cosmology rather than as devotion.

The Light of Christ is *one substance* that does at least seven things at once: (1) it shines as the visible light of the sun, moon, and stars; (2) it is the power *by which those bodies were made*; (3) it is in the earth itself; (4) it is in the eye that sees; (5) it is the same substance that *quickens understanding*; (6) it *proceeds forth* from God's presence; (7) it *fills the immensity of space*. And then a single line gathers the whole picture: it "is the law by which all things are governed."

In May 1833, five months later, Joseph received D&C 93. The same substance is named again, this time identified with intelligence:

> "Man was also in the beginning with God. Intelligence, or the light of truth, was not created or made, neither indeed can be."
> — [D&C 93:29](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93.md)

> "The glory of God is intelligence, or, in other words, light and truth."
> — [D&C 93:36](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93.md)

A decade later, in May 1843, the doctrine acquired its sharpest edge:

> "There is no such thing as immaterial matter. All spirit is matter, but it is more fine or pure, and can only be discerned by purer eyes."
> — [D&C 131:7](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/131.md)

And:

> "There is a law, irrevocably decreed in heaven before the foundations of this world, upon which all blessings are predicated— And when we obtain any blessing from God, it is by obedience to that law upon which it is predicated."
> — [D&C 130:20-21](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/130.md)

That is the textual base. Light fills space. Light is the law. All matter is one category, refined or coarse. Blessings are functions of obedience to law.

## II. The Words in 1828 English

A revelation lives inside a vocabulary. To read Joseph's words as Joseph and his hearers read them, we need the dictionary that was on the parlor table.

Webster's *American Dictionary of the English Language*, published 1828, is the closest record we have of what these words meant when Joseph spoke them.

**LAW** (Webster, 1828, "Physical laws, or laws of nature"): "The invariable tendency or determination of any species of matter to a particular form with definite properties, and the determination of a body to certain motions, changes, and relations, which uniformly take place in the same circumstances, is called a physical law. These tendencies or determinations … have been established by the Creator, and are, with a peculiar felicity of expression, denominated in Scripture, ordinances of heaven."

That is not a metaphor. In 1828, the physics sense of *law* was defined as established by the Creator — and Noah adds that scripture calls these very laws "ordinances of heaven." When the revelation says light "is the law by which all things are governed," Joseph and his contemporaries did not have to translate from spiritual to physical. The word already meant both.

**GLORY** (Webster, 1828, sense 1): "Brightness; luster; splendor." Noah's own gloss reaches for the Transfiguration — 2 Peter 1:17, where, he notes, the latter *glory* "refers to the visible splendor or bright cloud that overshadowed Christ at his transfiguration." Sense 3 adds the painter's term: "The circle of rays surrounding the head of a figure in painting."

So when D&C 93:36 says "the glory of God is intelligence, or, in other words, light and truth," the word *glory* in 1828 was already a physical term — brightness, luster, the visible splendor on the mount. The verse compresses what the dictionary already linked. Glory ≡ brightness ≡ intelligence ≡ truth. The chain is tight because three of those four were partly synonymous before Joseph wrote.

**INTELLIGENCE** (Webster, 1828, senses 1 and 4): "Understanding; skill." And: "A spiritual being; as a created intelligence. It is believed that the universe is peopled with innumerable superior intelligences."

In Joseph's English, *intelligence* could mean either understanding or an actual being. Both at once. So D&C 93:29, in saying "Intelligence, or the light of truth, was not created or made," used a word that already pointed in two directions: knowing as a capacity, and a knower as an entity. The doctrine that intelligences are eternal is *also* the doctrine that the substance of knowing is eternal. Joseph did not have to invent that double meaning. The dictionary supplied it.

**TRUTH** (Webster, 1828, sense 1): "Conformity to fact or reality; exact accordance with that which is, or has been; or shall be."

D&C 93:24, given five months after Webster's definition was three years in print: "Truth is knowledge of things as they are, and as they were, and as they are to come."

Webster: *was, is, shall be*. Joseph: *were, are, are to come*. Same triple temporal structure, near-identical phrasing. The revelation enters the language already prepared for it.

**MATTER** (Webster, 1828, senses 2 and 3): "Body; substance extended; that which is visible or tangible; as earth, wood, stone, air, vapor." But the genuine entry's third, philosophic sense already strains past that: "the substance of which all bodies are composed; the substratum of sensible qualities, though the parts composing the substratum may not be visible or tangible. … The imponderable substances are destitute of weight, as light, caloric, electricity, and magnetism."

In common speech, then, 1828 *matter* meant the visible and tangible — but the philosophic sense already admitted imponderable matter, with light first on the list. Joseph's 1843 revelation pushed in the same direction and further: "All spirit is matter, but it is more fine or pure, and can only be discerned by purer eyes." Not a rupture with his era's vocabulary; an extension of its finest sense to the one substance philosophy had walled off — spirit. The verse keeps the word *matter* while removing the perceptibility constraint entirely.

The vocabulary was loaded for what was coming. Webster handed Joseph a set of terms in which physical and spiritual meanings were already overlapping. The revelation collapsed the remaining distance.

## III. What Physics Discovered, and When

The revelations are 1832, 1833, 1843. Now the science.

In 1832, when D&C 88 was given, the dominant theory of light was Newton's corpuscular hypothesis — light as small particles emitted from luminous bodies. Thomas Young's 1803 double-slit experiment had embarrassed the corpuscular view by demonstrating wave interference, but the wave theory was not unified with anything else. Light was its own thing, separate from electricity, separate from magnetism, separate from heat.

The unification came in stages, all of it after Joseph was killed in 1844:

- **1820** — Hans Christian Ørsted discovered that electric current produces a magnetic field. Electricity and magnetism were related, but no one knew how.
- **1831** — Michael Faraday demonstrated electromagnetic induction. He proposed that fields, not particles, were the right way to think about electric and magnetic phenomena. (One year before D&C 88. Joseph could not have read Faraday's *Experimental Researches in Electricity*, which began publication in 1839.)
- **1865** — James Clerk Maxwell published *A Dynamical Theory of the Electromagnetic Field*, unifying electricity, magnetism, and light into a single set of equations. He showed that light is an electromagnetic wave propagating at a calculable speed. *Twenty-one years after Joseph's death.*
- **1900** — Max Planck introduced energy quanta to explain blackbody radiation, the first crack in classical physics ([V3 §6.1](../../../books/openstax/university-physics-volume-3/chapters/6-1-blackbody-radiation.md)).
- **1905** — Einstein explained the photoelectric effect by treating light as discrete packets of energy ([V3 §6.2](../../../books/openstax/university-physics-volume-3/chapters/6-2-photoelectric-effect.md)). Light is a wave *and* a particle. He also published special relativity, which proved $E = mc^2$ — that mass and energy are interconvertible. The matter/energy dualism that physics had assumed since Newton was collapsed into a single category.
- **1924** — Louis de Broglie proposed that matter has wave properties ([V3 §6.5](../../../books/openstax/university-physics-volume-3/chapters/6-5-de-broglies-matter-waves.md)). Confirmed in 1927 with electron diffraction.
- **1947–1950s** — Quantum electrodynamics (QED) and quantum field theory established that all particles are excitations of underlying fields. The electromagnetic field is fundamental; photons are its quanta. Matter is not made of *stuff* in the classical sense; it is made of patterned, persistent excitations of fields that fill space.

The OpenStax *University Physics Volume 3* states the consensus this way:

> "All bodies emit electromagnetic radiation over a range of wavelengths."
> — [V3 §6.1](../../../books/openstax/university-physics-volume-3/chapters/6-1-blackbody-radiation.md)

> "Wave-particle duality exists in nature: Under some experimental conditions, a particle appears to act as a particle, and under different experimental conditions, a particle appears to act a wave."
> — [V3 §6.6](../../../books/openstax/university-physics-volume-3/chapters/6-6-wave-particle-duality.md)

Set the timeline next to the doctrine. Joseph wrote in 1832 that one substance, called light, fills space, gives life to all things, is in the sun *and* in the eye, and is the law by which all things are governed. In 1832 no human knew this. The unification of light with electricity, magnetism, and energy did not arrive until Maxwell in 1865 at the earliest, and the quantum-field picture of matter as patterned excitations of fields that fill space did not arrive until the mid twentieth century.

This is not the claim that Joseph did physics. He gave us no equations. He gave us no constants. He did not foresee blackbody radiation or the photoelectric effect or wave-particle duality. The claim is narrower and stronger: he gave us a *structural picture* of reality — one substance, filling space, governing law, present in matter and in mind — that nineteenth-century natural philosophy did not yet possess and that twentieth-century physics independently built up to.

## IV. Where the Two Methods Touch

Now the actual conversation. Five places where the two methods reach the same shape from opposite sides.

### 1. Light fills space

Joseph (1832): light "proceedeth forth from the presence of God to fill the immensity of space" ([D&C 88:12](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)).

Physics (post-1865): the electromagnetic field is defined at every point in space. Light is an oscillation of that field. There is no place in the universe without it. The cosmic microwave background is the residue of the early universe's radiation, still filling all of space at about 2.7 Kelvin.

Same shape, different vocabulary. Joseph said *fill the immensity*. Maxwell said the field is everywhere defined. Both are talking about a continuous medium-like reality that has no gaps.

### 2. The physical light and the light that quickens understanding are one substance

Joseph (1832): "the light which shineth, which giveth you light, is through him who enlighteneth your eyes, which is the same light that quickeneth your understandings" ([D&C 88:11](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)).

Physics (modern): vision works by photons striking retinal photoreceptors, which trigger biochemical cascades that propagate as electrochemical signals through the optic nerve to the visual cortex. Cognition runs on the same physics — neurons fire because of electromagnetic interactions in cell membranes. From the moment the photon enters the eye to the moment a thought forms, the entire process is electromagnetic.

Joseph said the light that lets you see is the same light that lets you understand. Physics now says: yes, that is true at the level of the underlying mechanism. Photons, then ion channels, then neuronal firing, then cognition — one continuous chain governed by electromagnetic law.

This was the move Elder Burton made plainly in April 1981, and it was right: "all kinds of light are related to intelligence and truth" ([Burton, "Light and Truth"](../../../gospel-library/eng/general-conference/1981/04/light-and-truth.md)). What is new in 2026 is that we can open the photoelectric-effect chapter of an undergraduate physics text and watch the mechanism work.

### 3. All matter is one substance, with degrees

Joseph (1843): "There is no such thing as immaterial matter. All spirit is matter, but it is more fine or pure" ([D&C 131:7](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/131.md)).

Physics (post-1905): mass and energy are interconvertible by $E = mc^2$. Matter is condensed energy. In quantum field theory, particles are excitations of fields, distinguished from one another by their patterns and properties, not by being a different category of thing.

The Cartesian dualism of spirit-versus-matter that Western philosophy had assumed for two centuries was overturned by the revelation in one sentence and by physics in roughly two generations of work. The two answers are not identical. Physics does not say "spirit is fine matter." But they share a structural move: there is one category, ranging over a spectrum of refinement or condensation, not two categories sealed off from each other.

This was already worked carefully in [study/truth.md](../../truth.md) Part 3 ("The Matter Spectrum"). The new note here is just the physics timeline: the spirit-matter dualism collapsed in revelation in 1843. The mass-energy dualism collapsed in physics in 1905. Both collapses point to a unified substrate.

### 4. All things are governed by law

Joseph (1832): "All kingdoms have a law given; And there are many kingdoms; for there is no space in the which there is no kingdom; and there is no kingdom in which there is no space, either a greater or a lesser kingdom. And unto every kingdom is given a law; and unto every law there are certain bounds also and conditions. All beings who abide not in those conditions are not justified" ([D&C 88:36-39](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88.md)).

Joseph (1843): blessings are predicated on law; obedience to law produces the blessing ([D&C 130:20-21](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/130.md)).

Physics: every domain of nature is law-governed. Quantum mechanics governs the atomic scale. Relativity governs the high-velocity and high-mass scale. Thermodynamics governs energy flow. Each "kingdom" of phenomena has its own equations, with bounds and conditions of applicability. The Schrödinger equation does not describe a galaxy; the Einstein field equations do not describe an electron. But every regime is governed.

The 1828 dictionary entry for *law* in the physics sense already carried Joseph's frame: a rule of being "conceived of as imposed by the will of God." The sentence in D&C 88:36 — "All kingdoms have a law given" — is the doctrinal compression of what physics, two generations later, would discover by experiment in regime after regime.

### 5. Intelligence/energy is uncreated

Joseph (1833): "Intelligence, or the light of truth, was not created or made, neither indeed can be" ([D&C 93:29](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93.md)).

Physics: the first law of thermodynamics — that energy is neither created nor destroyed but only changes form — was established between 1842 (Mayer) and 1847 (Helmholtz), with experimental confirmation by Joule across the 1840s. In quantum field theory, the underlying fields are not "created" within the system they describe; they are the ontological floor.

Joseph's claim is about *intelligence*, not energy. The mapping is not one-to-one. But the structural move is shared between the two methods: the most fundamental layer of reality was not made and cannot be. Doctrine asserts it of intelligence. Physics asserts it of energy. Whether these are the same thing or two homologous things at different layers of the same reality is a question neither method, by itself, is positioned to answer.

## V. Where the Fit Breaks (and Why That's Fine)

The covenant from Oaks's two-methods framing requires honest reporting in both directions. Here are the places this study is not making a claim.

**The observer effect in quantum mechanics is not "watched until they were obeyed."** When the [V3 wave-particle duality chapter](../../../books/openstax/university-physics-volume-3/chapters/6-6-wave-particle-duality.md) says that asking "which slit?" destroys the interference pattern, the *observer* in question is any interaction that records information about the system. A photon detector "observes" without consciousness. The watching in [Abraham 4](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/pgp/abr/4.md) is something else entirely — an attentive shepherding of created processes by Beings with intent. The verbs share the surface form. The mechanisms do not. Conflating them is the kind of move that makes physicists roll their eyes and gives Latter-day Saint engagement with science the reputation it sometimes deserves.

**Joseph did not predict Maxwell's equations.** He gave us no $\nabla \cdot \mathbf{E} = \rho/\epsilon_0$. He named no constants. He did not describe the speed of light. The structural picture in D&C 88 is real, but it is not technical. Reading the revelation as if it were a coded textbook is a category error.

**Quantum mechanics does not prove agency.** The fact that quantum events are probabilistic is sometimes pressed into service as evidence for free will. It is not. Probabilistic does not mean willed. Heisenberg uncertainty is about the joint specifiability of conjugate variables, not about the soul. Agency rests on doctrinal grounds, not on hidden variables.

**The matter spectrum is not isomorphic to the energy spectrum.** D&C 131:7 says spirit is "more fine or pure" matter, discerned by purer eyes. This is a doctrinal claim about a layered reality with refinement levels, including ones that mortal instruments cannot access. Physics' particle physics is much more constrained — it describes interactions accessible to instruments, with equations that have been tested. The two pictures rhyme. They are not the same picture.

**Intelligence is not an energy field.** Joseph's intelligences have agency. They act for themselves ([D&C 93:30](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93.md)). They are personal. The electromagnetic field does not. To map "intelligence" onto "field" is to flatten the moral and personal weight of the doctrine into a metaphor that physics never intended to bear.

The disanalogies are honest and they are *not damaging*. Where the two methods touch, they touch. Where they don't, they don't. The covenant only requires that we report both, not that every detail line up.

## VI. So What Did Joseph Know That Maxwell Hadn't Discovered?

Now the answer to Michael's question, plainly.

Joseph Smith did not know electromagnetism in 1832 the way Maxwell would describe it in 1865. He had no math, no equations, no field formalism. What he had was a **structural picture of reality** that was not available in his vocabulary, his curriculum, or his time, and which the natural philosophy of his day did not contain:

1. **Light is one substance with multiple roles** — visible illumination, the power that made the heavenly bodies, the medium of cognition, the law of governance. Maxwell's unification of optics with electromagnetism in 1865 was the first time this *kind* of picture appeared in physics.

2. **Light fills space continuously** — not as scattered emissions but as a presence everywhere. The field concept arrived with Faraday in the 1830s–40s and was made rigorous by Maxwell. Joseph had it in 1832.

3. **Spirit and matter are one category** — Cartesian dualism, dominant in Western thought, was philosophically settled in 1832. Joseph called it false in 1843. Physics dissolved the analogous matter-energy duality in 1905.

4. **All scales of reality are law-governed** — including the spiritual. The blessing-predicated-on-law statement in [D&C 130:20-21](../../../gospel-library/eng/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/130.md) is the form of physical causation, applied to spiritual outcomes.

5. **The fundamental layer of reality is uncreated** — Joseph said this of intelligence in 1833. The first law of thermodynamics, that energy can be transformed but neither created nor destroyed, was established by Mayer, Joule, and Helmholtz across the 1840s. Both claims share the same shape: the deepest layer of the system is not made; it *is*.

What this is *not* is proof of inspiration. A skeptic can always say: lucky guesses, vague language, easy retrofitting. The two-methods framework does not need the proof. It needs only the consistency. Where the methods agree, the agreement strengthens the disciple's confidence that one God stands behind both. Where they disagree, the disciple keeps studying, knowing the disagreement is provisional.

What it *is* is a record that, in the year 1832, in a frontier letter dictated by a young man with three years of formal schooling, statements appeared about the nature of light and the structure of reality that the science of his day could not have produced and would not catch up to for thirty-three years on the easy items and over a century on the hard ones.

That fact does not need to be inflated. It is just a fact, sitting where it sits, available to anyone who wants to lay the dates next to the verses.

## VII. Becoming

The two-methods covenant lands somewhere personal. Three places.

**Trust the reading.** When you study the gospel, you are studying reality. The same reality the physics text describes. The relationship is not "spiritual stuff over here, real stuff over there." There is one God, one reality, two methods. When the Come Follow Me lesson lands on D&C 88, treat it as serious cosmology. The text means what it says.

**Take the laboratory seriously.** Oaks's covenant runs in both directions. The physics text is not a competitor to the scriptures. It is the second witness, working from the other side. When Sister Caitlin asks, ten years from now, why an apple falls, the answer is *both* "because Christ is the law by which all things are governed" *and* "because mass curves spacetime." Both. Without flinching at either.

**Refuse the cheap synthesis.** The temptation to make the gospel sound impressive by lashing it to whatever physics is currently fashionable is real, and it gets stronger every conference cycle. The doctrine of light in D&C 88 was true before Maxwell; it is true now; it will still be true when our current physics is replaced by something better. The *resonance* with physics is interesting and worth naming. The doctrine *does not need* physics to be true. Letting the reader feel the difference between *the gospel happens to rhyme with this* and *the gospel depends on this* is part of the work.

The conviction worth carrying out of this study is not "Joseph predicted modern physics." It is something quieter and stronger:

> God knows all truth. He gave Joseph a description of light in 1832 that fits with what Maxwell described in 1865 and what Einstein described in 1905 and what Feynman described in 1948. This is not because Joseph was a physicist. It is because there is one God and one reality, and revelations from God describe the reality God made.

That is the Oaks covenant in working order.

## Open Questions

1. The Joseph-precedes-Maxwell timeline is checkable. Are there earlier writings or known sources Joseph could have drawn on for the "light fills space" picture? Faraday's *Experimental Researches* began in 1839 — too late, even if Joseph had read British natural philosophy, which is itself unlikely. Newton's *Opticks* (1704) treated light as corpuscular, not field-like. Christian Huygens' *Traité de la Lumière* (1690) had a wave-in-aether picture that was roughly compatible with continuous medium, but the aether was mechanical, not law-substance. The 1832 picture in D&C 88 has no good antecedent in publicly available 1832 sources. This is a finding worth holding as provisional pending more thorough literature work.

2. Is there a Joseph Smith Papers commentary on the cosmological verses of D&C 88 that traces what Joseph and the high priests at Kirtland understood by these verses at the time? Worth checking.

3. The book of Abraham facsimiles (especially Facsimile 2 with Kolob and the governing-stars diagram) makes additional cosmological claims that intersect physics differently. A second study should look at Abraham 3 (governing intelligences and times-and-seasons) alongside modern astronomy and the question of celestial reference frames.

4. Lectures on Faith (1834-1835) addresses the attributes of God and includes language about light. We have it in the library at [books/lecture-on-faith/](../../../books/lecture-on-faith/). A pass through Lectures 1, 3, and 5 to see how Joseph and Sidney Rigdon reasoned about light, intelligence, and law in the same period would tighten the picture.

5. Is the wave-function-collapse / "watched until obeyed" question worth a separate, careful study? The disanalogy work above closes the door on the easy version of the analogy. A harder version might still be doctrinally useful, but it needs more careful philosophy of physics work than this study attempts.
